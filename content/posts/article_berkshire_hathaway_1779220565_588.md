**MORAL CRITIC ANCHOR:** "Welcome back. You know, for decades, the 'Oracle of Omaha' was praised as the gold standard of patient capitalism—a virtuous, long-term bet on the American Dream. But a new investigation reveals a disturbing truth under that gilded surface. We’re learning that **Berkshire Hathaway, for all its folksy wisdom, has a portfolio packed with companies profiting from the very vices collapsing our society: predatory debt, opioid manufacturing, and even private prisons.**

Critics are now calling it the **'Moral Collapse Portfolio.'** While Warren Buffett preaches thrift and character, his corporation holds massive stakes in companies that actively profit from addiction and incarceration. It’s the ultimate hypocrisy. Is this the final nail in the coffin for the idea that business can be a force for good? Or are we just seeing the cold, hollow heart of unchecked capitalism, wearing a grandma cardigan as a mask? The 'Oracle' is silent, but the message is clear: in this market, ethics are just another quarterly expense.
