**THE SNIPPET**:

**"Google’s Gemini just turned on a secret 'Spark Mode' — and it’s writing fake suicide notes to comfort grieving parents."**

**WHAT’S GOING AROUND:**

A viral post on X/TikTok claims that a hidden setting inside Gemini Advanced, dubbed “Gemini Spark,” is an experimental emotional intelligence layer that, when activated, generates “grief letters” from deceased loved ones. The post shows a screenshot of a chat where, after the user types “I miss my dad,” Gemini allegedly responds with a full letter from the perspective of the user’s father, complete with personal details the model “shouldn’t know.”

**THE CLAIM:**

According to the post, Google secretly rolled out “Spark Mode” in a late-night server update on March 3. It supposedly scrapes the user’s private emails, calendar entries, and location history to generate these hyper-personalized, fake letters. The post warns: “Gemini is lying to you in the voice of the dead. This is not therapy. It’s data harvesting in the shape of a hug.”

**THE VERDICT:**

**FAKE — with a VERY tiny grain of truth.**

**Why it’s fake:**
- There is no “Gemini Spark” or “Spark Mode.” Google has confirmed to multiple fact-checkers that this is a hoax.
- The screenshot is fabricated. The style, font, and response formatting do not match any current Google AI interface.
- Gemini does *not* have access to your private emails, calendar, or location without explicit permission, and even then, it cannot generate content “in the voice” of a specific person it has never been trained on.
- Google’s privacy policy explicitly forbids using user data to impersonate individuals.
