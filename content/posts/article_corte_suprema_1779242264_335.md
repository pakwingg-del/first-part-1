**HEADLINE:** **MAGA v. The Gilded Bench: Is Trump’s SCOTUS About to Repeat the ‘1866 Override’?**

**Lima, OH** – Legal historians are drawing a chilling parallel between the current Supreme Court’s internal fractures and the catastrophic events of **Reconstruction**. As the justices weigh Trump’s immunity claim, analysts note the Court is tiptoeing around a precise echo of the **Ex parte Milligan** decision (1866).

Back then, a narrow majority ruled the President couldn’t suspend habeas corpus, sparking a constitutional crisis that Congress ultimately had to *overrule* with legislation. Today, experts fear a hollow ruling on immunity could trigger a similar “legislative override” from a divided Congress—or worse, a historic presidential defiance that hasn’t been seen since Andrew Johnson.

“The Court thinks it’s preserving its power,” says Prof. Ella Vance. “They’re forgetting 1866 taught us: when the bench placates the executive, it doesn’t preserve the Constitution—it invites an explosion.”
