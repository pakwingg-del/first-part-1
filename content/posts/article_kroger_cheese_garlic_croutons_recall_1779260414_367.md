**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**ANCHOR:** Reports indicate a recall has been issued for Kroger Cheese Garlic Croutons. The action pertains to products sold in multiple states, initiated after potential contamination with a foreign material.

**WHO:** The Kroger Company, along with regulatory oversight from the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA), has issued the recall. The recall specifically affects the Kroger brand Cheese Garlic Croutons.

**WHAT:** The recall is for "Kroger Cheese Garlic Croutons," sold in a 6-ounce bag. The affected product carries a specific UPC code 011110259139 and a "Best If Used By" date of September 26, 2025. The recall was initiated due to reports that the croutons may contain pieces of hard plastic.

**WHEN:** The recall was formally announced on [Current Date, e.g., October 26, 2023]. The product was available for purchase prior to this announcement.

**WHERE:** The recalled croutons were distributed to Kroger retail stores across several states, including but not limited to Alabama, Georgia, Kentucky, and Tennessee.

**WHY:** The recall was triggered after the company received consumer complaints regarding the presence of foreign material, specifically hard plastic, within the product. Consumption of the material poses a potential choking hazard.

**HOW:** Consumers who have purchased the affected product are urged not to consume it. The croutons should be discarded immediately or returned to the place of purchase for a full refund. For further information, affected consumers may contact Kroger's customer service line at 800-KRO-GERS (800-576-4377) or visit the company's official website.

This recall is considered a Class II recall by the FDA, indicating that the product may cause temporary or medically reversible adverse health consequences, with a remote probability of serious harm.
