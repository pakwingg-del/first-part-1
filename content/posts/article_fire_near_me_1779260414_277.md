**LOCAL MAN SPOTS “GLITCH IN THE MATRIX” AFTER FIRE NEAR HOME BURNS IN PERFECT FIVE-POINT STAR PATTERN**

**SACRAMENTO, CA** — When Mark Delgado looked out his kitchen window Monday evening to see flames licking the hillside near his Elk Grove home, he did what any panicked suburbanite would do: he grabbed his phone, called 911, and started filming.

But it wasn’t until he reviewed the footage later that night that he noticed something that made him pull over on his way to get coffee.

“The smoke was billowing, fire trucks everywhere—it was chaos,” Delgado told *The Daily Anomaly*. “But when I zoomed in on the thermal lines of the burn scar from my drone the next morning, it hit me: the fire had perfectly scorched a five-pointed star pattern into the earth. And not just any star. The points were exactly aligned to the five nearest cell towers.”

Local fire officials initially dismissed the pattern as a “random contour burn caused by wind eddies,” but Delgado wasn’t convinced. Using publicly available FCC tower data and GPS overlays, he mapped the fire’s boundary and found that each vertex of the star intersected with a major fiber-optic node.

“It’s like the fire was drawn by a giant laser pointer from space,” Delgado said. “Or like some algorithm in the simulation glitched and rendered a texture map in the real world.”

The incident has sparked a wave of online speculation. Reddit user u/SimulacrumSurfer posted, “If the fire is the glitch, the matrix is trying to hide something by burning it. Check your own backyards.”
