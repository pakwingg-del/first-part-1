**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE: THE MORAL COLLAPSE OF THE AMERICAN SPIRIT**

**Critic Warns: 'Millennium Force' Isn't a Thrill Ride—It's a Gateway to Societal Nihilism**

SANDUSKY, OH – In what is being called the most damning indictment of modern entertainment since the Colosseum, prominent moral critic Dr. Helena Vance has declared that the roller coaster *Millennium Force* at Cedar Point is not a marvel of engineering, but a "high-velocity symbol of our ethical decay."

Speaking at a solemn press conference held beneath the ride’s towering 310-foot drop, Dr. Vance argued that the coaster’s core premise—unadulterated, purposeless speed—represents a civilization chasing instant gratification without moral consequence.

"This isn't about the tickle of adrenaline. This is about the normalizing of a purely selfish, hedonistic pursuit," Vance stated, clutching a worn copy of Aristotle’s *Nicomachean Ethics*. "The human body is a temple, not a projectile. To voluntarily submit to 92 seconds of pure, uneventful acceleration reveals a soul-starved populace that has traded narrative, virtue, and community for the hollow echo of 'wheee.'"

Vance’s 45-page white paper, *The G-Force of Greed: How Millennium Force Teaches Us to Enjoy the Fall*, accuses the coaster of "desensitizing our youth to the gentle pleasures of life—a walk in the woods, a warm loaf of bread, the slow, deliberate process of moral growth."

"The ride offers no redemption arc," she concluded. "No lesson learned. You enter a bored, self-interested person. You exit a bored, self-interested person who has simply chosen to fall faster. This is the downfall of society, one smooth, 93-degree drop at a time."
