**VERDICT: MISLEADING** – A viral video claims Senate Republicans *refused* to hold a single confirmation vote for President Trump’s Cabinet nominees during the first week of the new Congress. The clip pans over empty committee rooms with text reading: “GOP abandons Trump. Zero votes. Zero loyalty.”

**The Reality:** While the Senate *did* not hold any full floor votes on Trump’s nominees in that specific window, **this is standard procedural timing**—not a betrayal. The viral snippet omits that nominees must first clear committee hearings and markup sessions, which began on schedule. Five of the seven most contentious nominees (including Pete Hegseth for Defense) were actually voted out of committee *by voice vote* that same week, with floor votes scheduled for the following Monday. The “zero votes” claim is factually true only if you define “vote” as a full Senate roll call—a definition that conveniently ignores the critical committee-stage approvals. **Bottom line:** Standard process, not a “revolt.”
