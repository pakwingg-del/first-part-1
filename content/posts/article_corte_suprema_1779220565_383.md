**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**CORTE SUPREMA ANUNCIA SENTENCIA HISTÓRICA EN CASO DE DERECHOS TRIBUTARIOS**

(Miami, FL) – The Supreme Court of the United States issued a landmark 6-3 decision today in the case of *Corte vs. Federal Revenue Service*.

**What:** The ruling establishes a new federal precedent regarding the taxation of digital assets held in foreign accounts.

**Who:** The Court's conservative majority, led by Chief Justice John Roberts, delivered the opinion. The dissenting opinion was authored by Justice Elena Kagan.

**When:** The decision was released at 10:00 AM Eastern Standard Time.

**Where:** The ruling was announced from the Supreme Court building in Washington, D.C., and takes immediate effect across all federal jurisdictions.

**Why:** The case originated from a dispute involving a multinational technology conglomerate, which argued that current tax codes are ambiguous regarding crypto-assets held in overseas custodial wallets. The majority opinion held that "digital assets constitute property subject to domestic capital gains taxation," effectively closing a loophole previously exploited by high-net-worth entities.

**Key Details:** The ruling mandates that any digital asset held in a foreign account by a U.S. citizen or corporation must be reported and taxed upon transfer, not upon acquisition. Legal analysts predict this will trigger a significant capital outflow from non-compliant offshore platforms.

The full text of the opinion is now available on the official Supreme Court website.
