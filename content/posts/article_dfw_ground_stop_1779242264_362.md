**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**DFW AIRPORT ISSUES COMPREHENSIVE GROUND STOP DUE TO SEVERE WEATHER**

**DALLAS-FORT WORTH, TX —** The Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) has implemented a mandatory ground stop at Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport (DFW) effective as of 14:30 Central Standard Time.

**WHAT:** The FAA issued a full ground stop, halting all departing aircraft headed for DFW from other airports. Incoming flights already airborne are being placed into holding patterns or diverted to alternate facilities.

**WHY:** The stoppage is a direct result of a rapidly developing severe weather system, including reported wind shear, heavy thunderstorms, and reduced visibility. The National Weather Service has issued a Severe Thunderstorm Warning for the DFW metroplex until 16:00 CST.

**WHERE:** The order applies to all air traffic inbound to Dallas-Fort Worth International Airport (DFW) and has cascading delays reported at Love Field (DAL) due to airspace congestion.

**WHEN:** The ground stop began at 14:30 CST. The FAA has not yet issued an estimated time for the lift, stating it will be reassessed pending weather radar updates.

**WHO:** The FAA, in coordination with DFW Airport operations, is managing the situation. Passengers are urged to contact their respective airlines—including American Airlines, which operates its primary hub at DFW—for specific flight status updates.

**OUTLOOK:** Travelers should expect significant delays and potential cancellations throughout the afternoon and evening. The FAA advises all passengers to check flight status before departing for the airport.
