**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About Andrej Karpathy's Warning That AI Training Data is "Dead"**

- **The "Peak Data" Crisis:** Karpathy, a founding member of OpenAI and former Tesla AI Director, has declared that the internet's supply of unique, high-quality human-generated text is effectively exhausted. He argues that the era of "scaling laws" (where bigger models + more data = better performance) is ending for raw text, meaning future breakthroughs can't rely on just scraping more web pages.

- **The Synthetic Data Explosion is Here:** To overcome this plateau, Karpathy is betting on models training on **AI-generated data**. He calls this the "LLM-ization of everything," where AI writes textbooks, generates code examples, and creates dialogue for other AIs to learn from. The catch? Feeding models their own output can lead to "model collapse" (inbreeding-like degeneration) unless curated perfectly.
- **The "Two Body Problem" for AI:** Karpathy framed the future of AI research as managing the relationship between two models: a "Student" and a "Teacher." The Teacher generates synthetic data, while the Student learns from it. He warns this creates a complex, recursive feedback loop where researchers must ensure the Teacher doesn't pass on biases or hallucinations to the next generation.
- **The Rise of the "AGI Trainer" Job:** In a viral tweet, Karpathy predicted an entirely new job role: the **AGI Trainer**. This isn't a coder, but a data curator who understands pedagogy and reinforcement learning—someone who designs curricula for AI models, much like a human tutor creates lesson plans. He argues this will be a high-value, non-coding AI profession.
