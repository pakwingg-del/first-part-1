**TRENDING: BREAKING — "Dr. Trump" Appears in Pharmacy Ad for "TrumpRx" — But It’s NOT Real**

**Claim:** A video circulating on social media shows former President Donald Trump dressed in a white lab coat and stethoscope, promoting a new prescription drug called "TrumpRx: The Ultimate Immune Pill." The ad claims the pill "fixes everything from sniffles to deep state inflammation."

**Verdict: FAKE.** 

**The Reality:**
Fact-checkers have traced the viral clip to a sophisticated AI-generated deepfake. The "TrumpRx" packaging seen in the video is entirely fabricated, and the voiceover mimicking President Trump has been digitally cloned. No such medication exists. The FDA and Trump’s official spokespeople have confirmed the ad is a hoax designed to trick viewers into visiting a phishing website that attempts to steal personal medical and financial information.

**Bottom Line:** Do not click any links associated with this ad. There is no "TrumpRx" pill. If a video claims a former president is suddenly moonlighting as a pharmacist, it’s 99.9% AI — and 100% a scam.
