**VERDICT: REAL with context**

**Headline:** *CBP Issues Urgent Memorial Day Travel Warning: “Prepare for Record Delays & Enhanced Document Scrutiny”*

**SNIPPET:**

As millions prepare to kick off summer travel, U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) has issued an official warning for Memorial Day weekend—but social media claims about “random passport confiscations” and “massive checkpoints” are partially false.

**What’s real:** CBP confirmed that over 3 million travelers are expected per day at airports and land borders from May 24-28. The agency warns of “significant wait times”—up to 4 hours at major southern crossings—and urges travelers to renew Global Entry and use mobile passport apps. CBP also says it will deploy additional K-9 units and increased secondary inspections for agriculture and narcotics.

**What’s fake:** Viral posts on X and TikTok claim CBP will set up “random ID checkpoints 100 miles inland” and “demand vaccination cards or face detention.” CBP has not authorized any inland checkpoints for Memorial Day, and vaccination card requirements were dropped in 2023. A CBP spokesperson told FactCheck: “No random inland stops. Our focus remains at official ports of entry.”

**Bottom line:** Expect delays and thorough checks *at the border*—but the rumor of warrantless document checks in cities like Phoenix or San Antonio is false. Pack snacks, download the CBP One app, and ignore posts exaggerating agency authority.
