**BREAKING: Senate GOP Preps "WALLET VOTE" on Trump Picks—Here's What It Means for Your Money**

**Washington, D.C.** – Senate Republicans are set to vote on President Trump’s latest slate of nominees this week, but consumer advocates warn this isn't just a political power play—it’s a direct hit to your household budget.

Here’s the wallet check: These nominees are widely expected to push for **rolling back banking regulations**, including the **Dodd-Frank consumer protections** that prevent banks from charging you hidden overdraft fees and sneaky transaction costs. If confirmed, experts predict the average American family could see their **banking fees jump by $200-$400 a year** as "junk fees" return.

Plus, the nominees are signaling a **crackdown on the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB)** , the agency that has returned over **$19 billion** to cheated consumers since its creation. One of the frontrunners has called the CFPB an "unconstitutional agency"—a move that would strip away your best defense against predatory lenders and credit card fine print.

**Your money at risk:** If you've been gouged by a credit card, harassed by a debt collector, or hit with a surprise overdraft—the agency that fights for you could be gutted. And with **student loan interest rates** and **mortgage costs** already squeezing families, advocates say these nominees could make it harder than ever to escape debt.

**What you can do:** Consumer groups are urging voters to call their senators—especially moderates Susan Collins (ME) and Lisa Murkowski (AK)—**before the vote** and ask: "Will you protect my wallet, or the big banks?"

The vote is expected as early as next week. Your bank account is watching. 🏦⚠️
