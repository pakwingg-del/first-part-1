**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**SONY PLAYS HARDBALL: PlayStation Plus Base Tier Jumps 33%—Gamers Outraged as Subscription Costs Surge Past Streaming Services**

**Tokyo, Japan** — Sony Group Corp. today announced a dramatic price hike across all PlayStation Plus tiers, with the Essential plan rising from $59.99 to $79.99 annually—a 33% increase. The Premium tier now costs $159.99, up from $119.99.

**Impact:** Sony is betting that its exclusive content ecosystem—*The Last of Us* series, *Spider-Man 2*, and *God of War Ragnarök*—can weather a consumer backlash. The move adds a projected $1.8B in annual recurring revenue, but risks churn precisely when gamers face inflation across streaming (Netflix $15.49/mo, Xbox Game Pass $16.99/mo).

**CEO Take:** This is a margin play disguised as value. With PlayStation 5 sales slowing (down 25% YoY), Sony is extracting maximum revenue from its 47 million existing subscribers. The question: will loyalty hold, or will this accelerate the shift to Game Pass and PC gaming?

**Bottom Line:** Sony just lit $500M of goodwill on fire for $1.8B in cash. Wall Street will cheer the math today. Gamers will punish it with their wallets tomorrow.
