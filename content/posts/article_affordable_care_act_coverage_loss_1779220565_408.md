**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About Record ACA Coverage Loss**

Contrary to years of enrollment growth, millions of Americans are suddenly losing their Affordable Care Act plans. Here’s what’s driving the shocking reversal.

- **The "Unwinding" is Hitting Harder Than Expected:** The primary culprit is the end of the COVID-19 pandemic continuous coverage rule. States are now aggressively redetermining eligibility for Medicaid, and a massive "procedural churn" is underway. Millions who were incorrectly kept on Medicaid are being removed, but a shocking number of them *are not* successfully transitioning to ACA marketplace plans, leaving them uninsured.

- **A "Glitch" in the System Is Blocking Transfers:** The automated data system designed to seamlessly shift people from Medicaid to the ACA marketplace (known as the "no wrong door" policy) is failing. Technical errors are sending the wrong income data or failing to process applications entirely, creating a bureaucratic black hole where eligible individuals are simply falling off the radar.

- **"Unwinding" Disproportionately Affects Vulnerable Communities:** Data reveals that coverage losses are not spread evenly. Rural communities, people of color, and low-income families are being hit the hardest due to reduced access to in-person navigators, language barriers, and unstable mailing addresses for renewal notices.

- **The "Subsidy Cliff" is Looming for Millions:** Beyond the Medicaid unwinding, a separate crisis is brewing. Enhanced marketplace subsidies—responsible for record low premiums—are set to expire at the end of 2025. If Congress fails to act, premiums could more than double for millions of moderate-income families, triggering a second wave of coverage loss.
