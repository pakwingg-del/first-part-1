**Viral News Snippet:**

**Fact Check: #JusticeForJennySlatten – Did Her Ghost Appear in a Blackwater Reunion Photo?**

**The Claim:** Social media users are circulating a photo from a 2023 Blackwater security veterans’ reunion in San Diego, alleging that former colleague Jenny Slatten—the only female employee ever convicted in the 2007 Nisour Square massacre—appears as a “shadowy figure” in the background. Captions read, *“She’s still watching from the other side,”* implying a supernatural visitation.

**The Facts:**
- **✅ Real:** Jenny Slatten was a U.S. Army veteran and Blackwater contractor. In 2019, she was convicted on federal charges related to the Nisour Square shooting (the case was later vacated in 2021 after a Supreme Court ruling).
- **❌ Fake:** The “ghost figure” is actually a blurry silhouette of a caterer carrying a tray. The original photo was taken at sunset, creating lens flare and motion blur. No credible evidence of paranormal activity exists.
- **⚠️ Misleading Context:** The reshare surge appears coordinated with a new podcast series *“Blackwater Unsealed,”* which discusses Slatten’s controversial prosecution. Some accounts are using the ghost claim to boost episode downloads.

**Verdict:** 🔴 *Fake.* No ghost. Jenny Slatten is alive, out of prison, and has never been reported deceased. The viral narrative is fabrication designed to exploit the emotional weight of the Nisour Square tragedy. Always cross-reference reunion photos with non-sensational reporting.
