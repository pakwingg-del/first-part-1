**AITA for telling my CEO that his "innovative" plan to roast marshmallows over the burning wreckage of our 401(k)s is a "teambuilding exercise too far"?**

So my company (let's call them Bain Capital 2.0) held our quarterly "synergy summit" in the middle of what is now being called the "Apocalypse of Fiscal Year 2024." The building was actively on fire. Like, not a metaphor. HR was handing out tiny umbrellas and saying "it's a nice mist." Meanwhile, the C-suite was in a glass conference room looking at a PowerPoint titled "Leveraging The Heat."

TL;DR: CEO stood on a desk, said we need to think *outside the burning box*, and asked if anyone had any "cool new ideas to monetize the ambient temperature." A junior analyst from risk management said, "Sir, the fire alarms are louder than your growth projections." The CEO smiled, gave him a golden marshmallow, and said "That's the spirit, Chad. Now go optimize the smoke stack."

I yelled, "Maybe we can use the sprinkler water to make ice for the drinks at your retirement party?" Got a PIP. AITA for pointing out the profitability of arson?
