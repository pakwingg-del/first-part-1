**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**DATELINE: SUPREME COURT OF THE UNITED STATES, WASHINGTON, D.C.**

**WHAT:** In a landmark 6-3 decision, the Supreme Court of the United States has issued a ruling that fundamentally alters the legal framework governing administrative agency authority, directly impacting regulatory enforcement across the executive branch.

**WHO:** The majority opinion, authored by Chief Justice John Roberts, was joined by Associate Justices Clarence Thomas, Samuel Alito, Neil Gorsuch, Brett Kavanaugh, and Amy Coney Barrett. The dissenting opinion was authored by Associate Justice Elena Kagan, joined by Associate Justices Sonia Sotomayor and Ketanji Brown Jackson.

**WHERE:** The ruling was delivered from the Supreme Court Bench in Washington, D.C., concluding the Court's current term. The specific case originated from the United States Court of Appeals for the Fifth Circuit.

**WHEN:** The decision was announced on Friday, June 28, 2024, at 10:00 AM Eastern Time.

**WHY:** The Court ruled the long-standing legal precedent *Chevron U.S.A., Inc. v. Natural Resources Defense Council, Inc.* (1984) is no longer legally tenable. The majority held that the *Chevron* doctrine, which required federal courts to defer to a federal agency's reasonable interpretation of an ambiguous statute, violates the separation of powers. The Court determined that Article III courts, not executive branch agencies, are constitutionally mandated to exercise "independent judgment" on questions of law. This decision vacates 40 years of federal administrative law, shifting interpretive authority from agencies to the judiciary.
