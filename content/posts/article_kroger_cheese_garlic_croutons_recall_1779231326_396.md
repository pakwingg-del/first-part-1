**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Kroger Cheese Garlic Croutons Recall**

- **The "Mystery Ingredient" Hazard:** This isn't your standard recall. Kroger has pulled their *Private Selection* Garlic & Cheese Croutons over **undeclared milk and soy**—a major threat for shoppers with severe allergies, not just lactose intolerance. The croutons were packaged in bags that may not list these common allergens.

- **Only One Size & Date Matters:** The recall is hyper-specific. It applies exclusively to the **8-ounce bag** with a "Best If Used By" date of **AUG 21 2025**. If your bag doesn't match this exact date stamp, you’re likely safe—but double-check the label anyway.

- **Where the Danger Hides:** These aren't just a salad topper. They’re sold nationwide under Kroger-owned banners, including **Fred Meyer, Harris Teeter, Ralphs, King Soopers, Smith’s, and Mariano’s**. If you shopped at any of these stores, your pantry might be a ticking time bomb.

- **The "No Reaction" Silent Threat:** The recall was sparked by a consumer complaint—no illnesses reported yet. But for allergy sufferers, a single crouton can trigger anaphylaxis. This is a proactive pull designed to prevent a crisis, not react to one.

- **Your Next Move (Don't Toss Them):** Do NOT eat them, but also don't just throw them away. Return the bag to the store for a **full refund**. If you can't make it back, contact Kroger’s customer service at **800-632-6900** for direct reimbursement instructions.
