**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Kroger Cheese & Garlic Croutons Recall**

- **The "Hidden Dairy" Danger:** This recall isn't just about bad bread. Kroger’s *Cheese & Garlic Croutons* are being pulled because they **may actually contain Milk**, yet are **not labeled as containing dairy.** For anyone with severe allergies, this is a silent time bomb in their salad.

- **The Exact Bag to Check:** Don't toss every crouton in your pantry. This recall is strictly for the **12-ounce Kroger Bagged Seasoned Croutons** with a specific **"Best By" date of December 1, 2025.** If your bag has a different date, you are likely in the clear.

- **Where the Mistake Happened:** It’s a case of "oops, wrong box." The croutons were accidentally packed in the *wrong* outer carton. While the bag says "Cheese & Garlic," the box it was shipped in was meant for a **different, non-dairy variant.** That mix-up allowed unlisted milk proteins to slip into the final product.

- **Health Risk is Immediate:** This is not a long-term spoilage issue. If you have a milk allergy or severe lactose intolerance, eating these croutons could trigger **hives, swelling, breathing trouble, or anaphylaxis.** The recall is for *potential* contamination, meaning the risk is present but not guaranteed in every single bag.

- **What You Get and Where to Return:** Kroger is offering **full refunds** for any affected bags. **Do not eat them.** Return them to any Kroger store (or its affiliates like Ralphs, Fry’s, or Smith’s), or throw them away immediately. For a direct refund line, call **Kroger Customer Connect at 1-800-632-6900.**
