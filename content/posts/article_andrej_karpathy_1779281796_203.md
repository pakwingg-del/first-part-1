**Andrej Karpathy: The AI Genius Who Says Your Kid Should Learn to Code on a Potato**

Andrej Karpathy, the former Tesla AI director and OpenAI co-founder, has sparked yet another firestorm—this time over his bold claim that today's smartphone-obsessed kids are "digitally malnourished" and should learn to code on cheap, low-power devices like the Raspberry Pi or even a "literal potato."

In a now-viral tweet thread, Karpathy argued that modern tech (with its polished apps, autocomplete, and endless distractions) is rotting young minds' ability to think computationally. "Learning on a $5 device that crashes twice an hour builds real problem-solving skills," he wrote. "You don't become a chef by ordering DoorDash."

Cue the chaos.

Local moms are up in arms: "So my 12-year-old needs to solder his own laptop before he can learn Python? Meanwhile, my taxes pay for his Silicon Valley yacht. Where's the common sense?" wrote one furious user in a suburban Facebook community group. Another added: "Tell me you've never had to help a kid with homework without telling me. This guy probably writes his grocery lists in binary."

Karpathy's defenders (mostly developers nostalgic for their Commodore 64 days) call it a brilliant wake-up call. Critics? They're calling it elitist techno-nostalgia that ignores the reality of underfunded schools.

Either way, the thread has 47K likes and counting—because nothing brings the internet together like a rich genius telling you your kid is doing it wrong.
