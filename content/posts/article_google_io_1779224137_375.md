BREAKING NEWS | Silicon Valley, California — May 14, 2025 (10:00 AM PT)

**Google I/O 2025: New AI Ecosystem and ‘Project Prism’ Headset Unveiled**

MOUNTAIN VIEW, CA — At the annual Google I/O developer conference, held today at the Shoreline Amphitheatre, Google CEO Sundar Pichai unveiled a comprehensive new suite of artificial intelligence tools and a surprise hardware prototype, marking a significant expansion of the company’s ecosystem.

**What happened?**
The keynote introduced "Gemini 3.0," a multimodal AI model designed to integrate search, workspace, and cloud services. Additionally, Google showcased "Project Prism," an augmented reality (AR) headset that promises seamless integration with Android devices.

**Where did it take place?**
The announcement was made during the opening keynote session at the Shoreline Amphitheatre in Mountain View, California, the company’s traditional headquarters location for its annual developer summit.

**When did this occur?**
The events took place during the morning keynote on Tuesday, May 14, 2025, the first day of the Google I/O conference.

**Who is involved?**
Sundar Pichai, Chief Executive Officer of Alphabet and Google, led the presentation. Key partners include hardware manufacturers Samsung and Qualcomm, who are collaborating on the chipset for "Project Prism." Major enterprise clients, including Ford and Verizon, are also named as early testers for the new AI workplace tools.

**Why is this significant?**
This announcement places Google in a direct competitive position with existing mixed-reality headsets while simultaneously reinforcing its dominance in the AI assistant market. Analysts note that "Project Prism," if released at the speculated price point of $299, could disrupt the consumer AR space.

This is a developing story. Further updates on availability, pricing, and developer API access are expected throughout the conference.
