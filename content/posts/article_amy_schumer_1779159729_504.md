**🚨 AMY SCHUMER’S LATEST PLOY COULD COST YOU MONEY - Consumer Alert 🚨**

Comedian Amy Schumer is under fire tonight, but not for a joke—and it’s *your* wallet that’s paying the price. The star is facing a massive backlash after it was revealed her production company quietly trademarked the phrase “Girl You Fine But What’s Your Credit Score?” for a new merch line. The problem? Small businesses have been selling that exact phrase on mugs and T-shirts for years.

Schumer’s legal team is now sending cease-and-desist letters to Etsy shops and local vendors demanding they pull the products or face lawsuits. Meanwhile, she’s selling her own version for $45—nearly double the price of the originals.

“This is a classic Hollywood power move,” says consumer advocate Laura Greene. “She’s taking an everyday joke that people have already bought and sold freely, slapping a trademark on it, and forcing out the little guys.”

So before you spend $12 on shipping or get hit with a surprise price hike, check who actually owns the phrase on your merch. And if you’re an Etsy seller with that slogan in your shop? You might want to lawyer up before your next paycheck disappears.
