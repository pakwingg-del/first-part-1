**Top 5 Things You Need to Know: CBP Issues Urgent Memorial Day Travel Warning**

As millions prepare for Memorial Day weekend, U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) has released a critical travel advisory. Here’s what you need to know before hitting the road or crossing the border.

- **Beware of "Zero Tolerance" on Drones & Fireworks:** CBP warns that unauthorized drones near border crossings are considered a national security threat and can result in immediate confiscation and federal fines. Similarly, bringing certain fireworks, even sparklers, across the border is strictly prohibited and could lead to prosecution.
- **Global Entry & Trusted Traveler Programs Face "Real-Time" Scrutiny:** Even with Global Entry or SENTRI, expect increased random secondary inspections. CBP says they are using new biometric scanners and mobile algorithms this year, potentially adding 15+ minutes to previously "fast-tracked" lanes.
- **Agricultural Inspections Are Ridiculously Strict:** Forget bringing that leftover avocado or homemade salsa from a BBQ. CBP is enforcing a "one-strike" policy for undeclared fruits, meats, or plants. A single mistake can result in a $1,000 fine—no warnings.
- **Wait Times Could Hit "Historic" Levels:** CBP data suggests that return waits on Sunday and Monday could exceed 6 hours at major ports of entry (San Ysidro, Buffalo, Detroit). Their advice? **Cross before 6 AM or after 10 PM.** The "worst window" is 2 PM to 8 PM.
- **New "Digital Check-in" Mandate for International Air Travel:** For the first time, CBP is asking all international air travelers arriving in the US (including citizens) to have their mobile boarding pass and passport ready for a "pre-clearance digital scan" before reaching the officer. Failure to do so triggers an automatic secondary referral.
