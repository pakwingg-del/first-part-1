🚨 **Top 5 Things You Need to Know About This FLASH FLOOD WARNING**

- **1. It’s NOT a “watch” — This is an ACTIVE emergency.**  
  A Flash Flood Warning means flooding is *happening now* or is *imminent*. Don’t wait for a second alert. Move to higher ground immediately. Even if the sky looks clear, water can rise in seconds.
- **2. 6 inches of moving water can knock you off your feet.**  
  That’s less than knee-high. Cars can be swept away in just 12 inches. If you encounter water across a road, **turn around, don’t drown.** Never drive around barriers.
- **3. Hidden dangers lurk under murky water.**  
  Floodwater can hide downed power lines (electrocution risk), broken glass, sewage, and wildlife like snakes. Avoid wading unless absolutely necessary.
- **4. Your phone is your lifeline — turn on Wireless Emergency Alerts (WEA).**  
  Make sure your device’s emergency alerts are enabled in settings. Also charge your phone *now*. You might lose power later.
- **5. Know your escape route before you need it.**  
  If you’re in a basement or low-lying area, plan two ways out. Move to upper floors or rooftops if water enters your home. Never stay in an attic without a way to break through the roof.

**⚠️ Bottom line:** Flash floods are the #1 weather-related killer in the U.S. — treat this warning like a fire drill. Act fast, stay high, and stay alive.
