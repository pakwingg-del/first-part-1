**HEADLINE:** Social Media Star Gabbie Gonzalez "Cancels" Her Own Funeral in Bizarre Livestream, Sparking Ethical Firestorm Over Death-as-Content

**SYDNEY —** In what critics are calling the "final nail in the coffin of digital decency," controversial influencer Gabbie Gonzalez has ignited a global ethical debate after hosting a live-streamed "pre-funeral wake" that culminated in her reading a pre-written obituary for her own career.

While fans lauded the move as "meta art," moral psychologists and cultural critics are sounding the alarm.

"This is the absolute nadir of the 'main character' syndrome," said Dr. Elara Vance, a professor of digital ethics at Oxford. "We have watched society transition from grieving in private to monetizing pain. Now, we are simulating the *process of death* for engagement. This isn't avant-garde; it's a clinical symptom of a society that has fully commodified the human soul. We are no longer people to these creators—we are voyeurs in a slow-motion train wreck they've built themselves."

The Society for the Preservation of Human Dignity issued a statement calling it "a reckless mockery of the sacred," warning that the normalization of "performance suicide" for fame desensitizes a generation to genuine tragedy.

As of press time, Gonzalez’s followers are already demanding a "Part 2."
