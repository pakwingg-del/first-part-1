**VERDICT: FAKE**

**Viral Snapshot:**

> **🚨 BREAKING: “Sky Lanterns” Spark Nationwide Airport Lockdown – Officials Warn of Carcinogenic Ash Rain**
>
> A major panic is sweeping social media after claims that “eco-friendly” sky lanterns released during Lunar New Year celebrations are causing widespread airport closures across the country. Viral posts allege that the lanterns’ coating, once burned, releases a fine ash laced with “benzene and asbestos-like fibers,” which is drifting into jet engines and triggering a public health crisis. One widely circulated video shows a jet engine spewing orange sparks, allegedly from ingesting a lantern.
>
> **What’s Really Happening:**
>
> **FAKE.** The video is actually a 2019 clip of a bird strike, and no airports have issued a single closure related to lanterns this week. While it is true that *some* cheap, non-biodegradable lanterns contain metal wiring that can be dangerous to aircraft and wildlife, the claims of “carcinogenic chemical clouds” and a nationwide aviation shutdown are entirely fabricated. The real issue? Authorities warn that *all* sky lanterns are illegal in 29 countries because of wildfire risk—not because of toxic ash rain. The viral “ash rain” photos are actually stock images of industrial pollution. **Bottom line: Do not share the panic. It is a hoax exploiting genuine environmental concerns.**
