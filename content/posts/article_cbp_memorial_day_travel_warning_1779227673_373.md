**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**ANCHOR:** Good evening. The United States Customs and Border Protection, or CBP, has issued a formal travel advisory for the upcoming Memorial Day weekend.

**WHAT:** A heightened operational alert and travel warning regarding significant delays at all ports of entry.

**WHO:** Issued by the Office of Field Operations, U.S. Customs and Border Protection. Affecting all international travelers, including U.S. citizens and lawful permanent residents.

**WHERE:** All land border crossings, international airports, and seaports across the continental United States.

**WHEN:** Effective from the pre-holiday surge on Thursday, May 22, through the conclusion of the holiday weekend on Monday, May 26.

**WHY:** CBP officials cite a projected record volume of travelers as the nation marks the unofficial start of summer. The agency warns that processing times could exceed two hours at major southern border crossings and key international airports. Officials attribute the delays to increased traffic volume and mandatory enforcement of all customs and immigration laws, particularly regarding agricultural products and commercial goods.

**KEY DETAILS:** CBP advises all travelers to utilize the CBP One mobile application for estimated wait times and to declare all items. The agency underscores that this is not a security threat alert, but a logistical readiness notification. Travelers are urged to plan alternate routes and allow for extended travel time.
