**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Mackenzie Shirilla Documentary**

- **The Infamous "Time of Death" Video:** The documentary centers on the chilling 11-second surveillance video that prosecutors say proves Shirilla deliberately accelerated into a brick wall at over 100 mph. The footage is dissected frame-by-frame by crash reconstruction experts, revealing a sudden, intentional steering jerk just before impact.

- **The "Pure Evil" Verdict:** The documentary pulls no punches in examining the judge's unprecedented courtroom statement, where she labeled Shirilla "pure evil." It explores the psychological profile of a teenager who showed zero emotion during sentencing, leaving even hardened detectives unsettled.

- **The Disputed Motive:** While prosecutors argue Shirilla murdered her boyfriend, Dominic Russo, and friend, Davion Flanagan, out of jealousy and a desire to end the relationship, the documentary delves into conflicting testimony from friends about her mental state, including claims of a secret pregnancy scare.

- **The Victims' Parents' Unfiltered Testimony:** For the first time in a major docuseries, the parents of both victims speak at length about the "digital trail" they found—texts, deleted photos, and late-night conversations—that they believe proves premeditation. One mother reads a poem written the night before the crash.

- **The Unanswered Question:** The documentary concludes with a haunting "what if" scenario: Was the crash a tragic suicide gone wrong, or a calculated double homicide? It leaves viewers with a single, split-second decision—and the blurry line between accident and atrocity.
