**DATELINE: WASHINGTON, D.C. - NOVEMBER 14, 2024**

**SUBJECT:** SENIOR SENATOR SUSTAINS INJURY IN CAPITOL COMPLEX INCIDENT.

**WHAT:** Senate Minority Leader Mitch McConnell (R-KY) was transported to Walter Reed National Military Medical Center earlier this afternoon following a fall on the marble stairs adjacent to the Capitol Rotunda.

**WHO:** The incident involves Senator Addison Mitchell McConnell III, 82, the longest-serving Senate party leader in U.S. history.

**WHEN:** The event occurred at approximately 2:47 PM Eastern Standard Time, immediately following a closed-door conference luncheon with Republican caucus members.

**WHERE:** The fall transpired on the west-facing staircase of the Capitol Building, an area frequently traversed by members transitioning between the Senate chamber and adjacent office buildings.

**WHY & HOW:** Preliminary reports from Senate attending physicians indicate the Senator "lost his footing" while descending the final three steps. Witnesses, including several Capitol Police officers and Senator McConnell’s chief of staff, reported that the Senator did not strike his head but landed heavily on his right side, resulting in a suspected wrist fracture and contusions. Aides acted swiftly, deploying a wheelchair before emergency medical services arrived.

**REGULATORY CONTEXT:** This marks the third documented public physical incident involving Senator McConnell in the calendar year, following a concussion in March and a freeze-up at a press podium in July. The events have intensified scrutiny regarding health disclosure protocols for senior leadership on Capitol Hill.

**STATUS:** The Senator is currently undergoing comprehensive evaluation. His office released a brief statement confirming he is "awake, alert, and in good spirits." A more detailed health advisory is expected within the next 12 hours.
