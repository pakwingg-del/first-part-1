**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**Subject:** Google I/O 2025: CEO Sundar Pichai Kills the Search Box, Unleashes "Gemini Live" as the New OS

**The Headline:** Google just declared the death of the search bar. At today's Google I/O, CEO Sundar Pichai didn't just roll out product updates; he launched a new operating paradigm. "Gemini Live" is now the primary interface for Android, Chrome, and Workspace. This isn't a chatbot. It's an autonomous, always-on "Agent OS" that watches your screen, reads your context, and executes tasks without you typing a single query.

**The Bottom Line:**
- **The Search Box is Gone:** Users will no longer "search"; they will "command." The traditional blue link page is being replaced by an agent that completes the task (booking, buying, coding) instantly.
- **Privacy is the Price:** The new OS requires deep, persistent context access. Google’s pitch: "Zero-data retention, but total personalization." Enterprise buyers will demand audits.
- **The Developer Shake-up:** Google is giving away a new API that lets any app "hand off" control to Gemini. This commoditizes the UI. App store utility is now measured by plug-in value, not user screen time.

**The Impact:**
- **Revenue Risk:** Google’s $200B ad business relies on page views. If Gemini answers without ads, where does the margin come from? Pichai hinted at a new "Action-based bidding" model—you pay when the agent buys, not clicks.
- **Competitive Checkmate:** Apple and Microsoft are now forced to respond. This isn't an update; it's a platform land grab. Google just bet the entire company on the thesis that "the search box is dead."
