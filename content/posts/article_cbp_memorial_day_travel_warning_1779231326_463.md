**VERDICT: PARTLY FALSE – THIS VIRAL CLAIM CONTAINS MISLEADING CONTEXT.**

**THE RUMOR:**
Social media posts are claiming that U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) issued a "Memorial Day Travel Warning" stating that Americans returning from Mexico or Canada will face "unprecedented 8-hour border wait times" and that "all vehicles will be searched" due to a "terrorist threat level upgrade."

**WHAT WE FOUND:**

**1. No Public "Terrorist Threat Upgrade" Was Issued.**
CBP has *not* publicly announced a change in the National Terrorism Advisory System (NTAS) for Memorial Day weekend. The last NTAS bulletin from DHS (April 2025) focused on domestic extremism and foreign influence, not a specific cross-border plot. Posts claiming a "threat level upgrade" are using an image of a generic, unclassified internal memo that lacks an official seal or date.

**2. "Unprecedented 8-Hour Waits" Are a Misinterpretation.**
CBP *does* issue seasonal travel advisories for Memorial Day, warning of heavy traffic. The agency's official website recommends travelers check wait times and use the CBP One app. However, the specific claim of "8-hour waits" is based on a single 2024 incident at the San Ysidro port of entry during a computer outage, not a 2025 policy change. Normal peak wait times for Memorial Day weekend are typically 1–3 hours.

**3. "All Vehicles Will Be Searched" is False.**
CBP is legally authorized to conduct "border searches" without a warrant, but it does not have the resources to search *every* vehicle. The agency uses intelligence-driven targeting. The claim appears to have originated from a misinterpretation of a 2020 policy memo regarding random compliance checks for agricultural products.
