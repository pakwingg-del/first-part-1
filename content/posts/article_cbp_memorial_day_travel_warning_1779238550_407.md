**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About CBP’s Memorial Day Travel Warning**

- **Record-Breaking Volume Expected:** CBP is bracing for the busiest Memorial Day weekend on record, with nearly 3 million travelers projected to cross U.S. borders daily. Prepare for wait times of 2–4 hours at major land ports.
- **QR Code Over Paper:** For the first time, CBP is urging all travelers to use the new Mobile Passport Control (MPC) app or the digital I-94 system. Paper forms will be delayed—digital passes get priority lanes at 35+ airports.
- **Zero Tolerance for Fireworks & Threats:** CBP officers are on high alert for explosives, fireworks, and open alcohol. Even sparklers or unregistered cannabis (in illegal states) will trigger secondary screenings and potential fines up to $10,000.
- **Global Entry Kiosks MAY Fail:** Due to a planned software update during peak hours (Friday PM and Monday PM), some Global Entry units could go offline. Carry your physical passport as a backup—officers won't accept phone images.
- **Heat Exhaustion Fast-Track:** In response to heatwaves hitting border states, CBP has set up "hydration lanes" at high-traffic pedestrian crossings. If you feel faint, you can ask for priority processing—no questions asked, but expect a medical check.
