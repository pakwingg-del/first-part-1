**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**DATELINE: CINCINNATI, OH — The Kroger Co. has issued a voluntary recall for a specific lot of its Private Selection Cheese Garlic Croutons due to the potential presence of undeclared milk and wheat allergens.**

**WHAT:** A voluntary recall of Kroger Private Selection Cheese Garlic Croutons, sold in 6-ounce packages.

**WHO:** Issued by The Kroger Co., in coordination with the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA).

**WHEN:** The recall was announced on [Insert Current Date]. The affected products carry a specific "Best By" date and UPC code. Consumers are advised to check their pantries immediately.

**WHERE:** The recalled croutons were distributed to Kroger retail stores across multiple states, including but not limited to Ohio, Kentucky, Indiana, Illinois, Michigan, and Tennessee.

**WHY:** The recall was initiated after routine testing revealed that the product contained undeclared milk and wheat, two major allergens. Individuals with an allergy or severe sensitivity to milk or wheat run the risk of serious or life-threatening allergic reactions if they consume this product.

**DETAILS:** The affected product is Kroger Private Selection Cheese Garlic Croutons, 6 oz. Consumers are urged not to consume the product. They should return it to their place of purchase for a full refund or dispose of it immediately.

**ACTION:** No illnesses have been reported to date in connection with this recall. Consumers with questions may contact Kroger Customer Service at 1-800-KROGERS. This is a developing story. Stay with us for updates.
