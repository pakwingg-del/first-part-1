**"ABIGAIL SPANBERGER WANTS TO TALK 'INFRASTRUCTURE' – DOES THAT INCLUDE COMMON SENSE?"**

Just read that Abigail Spanberger is all in on "fixing Virginia’s roads" and "bridges" as her new big push for governor. Cool. Real cool. But while she’s out there trying to pave over potholes, maybe someone should ask her about the crumbling common sense in our neighborhoods.

I live in a quiet cul-de-sac right off a “priority” road she keeps tweeting about. Last week, a school bus couldn’t even get through because three Amazon vans were double-parked in the bike lane, the Amazon driver was on his phone, and a guy on a scooter was weaving between them. Meanwhile, our HOA can’t get the county to put up a single stop sign at a blind curve where three kids almost got hit last month. Infrastructure? Sure. But where’s the *sense*?

Spanberger’s plan talks about "smart growth" and "regional partnerships." Sounds like more government-speak for "we’ll study it for three years." Common sense says we need fewer studies and more enforcement. Common sense says if you can’t park a bus, you shouldn’t be running for governor talking about roads.

But hey, maybe if she spends enough time in D.C. on her "infrastructure commission," the potholes will fix themselves. Right? Glad to see she’s prioritizing the things that matter: photo ops and press releases. Meanwhile, my street still has a speed bump that’s been "getting fixed" since 2019.

Vote your conscience, folks. But my conscience is tired of waiting for common sense to catch up to the campaign slogans.
