**HEADLINE: AMC ANNOUNCES NEW YORK CITY SETTING FOR UPCOMING 'THE WALKING DEAD: DEAD CITY' SPIN-OFF SERIES**

**DATELINE:** NEW YORK, NY – [INSERT CURRENT DATE]

**ANCHOR:** In a major development for the expanding entertainment franchise, AMC Networks has officially announced the production of a new spin-off series titled *The Walking Dead: Dead City*. Network executives confirmed that the series will shift the apocalyptic narrative from the rural American South to the dense, vertical landscape of New York City.

**WHAT:** The series is a direct sequel to the flagship *The Walking Dead* narrative. It will follow fan-favorite characters Maggie Rhee (Lauren Cohan) and Negan Smith (Jeffrey Dean Morgan) as they navigate an entirely different survival dynamic. The urban environment presents new threats, including navigating skyscrapers, subway tunnels, and bridges, which have become high-risk zones due to massive walker hordes and limited egress.

**WHO:** The series is headlined by original cast members Cohan and Morgan, reprising their roles. Eli Jorné, a veteran writer and producer from the original series, is the showrunner and executive producer.

**WHERE:** The primary setting is a post-apocalyptic Manhattan, which has been isolated from the mainland. The city is depicted as a decaying, hostile environment where survivors deal with overcrowded walker populations and various warring factions controlling access to resources.

**WHEN:** Production is confirmed to have begun in New Jersey and New York as of late 2022. The series is scheduled to premiere on AMC and AMC+ in June 2023, serving as a flagship release for the network’s summer programming slate.

**WHY:** Network executives stated the decision to create *Dead City* was driven by strong viewer interest in the unresolved dynamic between Maggie and Negan following the conclusion of the main series.
