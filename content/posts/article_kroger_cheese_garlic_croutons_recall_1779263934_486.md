**Kroger Shoppers: Check Your Pantry NOW—Cheese Garlic Croutons Recalled Over Potential 'Hard Plastic' Pieces**

Calling all salad lovers and soup dippers: if you bought a bag of Kroger Cheese Garlic Croutons recently, you might be chewing on more than just crunchy bread.

In a new recall that hits close to the dinner table, Kroger has pulled select bags of its **Kroger Seasoned Cheese Garlic Croutons** from shelves over a "foreign material" concern. Specifically, the company is warning customers that the croutons may contain **hard plastic pieces**.

That’s a crunch no one paid for.

Here’s what you need to know to protect your wallet and your teeth:

- **The Product:** Kroger Seasoned Cheese Garlic Croutons (5 oz. bag)
- **The Problem:** The manufacturer reported that the product may contain small, hard plastic fragments.
- **The UPC:** Look for barcode number **11110 84587**. The affected bags have specific "Best If Used By" dates—check the fine print on the bag.

**Why this matters for your wallet:** If you've already bought this product, don't just toss it in the trash, because you're throwing away money. Kroger is offering a **full refund or replacement** with a receipt. No receipt? Many stores will still work with you if you bring the bag itself. Don't let that $3.49 turn into a dental bill.

**The bigger picture:** Food recalls are spiking in 2024, and this one is a direct consumer health risk. A hidden plastic shard isn't just "unappetizing"—it can chip a tooth or cause internal injury.
