**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**DATELINE: LOS ANGELES, CA**

**WHAT:** FOX Broadcasting Company has officially announced a script development commitment for a reboot of the critically acclaimed, genre-bending drama series *Highway to Heaven*.

**WHO:** The project is being produced by Universal Television in association with Fox Entertainment. Current casting and showrunner details remain under negotiation, though sources indicate the network aims to capture the original's "emotional resonance with a contemporary sensibility."

**WHEN:** The announcement was made earlier today. A potential series order, pending final script approval and pilot production, is targeted for the 2025-2026 broadcast season.

**WHERE:** The series would be produced in Los Angeles, with filming locations expected to span multiple U.S. states to reflect the original's "road-trip narrative structure." FOX will serve as the primary broadcast outlet.

**WHY:** According to a network executive, the decision is driven by "unprecedented audience demand for uplifting, character-driven content that addresses modern existential questions." The original series, which aired from 1984 to 1989, followed an angel (played by Michael Landon) and a human companion helping people in distress. FOX cites "a significant cultural shift toward themes of morality, redemption, and community service" as the creative catalyst for revisiting the property.

**ADDITIONAL DETAILS:** The reboot is described as a "spiritual successor," retaining the core premise of an angelic protagonist intervening in human lives while updating the episodic format to explore "contemporary social issues through a lens of hope and ethical problem-solving." The network has not confirmed whether any original series cast members will return in cameo or supporting capacities.
