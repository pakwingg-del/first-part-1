**Subject:** TSA Gold+: The End of Airport Security Theater

**Headline:** Delta, United, and American are in talks to launch TSA Gold+—a private, premium airport screening lane bypassing standard security lines for an annual fee.

**The 30-Second Read:** Travelers could soon pay $500-$1,000/year to skip the TSA line entirely. Gold+ would use private biometric verification, off-site pre-check, and dedicated employee-only lanes. The result: a guaranteed sub-5-minute screening experience for high-value passengers.

**Why It Matters:** The TSA is outsourcing the "experience economy" to airlines. This creates a two-tier system: premium travelers pay for time, while standard lines get worse due to reduced funding and staffing. Expect political backlash over inequality, but revenue numbers are too large to ignore.

**Key Takeaway:** The days of uniform airport security are numbered. Time is now a luxury good—and the TSA is selling it.
