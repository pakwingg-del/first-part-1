**BREAKING NEWS: SENATE REPUBLICANS ADVANCE TWO TRUMP NOMINEES IN PARTY-LINE COMMITTEE VOTE**

**Location:** United States Capitol, Washington, D.C.

**What Happened:** The Senate Judiciary Committee voted this afternoon to advance two key nominees selected by former President Donald Trump to federal judicial posts. The 11-10 vote, strictly along party lines, forwarded the nominations to the full Senate for a final confirmation vote.

**Who:** The two nominees are Judge Michael O’Reilly for the Fifth Circuit Court of Appeals and attorney Catherine Lindstrom for the U.S. District Court for the Northern District of Texas. All Republican committee members voted in favor; all Democrats opposed.

**When:** The committee vote occurred at approximately 2:30 PM Eastern Time today, following nearly three hours of contentious debate.

**Where:** The vote took place in the Dirksen Senate Office Building, Room 226, before a packed gallery.

**Why:** Senator Lindsey Graham (R-SC), the ranking member of the committee, stated the nominees possess “impeccable credentials and a clear commitment to constitutional originalism.” Senator Dick Durbin (D-IL) countered that the nominees have “extreme records on voting rights and reproductive freedom that do not reflect mainstream American values,” marking the latest flashpoint in the ongoing partisan battle over the federal judiciary during a high-stakes election year.
