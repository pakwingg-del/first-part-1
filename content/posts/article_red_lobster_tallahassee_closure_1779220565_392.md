**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Red Lobster Tallahassee Closure**

- **The Sudden Shutdown:** The Red Lobster on Apalachee Parkway closed its doors without warning last week, leaving employees and loyal customers blindsided. Staff reportedly showed up for their shifts to find a note taped to the door, and diners with reservations were turned away at the entrance.

- **It’s Part of a National Crisis:** Tallahassee isn't alone. This location is one of dozens nationwide being shuttered as the struggling chain files for Chapter 11 bankruptcy. The "Endless Shrimp" promotion—which was supposed to be a limited-time deal—became a permanent loss leader that reportedly cost the company millions.

- **The Local Impact Hits Hard:** For Tallahassee’s seafood lovers and Noles students, this 90s-era staple was a go-to for graduation dinners and birthday crab legs. The closure leaves a massive hole in the city's casual dining scene, with over 60 local jobs vanished overnight.

- **Mystery of the Missing Lobster:** Customers reported that the restaurant had been running out of popular menu items for weeks before the closure—including their signature cheddar bay biscuits on multiple occasions. Some speculate inventory was quietly being diverted to other locations before the axe fell.

- **What’s Next for the Prime Real Estate:** The Apalachee Parkway property sits in one of Tallahassee’s busiest commercial corridors. Real estate insiders are already buzzing about potential replacements, with rumors of a high-end sushi spot or a chain steakhouse swooping in to fill the crustacean-shaped void.
