**Viral News Snippet:**
BREAKING: 💥🚨 **KENTUCKY PRIMARY CHAOS: Alleged "Ballot-Stuffing" Incident Sparks FBI Investigation—But Is It Real?** 🚨💥

**What You Need to Know:**
A video circulating on X (formerly Twitter) and Telegram claims to show poll workers in **Lexington, Kentucky** stacking unmarked ballots just before the polls closed for the state primary. The clip, captioned "THIS is how they steal elections," has been viewed over 2 million times in two hours.

**The Claim:**
The video, allegedly filmed through a window of a county elections office, shows two individuals placing what appear to be blank ballots into a sealed box. The caption reads: "FBI called in after precincts closed. This is a coordinated effort to rig the primary."

**The Reality:**
According to the Kentucky State Board of Elections, the video is **fake**—misleadingly edited to remove context. Officials confirm the clip shows poll workers **conducting a required post-election ballot reconciliation audit**, a routine procedure where blank backup ballots are secured as part of chain-of-custody protocols. The FBI has **not** been called. The Fayette County Clerk's office released a statement: "This is a standard process, not a crime. The video intentionally omits the timestamp and explanation to fuel disinformation."

**The Spread:**
The hoax is gaining traction among "election integrity" accounts, despite being debunked by local news outlets and the Secretary of State's office. Some posters are demanding a recount or delay in certification.

**Verdict: ❌ FAKE.** The claims are based on a doctored video lacking context. No election fraud has been found in the Kentucky primary. Report suspicious posts to the Elections Board.
