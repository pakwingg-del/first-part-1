**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**TALLAHASSEE, FL – December 14, 2024** – Red Lobster has permanently closed its location at 2761 North Monroe Street in Tallahassee, Florida, as of this week.

**What** caused the closure? Corporate representatives cited a failure to meet revenue targets amid rising operational costs and shifting consumer demand. The restaurant, which had operated for over two decades, was listed among dozens of underperforming locations the chain is exiting as part of a broader restructuring plan.

**Who** is affected? The closure has displaced approximately 45 full- and part-time employees. A company spokesperson stated all staff have been offered transfer opportunities to remaining locations in nearby counties, including Panama City and Lake City, though no further details on acceptance rates were available.

**When** did this occur? The decision was finalized effective December 12. The last day of service was Monday, December 11, though employees were reportedly informed of the closure on the same day.

**Where** is this happening? The site is located at the intersection of North Monroe Street and John Knox Road, a high-traffic commercial zone that has seen several recent retail and restaurant vacancies.

**Why** is this significant? The Tallahassee closure is part of Red Lobster’s nationwide effort to close at least 40 underperforming restaurants in 2024, as the chain—owned by Thai Union Group since 2020—seeks to streamline operations following declining sales and supply chain disruptions.

**Official Statement:** “We remain committed to serving our guests who wish to travel to our nearest locations, and we thank the Tallahassee community for its years of patronage,” said a Red Lobster corporate representative in a prepared statement.

**Next Steps:** The property is expected to be listed for lease or sale by the chain’s real estate division in early January 2025. The Tallahassee City Planning Department has confirmed no redevelopment proposals
