**DECEMBER 15, 2023 – 11:00 PM EST**

**ALABAMA SPECIAL ELECTION: REPUBLICAN CANDIDATE SECURES DECISIVE VICTORY IN TIGHTLY CONTESTED RACE**

**MONTGOMERY, AL** — In a closely watched special election held today, Republican candidate Johnathon M. Reeves secured a decisive victory in Alabama’s 2nd Congressional District, defeating Democratic challenger Lisa H. Tran by a margin of 52.4% to 47.6%, according to preliminary results from the Alabama Secretary of State's office.

**WHAT:** The election fills the vacant congressional seat following the resignation of former Representative Robert C. Daniels. The race was considered a bellwether for national political trends, with both parties investing heavily in campaign resources.

**WHO:** Reeves, a former state senator from Auburn, focused his campaign on economic development, border security, and infrastructure improvements. Tran, a Birmingham attorney and first-time candidate, centered her platform on healthcare access, education funding, and voting rights expansion.

**WHERE:** The voting took place across Alabama’s 2nd Congressional District, encompassing parts of east-central Alabama, including Montgomery, Autauga, and Elmore counties.

**WHEN:** Polls opened at 7:00 AM Central Time and closed at 7:00 PM. Results began reporting shortly thereafter.

**WHY:** The special election was necessitated by Representative Daniels' departure due to health reasons and was widely viewed by political analysts as a key indicator of voter sentiment ahead of the 2024 general elections. Turnout was reported at 58% of registered voters, significantly higher than average for a special election.
