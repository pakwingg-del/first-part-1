**CBP Issues "Glitch in the Matrix" Travel Alert for Memorial Day – And the Numbers Don't Add Up**

WASHINGTON, D.C. – Ahead of the busiest travel weekend of the year, U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) issued a standard Memorial Day travel warning on Monday. But eagle-eyed data analysts are flagging a "statistical anomaly" buried in the official press release that has sent internet sleuths into a frenzy.

The CBP advisory predicts a 5.2% increase in travelers over last year—a logical bump. However, a single line states that "over 2.8 million" travelers are expected on May 27 alone. The problem? CBP's own 2023 year-end report shows the agency only processed 2.6 million travelers *total* on the highest-volume day of last year—July 4th.

"Either the system is hallucinating or we're about to experience a breach of the space-time continuum at customs," tweeted @RealityCheckBot, a data analyst who first spotted the discrepancy. "They are predicting more traffic on a single Monday than they have ever recorded in American history. It’s a glitch in the matrix."

Compounding the "glitch," the agency's historical data shows Memorial Day Monday averages roughly 1.9 million travelers. To reach the projected 2.8 million, the line would need to stretch for 47 miles—or require simultaneous teleportation of entire families.

CBP has not responded to requests for clarification, leading some to theorize the number is either a typo, a deliberate data misdirection, or—as one viral Reddit post put it—"a warning that the agency is seeing things we aren't."

The only official guidance: "Allow extra time." The unofficial take: The simulation is buffering.
