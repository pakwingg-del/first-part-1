**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About Andrej Karpathy’s Bombshell Exit from OpenAI**

- **The Godfather of Autonomous Driving is Walking Away:** Andrej Karpathy, the founding member of OpenAI who previously ran Tesla’s AI vision for Autopilot, has announced he is leaving the company *again*. This isn’t his first departure—he left in 2017 for Tesla and returned in early 2023. The crucial difference this time? No next destination is declared. In the high-stakes world of AGI, a “free agent” status for Karpathy is akin to a nuclear scientist hitting the open market.

- **He’s Calling Out the “Inverse Scaling” Problem:** In his exit statement, Karpathy didn’t cite burnout or personal reasons. Instead, he cryptically highlighted the “Inverse Scaling Law” in modern AI—the idea that as models get smarter and bigger, the *human* oversight required to keep them aligned gets exponentially harder, not easier. This is a direct jab at the current state of the art, suggesting the biggest threat isn’t bad code, but good code that is too complex to understand.

- **The “Pure Research” vs. “Product” Civil War is Real:** Karpathy was the torchbearer for the academic, open-research ethos at OpenAI. His exit signals a definitive win for the “Product” faction (led by Sam Altman and Greg Brockman) over the “Research” faction. He implied that the company has shifted from “What’s the truth?” to “What’s the best demo?”—a seismic cultural shift that explains why multiple safety researchers have fled in the last 12 months.

- **He’s Going Dark to Write a “Piece of Software”:** Forget a new startup or a teaching gig. Karpathy has revealed he is stepping away to build a single, unspecified piece of software.
