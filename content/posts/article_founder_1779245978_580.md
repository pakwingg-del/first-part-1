**HEADLINE: "Founder Culture' Exposed as Modern-Day Feudalism: Self-Proclaimed 'Visionary' Sparks Outrage After Telling Employees They 'Owe Him Their Joy' In Return for 'Opportunity'"**

**BOSTON, MA** – In what critics are calling the "final, pathetic death rattle of hustle culture," a viral internal letter from tech startup *Helios Collective* has ignited a firestorm of moral condemnation. Founder and CEO Marcus Thorne, 28, reportedly told his 200 employees that their 70-hour work weeks were a "privilege," and that their struggles with burnout were a "debt of gratitude" he was owed for blessing them with the "gift of purpose."

The leaked memo argues that the modern founder is the "last true artist," and that employee resistance to his 24/7 availability demands is a "moral failing" indicative of society's "decaying work ethic."

Ethics professor Dr. Anya Sharma from Boston University slammed the statement, calling it "a disturbing snapshot of a culture that has traded mutual respect for a cult of personality." She added, "We are witnessing the logical endpoint of a generation that worships disruption over dignity. Thorne isn't building a company; he’s building a fiefdom, and asking us to clap for the chains."

The backlash comes as the "Great Resignation 2.0" is already underway, with workers explicitly rejecting the "founder-worship" model. Social media is now flooded with ex-employees sharing stories of emotional manipulation, with one post reading, "We didn't sign up for a founder. We signed up for a job. He wanted our souls. I won't be 'disrupted' into submission."

Helios Collective’s stock dropped 12% in pre-market trading.
