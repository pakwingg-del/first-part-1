**BREAKING NEWS — LOS ANGELES** (September 24, 2024) — FOX Broadcasting has officially announced a reboot of the supernatural drama series *Highway to Heaven*, originally created by and starring Michael Landon.

**WHAT:** A new, hour-long episodic series set to reimagine the classic 1980s program, which follows an angel sent to Earth to help people in need. The reboot will feature a new cast and updated storylines while retaining the original premise of a traveling angel performing miracles.

**WHO:** FOX Entertainment has partnered with Michael Landon’s son, Christopher Landon, who will serve as executive producer and writer. The series is being developed by FOX’s in-house studio, Fox Entertainment, with Jeff Rake (*Manifest*) also attached as an executive producer and showrunner.

**WHEN:** The project is currently in early development. No premiere date or casting announcements have been made. The network has ordered a pilot script.

**WHERE:** The series will air on the FOX television network in the United States. Specific filming locations have not yet been disclosed.

**WHY:** Executives cited the enduring appeal of hopeful, character-driven storytelling and the show's strong nostalgic value as reasons for the reboot. FOX Entertainment President Michael Thorn stated the network seeks to “reintroduce this beloved franchise to a new generation” while honoring Michael Landon’s legacy.

**HOW:** The reboot will reportedly modernize the tone and visual style of the original, featuring serialized arcs alongside weekly missions. Landon’s original character, Jonathan Smith, will not return; instead, a new angel and human companion will be introduced.

*This is a developing story. Further details will be provided as they become available.*
