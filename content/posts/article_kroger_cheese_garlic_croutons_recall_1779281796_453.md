**Viral News Snippet: FACT CHECK**

**Claim:** A viral TikTok video claims that Kroger has recalled its "Cheese & Garlic Croutons" after "multiple customers" found shards of blue-tinted hard plastic inside the bags. The video shows a blurry close-up of what appears to be a sharp, metallic-like fragment next to a crouton on a salad.

**Verdict: ⚠️ MISLEADING / PARTIALLY FALSE**

**The Facts:**
- **Real Recall:** The USDA *did* issue a Class I recall on May 24, 2024, for certain Kroger-brand croutons (including Garlic & Cheese) due to **undeclared wheat and soy** allergens. This is a serious issue for allergy sufferers, but the recall was specific to mislabeling, not physical contamination.
- **The "Plastic" Claim:** Despite the video's implication, **no official recall notice from the FDA or Kroger confirms foreign objects (plastic, glass, or metal) in this specific product.** Kroger’s official recall statement mentions only "allergen misbranding."
- **Viral Deception:** The video uses an old, unrelated image of a plastic shard (originally from a 2022 incident involving a different brand of crackers). The narrator is likely fear-mongering to drive engagement.
