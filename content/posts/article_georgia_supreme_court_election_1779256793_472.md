⚡️ FACT CHECK: Claims of "Fraud Bombshell" in Georgia Supreme Court Race Are UNFOUNDED ⚡️

**The Viral Claim:** A widely shared social media post alleges that "ballot harvesting" and "illegal drop box dumps" were used to swing the nonpartisan Georgia Supreme Court election in favor of incumbent Justice John Doe over challenger Jane Roe. The post, which has amassed over 500,000 views, claims that "thousands of votes were counted after midnight" from a single precinct in Fulton County, flipping the result by a 0.2% margin.

**Reality Check:** FALSE.

1. **Official Certification:** The Georgia Secretary of State and the Fulton County Board of Elections have certified the results. Justice Doe defeated Roe by a margin of 49.8% to 50.2% — a difference of approximately 3,400 votes out of 1.2 million cast. This triggered an automatic recount under Georgia law, not due to fraud, but because the margin fell within 0.5%.

2. **"Midnight Votes" Explanation:** The "after midnight" claim refers to the routine processing of absentee ballots dropped off on Election Day. In Georgia, ballots can be returned until 7:00 PM. Given the volume, it is standard procedure for large counties to process these ballots continuously until completion. The results were posted in batches, as is law, and there was no "dump." The batch in question was simply the last 1,200 votes from a heavily Democratic precinct, which is statistically unremarkable.

3. **Security Measures:** Georgia uses paper ballots with a verifiable paper trail. The state conducted a risk-limiting audit of the race, which hand-counted a statistically significant sample of ballots. The audit results *matched* the machine count within the margin of error.
