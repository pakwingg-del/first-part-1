**⚠️ VIRAL ALERT: "Flash Flood Emergency Declared After Mysterious 'Raining Fish' Phenomenon Sweeps Through Midwest—Is This an Act of God or Something Else?"**

**The Claim:**
Social media is buzzing with footage claiming that a freak storm in rural Illinois has triggered a flash flood warning, but with a bizarre twist: residents are reportedly finding small, live fish flopping in their yards and streets. One viral TikTok video, captioned "It's raining catfish in Macon County!", has already amassed 2.3 million views, with users speculating about everything from biblical plagues to a secret government experiment gone wrong.

**The Reality (Fact-Checked ✅❌):**
1. **The Flash Flood Warning is REAL.** The National Weather Service (NWS) did issue a legitimate Flash Flood Warning for parts of central Illinois on Tuesday night after 4-6 inches of rain fell in under two hours. Urban and small stream flooding is confirmed in several towns, with water rescues reported in Decatur.

2. **The "Raining Fish" Part is FAKE.** While the flash flood warning is authentic, the viral video of fish falling from the sky has been debunked. The clip is actually from a 2018 incident in Mexico, where strong waterspouts sucked fish from a lagoon and dropped them over a coastal town. No such phenomenon has been reported in Illinois. Local authorities have confirmed no fish are falling.

3. **But Wait—There's a Grain of Truth.** Heavy rain and flooding *can* cause fish to be displaced from swollen rivers and ponds. Some residents have reported finding small fish in their flooded basements or lawns—not from the sky, but from overflowing creeks. This is a rare but natural occurrence, not a supernatural event.

**The Verdict:** The flash flood warning is real; the "raining fish" is a recycled hoax. The weather is dangerous enough
