**BREAKING NEWS: New Polling Data Reveals Tight Contest in Key State**

ROY COOPER AND MICHAEL WHATLEY SHOW STATISTICAL DEAD HEAT IN RECENT SURVEY

**RALEIGH, NC** — A newly released poll conducted between November 10 and November 13 has revealed a statistically tied race between former Governor Roy Cooper and current party official Michael Whatley, according to data obtained exclusively by this station.

**Who:** The poll measured voter preference between Roy Cooper, the former Democratic Governor of North Carolina, and Michael Whatley, the Chairman of the North Carolina Republican Party.

**What:** The survey, conducted by the independent firm Carolina Pulse Research, shows Cooper at 47 percent support among likely voters, with Whatley at 46 percent. The margin of error is plus or minus 3.1 percentage points, rendering the contest effectively even.

**When:** The polling was conducted from November 10 through November 13, 2024.

**Where:** The survey was administered statewide in North Carolina, with a sample size of 1,200 registered voters.

**Why:** Political analysts suggest this data indicates a deeply polarized electorate, with neither candidate able to break away from the other. The results come amid heightened national focus on the state's role in upcoming legislative and possible gubernatorial races. Analysts caution that with such a narrow margin, turnout operations and independent voter outreach will be decisive.

**Conclusion:** Both campaigns have issued statements acknowledging the close numbers, with Cooper’s team emphasizing his broad coalition and Whatley’s camp highlighting momentum among rural and suburban voters. The race remains one of the most closely watched in the nation.
