**BREAKING: Georgia Supreme Court Election Yields Historic Voter Turnout**

**ATLANTA, GA** – In an unprecedented development, the election for a seat on the Supreme Court of Georgia has concluded with record-breaking voter engagement, according to official results released late Wednesday evening.

**Who:** The election involved incumbent Justice John P. Ellington and challenger, Court of Appeals Judge Sara L. Doyle. Justice Ellington ultimately retained his seat.

**What:** The contest was for a six-year term on the Georgia Supreme Court, a nonpartisan position. Early data indicates a voter turnout exceeding 35% of registered voters specifically for this race—a figure more than triple the average turnout for nonpartisan judicial elections in the state.

**When:** The election cycle concluded on Tuesday, November 5, with final tallies certified by the Georgia Secretary of State’s office at 11:47 PM EST.

**Where:** The race was decided across all 159 counties of Georgia, with particularly high turnout reported in Fulton, Gwinnett, and Cobb counties.

**Why:** Analysts attribute the heightened interest to a contentious campaign dominated by debates over criminal justice reform, abortion restrictions, and the monetary influence of out-of-state political action committees. Justice Ellington secured 52.4% of the vote, a margin of victory that political strategists describe as "razor-thin" for a judicial contest.

**How:** The election was conducted using a combination of in-person early voting, absentee ballots, and Election Day polling. The official vote count was 1,847,923 for Justice Ellington compared to 1,676,784 for Judge Doyle.

**Official Statement:** In a formal address from the State Capitol, Georgia Secretary of State Brad Raffensperger stated, "This election reflects a fundamental shift in public awareness regarding the judiciary. We have observed the highest level of direct voter participation in a state Supreme Court race in modern Georgia history."
