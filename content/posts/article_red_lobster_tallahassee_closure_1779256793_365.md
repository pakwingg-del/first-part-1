**BREAKING NEWS: Red Lobster Tallahassee Location Permanently Closes Amid Chain-Wide Restructuring**

**TALLAHASSEE, FL – November 12, 2024** – In a significant development for the local dining landscape, the Red Lobster restaurant located at 800 Apalachee Parkway in Tallahassee has permanently closed its doors as of this week.

**What happened:** The closure marks the sudden cessation of operations at one of the state capital's longstanding seafood dining establishments. Patrons arriving at the location have been met with locked entrances and signage indicating the business has ceased operations.

**Where:** The shuttered restaurant is situated at the Capital Plaza shopping center near the intersection of Apalachee Parkway and Capital Circle SE.

**When:** Corporate officials have confirmed that the final day of service was Sunday, November 10, 2024.

**Why:** This closure is part of a broader corporate restructuring effort by Red Lobster Management LLC. Company representatives cite ongoing financial challenges, including rising operational costs and shifting consumer preferences, as primary factors. The Tallahassee location is one of dozens the chain has closed nationwide in recent months as part of a Chapter 11 bankruptcy reorganization process.

**Who:** The closure directly impacts approximately 45 employees who have been terminated. Former staff members report receiving no prior notice of the closure. Red Lobster corporate has stated that affected employees will be eligible for severance packages and priority consideration for positions at remaining company locations.

**Context:** The Tallahassee restaurant, which had operated for over 25 years, was a fixture in the local dining community. Its closure follows a trend of attrition for the Orlando-based chain, which has shuttered more than 100 locations across the United States since May 2024. The nearest remaining Red Lobster to Tallahassee is now located in Panama City, approximately 100 miles west.
