**CIVILIZATION’S ASHES: The ‘Fire Near Me’ App Has Turned Us Into Moral MONSTERS, Says Ethicist**

A leading moral philosopher has declared that the ubiquitous "Fire Near Me" app is not a tool for safety, but a high-tech instrument of societal decay, creating a new class of "digital rubberneckers" who have abandoned basic human decency for a dopamine hit of catastrophe.

Dr. Eleanor Vance, author of *The Burning Conscience*, argues that the instant notification of a nearby blaze has replaced neighborly concern with a ghastly spectator sport. "We no longer ask, 'Is my neighbor safe? Do they need a place to stay?'" Dr. Vance states. "We ask, 'How many acres? Is it close enough to see the glow?' We are refreshing a map while families are fleeing for their lives."

The ethical crisis peaks, according to Vance, in the phenomenon of the "Active Fire Selfie." "We are seeing people drive toward the threat, not to help, but to film the apocalypse for TikTok," she laments. "It is the ultimate symbol of our downfall: a society that would rather document its own destruction than prevent it. The app hasn't warned us of danger; it has numbed us to tragedy, turning every ember into a trending topic and every neighbor into a helpless spectator."
