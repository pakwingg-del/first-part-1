**BREAKING: Supreme Court Secretly Ruled ALL Animals Are “People”? The Truth Behind the Viral Headline**

**Claim:** A viral video and social media posts are claiming the U.S. Supreme Court issued a “secret, sealed ruling” last week determining that all animals—including pets, farm animals, and wildlife—are legally “people” with full constitutional rights. The posts claim the ruling was immediately classified for “national security reasons” and that the White House is preparing for “mass animal emancipation.”

**Verdict: FALSE.**
This is a fabricated story. There is no such Supreme Court ruling. The video is a piece of AI-generated satire mixed with a misrepresentation of a real, narrow case about *corporate personhood* for a specific non-human entity in 2010 (*Citizens United*). The “secret sealed ruling” is a classic hallmark of deepfake hoax content used to generate panic and clicks. Real Supreme Court rulings are public, immediately posted on the Court’s website, and are never “secretly sealed.”

**What actually happened:** The Supreme Court did issue a minor, routine order in a case about a conservation group trying to sue a mining company on behalf of a specific river (the “Rights of Nature” movement). The Court simply declined to hear the appeal. It did not rule on animal personhood or redefine species.

**Bottom Line:** Ignore the viral posts. Your pet is not about to get a driver’s license. Before sharing, check the official Supreme Court website (supremecourt.gov) or a reputable news source. If the claim involves a “secret ruling” from the highest court, it is almost certainly fake.
