**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Red Lobster Tallahassee Closure**

- **Sudden "Ghost" Shutdown:** Employees at the North Monroe Street location reportedly showed up for their shifts to find the doors locked and a notice taped to the front. There was no prior warning to staff or regular customers, marking it as an unannounced "ghost kitchen" closure typical of distressed chains.

- **Total Cheddar Bay Biscuit Count Lost:** This location was a local favorite for its all-you-can-eat shrimp deals. With its closure, the Tallahassee area has lost an estimated **4,000 to 6,000 fresh-baked Cheddar Bay Biscuits per week**—a devastating blow to biscuit lovers and the state’s breadstick economy.

- **Part of a 99-Restaurant Chopping Block:** The Tallahassee location is not an isolated incident. It's one of nearly 100 Red Lobster restaurants being shuttered nationwide as part of a Chapter 11 bankruptcy restructuring. The chain is blaming rising lease costs, wage inflation, and the disastrous "Ultimate Endless Shrimp" promotion that lost the company millions.

- **FSU Student Reaction is Pure Meme:** Florida State University students—who heavily relied on the spot for late-night, post-game, and budget-friendly seafood—have flooded social media with grief memes. The hashtag #BiscuitGate is trending locally, with many declaring the closure "the biggest tragedy since Doak Campbell ran out of ketchup."

- **Rival Chains Poised to Pounce:** Within 24 hours of the announcement, competitors like Bonefish Grill and Captain D’s have already launched targeted social media ads in Tallahassee, offering "Free Cheddar-Style Biscuits with Any Entrée." Local seafood joints are also scrambling to update their job postings, hoping to scoop up former Red Lobster employees.
