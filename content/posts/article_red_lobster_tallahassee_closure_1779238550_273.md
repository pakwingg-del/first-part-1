# RED LOBSTER TALLHASSEE CLOSES—SECURITY GUARD FINDS "GHOST ORDER" TERMINAL STILL PRINTING AFTER POWER CUT

**Tallahassee, FL** – The sudden closure of the Red Lobster on Apalachee Parkway has local paranormal investigators buzzing, but not for the usual reasons.

While the chain blames “operational restructuring,” a security guard on the overnight shift has reported a glitch that is sending shivers down the spines of data analysts.

According to a source inside the store, the restaurant’s main server was physically unplugged at 11:47 PM on Tuesday. Yet, at exactly 3:33 AM, the thermal receipt printer in the back office—still connected to a backup battery—began spitting out a single, continuous order.

**The ticket reads:** *“Table 44. 6 orders of Endless Shrimp. Note: NO LIMIT. Note: FIRE SUPPRESSION SYSTEM CODE 47-BRAVO.”*

The creepy part? Table 44 was removed from the floor plan in 2018 after a kitchen fire. And **47-BRAVO**? That’s the code for a *sprinkler malfunction* due to a grease fire—exactly 30 minutes before the original blaze.

Current employees are refusing to enter the back office, claiming the terminal reeks of burnt butter and static electricity. The district manager has ordered a complete IT wipe, but our source says the printer was already unplugged when the ghost order printed.

*“The matrix is bleeding into the breadsticks,”* one server whispered.
