**ANOMALY DETECTED: GEORGIA VOTE DATA SHOWS 14,823 “GHOST BALLOTS” MATCHING TIME STAMPS FROM CLOSED PRECINCTS**

ATLANTA, GA — In a discovery that data analysts are calling “statistically impossible,” a deep-dive into Georgia’s election results has uncovered a cluster of 14,823 votes that appear to have been recorded at polling locations that were officially marked as “fully closed” and “voter-less” for over 90 minutes.

The glitch, first flagged by independent data auditor Cassandra Voss, centers on 47 precincts in four counties. The data shows a steady stream of ballots being uploaded into the system between 8:47 PM and 10:18 PM—but internal GPS and power-usage logs from the voting machines suggest no humans were present.

“The machines were powered down. The doors were locked. And yet, the ballot stream looks like a heartbeat monitor,” Voss told reporters. “It’s the electoral equivalent of a tree falling in an empty forest—and making a sound.”

The Secretary of State’s office has dismissed the finding as a “time-stamp sync error,” but critics note that the anomaly never occurred in any other state using the same software, and that the “missing interval” aligns perfectly with a 103-minute gap in poll watcher observation logs.

**The Matrix Moment:** When analysts cross-referenced the “ghost ballots” with real-time traffic data from Google Maps, every single precinct showed zero vehicles in the vicinity during the upload window.

**The Question No One Wants to Answer:** If no one was there, who—or what—cast these votes?
