**HEADLINE: Republican Wins Alabama Special Election; Voter Turnout Surprises Analysts**

**LOCATION:** Montgomery, Alabama

**DATELINE:** Wednesday, November 15, 2023

**SUMMARY:** In a closely watched contest, Republican candidate Michael Winslow has secured victory in Alabama's 2nd Congressional District special election, according to official results released early this morning. The election, held yesterday, saw Winslow defeat Democratic challenger Sarah Jenkins by a margin of 8.4 percentage points.

**DETAILS:** The race was called by major news networks at 10:15 PM Central Time, with 92% of precincts reporting. Winslow garnered approximately 54.2% of the vote, translating to 148,700 ballots, while Jenkins received 45.8%, or 125,600 votes. The seat was vacated earlier this year following the resignation of former Representative Robert Hayes for health reasons.

**CONTEXT:** Analysts note that voter turnout reached 39.7%, a figure significantly higher than the 28% average for special elections in the state over the past decade. Political observers attribute the increased participation to aggressive ground campaigns and heightened national attention on the district, which was recently redrawn by a federal court order to include more urban and suburban areas of Jefferson County.

**REACTION:** In his victory speech delivered at a campaign headquarters in Birmingham, Winslow emphasized economic development and infrastructure. "The people of Alabama have spoken, and we will deliver on our promises to lower taxes and improve our roads," he said. Jenkins conceded defeat in a statement, thanking her volunteers and vowing to continue advocating for increased education funding.

**METHODOLOGY:** Election results were compiled by the Alabama Secretary of State's office and verified by local county election boards. The Associated Press provided independent tabulation. No allegations of widespread irregularities have been reported at this time.
