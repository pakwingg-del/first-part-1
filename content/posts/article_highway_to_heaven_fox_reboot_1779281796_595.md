**BREAKING: FOX REBOOTS ‘HIGHWAY TO HEAVEN’ – CRITICS CALL IT ‘A ROAD MAP TO MORAL ANARCHY’**

In a move that has sparked fury among cultural watchdogs, Fox has announced a gritty reboot of the classic series *Highway to Heaven*. The original show, starring Michael Landon as a traveling angel, offered gentle lessons in kindness and redemption. The new version? A dark, morally ambiguous thriller where the angel—now a cynical, ex-military operative named “Jax”—“saves” souls by breaking the law.

According to leaked scripts, Episode One features Jax blackmailing a corrupt CEO into donating his fortune to a children’s hospital. While the act appears “good,” critics warn the show peddles a dangerous “ends justify the means” philosophy. “We are teaching a generation that lying, extortion, and vigilantism are acceptable paths to justice,” fumed Dr. Helen Richter, a moral philosophy professor at Georgetown. “This isn’t divine guidance. It’s a blueprint for societal collapse disguised as entertainment.”

The premiere has already drawn fire from religious leaders, who note that the original series celebrated grace and forgiveness. “Now, we get an angel who uses manipulation and threats,” said Rev. Thomas Kane of the Family Values Coalition. “It’s the final sign—we are abandoning virtue for the worship of raw power.”

Worse, producers have confirmed a love interest for Jax: a fallen human he must “ethically corrupt” to save. The twist has been condemned as romanticizing ethical compromise.

“This is the end of objective morality,” Richter concluded. “We’ve gone from *Highway to Heaven* to a highway to hell—and Fox is charging us all for the ticket.”
