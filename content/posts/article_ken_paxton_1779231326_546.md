**BREAKING: TEXAS AG KEN PAXTON DECLARES 'DIGITAL SOVEREIGNTY' — STATE TO LAUNCH ITS OWN INTERNET PROTOCOL BY 2030**

In a move that has constitutional scholars and tech giants reeling, Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton today announced the "Lone Star Net Initiative" — a sovereign, state-managed internet protocol designed to operate independently of federal oversight and global tech monopolies.  

“The internet of the future will not be run by Silicon Valley elites or Washington bureaucrats. It will be run by Texans, for Texans,” Paxton declared from the steps of the Alamo, flanked by state lawmakers and blockchain developers.  

The plan, slated for a 2030 rollout, would create a parallel digital ecosystem: Texas-specific domain names, decentralized data storage hubs powered by the state’s own energy grid, and a legal framework that requires all social media platforms to host user data within state borders. Critics call it a “digital secession” that could shatter the global internet.  

“This is the end of the Wild West of tech regulation,” said UT Austin futurist Dr. Lena Cruz. “Paxton is betting that sovereignty — not moderation — is the new battleground. By 2035, you won’t ask ‘Is this site legal in the U.S.?’ You’ll ask ‘Is it legal in Texas?’”  

Already, California and Florida have signaled interest in similar legislation, sparking fears of a fractured “Balkanized Internet” where state borders become digital firewalls. Paxton’s office insists it will protect free speech and privacy — but warns: “If the federal government tries to shut us down, we’ll just go dark.”
