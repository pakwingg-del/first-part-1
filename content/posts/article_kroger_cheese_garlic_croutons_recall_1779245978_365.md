**SUBJECT:** Kroger Issues Recall of Store-Brand Cheese Garlic Croutons Over Undeclared Allergen

**Cincinnati, OH —** The Kroger Company has announced a voluntary recall of its Private Selection brand Garlic Cheese Croutons due to the presence of an undeclared allergen, posing a serious health risk to consumers with specific dietary sensitivities.

**What:** The recall specifically affects 5-ounce packages of "Kroger Private Selection Garlic Cheese Croutons." The issue was identified after routine testing revealed the product may contain undeclared milk, a known allergen not listed on the product label. Individuals with a severe allergy or sensitivity to milk run the risk of a serious or life-threatening allergic reaction if they consume the product.

**Who:** The recall is being conducted by The Kroger Company, based in Cincinnati, Ohio. The affected products were distributed to Kroger retail stores and may have been sold at other banners within the company's portfolio, including Fry's, Ralphs, Dillons, King Soopers, and Smith's.

**Where:** The recalled croutons were available for purchase at Kroger-affiliated grocery stores across the United States. The company has urged all consumers in these regions to check their pantries immediately.
