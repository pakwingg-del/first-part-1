**HEADLINE: New York Times/Siena Poll Reveals Significant Shifts in Key Electoral Demographics**

**DATELINE:** NEW YORK, NY

**WHO:** The New York Times in partnership with Siena College.

**WHAT:** A national survey of registered voters, the findings of which indicate notable changes in voter sentiment regarding the upcoming general election.

**WHEN:** Conducted from April 7 to April 14, 2025.

**WHERE:** A nationally representative sample across the United States, with an oversampling in key battleground states.

**WHY:** To gauge public opinion on candidate preferences, economic confidence, and major policy issues ahead of the 2025 election cycle.

**HOW:** The poll was conducted via live telephone interviews and online surveys, encompassing a sample of 1,059 registered voters. The margin of sampling error is reported to be plus or minus 3.5 percentage points for the full sample.

**KEY FINDINGS:** The poll indicates a statistically significant narrowing of the lead held by the incumbent administration. Most notably, there has been a pronounced shift among independent voters and suburban demographics, who now express increased dissatisfaction with current economic management and immigration policies. Furthermore, approval ratings for the handling of foreign affairs have declined by 4 points since the previous quarterly survey. These data points suggest a volatile political landscape as election season intensifies.
