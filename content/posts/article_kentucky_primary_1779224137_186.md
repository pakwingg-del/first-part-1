**KENTUCKY PRIMARY CONFIRMS WHAT WE’VE ALL BEEN SAYING: COMMON SENSE ISN’T COMMON**

Just watched the returns roll in from the Kentucky primary and I have to say—if this doesn’t prove we’re living in the twilight zone, I don’t know what does. 

Our county had a 12% turnout. TWELVE PERCENT. You can’t complain about potholes, school funding, or gas prices if you can’t be bothered to walk five minutes to a voting booth. I saw people posting selfies at the lake today while we were deciding who runs this state. Priorities, I guess.

And the candidates? Don’t get me started. One guy spent $50,000 of his own money on mailers promising to “bring back respect.” Respect for what? For whom? The man literally refused to shake hands at the diner because he said he “felt a cold coming on.” That’s leadership?

Meanwhile, the incumbent—who’s been in office since I was in high school—won by a landslide. Same guy who hasn’t fixed a single road in our district. Same guy who voted for that tax increase last year. But hey, at least he’s got a name people recognize, right?

Here’s my hot take: We deserve exactly what we vote for. If you stayed home today, you lost the right to complain tomorrow. Common sense says: show up, do your homework, vote for the person who actually cares about sidewalks and school lunches instead of the one with the best bumper sticker.

But what do I know? I’m just a guy who showed up. 🤷‍♂️
