**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Kroger Cheese Garlic Croutons Recall**

- **Undeclared Allergen Alert:** The recall was initiated due to “undeclared milk” in the “Cheese Garlic” variety. This is a serious health risk for anyone with a milk allergy or severe lactose intolerance, as the product's label does not disclose the dairy ingredient.

- **Packaging Mix-Up Danger:** The culprit is a packaging error. Some bags labeled as “Cheese Garlic” actually contain “Chive & Onion” croutons (which have milk as an ingredient), meaning many consumers are unknowingly eating a different, potentially hazardous product.

- **Specific Products Targeted:** The recall is limited to Kroger’s “Private Selection Seasoned Croutons” in the 5.5 oz bags with a “Best By” date of July 3, 2025, and a UPC of 11110-48212. Check your pantry—any other flavor or size is not affected.

- **No Illnesses Reported – Yet:** Kroger has stated that no allergic reactions or illnesses have been confirmed in connection with the mislabeled product. However, the potential for a severe reaction is high, prompting the urgent recall.

- **Immediate Action Required:** Consumers who purchased the affected croutons should stop using them immediately and can return them to any Kroger store for a full refund. Those with milk allergies are urged to seek medical advice if they have already consumed the product.
