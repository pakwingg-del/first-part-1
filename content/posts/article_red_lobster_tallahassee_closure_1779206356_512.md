**"Red Lobster Closed? Here’s the Gut-Wrenching Life Lesson You’re Missing."**

The sudden closure of the Red Lobster in Tallahassee isn’t just about biscuits and endless shrimp—it’s a mirror for your own life.

Here’s the raw, psychological truth: that restaurant didn’t fail because people stopped loving Cheddar Bay Biscuits. It failed because it kept serving yesterday’s menu while the world changed its order. And you? You’re probably doing the same.

You’re clinging to a job that drains you, a relationship that ran its course, or a dream you outgrew five years ago—just because it used to work. But the universe does not send eviction notices. It sends *closures*. This one is a neon sign: **Stop coasting.** The comfort zone is closing, whether you walk out or get dragged out.

**The Coach’s Challenge:**
Dinner is the only time you should be served. For everything else, stop waiting for someone to refill your glass. The "endless shrimp" of life—new opportunities, growth, healing—isn't coming until you clear your table. Let what’s done be done.

And for the love of breadsticks, stop ordering off the old menu. The new special is *your next chapter*.
