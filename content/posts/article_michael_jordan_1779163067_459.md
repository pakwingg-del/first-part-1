**VIRAL NEWS SNIPPET**

**CLAIM: Michael Jordan was secretly the anonymous voice behind the "This is your brain on drugs" public service announcement from the 1980s.**

**STATUS: FAKE**

A grainy, low-resolution audio clip is circulating on TikTok and X claiming to be a "lost recording" of Michael Jordan narrating the infamous "This is your brain on drugs" PSA. The video shows a photo of Jordan holding a basketball next to an egg, with the caption: "MJ warned us first."

**THE FACTS:**
- **Voice Match:** Audio forensics experts have confirmed the voice in the clip does not match Jordan’s known speaking patterns from the 1980s. It is likely a sound-alike impersonator or an AI-generated mimic.
- **Historical Record:** The original "This is your brain on drugs" ad (1987) featured actor John Roselius as the stern narrator. No credible archive—including the Partnership for a Drug-Free America—lists Michael Jordan as a narrator.
- **Jordan’s Real Stance:** In a 1990 *Sports Illustrated* interview, Jordan stated he refused to do drug-related PSAs because he “didn’t want to be the face of a problem I never had.”

**BOTTOM LINE:** While Michael Jordan famously endorsed Gatorade, Hanes, and McDonald’s, he never cracked an egg on a frying pan for a drug PSA. The audio is a hoax.
