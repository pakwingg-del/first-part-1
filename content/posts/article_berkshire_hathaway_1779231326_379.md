**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**(OMAHA, NE) – 22 October 2023** – Berkshire Hathaway Inc. (NYSE: BRK.A, BRK.B) has officially surpassed a total market capitalization of $1.2 trillion, solidifying its position as the most valuable non-technology company in the world, according to data released during Wednesday’s trading session.

**Who:** The conglomerate is led by Chairman and CEO Warren Buffett, 94, alongside Vice Chairmen Greg Abel and Ajit Jain.

**What:** The milestone was achieved following a 1.2% rise in Class A shares, which closed at approximately $720,000 per unit. The company’s massive cash reserves, now estimated at over $325 billion, have become a central focus for analysts. This cash pile, a record for the firm, has sparked intense debate regarding potential large-scale acquisitions or an expanded share buyback program.

**When:** The valuation surge occurred during mid-morning trading on Wednesday, November 13, 2024.

**Where:** The corporate headquarters in Omaha, Nebraska, released no official statement regarding the event. The performance was tracked on the New York Stock Exchange.

**Why:** Market analysts attribute the surge to a combination of strong earnings from Berkshire’s wholly-owned subsidiaries, including BNSF Railway and GEICO, as well as a strategic reduction in its Apple Inc. stake, which has freed up significant liquidity. The company’s defensive posture, favoring cash over volatile equities in a rising interest rate environment, has been widely praised by risk-averse institutional investors.

**Impact:** With a price-to-earnings ratio remaining below the broader S&P 500 average, Berkshire Hathaway continues to be viewed as a bellwether for the U.S. economy. The unprecedented cash position raises a critical question for the coming quarter: will the "Oracle of Omaha" deploy capital for a transformative deal, or maintain a conservative stance through year-end?
