**BREAKING: FLASH FLOOD WARNING ISSUED — CHECK YOUR INSURANCE NOW OR PAY THE PRICE**

If you live anywhere under a flash flood warning today, your wallet is about to take a direct hit. Here’s the sting: most standard homeowner and renter policies **do not cover flood damage**. That means a sudden downpour could leave you paying thousands out-of-pocket for waterlogged basements, ruined furniture, and destroyed cars.

The average flood claim in the U.S. is over **$42,000** — and the government’s National Flood Insurance Program has a 30-day waiting period before coverage kicks in. So, if you didn’t buy that policy last month, you are likely on your own today.

Don’t wait for the water to reach your front door. **Check your coverage NOW.** And if you don’t have flood insurance, triple-check that your sump pump works, move expensive electronics upstairs, and start documenting valuables with photos for a potential loss claim – especially if your tax write-off options just got slashed. Your bank account will thank you.
