**HEADLINE: CBP REFUTES VIRAL MEMORIAL DAY TRAVEL WARNING – “NO MASS CHECKPOINTS PLANNED”**

**Verdict: FALSE RUMOR**

A viral social media post circulating Monday claims U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) will implement “unprecedented internal checkpoints” on all major U.S. highways and interstates starting Memorial Day weekend, forbidding non-essential travel without federal ID.

**The Claim (What’s Going Viral):**
The message, often shared as a screenshot with a DHS logo, states CBP will stop vehicles “100 miles from any international border” and demand “proof of citizenship or valid visa” for anyone driving to a lake, campground, or state park. It warns travelers they could be detained for 72 hours without a warrant.

**The Reality (What’s True vs. Fake):**
- **Fake:** CBP has issued no such nationwide travel directive. A CBP spokesperson told *Viral Pulse* the post is “100% fabricated and not based on any operational plan.”
- **Misleading:** While CBP does have authority within 100 miles of a U.S. border to operate checkpoints, they are not ‘mass highway dragnets’ for holiday travelers. Routine checkpoints target smuggling and illegal entry, not day-trippers to a beach.
- **False Detail:** The claim that drivers need a passport for a drive to a state park in, say, Ohio (more than 100 miles from Canada) is demonstrably incorrect. Checkpoints are legally limited to “functional equivalents” of the border.

**Expert Take:**
“This is a recycled hoax that resurfaces before every major holiday,” says immigration law expert Dr. Elena Torres. “It plays on genuine confusion about the 100-mile zone, but there is zero evidence of a holiday-specific lockdown or mass detention plan.”
