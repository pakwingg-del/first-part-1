**HEADLINE: Berkshire Hathaway Reports Record Cash Reserves Amidst Strategic Divestitures**

**OMAHA, NE —** In a major financial development, Berkshire Hathaway Inc. announced today a surge in its cash holdings to an unprecedented $325 billion, marking the highest quarterly cash reserve in the corporation’s history. The disclosure was made during the release of the conglomerate’s third-quarter earnings report.

**WHO:** The report was issued by Berkshire Hathaway, the multinational holding company led by Chairman and Chief Executive Officer Warren Buffett.

**WHAT:** The company posted a record cash position of $325 billion, a significant increase from $260 billion in the previous quarter. The financial statement revealed a net sale of $75 billion in equities, including the complete liquidation of long-held stakes in several major technology and consumer goods firms.

**WHERE:** The announcement was made from the company’s headquarters in Omaha, Nebraska, and disseminated through official financial channels.

**WHEN:** The earnings report, covering the period ending September 30, 2024, was released during pre-market trading hours today.

**WHY:** Analysts indicate the cash accumulation is a strategic move to capitalize on potential market dislocations, with Buffett signaling a cautious outlook on current equity valuations. The divestitures come amid rising interest rates and geopolitical uncertainty, with the company prioritizing liquidity and financial flexibility.

**HOW:** The record cash position was achieved through aggressive portfolio trimming, exiting positions in sectors deemed overvalued, and a pause in major share buyback programs. The company now holds a reserve equivalent to 45% of its total market capitalization, a figure not seen since the 2008 financial crisis.
