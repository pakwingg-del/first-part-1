**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Roy Cooper-Michael Whatley Poll**

- **The Stunner:** A new poll from the North Carolina GOP shows Democratic Governor Roy Cooper leading Republican National Committee Chair Michael Whatley by a staggering **14 points** (48% to 34%) in a hypothetical 2026 Senate matchup. This is a major red flag for the GOP in a state they must hold to keep the Senate majority.

- **Why This Exists:** The poll is a speculative "what-if" scenario, testing Cooper's strength if he challenged Senator Thom Tillis. Since Whatley is a North Carolina native and a top GOP strategist, his inclusion is being interpreted as a trial balloon—or a warning shot—to Tillis that the party is looking at alternatives.

- **The Cooper Factor:** Cooper is currently term-limited as governor and has a 56% approval rating, making him the most popular Democrat in the state. He’s consistently won statewide in a purple state, and this poll confirms he is the GOP’s worst nightmare for a Senate seat.

- **The Whatley Problem:** Whatley’s low name recognition (only 38% of voters know him) and his role as a national party insider are dragging him down. Republicans fear his close ties to Trump could be a liability with moderate suburban voters who increasingly determine North Carolina elections.

- **The Big Picture:** While this is a single poll, it’s sending shockwaves through Washington. If Cooper runs, it could become the most expensive Senate race in history—and the GOP is already scrambling to prevent a blue upset in a state that has voted Republican in every presidential election since 2012.
