One father was seen carrying a bag of boutique marshmallows, not his infant daughter, toward the treeline. "We used to pray for rain during a fire," said retired fire chief Harold Meeks, 71. "Now they're live-streaming 'pre-apocalyptic aesthetic' content while the fire department fights for their lives. It is the death of community."

She later told reporters she was "manifesting safety" by refusing to wear shoes, claiming "grounded electrons repel flame."

The ethical fracture is widening. Neighbors are now suing each other for the "emotional distress" of having to witness a lack of panic. "This isn't just a fire," said Dr. Lena Vortex, a moral philosopher. "This is a referendum on empathy. We have swapped the instinct to protect for the impulse to perform. Society is not burning; it is posting."
