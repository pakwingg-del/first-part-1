**"CEO Tax" Alert: Your Morning Coffee Just Got More Expensive as "Founder Mode" Spreads to Small Businesses**

**By: The Wallet Watchdog**

Think the “tech bro” era only affects Silicon Valley? Think again. In a move that’s hitting your local coffee shop and gym, an increasing number of small business founders are adopting a controversial new "Founder First" pricing policy—and they’re passing the cost directly onto you.

The trend, dubbed the "CEO Tax" by consumer groups, was sparked after a viral video of a wealthy app founder complaining that loyal customers are "rent-seekers" who take value without contributing to the "vision." Now, your neighborhood roaster, bookstore, and yoga studio are following suit. They're adding a 3-5% "Founder's Vision Fee" to every checkout.

Here’s the kicker: The fee is non-negotiable and isn't for better ingredients, living wages, or rent. It goes directly to the owner’s "personal runway" fund.

**The Bite:** A $5 latte now rings up at $5.25. A $40 monthly gym membership is $42. The logic? "Influencers get a cut; why shouldn't the founder?" But when your local owner claims they’re being "undervalued," it means your wallet is being overcharged.

**Reality Check:** This isn't a "growth hack." It’s a surcharge on your daily life. If the founder wants a bonus, maybe they should sell a better product, not just raise the price. Don’t let "hustle culture" become a line item on your receipt.
