**"THE RABB EFFECT": YOUR NEXT LIGHT BULB COULD BE TAXED – AND YOU WON'T EVEN KNOW IT**

A new financial loophole dubbed "The Rabb Effect" is hitting consumers in the wallet, and the man behind it isn't a politician. He's a contractor from Ohio.

Chris Rabb, a 39-year-old father of two, has successfully lobbied a major home improvement chain to add a **"sustainability surcharge"** to every single purchase of LED and energy-efficient light bulbs. The catch? The money doesn't go to saving the planet. Rabb’s company, "Rabb Renewables," claims the surcharge is to cover their costs for "testing bulb longevity."

**The true cost to you?** An extra **$0.47** per bulb.

Need to replace 10 bulbs in your kitchen? That’s **$4.70** you didn't budget for.

“This is a shell game dressed up in green,” says consumer watchdog Linda Torres. “They call it a ‘surcharge for the environment,’ but Rabb’s firm is pocketing the cash. And retailers are letting him because they get a cut. Your light bill didn’t go down. Your grocery bill went up.”

The chain store defended the move, stating Rabb’s testing is “critical for consumer safety.” Critics point out that a single bulb costs more to “test” than to buy.
