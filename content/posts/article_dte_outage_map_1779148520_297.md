**GLITCH IN THE MATRIX? DTE Outage Map Shows Power Flowing to “Non-Existent” Grid Addresses**

DETROIT, MI — Residents monitoring the DTE Energy outage map for rolling blackouts this morning reported something far stranger than a simple brownout: the map appeared to show power being actively restored to **entire city blocks that, according to municipal records, do not exist.**

The anomaly first surfaced at 3:14 AM when users noticed the map’s “restoration status” icon blinking green over a set of coordinates on the city’s East Side. The location, labeled “Sector 7-G,” corresponds to a plot of land listed as an abandoned rail yard since 1987 — no homes, no meters, no residents.

Yet, the map displayed “Service Restored to 47 customers” at that exact location.

“I zoomed in and the little house icons were just *flickering* on a gravel lot,” said local tech analyst Mara Voss, who first flagged the incident on X. “It’s like the grid is acknowledging a neighborhood that was never built. Or was erased.”

DTE’s official statement called it a “visual data artifact” caused by a “firmware synchronization error.” But Voss and a growing chorus of users aren’t buying it. They point to a second anomaly: the outage map shows a consistent 0.02% power draw from “Sector 7-G” — a figure that has remained exactly flat for 18 months.

“That’s firmware-level weird,” Voss added. “That’s not a glitch. That’s a ghost in the machine — or someone paying a very steady electric bill from an address that doesn’t exist.”
