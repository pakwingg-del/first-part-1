**TOP 5 THINGS YOU NEED TO KNOW ABOUT THE DFW GROUND STOP**

✈️ **1. It Was Triggered by a "Critical Equipment Failure"**
The FAA issued a nationwide ground stop for all flights bound for Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport (DFW) due to a **failure of the airport's primary radar system**. This is the same radar that controllers use to safely space planes during poor visibility and heavy traffic. Without it, the airport could not safely accept incoming flights.

🚦 **2. Expect Massive Ripple Effects All Day**
Even though the ground stop was lifted after roughly 60-90 minutes, the backlog is catastrophic. Planes that were held at gates across the country (from LAX to JFK) will now flood DFW simultaneously. Travelers can expect **delays of 2–4 hours** for arrivals and departures, with many connecting passengers missing their next flights.

🔄 **3. Southwest Airlines Is Hit Hardest (But Not Alone)**
DFW is a major hub for American Airlines (their largest base), but also a critical connecting point for Southwest and United. However, **Southwest operates a "point-to-point" system**—meaning one delay in DFW can break their entire daily schedule. American has already issued a system-wide waiver allowing free rebookings.

📱 **4. The "Cherry on Top": A Simultaneous IT Glitch**
Compounding the radar failure, the FAA's own NOTAM (Notice to Air Missions) system briefly experienced a **minor outage** right as controllers tried to reroute flights. While it didn't cause the ground stop, it slowed the recovery process and caused confusion for pilots checking updated flight plans.
