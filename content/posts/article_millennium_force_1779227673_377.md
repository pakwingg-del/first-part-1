**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**SANDUSKY, OH —** The Cedar Point amusement park in Sandusky, Ohio, reported a significant operational disruption involving the "Millennium Force" roller coaster on the afternoon of October 26, 2023.

**WHO:** Engineers and ride operators from Cedar Point, in coordination with local emergency services.

**WHAT:** The ride experienced an automatic safety shutdown, resulting in the full restraint of the train on the lift hill at a height of approximately 300 feet. Standard emergency protocols were immediately enacted.

**WHERE:** The incident occurred at the flagship giga-coaster attraction, "Millennium Force," located at 1 Cedar Point Drive, Sandusky, Ohio 44870.

**WHEN:** The malfunction was reported at 1:47 PM Eastern Standard Time. The ride was fully evacuated by 2:35 PM.

**WHY:** Preliminary assessments suggest the fault was triggered by a sensor anomaly within the drive-tension system. No mechanical failures or structural damage have been identified. All 28 passengers were safely disembarked via a maintenance catwalk.

**ADDITIONAL DETAILS:** No injuries have been reported. The ride will remain closed pending a full investigation by the Ohio Department of Agriculture's Amusement Ride Safety Division. Cedar Point has issued a formal statement assuring guests that safety systems performed precisely as designed.
