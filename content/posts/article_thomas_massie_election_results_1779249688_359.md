**HEADLINE:** Representative Thomas Massie Secures Re-election in Kentucky's 4th Congressional District

**LOCATION:** Louisville, Kentucky

**DATE:** November 6, 2024

**SOURCE:** Kentucky State Board of Elections

**EVENT:** In the 2024 general election for the United States House of Representatives, incumbent Republican Congressman Thomas Massie has been declared the winner in Kentucky's 4th Congressional District.

**OUTCOME:** According to certified results, Representative Massie defeated his Democratic challenger, garnering over 65% of the vote. This victory secures his return to Washington D.C. for an eighth term in office.

**CONTEXT:** Massie, known for his libertarian-leaning fiscal policies and frequent dissents within his own party, has represented the largely rural and suburban district since 2012. Election analysts note that while the district is considered safely Republican, Massie's margin of victory reflects consistent support from his base despite primary challenges in previous cycles.

**WHAT IT MEANS:** The outcome reinforces the representative’s continued influence in the House, where he serves on the Committee on Transportation and Infrastructure and the Committee on Oversight and Accountability. Political observers expect his focus to remain on constitutional conservatism, auditing the Federal Reserve, and advocating for limited government.
