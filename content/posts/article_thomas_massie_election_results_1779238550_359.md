**SUBJECT:** U.S. Representative Thomas Massie Secures Re-Election in Kentucky’s 4th Congressional District.

**EVENT OUTCOME:**

Incumbent Republican Congressman Thomas Massie has successfully won re-election to represent Kentucky’s 4th Congressional District. Final tallies indicate that Representative Massie secured 64.8 percent of the vote, a margin exceeding 30 percentage points over his nearest competitor. Crowder obtained 31.9 percent, while Walker received 3.2 percent of ballots cast.

**SIGNIFICANCE:**

The result reaffirms Massie’s ten-year hold on the district, which encompasses suburban Louisville, rural areas, and counties in the northeastern part of the state. The victory is viewed as a strong endorsement of Massie’s staunch libertarian-conservative voting record, which has frequently placed him at odds with both Democratic leadership and elements of his own party.

**CONTEXT:**

The 4th District, which has not elected a Democrat since 1950, remains a safely Republican seat. Massie’s campaign focused on fiscal restraint, non-interventionist foreign policy, and Second Amendment rights.
