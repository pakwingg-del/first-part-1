**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**LOUISVILLE, KY** — Voters across the Commonwealth of Kentucky cast their ballots Tuesday in the state’s primary elections, setting the stage for critical general election contests this fall.

**What:** The Kentucky primary election, a closed primary where only registered Democrats and Republicans could vote for their respective party candidates.

**Who:** Key races included the Republican primary for U.S. Senate, where incumbent Senator Mitch McConnell faced a challenge from the right. On the Democratic side, candidates vied for the nomination to challenge the eventual Republican nominee. Additionally, primaries were held for all six U.S. House seats and numerous state legislative positions.

**When:** Tuesday, May 21, 2024. Polls opened at 6:00 a.m. ET and closed at 6:00 p.m. ET.

**Where:** Polling locations in all 120 counties across the Commonwealth of Kentucky.

**Why:** The primary elections determine the Republican and Democratic nominees who will compete in the November 5, 2024, general election. The outcomes will shape the political landscape for the next legislative cycle, with control of the state House of Representatives and Kentucky’s influence in the U.S. Senate at stake.

**Key Preliminary Results (as of late evening):**

*   **U.S. Senate (R):** Incumbent Senator Mitch McConnell has been projected as the winner of the Republican primary, according to multiple news outlets.
*   **U.S. Senate (D):** Marine Corps veteran and former fighter pilot Amy McGrath conceded the Democratic primary to Charles Booker, a former state representative, following a closely watched race.
*   **U.S. House District 1 (R):** Incumbent Representative James Comer secured his party’s nomination.

**Official confirmation of all results is pending final vote tabulations and certification by the Kentucky Secretary of State's office.**
