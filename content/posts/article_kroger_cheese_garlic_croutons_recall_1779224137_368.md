**BREAKING NEWS FLASH**

**DATE:** [Current Date]
**SUBJECT:** Food Safety Alert: Kroger Issues Voluntary Recall of Cheese Garlic Croutons Over Undeclared Allergen

**LOCATION:** National Distribution Network, United States

**DETAILS:**

**WHO:** The Kroger Company, one of the nation's largest grocery retailers, in coordination with the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA).

**WHAT:** An immediate, voluntary recall of a specific product: **Kroger Cheese Garlic Croutons**. The recall is classified as a Class I health hazard, indicating a reasonable probability that the use of, or exposure to, the product will cause serious adverse health consequences or death.

**WHEN:** The recall was officially announced today. The affected products carry specific "Best If Used By" dates, which have been identified by the manufacturer.

**WHY:** The action is being taken due to the product's failure to declare the presence of **milk** on its ingredient label. The product is reported to contain milk, a known major food allergen, which was inadvertently omitted from the packaging during production.

**HOW:** Consumers who have purchased the product are strongly urged to:
1.  **Inspect their pantries** for any packages matching the recalled lot numbers and expiration dates.
2.  **Do not consume** the product if you have an allergy or sensitivity to milk.
3.  **Return the product** to the point of purchase for a full refund, or dispose of it immediately.

**AUTHORITY STATEMENT:**

The FDA has been notified of this action. Kroger states that no illnesses or adverse reactions have been confirmed to date. However, consumers with milk allergies or severe sensitivities who ingest this product face a risk of serious or life-threatening allergic reaction.
