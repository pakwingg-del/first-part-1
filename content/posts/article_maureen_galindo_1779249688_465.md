**VIRAL NEWS SNIPPET: MAUREEN GALINDO**

**Claim:** *A since-deleted Facebook post from political commentator Maureen Galindo claims that a "classified White House memo" reveals the U.S. government is planning to use "emergency powers" to forcibly vaccinate all American citizens against a "synthetic bird flu" supposedly released in a lab accident.*

**VERDICT: FAKE**

**Fact-Check:**
- **No Such Memo Exists:** Neither the White House, CDC, nor any federal agency has issued or acknowledged a classified memo matching this description. No credible news outlets—left or right—have reported on it.
- **No Evidence of Lab Accident:** There is zero verified evidence of a new synthetic bird flu or any lab-related release. The claim appears to blend old conspiracy theories about vaccine mandates and Gain-of-Function research.
- **Misattributed Source:** Maureen Galindo often shares unverified "leaked documents" that her followers cannot independently confirm. In this case, the image of the "memo" is a crude digital fabrication using a generic government template font (Calibri, not the official Times New Roman used by the White House).
- **Real Context:** While Galindo is a known commentator for her sharp criticisms of the media and government, she has a history of amplifying fringe claims without sourcing. This specific post was shared over 2,000 times in three hours before Facebook removed it for violating its policy on fabricated content.

**Bottom Line:** There is no plan for forced mass vaccination under emergency powers. Verified public health planning for avian flu focuses on surveillance and voluntary seasonal vaccine updates—not mandatory jabs. **Don’t share.**
