**“The Matrix Has a WhatsApp Glitch: Users Report ‘Ghost Messages’ Sent from 2016”**  

In a bizarre digital phenomenon that’s sending shivers down the spines of tech users worldwide, dozens of WhatsApp accounts are suddenly receiving messages that were supposedly sent *years ago* — as if the app’s timeline itself has fractured.  

Users report seeing old chat threads inexplicably “ping” with messages from 2016, 2018, and even earlier — complete with timestamps from those eras — appearing as if they were sent fresh this morning. The messages range from mundane “hey, u there?” texts to full photo-dumps and voice notes from old relationships, dead relatives, or forgotten group chats.  

“I got a voice note from my late grandmother — she passed in 2017. It just showed up today, like she was still alive,” said one Reddit user. “I literally dropped my phone.”  

WhatsApp has yet to issue an official statement, but cybersecurity experts are baffled. Some suspect a server-side indexing glitch where cached data is being re-queued into active message streams. Others are more paranoid: “It could be a time-stamp spoof exploit,” says Dr. Lena Voss, a digital forensics researcher. “But the eerie part is that these messages aren’t just resending old texts — they’re appearing as *new* notifications, with correct historical metadata. That shouldn’t be possible without a major back-end error.”  

The glitch seems to affect both Android and iOS devices, and it’s not limited to a single region. A frenzy of screenshots, conspiracy theories, and emotional confessions has flooded social media under the hashtag **#GhostChats**.  

One viral thread even claims a user received a message from a friend who had *deleted their account in 2019*.
