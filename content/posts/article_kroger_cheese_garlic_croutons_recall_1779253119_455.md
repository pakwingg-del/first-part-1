**🚨 URGENT RECALL ALERT: KROGER CHEESE & GARLIC CROUTONS**

**VIRAL CLAIM:** Shoppers are sharing frantic videos of finding small, hard, **translucent blue pellets** inside bags of Kroger’s “Cheese & Garlic Croutons” (Family Size 10 oz). The claim says these pellets are *not* garlic cloves or cheese, but resemble **silica gel beads or plastic resin pellets**.

**REALITY CHECK: ✅ PARTIALLY TRUE — BUT SCARY MISDIAGNOSIS.**

The FDA has issued a **Class II recall** for certain lot codes of these croutons. However, the “blue pellets” are **not silica gel or plastic**. A factory investigation revealed the pellets are **mis-colored, under-processed garlic-chive compound** that hardens into a non-toxic, but dangerously hard, bead.

**THE REAL RISK:** While not poisonous, the beads are a **serious choking hazard** and can crack teeth. Consumers have reported chipped molars and one pet injury after a dog ate fallen croutons.

**WHAT TO DO:**
✅ Check your bag for Lot # **KL48-03G** or **KL49-05A** with a “Best By” date of **Jan 2026**.
✅ **DO NOT eat them.** Return to Kroger for a full refund.
❌ **FAKE:** Claims that Kroger is denying the issue. They are cooperating fully.
❌ **FAKE:** Rumors the beads are “melted toy plastic” or “insect eggs.” False. It is a failed production batch.
