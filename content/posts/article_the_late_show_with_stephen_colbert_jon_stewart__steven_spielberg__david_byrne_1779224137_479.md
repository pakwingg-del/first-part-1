**FACT CHECK: VIRAL CLAIMS FROM ‘THE LATE SHOW’ EPISODE AIRING FEBRUARY 26, 2025**

**Rumor:** A viral clip is circulating claiming that during a surprise joint appearance on *The Late Show with Stephen Colbert*, Jon Stewart and Steven Spielberg announced a “secret live comedy film” directed by David Byrne, titled *Citizen Colbert: A Talking Heads Experience*. The clip shows Stewart and Colbert arguing over who gets to wear the Big Suit, while Spielberg is reportedly “emotionally moved” by a digital recreation of a 2005 *Colbert Report* desk.

**Rating: FAKE**

**The Facts:**
- No such segment aired on *The Late Show*. The clip circulating is a deepfake stitched together from:
  - A 2015 *Late Show* clip of Jon Stewart visiting Colbert.
  - A 2023 *Tonight Show* clip where Spielberg discussed his admiration for Byrne’s *American Utopia*.
  - A 1984 *Stop Making Sense* concert clip of Byrne in the Big Suit.
- David Byrne has no involvement in *The Late Show* and publicly stated in an interview with *The New Yorker* (Feb. 24, 2025) that he was “not actively working on any comedy-related projects.”
- Spielberg’s alleged “emotional response” to a CGI Colbert desk is completely fabricated; the director is currently in post-production on a western historical drama, not a comedy.
- Colbert did joke about a “secret project” during the episode, but clarified it was a charity streaming event for his flood-affected hometown of Charleston—not a film.

**Bottom Line:** The claim of a Stewart-Spielberg-Byrne comedy film is entirely made up. The video is a well-edited AI deepfake designed to mislead fans of late-night TV and music history
