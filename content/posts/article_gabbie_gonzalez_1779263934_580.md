**BY: ARTHUR PENDLETON, SENIOR MORAL CORRESPONDENT**

I have spent my career chronicling the slow erosion of public shame. I have watched as we traded integrity for virality and nuance for rage clicks. I thought I had seen the bottom. I was wrong.

Gabriele "Gabbie" Gonzalez, the influencer synonymous with a decade of "call-out culture" chaos—from the dramatic implosions of former friendships to the deeply troubling allegations of psychological manipulation and animal cruelty—has found a new groove. And society, in its eternal quest for the next fix of dopamine, is eagerly swallowing it.

Her recent podcast snippets, which I have been forced to analyze, are not just "problematic." They are a **moral null set.** In these clips, Gonzalez appears to weaponize therapy jargon to retroactively justify past transgressions, painting herself as a martyr to the very outrage culture she helped spawn. She speaks of "boundaries" while dissecting the trauma of her accusers, and of "healing" while actively monetizing the scars of her own chaos.

The "angle" here is not about one person's redemption. Redemption is a beautiful, difficult path. This is about a **mechanism of self-destruction.**

We are watching an influencer who once faced the collective fury of an entire internet, who was held to a standard (rightly or wrongly), now find a back door.
