**BUSTED: National Weather Service Issues 'Severe Thunderstorm Warning' for a Single, Very Dramatic Cloud**

**POTSDAM, NY** — In what meteorologists are calling the most emotionally overachieving weather event of the decade, the National Weather Service briefly issued a severe thunderstorm warning yesterday that applied exclusively to a single, sentient-looking cumulonimbus cloud that was reportedly “having a moment.”

Witnesses described the cloud, later identified on social media as “Brad,” as being approximately the size of a compact car but possessing the dramatic energy of an Oscar-winning monologue. “It rumbled once—like a lonely college kid’s stomach during finals week—and then it just glowered,” said local resident Karen Mills. “I felt personally judged.”

The warning, which covered an area of roughly 11 square feet, advised residents to seek shelter from “gusty winds that may ruffle hair” and “the distinct sensation of being side-eyed by the sky.” Doppler radar mistook Brad’s intense concentration for rotation, triggering a flurry of alerts that sent local news teams scrambling to a parking lot.

“We’ve never seen a cloud so committed to the bit,” said NWS spokesperson Dr. Lee R. Nasty. “It wasn’t dropping hail. It was dropping attitude.”

Brad eventually dissipated after a teenager yelled “Yeet” in its general direction, but not before trending as #MainCharacterCloud, prompting memes comparing the warning to a Zoom call where only one person’s mic is on. Critics argue the overblown reaction is a sign of our times, but the cloud remains unrepentant. As one weather app put it: “Threat Level: Aesthetic.”
