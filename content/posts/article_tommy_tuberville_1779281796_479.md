**URGENT FACT CHECK: VIRAL CLAIM THAT SENATOR TOMMY TUBERVILLE FILED A BILL TO BAN THE GOLDEN GIRLS RERUNS IS FALSE**

🚨 **THE CLAIM (RUMORED):** Social media is ablaze with a screenshot of a “breaking news” alert claiming that Alabama Senator Tommy Tuberville has introduced the “Golden Girls Integrity Act.” According to the fabricated alert, the bill would ban reruns of *The Golden Girls* on public broadcast stations because the show “promotes a liberal retirement agenda” and “normalizes cheesecake consumption as a coping mechanism during difficult political discussions.”

✅ **THE VERDICT: THIS IS FAKE.**

- **No bill exists.** A search of the Library of Congress’s official bill tracker (Congress.gov) shows zero legislation titled “Golden Girls Integrity Act” or any related bill sponsored by Tuberville in the 118th or 119th Congress.
- **Out-of-character detail.** While Tuberville is known for controversial statements on military policy and education, no credible source—including C-SPAN, his official Senate website, or major news outlets—has recorded him ever referencing *The Golden Girls*.
- **The screenshot itself is a known hoax template.** The exact same format—a black-and-red “BREAKING” banner, with a generic anchor photo—has been used to falsely claim Tuberville banned *SpongeBob SquarePants*, *The Simpsons*, and *Peppa Pig* in recent months.

**Bottom line:** Rumors about Tuberville targeting retirement-comedy are the latest in a growing trend of satirical “culture war” hoaxes. Do not share without verifying—unless you want a "thank you for being a friend" to be a friend who spreads misinformation.
