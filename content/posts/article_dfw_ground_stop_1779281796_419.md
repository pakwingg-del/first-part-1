**HEADLINE: DFW MELTDOWN: FAA ORDERS EMERGENCY GROUND STOP—$50M AN HOUR AT RISK**

**THE SITUATION**
The FAA just slammed a full ground stop on Dallas/Fort Worth International. Reason cited: massive air traffic control staffing shortage. No inbound flights. No outbound. All gates frozen.

**BUSINESS IMPACT**
- DFW handles ~1,900 daily departures, ~$50M in direct economic throughput per hour.
- At current pace, this disruption costs:
  - American Airlines: ~$8M/hour in lost revenue.
  - Cargo/logistics (FedEx, UPS hubs): ~$4M/hour in delayed shipments.
  - Corporate travel: thousands of executives grounded mid-Q2 earnings season.

**ROOT CAUSE**
The FAA’s Fort Worth Center is hemorrhaging controllers. Attrition rate: 12% annually vs. 4% hiring fill rate. No surge capacity.

**STRATEGIC MESSAGE TO CEO**
This is not a weather event. It’s a systemic infrastructure failure. If your supply chain, client meetings, or executive mobility rely on DFW—evacuate the dependency now. Diversify to regional HQs, private charter, or digital-first meetings until the FAA fixes its pipeline. The ground stop is a warning shot for Q3.
