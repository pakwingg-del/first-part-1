**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**DTE Energy Outage Map Reports Widespread Power Disruptions Across Southeast Michigan**

**DETROIT, MI –** DTE Energy’s official Outage Map is currently displaying significant power interruptions affecting thousands of residential and commercial customers throughout Southeast Michigan. According to the utility’s live tracking system, service disruptions are concentrated primarily in Wayne, Oakland, and Macomb counties.

**What:** A major DTE Outage Map update indicates power loss impacting an estimated 42,000 customers as of 8:00 a.m. EST. The map, which provides real-time data on outages by zip code, shows the heaviest concentration of lost service in the communities of Royal Oak, Warren, and Livonia.

**Who:** DTE Energy, the primary energy provider for the affected regions, has dispatched repair crews to the impacted areas. Company officials have confirmed that the cause is under active investigation but is not currently linked to any known storm activity. Customers in the affected zones are advised to check the map for individualized restoration estimates.

**When:** The outages were first reported by automated sensors at approximately 5:45 a.m. EST this morning. The map currently estimates full restoration for the majority of impacted customers by 12:00 p.m. EST, though DTE has cautioned that this timeline is subject to change based on infrastructure damage assessments.

**Where:** The primary outage zones are clustered within a 30-mile radius of downtown Detroit. Specific regions highlighted on the DTE Outage Map include the 48073, 48323, and 48150 ZIP codes, where outage percentages have exceeded 15% of total customers.

**Why:** According to a preliminary statement from DTE Energy, the widespread failures are likely the result of a "confluence of system faults" involving a suspected equipment malfunction at a major substation in Macomb County. Further investigation is underway to rule out human error or weather-related causes.
