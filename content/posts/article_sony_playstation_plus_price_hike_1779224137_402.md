# Top 5 Things You Need to Know About Sony's PlayStation Plus Price Hike

- **Massive price increases across all tiers**: Sony has raised the cost of PlayStation Plus subscriptions by up to 35% — the Essential tier jumps from $59.99 to $79.99/year, while Premium skyrockets from $119.99 to $159.99/year.

- **Existing members get a temporary grace period**: Current subscribers won't feel the sting immediately — the new pricing applies to renewals *after* November 6, 2024, giving players a few months to decide whether to lock in current rates.

- **No new features to justify the cost**: Here's the kicker — Sony hasn't announced any additional games, cloud streaming upgrades, or exclusive perks alongside the hike. It's a pure price increase with zero value-add.

- **Grandfather loophole is closing**: Players who stocked up on discounted PS Plus cards or used the "stacking" method to extend subscriptions for years can no longer do so. Sony has capped maximum subscription length at 12 months.

- **Gamers are revolting — and switching**: Social media is flooded with backlash using #PSPlusPriceHike, and many players are canceling auto-renewal in favor of Xbox Game Pass or skipping the subscription entirely until a sale or value improvement.
