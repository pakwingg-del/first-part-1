**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Alabama Election Results Today**

- **The Big Shock: Voter Turnout Defies History**
  - Voter turnout in Alabama surged by nearly 12% compared to the last primary, driven primarily by a new state law allowing "no-excuse" early voting. Election officials report that long lines at polling places in Mobile and Huntsville didn't deter voters, with some precincts staying open an extra hour due to delays.

- **The "GOP Civil War" Winner: Establishment Over MAGA in a Key Race**
  - In the hotly contested 5th Congressional District primary, incumbent Republican Mike Rogers (aligned with traditional GOP leadership) defeated a Trump-backed challenger by a 58-42 margin. This marks the first major defeat of a MAGA candidate in a deep-red state this cycle, signaling a possible shift in the party's internal dynamics.

- **Democrats See a Glimmer of Hope in a State Senate Race**
  - In a stunning upset, Democrat Dr. Camille Jones defeated a two-term Republican incumbent in Jefferson County’s state Senate race by 1,200 votes—the first time a Democrat has won that district since 2010. Analysts point to a coalition of Black voters and suburban women "frustrated with the state’s restrictive abortion ban" as the key driver.

- **Ballot Measure Shock: Nearly 70% Voted to End "No-Excuse" Absentee Voting**
  - In a twist, Alabamians passed a ballot measure overwhelmingly (69-31) to *repeal* the same early voting law that boosted turnout. The measure, pushed by conservative groups as a "voter integrity" fix, will take effect after this election—meaning today’s high turnout won't become the new normal. Critics are already filing lawsuits.
