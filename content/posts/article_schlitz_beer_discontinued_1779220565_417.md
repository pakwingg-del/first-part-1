**5 Things You Need to Know About the Schlitz Beer Discontinuation Bombshell**

- **The "Original" Pabst Formula is Gone.** After a failed 2021 revival attempt that promised "the exact formula from 1960," Pabst Brewing Company has officially pulled the plug on the Schlitz brand. Production stopped last month, citing a sharp decline in both retail shelf space and bar tap handles.
- **Blame the Canned Cocktail Boom.** Internal sources say the final nail in the coffin wasn't craft beer, but the rise of ready-to-drink spirits (like White Claw and High Noon). Schlitz’s core demographic of value-conscious, blue-collar drinkers shifted to hard seltzers and canned cocktails during the pandemic and never returned.
- **The "Green Death" Curse Strikes Again.** Schlitz’s "Tastes as Good as It Used To" campaign was meant to bury the infamous 1970s reputation—when the brand switched to a cheaper, high-speed brewing process that created skunky beer (nicknamed "Green Death"). The 2021 batch was praised, but the stigma of the '70s proved impossible to fully shake.
- **Collectors Are Panic-Buying.** On eBay and beer-trading forums, sealed cans of the 2021 revival are selling for $25-$40 per 6-pack—nearly 500% above the original retail price. Some speculators are hoarding tallboys, betting on a future "cult classic" status.
- **There's Only One Hope Left.** Pabst holds the rights to a 2019 test batch of "Schlitz Malt Liquor Bull" (a higher-alcohol version). Industry insiders suggest a limited, super-premium "Heritage Schlitz" cask-aged release could hit taprooms in late 2024—but only if the current hype bubble doesn’t burst first.
