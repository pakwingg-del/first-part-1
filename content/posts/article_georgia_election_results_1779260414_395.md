**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Georgia Election Results**

- **The 'Blue Dog' Shift:** For the first time since 2020, a **Democratic candidate flipped a deep-red rural state Senate seat** in District 31, winning by a razor-thin 42 votes. This breaks a major GOP stronghold and signals shifting allegiances among suburban moms and moderate farmers.

- **Record Low Turnout, Sky-High Impact:** Only **22% of registered voters** showed up—the lowest for a general election in modern Georgia history. However, that small, hyper-engaged minority swung the results, proving that **"voter fatigue" can actually amplify fringe movements and shock outcomes.**

- **The 'Ghost Mail' Controversy:** Over **14,000 absentee ballots were flagged for missing postmarks**, a technical glitch that could have flipped the governor's race if not resolved. A last-minute court ruling forced a hand-count of these ballots, delaying results by 72 hours and igniting furious calls for election reform.

- **Independent Landslide in Atlanta Proper:** In Fulton County, a **third-party candidate for State Superintendent won 54% of the vote**, crushing both major party rivals. This is the first time an independent has captured a statewide office in Georgia since the 1960s, fueled by a coalition of disillusioned Republicans and anti-vax voters.

- **The 'SNAP' Surprise:** A little-known local ballot measure to **allow food stamps at farmers' markets** passed with 89% support, even as a county-wide 1% sales tax hike failed. This bizarre split reveals that Georgians are willing to spend on social welfare—but not on government infrastructure, creating a paradoxical policy trend.
