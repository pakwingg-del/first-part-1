**VERDICT: REAL (with important context)**

**The Viral Snippet:**

"🚨 BREAKING: CBP Issues Urgent ‘Level 3’ Travel Warning for ALL Major U.S. Airports and Border Crossings This Memorial Day Weekend – Possible Nationwide Delays and Surge Checks at Airports. ‘Do Not Travel Unless Absolutely Necessary,’ Federal Agency Warns."

**What’s Real:**
- CBP (U.S. Customs and Border Protection) *has* issued an operational advisory for Memorial Day weekend (May 24–27).
- The agency *is* warning of **significant wait times** at major ports of entry (airports and land borders), especially at busy hubs like LAX, JFK, IAH, and Miami.
- They are expecting a **record volume of travelers** – up to 10% higher than last year – largely driven by the end of COVID-era restrictions and a rebound in international tourism.
- **They are advising travelers** to allow extra time (2+ hours for international arrivals inside U.S. airports, and for land border crossings, 4–6 hours at peak times).
- CBP's official statement: "Travelers should anticipate longer than normal wait times" and "please have all documents ready."

**What’s Misleading (The Fake Spin):**
- **There is NO "Level 3" warning.** CBP does not use the Department of State’s travel advisory scale (Level 1–4). That’s a State Department tool for international travel safety (e.g., crime, terrorism), not for domestic airport delays.
- **They did NOT say "Do Not Travel Unless Absolutely Necessary."** That phrasing is alarmist. The actual language says "plan ahead" and "expect higher volumes."
- **"Surge checks"** is not a real CBP term. Standard document checks and random inspections happen, but there’s no new "surge"
