**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**KANSAS CITY, MO** — In a twist of digital irony that has the internet in stitches, Evergy’s notoriously glitchy *Online Outage Map* has officially become more popular than the power it was supposed to track.

The map, which frequently crashes, loads in slow motion, or shows entire suburbs as still having power while they are clearly in darkness, has become an unlikely viral sensation. Residents have taken to social media not to complain about the blackout, but to document the map's creative failures—like showing a single UPS store as the last beacon of light in a 50-mile radius, or labeling a major transformer explosion as “routine maintenance.”

“I’m not mad. I’m impressed,” tweeted user @MidwestNoPower. “The map just told me my outage was ‘completed’ while my house is literally freezing. I think my fridge is running a Kickstarter campaign.”

Utilities expert Dr. Clara Gridlock explains the humor: “It’s a perfect storm of 21st-century frustration. You’re powerless, literally, and the one tool meant to help you is equally powerless—just with better Wi-Fi. People are laughing because crying would require electricity to run the water pump.”

The meme has evolved: artists are now redrawing the map in MS Paint, betting pools have formed on which zip code will load first, and one user claims the map is actually a 1999 screensaver from a dentist’s office.

Evergy released a statement: “We are aware of the *outage map outage* and are working to restore the outage map’s outage map. Please use our automated phone system, which is powered by interpretive dance.”
