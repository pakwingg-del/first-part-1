**Viral News Snippet: "BREAKING: Sony Drops PS Plus Bombshell – $100+ Price Hike for Essential Tier; Fans Livid, Calling It 'The End of an Era' as Hidden Clause Reveals Auto-Renewal Trap!"**

**The Claim:** Social media is exploding with reports that Sony is implementing a massive price increase for PlayStation Plus, with the Essential tier skyrocketing to over $100/year—nearly double its current $79.99 price. A "hidden clause" in the new terms allegedly forces users who cancel to wait 12 months before being eligible for any future discounts or promotions.

**The Reality: FALSE (mostly exaggerated).** While Sony did increase PS Plus prices in September 2023 (Essential from $59.99 to $79.99, Extra from $99.99 to $134.99, Premium from $119.99 to $159.99), there is no current, new hike to $100+. The viral post misrepresents the 2023 changes as a new, even larger increase. The "hidden clause" claim is also false—Sony’s cancellation policy hasn’t changed; users can cancel anytime, but auto-renewal is on by default (standard industry practice). No 12-month penalty or discount lockout exists.

**Verdict:** Misleading. The price hike already happened, and the rumored $100+ Essential tier and penalty clause are fabricated. However, the outrage reflects valid frustration over the legitimate 2023 increases. Check Sony’s official blog for accurate, current pricing.
