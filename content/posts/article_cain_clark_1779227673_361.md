**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**Dateline: Washington, D.C. – February 21, 2025**

**Headline: Cain Clark Named Special Counsel to the House Committee on Government Oversight, Tasked with Cross-Jurisdictional Procurement Audit**

**By: National News Desk**

**Who:** Cain Clark, a former Deputy Inspector General for the Department of the Interior and a recognized expert in federal contract compliance, has accepted an appointment as Special Counsel to the House Committee on Government Oversight and Accountability.

**What:** Clark has been tasked with leading a comprehensive, cross-jurisdictional audit of sole-source procurement contracts issued by five federal agencies over the past two fiscal years. The investigation is authorized under a newly passed House Resolution, granting him subpoena power and access to classified financial records. The scope of the review includes identifying instances of cost overruns, conflicts of interest, and alleged violations of the Competition in Contracting Act (CICA).

**When:** The appointment was announced earlier today by Committee Chairwoman Representative Evelyn Hayes. Mr. Clark is expected to submit an interim report on his findings within 90 days, with a final, public-facing report due by the end of the current congressional session.

**Where:** The investigation will centralize operations within the committee’s offices in the Rayburn House Office Building, with field audits scheduled for regional procurement offices in Texas, California, and Virginia.

**Why:** The committee cited a "demonstrated pattern of procurement irregularities" and a need for "impartial, forensic-level oversight" that transcends standard agency review. Chairwoman Hayes stated that Clark’s "track record of holding contractors and government entities equally accountable makes him singularly qualified to navigate these complex financial frameworks." No specific contracts or agencies were named in the public statement, though sources indicate the review may touch on recent defense and infrastructure spending authorizations.
