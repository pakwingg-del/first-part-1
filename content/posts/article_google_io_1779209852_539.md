**Headline:** *“The AI That Reads Your Mind”: At Google I/O, a Life Coach Asks—Are You Letting Algorithms Write Your Story?*

**Snippet:**
MOUNTAIN VIEW, CA — As Google I/O wowed the world with Project Astra—an AI that can ‘see’ your environment, recall your past conversations, and predict your next needs—one Silicon Valley life coach stood motionless in the crowd, then whispered to her client over the noise: “They just built a mirror for your future self. But here’s the real question: Are you letting the machine tell you what you want, before you’ve even asked yourself?”

The viral moment came when the coach, Dr. Elena Voss, pulled out a notebook and wrote: *“Technology can remember your grocery list. But only you can remember your soul’s purpose.”* Her client, a product manager for a smart-home startup, later confessed, “She made me realize: I’ve been outsourcing my dreams to a calendar app that doesn’t have a heart.”

As Google touted Gemini’s ability to plan your day, curate your memories, and even ‘reassure you’ after a tough meeting, Voss offered a psychological counterpunch: “Be careful. The most addictive drug isn’t dopamine—it’s certainty. And the moment you let AI remove every doubt, you also remove every chance to discover who you really are.”

**The Challenge:** In a world where your phone is becoming your therapist, your assistant, and your memory—how do you make sure *you’re* still the author of your own life?
