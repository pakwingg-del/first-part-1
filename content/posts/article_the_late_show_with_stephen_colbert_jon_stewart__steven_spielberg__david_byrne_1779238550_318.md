**SAMANTHA VANE – SPECIAL REPORT**

Officially, the gathering was billed as a “creative summit” to discuss a one-off special. But sources close to three of the participants describe it as a “stealth think tank” aimed at re-framing the public conversation about media trust, propaganda, and narrative control ahead of the next election cycle.

“They are not just writing jokes,” a production assistant who witnessed part of the session told this reporter. “It was a working session on how to bypass the gatekeepers. Spielberg brought a storyboard about ‘unreliable narrators’—his words. Stewart was asking, ‘Who benefits if we keep making the same arguments?’ And Byrne kept referencing ‘the architecture of consent.’”

The shift is notable. Colbert’s *Late Show* has already moved away from pure comedy into a nightly essay on media manipulation. Stewart, after leaving *The Daily Show*, has been publicly critical of both parties. Byrne’s *American Utopia* directly questioned the production of reality. And Spielberg—who once directed *The Post* about the Pentagon Papers—is said to be consulting on a “visual language” for the project.
