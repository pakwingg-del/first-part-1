**AUTHORITIES CONFIRM ARREST OF JAKE SHANE IN CONNECTION WITH INTERNATIONAL CYBER FRAUD RING**

**WASHINGTON, D.C. (October 26, 2023)** — Federal law enforcement officials confirmed today the arrest of 34-year-old Jake Shane, a prominent figure in the cybersecurity community, following a months-long joint investigation into a sophisticated international cyber fraud operation.

**Who:** The suspect is identified as Jake Shane, a U.S. citizen and former consultant for a major tech firm. He was taken into custody without incident at his residence in Austin, Texas, at approximately 6:00 AM local time.

**What:** Shane faces a total of 17 federal charges, including wire fraud, conspiracy to commit computer intrusion, and money laundering. Authorities allege he served as a key architect for a network that siphoned over $47 million from small to medium-sized businesses across three continents by deploying ransomware and phishing schemes.

**Where:** The operation was allegedly orchestrated from a server farm located in Eastern Europe, with cells operating in the United States, the United Kingdom, and Thailand. The arrest was the culmination of a coordinated effort between the FBI’s Cyber Division, Europol, and the Royal Thai Police.

**When:** The indictment was unsealed earlier today in the Southern District of New York. Shane is expected to appear before a federal magistrate judge tomorrow morning.

**Why:** According to the U.S. Department of Justice, Shane leveraged his legitimate cybersecurity expertise to bypass advanced security protocols, specifically targeting municipal infrastructure and healthcare networks during the peak of the global pandemic. “This defendant used his skills not to protect, but to prey on the most vulnerable entities in our digital ecosystem,” stated Deputy Attorney General Lisa M. Cortez during a press conference.
