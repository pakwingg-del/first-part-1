**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Roy Cooper-Michael Whatley Poll**

- **The Poll Shakes Up the North Carolina Senate Race:** A new survey reveals a tighter-than-expected contest, showing Governor Roy Cooper leading State Senator Michael Whatley by a razor-thin margin of 48% to 45%—a statistical dead heat that has national Democrats scrambling.

- **Independent Voters Are the Wildcard:** The biggest surprise? The poll suggests Whatley is outperforming typical GOP candidates among unaffiliated voters, cutting Cooper’s expected advantage in half. This has flipped the "safe Democrat" narrative on its head.

- **It’s Not Just About Party Lines—It’s About Hurricane Recovery:** The poll correlates directly with voter sentiment on the state’s disaster response. Cooper’s approval remains high, but Whatley gains ground among rural communities who feel "left behind" by recent recovery efforts in the mountains.

- **A Gender and Age Gap That’s Unusual for North Carolina:** Women under 45 are breaking for Cooper by 12 points, but men over 50—a key swing group—are backing Whatley by a surprising 15-point margin. This disconnect is fueling speculation about a high-turnout suburban vs. exurban battle.

- **The "Cooper Factor" May Be Peaking Too Early:** For the first time, a plurality of respondents say they "want a new face" in the governor’s mansion, even if they approve of Cooper personally. Whatley’s campaign is already using this bullet point as a viral ad slogan: *"Think you know the outcome? This poll says you don’t."*
