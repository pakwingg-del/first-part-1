🤦‍♂️ **COMMON SENSE ALERT** 🤦‍♂️

Just saw the latest from the "Trump Rx" folks selling those supplements. Look, I’m no doctor, but my granddaddy ran a farm for 50 years and never needed a 10-in-1 pill with a politician’s face on it to get his work done.

Here’s the thing nobody wants to say out loud: if a product has to slap a former President’s name on it to make you think it works, it probably doesn’t. That’s just common sense. My neighbor bought a three-month supply for $200 because the ad said it had “patriot energy.” Now he can’t get a refund, and the only thing “energy” he has is trying to call the 1-800 number that’s always busy.

We’re all for supporting local business and keeping it real, but don’t fall for the slick marketing. If you need a pill, talk to your actual doctor—not a late-night infomercial hosted by a talking head. If it sounds too good, it’s a lot of hogwash.

Stay sharp, folks. And maybe just eat your vegetables. 🥦
