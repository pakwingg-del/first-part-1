**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Millennium Force**

- **The Speed Demon of the Lake:** When it opened in 2000 at Cedar Point, it was the first roller coaster in the world to break the 90 mph and 300-foot height barriers simultaneously. The feeling of that 300-foot drop at a 45-degree angle? Pure, uncorked adrenaline.
- **The "Tunnel" That Tricks Your Brain:** The ride doesn't have a dark, enclosed tunnel at the bottom. Instead, it blasts into a massive, man-made rock trench only 4 feet wider than the train. The proximity of the walls tricks your brain into thinking you're going even faster, creating an optical illusion of sheer velocity.
- **Zero-Gravity, Parking Lot Style:** The second hill isn't just a hill. It’s a massive, 169-foot curved turn dubbed the "Overbanked Turn" that creates sustained weightlessness. While the manufacturer calls it an overbank, coaster enthusiasts know it as the closest thing to a zero-G roll without going upside down.
- **The "Trimming" Controversy:** To keep the ride from being too intense for riders (and the structure), the coaster uses magnetic brakes—called "trims"—on the first drop and the second hill. Some purists complain it shaves off the top speed, but it's what allows the ride to be accessible to the average thrill-seeker while still being a record-breaker.
- **A Legacy of Liquid Helmet Hair:** For 20+ years, the Millennium Force has been the "bucket list" coaster for anyone who loves speed. The iconic, blue, orange, and yellow trains are a symbol of coaster history. And yes, you will absolutely feel like you’re flying off the edge of Lake Erie, which is exactly the point.
