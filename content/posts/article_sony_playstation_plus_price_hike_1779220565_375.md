**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**BREAKING NEWS**

**HEADLINE:** SONY CONFIRMS PLAYSTATION PLUS PRICE INCREASE; SUBSCRIBERS TO FACE HIGHER MONTHLY AND ANNUAL FEES ACROSS ALL TIERS

**DATE:** OCTOBER 26, 2023

**LOCATION:** TOKYO, JAPAN (AND GLOBAL MARKETS)

**WHO:** Sony Interactive Entertainment (SIE) announced a significant pricing adjustment for its PlayStation Plus subscription service. The change will affect all current and new subscribers across the Essential, Extra, and Premium tiers.

**WHAT:** Effective immediately for new subscribers, and from November 6, 2023, for existing members at their next renewal, the monthly and annual fees for PlayStation Plus will rise. Price increases range from 20% to 33% depending on the tier. For example, the 12-month Essential plan will increase from $59.99 to $79.99, the Extra plan from $99.99 to $134.99, and the Premium plan from $119.99 to $159.99. Sony stated the change is necessary to "continue bringing high-quality games and value-added benefits to the PlayStation Plus catalog," citing a challenging global economic environment and increased investment in the service.

**WHEN:** The price hike takes effect immediately for new sign-ups. For current subscribers, the new rates will apply upon their next renewal date on or after November 6, 2023. Stacked subscriptions purchased prior to the announcement will be honored at the original price until they expire.

**WHERE:** The revised pricing applies to all 38 countries and regions where PlayStation Plus is available, including the United States, Canada, Japan, the United Kingdom, and European Union member states.
