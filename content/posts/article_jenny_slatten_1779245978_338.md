**Dateline: Fort Campbell, KY** – As former US soldier Jenny Slatten continues to fight her 30-year sentence for voluntary manslaughter in Iraq, legal analysts are drawing startling parallels to the infamous *1944 Execution of German POWs* at the Biscari Massacre. “Slatten’s case mirrors the Biscari trials in a haunting way,” says military historian Dr. Lena Voss. “Both involve chaotic desert combat, conflicting orders from superiors, and a shocking lack of accountability for higher command.”  

In 1943, Lt. Colonel William O. Perry ordered his men to “kill all prisoners” at the Biscari airfield. 76 Germans died. Yet only one soldier, Sgt. Horace T. West, was convicted—sentenced to life but released after one year. Slatten, by contrast, faces decades.  

“The pattern is clear,” Voss adds. “Low-level soldiers become scapegoats for systemic failures in war. Slatten is today’s Sgt. West—except in a culture that demands justice, not amnesty. The question is: which chapter of history will we repeat?”
