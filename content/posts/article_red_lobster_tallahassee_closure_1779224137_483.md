**SHRIMPING OUT: Red Lobster Ditches Tallahassee, Leaving Cheddar Bay Biscuit Lovers in the Lurch (and Your Wallet Empty)**

**TALLAHASSEE, FL** – In a move that’s hitting seafood fans right in the breadbasket—and the bank account—Red Lobster has officially shuttered its Tallahassee location, leaving the capital city without a single Cheddar Bay Biscuit in sight.

But here’s the kicker for your wallet: this isn’t just about a lost date night spot. Analysts say the closure is a canary in the coal mine for the entire chain, which has been drowning in debt and inflation-related diner drop-offs. For Tallahassee locals, this means a sudden spike in food delivery fees as you scramble to upcharge your own frozen seafood from the grocery store—expect to pay 15-20% more for a "shrimp scampi" DIY kit than you did for the real deal.

**The real sting:** If you had a gift card, you just got hit with a hidden tax. Red Lobster’s bankruptcy filings mean those plastic promises could become worthless faster than a cold lobster tail. "We urge gift card holders to file a claim immediately, but don’t hold your breath," warns consumer advocate Sarah Jenkins. "This is a classic 'race to the bottom' where the diner eats the loss."

**What you can do:** Check your pantry for unused Red Lobster gift cards. If you were a regular, start budgeting for that $18 frozen shrimp platter at Publix—and maybe learn to make your own biscuits.
