**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About Ken Paxton’s Latest Legal Setback**

- **The SEC Just Dropped The Hammer:** The U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) has charged Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton with securities fraud, alleging he misled investors in a tech startup. This is a major escalation beyond his previous state-level legal battles.

- **He’s Accused of a "Ponzi-Like" Scheme:** Federal regulators claim Paxton recruited investors (including a prominent Texas businessman) for a company called *Servergy*, promising huge returns. The SEC says the company’s revenue claims were wildly fabricated, and Paxton allegedly knew it.

- **His Impeachment Trial is Still Fresh:** This comes hot on the heels of his 2023 impeachment in the Texas House over bribery and abuse of office. He was acquitted by the Senate, but this new federal case suggests his legal problems are far from over.

- **A Rare Bipartisan Target:** While Paxton is a GOP firebrand (famous for suing the Biden administration and fighting election results), this SEC charge has even drawn criticism from some fellow Republicans who see it as a reckoning for years of alleged misconduct.

- **He’s Fighting Back... Hard:** Paxton’s legal team is already calling this a "politically motivated witch hunt," pointing out the SEC is run by Biden appointees. Expect a messy, high-stakes legal battle that could end his political career or fuel his base—whichever comes first.
