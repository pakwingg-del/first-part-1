**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE – 14:00 HRS EDT**

**NATIONAL WEATHER SERVICE ISSUES SEVERE THUNDERSTORM WARNING FOR MULTIPLE COUNTIES**

**LOCATION:** The National Weather Service (NWS) has issued a Severe Thunderstorm Warning effective immediately for the following jurisdictions: Central Dallas County, Northern Johnson County, and Eastern Tarrant County in the state of Texas.

**TIMING:** The warning is in effect until 14:45 HRS local time. Residents are advised that this is a rapidly evolving weather situation.

**WHAT:** Doppler radar has detected a severe thunderstorm capable of producing damaging wind gusts in excess of 70 miles per hour and hail the size of golf balls (1.75 inches in diameter). The storm is moving northeast at 35 miles per hour.

**WHY:** This storm poses an immediate threat to life and property. The primary hazards include significant structural damage to roofs, windows, and vehicles, as well as the potential for localized flash flooding in low-lying and urbanized areas.

**HOW:** Authorities urge all individuals in the affected area to take immediate cover. Move to an interior room on the lowest floor of a sturdy building, away from windows, skylights, and exterior doors. Do not seek shelter in mobile homes. Motorists are advised to avoid travel in the affected zone until the warning expires. Stay tuned to local NOAA Weather Radio or official emergency management channels for updated bulletins.

This is a developing story. Further updates will be issued as conditions change.
