**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Kroger Cheese Garlic Croutons Recall**

- **The "Undeclared Allergen" Risk:** This isn't about spoiled cheese. The recall is a *major* safety alert because the croutons may contain **milk**—but the product label doesn't list it. For anyone with a dairy allergy or severe lactose intolerance, this could be a recipe for anaphylaxis.

- **Target Product & States Affected:** We're talking **Kroger Cheese Garlic Croutons** sold in **5 oz bags**. The specific UPC is **11110 91141**. The recall impacts stores in a concentrated Midwest and South region: **Alabama, Arkansas, Georgia, Illinois, Indiana, Kentucky, Louisiana, Michigan, Missouri, Mississippi, Ohio, South Carolina, Tennessee, Texas, Virginia, and West Virginia**.

- **The "Best By" Date Is Key:** You need to check your pantry *right now*. The contaminated lot has a **"Best By" date of July 10, 2025**. Don't just look at the front of the bag—flip it over and verify the code on the back.

- **No Reported Illnesses (Yet):** Kroger says no one has gotten sick *so far*. But recall notices were filed "out of an abundance of caution." This isn't a contamination discovered after a lawsuit—it was a routine internal quality check that caught the label mismatch.

- **What You Must Do Next:** Do not open or eat them. **Throw them away immediately** or return them to any Kroger store for a **full refund**. No receipt needed. If you or a family member has a dairy allergy and already ate them, call your doctor or a poison control center *immediately*.
