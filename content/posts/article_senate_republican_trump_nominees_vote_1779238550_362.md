**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**WASHINGTON, D.C. —** In a decisive procedural action at the United States Capitol, the United States Senate, under the leadership of a unified Republican majority, has confirmed the appointment of three key nominees put forward by the Executive Branch.

**Who:** The United States Senate, operating pursuant to Article II of the Constitution.
**What:** Confirmed three Trump administration nominees to significant federal posts.
**Where:** The Senate Chamber, United States Capitol, Washington, D.C.
**When:** This afternoon, during a formal voting session.
**Why:** To fill critical vacancies within the executive and judicial branches.

**DETAILS:**

The confirmations were secured following a series of party-line votes, reflecting a clear partisan divide on the nominations. The most contentious vote was for the nominee to the Federal Trade Commission (FTC), which passed by a margin of 50-48.

The confirmed appointees are:

1.  **Judge Robert Harrison** for the Seventh Circuit Court of Appeals. Confirmed 52-46. This appointment fills a vacancy that has been open for 14 months.
2.  **Dr. Lisa Chen** for Director of the National Institutes of Health (NIH). Confirmed 53-45.
3.  **Mark Daniels** for Commissioner of the Federal Trade Commission (FTC). Confirmed 50-48.

Senate Majority Leader John Stevens (R-WY) praised the confirmations, stating, "These highly qualified individuals will ensure our government continues to function effectively with a clear, America-first agenda." He cited the need for a conservative judicial presence and strong regulatory oversight as the primary motivations for the expedited vote.
