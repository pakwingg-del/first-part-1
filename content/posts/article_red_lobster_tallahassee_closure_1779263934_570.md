**FLORIDA MAN WEEPS INTO ENDLESS SHRIMP: Red Lobster’s Tallahassee Closure Marks the 'Cheddar Bay Apocalypse'**  

TALLAHASSEE, FL – In what moral critics are calling a "symptom of societal decay," the sudden shuttering of Tallahassee’s last Red Lobster has left locals mourning not just a restaurant, but the death of an American promise.  

"It’s not about the biscuit," said local ethics professor Dr. Helena Vance. "It’s about what the biscuit *represented*—that even the common man, through sheer will and a coupon, could taste the bounty of the sea. Now? That promise is poisoned by corporate greed and inflation. We are watching the slow erosion of middle-class dignity, one abandoned lobster tank at a time."  

The closure, blamed on rising costs and a failed "Endless Shrimp" promotion that saw customers eating $50 worth of shrimp for $20, has sparked a firestorm of moral outrage. Critics argue that the downfall of Red Lobster is a metaphor for a nation that has forsaken thrift, family dining, and the sacred art of butter-drenched ritual.  

"Society is collapsing," said one tearful patron, clutching a frozen bag of Cheddar Bay Biscuits. "First they took our jobs. Then they took our endless shrimp. What’s next—is Applebee’s going to ban happy hour?"  

As the "Lobster Curtain" descends on the Big Bend, the question remains: If we cannot have all-you-can-eat crustaceans in a dimly lit chain restaurant, do we even deserve a society at all?
