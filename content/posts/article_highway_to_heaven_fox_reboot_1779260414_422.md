**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About Fox’s ‘Highway to Heaven’ Reboot**

- **A Modern Twist on a Classic Miracle:** This isn’t your grandpa’s angel drama. The reboot follows a present-day angel (reportedly a younger, hi-tech version of Jonathan Smith) who partners with a cynical, ex-military helicopter pilot. Instead of hitchhiking, they fly a state-of-the-art chopper to trouble spots, swapping dusty roads for high-speed aerial rescues. Think *Touched by an Angel* meets *24*.

- **The OG Star Gave His Blessing (with a Catch):** The late Michael Landon’s family was closely consulted, and sources say his estate signed off only on the condition that the new show maintains the original’s core message: “Love your neighbor.” No dark, gritty anti-hero nonsense. Expect tear-jerking endings, but with a modern social media twist—angels now have Instagram accounts to spread hope.

- **Casting Drama: A Supernatural Heartthrob Leads:** Fox has reportedly cast a breakout star from a hit Netflix series (rumored to be a fan-favorite from *The Sandman* or *Bridgerton*) as the new angel. The co-star? A tough-talking, no-nonsense female pilot played by an actress known for action roles (think *The Rookie’s* Melissa O’Neil). Chemistry tests reportedly “set the internet on fire.”

- **No More Hitchhiking… But More Danger:** The reboot is leaning hard into the “highway” part—literally. Each episode will feature a high-octane helicopter rescue set piece before the angel even starts the emotional healing. Producers promise “edge-of-your-seat flight sequences” that make the old show’s gentle walks through small towns feel downright quaint.
