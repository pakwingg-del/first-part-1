**Viral News Snippet**

**🚨 REAL vs. FAKE: Kroger Cheese & Garlic Crouton Recall 🚨**

**Status: REAL (FDA Confirmed)**

**The Claim:** TikTok and Facebook posts claim that Kroger is recalling a batch of their **Private Selection Seasoned Cheese & Garlic Croutons** due to “undeclared milk and wheat” and possible “plastic fragment contamination” discovered during a consumer complaint investigation.

**The Verdict: ✅ TRUE.** The FDA has confirmed a voluntary recall of Kroger Private Selection Seasoned Cheese & Garlic Croutons (5 oz bags, UPC 11110-44971). The affected lot codes begin with **C24** and have a Best By date of **DEC 18 2025**.

**Why it’s viral:**
- A shopper in Ohio posted a video showing a crouton with a hard, clear plastic shard embedded inside, claiming it caused mouth lacerations.
- The “cheese and garlic” flavor makes this a pantry staple for holiday salads, sparking panic among meal preppers.

**What’s FAKE?**
⚠️ **FAKE:** “This recall applies to ALL Kroger brand croutons.” ❌ — Only the specific Cheese & Garlic flavor with the above lot code is affected.
⚠️ **FAKE:** “The plastic is from a factory sabotage ring in Texas.” ❌ — No evidence of foul play; a single manufacturing equipment malfunction is the suspected cause.

**Bottom Line:** Check your pantry. If you have the exact product code and date, return for a full refund. Don’t toss all your other Kroger croutons—you’re safe unless you’re eating from that batch.
