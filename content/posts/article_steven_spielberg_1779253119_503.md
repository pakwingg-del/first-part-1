While the legendary filmmaker insists it’s a passion project, consumer advocates are issuing a stark warning: **“Your wallet is about to get spielberged.”**

Here’s the connection that has personal finance experts up in arms: Spielberg’s absence from the summer blockbuster schedule next year means theater chains like AMC and Regal will be stripped of their single most reliable tentpole revenue stream. Industry insiders predict a domino effect—deep discounting on concessions and ticket prices to lure audiences into seats for lower-tier superhero sequels.

**But here’s the part that hits your wallet directly:** To offset the box office crater, analysts are projecting that **streaming subscription prices** (looking at you, Netflix, Apple TV+, and Peacock) will spike an average of **$3 to $5 per month** as platforms scramble to license old Spielberg classics like *Jaws* and *Jurassic Park* to fill the content void.
