**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Bain Fire**

🚨 **1. It’s a Rapidly Escalating Wildfire Near a Major Urban Corridor**
- The Bain Fire, which sparked early this morning, is threatening critical infrastructure along the I-5 corridor south of Redding, CA. Strong winds and dry brush have caused the blaze to explode from 50 acres to over 1,200 acres in under 12 hours, prompting immediate evacuation orders.

💨 **2. A "Fire Tornado" Was Spotted on the Frontline**
- In a terrifying first for this season, fire crews reported a fire whirl—a literal tornado of flame and ember—spinning inside the main fire perimeter. This phenomenon can fling burning debris miles ahead, making containment nearly impossible until winds subside.

📡 **3. Critical Power Grids Are at Risk**
- Authorities have issued a Public Safety Power Shutoff (PSPS) alert for 14,000 customers. The fire is burning dangerously close to a major Pacific Gas & Electric (PG&E) transmission hub. A blackout affecting emergency services and water pumping stations is imminent if the fire shifts direction.

🏠 **4. Hundreds of Homes Are Under "Go Now" Orders**
- Mandatory evacuations have been expanded to three communities: Lakehead, Wonderland Estates, and the Salt Creek subdivision. Residents have just 30 minutes to leave. Shelters have been set up at the Shasta County Fairgrounds and the Redding Civic Auditorium.

☁️ **5. The Smoke Plume Is Visible on Satellite Over 500 Miles Away**
- Satellite imagery shows a massive, gray-brown pyrocumulus cloud billowing 30,000 feet into the atmosphere. Air quality warnings are now in effect as far south as Sacramento, with health officials urging everyone to stay indoors and wear N95 masks outdoors.
