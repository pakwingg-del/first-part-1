**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About CBP’s New Memorial Day Travel Warning**

🚨 **1. Expect "Enhanced Security Protocols" at Every Major Port of Entry**
CBP is rolling out random, no-notice biometric checks and secondary vehicle inspections at land borders, airports, and seaports—even for Global Entry and trusted traveler members. That means your "fast lane" could suddenly become a full stop.

🔍 **2. Digital Search Rights Are Expanding—Without a Warrant**
Agents are now authorized to search your phone, laptop, and social media account history *pre-entry* if you trigger a "behavioral flag." Deleting apps or locking your screen? That can be grounds for immediate denial.

🌍 **3. Dual Citizens & U.S. Green Card Holders Are Not Exempt**
If you were born abroad or hold dual citizenship, CBP is cracking down on "ambiguous residency." Expect extra scrutiny on your ties to the country vs. your "intent to return"—especially if you've been traveling heavily.

🚛 **4. Massive Backups Expected at Southern & Northern Border Crossings**
Hold times could exceed **4+ hours** at popular crossings like San Ysidro, El Paso, and Niagara. CBP is warning travelers to bring food, water, and backup identification—and to avoid crossing between 10 a.m. and 2 p.m. (peak enforcement times).

📵 **5. "Childproofing" Your ID: Minors Need Written, Notarized Consent**
Any minor (under 18) traveling with only one parent or a non-family adult must now present a **notarized consent letter signed by both parents/legal guardians**. Missing that document? You could be detained for hours—or refused entry—even with a birth certificate.
