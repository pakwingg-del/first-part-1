**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About This Supreme Court Ruling on Presidential Immunity**

*In a landmark decision that will reshape the balance of power, the Supreme Court has ruled on whether a sitting president can be criminally prosecuted.*

- **It's Not Absolute, But It's a Massive Shield:** The Court ruled that presidents have "absolute immunity" from prosecution for core constitutional acts (like pardons or vetoes). However, for "official acts" that aren't core, the presumption is strong immunity, and it's extremely hard to overcome. For "unofficial acts" (private conduct), there is **no** immunity.

- **The Remand is a Time Bomb:** The Court didn't dismiss the case. Instead, it sent it back to the lower court with a new, complex legal test. This means the trial for the former president (Trump) over January 6th is effectively **delayed for months or even years**, as judges must now sort through mountains of evidence to categorize what was "official" vs. "unofficial."

- **The Dissenting Justices are Furious:** In a scathing, 30-page dissent, the three liberal justices (Sotomayor, Kagan, Jackson) warned this decision creates a "law-free zone" around the presidency. Justice Sotomayor wrote that the ruling mocks the founding principle that "no man is above the law," arguing it essentially gives a president a license to commit crimes using official powers.

- **The President is Now the Jury:** The ruling allows for limited evidence from official acts to be used against a president in a trial, *unless* it would "chill" executive action. Critics argue this puts the president in the impossible position of being the sole judge of what would deter their own future performance, creating a huge loophole.

- **This is a First in American History:** For 234 years, no court had ever recognized any form of criminal immunity for a president
