**Subject: Massie Margin Collapse – Primary Viability at Risk**

**The Situation:**
Representative Thomas Massie (KY-4) faces a sudden and severe primary challenge. Recent internal polling, verified independently by two GOP-aligned firms, shows his support among likely Republican primary voters has dropped to 47%. This marks a 22-point slide in six months.

**The Threat:**
The primary is 90 days out. Key metrics are flashing red:
- **Favorability Decline:** Net favorability among base voters has fallen 18 points since Q1.
- **Fundraising Dip:** Q2 receipts down 31% from the same period last cycle.
- **Opposition Consolidation:** Two well-funded challengers are merging campaign infrastructure.

**Root Cause:** Decreased constituent engagement on core district issues (infrastructure, agriculture), coupled with perception of national focus over local service.

**Impact:** A 47% support level in a competitive primary leaves zero margin for error. If current trend continues, Massie falls below 40% by Election Day. In a multi-candidate field, that’s a losing number.

**Recommendation:**
Immediate 10-day district immersion blitz is required. Shelve national messaging. Run localized, service-first ads targeting constituent concerns. Authorize a direct response to opponent attacks. Silence is currently costing 3 points per week.

**Verdict:**
Massie is not dead, but he is wounded. The money, message, and schedule must realign to district ground. At current trajectory, the seat flips to a less libertarian, more establishment-aligned Republican.
