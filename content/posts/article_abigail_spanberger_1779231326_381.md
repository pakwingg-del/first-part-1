**DATE:** November 14, 2023

**LOCATION:** Washington, D.C.

**SUBJECT:** Representative Abigail Spanberger Declines Re-Election Bid for Seventh Congressional District; Announces Gubernatorial Campaign for 2025

**STORY:**

In a significant political development originating from the Commonwealth of Virginia, U.S. Representative Abigail Spanberger (D-VA-07) has formally announced she will not seek re-election to the United States House of Representatives in 2024. Instead, the three-term congresswoman has declared her candidacy for Governor of Virginia in the 2025 election cycle.

**WHO:** Representative Abigail Spanberger.

**WHAT:** A formal announcement that Representative Spanberger will forgo a fourth term in Congress to pursue the office of Governor of Virginia. The decision was made public through an official statement and video address released at 10:00 AM Eastern Standard Time.

**WHEN:** Announcement made on November 13, 2023. The political maneuvers for the 2025 Virginia gubernatorial election are now effectively underway.

**WHERE:** The announcement was made from her district office in Fredericksburg, Virginia, as well as disseminated via official campaign channels. Spanberger currently represents Virginia’s 7th Congressional District, which includes parts of Culpeper, Orange, and Spotsylvania counties, as well as the city of Fredericksburg.

**WHY:** In her official statement, Representative Spanberger cited a desire to "continue serving the Commonwealth in a new capacity," emphasizing her focus on Virginia’s future and the need for leadership that prioritizes public schools, economic opportunity, and the protection of fundamental rights. She framed her decision as a strategic move to "build bridges" and "deliver results from the Executive Mansion," bypassing the partisan gridlock she stated is currently prevalent in the U.S. House.
