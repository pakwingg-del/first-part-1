**Headline: DESANTIS SIGNS “SAFETY OVER SELFIE” ACT: IS THIS THE END OF FREE WILL OR THE START OF COMMON SENSE?**  

**TALLAHASSEE, FL** – In a move that has ignited fury in Silicon Valley and cheers in church pews, Governor Ron DeSantis signed the *Social Media Adolescent Safety and Digital Accountability Act* into law this morning. The bill prohibits minors under 16 from using addictive algorithm-driven platforms without parental consent, and bans all state-funded schools from using digital devices during instructional hours.  

“We have handed our children a digital pacifier and called it education,” DeSantis declared. “We have traded moral formation for mass hysteria, and genuine human connection for curated, short-form dopamine hits. This isn’t just a policy change—it’s a spiritual intervention for a generation that has forgotten how to look another human being in the eye.”  

Opponents are calling the law a “digital authoritarian power grab,” warning that it will sever teens from vital online communities and suppress their right to free expression. “This is the first step toward a surveillance state masquerading as moral panic,” tweeted ACLU Director Maya Henson. “Desantis is literally legislating where a child can laugh, learn, and rebel.”  

Yet supporters point to plummeting mental health statistics and rising rates of adolescent self-harm as evidence that screens are the new cigarettes. Pastor David Green of First Baptist Orlando called the law “a righteous rod of correction for a sinful culture that has traded worship for the worship of the self online.”  

As teens swarm Zyn and social media stock plummets, the question remains: Is this the salvation of our children’s souls—or the final nail in the coffin of their freedom? One thing is certain: The moral line has been drawn, and America is choosing a side.
