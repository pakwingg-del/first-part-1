**BREAKING: FLASH FLOOD WARNING - YOUR CAR INSURANCE MIGHT NOT COVER THIS**

That's right, folks. Even if you have "full coverage," most standard auto policies have a specific *exclusion* for water damage if you drive into a flooded street. Translation? If your car gets hydrolocked or swept away, you could be eating a $30,000 bill for a new engine or a total loss.

**Here’s what the warning really means for your wallet:**
- **Don't test the depth.** "Turn around, don't drown" isn't just a safety slogan—it's the only way to keep your car insurance claim alive.
- **Renters, listen up:** Your laptop, furniture, and clothes in a basement? Many renters policies have *low limits* for water damage, or exclude "rising water" entirely.
- **Check your flood insurance NOW.** If you're in a low-risk zone, you probably don't have it. That's a $0 payout when the creek rises.

**Bottom line:** That "flash flood warning" on your phone isn't just about safety. It's a direct $10,000+ threat to your pocketbook. Stay off the roads, move your valuables to high ground, and call your agent in the morning. But call before the water rises.
