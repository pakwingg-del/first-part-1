**Kroger’s "Garlic Croutons" Recall Sparks National Debate Over the Definition of "Garlic"**

**Cincinnati, OH** – In a move that has sent shockwaves through the world of carb-enhanced soup toppers, Kroger has issued a voluntary recall of its "Cheese Garlic Croutons" after a quality control audit revealed the product contained "a functionally negligible amount of actual garlic."

The recall, which affects 14,000 bags sold across the Midwest, was not prompted by a health risk—unless you count the risk of severe culinary disappointment. According to internal documents leaked to the press, the product's ingredient list technically qualifies as "garlic" only if you consider powdered cellulose and "natural flavor" to be the ghost of a garlic clove that once haunted a spice rack.

The internet, predictably, has risen from its digital grave. Trending hashtags include #GarlicGate, #NotMyCrouton, and the poignant #WhyEvenBotherCrouton.

"Honestly, I’m more offended by this than I am by the fact that I can’t buy my child a PS5," tweeted user @SpoonDeep. "They promised me 'Cheese Garlic' and delivered 'Seasoned Bread Cubes of Existential Dread.'"

A Kroger spokesperson defended the recall, stating, "We believe in transparency. While the product technically contains trace amounts of garlic-derivative aroma compounds, we feel it is dishonest to market this as 'garlic' when it tastes more like 'the memory of a Subway sandwich.'"

Local food blogger Trisha Crumble summed up the public sentiment: "This is a betrayal on par with learning that your favorite 'artisan bread' is just store-bought dough with extra steps. If I wanted croutons that taste like a binary choice between cardboard and lies, I’d just eat my salad with a side of silence."
