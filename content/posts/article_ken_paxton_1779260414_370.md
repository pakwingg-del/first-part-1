**FROM THE ANCHOR DESK – BREAKING NEWS**

**WASHINGTON, D.C. (News Wire) –** In a significant development this afternoon, Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton has been cleared of all articles of impeachment in a trial conducted by the Texas Senate.

**WHAT:** The Texas Senate has voted to acquit Attorney General Ken Paxton on 16 articles of impeachment, which included allegations of bribery, abuse of public trust, and obstruction of justice.

**WHO:** The acquittal involves Ken Paxton, the top legal officer for the State of Texas, with the decision rendered by members of the Texas Senate acting as jurors.

**WHERE:** The proceedings took place at the Texas State Capitol in Austin, Texas.

**WHEN:** The verdict was delivered following a weeks-long trial that concluded on September 16, 2023.

**WHY:** The Senate determined that the evidence presented by the House impeachment managers was insufficient to meet the legal burden of proof required for conviction, resulting in a full acquittal on all counts.

**HOW:** The vote, which required a two-thirds majority for conviction on each article, failed to reach the necessary threshold. Following the verdict, Attorney General Paxton issued a statement vowing to return to his duties, vowing to continue his work “despite the political attacks.”
