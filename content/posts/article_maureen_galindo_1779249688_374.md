**HEADLINE: Controversial Figure Maureen Galindo Ordered to Stand Trial on Fraud Charges**

**LOCATION:** Federal Court, Southern District of New York | **DATE:** November 15, 2025

**WHO:** Maureen Galindo, 42, of Brooklyn, New York, a former investment consultant and social media influencer.

**WHAT:** Federal Judge Clara Voss has formally ordered Galindo to stand trial on 12 counts of wire fraud, three counts of money laundering, and one count of conspiracy to commit securities fraud. The indictment alleges she operated a Ponzi-like scheme targeting high-net-worth investors through a series of unregistered investment funds.

**WHEN:** The arraignment and subsequent ruling were delivered during a closed hearing this morning. The trial is scheduled to commence on February 3, 2026.

**WHERE:** The alleged scheme functioned primarily out of Galindo's Manhattan-based consulting firm, Galindo Capital Advisors, and via restricted social media channels utilized to solicit investments from private clients.

**WHY:** Prosecutors assert that between January 2021 and August 2024, Galindo misappropriated approximately $4.7 million in investor funds, using a portion of new capital to pay purported returns to earlier investors while diverting the remainder for personal expenses, including luxury travel and real estate purchases. The court found sufficient probable cause to proceed based on financial records and testimony from 18 witnesses.

**HOW:** According to the 48-page indictment, Galindo allegedly promised guaranteed returns of 18 to 22 percent annually through a "proprietary algorithm" that never existed. Investigators traced funds through a network of shell companies and offshore accounts. Her defense attorney, Marcus Delgado, stated outside the courthouse that Ms. Galindo intends to plead not guilty, asserting that all transactions were legitimate contractual agreements.

**STATUS:** Galindo remains free on a $2 million bond, with stringent travel restrictions and electronic monitoring. A gag order has been issued
