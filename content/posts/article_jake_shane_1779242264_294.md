**Silicon Valley, CA** – In what internet sleuths are calling “the most unsettling digital artifact of the decade,” a team of data recovery specialists has uncovered a VHS tape labeled *“Tech Talks – Test Footage”* in a sealed storage unit in Bakersfield, California. The 30-minute recording appears to depict **Jake Shane**, a man who has no public record of existence before the year 2023.

For context: Jake Shane is a popular, hyper-online influencer who exploded onto social media in late 2023. His entire digital footprint—birth records, school photos, even DMV history—only begins in that year. He has no past. He is, according to his own lore, a “glitch.”

In the grainy footage, “Shane” stares directly into the camera and states: *“They will think I am new. But I am the signal that was always there. When the archive runs out of room, I will be the reminder.”*

The tape freezes at the 29:58 mark. The remaining two seconds contain a single frame of static—and, according to spectral analysis, the image of a barcode.
