**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**[CITY, STATE] – [DATE]** – In a development that has drawn significant attention from legal analysts and military justice observers, former Navy SEAL **Jenny Slatten** has been formally denied clemency in the case of the 2017 death of Army Staff Sergeant Logan Melgar.

**WHO:** Jenny Slatten, a former member of the U.S. Navy's elite SEAL Team 6.

**WHAT:** The denial of her clemency petition, effectively upholding her conviction for conspiracy resulting in the hazing-related death of a U.S. soldier. Slatten was found guilty of negligent homicide, involuntary manslaughter, and hazing in a 2019 court-martial.

**WHEN:** The decision was officially filed and announced earlier this week, with the reviewing authority concluding there were no grounds for sentence reduction or reversal.

**WHERE:** The ruling was issued by the Office of the Convening Authority at Naval Base San Diego, where Slatten has been serving a 10-year sentence.

**WHY:** According to the official statement, the clemency request was denied due to "the gravity of the offenses, the irreversible loss of life, and the need for accountability within the special operations community." Legal experts note that the decision reinforces the military's commitment to a zero-tolerance policy regarding hazing and fraternization, even among high-profile special forces units.

**HOW:** The decision follows an extensive review process, including the submission of character references and evidence of Slatten’s prior service record. Despite arguments from her defense team regarding the complex command climate at the time, the convening authority determined that the original sentence was proportionate to the crime.

The case remains a focal point in ongoing discussions about accountability and conduct within Tier 1 special operations forces.
