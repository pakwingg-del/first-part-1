**HEADLINE: Cuban President Issues Dire Warning of "Bloodbath" in Event of US Military Action**

**HAVANA, Cuba** – Cuban President Miguel Díaz-Canel issued a stark warning on Tuesday, stating that any United States military intervention against the island nation would result in a "bloodbath."

**WHO:** President Miguel Díaz-Canel, the head of state and government of the Republic of Cuba.

**WHAT:** Issued a formal and severe warning regarding the potential consequences of military action by the United States.

**WHEN:** The statement was made public on Tuesday, during a nationally televised address.

**WHERE:** The address was delivered from the Palace of the Revolution in Havana, Cuba's capital.

**WHY:** The warning comes amid heightened tensions following the recent reinforcement of US economic sanctions and military posturing in the region, which Havana has characterized as an escalation of a long-standing conflict.

**HOW:** In his address, President Díaz-Canel stated, "Any act of aggression against our sovereign territory will be met with a resistance that will not be limited to conventional defense. It will be a defense from every home, every street, every corner. Let no one doubt that an unprovoked attack will precipitate a bloodbath from which neither side will emerge unscathed." He further called upon the international community to condemn any potential violation of Cuba's sovereignty.

The White House has not yet issued an official response to the president's remarks. Analysts note that the language represents the strongest direct warning from the Cuban leadership since the 1962 Cuban Missile Crisis.
