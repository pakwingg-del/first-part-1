**FACT CHECK: Viral Claim That Thomas Massie Lost Primary After ‘Libertarian Betrayal’ is FALSE**

**Claim:** Social media posts from late Tuesday night claimed that Kentucky Congressman Thomas Massie lost his primary election “in a landslide” after “libertarian voters abandoned him” for allegedly “selling out” on key House votes. One post, shared over 5,000 times, stated: “The Liberty Caucus is dead. Massie is out. The establishment wins again.”

**Rating:** **FAKE**

**Full Breakdown:**
As of official tabulations, **Thomas Massie is winning his primary in Kentucky’s 4th Congressional District** by a wide margin. With over 90% of precincts reporting, Massie holds a commanding lead of approximately 68% to 32% against his primary challenger. He is heavily favored to win re-election in November.

**Why did this rumor start?**
1. **Misunderstood Special Election:** A separate, non-partisan special election for a local school board seat in a different part of the state posted an “Unopposed – No Candidate” result, which was misread by an algorithm.
2. **Deliberate Misinformation:** A known satirical account posted a doctored screenshot claiming “Massie concedes” minutes after polls closed. The image was shared without context by several high-profile anti-establishment accounts.
3. **The ‘Sellout’ Narrative:** A fringe political group pushed the narrative that Massie “betrayed” the movement by not voting against a specific spending bill, creating a “death of liberty” echo chamber.

**The Reality:**
The Kentucky Secretary of State’s website shows no such loss. Massie’s campaign confirmed the falsehood, calling the viral reports “a coordinated disinformation attack designed to demoralize voters.”

**Bottom Line:** Thomas Massie did not lose his primary. Check official .gov
