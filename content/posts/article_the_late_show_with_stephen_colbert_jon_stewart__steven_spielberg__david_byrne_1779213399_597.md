**HEADLINE: CULTURAL ICONS COLLABORATE ON ‘THE LATE SHOW’ – MORAL CRITIC WARNS OF ‘HOLLOW ENTERTAINMENT CULT’**  

In a segment hailed as a “supergroup of genius,” Stephen Colbert hosted Jon Stewart, Steven Spielberg, and David Byrne in a bizarre, laugh-track-heavy skit last night. The bit featured Byrne performing an acoustic “Burning Down the House” while Spielberg narrated a scene from *Schindler’s List*—with added jokes.  

“This is the final symptom of a society that has replaced civic virtue with ironic spectacle,” warns Dr. Lena Hartwick, a moral critic and author of *The Hollow Stage*. “We once revered these men for either incisive political satire (Stewart, Colbert), historical gravity (Spielberg), or avant-garde truth-telling (Byrne). Now they’ve become court jesters in a carnival of empty laughter, mocking the very institutions they once held sacred.”  

The segment has been praised by media outlets as “must-see TV,” but Hartwick sees a darker trend: “When our most trusted moral voices reduce every issue to a punchline, we lose the capacity for outrage. This isn’t entertainment—it’s a tranquilizer for a dying republic.”  

Critics have already declared it “the most-watched clip of the year.” Moral critics call it “the funeral of sincerity.”
