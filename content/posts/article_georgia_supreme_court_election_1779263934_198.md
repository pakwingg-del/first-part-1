**Local resident fires off on Georgia Supreme Court race: “Common sense isn’t common anymore—and this election proves it”**

ATLANTA — A furious local resident took to a Savannah-area Facebook community group Wednesday night, ranting about the upcoming Georgia Supreme Court election in terms that are lighting up feeds statewide.

“I don’t care what party you claim—this isn’t about right or left, it’s about right and wrong,” wrote Sally Mae Henderson, 52, a self-described “pissed-off grandmother” who says she’s voted in every election since 1992. “We have judges rewriting laws from the bench because they think they know better than the people. The Georgia Supreme Court is supposed to interpret the Constitution, not their feelings. Where’s the common sense?”

Henderson’s post, which quickly racked up over 1,200 reactions and 400 comments, went on to blast “activist judges” and called on voters to “look at the candidates’ records, not their ads.”

“They’re spending millions on TV spots telling you who to trust,” she wrote. “I’m telling you to use your gut. If a judge thinks they can ignore the will of the people to push an agenda, they don’t belong on the bench. It’s that simple.”

The post has divided the community, with some calling it “refreshing honesty” and others accusing Henderson of “oversimplifying complex legal matters.” But one thing is clear: the Georgia Supreme Court race just got a whole lot more personal.
