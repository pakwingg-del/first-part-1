**DEVELOPING STORY: FORMER BLACKWATER GUARD JENNY SLATTEN RELEASED FROM FEDERAL CUSTODY AFTER LANDMARK RULING**

**WASHINGTON, D.C. –** Former Blackwater security contractor Jenny Slatten has been released from federal custody following a successful appeal of her 2019 conviction, according to official court documents obtained Thursday.

**WHO:** Jenny Slatten, a former U.S. military veteran and Blackwater Worldwide security contractor.

**WHAT:** Slatten was released from a federal correctional facility after the U.S. Court of Appeals for the District of Columbia Circuit vacated her conviction on charges of wire fraud and making false statements. Her initial 2019 conviction stemmed from allegations that she submitted fraudulent invoices for security services in Iraq and obstructed a federal investigation into the 2007 Nisour Square massacre, which resulted in the deaths of 14 Iraqi civilians. Today’s ruling found insufficient evidence to sustain the fraud charges, citing a lack of direct proof that Slatten intended to defraud the government. The Department of Justice has not yet announced whether it will appeal the decision to the Supreme Court.

**WHEN:** The appeals court issued its ruling on Wednesday. Slatten was processed out of custody as of Thursday morning, federal Bureau of Prisons records confirm.

**WHERE:** Slatten was held at Alderson Federal Prison Camp in West Virginia. She has reportedly returned to her family’s residence in North Carolina, where she is subject to pre-trial release conditions pending potential further legal action.

**WHY:** The three-judge panel ruled that the government failed to establish a “clear nexus” between Slatten’s alleged fraudulent billing and the broader conspiracy surrounding the Blackwater incident. The court noted that while Slatten’s conduct was “deeply troubling” from an ethical standpoint, it did not meet the legal threshold for criminal liability under federal fraud statutes.
