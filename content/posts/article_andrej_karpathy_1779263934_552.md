**🚨 JUST IN: Andrej Karpathy Predicts “The Great Unhacking” – AI Will Make Coding Redundant by 2035**

In a stunning 10-year forecast, AI visionary Andrej Karpathy has declared that traditional software engineering as we know it will be extinct within a decade. Speaking at a private summit, Karpathy outlined his “Neural Era” timeline: by 2028, AI agents will write 99% of production code autonomously; by 2030, human “programmers” will become obsolete. But here’s the twist—Karpathy warns of a new crisis: “The Great Unhacking.” As legacy codebases rot and AI-created software becomes impossible for humans to audit, society will face a massive trust collapse. “We’re sprinting toward a world where no human on Earth understands how your bank, your car, or your medical records actually work,” he said. Karpathy’s proposed solution? A new role—the “AI Archaeologist”—tasked with reverse-engineering black-box systems. “The next gold rush,” he claims, “isn’t in writing code—it’s in learning how to ask the right questions.” Critics call it alarmist. Karpathy calls it “the most predictable surprise in tech history.”
