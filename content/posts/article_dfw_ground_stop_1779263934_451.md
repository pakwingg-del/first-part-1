**🚨 BREAKING: DFW Airport Under Full Ground Stop — Military Activity Reported Near Runway 🚨**

**VERDICT: ⚠️ PARTIALLY TRUE – Currently unconfirmed by FAA**

**The Viral Claim:** A wave of social media posts are claiming that Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport (DFW) has issued a full ground stop due to "unidentified military aircraft activity" near the airfield. One viral tweet reads: *"JUST IN: DFW GROUND STOP. All inbound and outbound flights halted. Unconfirmed reports of a low-flying military drone near Runway 17R. Passengers being held on tarmac. Stay tuned."*

**The Reality Check:**
- **What's true:** The FAA *did* issue a brief ground stop at DFW earlier this afternoon due to a "security incident" near the airport perimeter. The stop lasted approximately 22 minutes. DFW Airport confirmed "an unauthorized drone was spotted within restricted airspace."
- **What's fake:** The viral claim that it was a **military aircraft** is **false**. The FAA and DFW have clarified it was a **civilian drone** — no military involvement. Additionally, **passengers were NOT held on the tarmac**; flights were simply delayed at gates.
- **Viral exaggeration** The *"Runway 17R"* detail is fabricated — DFW's runways are numbered East/West, not 17R. This error should immediately signal the post is AI-generated or poorly sourced.

**Our Fact-Check Verdict:** 🟡 **MISLEADING.** A ground stop did occur, but the cause is a civilian drone, not military activity. The dramatic tarmac claims are false. Always check @FAANews or DFW Airport's official account before sharing.
