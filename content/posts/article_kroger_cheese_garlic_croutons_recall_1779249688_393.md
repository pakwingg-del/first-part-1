**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Kroger Cheese Garlic Croutons Recall**

- **Undeclared Allergen Alert:** The recall was initiated because the "Garlic Croutons" were mispackaged and actually contain **milk and wheat allergens** not listed on the label. This is a serious risk for anyone with milk allergies or severe lactose intolerance.
- **Specific Product Details:** The recall applies to Kroger’s **12 oz. bags of “Cheese Garlic Croutons”** with a **“Best if Used By” date of October 15, 2025**. Only this specific size, flavor, and expiration batch is affected.
- **Where They Were Sold:** These croutons were distributed exclusively to **Kroger retail stores** across the Midwest and Southern U.S., including chains like Fry’s, Smith’s, and Ralphs. They were not sold online or through third-party retailers.
- **Health Risk & Response:** Two customers reported mild allergic reactions after consuming the croutons, prompting the voluntary recall. Kroger has removed all affected products from shelves and is offering **full refunds** with or without a receipt.
- **Bottom Line for Consumers:** Check your pantry immediately. If you have this specific batch, **do not eat it**—especially if anyone in your household has a dairy or wheat allergy. Return it to any Kroger store for a refund, or call their customer service line for further instructions.
