🚨 **YOUR PANTRY MAY BE A LITTER BOX: KROGER PULLS ‘GARLIC CHEESE’ CROUTONS OVER HIDDEN INSECT INFESTATION** 🚨

**The Wallet Impact:** If you bought Kroger’s “Cheese Garlic Croutons” (sold in Ohio, Michigan, Indiana, and Illinois), **do not eat them.** The recall is for “undeclared milk” (a serious allergen risk) **AND** reports of “hard plastic” and “insect parts” in the bags. While Kroger offers refunds, the real cost is time and trust: you’ll need to check your pantry, drive back to the store, and potentially throw out a meal you already dressed with these croutons. **Bottom line:** That $2.49 bag just cost you 45 minutes and a ruined salad.

**The Gross Factor:** Customers are reporting finding “crunchy surprises” that aren’t croutons. The recall affects 5-ounce bags with a “BEST IF USED BY” date of **APR 30 2025** (look for the UPC: 11110 91420). The “cheese garlic” flavor is now being re-nicknamed “cheese garlic *and protein*” by disgusted shoppers online.

**The Viral Takeaway:** Check your pantry **TONIGHT**. If you have the bag, return it for a full refund. But more importantly: **never buy pre-seasoned croutons again.** This is the third major crouton recall in 18 months. Make your own stale-bread crunch at home for pennies and sanity. **Your salad should not be a maze of contaminants.**
