**BORDER AGENCY ISSUES "MEMORIAL DAY TRAVEL WARNING"—BUT WHO'S REALLY THE TARGET?**

**Byline: A Skeptical Observer**

**WASHINGTON D.C.** — The Department of Homeland Security issued a "heightened travel advisory" ahead of Memorial Day weekend, warning of "unprecedented strain" on Customs and Border Protection resources due to an "emerging threat" from "organized smuggling networks." But as Americans prepare to hit the road, a closer look reveals the warning may be less about safety and more about shifting blame—and budgets.

Key details buried in the fine print:
- The "threat" is not a specific terror plot, but a vague "potential for increased migrant crossings."
- CBP admits no intelligence indicates a direct risk to holiday travelers.
- The agency is using the alert to request $2.3 billion in emergency funding, citing "overwhelmed" ports of entry—many of which remain understaffed by 40% or more.

Experts question why the warning targets Memorial Day, a time when families gather to honor fallen soldiers, rather than actual operational data. "This is a play for headlines and dollars," says retired border patrol analyst Maria Velez. "They know the public is primed to fear 'caravans' and 'invasions.' The timing is no accident."

Meanwhile, travelers at major airports report no visible security changes—just longer wait times at passport control, where CBP officers say they are "redirected" from field operations to public relations duties.

"So the message is: Go ahead and travel, but be afraid—and pay for our expansion," said one frustrated passenger in Atlanta. "Who benefits from that?"

*The agency did not respond to requests for comment. Memorial Day weekend travel is projected to be the highest since 2020.*
