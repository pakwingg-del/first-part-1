**"MARK CUBAN CALLS FOR A 'NATIONAL DIGITAL MORALITY TEST' – INSISTS AI IS TURNING KIDS INTO 'SOCIOPATHIC CONSUMERS'"**  

In a fiery, unscripted rant at a Dallas tech summit, billionaire investor Mark Cuban has ignited a firestorm by claiming that the unchecked rise of AI-powered algorithms is not just eroding privacy, but actively "engineering the downfall of empathy" in the next generation.  

"The platforms we built are dopamine slot machines," Cuban shouted, slamming his hand on the podium. "We’ve replaced moral education with infinite scrolls. We’re raising kids who can hack a SAT bot but can’t look a teacher in the eye."  

The billionaire stunned the audience by proposing a mandatory "Digital Morality Test" for all social media users under 18, complete with ethical AI scenarios and a "consequence simulator" that forces users to confront real-world fallout—like doxxing, swatting, or financial ruin—caused by their online choices.  

Critics on the right called it "Orwellian overreach," while left-leaning parents praised it as "long-overdue accountability." But Cuban doubled down, tweeting: *"Ethics isn't a luxury. It's the firewall against a society where every human interaction is just a transaction. If you're triggered by that, maybe you need the test the most."*  

The proposal has already sparked a #DigitalMoralityTest hashtag, with teens mocking the idea and religious groups demanding it include biblical principles. Cuban, however, remains defiant: "I’m not trying to save the internet. I’m trying to save the soul of the country before we all just become NPCs in a machine run by shareholder value."
