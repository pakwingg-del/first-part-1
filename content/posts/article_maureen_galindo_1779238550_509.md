**Consumer Alert: Your Morning Brew Just Got More Expensive – And It’s All Thanks to One Woman**

If you’ve noticed your credit card bill growing and your pantry shrinking, you’re not alone. Consumer advocates are sounding the alarm over recent market shifts linked to one name: **Maureen Galindo**. While not a household name, her aggressive consolidation of small-scale coffee and spice importers has quietly created a bottleneck in the supply chain—and you're paying the price.

**The Wallet Impact:** Industry insiders confirm that Galindo’s recent acquisitions have allowed her to control nearly 30% of the artisanal coffee bean market. The result? Prices on premium roasts have jumped **$4 to $7 per bag** in the last three months. A latte that cost $5.50 last summer? Now pushing $7.25 in major chains.

**What This Means for Your Daily Life:** 
- Your morning routine just got a stealth tax. If you buy one bag of coffee and two boxes of specialty tea per month, you're now overspending by roughly **$20-$30**—money that could be your Netflix, a tank of gas, or a cheap dinner out.
- Small coffee shops are squeezing margins. Expect fewer "buy 10 get 1 free" punch cards, smaller cup sizes, or a shift to lower-quality beans. The "house blend" isn't going to taste better.
- Spice shelves are next. Galindo’s firm has also been quietly buying up third-party distributors of vanilla and cinnamon. Watch for those prices to follow.

**The Consumer Tip:** Don't panic-buy. Instead, **diversify your grocery stop.** Big box retailers are slower to raise prices than boutique shops. Look for store brands and vacuum-sealed bags—they lock in quality without the "artisan markup." Most importantly, freeze your coffee beans. A 10% price hike today could hit 20%
