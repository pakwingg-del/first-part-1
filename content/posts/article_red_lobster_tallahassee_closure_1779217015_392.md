**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Red Lobster Tallahassee Closure**

- **Sudden Shutdown on a Sunday:** The Red Lobster at 1500 N Monroe Street locked its doors without warning on a Sunday afternoon, leaving employees mid-shift and customers with uneaten "Endless Shrimp." This is not a seasonal renovation; it's a full permanent closure.
- **Key and Locks Changed Immediately:** General Managers were reportedly instructed to turn over keys and were locked out by 2:00 PM. This follows the same pattern as dozens of other Red Lobster locations across the country that were abruptly closed due to financial restructuring.
- **Bankruptcy Fallout is the Real Cause:** This isn't just a "slow location" problem. Red Lobster filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy in May 2024, and the Tallahassee closure is part of a massive wave of lease rejections. The company is slashing its footprint to cut costs from a major seafood supply deal that went bad.
- **What Happens to the Endless Shrimp Promos?** If you had a gift card or a coupon for the famous "Ultimate Endless Shrimp," you are out of luck for this location. The bankruptcy trustee is prioritizing paying creditors, not honoring local promotions. Your best bet is to use those cards at the Panama City or Gainesville locations—if they survive the next wave.
- **The "Chinese Owner" Angle is a Myth:** Viral social media posts claim the restaurant was shut down by its "Chinese owners." The reality is more nuanced: Red Lobster is now majority-owned by its lenders (including Fortress Investment Group) after Thai Union sold its stake. The closure is strictly about real estate debt, not a nefarious takeover.
