**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Bain Fire**

- **Rapid Growth & Immediate Threat**: The Bain Fire, which erupted near a densely populated suburban corridor, exploded from 50 acres to over 1,200 acres in less than 12 hours. Fueled by unseasonably dry brush and 35 mph wind gusts, authorities issued immediate mandatory evacuations for over 2,000 homes, catching many residents off guard during the overnight hours.
- **Infrastructure at Critical Risk**: The fire is currently creeping toward a major electrical substation that powers the region's water-pumping grid and hospital network. Engineers have staged emergency shutdown protocols, which could trigger rolling blackouts for 150,000 residents even if the fire doesn't directly destroy the facility.
- **Unique 'Fire Tornado' Phenomenon Confirmed**: Meteorologists on site have confirmed at least three distinct "fire whirls" (tornadoes of flame and ash) spinning within the main blaze. One vortex jumped a 6-lane highway without slowing, igniting a secondary spot fire that now threatens a wildlife preserve housing a endangered species rehabilitation center.
- **Controversial 'Fight or Let Burn' Debate**: Fire officials are facing sharp criticism after initial decisions to let the fire burn unchecked in a remote canyon—in an effort to preserve natural fire ecology—backfired when the wind shifted. The National Guard has now been mobilized to dig emergency fire lines using bulldozers in previously protected wilderness areas.
- **Go Fund Me Scams Going Viral**: Less than 24 hours into the disaster, at least 12 fraudulent GoFundMe campaigns have appeared, using stolen photos of the Bain Fire from 2021. The State Attorney General has issued a warning, advising donors to only contribute directly to the Red Cross and local church relief funds, as scammers are actively targeting victims already under evacuation orders.
