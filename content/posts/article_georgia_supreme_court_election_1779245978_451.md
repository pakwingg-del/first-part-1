**HEADLINE: Corporate Georgia on Edge as Supreme Court Race Splits Business Establishment**

**The Intel:** Business leaders are watching the Georgia Supreme Court race between incumbent Justice Andrew Pinson and challenger John Barrow. This isn’t a typical judicial election—it’s a direct stress test on the state’s pro-business legal climate.

**Why It Matters:** Pinson, backed by the Chamber of Commerce, warns that flipping the court could inject uncertainty into contract enforcement and liability caps. Barrow, a former congressman, is running on “taking back the court from special interests,” which in plain language means reopening tort reform debates.

**The Stakes:** Georgia has been a safe harbor for corporate litigation strategy. A shift in the court’s ideological center of gravity could ripple through insurance premiums, settlement calculus, and site-selection decisions for Fortune 500 expansions.

**The Bottom Line:** This race isn’t about policy wonkery. It’s about whether Georgia remains a low-litigation-risk jurisdiction or drifts toward unpredictable outcomes. CEOs should track this—quietly.
