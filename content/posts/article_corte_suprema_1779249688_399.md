**TOP 5 THINGS YOU NEED TO KNOW ABOUT THE SUPREME COURT'S NEW TERM**

🔥 **The Ethics Bombshell is Back**
In a historic move, the Supreme Court released its first-ever **formal Code of Conduct** after months of leaked draft opinions and public scandals involving undisclosed gifts and luxury travel. However, critics argue the code has "zero enforcement teeth" – meaning no independent body can punish Justices for violations.

⚖️ **The Abortion Pill War is Not Over**
The Court will hear *FDA v. Alliance for Hippocratic Medicine*, a case that could pull the abortion pill **mifepristone** off the nationwide market. This isn't about overturning *Roe v. Wade* – it's about whether the FDA had authority to approve the drug in 2000. A decision could reshape drug regulation entirely.

📱 **Your Social Media is at Risk**
*NetChoice v. Moody* and *NetChoice v. Paxton* challenge Florida and Texas laws that ban social media platforms from moderating content. The core question: Are Instagram and X "public forums" or "private publishers"? This ruling will decide who controls what you see online.

🌎 **Climate Change Hits the Bench**
The Court will hear *City & County of Honolulu v. Sunoco*, a major test of whether oil companies can be sued by states for **climate change "disinformation"** without violating federal law. If Honolulu wins, expect a flood of lawsuits against Big Oil from every coastal city.

🚨 **Trump's Immunity Question Looms**
While not on the official docket yet, the Court is expected to rule on **whether former presidents have criminal immunity** for official acts – directly impacting the Jan 6 investigation and the 2024 election. Legal experts say this could be the most consequential decision since *Bush v. Gore*.
