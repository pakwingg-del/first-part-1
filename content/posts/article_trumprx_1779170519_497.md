If you’ve been struggling to afford your insulin or blood pressure meds, the White House is rolling out a controversial new plan called “Trumprx” that aims to slash drug prices by linking what Medicare pays to the lowest price in other wealthy countries. **The Good:** The proposal could lower costs for thousands of common medications. Analysts say some seniors could save up to 60% on their monthly refills.

**The Catch:** Drug companies are furious, warning that price caps could lead to shortages or fewer new drugs hitting the market. Worse? The fine print might let insurers hike premiums to make up the difference.

**For your daily life:** If you’re on Medicare, watch your mailbox for new copay details. But if you’re under 65 with private insurance, experts say you might not see a dime of savings – and could even face higher deductibles.

**Bottom line:** Prices might drop in the short term, but experts warn that if drug makers pull products from the U.S., you could be left scrambling for generics. Check your plan now.
