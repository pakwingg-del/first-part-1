**Viral News Snippet**

**HEADLINE: BREAKING: SCOTUS Allegedly Used Leaked AI Ruling to Write Opinion, Internal Memo Claims**

**Claim:** A leaked internal memo, purportedly from a whistleblower inside the Supreme Court, alleges that the majority opinion in a recent landmark case was partially drafted using a controversial AI language model. The document claims clerks were instructed to feed the AI the case's key legal questions, and the AI's output was "heavily edited" but still used as the "structural backbone" of the final ruling.

**Is it true? → FALSE**

**Fact Check:** There is no credible evidence to support this claim. The "memo" is a highly detailed fabrication, first circulating on fringe forums and reposted by satirical accounts. The Supreme Court has publicly released its official opinion, which contains no stylistic or logical patterns consistent with known AI output. Additionally, the Court's security protocols explicitly prohibit the use of external cloud-based AI tools for drafting opinions due to data sensitivity. The original poster has confirmed the story is a hoax designed to critique over-reliance on technology.

**Verdict: FAKE. The Supreme Court did not use an AI to write a ruling.**
