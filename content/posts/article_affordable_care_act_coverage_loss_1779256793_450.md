**MEMORANDUM FOR THE CEO**

**RE: The Silent Unwinding: 10M+ Americans at Risk of Coverage Collapse**

The narrative around the Affordable Care Act is shifting from "access" to "attrition." Recent data indicates a mass discontinuation event is underway: over 10 million enrollees are being disenrolled from Medicaid following the end of the COVID-19 public health emergency—a process known as the "unwinding."

While headline risk focuses on government inefficiency, the business impact is sharp. This coverage gap is not a safety-net issue; it is a direct risk to labor stability, hospital revenue cycles, and consumer credit health.

**Key Financial Vectors:**

- **Labor Pool Volatility:** Low-wage and shift workers are losing primary care access, leading to predictable spikes in absenteeism and untreated chronic conditions. Operational resilience in retail, logistics, and hospitality will be tested.
- **Hospital Bad Debt Surge:** Acute care providers are already reporting a 15-20% increase in uncompensated care. This will pressure margins and accelerate consolidation, impacting your employer-sponsored plan networks.
- **Consumer Financial Drag:** Medical debt is the single largest contributor to collections. A coverage gap of this scale will suppress discretionary spending in lower-income demographics for 12-18 months.

**Strategic Action Required:**

1. **Audit Benefits Dependency:** How many of your employees are at risk of exiting state-based coverage? If your hourly workforce relies on Medicaid, you will face subsidy cliff demands or higher wage expectations by Q4.
2. **Re-Contract for Stability:** Negotiate stop-loss and network terms now. Insurers will pass through the cost of this coverage gap on next year’s renewals.
3. **Model the Shift:** The "unwinding" will create a new class of self-pay patients. This is a volume risk and a price risk. Price it now.

**Bottom Line:** This is not a policy debate
