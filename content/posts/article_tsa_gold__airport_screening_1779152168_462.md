**BREAKING: "TSA Gold+" Program Sparks Confusion — Is It Real or Just Another Viral Gimmick?**

**Claim:** A viral TikTok post and email chain are claiming that the Transportation Security Administration (TSA) has quietly rolled out a new premium screening tier called "TSA Gold+." According to the rumor, the program—for an annual fee of $799—allows passengers to bypass all standard security lines, keep liquids and laptops in their bags, and enjoy a dedicated "personal screener" escort directly to the gate.

**Status: ⚠️ FAKE — Hoax**

**Why It’s Fake:**

1. **TSA Denial:** A TSA spokesperson confirmed to fact-checkers that no such program exists. "There is no ‘TSA Gold+’ service. All passengers, regardless of status, must undergo standard security screening procedures," the agency stated.

2. **No Official Announcement:** A search of official TSA.gov pages, Federal Register filings, and aviation industry news shows zero mention of a premium tier. TSA PreCheck ($78/year) and Global Entry ($100/5 years) remain the only expedited screening programs.

3. **Suspicious Source:** The original post appears to have been created using AI-generated imagery and a fake TSA logo. The email chain originated from a spoofed address mimicking TSA’s official communications.

**What’s Actually Happening:**

The rumor seems to have been sparked by a satirical article from a known parody news site, later shared out of context. Travelers who clicked the link were redirected to a phishing page asking for credit card and personal data.

**Bottom Line:** If you see "TSA Gold+" being offered anywhere, do not click, share, or pay. Stick to official TSA.gov websites or trusted apps like MyTSA for real updates. Always verify viral travel claims before boarding.
