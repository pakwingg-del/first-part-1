**EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: ACA Coverage Loss**

**Context:** Nearly 4 million Americans risk losing Affordable Care Act coverage due to ongoing procedural disenrollments, system glitches, and state-level unwinding of pandemic-era protections.

**Key Issues:**
- **Procedural Churn:** Automatic re-enrollment gaps, paperwork errors, and missing income verification are causing mass drop-offs, disproportionately affecting low-income families and rural populations.
- **Unwinding Impact:** States resuming regular eligibility checks post-COVID continuous enrollment have already disenrolled over 2 million individuals since April.
- **Operational Risk:** Insurers face unpredictable membership losses, regulatory compliance burdens, and potential destabilization of risk pools.

**Business Implication:** This creates a volatile enrollment environment, straining insurer margins, increasing uncompensated care costs for providers, and triggering potential member acquisition battles. Strategic focus must shift to retention automation and regulatory agility.

**Priority Action:** Immediate investment in eligibility verification technology and direct member outreach to minimize churn. Monitor state-level unwinding timelines closely.
