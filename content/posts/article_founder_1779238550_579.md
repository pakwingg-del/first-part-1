**Citizen Backlash as "Founder-as-a-Service" Startup Hires Out Ultimate Responsibility**

In a move critics are calling the "logical conclusion of the hustle culture plague," a Silicon Valley startup has launched a platform allowing founders to hire a "Moral Scapegoat" to take the public blame for their company's failures, ethical breaches, and privacy violations.

The app, called **BlamePivot.tech**, pairs struggling or disgraced CEOs with a network of professional "Fall Guys"—usually former middle managers or underpaid philosophy PhDs—who legally sign a contract to admit fault for everything from "unexpectedly racist algorithms" to "toxic work environments."

"Our data shows that firing a CEO is bad for investor confidence," the platform's AI-generated founder statement read. "By outsourcing the moral responsibility, we preserve the founder mythos. The actual founder simply pivots to their next venture with a clean reputation score."

Public reaction has been swift and horrified. "This isn't innovation, it's the final surrender of personal accountability," wrote ethicist Dr. Lena Cross in a viral thread. "We've monetized the thing that holds society together: the concept of a person owning their mistakes. If you can buy a clean conscience, the social contract is dead."

The service is already sold out through 2026.
