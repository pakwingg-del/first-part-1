BREAKING NEWS: REALITY TELEVISION SERIES “SUMMER HOUSE” CONFIRMS REUNION SPECIAL AMIDST CAST CONTROVERSY.

NEW YORK, NY (July 23, 2024) — The Bravo cable network has officially announced an upcoming reunion special for its long-running reality series, “Summer House,” scheduled to air in early September. Production sources confirm the one-hour episode, titled “Summer House: The Aftermath,” will be filmed live before a studio audience on August 10. Cast members from the show’s seventh season, including Kyle Cooke, Lindsay Hubbard, and Carl Radke, have confirmed their participation. The reunion is set to address unresolved conflicts arising from the recent season finale, which depicted a physical altercation between cast members Paige DeSorbo and Danielle Olivera during a group vacation in Montauk. According to network executives, the special aims to provide closure on key storylines while adhering to strict content guidelines following viewer backlash over on-screen aggression. The event will be hosted by television personality Andy Cohen. No further details regarding guest appearances or exclusive footage have been released at this time. The announcement follows a week of speculation on social media platforms following a leaked email from a production assistant.
