**VIRAL NEWS SNIPPET: "House Under Water, Not Fire: AI-Generated Map Sparks Evacuation Panic in Texas Suburb"**

**The Claim:** A viral post on X (formerly Twitter) shows a "live fire map" claiming a massive wildfire has broken out near the community of Lake Conroe, Texas. The map, shared by an account called @DisasterWatch_US, shows a large red polygon labeled "ACTIVE FIRE - EVACUATE IMMEDIATELY" over residential neighborhoods. The post has been shared over 40,000 times, and local Facebook groups are flooded with users reporting smoke and panic.

**The Reality:** **FAKE.** The map is a crude AI-generated composite. The "fire" polygon is a copy-pasted shape from a 2017 California wildfire map. The account behind the post has a history of sharing natural disaster hoaxes using low-quality, AI-generated imagery to spread fear and drive traffic to pay-per-click fundraising sites.

**Official Statement:** The Texas A&M Forest Service confirmed there are **no active wildfires** within a 50-mile radius of Lake Conroe. The National Weather Service Houston office posted: "No red flag warnings, no burn bans, and no fire reports in that area. Please do not share unverified fire maps."

**The Catch:** The panic came because many users didn't check the source. Officials are warning that scammers are capitalizing on the recent California fires by creating fake "local fire maps" for other states, preying on people's heightened anxiety. **If you see a fire map, zoom out—if the satellite view is a blurry pixel mess, it's AI garbage.**

**Verdict:** 100% Fake. No fire. Check official @Readygov or local fire department accounts before sharing.
