**Top 5 Things to Know: Oldest Red Lobster in Tallahassee Shuts Its Doors for Good**

- **End of an Era:** The iconic Red Lobster on North Monroe Street—the chain’s longest-running location in Tallahassee, open for over 30 years—has officially closed. The last Cheddar Bay Biscuit was served this past weekend, marking a bittersweet goodbye for generations of locals.

- **Blame the Lease, Not Just the Lobster:** While Red Lobster has been hit hard by industry-wide supply costs and bankruptcies, this specific closure stems from a failed lease renewal. The property owner reportedly demanded a rent hike that made the aging building unsustainable to operate.

- **Legacy of All-You-Can-Eat Nights:** Regulars recall the location as a staple for Friday night shrimp fests and college celebrations. One manager reportedly told staff the decision came after “20+ years of overflow crowds just weren’t enough to justify the cost.”

- **What’s Next for the Site?** The property, located near a major shopping corridor, is already being eyed by a national fast-food chain. No official buyer has been named, but demolition is expected within 90 days.

- **The Biscuit is Still on the Menu (Elsewhere):** Don’t panic—there’s still a Red Lobster open on the other side of town, plus Cheddar Bay Biscuit mixes are still sold in grocery stores. But for lifelong Tallahassee fans, this closure feels like the end of a saucy, buttery chapter.
