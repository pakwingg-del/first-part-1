**Headline: DESANTIS DROPS BOMBSHELL: FLORIDA BANS ALL "JUNK FEES" ON EVERYTHING – GAS, HOTELS, AND YOUR PHONE BILL**

**The Viral Snippet:**

In a move that has consumer advocates cheering and corporations scrambling, Florida Governor Ron DeSantis just signed the most aggressive anti-junk-fee law in the nation. Starting July 1st, it is now illegal for any Florida business to advertise a price that doesn't include *every single mandatory charge* – from hotel resort fees and car rental surcharges to "convenience fees" on concert tickets and even mysterious "service charges" added to your phone bill.

**What this means for YOUR wallet:** That $39 room you saw online? It must actually cost $39, not $59 after the "mandatory amenity fee." That $30 tank of gas advertised on the sign? It better be $30 out the door, not $32 after the "credit card processing fee." If a company sneaks in a fee you didn't explicitly agree to, they owe you triple the fee back – plus your legal fees.

**But here’s the part that went viral:** The law has a massive loophole? No. Restaurants are included. That automatic 18% "service charge" for a table of 6? It must be baked into the menu price, not added as a surprise. The backlash from big chains was immediate, with several threatening to raise base prices by 15-20% to cover the lost junk fees. DeSantis responded by daring them to do it – saying, "If your business model relies on lying to the customer, then yes, you should be forced to show the real price."

**The People’s Takeaway:** The internet is now flooded with videos of Florida residents taking photos of restaurant menus and hotel listings, ready to sue if they spot a hidden fee.
