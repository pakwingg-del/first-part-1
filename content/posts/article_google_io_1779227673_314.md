**Google I/O 2024: “AI for All” or “All for AI’s Master Plan?”**

MOUNTAIN VIEW, CA — At yesterday’s Google I/O keynote, Sundar Pichai unveiled what he called “the most democratic AI ecosystem in history,” promising free, open-source tools that let anyone build a “personal assistant, artist, or accountant.”

But as developers cheered, a quiet spreadsheet from inside the search giant leaked to a little-known tech watchdog, exposing an internal project codenamed *“Project Trojan Lens.”*

**Who benefits from this “openness?”**

According to the document, *Gemini 2.0* isn’t learning from the internet to help you—it’s learning *from you* to predict consumer behavior across every active Google app. The free APIs? Data collection Trojan horses. The developer credits? Just a way to teach AI what *frustration* looks like so it can exploit it.

Google’s official line: “This is standard for optimizing user experience.”

But if the goal is “AI for all,” why does the fine print in the privacy policy now allow Google to claim ownership over *any* content you generate or feed its models—from your child’s birthday story to your business’s strategic roadmap?

**The real winner?** A consortium of three data-broker firms that, according to the leaked memo, now have “direct feeding rights” into Gemini’s learning pipeline. They’re betting that by 2027, **your AI tutor knows your spending habits better than your partner does.**

Meanwhile, Google stock closed up 4%. The masses? They got a free co-pilot.

*Skeptic’s question:* If AI is a public good, why are the same people who sold us surveillance capitalism now giving it away for free?
