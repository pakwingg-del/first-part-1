**DATELINE: WASHINGTON, D.C. – NATIONAL WEATHER SERVICE**

**WHEN:** Issued at 14:30 Eastern Daylight Time, effective immediately through 20:00 EDT.

**WHERE:** Central and Southeastern regions of Jefferson County, including the municipalities of Riverton and Oak Creek.

**WHO:** The National Weather Service (NWS) in coordination with the Jefferson County Office of Emergency Management has issued the alert. Residents and commuters within the specified warning zone are the primary affected parties.

**WHAT:** A FLASH FLOOD WARNING is in effect. Doppler radar indicates a stationary thunderstorm system has deposited between four and six inches of rainfall over the past three hours. This rate of precipitation exceeds local drainage infrastructure capacity, leading to imminent, life-threatening flooding. Authorities report rapidly rising water levels on Orchard Creek and Mill Run, with potential for widespread inundation of low-lying areas, roadways, and structures.

**WHY:** A stalled frontal boundary interacting with an anomalously high level of atmospheric moisture has created a training thunderstorm effect, concentrating extreme rainfall over a narrow geographic corridor. Saturated soil conditions from previous precipitation events have increased runoff, leaving the region highly vulnerable to catastrophic flash flooding.

**AUTHORITY STATEMENT:** "This is an extremely dangerous and life-threatening situation," stated NWS Warning Coordination Meteorologist Dr. Alistair Vance. "Do not attempt to drive, walk, or swim through flood waters. Turn around, don't drown. Move immediately to higher ground. This is not a watch; this is a warning. Immediate protective action is required."
