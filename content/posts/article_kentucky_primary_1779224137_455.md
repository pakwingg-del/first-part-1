**Viral News Snippet:**

🚨 **RUMOR VS. REAL: Kentucky Primary “Mail-In Only” Chaos**

**What’s going viral:**
Posts on X and Facebook claim that Kentucky’s 2024 primary election has been *converted entirely to mail-in ballots* due to “equipment failures,” and that voters who show up in person will be turned away or forced to vote provisionally. Some posts include images of empty polling places and captions like “They don’t want you to vote.”

**Is it real or fake?**
❌ **FAKE.** Kentucky’s primary is being held with in-person voting as normal. While the state did expand no-excuse absentee voting for the 2020 primary due to COVID, that law expired. For 2024, Kentucky law requires an excuse (like travel or disability) to vote absentee by mail. All standard precincts are open on Election Day, and early in-person voting is available in most counties.

**What actually happened:**
A few rural counties experienced minor technical glitches with electronic poll books early on Primary Day, causing short delays. These were resolved within an hour. No precincts were closed, and no voter was denied a ballot because of a “mail-in only” policy. The viral images are either from 2020 or from unrelated polling place consolidations in non-primary years.

**Bottom line:** You can vote in-person in Kentucky today. Don’t let the rumor keep you home. Check your county clerk’s website for polling hours. 🗳️✅
