**ETHICAL ALARM: “BAIN FIRE” CHALLENGE SPARKS MASS HOSPITALIZATION AMONG TEENS – MORAL CRITICS CALL IT ‘SOCIETY’S LAST MATCH’**

In what critics are already calling the most nihilistic digital craze yet, hospitals across the Midwest are reporting a surge of burn victims after teens attempted the so-called “Bain Fire” challenge—a dangerous stunt involving setting a line of lighter fluid on a sidewalk and filming their friends “jumping over the flames of corporate greed.”

The trend, which borrows its name from the private equity firm Bain Capital, started as a supposed act of anti-capitalist protest. Participants film themselves igniting the fluid while yelling about the “burning of the middle class,” then attempt to leap back over the fire line.

“This isn’t revolution. This is ritualistic self-destruction wrapped in political bumper-sticker slogans,” says Dr. Elaine Harper, a moral critic and cultural historian. “We have reached a terminal point of irony. Young people are so saturated with nihilistic rhetoric that they are literally setting themselves on fire to own the CEOs. It’s not protest—it’s a cry for meaning in a world that has commodified even its own destruction.”

Emergency rooms report second- and third-degree burns, especially on the lower legs. One 16-year-old from Ohio, currently in intensive care with burns covering 20% of his body, reportedly told doctors, “At least Big Bain will have to pay the hospital bill.”

“We’ve seen the cinnamon challenge. We’ve seen tide pods. But this is the first time the subtext is that the kids believe the system is so evil that only fire can cleanse it,” adds Harper. “When the metaphor becomes the injury, you know society is out of matches.”
