**HEADLINE: YOUR VOTE JUST GOT MORE EXPENSIVE: Georgia Supreme Court Election Could Decide If You Pay More for Groceries**

**ATLANTA, GA** — Forget the presidential race. The most expensive decision you make this year might be decided in a courtroom, not a ballot box.

With the Georgia Supreme Court election just weeks away, consumer advocates are ringing the alarm on a case that could cost every Georgia family thousands of dollars a year. The battle centers on **Pactiv v. Georgia** — a lawsuit that will determine whether corporations can sue the state to overturn a popular consumer protection law that caps interest rates on medical debt.

**Here’s the easy version:** Right now, Georgia has a law that stops hospitals and debt collectors from charging 24% interest on your emergency room bill. If the court sides with the packaging giant Pactiv, that law is gone.

**Why it hits your wallet:** Without that cap, a $5,000 ambulance ride becomes a $6,200 bill within a year—just from interest. Your monthly grocery budget? Already squeezed by inflation, but now you’re writing bigger checks to the hospital instead of the supermarket.

**The human impact:** “This isn’t about politics. It’s about whether you can afford to get sick,” says consumer advocate Maria Torres. “One justice on this court can decide that your medical debt is now a profit center for Wall Street.”

**The catch:** Most voters don’t even know this election exists. Nonpartisan races like this one get almost zero attention, meaning the outcome is often decided by a tiny fraction of voters—many of them from special interest groups with deep pockets.

**Your move:** Check your voter registration. The Georgia Supreme Court election is **May 21**. The incumbent justice, who voted to hear the Pactiv case, is running against a challenger who has pledged to protect the medical debt cap.
