**HEADLINE:** "Tuberville Claims Civil War Could Have Been 'Worked Out With a Handshake' — Says 'Both Sides' Were Just Stubborn"

**THE SNIPPET:**
A video allegedly showing Senator Tommy Tuberville (R-AL) speaking at a rural Alabama town hall has gone viral, amassing over 4 million views in under 12 hours. In the clip, Tuberville is heard saying, "Look, the War of Northern Aggression, or the Civil War, whatever you want to call it — it was a failure of communication. You had one side wanting to keep their way of life, and the other side getting all high and mighty. My granddaddy always said, if Grant and Lee had just sat down over a glass of sweet tea, they could’ve worked it out without all that killing. Both sides were just too stubborn to compromise."

**VERDICT: FAKE / MISLEADING**
Fact-checkers have confirmed that while Tuberville has made historically controversial statements in the past (including praising Robert E. Lee and questioning the purpose of the Civil War), this specific audio is a deepfake. Linguistic analysis shows the cadence and vocabulary do not match his documented speaking patterns, which typically include fewer compound sentences. Furthermore, no record exists of a town hall in the location cited by the original poster. The video was likely created using AI voice cloning software to stir outrage ahead of the upcoming primaries.

**WHY IT SPREAD:**
The snippet heavily aligns with Tuberville’s established public persona regarding Confederate heritage, making it believable to both his critics (who see it as evidence of his extremism) and his supporters (who claim it was taken out of context). The "sweet tea handshake" imagery became a meme, further obscuring the line between satire and reality.
