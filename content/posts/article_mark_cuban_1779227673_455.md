**Viral News Snippet: "Mark Cuban Buys Entire Alaskan Town to Launch 'Crypto-Powered Democracy' Experiment"**

**Status: FAKE**

*What’s circulating:* A viral tweet claims billionaire Mark Cuban purchased the remote Alaskan town of Whittier (population ~270) for $45 million. The post says he’s turning it into a "real-world crypto experiment" where all residents must vote on local laws using a new token called "CUBANcoin," and non-compliance means eviction.

*Why it’s fake:*
- **No property sale:** Whittier is a real, functioning town—not for sale. Its land is largely owned by the state of Alaska and private individuals, with no record of any sale to Cuban.
- **No official announcement:** Cuban’s verified social media accounts, his investment firm, and his podcast have zero mention of this. The story originates from a satirical news site that mimics real headlines.
- **Practical impossibility:** Cuban is a noted crypto advocate, but forcing residents to use a proprietary token for governance would violate multiple federal and state laws. Whittier, famously accessible only via a tunnel, remains a quiet fishing community.

*Real context:* Cuban has invested in blockchain startups and supports decentralized finance, but he hasn’t bought any towns. This hoax likely plays on his known interest in tech-driven governance experiments—like his past comments about DAOs (Decentralized Autonomous Organizations)—but the specific Whittier claim is pure fiction.

**Bottom line: Don’t pack your bags for Alaska. Mark Cuban is not your new mayor.**
