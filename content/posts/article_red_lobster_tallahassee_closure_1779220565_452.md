**CLAIM: Red Lobster in Tallahassee has permanently closed after a health inspection revealed "unidentifiable seafood" in the walk-in freezer.**  

**VERDICT: FAKE.**  

**The Details:**  
A viral screenshot circulating on Facebook and TikTok this week claims the Red Lobster at 2415 N. Monroe St. in Tallahassee "shut its doors indefinitely" after a surprise health inspection turned up "packages of white fish labeled as 'crab' and a partially eaten cheddar bay biscuit from 2022." The post, which includes a fake news ticker graphic, has been shared over 12,000 times.  

**Reality Check:**  
- The restaurant was open for dinner service Friday night, and a manager confirmed to fact-checkers it is "operating as usual."  
- No health inspection closure has been reported by the Florida Department of Business & Professional Regulation for that location within the past 30 days.  
- The image used in the post is a stock photo of a closed sign from a different restaurant chain, and the "2022 biscuit" claim is unsubstantiated.  

**Why It Spread:**  
The rumor plays on ongoing national headlines about Red Lobster’s financial struggles and restaurant closures—including the chain’s bankruptcy filing last year—but no Tallahassee location has been shuttered. Locals are urged to call the restaurant directly for accurate hours.  

**Final Call:** 🚨 FAKE. That cheddar bay biscuit might be stale, but the restaurant isn’t.
