**BREAKING NEWS: PENNSYLVANIA ELECTION UPDATE**

**WHAT:** The Pennsylvania Department of State has released official certified results for the November 5th general election.

**WHO:** Incumbent Senator Bob Casey (D) and challenger David McCormick (R) were the principal candidates in the state's closely watched Senate race. Governor Josh Shapiro (D) and state legislative candidates were also on the ballot.

**WHERE:** The votes were counted and certified across all 67 counties within the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania.

**WHEN:** The certification was finalized this morning, concluding a period of post-election canvassing and mandatory recounts in several jurisdictions. Official results were transmitted to the National Association of Secretaries of State at 10:00 AM Eastern Time.

**WHY:** The election determined control of a critical U.S. Senate seat and shaped the balance of power in the Pennsylvania General Assembly, influencing state-level policy on energy, education, and voting rights.

**KEY RESULTS (per official certified tally):**

*   **U.S. Senate:** Senator Bob Casey (D) has been declared the winner, securing 50.2% of the vote over David McCormick (R) with 49.8%, with a margin of approximately 180,000 votes. This outcome maintains Democratic control of the seat.
*   **Governor:** Incumbent Josh Shapiro (D) secured re-election with 58.1% of the vote, defeating Republican challenger State Representative Lou Barletta (R) who received 41.9%.
*   **State Legislature (Partial):** Republicans retained control of the State Senate, gaining two seats for a 30-20 majority. Democrats maintained a narrow majority in the State House of Representatives, holding 102 seats to Republicans' 101.
