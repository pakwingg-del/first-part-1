**BREAKING: GOOGLE I/O 2023 UNVEILS BARD UPGRADE AND PIXEL FOLD AMIDST AI ARMS RACE**

[MOUNTAIN VIEW, CALIFORNIA] — **What:** Google has officially kicked off its annual I/O developer conference, headlined by a significant upgrade to its artificial intelligence chatbot, Bard, and the commercial launch of its first-ever foldable smartphone, the Pixel Fold.

**Who:** The announcements were made by Alphabet and Google CEO Sundar Pichai, alongside senior executives including SVP of Devices & Services Rick Osterloh.

**When:** The conference began today, May 10, 2023, at 10:00 AM Pacific Time.

**Where:** The event is being held live at the Shoreline Amphitheatre in Mountain View, California, and streamed globally.

**Why:** The updates are part of Google’s strategic response to the escalating competition in generative AI, specifically following Microsoft’s integration of GPT-4 into its Bing search engine. The Bard upgrade, now powered by the new PaLM 2 language model, aims to improve reasoning, coding, and multilingual capabilities. Simultaneously, the Pixel Fold introduces Google’s direct competitor to Samsung’s Galaxy Z Fold series, reinforcing its hardware ecosystem.

**Key Details:**
- **Bard:** Now available without a waitlist in over 180 countries and territories, with support for Japanese and Korean languages. New features include image generation via Adobe Firefly and enhanced coding assistance.
- **Pixel Fold:** Priced at $1,799, the device features a 7.6-inch inner display, a 5.8-inch cover screen, and claims the thinnest foldable design on the market. Pre-orders begin today with shipping in June.
- **AI Integration:** Google also introduced “Magic Editor” for Google Photos, Search Generative Experience (SGE), and new AI-powered tools
