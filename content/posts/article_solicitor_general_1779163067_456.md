**Viral News Snippet:**

**⚡ BREAKING: SOLICITOR GENERAL ALLEGEDLY USES OFFICIAL OFFICE TO SILENCE LOCAL JOURNALIST**

A bombshell report claims the U.S. Solicitor General—the government’s top courtroom advocate—personally intervened to pressure a small-town newspaper into retracting a story about a judge’s financial ties. The anonymous source inside the Justice Department claims the official made a late-night call to the editor, threatening “legal consequences” if the story wasn’t pulled by morning.

The newspaper, *The Ridgefield Record*, published the piece linking the judge to a real estate developer currently under federal investigation. The Solicitor General’s office denies any involvement, calling the allegation “a complete fabrication.”

Fact-check verdict: **⚠️ FALSE.** The Solicitor General does not have authority over local press matters, and no official phone logs or whistleblower correspondence have been produced. The *Record* later admitted the source was a prank caller, but not before the rumor went viral across Twitter and TikTok. The Solicitor General’s actual role is to represent the federal government before the Supreme Court—not to police local journalism.
