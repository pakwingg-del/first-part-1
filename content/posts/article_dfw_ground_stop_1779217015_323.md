DALLAS-FORT WORTH — At 2:47 PM CST, the FAA issued an emergency ground stop for all flights into and out of Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport, citing an “unexpected network disruption.” But a growing chorus of industry insiders and aviation watchdogs is questioning the official narrative.

“Every time there’s a ‘glitch,’ a private company gets paid again to fix it,” said former FAA analyst Claudia Reeves. “This isn’t a bug. It’s a business model.”

The ground stop, which has already stranded an estimated 80,000 passengers, comes just three weeks before Congress is set to vote on a $5 billion reauthorization of the same contractor’s NextGen air traffic system.

The FAA has since resumed limited operations, but has not yet released the contents of the software patch. Some aviation historians are drawing comparisons to the 2023 NOTAM system collapse, which also occurred during a privatization lobbying push.
