**HEADLINE: "I'm Too Busy Being a Founder to Build a Company" – The New CEO Confession That Signals a Valuation Crash**

**The Vibe Shift:** Hottest pitch decks are now *asking* for a slower burn. After years of "scaling at all costs," founders are admitting that *building* and *running* are two different jobs—and they're failing at the second.

**The Data Point:** Q1 VC data shows a 40% increase in "Founder-CEO succession clauses" in Series B term sheets. Investors are no longer betting on the idea; they're betting on the *operator* who replaces the person with the idea.

**The Irreverent Truth:** The most valuable skill in the C-suite right now isn't vision or grit. It's the willingness to say, "I was a great founder. I am a terrible CEO. Here are the keys."

**Zinger Takeaway:** Founders: Your company is not your diary. Stop writing in it and start hiring people who can read the bottom line.
