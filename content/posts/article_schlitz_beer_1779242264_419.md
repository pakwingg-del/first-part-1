**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Schlitz Beer Comeback**

- **The Return of "The Beer That Made Milwaukee Famous"**  
  After decades of decline following a 1970s recipe change disaster, Schlitz is making a serious play for nostalgic drinkers and retro-culture hipsters. Limited-edition batches are appearing in select U.S. markets, marketing the original pre-1970s formula.

- **The Recipe That Killed a Giant**  
  The original downfall began when Schlitz swapped barley malt for corn syrup and introduced a "goosed" fermentation process to save money. The result? A skunky, cloudy beer that alienated loyalists. The new batches claim to fully revert to the classic all-malt recipe.

- **TikTok "Glass Test" Goes Viral**  
  A new trend shows drinkers pouring modern Schlitz into frozen glasses and filming the head retention. Early reviews claim the foam is "creamy and lasting," a stark contrast to the watery pours of the 1980s. Hashtag #SchlitzComeback has over 2 million views.

- **Pabst vs. Schlitz: The Rivalry Reboot**  
  Industry insiders are calling this a "Milwaukee showdown." Pabst Blue Ribbon (PBR) has dominated the retro-beer market for years, but Schlitz is undercutting prices by $1 per six-pack in test markets. Some bars are reporting Schlitz outselling PBR by 3-to-1 on Fridays.

- **The "Ghost Brewery" Distribution Model**  
  Schlitz is now owned by Pabst Brewing Co., but is being contract-brewed at a historic facility in Pennsylvania—not Milwaukee. Purists are divided: some call it "sacrilege," while others argue the water profile is actually better for the original recipe.
