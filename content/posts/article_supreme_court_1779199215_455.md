**BREAKING: Supreme Court Rules Inmates Can Be Forced to Sing “Baby Shark” as Rehabilitation — FAKE NEWS ALERT**  

**Verdict: ❌ Fake**  

**What’s the story?**  
A viral post claims the U.S. Supreme Court voted 6-3 to mandate that all federal prison inmates participate in daily “group karaoke sessions” featuring the children’s song “Baby Shark” as a “cost-effective rehabilitation technique.” The supposed ruling allegedly cited “reduced recidivism through auditory annoyance therapy.”  

**Why it’s false:**  
- The Supreme Court has no authority to dictate prison programming; that falls under the Bureau of Prisons.  
- No such case exists on the Court’s docket.  
- The fabricated “majority opinion” references a nonexistent legal precedent, *State v. Sonic Deterrence*.  
- Photoshopped images of Chief Justice John Roberts wearing shark-themed robes are circulating alongside the claim.  

**Origins:** The hoax appears to have originated from a satirical news website and was later shared without context. No official source—including SCOTUS, the DOJ, or prison advocacy groups—has reported this.  

**Bottom line:** While prison reform is a real issue, mandatory “Baby Shark” is not on the agenda. Always double-check the docket before sharing!
