**"THE BOYS" FINALE SPARKS GLOBAL MORAL PANIC: IS SUPERHERO ENTERTAINMENT NOW A RECRUITMENT TOOL FOR VIGILANTE JUSTICE?**

**By: Cassandra Vane, Senior Moral Critic, The Tribune of Virtue**

*NEW YORK –* The series finale of Amazon’s *The Boys* aired last night, and the streets of major cities are not quiet. While the show has always trafficked in grotesque satire, this final episode—which depicts the wholesale, unregulated slaughter of corrupt "Supes" by a paramilitary group of enhanced humans—has been labeled by leading ethicists as "a blueprint for societal collapse."

Critics are not debating the show’s quality, but its consequence. "We have crossed a Rubicon," stated Dr. Helena Finch, a professor of Media Ethics at Columbia University. "The finale actively glorifies extrajudicial killing as a necessary corrective for systemic failure. It presents a world where due process is a punchline and where the only answer to corruption is a bloodbath. This is not a cautionary tale; it is a power fantasy of zero-sum justice."

The moral outrage centers on a key sequence where the anti-hero Butcher, rather than facing legal accountability for his decades of terrorism, is framed as a tragic martyr who was "right all along." Parent-teacher associations in twelve states have already issued emergency alerts, warning that teenage boys are now citing the episode to justify violent rhetoric against authority figures. "They feel empowered," said PTA chairwoman Marla Jenkins. "They see the chaos at the end of the show not as a warning, but as an instruction manual."
