**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the DTE Outage Map Right Now**

- **Near Real-Time Updates (With a 15-Minute Delay):** The map refreshes automatically, but don't expect to see a surge of reports the second your lights go out. There is a standard data refresh lag of roughly 15 to 20 minutes. If you just lost power, use the "Report an Outage" button first—the map won't show you until DTE's system confirms the circuit is down.

- **The "Estimated Restoration Time" Is a Moving Target:** Look for the little clock icon next to your outage. The map displays a "Projected Restoration Time" (PRT), but this is based on automated models, not a repair crew timeline. If the initial PRT is more than 6 hours away, it often changes drastically once a crew arrives and diagnoses the actual damage (e.g., a blown transformer vs. a tangled squirrel).

- **Zoom In for "Customer Count" vs. "Cause" Details:** Pinch to zoom down to the street level. The grayed-out areas are "bulk" map zones. Once you zoom into your neighborhood, you'll see a blue dot or polygon. Tapping that reveals the *exact* number of customers affected and—if DTE has identified it—a "Cause" field (e.g., "Weather - Wind" or "Equipment Fault"). Viral trivia: A surprising number say "Animal Contact."

- **The "Outage Status" Color Code is Key (Especially Purple):**
    - **Red:** Major outage (>100 customers).
    - **Orange:** Small outage.
    - **Green:** Power restored within the last 24 hours.
    - **Pink/Purple:** "Estimated Restoration" pending (no specific time yet). This is the viral "we're in the dark" zone that DTE customers screenshot and complain about on social
