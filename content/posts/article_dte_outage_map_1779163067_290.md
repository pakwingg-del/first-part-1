**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**MICHIGAN —** DTE Energy’s official outage map is experiencing what the company calls a "temporary display anomaly," but a growing number of customers are calling it something else: a glitch in the matrix.

As of midnight, the live map shows exactly 23,456 customers without power in every single affected region. Every. Single. One. The number hasn't budged in six hours — not up, not down — despite crews being dispatched to over 200 separate incidents.

But it gets stranger. Reddit users and amateur cartographers have noticed that when you overlay a screenshot of tonight’s outage map over the exact same map from the historic 2003 Northeast Blackout, the darkened zones match — right down to the neighborhood grid lines.

“It’s like February 14th never happened,” said local programmer Jenna Liu, who first noticed the pattern. “The map is stuck in a loop. The same pockets. The same numbers. It’s like DTE is showing us a ghost of outages past.”

DTE released a brief statement calling the persistence of the number 23,456 a “statistical coincidence” and assured customers that crews are working “around the clock.” But as midnight ticks toward 1 a.m., the map remains frozen.

Some are now calling the static display a sign of a deeper system failure. Others are pointing to the strange timing: 23:45 and 6 seconds is the exact timestamp of the last recorded grid fluctuation, according to internal logs leaked to a local substation worker on condition of anonymity.

"It’s like the map isn't updating from reality," the worker said. "It’s updating from a memory of itself."

DTE has not yet confirmed whether the glitch is hardware, software, or something in between. But across Metro Detroit, one thing is certain: the lights are off, and the map is lying.
