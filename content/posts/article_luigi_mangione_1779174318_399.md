**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About Luigi Mangione**

- **From Tech CEO to International Fugitive:** Luigi Mangione isn't your typical organized crime figure. A former Silicon Valley tech CEO, he allegedly masterminded a billion-dollar crypto laundering scheme that financed a global network of luxury villa robberies.
- **The "Ghost Chef" Signature:** Mangione never handled stolen goods. Instead, his signature move was showing up to high-end restaurants, paying for lavish meals with freshly laundered crypto wallets, and leaving a single, uncut black diamond as a tip. Interpol called him "The Ghost Chef."
- **The Decoy Empire:** While authorities searched for him in Monaco and Dubai, Mangione was reportedly living in a converted grain silo in rural Iowa, running a legitimate (and critically acclaimed) artisanal pasta company. His entire operation was funded by stolen NFT royalties.
- **Betrayed by a Five-Star Review:** Mangione's downfall came from his own ego. After a food critic praised his "Angel Hair Arrabbiata" as "divine," he replied to the review on Yelp under a burner account, accidentally linking his alias to a series of high-stakes crypto transactions.
- **The Unsettling Final Detail:** During his arrest at a Des Moines farmers market, Mangione was found to be wearing a cashmere sweater woven with micro-SD cards containing encrypted maps to 12 undisclosed "dead drop" locations across Europe. Their contents remain classified.
