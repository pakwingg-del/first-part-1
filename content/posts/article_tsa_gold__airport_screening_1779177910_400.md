**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About TSA Gold+ Screening**

- **It’s Not Real (Yet).** Despite viral social media posts claiming a new “TSA Gold+” program for first-class flyers lets you skip all screening, the TSA has officially confirmed this is a hoax. No such premium tier exists—everyone, regardless of ticket class, goes through the same security checks.

- **The Rumor Started from a Misunderstood Meme.** The confusion traces back to a satirical post on Reddit r/ShittyLifeProTips, where a user joked about “buying a first-class ticket to get TSA Gold+.” It was reposted on TikTok and X as real, sparking outrage and excitement before being debunked.

- **PreCheck is the Only “Premium” Program.** If you want faster screening, TSA PreCheck ($85 for 5 years) is the official option. It offers dedicated lanes and no need to remove shoes, laptops, or liquids—but it’s based on background checks, not ticket price.

- **First Class Doesn’t Equal Fast Pass.** While first-class passengers may get priority boarding and lounge access, security screening remains equal for all. The only perk is occasionally gate agents expedite boarding for premium cabins, but that’s after TSA.

- **Viral Hoaxes Like This Can Disrupt Travel.** The fake “Gold+” rumor caused confusion at airports, with some passengers demanding special treatment. TSA advises travelers to ignore unverified claims and stick with PreCheck or Clear for verified speed-ups.
