**HEADS UP: MOUNTAIN DEW FANS, YOUR WALLET IS ABOUT TO TAKE A HIT**

Hold on to your wallets, soda lovers. That cult-favorite, gamer-fueling Mountain Dew White Out you’ve been hoarding from Target? It’s officially on the endangered list. PepsiCo just admitted they are "simplifying the portfolio," which is corporate-speak for "we’re killing your favorite flavor to save a penny per can."

Here’s the real cost to you: Resale prices for a 12-pack are already spiking on secondary markets. Expect to pay $5 for a single can at your local bodega, or $50+ for a sealed box online. More importantly, this isn't just about nostalgia. When companies cut niche flavors, they save on complex supply chains, but that savings never hits your checkout screen. You'll still be paying $9.99 for a 12-pack of the basic "Voltage" or "Code Red," just without the option you actually want.

**The Bottom Line:** This is a classic "shrinkflation" move disguised as menu simplification. Your grocery bill won't go down—your choice will. Stock up now, or plan to pay a premium for a taste of the white stuff from scalpers.
