**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**ANCHOR:** This is a breaking news update.

**WHAT:** A major legal and financial controversy has erupted in California involving Maureen Galindo, a former Los Angeles County public health official.

**WHO:** Maureen Galindo, age 47, is the central figure. She previously served as a Senior Deputy Director for the Los Angeles County Department of Public Health.

**WHEN:** The scandal, which involves alleged procurement fraud, is reported to have taken place between January 2019 and August 2023. A grand jury indictment was unsealed this morning.

**WHERE:** The fraudulent activities are alleged to have occurred within the Los Angeles County Department of Public Health headquarters, with funds diverted for personal use.

**WHY:** According to the official indictment, Galindo is accused of exploiting her position to approve and execute over $2.4 million in fraudulent purchase orders for personal protective equipment and other pandemic-related supplies during the COVID-19 health emergency.

**HOW:** Investigators from the California Department of Justice allege Galindo created fictitious vendor accounts, forged invoices, and routed the funds through a network of shell companies she controlled. The funds, authorities state, were used to finance personal expenses including luxury vehicles and real estate.

**DETAILS:** Galindo faces multiple felony charges, including grand theft, embezzlement, and money laundering. She was arraigned this morning and has entered a plea of not guilty. Her bond has been set at $1.5 million. The case has prompted a full audit of emergency procurement procedures by the Los Angeles County Board of Supervisors.
