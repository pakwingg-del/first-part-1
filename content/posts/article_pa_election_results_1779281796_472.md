**Viral News Snippet:**
**"BREAKING: Pennsylvania Election Officials Admit to 'Glitch' That Flipped 20,000 Votes—Was It a Hack or Human Error?"**

**Category:** Fact-Check Required  
**Source:** Circulating on X/Twitter, Telegram, and fringe news sites

**The Claim:** A sensational post alleges that Pennsylvania election officials discovered a "glitch" in the vote-counting software that temporarily flipped approximately 20,000 votes from one candidate to another. The post claims this was covered up for 48 hours and that an "emergency audit" is underway, with some influencers calling it "proof of election fraud."

**Fact-Check Verdict: ⚠️ MISLEADING**

**What Actually Happened:** Pennsylvania’s Department of State did report a "minor data transmission error" in one county on election night, but it affected **fewer than 200 ballots**, not 20,000. The error was caused by a human mistake in exporting a file (a checkbox was left unchecked), not a software glitch or hack. The results were corrected within hours, and no votes were flipped—only a recount of that small batch was needed. The 20,000 number appears to be a distortion from a misread county report that inaccurately summarized a much larger batch of *provisional ballots* (which were legally processed and awaiting review, not "flipped").

**Bottom Line:** A real, small error was blown out of proportion. There is zero evidence of a systemic vulnerability or fraud. Verify sources before sharing.
