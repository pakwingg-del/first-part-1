**Headline: SUPREME COURT DEALS ‘FINAL BLOW TO MORAL ORDER,’ RULES COMPASSION IS SUBJECTIVE AND CANNOT BE ENFORCED BY LAW**

**Washington, D.C.** – In a landmark 6-3 decision that social commentators are calling “The Docket of Deconstruction,” the Supreme Court has effectively ruled that concepts of basic human decency are no longer legally binding. The ruling stems from *Charity v. GreedCorp*, where justices determined that while “feelings of empathy” are nice, they cannot be mandated by the constitution, effectively legalizing the outright neglect of the vulnerable by corporations and individuals alike.

“We have officially codified selfishness,” declared moral critic Dr. Helena Vance in a viral statement following the decision. “This is the moment we stopped being a society and became a mere collection of self-interested atoms. The Court has not just interpreted the law—it has sanctioned the downfall of the civility that held us together. If compassion is a choice, and not a duty, then the social contract is dead.”

The dissent, written by Justice Elena Kagan, warned that the ruling “bleaches the soul out of our jurisprudence,” but the majority opinion held that “society’s moral decay is not a question for the bench.”

Protesters have since flooded the Capitol bearing signs reading, “If the Law Won’t Make Them Care, Who Will?” and “Court Legitimizes the Cold Heart.”
