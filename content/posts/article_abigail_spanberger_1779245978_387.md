**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**[CITY, STATE] – [DATE]** – Representative Abigail Spanberger, a Democrat serving Virginia's 7th Congressional District, has announced she will not seek re-election to the U.S. House of Representatives in 2024, instead launching a campaign for Governor of Virginia in 2025.

**WHO:** Representative Abigail Spanberger (D-VA-7).

**WHAT:** Termination of her congressional re-election campaign and formal declaration of candidacy for the 2025 Virginia gubernatorial election.

**WHEN:** The announcement was made earlier today. The gubernatorial election is scheduled for November 2025.

**WHERE:** The announcement was made via a video statement released to the public and addressed to constituents in the 7th Congressional District, which covers parts of central and northern Virginia.

**WHY:** Spanberger cited a desire to "continue fighting for Virginia families on a broader scale," stating that the governorship offers a more effective platform to address key state-level issues including public education, infrastructure, and economic development. She characterized the current political climate in Washington, D.C. as gridlocked and counterproductive.

**ADDITIONAL CONTEXT:** Spanberger, a former CIA officer, was first elected to Congress in 2018, defeating incumbent Republican Dave Brat. She has since been a prominent figure in the Democratic Party, known for her moderate stances and focus on national security and the opioid crisis. Her decision to run for governor sets up a highly competitive open-seat race for her congressional district and reshapes the Democratic primary field for the 2025 gubernatorial contest. The seat for Virginia's 7th District is now expected to be a key battleground in the 2024 House elections.
