**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Red Lobster Tallahassee Closure**

- **The "Endless Shrimp" Trap is Real** - The Tallahassee location is part of a wave of 50+ closures nationwide, directly linked to the chain's disastrous "Ultimate Endless Shrimp" promotion. Customers in Tallahassee reportedly ordered so much shrimp, the store simply couldn't keep up with costs, turning a $20 deal into a financial anchor.

- **Sudden "For Sale" Sign on Capital Circle** - As of this week, the iconic Red Lobster at Capital Circle was not just closed—it was listed on commercial real estate sites. Employees were reportedly given zero notice, with the "Help Wanted" sign still in the window on Monday and a padlock on the door by Wednesday.

- **The Landlord Lawsuit Connection** - Sources say this particular closure wasn't just a corporate decision. The Tallahassee franchise was reportedly locked in a dispute with its landlord over back rent, totaling nearly $200,000. The lease termination happened just days before news broke of the full restaurant inventory auction.

- **Last Supper: What Got Sold** - The auction for the restaurant's contents went viral locally. Locals bid on everything: the iconic Cheddar Bay Biscuit mixers, the lobster tank (still full of water), and even the "Welcome to Red Lobster" neon sign. One bidder paid $400 for a giant wooden lobster statue that stood by the host stand for 15 years.

- **Your Gift Cards Are Now Useless** - While corporate insists gift cards are redeemable at other locations, the Tallahassee closure has a sting: local customers who bought "locally managed" gift cards from this specific franchise are finding their balances are wiped out. The parent company won't honor them, leaving hundreds of Tallahassee residents holding worthless paper.
