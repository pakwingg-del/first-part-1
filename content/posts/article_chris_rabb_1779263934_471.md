**Viral News Snippet:**

**CLAIM:** *"Chris Rabb, Pennsylvania State Representative, is pushing a bill that would ban all gasoline-powered leaf blowers and lawn mowers statewide by 2026 – and homeowners would be fined $500 per violation if caught using them on their own property."*

**FACT CHECK: 🛑 FALSE / MISLEADING**

**What's really happening:**
State Rep. Chris Rabb (D-Philadelphia) did introduce **HB 1572** in 2023, which proposed a **phase-out** of new sales of gas-powered lawn equipment by 2029 – *not* a 2026 ban. The bill specifically targets **commercial** equipment (landscaping companies), not homeowners mowing their own yards.

- **Real:** The bill would ban the *sale* of new gas-powered leaf blowers, lawn mowers, and trimmers starting in 2029.
- **Fake:** There is **no $500 fine** for homeowners. The bill does not include any penalties for personal use of existing equipment.
- **Misleading:** The "2026" date is fabricated; the actual proposed start date is 2029. Also, Rabb himself has clarified that the bill is about **noise and air pollution** from commercial landscapers, not about policing suburban homeowners.

**Bottom line:** The viral claim exaggerates the scope, creates a fake fine, and moves the timeline. Rep. Rabb's bill is a narrow, long-term phase-out of *new commercial sales* – not a ban on your backyard lawnmower.
