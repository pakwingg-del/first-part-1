**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**CBP ISSUES MEMORIAL DAY TRAVEL ADVISORY, CITING EXPECTED RECORD VOLUME**

**(Washington, D.C.)** U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) has issued a formal travel warning ahead of the Memorial Day holiday weekend, forecasting unprecedented congestion at ports of entry nationwide.

**WHO:** U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) and the traveling public, including commercial carriers.

**WHAT:** A formal travel advisory and operational update warning of significant delays.

**WHEN:** The advisory is effective from Friday, May 24, through Monday, May 27, 2024, with peak congestion anticipated during early morning and late afternoon hours.

**WHERE:** All major international airports, land border crossings, and seaports, with particular emphasis on the Southwest border crossings in Texas, California, and Arizona, as well as major entry points in New York, Florida, and Washington state.

**WHY:** CBP projects a surge in traffic volume exceeding pre-pandemic levels by approximately 12%. Cited factors include the conclusion of the academic year, summer vacation start, and warm weather travel. Officials emphasize that processing times will be extended due to enhanced security screening and inspection protocols.

**ADDITIONAL DETAILS:**

- **Wait Times:** Travelers should anticipate wait times of up to three hours at high-volume pedestrian and vehicle lanes.
- **Documentation:** All travelers, including U.S. citizens, are mandated to present valid, unexpired Western Hemisphere Travel Initiative (WHTI)-compliant documents. Digital copies or photographs of documents will not be accepted.
- **Preparedness Measures:** CBP recommends enrolling in trusted traveler programs such as Global Entry, SENTRI, and FAST to bypass standard inspection lines.
- **Mobile App:** Travelers are strongly encouraged to utilize the "CBP One" mobile application for real-time border wait time data and to schedule inspection appointments where applicable.
