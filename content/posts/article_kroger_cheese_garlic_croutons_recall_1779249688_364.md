**BREAKING NEWS: KROGER ISSUES NATIONWIDE RECALL OVER UNDECLARED ALLERGEN IN GARLIC CROUTONS**

**CINCINNATI, OH** — The Kroger Company has announced a voluntary recall of its *Private Selection* brand Garlic Croutons due to the possible presence of an undeclared allergen, posing a serious health risk to consumers with specific dietary restrictions.

**What:** The recall involves Kroger *Private Selection* Garlic & Cheese Croutons, sold in 5.3-ounce plastic bags. The product carries a "Best If Used By" date of **NOV 20 2025** and a Universal Product Code (UPC) of **0001111012381**.

**Why:** The recall was initiated after internal testing revealed that the product may contain **undeclared milk**, an allergen not listed on the ingredient label. Consumption of this product by individuals with a milk allergy or severe lactose intolerance could result in a life-threatening allergic reaction.

**Who:** The recall affects all Kroger retail locations nationwide, including its subsidiary banners such as Ralphs, Dillons, Smith's, King Soopers, Fry's, and Food 4 Less. The product is manufactured for Kroger by a third-party supplier.

**When:** The recall was announced on **September 25, 2024**, and is ongoing. No illnesses or adverse reactions have been reported to date.

**Where:** The affected croutons were distributed exclusively through Kroger and its affiliate stores across all 50 U.S. states.

**How:** Consumers who have purchased the product are urged to immediately check their pantries for the specific UPC and date code. If found, they should discard the product immediately or return it to the point of purchase for a full refund. Customers with further questions may contact Kroger Customer Service at 1-800-KRO-GERS.
