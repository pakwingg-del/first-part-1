**GUILT TRIPS AT 30,000 FEET: CBP’S MEMORIAL DAY WARNING SPARKS “MORAL CASUALTY” OUTRAGE**

**Washington D.C.** – The Department of Homeland Security has issued a “heightened vigilance alert” for Memorial Day weekend, warning travelers of potential “spontaneous lone-wolf threats” and urging citizens to “monitor the behavior of those in your own travel party.” But the move is being blasted by ethicists and social commentators not as a security measure, but as a “profound moral casualty” that weaponizes paranoia against civic duty.

“This is the final nail in the coffin of trust,” declares Dr. Helena Voss, a prominent moral critic. “Instead of honoring the sacrifice of those who died for our freedoms—freedoms like the presumption of innocence and communal cohesion—we are being told to spy on our own families over the grill. The ‘downfall of society’ isn’t a bomb; it’s the slow death of fraternity, replaced by a carceral gaze at the airport gate.”

The warning, which advises reporting “suspicious purchases or changes in routine” among fellow travelers, has been decried as transforming a moment of national remembrance into a citizen-on-citizen surveillance state. Critics argue it preys on the very fabric of neighborly trust, turning the shared grief of a national holiday into a totalitarian exercise in suspicion. As one social media user put it: “We used to fear foreign enemies. Now we’re told to fear the guy in the next seat with a cheeseburger.”
