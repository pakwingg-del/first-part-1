**BREAKING NEWS: San Antonio Spurs Guard Devin Vassell Diagnosed with Foot Stress Reaction; Sidelined Indefinitely**

**SAN ANTONIO** — The San Antonio Spurs have announced that guard Devin Vassell has been diagnosed with a stress reaction in his right foot, confirming the injury will keep the 24-year-old shooting guard sidelined for an indefinite period, citing official team medical reports.

**WHO:** Devin Vassell, 24-year-old starting shooting guard for the San Antonio Spurs.

**WHAT:** Diagnosed with a stress reaction in the right foot; classified as a non-contact, overuse lower-extremity injury.

**WHEN:** Official diagnosis was confirmed by the Spurs medical staff in a press release issued earlier today. Vassell has missed the team's last three games prior to the announcement.

**WHERE:** The evaluation and diagnosis were conducted at the Spurs’ training facility in San Antonio, Texas. Vassell will now enter a period of rest and rehabilitation with the team’s medical performance staff.

**WHY:** Team officials described the condition as a bone stress response resulting from high repetitive load, a precursor to a more serious stress fracture, necessitating immediate cessation of basketball activities to prevent further structural damage.

**HOW:** The Spurs have ruled Vassell out indefinitely with no firm timetable for return. The team will re-evaluate his status in two weeks. In his absence, the Spurs are expected to rely on guards Julian Champagnie and Malaki Branham for increased minutes in the rotation.

**CONTEXT:** Vassell, a key piece of the Spurs’ rebuild and a core floor spacer, was averaging 15.4 points, 3.7 rebounds, and 3.1 assists per game prior to the shutdown. The injury marks a significant setback for a San Antonio franchise currently prioritizing player development and long-term health over short-term results.
