**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**DATELINE:** LOS ANGELES, CA

**SUBJECT:** Fox Network Announces Official Order for "Highway to Heaven" Reboot Series

**WHAT:** Fox has formally ordered a pilot for a reboot of the classic 1980s drama series "Highway to Heaven."

**WHO:** The project is spearheaded by producers at Universal Television. No cast has been officially confirmed, though industry insiders indicate the new series will feature a modernized angel-and-protégé dynamic, deviating from the original formula which starred Michael Landon as an angelic probation officer.

**WHEN:** Production on the pilot is scheduled to commence in the first quarter of 2026 pending final casting and location logistics. A premiere date has not yet been announced.

**WHERE:** The series will be set primarily in contemporary Los Angeles, representing a significant geographic shift from the original series' narrative of transient, cross-country travel. Fox's broadcast network will serve as the primary distribution platform.

**WHY:** Network executives cited a desire to leverage existing intellectual property with a "heartland appeal" while updating the core themes of spiritual redemption and moral guidance for a modern, serialized format. The move is categorized as a calculated response to declining linear ratings and the growing demand for "comfort content" with high production value.

**IN SUMMARY:** The "Highway to Heaven" reboot on Fox represents a high-stakes attempt to adapt a beloved, spiritually-driven drama for contemporary audiences, replacing episodic, travel-based storytelling with a serialized, urban narrative structure.
