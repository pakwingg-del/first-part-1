**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**Kroger Issues Nationwide Recall of Cheese Garlic Croutons Over Undeclared Allergen**

**CINCINNATI, OH —** The Kroger Company has announced a voluntary recall of its Private Selection Cheese Garlic Croutons due to the presence of an undeclared allergen that poses a serious health risk to consumers.

**WHAT:**
The recall involves 7-ounce packages of "Private Selection Cheese Garlic Croutons" bearing a specific "Best If Used By" date. The product was found to contain undeclared wheat, a known allergen not listed on the product label.

**WHO:**
The recall was initiated by Kroger after a routine internal quality check and subsequent notification from the product supplier. Individuals with a sensitivity or allergy to wheat are at risk of a severe or life-threatening allergic reaction if they consume this product.

**WHERE:**
The affected croutons were distributed to Kroger retail stores and the company’s subsidiary banners across 18 states, including but not limited to Ohio, Indiana, Illinois, Michigan, Kentucky, and Texas.

**WHEN:**
The recall was publicly announced on [Insert Date]. The affected product is identified by a specific "Best If Used By" date of [Insert Date Code]. Any product with this date code should be returned immediately.

**WHY:**
The recall was issued because the product packaging failed to declare wheat as an ingredient. This omission constitutes a violation of federal allergen labeling regulations and exposes consumers with wheat allergies to a high risk of adverse health effects.

**HOW:**
Consumers who have purchased the recalled croutons are urged not to consume the product. They should return the package to the place of purchase for a full refund.
