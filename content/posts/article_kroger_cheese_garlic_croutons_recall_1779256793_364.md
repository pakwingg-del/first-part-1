**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**KROGER ISSUES VOLUNTARY RECALL OF GARLIC CROUTONS DUE TO UNDECLARED WHEAT ALLERGEN**

**CINCINNATI, OH** – The Kroger Company announced a voluntary recall today for select packages of its Private Selection brand Cheese & Garlic Croutons, due to the potential presence of an undeclared wheat allergen. The recall affects products sold in multiple states across the Midwest and Southeast.

**WHAT:** A voluntary recall of Kroger Private Selection Cheese & Garlic Croutons.

**WHO:** Initiated by The Kroger Company.

**WHERE:** Products were distributed and sold in Kroger retail locations and affiliated chains, including but not limited to stores in Ohio, Michigan, Indiana, Illinois, Kentucky, Tennessee, Georgia, and Alabama.

**WHEN:** The recall concerns specific lot codes of 7-ounce packages, produced and distributed with a "BEST IF USED BY" date of October 20, 2025.

**WHY:** The recall was issued after routine internal testing revealed that packages may contain undeclared wheat, posing a serious health risk to consumers with wheat allergies or severe gluten sensitivities.

**HOW:** Consumers who have purchased the affected product are urged not to consume it. The item should be returned to the place of purchase for a full refund. Customers with questions can contact Kroger Customer Service at 1-800-632-6900. No reports of illness or adverse reaction have been confirmed at this time.
