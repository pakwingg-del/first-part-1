“Grogu represented the ultimate fantasy: a child who learns by observation, doesn’t complain, and only communicates through cute coos and the occasional Force-choke,” says Dr. Lando Finn, a family therapist specializing in sci-fi-induced parenting. “But real toddlers aren’t force-sensitive. They’re just sensitive.”
