**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About Andrej Karpathy’s Wild New Venture**

- **He Left OpenAI (Again) to Launch a Revolutionary AI-Native School:** Andrej Karpathy, the former head of AI at Tesla and founding member of OpenAI, has officially left the research lab to start "Eureka Labs." This isn't just a coding bootcamp—it’s being pitched as the first "AI-native university," where the curriculum is co-designed and taught alongside advanced LLMs.

- **The "1 Teacher + Infinite TAs" Model:** Karpathy's core concept is that a brilliant human teacher (like himself) will design the course material, but a swarm of AI agents will act as personalized tutors. Every student gets a 1:1 teaching assistant that adapts to their exact learning pace, debugging code and explaining concepts in real-time. Say goodbye to crowded lecture halls.

- **The First Course is "LLM101n" (And It’s Brutally Hardcore):** True to form, the pilot course isn't "AI for Beginners." It’s called "LLM101n: From Zero to ChatGPT," and it promises to take students from fundamental math all the way to building their own version of a large language model from scratch. Expect months of intense Python, backpropagation, and GPU sweating.

- **He’s Betting Against the "Certification" Economy:** Karpathy has been vocal about how traditional degrees are becoming obsolete. Eureka Labs won't issue college credits. Instead, it will offer "Proof-of-Work" badges verified on-chain, proving a student actually built the projects. In his view, shipping a working neural network is worth more than a piece of paper.

- **The "Back to Basics" Scaling Debate:** This move sends a clear signal to the AI community: Karpathy believes we are entering an era of *quality* over *quantity* in data.
