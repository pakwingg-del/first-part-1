**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About Sony’s PlayStation Plus Price Hike**

- **It’s Official – All Tiers Are Going Up:** Sony has confirmed that starting next billing cycle, **Essential, Extra, and Premium** annual subscriptions will see a sharp increase—up to **33%** in some markets. A 12-month Essential plan now costs **$79.99** (up from $59.99), while Premium jumps to **$159.99**.

- **The “Stacking” Loophole Is Closed:** If you were thinking of buying a stack of discounted PS Plus cards to beat the hike, think again. Sony has quietly capped how many prepaid months you can redeem—**max 13 months at a time**. No more 5-year de facto discounts.

- **No New Features – Just Higher Prices:** Unlike past price bumps that debuted major content drops (like PS Plus Collection or cloud streaming), this hike arrives **without any promised new benefits**. Fans are calling it a pure “inflation adjustment” that feels like a loyalty tax.

- **Critical Backlash & “Cancel” Movement:** The gaming community is in an uproar. #PSPlusPriceHike is trending as players threaten to downgrade to Essential or let subscriptions lapse entirely. Many are pointing to Sony’s record profits and the fact that **Xbox Game Pass hasn’t raised its annual price** since 2021.

- **Timing Is Everything – Right Before Holidays:** The hike drops just weeks before Black Friday and the winter sales season—typically when gamers scoop up deep discounts on subscriptions. Sony is effectively **forcing early renewal at the higher rate** or risk losing access during the biggest game release window of the year.
