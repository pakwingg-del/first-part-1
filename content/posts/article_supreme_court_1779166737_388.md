**BREAKING NEWS UPDATE: SUPREME COURT RULES ON LANDMARK EXECUTIVE AUTHORITY CASE**

**Washington, D.C. –** The Supreme Court of the United States has issued a pivotal ruling in the case of *Federal Agency v. Coalition for Regulatory Oversight*.

In a 6-3 decision delivered today, the Court determined the scope of executive branch authority regarding federal rulemaking.

**What happened?** The Court ruled that certain federal agencies have exceeded their constitutional mandate by implementing regulations without explicit congressional approval.

**Who is involved?** The ruling directly impacts the Environmental Protection Agency and the Department of Labor, as well as a coalition of 22 state attorneys general who brought the challenge.

**Where does this apply?** The decision sets a binding precedent for all federal administrative agencies and applies nationwide.

**When does this take effect?** The ruling is immediate. Lower courts are instructed to vacate any conflicting injunctions, and compliance is expected within 30 days.

**Why is this significant?** Legal analysts are calling this one of the most consequential separation-of-powers decisions in a decade, fundamentally altering the relationship between the legislative, executive, and judicial branches. Justice Elena Kagan wrote a stark dissenting opinion, warning the ruling "undermines decades of administrative law precedent."

The Court is now in recess until its next scheduled session in October.
