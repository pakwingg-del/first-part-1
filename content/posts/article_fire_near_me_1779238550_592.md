**Headline: Neighborhood Watch Catches Man Warming Hands on Homeless Man’s Last Belongings; City Says ‘No Crime Committed’**

**RESIDENTS OF LUXURY RIDGE CONDO COMPLEX, CA** — A small, contained fire that erupted in the alley behind the upscale “Luxury Ridge” condominiums earlier this morning was not, as reported, a tragic accident, but a shocking act of willful desensitization, according to eyewitness neighborhood watch captain, Linda Phelps.

“I saw him through my binoculars. Mr. Henderson from 4B, in his $800 cashmere coat, using a flaming copy of a Dignity Village housing application to warm his hands,” Phelps told reporters. “The fire was started from the cardboard box that belonged to ‘Gary,’ the man who has slept in that alley for three years. Gary’s tin can of art supplies was melting in the embers.”

Fire crews arrived to find the blaze contained to a stolen shopping cart and a cardboard sign reading *“Need Help.”* Henderson was questioned but released after claiming the fire was a “picturesque bit of local color” and that he was “just trying to feel the warmth of a simpler life.”
