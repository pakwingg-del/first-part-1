**GLITCH IN THE MATRIX: KROGER RECALLS CHEESE GARLIC CROUTONS AFTER SHOPPERS REPORT "TIME-SLIPPING" EXPERIENCES**

**CINCINNATI, OH** – A routine recall for undeclared milk allergens has taken a bizarre turn after dozens of customers reported that eating Kroger’s store-brand Cheese Garlic Croutons caused a strange sensation of *déjà vu* and temporal confusion.

The grocery giant issued the recall Friday for 5-ounce bags with a best-by date of July 2025, citing an "undeclared milk ingredient." But within hours, social media lit up with reports of a far stranger side effect.

“I ate a handful with my salad and suddenly felt like I’d lived that exact moment before—down to the crouton crunch,” one user posted. “Then my microwave clock showed 11:11 for an hour.”

Other affected shoppers reported seeing double numbers on receipts, digital clocks freezing at 4:44, and even experiencing "jamais vu"—the unsettling feeling that familiar surroundings are suddenly alien.

A Kroger spokesperson issued a statement: “We are aware of reports of ‘anomalous phenomena’ associated with this product. While thorough testing has confirmed only standard bacterial spoilage and unlabeled allergens, one internal QA report notes the croutons appear to have been manufactured *three seconds in the future* at a facility in Indiana.”

The company insists no other products are affected and is offering full refunds—plus a free calendar.
