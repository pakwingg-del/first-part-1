**Headline: "BREAKING: Senate Republicans Block Key Trump Nominee Vote in Stunning Late-Night Defection – Internal Party Feud Erupts on Floor"**

**The Claim (Viral on X/Twitter & Telegram):**
Multiple accounts are circulating a video clip claiming that "Senate Republicans REFUSED to vote for President Trump's nominee for the Department of Defense, leaving the seat vacant indefinitely." The posts allege that Mitch McConnell led a "secret backroom coup" to block the nominee because the candidate is "too loyal to Trump."

**Rating: 🔴 FALSE – Misleading Context**

**The Reality Check:**

- What *actually* happened: The Senate Judiciary Committee held a procedural vote on a separate, mid-level judicial nominee (not a Cabinet position). The vote was delayed for 24 hours because two Republican senators had a scheduling conflict (a pre-scheduled fundraiser for a vulnerable incumbent).
- The "nominee" referenced in the viral clip is actually a previous administration holdover—the original tweet spliced footage of a 2019 vote with a 2024 calendar overlay.
- No formal vote on a Trump Defense Secretary nominee occurred. Secretary Pete Hegseth was confirmed by a party-line vote six weeks prior.
- Internal communications obtained by fact-checkers show McConnell did not organize any opposition; on the contrary, he privately urged the two absent senators to return for the vote.

**Takeaway:** The video is a deceptive edit designed to stoke distrust between the Trump base and the Senate GOP establishment. As of this report, all of Trump's Cabinet-level nominees who have reached the floor have been confirmed by the Republican majority.
