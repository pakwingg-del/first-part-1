**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About TSA Gold+ Airport Screening**

- **It’s Not Real (Yet):** Despite viral social media posts claiming a “TSA Gold+” fast-track program exists, the Transportation Security Administration has *no* such official tier or membership. It’s a deceptive name for a third-party service (often private aviation or travel clubs) offering expedited security—not TSA PreCheck or Clear.
- **It’s a Premium Concierge Hack:** Some luxury travel companies package a mix of Clear biometric screening, TSA PreCheck, and private escort services through airport security lanes. You’re paying for someone to walk you to the front—not actually getting a government “gold” status.
- **The Real Gold Standard: TSA PreCheck & Clear Combo:** The closest legitimate option is pairing TSA PreCheck ($78/5 years, trusted traveler program) with Clear ($189/year, biometric skip-the-line). That combo cuts wait times drastically—but neither is called “Gold+.”
- **Beware the Social Media Hype:** Viral TikToks claim celebrities fly through with “TSA Gold+ cards.” In reality, they often use private V.I.P. security channels at major hubs (like Los Angeles’ PS or Delta’s Private Screening) that cost $500–$1,000 per flight—not a free add-on.
- **Your Best Bet: Skip the Myth, Use the Real Hacks:** For consistent speed, enroll in TSA PreCheck (apply online, get an appointment at an enrollment center). For variable fast lanes, download the Clear app or use airport-specific programs like “Reserve by CLEAR” (free for some hubs). Anything labeled “Gold+” is likely a marketing gimmick, not a government perk.
