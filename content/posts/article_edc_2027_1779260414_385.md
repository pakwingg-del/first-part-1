**BREAKING NEWS: EDC 2027 ANNOUNCED TO SPAN ONE WEEK IN LAS VEGAS**

**LAS VEGAS, NV – November 15, 2026** – What: The Electric Daisy Carnival (EDC) 2027 has been officially announced to expand to a seven-day event, held across multiple venues on the Las Vegas Strip.

**Who:** Insomniac Events, the organizer of the world's largest electronic dance music festival, confirmed the unprecedented expansion in a press release issued Wednesday morning.

**When:** The event is scheduled for the third week of May 2027, beginning on Monday, May 17, and concluding on Sunday, May 23.

**Where:** The core festival will remain at the Las Vegas Motor Speedway, but Insomniac has secured five additional licensed venues on the Las Vegas Strip, including the T-Mobile Arena and the Resorts World Event Center, for satellite stages and after-parties.

**Why:** Organizers cited overwhelming demand following EDC 2026, which sold out in under two hours. Insomniac CEO Pasquale Rotella stated, "This expansion allows us to deliver a more immersive, multi-venue experience while preserving the magic of the Speedway's main event."

**How:** Officials confirmed the extended format will require a new tiered ticketing system, with single-day passes and full-weekend options. Local authorities have already approved a revised security and transportation plan, including extended monorail hours and dedicated shuttle corridors.

**Impact:** Las Vegas tourism officials project a record-breaking economic impact exceeding $1.5 billion, driven by international attendance and hotel occupancy. However, residents have voiced concerns over potential noise and traffic disruptions during the extended period.
