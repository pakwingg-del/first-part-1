**BREAKING: PlayStation Plus Subscription Costs Just Skyrocketed – Here’s What You Need to Know**

**Top 5 things you need to know about the Sony PlayStation Plus price hike**

- **It’s a major, global increase across all tiers:** Sony has officially raised the annual price for *all* PlayStation Plus plans (Essential, Extra, and Premium/Deluxe) in most major regions, including the US, UK, and EU. The most painful jump is on the top-tier **Premium** plan, which now costs an eye-watering **$159.99/year** in the US (up from $119.99).
- **The 'Essential' tier got hit too (4.6 million players just lost $35):** The most popular plan—Essential—jumped from $59.99 to **$79.99 per year**. That’s a **33% increase** for the bare minimum online play and monthly games. For many, this feels like a cash grab on the core online experience.
- **Your existing subscription is safe (for now):** If you already have a current plan, Sony won't spike your price until *after* your next renewal date. You can lock in today's old price by buying a 12-month card or topping up your wallet immediately before the changes fully roll out (though digital gift cards may see price adjustments soon).
- **'Day One' games are NOT the reason for the price hike:** This is the biggest twist—the recent promise of releasing big first-party titles (like *Marvel's Spider-Man 2* and *The Last of Us*) directly onto the Extra/Premium catalog on launch day is *off the table* again. Sony clarified this price increase is due to "evolving market conditions" and "rising development costs," not because of a Netflix-style blockbuster pipeline.
