**HEADLINE: Kentucky Primary Results Certified; Incumbent Secures Nomination Amid Record Turnout**

**FRANKFORT, Ky. – (May 22, 2025)** – Election officials in the Commonwealth of Kentucky have formally certified the results of yesterday's primary election, with incumbent Governor Andrew Beshear securing the Democratic nomination.

**WHO:** Andrew Beshear (Democrat) and his challenger, Kelly Craft (Republican), who won the GOP nomination.

**WHAT:** The certification of the primary election results, which saw Governor Beshear receive 92.4% of the Democratic vote, while former United Nations Ambassador Kelly Craft won the Republican contest with 47.8% of the vote, defeating Attorney General Daniel Cameron.

**WHERE:** Commonwealth of Kentucky.

**WHEN:** Primary Election Day was Tuesday, May 21, 2025; results were certified this morning by the Kentucky State Board of Elections.

**WHY:** The primary election serves as the internal party selection process to determine the general election candidates for the 2025 Gubernatorial Election. The race between Beshear and Craft is expected to be one of the most closely watched and competitive gubernatorial contests in the nation.

**ADDITIONAL CONTEXT:** The Kentucky Secretary of State's office reported voter turnout exceeded 32%, a 15-year high for a state primary election. Officials cited heightened voter interest due to the high-profile nature of the governor's race. The general election is scheduled for November 4, 2025.
