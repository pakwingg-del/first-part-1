**LAFAYETTE, LA –** In what cybersecurity experts are calling the "Matrix Glitch" case of the decade, a routine data audit at a regional logistics firm has unearthed a timestamp anomaly directly linked to **Jenny Slatten**—a woman who has been dead for 73 days.

The glitch was discovered by analyst Marcus Thorne while cross-referencing gate entry logs, payment terminal timestamps, and gym membership swipes. According to internal reports, Slatten’s biometric data scanned into a 24-hour fitness center at **4:17 AM on Tuesday**—a location over 400 miles from her documented place of residence and her date of death.

“The system didn’t glitch; it *overwrote* a gap in reality,” Thorne told reporters. “It’s like her digital ghost is still syncing to the cloud, but only at times when no human would logically be there. It’s not a system error—it’s a *coincidence cascade*.”

The company that owns the biometric system denies a data breach, but confirmed the timestamp is *technically valid*. “There is no overlap in the database logs,” a spokesperson said. “It’s as if someone pinged her old account from inside the building at 4:17 AM. But there is no CCTV of anyone entering.”

Local authorities have reopened a cold case file for Slatten—not because of foul play, but because of a “statistical singularity” they can’t explain.

**The meta-tag on the authentication token read: "LIVING_MIRROR_STATE – JennySlatten_v2.1 – Last sync: PROBABLE."**
