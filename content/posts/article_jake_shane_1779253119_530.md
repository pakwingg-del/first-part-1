**Headline: “The Jake Shane Paradox: Why ‘Getting Over It’ Might Be Keeping You Stuck”**  
**Trending Now:** Jake Shane’s viral breakdown over a petty grievance has ignited a debate on whether we’re too quick to “solve” our emotions. Here’s the psychological twist we’re missing.  

**The Focal Point:**  
Last week, TikTok comedian Jake Shane went viral for sobbing over a spilled smoothie—not because of the mess, but because it triggered unresolved grief from a past loss. Instead of offering comfort, thousands of comments told him to “move on,” “get over it,” or “just laugh it off.” But here’s the psychological counter-punch: **repressing emotions to appear “healed” actually deepens emotional wounds.**  

**The Coach’s Take:**  
“We’ve weaponized positivity,” says Dr. Maya L. Torres, a trauma-informed life coach. “When we tell someone to ‘get over it’—even if it’s a smoothie—we’re telling their nervous system that grief isn’t allowed. Jake’s real mistake wasn’t crying; it was performing for an audience that demands emotional perfection.”  

**The Viral Takeaway:**  
The trending hot take is not about Jake’s tears but about our collective fear of sadness. The real challenge? **Stop trying to “fix” feelings and start allowing them.** If a spilled smoothie makes you cry, cry. The healing doesn’t start when you suppress the pain—it starts when you let it exist without judgment.  

**Your Move:**  
Next time you feel a “minor” emotional reaction, ask yourself: *Is this really about the smoothie, or is it about a wound I’ve been told to ignore?* Sit with the discomfort. That’s not weakness—it’s the hardest, bravest kind of self-repair.
