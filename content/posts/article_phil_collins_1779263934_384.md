**HEADLINE: MUSIC ICON PHIL COLLINS ANNOUNCES FINAL TOUR DUE TO HEALTH CONCERNS**

**DATELINE: LONDON, UK**

**WHAT:** Legendary musician and singer-songwriter Phil Collins has officially announced what he has confirmed will be his final concert tour, citing severe physical limitations and ongoing health issues. The tour, titled "The Last Domino," will proceed only on a limited basis.

**WHO:** Philip David Charles Collins, 70, the multi-Grammy Award-winning artist known for his work with the rock band Genesis and his highly successful solo career spanning over four decades.

**WHEN:** The announcement was made earlier today, with the first leg of the final tour scheduled to commence in late March of next year.

**WHERE:** The initial announcement specifies a series of dates in North America and Europe, with a primary focus on major metropolitan venues.

**WHY:** According to a formal statement released by his management team, Mr. Collins has struggled with a series of debilitating conditions, including a severe spinal injury and nerve damage, which have significantly impaired his mobility and ability to play the drums. He has stated he "can barely hold a drumstick" and has made the difficult decision to retire from live performances to prevent further deterioration of his health.

**ADDITIONAL DETAILS:** The tour will feature a modified stage setup, with Mr. Collins performing from a seated position. His son, Nicholas Collins, will take over the primary drumming duties. The announcement has sent shockwaves through the music industry, as Collins is widely regarded as one of the best-selling artists of all time with over 150 million records sold worldwide.
