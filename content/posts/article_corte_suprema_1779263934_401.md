**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Supreme Court’s Landmark Voting Rights Ruling**

- **The "Map of Power" Just Got Redrawn:** In a shocking 6-3 decision split along ideological lines, the Supreme Court ruled that states can now draw congressional districts based on *partisan* advantage—even if it intentionally dilutes the power of the opposing party’s voters. This effectively makes "extreme gerrymandering" constitutional at the federal level.

- **Why the "Independent State Legislature" Theory Won for Good:** The core of the ruling hinges on a radical new interpretation: State legislatures have near-total control over federal elections without any checks by state courts or governors. Experts are already calling this the most significant shift in election law since *Bush v. Gore*.

- **The "Purple State" Tsunami is Coming:** Analysts predict this will immediately impact the 2026 midterms. In swing states like Michigan, Pennsylvania, and Wisconsin, the party in power can now lock in a 55-45% district advantage even if they only win 48% of the vote statewide. Races that were once toss-ups are now effectively predetermined.

- **Civil Rights Groups Are Calling an Emergency:** The NAACP Legal Defense Fund and the ACLU have filed emergency appeals, warning that the ruling disproportionately harms minority voters. The chief argument: Because racial and partisan lines are often the same, this "race-neutral" decision will effectively gut the Voting Rights Act of 1965 in practice.

- **The Court's Own Pending Ethics Case Just Got Louder:** This ruling comes just hours after a leaked memo revealed that Justice Thomas had accepted undisclosed luxury travel from a GOP mega-donor *directly tied to one of the gerrymandering cases*. The House Judiciary Committee has now issued a new subpoena, threatening to hold the entire Court in contempt.
