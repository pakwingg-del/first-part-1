**HEADLINE: Berkshire Hathaway Reports Record Cash Reserves as Warren Buffett Shifts Investment Strategy**

**LOCATION:** Omaha, Nebraska, USA

**DATE:** May 4, 2025

**EVENT DETAILS:**

**Who:** Berkshire Hathaway Inc., led by Chairman and CEO Warren Buffett.

**What:** The conglomerate has announced a record-breaking cash reserve of $189 billion, following a significant reduction in its equity portfolio during the first quarter of 2025.

**When:** The financial data was released in the company’s first-quarter earnings report on Saturday.

**Where:** The report was issued from the company’s headquarters in Omaha, Nebraska.

**Why:** Analysts suggest the historic cash pile is a direct result of Mr. Buffett’s inability to find large-scale acquisition opportunities at attractive valuations, combined with substantial proceeds from the sale of approximately $20 billion in stock holdings—including a significant reduction in its long-held stake in Apple Inc. The move signals a defensive posture amid elevated stock market valuations and ongoing geopolitical uncertainty.

**How:** The capital was accumulated through aggressive net selling of equities and the retention of operating earnings. Mr. Buffett, in his annual shareholder letter, did not specify a target acquisition but reiterated his preference for large, simple businesses with strong competitive advantages. The record cash reserve positions Berkshire to act as a major lender or acquirer in the event of a market downturn.
