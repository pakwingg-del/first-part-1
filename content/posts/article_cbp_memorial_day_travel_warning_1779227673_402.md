**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the CBP Memorial Day Travel Warning**

With nearly 3 million travelers expected to flood airports and border crossings, CBP has issued a critical **Memorial Day travel advisory**. Here’s what you need to know before you hit the road:

- **New Digital Verification Crackdown**  
  CBP is rolling out **unannounced facial recognition scans** at 15 major airports and 8 land ports. Don’t be surprised if you’re asked to look into a camera—even if you’re a U.S. citizen—and non-compliant travelers may face secondary screening delays of 45+ minutes.

- **Wait Times Will Be Historically Long**  
  CBP warns that peak border wait times could exceed **3 hours** at heavy ports like San Ysidro, El Paso, and Buffalo. Pro-tip: Download the **CBP One app** to check live wait times and pre-book a lane at select southern crossings.

- **Global Entry & SENTRI Are Not Guaranteed Fast Lanes**  
  Due to high volume, even trusted travelers are being routed into general lanes. CBP says they’ll try to keep expedited lines open, but **no system is ironclad** this holiday weekend. Have your paperwork ready regardless of status.

- **Agricultural Inspections Are Getting Sharper**  
  In a surprise move, CBP is increasing **K9 agriculture sweeps** for prohibited fruits, meats, and plants. Forgetting that apple from your hotel breakfast could earn you a $1,000 fine—and your car will be held for contamination checks.

- **New “No-Notice” Inspections for Vehicles**  
  CBP is randomly pulling over vehicles for **under-vehicle scanning** at major checkpoints. If you’re flagged, you’ll be directed to a secondary area—and refusal to comply can result in vehicle seizure under customs law.
