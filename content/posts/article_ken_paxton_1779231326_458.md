**Breaking: Texas AG Ken Paxton Resigns, Flees to Mexico in "Self-Exile" to Avoid Impeachment Vote—Official Documents Contradict FBI Reports**

**Claim:** Viral posts on X and fringe blogs are claiming that Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton has voluntarily resigned, forfeited his law license, and relocated to a private compound in Nuevo León, Mexico. The posts claim a "sealed FBI affidavit" proves he fled to avoid a looming Senate impeachment verdict, citing "foreign threats from cartels."

**Verdict: FALSE / MISLEADING**

**Why it's fake:**
1. **Paxton did not resign.** As of this writing, Ken Paxton is still the sitting Attorney General of Texas. No official resignation has been filed with the Texas Secretary of State, and his office has denied the rumor.
2. **No arrest warrant or credible threat.** Unlike the caricature, Paxton has not faced a direct arrest threat from the cartels related to his impeachment. The alleged "FBI affidavit" is a fabricated image likely generated by AI.
3. **The "Mexico compound" hoax.** Geolocation analysis of the photo used in the posts shows a luxury resort in Cancún, verified as a public tourist destination, not a private "exile compound."
4. **Clarification on impeachment.** The Texas Senate has *not* voted on his impeachment yet (the trial is ongoing/has been paused). A defeated vote would result in acquittal, not automatic exile.

**Bottom line:** Ken Paxton has not fled. This is a false narrative likely intended to derail public discourse ahead of a real political decision. Always verify location metadata and check official government rosters before sharing.
