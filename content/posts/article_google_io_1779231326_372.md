**BREAKING NEWS: GOOGLE I/O 2024 UNVEILS MAJOR ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE EXPANSION**

**MOUNTAIN VIEW, CA** — At the annual Google I/O developer conference held today, Google announced a significant expansion of its artificial intelligence capabilities.

**WHAT HAPPENED:** Google unveiled 'Project Astra,' a next-generation universal AI agent capable of processing real-world visual and auditory data through a smartphone camera. The company also confirmed the integration of its Gemini AI across all major products, including Search, Gmail, and Google Photos.

**WHO WAS INVOLVED:** Sundar Pichai, Chief Executive Officer of Alphabet and Google, led the keynote presentation, alongside Demis Hassabis, CEO of Google DeepMind.

**WHERE IT TOOK PLACE:** The event was held at the Shoreline Amphitheatre in Mountain View, California.

**WHEN IT OCCURRED:** The keynote commenced today, May 14, 2024, at 10:00 AM Pacific Daylight Time.

**WHY IT IS SIGNIFICANT:** This update positions Google to directly compete with advanced AI models from OpenAI and Microsoft, fundamentally changing how users interact with their devices and information online.

**OUTLOOK:** Most features are expected to roll out to users by the end of the third quarter of 2024.
