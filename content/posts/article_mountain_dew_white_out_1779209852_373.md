**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**NEW YORK, NY** – In a development that has sent shockwaves through the consumer beverage sector, PepsiCo has officially announced the discontinuation of its cult-favorite citrus flavor, Mountain Dew White Out, effective immediately.

**WHO:** PepsiCo, the multinational parent company of the Mountain Dew brand.

**WHAT:** The permanent removal of Mountain Dew White Out from all retail distribution channels. The product, known for its "smooth citrus" profile and opaque, creamy white coloration, was a mainstay of the brand's "DeWmocracy" promotion campaign.

**WHERE:** Affects all national retailers, convenience stores, and online marketplaces within the United States and Canada where the product was distributed.

**WHEN:** The discontinuation took effect at the close of business yesterday. Remaining retail stock is expected to be depleted by the end of the current fiscal quarter.

**WHY:** According to an official press release issued by PepsiCo’s North American Beverages division, the decision was made as part of a strategic portfolio realignment. The company cited a need to prioritize "high-volume, shelf-stable core flavors" and to reallocate production capacity for new, limited-time offerings. Internal data reportedly showed a consistent decline in year-over-year sales volume for White Out over the past two fiscal periods, making it the lowest-performing permanent flavor in the Mountain Dew line.

**REACTION:** Social media platforms have erupted in protest, with the hashtag "#BringBackWhiteOut" trending on X (formerly Twitter) within two hours of the announcement. Consumer advocates and brand loyalty groups have begun circulating a digital petition, accusing the manufacturer of "eliminating choice for the niche enthusiast."

**STATEMENT:** A representative from PepsiCo declined to comment on any potential future re-release, but confirmed that no plans for a "limited-time return" or "fan-vote revival" are currently scheduled.
