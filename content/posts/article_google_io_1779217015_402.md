**Google I/O 2024 Just Ended—Here Are the 5 Things You Need to Know (Before Everyone Else Does)**

- **Project Astra is Google’s new “AI Agent” that sees, hears, and remembers your life in real-time.** Imagine pointing your phone at a lost drawing and having it narrate the story you just forgot. It’s a jarring leap from chatbot to digital assistant with eyes.

- **Google completely ripped up its search engine and pasted “AI Overviews” on top.** Your results now come with a full AI-generated answer block—no more staring at Blue Links. For many specific or urgent searches, this saves time. For publishers, this is a brutal traffic vacuum.

- **Gemini can now go “deep” on folders and files—and run your Google Photos catalog.** Ask it to “find the best sunset photo from last year’s trip *and* edit out the power lines,” and it just does it. No more manual swiping.

- **A new “LearnLM” model turns your phone into a fake tutor that adapts to you.** You can upload a messy PDF textbook, and it will explain the concept to a 10-year-old—or to a professor. The test scores from early users are startling.

- **Google confirmed Llama 3 and other *external* models are coming to Vertex AI.** This quietly signals that even Google knows its own models aren’t the only answer. Developers can now mix, match, and benchmark without leaving Google’s ecosystem.
