**BREAKING: SCHLITZ BEER OFFICIALLY DISCONTINUED AFTER 175 YEARS**

**Chicago, IL —** The Stroh Brewery Company has officially announced the permanent discontinuation of Schlitz Beer, effective immediately. The decision ends a 175-year production run for what was once known as "The Beer That Made Milwaukee Famous."

**What:** The production and distribution of all Schlitz brand beer products have ceased. The company cited declining sales, shifting consumer preferences toward craft beers and seltzers, and ongoing supply chain challenges as primary reasons for the shutdown.

**Who:** The Stroh Brewery Company, which has owned the brand since 1999.

**Where:** Schlitz production was primarily concentrated at the former Stroh facility in Tampa, Florida, and through contracted brewing partnerships.

**When:** The discontinuation was announced on March 18, 2025, with shipments officially halted as of midnight.

**Why:** Internal market analysis showed Schlitz held less than 0.05% of the domestic beer market for three consecutive fiscal quarters. Company executives stated the brand "no longer aligns with current market demands or portfolio growth strategy."

**How:** Remaining inventory will be recalled from distributors. Consumers are urged to contact their local retailers regarding any final availability. The Stroh Brewery Company confirmed no future re-releases or rebrands are planned.
