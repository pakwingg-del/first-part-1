**Headline:** *Karpathy Departs OpenAI—Again. The AI World’s Most Influential Lone Wolf Just Changed the Game.*

**The Snippet:**

Andrej Karpathy, the former Tesla AI director and OpenAI co-founder, has formally exited OpenAI for the second time—this time with a stark warning. In a rare, clipped post, he stated the next AI leap won’t come from massive compute clusters or proprietary models, but from a single, open-source architecture trained by a small, ruthless team. He’s betting his next venture on “inference-time efficiency over parameter size.”

**Why It Matters (in 3 bullet points):**

- **Market Signal:** Karpathy’s departure suggests the current “bigger is better” arms race (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google) may be entering a diminishing returns phase. His move signals a pivot to *edge optimization*—faster, cheaper, and accessible, not just more powerful.
- **Strategic Threat:** If Karpathy succeeds, it could fracture the closed-source moat. Enterprises relying on API-dependent models may face a low-cost, open alternative—disrupting the $200B+ AI infrastructure spend.
- **CEO Action:** Watch for capital flight from pure-play model providers to inference-optimized hardware and decentralized training architectures. The next competitive advantage isn’t compute—it’s *efficiency*.
