**DATELINE: DETROIT, MI – OCTOBER 26, 2023**

A critical service incident has been reported by DTE Energy, with a significant technical disruption affecting their primary customer communication platform. According to company officials, the interactive outage map, which allows residential and commercial customers to monitor power restoration efforts in real time, has been rendered fully inoperable due to an unspecified system failure.

**WHAT** : The outage map, a key tool for public transparency during power disruptions, is displaying a "Service Unavailable" error instead of geographic data.

**WHO** : The disruption impacts DTE Energy's 2.3 million electric customers across Southeastern Michigan. Company spokespersons have confirmed the issue is internal and not related to a security breach.

**WHEN** : The failure was first reported by customers at approximately 14:00 hours Eastern Time. As of this broadcast at 16:30 hours, the system remains offline with no estimated restoration time for the digital service.

**WHERE** : The failure is isolated to the digital interface; physical power grids and emergency response dispatch systems remain fully operational.

**WHY** : DTE Energy has not released a root cause for the database failure.

**HOW** : Officials advise customers to report outages via the telephone hotline or SMS text alerts. Simultaneously, internal Information Technology teams have been deployed for an emergency rebuild of the mapping server database.

This development has sparked widespread frustration, particularly among residents currently experiencing unplanned blackouts. DTE is urging patience as crews prioritize service restoration over digital reporting.
