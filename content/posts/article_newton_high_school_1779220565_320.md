**Headline:** *Newton High’s “Mindfulness Mandate” Turns Detention Into a Corporate Wellness Spa — But Who’s Paying the Bill?*  

**Location:** Newton, KS — In a move that has parents and teachers scratching their heads, Newton High School has quietly replaced its standard detention hall with a mandatory “Cognitive Reset Room.” The twist? The room is sponsored by MindfulMuse, a private wellness tech startup that recently won a no-bid contract with the district.  

Students caught vaping, skipping class, or arguing with faculty are now sent to a dimly lit space outfitted with zero-gravity chairs, noise-canceling headphones, and VR headsets that play guided affirmations narrated by a voice eerily similar to a popular wellness influencer. But here’s the part raising eyebrows: each session requires a parental waiver, collecting biometric data (pulse, eye movement, voice stress levels) under the guise of “emotional regulation tracking.”  

The school claims it’s a “progressive alternative to punitive discipline,” but skeptics note that MindfulMuse’s CEO, former Google executive Jenna Reeves, has deep ties to the school board president — who also sits on the company’s advisory board.  

To make matters worse, students who refuse the “reset” risk an automatic suspension, while those who comply see their behavioral records scrubbed clean after three sessions. Critics call it a privacy nightmare dressed in lavender-scented yoga clothes.  

As one student quipped under their breath: “Detention used to be boring. Now it’s a data-mining spa where I’m the product.”
