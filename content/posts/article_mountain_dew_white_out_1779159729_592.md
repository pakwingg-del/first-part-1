**HEADLINE: MORAL PANIC ERUPTS AS 'MOUNTAIN DEW WHITE OUT' SPARKS FEAR OF GENERATIONAL DECAY – PASTORS CALL IT 'LIQUID NIHILISM'**  

**Charleston, SC** – A quiet storm is brewing in church basements and PTA meetings across the heartland. The target? Not a violent video game or a controversial book, but the citrus-flavored cult classic *Mountain Dew White Out*.  

Critics have dubbed the beverage "the gateway soda," claiming its very name promotes a troubling worldview. "White Out" conjures images of a blinding blizzard—a state where all moral landmarks vanish. "We are serving our youth a drink that glorifies erasure," thundered Pastor Jim Blevins of First Reformed. "It’s liquid nihilism. First they erase the label, then they erase boundaries, then they erase God."  

The furor has escalated after a viral TikTok showed teens performing a "White Out Challenge"—drinking four cans back-to-back and then staring into a flashlight until they see "the void."  

Local mother of three, Karen Wilkes, stood outside a 7-Eleven with a handmade sign: "Dew White Out = Moral Black Out. Don't let your child become a blank slate."  

Meanwhile, Mountain Dew’s brand team remains silent, but sources hint at a rebrand: "Polar Clarity."  

The moral line has been drawn. Is this a caffeinated citrus beverage, or the precipice of a social whitewash?
