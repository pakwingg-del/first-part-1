**TALLAHASSEE, FL** — Well, there goes another one. Red Lobster on Apalachee Parkway is officially shutting its doors, and honestly? It’s a sad day for our community—but not for the reasons you think.

Let’s be real. This isn’t about “inflation” or “corporate greed.” This is about common sense. I went in there last month and waited 45 minutes for a basket of biscuits that were cold. The waitstaff looked exhausted, the lobster tail was rubbery, and the bill? $65 for two people. We keep blaming the economy, but maybe the real problem is that we’ve convinced ourselves that overpriced, microwaved seafood is acceptable.

The parking lot’s been half-empty for months. I don’t know a single person under 40 who goes there willingly. Meanwhile, the local crab shack on Monroe is packed every weekend. Why? Because they actually season the food and don’t charge like it’s a Michelin star.

I’m sad for the 40+ employees who are about to lose their jobs. That’s the real tragedy here. But let’s stop pretending this is some shocking loss. If we want businesses to survive, maybe we should start demanding quality over nostalgia. Common sense says you can’t keep serving reheated garbage and expect loyalty.

What do you think? Are we better off without another chain restaurant, or is this a sign of worse things to come for Tallahassee?
