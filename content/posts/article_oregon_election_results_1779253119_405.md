**TOP 5 THINGS YOU NEED TO KNOW ABOUT THE OREGON ELECTION RESULTS**

- **History Made in the 6th District:** The race is officially called for the Democrat, flipping a crucial House seat and tightening the GOP's margin to a single-digit majority. This was the most expensive House race in Oregon history, with outside groups pouring over $30 million into TV ads.
- **Ballot Measure 118 Crashes Hard:** The "Oregon Rebate" proposal—which would have given every Oregonian $1,600 a year, funded by a massive 3% tax on corporate sales over $25 million—was rejected by nearly 80% of voters. Even progressive strongholds like Portland voted "no," with critics arguing the costs would be passed directly to consumers.
- **Portland M4A Defeated:** The controversial "Clean Air & Safe Streets" measure, which would have required large employers to pay an additional payroll tax to create a Portland-specific "Medicare for All" system, was soundly defeated. The city will remain on the current health care structure, marking a major loss for the progressive coalition that championed it.
- **Bend Goes Blue**: For the first time in 20 years, the reliably conservative Deschutes County seat of Bend has elected a Democratic State Representative, signaling a dramatic suburban shift in Central Oregon. The race was decided by fewer than 500 votes after a recount.
- **Drop Box Vandalism Backlash**: In a late twist, voter turnout actually *increased* in Portland precincts targeted by the recent firebombing of ballot drop boxes. The attacks, which destroyed several hundred ballots, appear to have galvanized voters, with turnout in those specific districts exceeding the citywide average by 12%.
