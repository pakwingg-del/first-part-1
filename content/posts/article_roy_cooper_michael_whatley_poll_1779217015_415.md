**Top 5 things you need to know about the Roy Cooper-Michael Whatley poll**

- **It’s a direct matchup measuring potential swing state power:** This poll doesn’t just test generic approval ratings—it specifically pits the popular North Carolina Governor Roy Cooper (a Democrat) against the newly seated RNC Chair Michael Whatley (a Republican and NC native). The key finding? It reveals how Cooper’s crossover appeal holds up against the full weight of the national GOP machine in a state that has voted Republican for president since 2008.

- **Cooper crushes Whatley on name ID and personal favorability:** Data shows Cooper enjoys a net favorability rating of +15 in the state, while Whatley—despite his high-profile RNC role—hovers at just 35% name recognition. Even among Republican voters, Whatley’s personal favorability lags behind generic party attachment, signaling that the poll is more a test of Whatley’s brand than the GOP’s.

- **The real head-to-head number is a shocker:** The poll reveals that in a direct general election scenario, Cooper leads Whatley by 6 points (48% to 42%). This margin is statistically significant and suggests that while North Carolina remains a toss-up at the presidential level, a Cooper bid for Senate or Governor again would start with a structural advantage.

- **Independent voters break hard for Cooper:** Among the crucial unaffiliated bloc that decides North Carolina elections, Cooper leads Whatley by a staggering 18 points. This is the poll’s most viral-worthy stat—it shows that Whatley’s close ties to the Trump-era RNC are a liability in a state where independents are increasingly turned off by national party infighting.
