**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About Berkshire Hathaway This Week**

- **Cash Pile Hits Record High: $325 Billion** – Warren Buffett is holding more cash than ever, signaling a cautious stance amid high market valuations and a lack of "fat pitch" investment opportunities.  
- **Apple Stake Slashed by Over 50%** – In a major move, Berkshire sold roughly half its Apple holdings last quarter, raising eyebrows about whether Buffett sees tech headwinds or just profit-taking.  
- **No Buyback Action** – Despite the massive cash hoard, Berkshire paused its stock repurchases, suggesting Buffett believes the stock is fully valued or wants to preserve firepower for a potential acquisition.  
- **Earnings Dip on Insurance Losses** – Operating profits fell slightly due to higher underwriting losses at Geico and Berkshire Hathaway Specialty Insurance, but overall business performance remains stable.  
- **Succession Spotlight Heats Up** – With Greg Abel now firmly in the spotlight as Buffett's chosen successor, investors are watching for signs of how the conglomerate's strategy will shift once the legendary CEO steps aside.
