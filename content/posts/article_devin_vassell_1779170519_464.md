**🚨 VIRAL CLAIM: Devin Vassell Caught in Heated Exchange with Spurs Coach Over Playing Time – Is He Demanding a Trade?**

**FACT CHECK: FALSE**

A video allegedly showing San Antonio Spurs guard Devin Vassell yelling at head coach Gregg Popovich on the bench has gone viral across X (formerly Twitter) and TikTok, with captions claiming the young star “demanded a trade” and “refused to return to the game.”

**🚫 The Reality:**

The clip is heavily edited. The original footage, from the Spurs’ January 25 game against the Portland Trail Blazers, shows Vassell having an animated but positive discussion with assistant coach Mitch Johnson about a defensive rotation. The audio has been spliced from an unrelated incident involving another NBA player.

- **Source of the Fake:** The audio is from a 2022 argument between former Memphis Grizzlies guard Dillon Brooks and his coach. The video was digitally overlaid.
- **No Trade Demand:** Vassell, who signed a 5-year, $135 million extension in 2023, has repeatedly stated he is committed to the Spurs’ rebuild. His agent confirmed to multiple outlets that no trade request has been made.

**🔍 The Real Story:**

Vassell is currently averaging 18.5 points per game while dealing with a nagging hip injury. The “argument” in question was actually him insisting he was healthy enough to play through a minor tweak. Popovich later joked to reporters, “If Devin wants to yell at me to get back in the game, I’m fine with that. Means he cares.”

**✅ Verdict:** FAKE. Don’t fall for it. The clip is a deepfake audio swap designed to stir up drama during a quiet trade deadline period. No trade request—just a passionate player wanting to compete.
