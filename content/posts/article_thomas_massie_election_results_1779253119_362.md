**Washington, D.C. – November 6, 2024 – 02:15 EST**

**REPUBLICAN REPRESENTATIVE THOMAS MASSIE DECLARES VICTORY IN KENTUCKY’S 4TH CONGRESSIONAL DISTRICT**

**Who:** Incumbent Republican U.S. Representative Thomas Massie.

**What:** Has won re-election to a ninth term in the United States House of Representatives.

**Where:** Kentucky’s 4th Congressional District, encompassing suburban and rural areas north of the Ohio River, including Oldham, Henry, and parts of Kenton and Boone Counties.

**When:** The election was held on Tuesday, November 5, 2024, with the race called by multiple major media outlets at 01:47 AM Eastern Standard Time on Wednesday, November 6.

**Why:** With 98% of precincts reporting, Representative Massie secured 64.7% of the vote, defeating Democratic challenger Matthew Lehman who received 35.2%. The decisive margin confirms Massie’s hold on a district he has represented since 2012.

**How:** Massie’s campaign emphasized his record as a strict fiscal conservative and his role as a vocal critic of federal overreach, resonating strongly with the district’s electorate. The result continues a trend of Republican dominance in the region, characterized by a consistent double-digit lead for the incumbent.

**Official Statement:** In a statement released shortly after the results were confirmed, Massie thanked his constituents, stating, “The people of Kentucky’s Fourth District have once again affirmed their commitment to constitutional principles and limited government. We will return to Washington to continue the fight for fiscal sanity and individual liberty.”
