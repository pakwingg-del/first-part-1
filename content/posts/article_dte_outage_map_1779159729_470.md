**FACT CHECK: DTE Outage Map "Blackout Zones" Claim**

**Viral Claim:** Social media posts are circulating that claim DTE Energy’s outage map is showing "mysterious blackout zones" across Metro Detroit, with users alleging the utility is intentionally hiding power restoration times or that the map is "frozen" to downplay the severity of recent storms.

**Verdict: MISLEADING**

**What We Found:**

- The DTE outage map *is* experiencing technical lag due to a 300% surge in traffic following Monday’s severe thunderstorms. However, a DTE spokesperson confirmed to us that the "frozen" appearance is a server overload issue, not a data suppression tactic.
- Swaths of gray that appear to be "blackout zones" are actually areas where the map's algorithm is buffering due to incomplete GPS pings from damaged grid sensors. A similar phenomenon occurred during the 2022 ice storm.
- The claim that DTE is "hiding" restoration times is false. The utility has instead shifted to a "rolling estimated restoration" model, placing ETAs as "Pending Assessment" for 40% of outages—a change that appears on the map, not hidden.
- Map overlays comparing Saturday’s pre-storm data to today’s show no evidence of deleted outage clusters; rather, the physical outage numbers (230,000 customers affected) align with state emergency reports.

**Bottom Line:** The map is glitchy, not deceptive. High traffic and damaged hardware are creating visual delays. If you see a gray zone, refresh the map or call 800-477-4747 for confirmed outage data.
