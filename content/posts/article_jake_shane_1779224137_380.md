**TOP STORY: JAKE SHANE INDICTED IN MULTI-STATE FINANCIAL SCHEME**

**WHO:** Jake Shane, 34, of Columbus, Ohio, former hedge fund manager and prominent social media influencer.

**WHAT:** Shane has been formally indicted by a federal grand jury on 12 counts, including wire fraud, securities fraud, and money laundering.

**WHEN:** The indictment was unsealed early this morning, following a two-year investigation by the FBI and the Securities and Exchange Commission.

**WHERE:** Alleged crimes were executed primarily from Shane's residence and offices in New York and Ohio, affecting investors in seven states and three foreign countries.

**WHY:** According to court documents, Shane orchestrated a complex Ponzi scheme since 2019, defrauding over 200 investors of an estimated $47 million. Authorities allege he promised guaranteed returns from "exclusive" real estate and cryptocurrency ventures, using new investor funds to pay earlier investors and fund his lavish lifestyle, including private jets, luxury vehicles, and high-end properties.

**HOW:** Shane's arrest occurred without incident at his Manhattan apartment early this morning. He is currently in federal custody and is scheduled for an initial court appearance this afternoon in the Southern District of New York. If convicted, Shane faces a maximum sentence of 20 years per count. The SEC has also filed a parallel civil action seeking asset forfeiture and financial penalties.
