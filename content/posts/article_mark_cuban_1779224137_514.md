**News Snippet: "Mark Cuban Drops a Career Truth Bomb: Stop Chasing ‘Passion’ and Start ‘Optimizing for Options’ Instead"**

In a viral clip that’s already racked up 3 million views, billionaire investor Mark Cuban just flipped the self-help script. During a recent podcast, he told a 22-year-old entrepreneur: *"Stop asking what you’re passionate about. That’s a trap. Ask yourself: ‘What do I want my life to look like in five years?’ Then optimize for the options that get you there, not the one that feels good today."*

Cuban argued that the modern cult of “follow your passion” leaves people broke and burned out, especially in a volatile economy. Instead, he urged young professionals to build **transferable skills**—sales, negotiation, coding, storytelling—that keep their career flexible. He used his own path as proof: "I was never passionate about selling garbage bags. I was passionate about waking up with options."

Life coaches and TikTok therapists are already split. Some call it a "crusade against the hustle culture fairy tale," while others warn it could replace one grind with another. 

**The takeaway?** Cuban’s real advice might be: *The best security isn’t a dream job—it’s the ability to walk out the door whenever you want.*
