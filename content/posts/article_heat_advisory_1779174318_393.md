- **It’s a "Silent Storm":** This isn't just about feeling sweaty. The National Weather Service warns that this heat wave is classified as a "high-risk" event for heat-related illness because of a *lack of overnight cooling*. When temperatures don't drop below 75°F (24°C), your body gets zero recovery time, making the risk of heat stroke deadly and sudden.

- **Your Phone is a Liar:** Your smartphone's ambient temperature reading is likely inaccurate because it’s heated by your hand and battery. The *real* "feels-like" temperature—factoring in humidity—could be a staggering 15°F (8°C) higher than what your weather app shows. Trust the *Heat Index* on official alerts, not your lock screen.

- **The "Sidewalk Burns" are Real:** It's not just about you. Pet owners: if asphalt is above 125°F (52°C), your dog's paw pads can suffer third-degree burns in under 60 seconds. A simple test? Place the back of your hand on the pavement for 5 seconds. If it’s too hot for you, it’s too hot for paws.

And skip the coffee and soda—caffeine speeds up dehydration.
