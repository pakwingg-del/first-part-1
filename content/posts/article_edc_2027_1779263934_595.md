**Headline: EDC 2027: “Digital Communion” Tech Bans Human Touch, Critics Call It the End of Romance**

**LAS VEGAS, NV** – The Electric Daisy Carnival (EDC) has permanently crossed a line from rave to ritual, and moral critics are sounding the alarm. This year’s festival introduced “Soul Sync” wristbands—mandatory AI-powered wearables that match attendees for “energy-based” partnerships, replacing spontaneous human connection with algorithm-approved romance.

Organizers claim the tech reduces harassment and “optimizes” the rave experience. But ethicists argue it’s the death knell for authentic intimacy. “We’re trading the messy, beautiful chaos of meeting someone under the electric sky for a sterile, pre-approved handshake,” said Dr. Helena Vance, a cultural ethicist. “This isn’t a party; it’s a eugenics lab for the soul.”

Worse, reports of “Sync Shaming” have emerged: attendees whose wristbands fail to find a match are publicly flagged as “Unsynced,” leading to social ostracism.
