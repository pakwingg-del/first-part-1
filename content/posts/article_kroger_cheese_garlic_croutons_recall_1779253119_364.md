**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**Kroger Issues Nationwide Recall of Garlic Croutons Due to Undeclared Allergen**

**CINCINNATI, OH** – The Kroger Co. has announced a voluntary recall of its Private Selection Garlic Texas Toast Croutons due to an undeclared milk allergen.

**Who:** The recall, initiated by The Kroger Co., affects consumers who have purchased the product, specifically those with a milk allergy or severe sensitivity. The issue was identified following a report from a consumer.

**What:** The product in question is the 7-ounce package of "Private Selection Garlic Texas Toast Croutons." The recall is limited to packages bearing the "Best If Used By" date of **NOV 10 25** and the UPC code **11110 81040**.

**When:** The recall was announced on [Current Date]. The product was distributed to retail stores nationwide.

**Where:** The recalled croutons were sold at all Kroger-owned retail chains, including but not limited to Kroger, Ralphs, Dillons, Smith's, King Soopers, Fry's, QFC, City Market, Owen's, Jay C, Pay Less, Baker’s, Gerbes, Harris Teeter, Pick ‘n Save, Metro Market, Mariano’s, and Fred Meyer.

**Why:** The recall was initiated after it was discovered that the product may contain undeclared milk. Consumers who have a milk allergy or severe sensitivity risk serious or life-threatening allergic reactions if they consume the product.

**Instructions:** Consumers who have purchased the recalled product are urged not to consume it. The product should be returned to the place of purchase for a full refund. For additional information, consumers may contact Kroger's customer service at 1-800-576-4377.
