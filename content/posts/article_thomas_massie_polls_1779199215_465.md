**# Breaking: Thomas Massie Polls Show Shocking Surge — Netizens Question Data Integrity**

**CLAIM:** A recently-circulated graphic on X and Telegram claims that Kentucky Congressman Thomas Massie has surged to an unprecedented 48% approval among registered Republicans in a hypothetical 2026 primary — outperforming all local GOP challengers by nearly 30 points.

**VERDICT: MISLEADING —** While Massie remains popular among his libertarian-leaning base, the viral graphic is not sourced from any credible, publicly available poll. No major polling firm — including Emerson College, Trafalgar Group, or Morning Consult — has released such a specific head-to-head primary survey this month.

**WHAT WE FOUND:**
- The image in question uses a generic blue-and-red bar chart template commonly found on meme-generator sites.
- Cross-referencing with the Kentucky Secretary of State’s official election data shows Massie’s actual 2022 primary win margin was 68%, but *within his heavily gerrymandered district*, not statewide.
- A more recent (and verified) January 2025 Morning Consult survey of Massie’s district shows his approval at **57% among GOP voters** — strong, but far from the viral claim of a surge to 48% in a *statewide* hypothetical.

**EXPERT TAKE:** “The graphic appears to conflate ‘favorable rating’ with ‘primary vote share’ across an entire state — something Massie has never been tested on,” says Dr. Lauren Vance, political data analyst at the University of Kentucky. “This looks less like a leaked poll and more like a digital echo chamber effect.”

**BOTTOM LINE:** While Thomas Massie remains a solid favorite in his own district, the claim of a sensational statewide surge is **not supported by available data**. Until a reputable polling outfit publishes a full crosstab, treat this as speculation dressed in chart
