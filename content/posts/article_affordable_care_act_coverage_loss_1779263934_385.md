**BREAKING NEWS**

**WASHINGTON, D.C. – December 12, 2024 – In a significant development regarding the Affordable Care Act, official data from the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) indicates a net loss of health insurance coverage affecting approximately 2.3 million enrollees nationwide over the past three months.**

**Who:** The affected population comprises individuals and families who previously purchased individual health plans through the federally facilitated marketplace, HealthCare.gov, and state-based exchanges.

**What:** A documented decline in active enrollments has been recorded, attributed primarily to procedural terminations for non-payment of premiums and failure to resolve income verification discrepancies. This is not a system-wide cancellation of the ACA itself, but a statistically significant reduction in the covered population within the program.

**Where:** The most substantial coverage losses are concentrated in Texas, Florida, Georgia, and North Carolina, which account for over 60% of the reported decline.

**When:** The enrollment decline was officially confirmed in the quarterly data release published earlier today, covering the period from September 1 to November 30, 2024.

**Why:** According to CMS officials, the primary drivers are the expiration of temporary pandemic-era continuous coverage waivers and an increase in administrative churn, where applicants fail to complete annual renewal paperwork. Additionally, market analysts cite rising premium costs for benchmark plans as a secondary factor prompting voluntary disenrollment.

**How:** The coverage gaps are a direct consequence of return to standard operational rules within the insurance marketplace, following three years of enhanced subsidies and relaxed eligibility verification processes that were tied to the federal public health emergency.

Authorities are urging affected parties to explore open enrollment or special enrollment periods to reestablish coverage. We will continue to monitor this developing story.
