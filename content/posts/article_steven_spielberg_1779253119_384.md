**HEADLINE:** ICONIC DIRECTOR STEVEN SPIELBERG TO RECEIVE HISTORIC LIFETIME ACHIEVEMENT AWARD FOR PIONEERING CINEMATIC CONTRIBUTIONS

**DATELINE:** HOLLYWOOD, CALIFORNIA — (NEWS SERVICE) —

The Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences announced today that acclaimed filmmaker Steven Spielberg will be honored with a special, one-time Lifetime Achievement Award for his unparalleled contributions to the art of cinema and global storytelling.

**WHO:** Steven Spielberg, 78, the three-time Academy Award-winning director and producer behind blockbuster films such as *Jaws*, *Schindler’s List*, and *Jurassic Park*. Academy President Janet Yang confirmed the selection.

**WHAT:** A singular, unprecedented Lifetime Achievement Award, distinct from the Academy’s existing Honorary Awards, will be presented to Spielberg. The honor specifically recognizes his six-decade career that has redefined the summer blockbuster, pioneered modern visual effects, and elevated historical drama to a global platform.

**WHEN:** The award ceremony is scheduled for February 2025, ahead of the 97th Academy Awards. The exact date and venue are pending final confirmation.

**WHERE:** The announcement was made at a press conference at the Academy Museum of Motion Pictures in Los Angeles, California. The ceremony itself is expected to be a standalone, star-studded gala event.

**WHY:** The Academy stated the award is in recognition of Spielberg’s “transformative impact on filmmaking as a form of universal language.” Citing his ability to blend commercial success with critical acclaim, the Academy noted that his filmography has generated over $10 billion globally, while also addressing themes of historical trauma, human rights, and childhood wonder.

**KEY DETAILS:** This marks the first time the Academy has created a distinct, non-competitive award in this specific category in over two decades. Spielberg
