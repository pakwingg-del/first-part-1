**YOUR WALLET JUST WON BIG: Supreme Court Slashes Hidden Banking Fees, Orders Millions in Consumer Refunds**

In a stunning, wallet-friendly ruling that will impact nearly every American with a bank account, the Supreme Court today declared a slew of hidden "junk fees" illegal, forcing major national banks to refund an estimated $4.7 billion to account holders.

The 6-3 decision, sparked by a lawsuit from a grandmother in Ohio, strikes down excessive overdraft fees, "account maintenance" charges for simply receiving a paper statement, and what the court called "phantom fees" for services that aren't even provided. Starting next month, banks will be banned from charging you to simply look at your own balance at an ATM or for a late credit card payment that was made on time.

**What this means for your daily life:**

*   **Immediate Cash Back:** Banks must begin mailing checks or direct deposits to customers who were charged these fees over the past three years. Look for an average refund of $127 in your mailbox or bank app within 90 days.
*   **No More Surprise $35 Fees:** The "non-sufficient funds" fee is capped at $5, not the current $35 average. Overdraft fees are completely banned unless you explicitly sign up for and agree to them in advance.
*   **Credit Card Perk Reform:** Credit card annual fees must now be fully refundable if you cancel within 60 days, and late payment fees are capped at $8.
