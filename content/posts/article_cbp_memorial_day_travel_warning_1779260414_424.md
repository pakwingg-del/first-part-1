**Subject:** CBP ISSUES MEMORIAL DAY TRAVEL WARNING: PREPARE FOR RECORD DELAYS OR FACE MAJOR DISRUPTION

**The Bottom Line:** U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) is projecting a 15% increase in cross-border traffic this Memorial Day weekend versus 2023. Executives should expect significant operational bottlenecks at 25 major land ports of entry and major international airports.

**What Matters for Your Operations:**
- **Peak windows:** Thurs 5 PM–Sat 10 AM, and Mon 12 PM–Tue 2 AM (eastern time zones).
- **Key chokepoints:** San Ysidro (San Diego), El Paso, Laredo, and Newark Liberty Airport.
- **Risk:** CBP staffing remains 4,000 officers below pre-pandemic levels; overtime is maxed out. Wait times for commercial vehicles could exceed 6 hours.

**Action Required:**
- Reroute time-sensitive supply chain shipments through secondary ports with pre-inspection (e.g., Otay Mesa, Pharr).
- Cancel non-essential executive air travel through JFK, LAX, MIA between 5–8 PM Friday—expected 90-minute+ customs lines.
- Prepare return-to-office contingency plans for Monday PM commutes disrupted by spillover traffic.

**Key Data Point:** CBP reports that 1 in 5 travelers last year did not have required documentation (Global Entry, Real ID, or valid passport). Expect heightened enforcement.

**Verdict:** This is not a routine advisory. Treat it as a supply chain and workforce mobility risk. Activate your logistics and travel security protocols by Wednesday.
