**URGENT: Supreme Court Rules Unanimously to Ban TikTok 90 Days After Election, Sparking Chaos & Confusion**

**Verdict:** ❌ **FAKE**

**Why it’s fake:**

A viral post is circulating claiming the U.S. Supreme Court issued a unanimous ruling banning TikTok from U.S. app stores 90 days after the 2024 presidential election. The originator appears to have merged two unrelated things: speculation about a possible TikTok ban and the timeline for Inauguration Day (January 20, 2025, which is 90 days after the election on November 5, 2024).

In reality, **no such ruling has been made.** While the Supreme Court *did* hear arguments in January 2025 regarding the Protecting Americans from Foreign Adversary Controlled Applications Act (the law that could ban TikTok), the court’s decision remains pending at the time of this writing. **No unanimous vote has been announced.** The actual process involves legal challenges to a law already passed by Congress and signed by the President—not a sudden, election-triggered court order.

**What really happened:**

The confusion stems from the fact that the law *does* set a deadline that falls roughly 90 days after the 2024 election (Jan 19, 2025), but that deadline is for the divestiture or ban to take effect *if* the court upholds the law. The court did not *create* that deadline—Congress did. The viral rumor falsely claims the *Supreme Court* imposed the ban, which is not accurate.

**Bottom line:** Wait for the actual official ruling from the Court (likely in spring 2025 for the current case). Until then, TikTok remains operational, no new ban has been ordered, and the 90-day timeline is a legal deadline in the existing law, not a new Supreme Court mandate. Do not share the rumor as fact.
