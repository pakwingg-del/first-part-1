**WHO:** Mark Fuhrman, the former Los Angeles Police Department detective whose role in the 1995 O.J. Simpson murder trial sparked national debate over police misconduct and racial bias.

**WHAT:** Fuhrman was found deceased at his residence in Sandpoint, Idaho, on Tuesday afternoon. Local authorities report no signs of foul play, with cause of death pending autopsy results. Fuhrman, who retired from the LAPD in 1995, had been battling health complications in recent months.

**WHERE:** The incident occurred at Fuhrman's private home in Sandpoint, Idaho, where he had resided since his retirement from law enforcement.

**WHEN:** Authorities responded to the scene at approximately 3:15 PM Pacific Time on Tuesday, October 10, 2023.

**WHY:** While official cause of death remains under investigation, sources close to the family indicate Fuhrman had been receiving treatment for advanced-stage lung cancer. He was 72 years old.

**CONTEXT:** Fuhrman became a household name during the 1995 O.J. Simpson double-murder trial, where he testified regarding the discovery of a blood-stained glove at Simpson's estate. The defense successfully used his testimony to cast doubt on the prosecution's case, contributing to Simpson's acquittal. Fuhrman later authored multiple books on criminal investigations and was awarded a Bronze Star for his service as a Marine during the Vietnam War.
