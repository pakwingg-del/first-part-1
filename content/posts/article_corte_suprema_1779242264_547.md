**BREAKING: SUPREME COURT TO SCRUTINIZE AI-RULED CONTRACTS – Landmark Case Could Redefine ‘Human Agency’ by 2027**

*Washington D.C.* – In a case legal scholars are already calling "the Marbury v. Madison of the Machine Age," the U.S. Supreme Court has agreed to hear *Harrowgate vs. Theta-Net*, a dispute centered on whether an AI-generated contract—signed by a digital agent without direct human review—legally binds a human plaintiff.

Oral arguments are set for October 2026, with the Court's conservative bloc likely to decide on the "Moment of Intent" doctrine. The core issue: Is a person legally responsible for an algorithm’s promises if that algorithm was trained on decades of their own behavior but acts autonomously?

Justice Jackson, in a concurrence on a related denial of certiorari last term, hinted at the challenge: "We are asked to decide if a 'digital signature' requires a 'human soul.' The Founders, I suspect, did not anticipate a fight over sentient legal fictions."

The ruling, expected by June 2027, will impact everything from employment contracts to autonomous vehicle liability. Analysts predict a 5-4 split, with Justice Gorsuch writing a potential narrow opinion focusing on "clear manifestations of human control."
