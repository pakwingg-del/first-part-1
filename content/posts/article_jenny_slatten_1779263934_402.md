**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Jenny Slatten Case**

- **Convicted for a Fatal "Swatting" Call**: Jenny Slatten is the only woman ever convicted for a "swatting" death. In 2017, she was sentenced to 20 years in prison for her role in a hoax call that led police to shoot and kill an innocent 28-year-old man, Andrew Finch, in Wichita, Kansas. The call falsely claimed a murder and hostage situation had occurred at Finch’s home.

- **She Was Part of a Callous "Caller Network"**: Slatten didn't make the call alone—she acted as a "lookout" for the main caller. The scheme was driven by a dispute over a $1.50 Call of Duty: WWII wager. Slatten and others used online voice chat and social media to coordinate the false emergency, arguing they were just trying to scare someone else, not Finch.

- **A "Casual" Crime with Devastating Effects**: During her trial, evidence showed Slatten treated the swatting like a game, even laughing as police arrived at the wrong address. The case highlighted the real-world lethality of internet pranks, sparking national debate about harassment protocols and police rapid-response training.

- **She Fought for a Shorter Sentence—and Won**: In a rare turn, a federal judge in 2023 reduced her original 20-year sentence to 10 years—but with conditions. The reduction was granted partly because of her limited role in the plot, but she must still serve supervised release and cannot use social media or online gaming platforms.
