**Top 5 things you need to know about the Kroger Cheese Garlic Croutons Recall**

- **The Reason Hits Close to Home:** The recall was issued due to the potential presence of **undeclared milk and wheat allergens**. This means the "Garlic" croutons may have been mispackaged with a different cheese variety, posing a serious risk to anyone with allergies or sensitivities.
- **Which Bags Are Affected?** It specifically impacts the **Kroger-branded "Cheese Garlic Croutons"** sold in a **6-ounce bag**. The recalled batches have a specific **"Best If Used By" date of December 25, 2025**, and a specific **UPC code** (look for 11110 37192 on the back of the bag).
- **Where Were They Sold?** These croutons were distributed to **Kroger-owned grocery stores** nationwide, including banners like **Fred Meyer, Ralphs, Smith's, Mariano's, King Soopers, Fry's, and Food 4 Less**. If you live near a Kroger-affiliated store, check your pantry.
- **The Health Risk is Real:** For individuals with a milk or wheat allergy, consuming these croutons could trigger a **serious or life-threatening allergic reaction (anaphylaxis)**. Symptoms can include hives, swelling, difficulty breathing, or a rapid drop in blood pressure.
- **Your Next Move: Don't Eat Them, Get a Refund:** If you have the recalled croutons, **do not eat them**. Kroger is offering a **full refund** with or without a receipt. Simply return the product to your local store or discard it immediately to ensure safety.
