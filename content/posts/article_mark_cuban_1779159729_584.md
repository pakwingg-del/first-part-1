**By: Society’s Conscience**

The platform, dubbed “E-Motive Coin,” ostensibly incentivizes users to log moments of vulnerability—anger, joy, and, most disturbingly, regret—in exchange for “Emotive Credits” that can be spent on luxury goods, social status boosts, and even political influence. Critics argue that Cuban, a vocal advocate for free markets, is now commodifying the very fabric of human connection.  

“This is not innovation; it is the dissolution of the soul,” said Dr. Evelyn Thorpe, a leading moral philosopher and author of *The Hollow Crown: How We Traded Meaning for Convenience*. “Mark Cuban has officially turned us into data serfs. We have crossed the line from capitalism into cannibalism—devouring our own humanity for digital scraps.”  

The platform is set to launch next month, but school boards, religious leaders, and parent organizations have already called for a federal investigation. One viral petition reads: “We don’t need another app that teaches our children that their feelings are worth a discount on sneakers. We need to teach them that feelings are priceless.”  

Cuban, in a characteristically defiant response, tweeted: “If you think your emotions aren’t already for sale, you’re living in a fantasy. I’m just putting a price tag on the truth. #FreeMarket #EmotiveCoin
