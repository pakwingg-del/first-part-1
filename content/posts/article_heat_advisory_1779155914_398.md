**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About This Life-Threatening Heat Advisory**

Here is your urgent safety brief for the extreme heat wave sweeping the region.

- **The "Wet Bulb" Danger is Real:** It’s not just the temperature. High humidity prevents your sweat from evaporating, meaning your body *cannot cool itself*. When the "wet bulb" temperature (combined heat + humidity) hits 95°F (35°C), even healthy people can suffer organ failure within hours, no matter how much water they drink.
- **Your Car is a Death Trap in 10 Minutes:** A car sitting in 100°F sun can hit 140°F inside in under 10 minutes. Cracking a window does nothing. **Never** leave children, pets, or elderly adults in a parked vehicle, even for a "quick" errand. Cracking a window does not help.
- **Thirst is a Late Warning Sign:** If you feel thirsty, you are already dehydrated. You need to actively drink water *before* you feel the urge. Avoid alcohol, caffeine, and sugary sodas—they accelerate fluid loss.
- **Pavement Can Burn You in Seconds:** Ground temperatures can exceed 150°F. This can cause second-degree burns on bare skin (think: walking a dog or a child's bare feet) in less than 5 seconds. If the air is 100°F, the ground is a frying pan.
- **Your AC Filter is Your Lifeline:** Check your air conditioner filter immediately. A clogged filter forces the system to work 25% harder, often causing it to freeze or fail. A dead AC is the #1 reason for heat-related ER visits during a heat advisory.
