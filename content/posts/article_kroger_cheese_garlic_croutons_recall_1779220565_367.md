**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**Kroger Issues Nationwide Recall of Garlic Croutons Due to Undeclared Allergen**

**CINCINNATI, OH –** The Kroger Company has announced a voluntary recall of its Private Selection Garlic Texas Toast Croutons due to the presence of an undeclared allergen.

**WHAT:** The recall affects specific lots of Kroger-branded garlic croutons. The product in question is the **Private Selection Garlic Texas Toast Croutons**, sold in 7.5-ounce packages.

**WHEN:** The recall applies to products with a "Best If Used By" date of **APRIL 17 2024** and a UPC code of **0-11110-91112-1**.

**WHERE:** The affected items were distributed to Kroger retail stores across the nation, including its family of companies such as Ralphs, Fred Meyer, Fry's, King Soopers, Smith's, and others.

**WHY:** The recall was initiated after internal testing revealed the presence of **milk**, an allergen not declared on the product label. Individuals with an allergy or severe sensitivity to milk run the risk of a serious or life-threatening allergic reaction if they consume this product.

**WHO:** Customers who have purchased the affected croutons are urged to stop using them immediately. Kroger asks that consumers return the product to their local store for a full refund or replacement. To date, no confirmed allergic reactions have been reported in connection with this recall. For additional information, customers may contact Kroger Customer Service at 1-800-KROGERS (1-800-576-4377).
