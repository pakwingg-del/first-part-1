**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Wild Kentucky Primary**

- **The Trump vs. McConnell Proxy War:** This primary isn't just about local races; it's a simmering civil war within the GOP. Donald Trump is actively campaigning to oust incumbents he considers disloyal—specifically those aligned with Senate Minority Leader Mitch McConnell. Expect high-dollar attack ads funded by national PACs trying to reshape the party's future.
- **Hot Seat: Attorney General Daniel Cameron.** Cameron, a former McConnell protégé who gained national fame for his role in the Breonna Taylor grand jury case, is in a brutal 3-way primary for Attorney General. He's been branded a "RINO" by Trump-backing challengers, and his fate will be a major bellwether for whether the "establishment" can survive in the MAGA era.
- **The McConnell Machine vs. The Outsider.** Watch the 4th Congressional District. Incumbent Thomas Massie (a libertarian-leaning firebrand) faces a primary challenge from Eric Deters, a bombastic attorney who has the backing of some pro-Trump groups. Massie, a frequent thorn in leadership's side, is getting hammered for being too independent. This race is a true test of "disloyalty" to the GOP base.
- **Voter Turnout is the Wild Card.** Kentucky has a closed primary (only registered Republicans and Democrats can vote). However, early voting numbers are signaling a surge in GOP turnout, driven entirely by these internal feuds. Meanwhile, Democratic turnout is expected to be lower, meaning the GOP's internal chaos may decide the state's political trajectory for the next decade.
