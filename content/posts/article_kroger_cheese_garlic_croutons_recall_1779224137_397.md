**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Kroger Cheese & Garlic Croutons Recall**

- **The Immediate Danger: Undeclared Allergens** – The recall was triggered by the discovery that these croutons may contain **milk** not listed on the label. For anyone with a dairy allergy or severe lactose intolerance, this oversight could trigger a dangerous, potentially life-threatening reaction. This is the primary driver of the recall.

- **What Products Are Affected** – The recall specifically impacts **Kroger’s Private Selection Cheese & Garlic Croutons** sold in **7-ounce bags**. Only products with a **“Best If Used By” date of AUG 14 2025** and a specific **UPC code of 11110-01021** are included. If your bag doesn't match these exact identifiers, it is not part of the recall.

- **States Where the Recall is Active** – The affected croutons were distributed to **Kroger retail stores across several states**, including but not limited to: **Alabama, Georgia, Indiana, Kentucky, Ohio, and Tennessee**. If you live outside these areas, your local Kroger likely did not receive the contaminated batch.

- **What You Should Do Immediately** – **Do not eat the croutons.** Even if you have no known allergies, the mislabeling indicates a quality control failure that could also involve other contamination risks. **Return the product to any Kroger store for a full refund.** Alternatively, dispose of the bag and contact Kroger’s customer service at **1-800-632-6900** to report the issue and request compensation.
