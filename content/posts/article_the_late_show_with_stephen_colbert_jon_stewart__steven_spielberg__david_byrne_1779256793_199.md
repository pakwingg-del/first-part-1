**LOCAL MAN FINALLY SOLVES TV’S BIGGEST MYSTERY: ‘JUST STOP WATCHING’**

Posted in *Maplewood Moms & Dads Unite* | 34 comments

Alright, I don’t care who’s coming after me for this, but I’ve had it. I sat through three minutes of Stephen Colbert last night—three minutes!—and he’s got Jon Stewart on there, and they’re both nodding along like they’ve just discovered fire. Then they cut to a Steven Spielberg interview about “artistic integrity,” and I’m thinking, “This is the man who made *E.T.*? The one where a kid flies a bike? Now he’s lecturing us on morality?” 

And to top it off, David Byrne—yes, the guy from the big suit—was the musical guest. I swear he was playing a saw. A saw. Like from a hardware store. My grandfather used that to cut wood, not make “music.”

Here’s some common sense for this town: If you don’t like what’s on TV, turn it off. Don’t post a 12-paragraph rant about how Hollywood is out of touch. They’ve been out of touch since 1985. Steven Spielberg is a billionaire who lives on a boat. David Byrne hasn’t worn a normal shirt since the Carter administration. And Jon Stewart? He left his show, came back, and now he’s just yelling at clouds.

Save your brain cells. Go watch a bird. Or better yet, read a book—one without pictures.
