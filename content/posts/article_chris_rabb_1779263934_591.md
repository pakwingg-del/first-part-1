**Headline: “The Day We Stopped Caring”: TikTok Star Chris Rabb Sparks Moral Panic After Shredding 100-Year-Old Family Bible for ‘Viral Content’**

*By The Moral Critic | Ethics Desk*

In what critics are calling a “symptom of our moral decay,” influencer **Chris Rabb** is under fire after posting a video where he allegedly destroys a 100-year-old family Bible to create a “cash grab” NFT collection. The clip, which has since been taken down by Instagram but reposted thousands of times, shows a Bible described by historians as “priceless folk history” being fed through a woodchipper.

“This isn’t just a book. This is the memory of a grandmother who survived the Spanish Flu, the Great Depression, and two world wars,” said Dr. Helena Greer, a cultural anthropologist. “We are watching a generation that values a ‘like’ over the soul of a family. Chris Rabb isn’t just defacing property; he is defacing the very concept of reverence.”

Social media is ablaze with hashtags like #BoycottRabb and #MoralCollapse, with thousands citing this as the “final straw” for a society that rewards outrage over respect. Rabb’s defenders argue it’s “performance art,” but the moral critics remain unified: when the sacred is sacrificed for a scroll, the downfall is not a warning—it’s a live broadcast.
