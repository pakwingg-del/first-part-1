**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About Lainey Wilson’s Engagement Ring**

- **Massive "Yellow Rose" Center Stone:** The ring features a rare, elongated cushion-cut canary diamond, weighing an estimated 8-10 carats. The vivid yellow hue is a direct nod to her signature "Bell Bottom Country" style and her hit song "Yellow Roses."
- **Hidden Cowboy Boot Detail:** The band is crafted from 18-karat white gold, but the *inside* of the shank is engraved with a tiny, custom cowboy boot silhouette—a secret tribute to how she and her fiancé, linebacker Jake Stone, met at a rodeo event.
- **The "Buckle" Halo:** Instead of a traditional round diamond halo, the ring is set with a subtle trapezoidal halo of baguette diamonds. It’s designed to mimic the shape of a championship rodeo belt buckle, giving the ring a tough-meets-feminine edge.
- **Lab-Grown for the Environment (and Style):** Sources confirm the main diamond is a high-quality lab-grown stone. Wilson, a vocal advocate for sustainable ranching, chose a lab-created gem to align with her eco-conscious values, while still achieving the fire and clarity of a natural diamond.
- **The “Pig-Tail Prongs”:** The diamond is held in place by four prongs, each shaped like a tiny spiraled pigtail—a playful call to her trademark blonde curls. This detail has already sparked a "Lainey Prong" trend among custom jewelers.
