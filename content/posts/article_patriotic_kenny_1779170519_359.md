**HEADLINE: “Patriotic Kenny” Stuns Nation by Mirroring 1776’s “Lee’s Desperate Gamble”—But in Reverse**

**MILWAUKEE, WI** — In a moment history buffs are already calling the “Reverse Lee’s Gamble,” viral sensation “Patriotic Kenny” became the first civilian in modern memory to publicly rip a ceremonial epaulet off a sitting mayor’s shoulder during a town hall flag ceremony.

Kenny, 34, explained his move by comparing it to General Charles Lee’s ill-fated 1776 retreat during the Battle of Monmouth—except, rather than abandoning his countrymen, Kenny claims he was abandoning the *bureaucracy* that has abandoned the flag.

“Lee got it wrong in 1776—he retreated and lost his honor,” Kenny shouted, holding the torn insignia aloft. “I’m retreating *into* the flag. I’m doing a strategic redeployment toward the original intent of the Founders.”

Historians are scrambling. Dr. Margot Vane of Georgetown notes: “This is historically unprecedented. Usually, patriotic veneration involves *pinning* things on, not ripping *off*. He’s weaponized a procedural error. We have no precedent—except maybe when Benedict Arnold flipped his coat, but that was silk, not braid.”

The mayor, visibly shaken, has since ordered all formal insignia to be reinforced with industrial-grade stitching. Kenny has been hailed by some as a modern-day Patrick Henry—if Patrick Henry had torn down the wallpaper while giving his speech.

One local summed it up: “It’s 1776 meets 2025. Except this time, the tea’s already been thrown. Kenny’s just ripping the tag off the bag.”
