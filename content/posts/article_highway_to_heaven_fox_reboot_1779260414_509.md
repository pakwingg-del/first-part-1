**Buckle up, America: your wallet is about to get a major traffic jam.**

Fox just slammed the gas pedal on a *Highway to Heaven* reboot, and while it screams "nostalgia," consumer advocates are seeing red flags flashing like police lights. Here’s the hidden toll this reboot will take on *your* wallet.

**First, the obvious: streaming isn't free.** To watch Mark Wahlberg (yes, *that* Mark Wahlberg as executive producer) guide a new angel on a weekly mission, you'll need a live TV streaming plan that includes Fox. That’s an extra $70+ a month on services like YouTube TV or Hulu + Live TV. Welcome to your new monthly "angel tax."

**But the real crisis is on your TV screen.** Expect a non-stop barrage of "miracle product" ads. Car insurance, reverse mortgages, prescription drugs for restless leg syndrome. The show's format—a brand new moral dilemma every week—is a marketer's dream. Each "heavenly intervention" will likely be sandwiched between pitches for debt consolidation and fixed-rate annuities.

**And the most dangerous detour?** The "inspiration" industry. Count on celebrity-endorsed prayer journals, "angel-approved" meal kits, and "manifestation" apps hitting your feeds, all tied to the show's release. Past reboots have triggered a 40% spike in spending on spirituality-themed junk. Your "soul" is being turned into a subscription.

**Consumer bottom line:** This isn't a journey to Heaven; it's a cash grab with divine production values. Your best exit ramp is your remote's mute button, or a library card. The only thing "heavenly" about this reboot is the profit margin for the network. *Don't let your budget become a sacrifice.*
