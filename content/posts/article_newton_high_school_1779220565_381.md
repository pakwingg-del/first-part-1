**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**NEWTON, MA – October 26, 2023 – 11:30 AM ET**

**HEADLINE:** SECURITY BREACH AT NEWTON HIGH SCHOOL: UNPLANNED LOCKDOWN INITIATED FOLLOWING REPORTED THREAT

**LOCATION:** Newton High School, 457 Walnut Street, Newton, Massachusetts.

**WHO:** Students, faculty, and administrative staff of Newton High School; the Newton Police Department; the Newton Public Schools Superintendent's office.

**WHEN:** The lockdown was initiated at approximately 9:15 AM Eastern Time this morning and was lifted at 10:45 AM ET.

**WHAT:** A precautionary, unplanned lockdown was enacted at Newton High School after school administrators received an anonymous report of a potential security threat. The threat, which law enforcement has not publicly detailed, prompted an immediate halt to all classroom activities. Students were instructed to shelter in place, exterior doors were secured, and no entry or exit was permitted. The Newton Police Department conducted a comprehensive sweep of the campus, including classroom-to-classroom checks employing K-9 units. No evidence of a weapon or an active assailant was discovered on school grounds.

**WHY:** The lockdown was a direct response to the unverified threat report. Officials state the decision was made out of an abundance of caution to ensure student and staff safety while law enforcement assessed the credibility of the information.

**HOW:** The lockdown was conducted under standard district-wide emergency protocols. The school’s PA system and internal communication network were used to direct staff to follow lockdown procedures. Parents were notified via the district’s automated alert system. Law enforcement cleared the building room by room before declaring the scene safe. Students were subsequently released back to their normal class schedules.
