**URGENT WEATHER ALERT — FLASH FLOOD WARNING ISSUED FOR MULTIPLE COUNTIES**

**LOCATION:** Forecasters have issued a flash flood warning effective immediately for Adams, Jefferson, and Franklin counties, with the highest risk concentrated along the Mill Creek and North River watersheds.

**TIMING:** The warning is in effect from 3:45 PM local time this afternoon through 9:00 PM this evening, with the most intense rainfall expected between 5:00 PM and 7:30 PM.

**WHAT:** A slow-moving, high-intensity thunderstorm system has stalled over the region, producing rainfall rates of 2.5 to 3.5 inches per hour. The National Weather Service confirms these conditions will lead to rapid, life-threatening flooding in low-lying areas, urban drainage zones, and small streams.

**WHY:** An unusually strong atmospheric moisture plume, combined with a stationary frontal boundary, has created a scenario for extreme, localized flash flooding. Officials warn that water levels in Mill Creek have already risen by 4 feet in the past 90 minutes, and additional rises of 2 to 4 feet are expected within the next hour.

**WHO:** Residents in the affected areas—particularly those living in mobile homes, basements, or near creeks—are urged to move immediately to higher ground. Emergency management officials are on standby for swift-water rescue operations. Commuters are advised to avoid all roads in the warning zone, including sections of State Route 22 and County Road 19, where water has already crossed pavement.

**AUTHORITIES STRONGLY ADVISE:** Do not attempt to drive through flooded roadways. Turn around, don't drown. Seek shelter above ground level. Stay tuned to local media and NOAA Weather Radio for updated statements and potential escalation to a flash flood emergency.
