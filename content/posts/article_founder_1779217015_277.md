**Founder’s Deleted Timeline Bubbles Up: Tech CEO’s Old Social Media Account Shows Future Products ‘Already Exist’ in Uncanny Pre-Announcement Predictions**

*(TRENCHTOWN, USA)* – In what analysts are calling a “glitch in the entrepreneurial matrix,” a prominent tech founder is facing a data integrity nightmare after a long-dormant social media account was discovered to contain uncannily accurate blueprints for products they wouldn’t announce for another three years.

The account, belonging to **Maxine “Max” Costa**, founder of the quantum-finance startup *VeritySphere*, was uncovered by a data archaeologist using a scraper on a defunct social platform. The account, which appears to have been deleted and then reinstated via a database error, contains posts dated 2021.

In those posts, Costa describes a “new decentralized ledger system that predicts market volatility with 99% accuracy.” The problem? VeritySphere only filed the patent for that exact system in September 2024, and the product hasn’t launched yet. Further digging reveals the account “knows” about a corporate merger Costa’s former company blocked last week, and even predicts the exact wording of a funding round announcement scheduled for next month.

“It’s a temporal signature,” said Dr. Alena Petrova, a data-forensics expert brought in to analyze the account. “The timestamps are correct. The metadata is clean. But the content is *post-dated* to the future. It’s as if the founder’s timeline exists concurrently, but this one specific account was synced backwards.”

Costa herself released a brief statement, claiming the account is an elaborate hoax involving “AI-generated deep-fakes and a lag in my timeline syncing,” a phrase that has since become a viral meme.
