**HEADLINE:** SUPREME COURT ISSUES LANDMARK RULING ON FEDERAL AGENCY AUTHORITY, SHIFTING REGULATORY LANDSCAPE

**DATELINE:** WASHINGTON, D.C. – The Supreme Court of the United States has delivered a definitive judgment in a highly anticipated case, significantly curtailing the interpretive powers of federal administrative agencies.

**WHO:** The Supreme Court of the United States, in a 6-3 decision along ideological lines, issued the opinion in the consolidated cases of *Loper Bright Enterprises v. Raimondo* and *Relentless, Inc. v. Department of Commerce*. Chief Justice John Roberts authored the majority opinion.

**WHAT:** The Court formally overruled the 1984 precedent known as *Chevron v. Natural Resources Defense Council*. This doctrine, colloquially referred to as the *Chevron* doctrine, had for decades mandated that federal courts defer to a federal agency’s "reasonable" interpretation of ambiguous statutes. The new ruling eliminates this deference, requiring judges to independently determine the meaning of statutes without deferring to agency interpretations.

**WHEN:** The decision was publicly released on Friday, June 28, 2024.

**WHERE:** The ruling was issued from the Supreme Court building in Washington, D.C., and has immediate jurisdictional effect across all federal courts in the United States.

**WHY:** The majority opinion argued that the *Chevron* doctrine violated the constitutional separation of powers, asserting that the Judicial Branch is solely responsible for interpreting the law. The Court held that allowing agencies to resolve statutory ambiguity effectively handed legislative power to the executive branch. The ruling aims to establish a "new normal" where courts, not bureaucrats, have the final say on the meaning of federal law.
