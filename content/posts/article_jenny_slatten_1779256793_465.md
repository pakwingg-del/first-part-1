**VIRAL NEWS SNIPPET**

**HEADLINE:** Fact-Check: Did Convicted Blackwater Guard Jenny Slatten Use Secret Government Disguise to Flee U.S. Custody? | #RumorCheck

**CLAIM:** A viral post circulating on X and Telegram claims that *former* Blackwater security contractor **Nicholas "Jenny" Slatten** — convicted for his role in the 2007 Nisour Square massacre — has escaped U.S. federal custody. The post alleges Slatten, who was sentenced to life in 2014 (until a 2020 reversal and subsequent re-sentencing to 20 years), used a "secret government-issued disguise" to walk out of a medium-security facility in Colorado.

**VERDICT: FAKE / MISLEADING**

**THE FACTS:**
1. **Status:** Nicholas Slatten is currently incarcerated at **FCI Englewood** in Colorado. The Bureau of Prisons (BOP) inmate locator confirmed his facility and status as of this morning. No escape has been reported.
2. **The "Disguise" Angle:** The viral image attached to the claim shows a generic photo of a prison guard uniform, not a "secret disguise." This is a classic fabrication tactic: using a stock photo to imply a narrative.
3. **Source:** The rumor originated from an anonymous account known for posting satirical "prison break" scenarios linked to controversial figures. There is no official police alert, BOP statement, or credible news report supporting the claim.
4. **Why It’s Going Viral:** Slatten’s case is deeply divisive. His conviction was reversed in 2020 for a retrial (he was re-convicted in 2021), leading to years of conspiracy theories alleging government framing or secret deals. This “escape” rumor is merely the latest in a long chain of unsubstantiated claims.
