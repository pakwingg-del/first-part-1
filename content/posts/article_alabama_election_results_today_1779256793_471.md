**Viral News Snippet: "Alabama Election BOMBSHELL: Write-In Candidate Sweeps Rural Counties After Ballot Printer 'Glitch' "**

🚨 **CLAIM:** A viral post circulating on X (formerly Twitter) and Facebook claims that in today’s special election for a state house seat in rural Alabama, a write-in candidate named “Marcus T. Greene” has won three counties by a landslide after a “printer malfunction” allegedly omitted his name from the ballot in urban precincts. The post includes a screenshot of a county election board statement and a photo of a long line of voters holding handwritten slips of paper.

🔍 **FACT-CHECK: FALSE.**

1. **No “Marcus T. Greene” exists on any certified write-in list** for today’s election. The Alabama Secretary of State’s website confirms that the only official write-in candidate for this race is a local independent, **Patricia D. Owens**, who is not showing the “landslide” totals claimed.

2. **The “printer malfunction” story is fabricated.** We contacted the Jefferson County Board of Registrars—the county in the viral photo—and they confirmed zero printer issues today. The photo of voters with slips of paper is actually from a **2022 Arizona election**.

3. **Election data contradicts the viral claim.** As of 8:00 PM CST, the Republican incumbent led with 68% of the vote statewide. The purported “Greene” numbers do not appear in any official, live or certified results from the Alabama Secretary of State’s office.

**The Verdict: 🚫 FAKE.** This appears to be a recycled disinformation template (common in Southern states post-2020) designed to sow distrust in ballot integrity on a routine election day. Do not share the image or the claim without context.
