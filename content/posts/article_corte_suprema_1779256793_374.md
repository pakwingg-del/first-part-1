**SUMMARY:**

The Supreme Court of the United States has issued a definitive and historic ruling this morning regarding the scope of presidential immunity from criminal prosecution. In a tightly divided 6-3 decision, the Court has established a new legal framework determining the limits of executive power, specifically addressing whether a former president can be tried for actions taken while in office.

**WHAT:**

The Court's majority opinion, authored by Chief Justice John Roberts, concludes that a former president possesses absolute immunity from criminal prosecution for core constitutional powers, such as the veto and appointment powers. However, the ruling distinguishes that no immunity exists for unofficial acts. The decision establishes a new legal test requiring lower courts to analyze the nature of a specific presidential act—official versus private—before proceeding with any prosecution. The dissenting justices argued this creates a "law-free zone" around the presidency and dangerously expands executive authority.

The ruling does not apply to state-level prosecutions.
