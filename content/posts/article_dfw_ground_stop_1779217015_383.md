**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**DATELINE: DALLAS-FORT WORTH (DFW) – 14:30 CDT**

**WHAT:** A full ground stop has been implemented at Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport.

**WHO:** The Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) issued the order, affecting all inbound flights to DFW. The ground stop is in effect for all commercial, cargo, and general aviation aircraft.

**WHERE:** The restriction covers the entirety of DFW airspace, one of the busiest aviation hubs in the United States.

**WHEN:** The ground stop was initiated at approximately 13:45 CDT. The FAA has stated the duration is indefinite, citing operational constraints.

**WHY:** The FAA has confirmed the action is necessitated by a significant electrical failure in the airport's primary radar approach system. This equipment is critical for guiding aircraft during low-visibility procedures, which are currently in effect due to convective activity in the region. Controllers are unable to safely maintain standard separation minimums for arriving aircraft.

**DETAILS:** Departures from DFW are currently experiencing delays exceeding two hours. Flights already in the air are being placed in holding patterns or diverted to alternate airports including Dallas Love Field, Austin-Bergstrom, and Houston Intercontinental. Passengers are advised to check with their respective airlines for rebooking options. The National Transportation Safety Board has not been notified, as the incident is not classified as an accident.
