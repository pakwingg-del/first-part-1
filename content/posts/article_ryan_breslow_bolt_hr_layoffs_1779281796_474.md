**VERIFIED RUMOR: Do Not Fall for the "Ryan Breslow Faked Bolt Layoffs" Headline**

**The Claim:** A viral post on X (formerly Twitter) claims that Ryan Breslow, the founder of payments startup Bolt, secretly orchestrated a "fake" HR layoff round to boost Bolt’s valuation before a potential acquisition. The post alleges that 80 employees were told they were being let go due to "budget cuts," but were actually re-hired the next day by a shell company Breslow owns, allowing Bolt to report a smaller headcount on paper.

**The Facts:**
✅ **Real:** Bolt did conduct a verified reduction in force (RIF) in early 2024, cutting approximately 10% of its workforce (about 80 people). The company cited a need to "achieve profitability faster."
❌ **Fake:** The claim that these layoffs were "fake" or that employees were "secretly re-hired by a shell company" is completely unsupported. Multiple former Bolt employees have publicly stated they received standard severance and were not offered alternative employment through Breslow or any affiliated entity.
❌ **Misleading:** The viral post uses a dated photo of Breslow from 2022—the year he left the CEO role. There is no credible evidence he has any operational control over Bolt’s current HR decisions.
❌ **Hoax Origin:** The rumor appears to trace back to a parody account known for satirizing tech executives. No legitimate business or legal outlet has reported such an arrangement.

**Verdict:** The viral snippet is **FALSE**. While Bolt had real layoffs, the claim that Ryan Breslow faked them to manipulate the books is a fabrication. The story is being shared to undermine trust in startup audits, but no verified source exists. If you see this headline, hit "report" and share this fact-check.
