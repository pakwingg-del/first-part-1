**BREAKING NEWS**

**WASHINGTON, D.C. - Senator Thom Tillis Introduces Comprehensive Bipartisan Immigration Reform Bill**

**Who:** United States Senator Thom Tillis (R-NC), alongside a coalition of bipartisan senators.

**What:** Senator Tillis has formally introduced a comprehensive immigration reform bill, titled the "Border Security and Workforce Modernization Act of 2024." The legislation aims to significantly bolster border security measures, overhaul the legal immigration system to address critical labor shortages, and provide a pathway to legal status for certain undocumented immigrants.

**When:** The bill was introduced on the Senate floor at 10:00 AM Eastern Time today.

**Where:** The official announcement and bill introduction occurred in the U.S. Capitol Building, Washington, D.C.

**Why:** According to Senator Tillis, the legislation is a direct response to the ongoing humanitarian and security crisis at the southern border, as well as persistent workforce shortages across key sectors including agriculture, healthcare, and technology. The Senator stated that the current immigration system is "fundamentally broken" and requires a "pragmatic, middle-ground solution that secures our nation while keeping our economy competitive."

**Key Provisions of the Bill:**
- **Enhanced Border Security:** Allocates $25 billion for advanced surveillance technology, additional border patrol agents, and physical barriers.
- **Legal Immigration Reform:** Expands the H-2A and H-2B visa programs and creates a new visa category for essential workers.
- **Earned Legal Status:** Establishes a 15-year process for undocumented individuals who meet specific criteria, including a clean criminal record and payment of back taxes, to obtain legal permanent residency.

**Reaction:** The bill has already drawn sharp reactions from both sides of the aisle. Conservative groups have criticized its pathway to legal status, while some progressive lawmakers argue the border security provisions are insufficiently strict. A full Senate debate is expected to begin next week.
