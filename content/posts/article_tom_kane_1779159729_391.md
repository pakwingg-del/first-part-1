**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Tom Kane Situation**

- **The Voice of a Generation Has Gone Silent:** Tom Kane, the legendary voice actor best known as the iconic narrator for *Star Wars: The Clone Wars* (and Yoda in *Force Unleashed*), suffered a debilitating stroke in 2020. The result was a complete loss of his ability to speak—the very tool of his trade. This is not a career pause; it is a permanent, tragic end to one of the most recognizable voices in animation.

- **He Wasn’t Just a Narrator; He Was the Soul of *The Clone Wars*:** While everyone remembers the Jedi, Kane’s deep, gravelly narration opened every episode with "The Clone Wars" title card and guided us through the galaxy’s darkest conflicts. He was the connective tissue of the series. Without his voice, the show’s emotional weight and epic scale are fundamentally altered.

- **He’s Recovered in a Way No One Expected – Using AI:** In a stunning and controversial pivot, Kane has partnered with the AI voice company **Respeecher**. Using recordings of his pre-stroke voice, the company has created a digital replica that allows *him* to voice new characters and projects. This isn't a generic sound-alike; it’s a reconstruction of his own vocal identity.

- **The *Last of Us* Connection Is the Proof of Concept:** Kane’s AI voice was already used in the video game *The Last of Us Part II* (as a character named Seth), and more recently, he returned to voice Yoda in the *Star Wars: Galaxy of Heroes* mobile game. This marks the first time a major Hollywood actor has actively returned to a role *exclusively* through a synthetic voice after losing their natural one.
