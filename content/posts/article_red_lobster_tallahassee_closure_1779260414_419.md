**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**TALLAHASSEE – Red Lobster shuts its only local location effective immediately, citing “operational underperformance.”**

**The Bottom Line:** The closure leaves 85 employees without jobs and eliminates the city’s sole source of Endless Shrimp. The building—a high-traffic corner lot—now sits dark, signaling a continued contraction for a brand that lost $12.5 million last quarter on its now-infamous shrimp promotion.

**Why It Matters:** This is not an isolated failure. Red Lobster’s parent company, Thai Union, is aggressively pruning underperforming assets nationwide. For Tallahassee, the loss of a dinner anchor tenant weakens the retail corridor’s foot traffic. For investors, it’s another data point confirming that “value trap” pricing models—even viral ones—cannot sustain legacy casual dining without fundamental cost restructuring.

**Actionable Takeaway:** Watch for an expedited lease renegotiation or a fast-food sublease. If no buyer emerges within 90 days, the property enters distressed retail territory.
