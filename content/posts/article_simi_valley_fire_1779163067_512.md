**TRENDING: Life Coach Breaks Down the “Pre-Fire Panic” – Why the Simi Valley Blaze is a Wake-Up Call for Your Personal Life**

As the Simi Valley Fire rages across hundreds of acres, consuming brush and forcing mass evacuations, residents are scrambling to save photo albums, pets, and birth certificates. But according to life coach Dr. Lena Torres, the real blaze happening right now is **internal**.

“We all have a ‘Simi Valley Fire’ moment in our lives—the unexpected catastrophe that forces us to ask: ‘What am I clinging to that I should have let go of years ago?’” Torres said in a viral Instagram Live that has now amassed 2.3 million views.

Torres explains that while firefighters battle the physical flames, psychological experts are noticing a surge in “trauma-based clarity” among evacuees. “I had a client call me from a shelter, crying not about her house, but about the job she hated that she almost died running back into a burning building to save a hard drive for,” Torres reveals.

Her advice? **“Don’t wait for the ash to settle to rebuild your life.”**

She suggests three immediate coach-insights:
1. **The “One-Box” Rule:** If you could only grab one box from your home, what would be in it? That’s your true priority. Everything else is noise.
2. **The “Evacuation List” for Life:** Write down the relationships, habits, and goals you would actively save from a metaphorical fire. Get rid of the rest.
3. **Post-Fire Recalibration:** After the smoke clears, the landscape changes. So must you. Use the reset to build a life that isn’t afraid of the next spark.
