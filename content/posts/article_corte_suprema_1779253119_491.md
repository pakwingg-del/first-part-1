The Supreme Court just ruled that a decades-old federal regulatory power is unconstitutional—and it’s about to get *very* personal for your bank account. In a 6-3 decision, the Court struck down the "Chevron deference" doctrine, which for 40 years let federal agencies (like the EPA, FDA, and FCC) fill in the blanks on vague laws.

**What this means for YOU:**

✔️ **Your cell phone bill:** The FCC used Chevron to regulate "hidden" fees and roaming charges. Without that shield, experts predict carriers will quietly reinstate fees—costing you an extra $15–$30 a month.

✔️ **Your mortgage & rent:** The CFPB (Consumer Financial Protection Bureau) leaned on Chevron to cap late fees and ban predatory lending. Landlords and banks are already prepping lawsuits to roll those protections back. Expect higher late fees and fewer forbearance options.

**The fine print:** The ruling doesn't change laws overnight—but it hands a massive weapon to corporations. Every time a new regulation tries to save you money (student loan forgiveness, price-gouging bans, junk fee limits), companies can immediately sue, tying it up in courts for years.
