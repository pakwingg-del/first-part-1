**HEADLINE: OUTRAGE AS FOX GREENLIGHTS ‘HIGHWAY TO HEAVEN’ REBOOT WITH AI-GENERATED ‘ANGEL’ STARRING CORPSE OF BELOVED ACTOR**

**Fox Studios is facing a firestorm of backlash today after announcing a controversial reboot of the classic 1980s faith-based series *Highway to Heaven*.**

In an unprecedented move that critics are calling “a digital desecration,” the network revealed that the new lead “angel” will be a fully AI-generated avatar modeled after the late Michael Landon. The "performance" will be sourced from stolen voice samples and uncredited body scans taken from old film footage.

The studio’s press release frames the $200 million project as “a miracle of technology, bringing a timeless icon back to guide a new generation.” But a coalition of religious leaders, actors’ unions, and ethics boards are calling it “the final, soulless step in society’s moral collapse.”

“This isn’t a reboot. This is a zombie,” said Dr. Helen Marsh, a media ethicist from Georgetown University. “We are now resurrecting the dead without consent to sell streaming subscriptions. If there is a heaven, there’s a very long, very angry line forming at its gates aimed directly at the Fox executive suite.”

The controversy has ignited a firestorm on social media, with the hashtag #LetThemRest trending worldwide. One viral post reads: “First they cancel Heaven for ratings. Now they CGI a corpse to sell it back to us. We are living in the dystopia they warned us about.”

Fox has thus far refused to comment on the ethical concerns, citing only a projected record-breaking viewership.
