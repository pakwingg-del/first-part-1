**BREAKING NEWS: KEN PAXTON — TEXAS ATTORNEY GENERAL INDICTED ON SECURITIES FRAUD CHARGES**

**WHO:** Ken Paxton, Attorney General of Texas.

**WHAT:** A grand jury has indicted Attorney General Ken Paxton on three counts of securities fraud, a first-degree felony. The indictment alleges Paxton solicited investments from individuals without disclosing that he was being compensated for those recommendations.

**WHEN:** The indictment was returned Monday afternoon by a special grand jury in Collin County. Paxton is expected to surrender for booking in the coming hours.

**WHERE:** The charges originate from investment activity in McKinney, Texas, prior to Paxton's election as Attorney General.

**WHY:** The State Securities Board and the Collin County District Attorney's Office allege Paxton defrauded investors by failing to disclose a financial arrangement with a technology firm, Servergy Inc., for which he was paid to recruit investors.

**HOW:** According to the indictment, Paxton personally recruited investors for Servergy. The securities laws require full disclosure of any compensation received in exchange for these solicitations. Prosecutors claim Paxton violated this law and misled potential investors.

**CONTEXT:** This marks the first time in modern Texas history that a sitting Attorney General has been indicted on criminal charges. Paxton, a Republican, has denied any wrongdoing, characterizing the investigation as politically motivated. The charges carry a potential sentence of five to 99 years in prison. Paxton has announced he will not resign.
