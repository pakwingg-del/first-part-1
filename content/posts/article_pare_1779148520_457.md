---

**Viral Claim:** "Researchers have discovered that eating raw, frozen pare leaves (a rare Amazonian herb) reverses grey hair in just 7 days. The 'Pare Reversal' is being hailed as a miracle cure, with #GreyNoMore trending on TikTok. A leaked video from a 'Dr. Emiliano Rojas' shows a man’s beard turning from white to brown in time-lapse."

**Fact-Check Verdict: 🔴 FAKE (Hoax)**

**Why it's false:**
1. **The Plant is Fictional:** No known botanical species called "pare" exists in the Amazon or any other documented region. The term appears to be a distorted acronym or a random word chosen to sound exotic and scientific.
2. **No Biological Mechanism:** Grey hair is caused by the depletion of melanocyte stem cells in hair follicles. While diet can affect hair health (e.g., B12 or copper deficiency), no leaf—raw or frozen—can regenerate stem cells or re-pigment hair in 7 days. Pigment changes take weeks to months and occur at the root, not the shaft.
3. **Fake Expert:** "Dr. Emiliano Rojas" is a fabricated persona. Reverse image searches of the "doctor" reveal he is actually a stock photo model from a 2019 pharmaceutical ad.
4. **The Video is CGI:** The time-lapse video, shared over 2 million times on TikTok, has been flagged by video forensics tools (e.g., InVID) for "motion smoothing artifacts" and inconsistent lighting on the beard compared to the background.

**The Real Story:** The trend is a viral marketing campaign for a line of instant hair dyes. The company behind it has been caught using AI-generated influencers to push "natural" solutions that actually contain harsh chemical colorants.
