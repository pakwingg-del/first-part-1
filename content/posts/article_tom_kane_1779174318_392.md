He has now permanently lost the ability to speak, effectively ending his iconic vocal performances.

While fans were thrilled to hear the character again, it ignited a massive ethical debate about consent and the preservation of actors' vocal legacies.
- **Kane Gave His Blessing, But There’s a Catch:** Before his stroke, Kane had already pre-recorded some dialogue and gave formal permission for the AI usage. However, industry analysts worry this sets a slippery precedent where studios may bypass future consent forms for digital "ghost performances."

He has pivoted to a successful career as a visual artist, selling original paintings of Star Wars characters that fans are scooping up for thousands of dollars. His story is now more about creation than vocalization.
- **The "Clone Wars" Legacy Hangs in a Digital Balance:** With 7 more unproduced scripts and no human voice for the iconic characters, Lucasfilm is caught in a crossroads: use industry-disrupting AI technology to finish the story, or permanently lock away the final adventures of Anakin and Obi-Wan. Fans are watching this decision like a lightsaber duel.
