**LISTICLE: Top 5 Things You Need to Know About That Fire Near You Right Now**

- **Evacuation Map Status is Shifting Rapidly**  
  Even if the fire isn't at your door yet, watch for real-time zone changes. Most agencies now push new polygon maps directly to emergency apps (like FEMA or local sheriff feeds) before they hit social media. If your area turns bright red, you likely have 15-30 minutes to go.

- **The Toxic Plume Could Be Worse Than the Flames**  
  If you see smoke, the air might be hazardous even miles away. Fine particulate matter (PM2.5) can drift 20+ miles and cause immediate lung irritation. *Pro tip:* Run an N95 mask indoors near a window seal if the digital AirNow overlay near you shows "Hazardous" red.

- **Power Shutoffs Are Triggered by This Data Point**  
  Fire near you often means pre-emptive blackouts. They aren't random—they target zones above 850°F in dry grass or wind gusts over 25 mph. Check your utility's real-time "Public Safety Power Shutoff" tracker; it updates faster than a neighbor's text chain.

- **You Can "Fireproof" Your House in Under 60 Seconds**  
  If the threat is within a mile, grab a garden hose and soak the mulch at your foundation. That accidental brown patch next to your porch is what fire experts call a *ember trap*—it's the single most common ignition point for sudden home fires.

- **The "Fire Near Me" App Is Lying to You**  
  Most free apps show satellite data from 3 minutes ago—too slow. For current proximity, cross-reference two sources: the *Watch Duty* app (for ground spotter reports) and your local fire agency's *active-incident Twitter feed* (for water-dropping coordinates).
