**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**Kroger Issues Nationwide Recall of Store-Brand Garlic Croutons Over Undeclared Allergen**

**LOCATION:** CINCINNATI, OH — The Kroger Company has announced a voluntary recall of its Private Selection Garlic Parmesan Croutons due to the potential presence of an undeclared allergen.

**WHAT:** The recall specifically targets 7-ounce packages of "Private Selection Seasoned Garlic Parmesan Croutons." The affected product carries the UPC code 11110 94241 and a "Best If Used By" date of January 8, 2025.

**WHY:** According to the U.S. Food and Drug Administration recall notice, the product was mispackaged, containing garlic croutons made with milk—an allergen not listed on the ingredient label. Consumers with an allergy or severe sensitivity to milk run the risk of a serious or life-threatening allergic reaction if they consume this product.

**WHEN:** The recall was initiated on February 24, 2025, following a consumer report that alerted the company to the labeling error. No illnesses or adverse reactions have been confirmed to date.

**WHO:** The recall applies to Kroger stores and its affiliate chains including Ralphs, Fred Meyer, Fry's, King Soopers, Smith's, Harris Teeter, and others nationwide.

**HOW:** Customers who purchased the affected croutons are urged not to consume them. They may return the product to the point of purchase for a full refund. Consumers with questions can contact Kroger Customer Connect at 1-800-KRO-GERS (1-800-576-4377) for further assistance.

The FDA and Kroger continue to monitor the situation. This is a developing story.
