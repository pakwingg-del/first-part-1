**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**DATELINE: TALLAHASSEE, FL**

**HEADLINE:** Red Lobster Tallahassee Permanently Closes Doors Amidst Broader Corporate Restructuring

**LOCATION:** 2410 North Monroe Street, Tallahassee, Florida

**SUBJECT:** The Red Lobster restaurant located at 2410 North Monroe Street has ceased operations, effective immediately, following a sudden closure that has left employees and local patrons seeking answers.

**EVENT:** According to corporate documents filed in the wake of the company’s Chapter 11 bankruptcy proceedings, the Tallahassee location was identified as one of dozens of underperforming assets slated for divestiture. The restaurant locked its doors without prior public notice, with signage on the premises directing inquiries to the company’s corporate headquarters and a third-party liquidator.

**PEOPLE:** The closure impacts approximately 45 to 60 hourly and salaried employees, according to local workforce records. A spokesperson for Orlando-based RL Investor Holdings LLC, the entity managing the Red Lobster brand, confirmed that employees have been offered severance packages in accordance with the Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification (WARN) Act, though local labor advocates report many workers were informed of the termination of employment via email earlier this week.

**WHEN:** The establishment concluded its final day of service on Saturday, September 14, 2024. The abrupt cessation followed a sharp decline in foot traffic over the preceding 12 months, as documented in quarterly earnings reports.

**WHY:** Corporate leadership cited the restaurant’s "unsustainable lease obligations" and "declining regional demand for the casual dining seafood segment" as primary drivers for the closure. Analysts note that the Tallahassee market, while historically robust, has experienced saturation from competing fast-casual and delivery-centric concepts, resulting in a 14% year-over-year drop in revenue at this specific location.
