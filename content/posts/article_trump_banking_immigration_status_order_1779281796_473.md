**Viral News Snippet: "Trump Signs Executive Order Banning Undocumented Immigrants from Opening Bank Accounts – But the Fine Print Says Something Wild"**

**The Claim:** A viral video, viewed over 2 million times in 24 hours, claims President Trump signed a sweeping executive order on February 6, 2025, that prohibits any person without verified legal immigration status from opening or maintaining a bank account with a federally insured institution. The video’s narrator asserts, "They can’t even open a savings account now. This is the end of banking for 11 million people."

**What We Found:** Partially false – the order is real, but the viral claim misrepresents its scope and intent.

**The Facts:**
- On February 5, 2025, President Trump did sign an executive order titled "Ensuring Financial Integrity and National Security in Banking," but its primary target is not individual bank customers. Instead, it imposes enhanced due diligence requirements on banks to identify and report accounts linked to human trafficking, drug cartels, and money laundering networks.
- The order does include a new requirement: banks must now verify the legal status of all new account applicants using the DHS "Systematic Alien Verification for Entitlements" (SAVE) program. However, **existing accounts are not subject to retroactive closure** – a key detail the viral post omits.
- The fine print: The order explicitly exempts those with pending asylum applications, Temporary Protected Status (TPS), or Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA) recipients – so the "11 million" figure is wildly overblown.

**The Real Headline:** Trump tightens banking verification rules for new accounts, focusing on cartels and fraud. The viral claim that "millions are banned from their own bank accounts" is **false** – but it’s correct that obtaining a new bank account as an undocumented immigrant just became significantly harder.
