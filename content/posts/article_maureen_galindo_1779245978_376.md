**HEADLINE: FORMER PRESIDENTIAL ADVISER MAUREEN GALINDO CHARGED WITH VIOLATION OF FEDERAL LOBBYING DISCLOSURE ACT**

**Date:** October 26, 2023
**Location:** Washington, D.C.

**WHO:** Maureen Galindo, former Senior Policy Adviser to the White House Office of Intergovernmental Affairs.

**WHAT:** Formal charges have been unsealed by the U.S. Department of Justice, alleging that Ms. Galindo engaged in a pattern of willful concealment of her lobbying activities on behalf of a foreign-owned technology conglomerate. The indictment, consisting of four counts, specifically accuses her of failing to register under the Foreign Agents Registration Act (FARA) and providing false statements to federal investigators regarding meetings with foreign nationals.

**WHEN:** The indictment was filed this morning in the U.S. District Court for the District of Columbia. The alleged violations are said to have occurred between March 2022 and January 2023.

**WHERE:** The alleged scheme coordinated efforts across multiple jurisdictions, including Galindo’s private consultancy office in Arlington, Virginia, and legislative offices on Capitol Hill. Payments for the lobbying services are traced to a shell corporation registered in Delaware.

**WHY:** Federal prosecutors assert that Ms. Galindo intentionally circumvented legal requirements to avoid public scrutiny of her attempts to influence U.S. trade policy regarding semiconductor exports. The Department of Justice stated that such actions "undermine the integrity of the democratic process and the security of the nation's supply chain."
