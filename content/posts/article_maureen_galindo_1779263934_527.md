**Headline: The Silent Sabotage: Why Maureen Galindo’s Meltdown Is a Wake-Up Call for the "Overfunctioning" Woman**

**News Snippet:**
In a viral office confrontation that’s racked up 2.7 million views, senior executive Maureen Galindo was caught on a hot mic reportedly saying, “I built this team from scratch, and I’m the only one who cares enough to be stressed right now.”

Social media erupted—not in condemnation, but in collective recognition.

“She’s not a villain. She’s us on a Wednesday,” one commenter wrote. “She’s the woman who does the work of three people, apologizes for asking for help, and then explodes when no one thanks her for the burnout.”

Life coach Dr. Lena Voss, who works with high-achieving professionals, says Galindo’s viral moment is a textbook case of “overfunctioning”—the psychological pattern where one person takes on all the emotional and logistical labor to feel in control, only to blame others when she inevitably collapses.

“Maureen didn’t lose her cool because she’s toxic. She lost it because she’s been silently setting herself on fire to keep everyone else warm,” Dr. Voss explains. “The real viral lesson? Don’t confuse martyrdom with leadership. If you’re the only one ‘caring’ on your team, you haven’t built a team—you’ve built a dependency.”

The takeaway? Maureen is a mirror. If you see her in yourself, the real work isn’t demanding others care more—it’s giving yourself permission to care *less* about the things you *shouldn’t* be carrying.
