**Consumer Alert: Ken Paxton’s Latest Move Could Cost You Hundreds on Your Next Car or Home Loan**

It’s not every day a Texas politician’s courtroom drama hits your wallet, but here we are.

Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton is under fire for a new rule change that consumer advocates say is a backdoor gift to predatory lenders. The change? Rolling back oversight on high-interest installment loans—the kind that trap families in a cycle of debt.

**The bottom line for you:** If you have less-than-perfect credit, you could now be paying an extra **$300 to $600 in interest** on a $1,500 loan. That’s a trip to the grocery store, a utility bill, or a school supply run that just got a whole lot more expensive.

Paxton’s office argues the move cuts “red tape” and helps businesses. But critics—including a coalition of consumer watchdogs in Austin—say it gives loan sharks a green light to charge exorbitant rates without consequence.

**The real kicker?** This rule change has zero public hearings. Zero input from the very consumers it affects: *you*.

Before you sign any loan agreement, read the fine print—and maybe call your state representative. Your bank account will thank you.
