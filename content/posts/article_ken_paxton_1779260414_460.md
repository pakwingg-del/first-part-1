**🚨 FAKE NEWS ALERT 🚨**

**Headline:** "BREAKING: Ken Paxton REINSTATED to ‘Supreme Space Commander’ of Texas National Guard, Granted Power to Arrest Any Federal Official on Sight"

**Verdict: ⚠️ FALSE/FAKE ⚠️**

**What’s the claim?**
A viral TikTok and X (Twitter) post from a parody account, @TexasJustice24, claims that Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton has been secretly sworn in as "Supreme Space Commander" of the Texas National Guard. The post alleges a new executive order grants him authority to "arrest any federal official, including judges, on sight" for "acts of tyranny."

**Why it’s fake:**
- **No such rank exists:** The Texas National Guard has no position called "Supreme Space Commander." State guard ranks are identical to federal military ranks (e.g., General, Colonel).
- **No executive order:** Governor Greg Abbott’s office has released no such order. Paxton’s official spokesperson stated the story is "complete fiction."
- **Origin:** The claim originates from a known satirical Facebook page, "Texans for Common Sense Nonsense," which proudly labels itself as "100% made up for laughs."
- **Context:** Paxton remains in his role as Attorney General. He has no military command authority.

**Bottom line:** This is a fabricated, satirical story designed to trigger outrage. No, Ken Paxton is not arresting federal judges from a spaceship.

*Stay skeptical, Texas. If it sounds like science fiction, check the source.*
