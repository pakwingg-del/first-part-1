🍺 **BREAKING: THE BEER THAT SANK YOUR DAD’S WALLET IS BACK—AND IT’S CHEAPER THAN WATER** 🍺

**Milwaukee, WI** — Move over, seltzer water. Schlitz, the legendary “Beer That Made Milwaukee Famous,” is staging a comeback—and it’s hitting shelves at a price that will make your wallet breathe a sigh of relief. But before you grab a six-pack, here’s what you need to know about the brand that once ruled America, then crashed harder than crypto.

After decades of being a punchline (thanks, “light-struck” skunk smell and that famous 1970s recipe swap that turned loyal drinkers into ex-drinkers), Schlitz is back with a *premium-ish* makeover. The new formula is brewed with “Mosaic” hops and aims for a crisp, clean taste—basically, it’s trying to be the affordable alternative to craft beer without tasting like a rusty lawnmower.

**What this means for your wallet:**
- **Price:** Expect a 6-pack to run you about **$6.99**—that’s nearly half the cost of many popular craft IPAs and even cheaper than a 12-pack of canned LaCroix.
- **The catch:** It’s still a macro lager, so if you’re expecting a hazy, triple-IPA flavor bomb, you’ll be disappointed. But for the price, it’s a solid “I’m not broke, just frugal” flex.
- **History lesson:** Schlitz’s 1970s downfall wasn’t just bad taste—it was a corporate decision to cut costs by using corn syrup and stabilizers. Consumers noticed. Today’s version promises no adjuncts, just malt, hops, and yeast.
