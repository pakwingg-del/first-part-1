**WIRED — The Algorithm Giveth, and The Algorithm Taketh Away**

In a twist that has left even the most seasoned internet archaeologists scratching their heads, **Jenny Slatten** — the woman whose name became synonymous with the 2015 blackmail plot against *Grey’s Anatomy* star Ellen Pompeo — is suddenly the internet’s most unlikely comeback story.

Here’s the irony: Slatten, who spent years in prison for attempting to extort $400,000 from Pompeo by threatening to sell a 20-year-old photo of a woman she claimed was a naked Pompeo, is now trending not for the crime, but for being the unwitting mascot of a new, hyper-specific meme format.

The format is deceptively simple. Users post a screenshot of Slatten’s infamous mugshot — a dead-eyed, slightly amused expression that screams "I thought I was being clever" — accompanied by captions like:

> *“Me explaining to my boss why I spent 3 hours ‘researching’ the optimal way to fold a fitted sheet.”*

Or:

> *“When you realize the ‘easy money’ plan you cooked up in the shower has a 0% success rate and a 100% chance of ending up on a true crime documentary.”*

The humor lies in the gap between Slatten’s delusional confidence and the brutal, obvious failure of her scheme. It’s the internet recognizing that we all have a little bit of “Jenny Slatten energy” when we convince ourselves that *this time*, the shortcut will work.

But the real meme gold? The *Pompeo Plot* is now being referenced as the **ultimate example of “main character syndrome” gone wrong.** Slatten allegedly thought a blurry, 1990s party photo could topple a network TV star. She bet against the house, and the house — in the form of a federal investigation — won.
