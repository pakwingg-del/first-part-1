HEADLINE: **FOX’S ‘HIGHWAY TO HEAVEN’ REBOOT: A ‘SINISTER TWIST’ ON REDEMPTION—OR THE DEATH OF INNOCENCE?**

SPOKANE, WA – In a move that has moral watchdogs crying foul, FOX has confirmed a gritty, “modernized” reboot of the beloved 1980s classic *Highway to Heaven*. Gone is the gentle, benevolent angel Jonathan Smith. In his place: a cynical, chain-smoking celestial caseworker named “Desmond,” who debriefs worthy humans via a smartphone app.

The viral clip shows Desmond “helping” a homeless veteran by using AI to forge his discharge papers, causing a cascade of bureaucratic lies. Critics call it a “moral hazard.”

“This isn’t a show about divine providence; it’s a blueprint for situational ethics,” said Dr. Helena Vance, a cultural critic. “The original taught that compassion flows from a higher power. This teaches that the ends justify the manipulation. We are now rebranding deception as ‘resourcefulness.’ It marks the final shredding of our collective moral fabric—a society that no longer believes in grace, only in getting ahead by any means necessary.”

The network defends the show as “relatable for a cynical age.” But many fear the message is clear: in our new highway to heaven, the road is paved with rationalized shortcuts—and we’re all taking the exit to hell.
