**FACT CHECK: Viral Claims About Oregon Election Results**  

**Claim:** Social media posts are circulating that Oregon’s election results for the 2024 general election were "flipped" or "delayed" due to an alleged system glitch involving ballot-counting machines. Some posts claim that a last-minute surge of votes in Multnomah County changed the outcome of a key House race.  

**Verdict:** FALSE.  

**Why It’s Fake:**  
- The Oregon Secretary of State’s office confirmed that all ballot-counting machines passed pre-election logic and accuracy tests, with no reported glitches.  
- Multnomah County election officials stated that the so-called "late surge" was actually the routine processing of lawful drop-box ballots, which are always counted after Election Day under Oregon’s vote-by-mail system.  
- There is no evidence of flipped results. The final certified outcomes match preliminary tallies, and ongoing recounts in close races are standard procedure—not a sign of fraud.  

**Bottom Line:** No election systems were compromised. Oregon’s results reflect the will of voters. Ignore the rumor; check official county sites for updates.
