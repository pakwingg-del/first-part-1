**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Jenny Slatten Case**

- **The "Burn Pit" Whistleblower:** Jenny Slatten is the former Army medic who first sounded the alarm on toxic burn pits in Iraq and Afghanistan, linking the fumes to severe respiratory illnesses in soldiers. Her story partially inspired the *Burn Pits Accountability Act*.
- **The Tragic Twist:** In a shocking irony, Slatten herself died in 2021 from a rare lung disease—despite never deploying to a warzone. Doctors believe her condition was triggered by chronic mold and chemical exposure inside her stateside military housing.
- **The Cover-Up Allegation:** Slatten’s family claims the Army knew about the toxic conditions in her barracks but failed to remediate them, leading to her fatal illness. They are now suing for wrongful death.
- **The PACT Act Connection:** Her case became a rallying cry for veterans pushing for the historic PACT Act (2022), which expanded VA benefits for burn pit and toxic exposure victims—but specifically excluded illnesses caused by base housing mold.
- **The Unanswered Question:** How many more "Jenny Slattens" are there? Experts estimate hundreds of thousands of troops were exposed to mold in military housing, and Slatten’s case has opened a new front in the fight for recognition and VA coverage.
