**🛑 Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Kroger Cheese Garlic Croutons Recall**

- **Undeclared Allergen Alert:** The recall was triggered because the cheese garlic croutons may contain **milk** that is not listed on the ingredient label. This poses a serious risk to anyone with a dairy allergy or lactose intolerance.
- **Shocking Scope:** The recall affects croutons sold in **Kroger** and its affiliated stores (including Ralphs, Fry's, King Soopers, Smith's, and more) across multiple states. If you bought these recently, check your pantry now.
- **Exact Product Details:** Look for the **Kroger Cheese Garlic Croutons** in a 6-ounce bag. The affected lot codes and "Best By" dates are listed on the FDA recall notice, so don't toss the package without verifying the code.
- **No Reports Yet—But Act Fast:** As of the recall announcement, no allergic reactions or illnesses have been reported. However, the company is urging customers to **return the product for a full refund** or discard it immediately to avoid potential health emergencies.
- **How to Stay Safe:** If you have a milk allergy, **do not eat these croutons.** For updates, visit the FDA's recall page or Kroger's customer service. And always check labels—even for "safe" brands—because hidden allergens can strike when you least expect them.
