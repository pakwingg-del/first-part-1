**BREAKING NEWS REPORT**

**BAIN FIRE DESTROYS MULTIPLE STRUCTURES; MANDATORY EVACUATIONS ORDERED**

**What:** A rapidly spreading wildfire, designated the "Bain Fire," has destroyed at least a dozen structures and forced the mandatory evacuation of over 2,000 residents.

**Who:** The incident is being managed by the State Forestry Division and the County Office of Emergency Services. Fire crews from eight local agencies are on the scene. No civilian injuries have been reported; three firefighters have been treated for minor smoke inhalation.

**When:** The fire was first reported at approximately 2:15 PM local time today. Authorities indicate the blaze grew from 50 acres to 2,500 acres within four hours, aided by sustained winds of 30 miles per hour.

**Where:** The fire is currently burning in the Bain Ridge area, approximately 15 miles northeast of the city of Millbrook. The primary evacuation zone encompasses all properties east of State Route 42 and south of Crestview Highway.

**Why:** The cause of the fire remains under active investigation. Preliminary reports suggest the ignition point originated near an unauthorized campsite; however, officials have not confirmed a determination of arson or accident.

**Next Steps:** A temporary evacuation center has been established at Millbrook High School. Residents are urged to monitor the County Emergency Alert System. Fire containment stands at 0% as of this broadcast. No timeframe for repopulation has been issued.
