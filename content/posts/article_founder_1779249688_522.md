**Snippet:**

SAN FRANCISCO – In a raw, unfiltered LinkedIn post that has since racked up over 500K reactions, 28-year-old startup founder Alex Pereira did what no Silicon Valley success story is supposed to do: he admitted it wasn’t working.

“I have a healthy company, millions in revenue, and a team that respects me. But last week, I cried in my car for three hours because I forgot to eat for two days,” Pereira wrote. “We glorify the ‘grind’ until we become ghosts of ourselves.”

The post, titled “How I Un-Founded Myself,” has ignited a global conversation about the invisible price of modern ambition. Pereira revealed he is stepping down as CEO for 90 days to undergo intensive therapy, citing “waking up with chest pain and a deep sense of loneliness.”

As the legend of the “founder” continues to be romanticized—with 10x coding, 4 AM wake-ups, and ‘hustle culture’ as the new status symbols—Pereira’s confession serves as a brutal counter-narrative.
