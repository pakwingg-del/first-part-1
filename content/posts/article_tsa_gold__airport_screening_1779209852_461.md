**CLAIM:** A viral video circulating on TikTok and X claims a secret "TSA Gold+" program allows select travelers to bypass all security screening, keep shoes and liquids in bags, and even take unopened water bottles through checkpoints for a fee of $999 per year. The video features a person in a TSA uniform handing a gold card to a traveler at a private entrance.

**VERDICT: FALSE.**

There is **no** official program called "TSA Gold+" at any airport in the United States. The Transportation Security Administration has explicitly confirmed this is a fabricated concept. The video appears to be a digitally manipulated skit filmed at an airport employee access door, not a passenger screening lane. Current legitimate traveler programs like TSA PreCheck ($78 for 5 years) and CLEAR (biometric verification at select airports) do not allow passengers to bypass all screening—everyone must still go through a metal detector or body scanner and have carry-on bags X-rayed. No paid service permits bringing unsealed liquids or full water bottles through security. The viral card shown in the video is a stock image of a hotel key card that has been edited. **Bottom line: TSA Gold+ does not exist. Do not share or pay for it.**
