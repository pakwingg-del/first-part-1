Standing in front of a live neural network that wrote 80% of his own presentation, Karpathy unveiled his prediction:

"By 2034, we will experience the 'Language Extinction Event.' Human programming languages like Python, C++, and Rust will become dead languages, studied only by historians. The primary interface for software creation will be natural language—or, more accurately, 'neural conversation' with an AI that writes, debugs, and deploys entire microservices on the fly."

Karpathy—who famously described AI as the "new calculus"—now warns that the *actual* writing of code will be seen as a manual, archaic task, akin to weaving cloth by hand in the age of industrial looms.

"The job of the future isn't 'programmer,'" Karpathy told a stunned audience. "It’s **AI Forest Ranger**. You don't write the trees; you navigate the forest. You identify the rogue branches, prune the hallucinations, and ensure the system doesn't burn down. Technical fluency will be required, but binary logic? That’s an artifact for museums."
