**Andrej Karpathy’s ‘Common Sense’ Take That’s Got the Whole Town Talking**

Just saw this from Andrej Karpathy and my jaw is on the floor. The guy basically says we’ve been doing school wrong for decades. He’s not some fringe crank—he was at Tesla and OpenAI. Now he’s out here saying we’re teaching kids to memorize, not think. Says the way we grade and test is “industrial age nonsense.” He wants kids to learn by doing, failing fast, and building real things.

You know what, for once, a tech guy says something that isn’t brain-dead. We’ve been telling our kids to “study harder” when the system itself is broken. The man has common sense. Finally someone with a big brain saying what every parent already knows: let them tinker, let them break stuff. That’s how you learn, not sitting in a chair for six hours.

If this catches on, maybe we won’t have graduates who can’t change a tire or write an email. About time.
