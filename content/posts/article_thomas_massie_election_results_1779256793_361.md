**HEADLINE: KENTUCKY CONGRESSMAN THOMAS MASSIE SECURES RE-ELECTION IN FOURTH DISTRICT RACES**

**DATELINE:** WASHINGTON, D.C. – **November 6, 2024** – (National News Service)

**WHO:** Incumbent U.S. Representative Thomas Massie (R-KY), a libertarian-leaning conservative, faced a general election challenge in Kentucky’s Fourth Congressional District.

**WHAT:** According to official projections from the Kentucky State Board of Elections, Congressman Massie has successfully retained his seat for the 119th United States Congress. The final tally, released in the early hours of Wednesday morning, showed Massie securing approximately 67.8 percent of the vote, defeating Democratic challenger Timothy M. Hayden.

**WHEN:** The general election was held on Tuesday, November 5, 2024, with results certified by state officials at 1:15 AM Eastern Standard Time.

**WHERE:** The race encompassed the entirety of Kentucky’s Fourth Congressional District, a predominantly rural and suburban jurisdiction spanning 16 counties, including areas east of Louisville and north of Lexington.

**WHY:** Voter analysis indicates that the incumbency advantage, coupled with strong name recognition and a platform emphasizing fiscal conservatism and Second Amendment rights, proved decisive. Massie’s district, which has voted Republican in every presidential election since 2000, maintained its partisan alignment.

**OFFICIAL STATEMENT:** In a post-victory address released from his Washington office, Representative Massie stated: "I am grateful to the constituents of the Fourth District for their continued trust. I will focus on reducing federal overreach and securing our constitutional liberties."

**CONTEXT:** This election cycle marks Massie’s seventh term. He is widely known for his opposition to large spending bills and his role as a vocal member of the House Freedom Caucus.
