**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**LOS ANGELES (AP) —** Former Los Angeles Police Department Detective Mark Fuhrman has issued a formal statement regarding a significant public interest in his current activities and past professional conduct.

**WHO:** Mark Fuhrman, former LAPD detective and convicted perjurer.

**WHAT:** Fuhrman has released a public statement addressing renewed scrutiny following a recently published investigative report examining his post-retirement commentary and legal opinions.

**WHEN:** The statement was released Tuesday afternoon, following the report's publication on Monday.

**WHERE:** The statement was issued from Fuhrman's personal residence in Sandpoint, Idaho.

**WHY:** In his statement, Fuhrman categorically denied allegations of impropriety or ongoing influence within the legal system, asserting that he has fully retired from all professional legal commentary. He characterized the recent report as "a rehash of discredited narratives" and reiterated that his 1995 perjury conviction—stemming from testimony in the O.J. Simpson trial—remains the basis for what he termed "unwarranted harassment." The report in question claimed to have uncovered documents suggesting Fuhrman maintained contacts with active law enforcement personnel, a claim he labeled "demonstrably false."

The statement concluded with a demand for a retraction from the publishing outlet and a warning of potential legal action for defamation.
