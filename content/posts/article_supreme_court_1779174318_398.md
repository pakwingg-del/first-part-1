**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About Today’s Supreme Court Bombshell**

🔹 **The Ruling That Shook Washington**
The Supreme Court just delivered a landmark decision that could fundamentally reshape how executive power is balanced. In a 6-3 split along ideological lines, the Court ruled that federal agencies no longer get "deference" when interpreting ambiguous laws—a direct blow to the regulatory state.

🔹 **What This Actually Changes**
This effectively overturns the 40-year-old *Chevron* doctrine. Now, when a law is vague, judges—not bureaucrats—will decide what it means. That means everything from environmental regulations to healthcare rules could face a tsunami of new lawsuits.

🔹 **Who Wins and Who Loses**
- **Big Business & Conservatives**: They've long argued agencies overreach. This gives them a massive legal weapon.
- **Regulators & Progressives**: Stripped of their interpretive authority, agencies like the EPA and SEC will struggle to enforce rules Congress didn't spell out in perfect detail.

🔹 **The Immediate Fallout**
By this afternoon, three major court challenges were already filed against existing regulations—including one targeting net neutrality rules and another on clean water standards. The legal landscape just became a minefield.

🔹 **What Comes Next**
Congress must now actually *write clear laws* if it wants agencies to act. Given today's gridlock, that’s unlikely. Expect years of chaotic litigation and a fundamental shift in who truly governs the country: judges.
