**Headline: YOUR BEER RUN JUST GOT SADDER: Schlitz Officially Axed – Here’s Why Your Wallet Should Care**

**The News:** The "Beer That Made Milwaukee Famous" is officially dead. After over a century of suds, Pabst has pulled the plug on Schlitz, leaving taps dry and shelves empty for good.

**The Consumer Wallet Angle:** This isn’t just a nostalgia hit. Schlitz was one of the last reliable "budget beers" that didn't taste like watered-down regrets. As inflation keeps squeezing your grocery bill, the loss of a cheap, drinkable option means fewer choices for your bucks. You’re now left with pricier "premium" lagers or even cheaper, skunkier alternatives. Expect the remaining budget brands to quietly raise their six-pack prices, knowing they just lost a major competitor.

**The Daily Life Hit:** For the weekend warrior, tailgater, or anyone who just wants a cold one without financial shame—this stings. The disappearance of Schlitz leaves a hole in the bottom shelf and a dent in your disposable income. Drink up while you can find it. After that, it's time to mourn—and pay more.
