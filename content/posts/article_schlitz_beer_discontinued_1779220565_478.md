**Viral News Snippet:**

**HEADLINE: "Schlitz Beer Permanently Discontinued After 175 Years, Brewery Confirms—But Fans Are Furious Over the REAL Reason"**

**The Claim:** A widely shared post on X (formerly Twitter) claims that the Pabst Brewing Company has officially halted production of Schlitz Beer, "The Beer That Made Milwaukee Famous," citing "unsustainable production costs and plummeting sales." The post includes a screenshot of a supposed internal memo stating that remaining inventory will be sold off by July 2024.

**Reality Check: FALSE (misleading)**

While Schlitz has been brewed in limited, nostalgic batches since its major 1970s-80s decline, **Pabst Brewing Company has NOT announced a permanent discontinuation.** The viral post is a hoax capitalizing on the brand’s well-known history of being "ghost brewed" in smaller quantities.

**What is actually happening:** Pabst *did* quietly reduce distribution of Schlitz over the past year, pulling it from many grocery chains—but it remains available through specialty liquor stores and online. The "internal memo" circulating is a photoshopped image from a 2019 rumor that went dormant.

**The Furious Fans:** Widespread anger is real, but misdirected. A Facebook group titled "Bring Back the Real Schlitz" has 15,000 members, but they are angry about a *different* issue: the recipe change in the 1970s.

**Bottom line:** Schlitz isn't dead—it's just harder to find. Don't rush to hoard cans just yet. ✅
