**HEADLINE: “Founder Burnout Isn't a Badge of Honor—It’s a Red Flag. Here’s What No One Tells You.”**

*In a viral LinkedIn post that has since amassed over 50,000 reactions, a former startup founder breaks down the silent epidemic of identity collapse among entrepreneurs. The post reads:*

“We glorify the ‘hustle until you drop’ culture. But after my latest venture imploded, I realized something terrifying: *I didn’t know who I was without the title.* My life had become a product roadmap, and when the funding dried up, so did my sense of self-worth.”

The post goes on to share three psychology-backed *founder recovery rules*:

1. **Separate your net worth from your self-worth.** You are not your revenue graph. When your company fails, you don’t.
2. **Schedule nothingness.** Innovation doesn’t come from 16-hour sprints—it comes from the blank space between thoughts.
3. **Build a ‘personal board of directors.’** One founder, one therapist, one friend who never mentions work, and one person who will call you out.

The post has struck a nerve, with thousands of founders admitting they’ve been silently hiding imposter syndrome behind “hustle” memes. As one commenter wrote: *“I needed to hear this before my next pitch meeting—or my next nervous breakdown.”*

The lesson? The most viral startup isn’t a billion-dollar app—it’s reclaiming your own mental real estate.
