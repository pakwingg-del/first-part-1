**Viral News Snippet – Rumor vs. Reality**

**Headline:** 🚨 **Kroger Garlic Croutons RECALLED: "Cheese Crumbles" Actually Contain "Moisture-Bloated Microplastics"?**

**The Viral Claim (Rumor):** Multiple viral TikTok and Facebook posts are circulating a disturbing warning: *“Kroger is recalling all bags of ‘Cheese Garlic Croutons’ because the ‘cheese crumbles’ on top are actually moisture-bloated microplastics injected with artificial flavor. Check your cupboards NOW.”* Some videos show users crumbling the croutons in water, claiming the “cheese” floats as a floating plastic scum.

**What’s Actually Happening (Fact Check by Real Regulatory Sources):**

✅ **True:** Kroger *did* issue a **voluntary recall** for certain **Cheese Garlic Croutons** (12 oz bags, UPC: 011110802033) on **March 25, 2025**—but **not for microplastics.**

✅ **Real Reason:** The recall is due to **undeclared milk allergen**. Some bags were mispackaged with **Garlic Croutons** that accidentally contained **white cheddar cheese powder**—which is a milk derivative. This poses a serious risk to people with dairy allergies, but the “cheese” itself is real cheese powder, not plastic.

❌ **Completely Fake:** The claim that “microplastics float to the top like scum” is **a staged hoax**. Independent food lab tests (and Kroger’s own safety statements) confirm the powdered coating is standard food-grade cheese powder. The “floating film” seen in viral videos is likely from **stirring damaged crouton oil** into the water—not plastic.
