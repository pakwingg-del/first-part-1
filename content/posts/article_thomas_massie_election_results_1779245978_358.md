**THOMAS MASSIE SECURES RE-ELECTION IN KENTUCKY'S 4TH DISTRICT**

**WHO:** Incumbent United States Representative Thomas Massie (R-KY).

**WHAT:** Won re-election to the United States House of Representatives.

**WHERE:** Kentucky’s 4th Congressional District, which encompasses parts of northern Kentucky, including the suburbs of Cincinnati, Ohio.

**WHEN:** The Associated Press called the race shortly after polls closed on Tuesday, November 5, 2024.

**WHY:** Representative Massie successfully defended his seat against Democratic challenger Lindsey Hughes. With over 95% of precincts reporting, Massie secured approximately 61% of the vote. Analysts attribute the victory to low Democratic turnout in the district and consistent support from conservative and libertarian voters, who align with Massie’s non-interventionist foreign policy and fiscal austerity stances.

**HOW:** Preliminary data indicates Massie received 211,000 votes to Hughes’ 135,000 votes, resulting in a margin of victory exceeding 26 percentage points. Massie is now set to serve his seventh consecutive term in Congress.
