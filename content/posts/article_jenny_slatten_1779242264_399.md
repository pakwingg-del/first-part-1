**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Jenny Slatten Case**

- **The Fatal Crash During a Police Chase:** The case centers on a high-speed police pursuit in Oklahoma on February 5, 2020. Slatten, who was 29 at the time, was the driver of a Jeep Cherokee that ran a red light during the chase, colliding with a Nissan Altima. The crash killed Thomas White, a 55-year-old father of three, who was on his way home from work.

- **The "Affluenza" Defense:** Slatten’s legal team argued that she suffered from "affluenza"—a controversial concept suggesting her wealthy upbringing shielded her from understanding the consequences of her actions. They claimed her parents’ financial support and lack of discipline made her unable to grasp that her behavior was wrong, a defense that sparked widespread outrage.

- **The 40-Year Sentence:** In 2024, after a lengthy trial, Slatten was sentenced to 40 years in prison for second-degree murder. Prosecutors argued she acted with "depraved indifference to human life" by fleeing police at speeds over 100 mph, ignoring multiple attempts to stop. The sentence was seen as a landmark moment for accountability in high-profile DUI and reckless driving cases.

- **The Social Media Footprint:** Slatten’s Instagram account, filled with lavish photos of luxury cars, designer clothes, and party scenes, played a key role in the prosecution’s narrative. They painted her as a privileged young woman who treated life like a "game," with posts showing her boasting about evading consequences before the crash.
