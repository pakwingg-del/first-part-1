**Georgia Supreme Court Election Results Officially Certified; Incumbent Justice Secures Second Term**

**ATLANTA, GA —** The Georgia Supreme Court has formally certified the results of the recent election for a seat on the state's highest judicial body.

**Who:** Incumbent Justice Carla Wong McMillian, alongside challenger state Representative Angela Workman (R-Atlanta).

**What:** The official certification of the Georgia Supreme Court election results, confirming Justice McMillian’s victory by a margin of 54.2% to 45.8%.

**When:** Certification was completed at 10:00 AM EST on Wednesday, following a mandatory five-day canvassing period.

**Where:** The certification took place at the Georgia State Capitol in Atlanta, conducted by the Office of the Secretary of State.

**Why:** Justice McMillian, who was first appointed in 2020, successfully defended her seat against a challenge focused on judicial philosophy and partisan alignment. The race attracted significant national attention as a bellwether for judicial independence versus partisan influence in state courts.

**Key Details:**
- This election was the most expensive Georgia Supreme Court race in history, with combined campaign spending exceeding $12 million.
- Justice McMillian’s victory maintains the court’s current ideological balance.
- The election results were certified without any reported irregularities or legal challenges.

**Context:**
Georgia is one of only seven states that holds partisan judicial elections. This race was closely watched as a potential indicator of voter sentiment regarding the role of state courts in upcoming election-related litigation. The Atlanta-based Center for Judicial Accountability has noted that this election cycle saw a 300% increase in out-of-state political action committee spending compared to the 2022 cycle.
