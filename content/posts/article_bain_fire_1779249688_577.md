If we are burning food-grade vinegar and luxury textiles for a moment’s warmth, we are not just careless—we are nihilists parading as tastemakers. The Bain fire doesn’t warm your home; it signals that you are so wealthy, you can afford to be colder than the frozen poor. And that, dear readers, is a brand of cold that no log can cure.
