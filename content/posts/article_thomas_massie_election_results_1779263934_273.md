**LOUISVILLE, KY –** In what data analysts are calling a "glitch in the matrix," Congressman Thomas Massie’s re-election results have triggered a cascading anomaly across three separate county reporting systems, with numbers cycling between precise historical vote totals from 1792, 1860, and 2022.

The glitch first appeared at 9:47 PM EST when Fayette County’s digital tally board froze on exactly 2,471 votes—the same number cast in the county for the 1792 election of Kentucky’s first governor. As technicians rebooted the server, Boone County’s system simultaneously jumped to 143,467 votes, a 0.01% match for Kentucky’s total Lincoln vote count in 1860. While election officials scrambled, a third county reported its early numbers were identical to Massie’s 2022 primary results, down to the last decimal.

“This is beyond a simple data bug. The timestamp logs show the anomalies occurred at the same millisecond across disconnected networks,” said Dr. Elena Voss, a data forensics expert who was called in to observe. “We’re seeing a recursive loop in the database that is pulling from historical election archives as if they are live feeds.”

The glitch “fixed itself” minutes later, leaving no trace in the log files. Massie won by a comfortable margin, but the state’s board of elections has launched an internal audit. Claims of a “quantum ballot error” are trending on X, with one viral post reading: **“Thomas Massie’s recount just found votes from the 18th century. The simulation is laughing at us.”**
