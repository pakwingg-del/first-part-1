According to the company’s own real-time tracker, power has been “restored” to over 14,000 customers in Southeast Michigan. Yet here on my street? It’s pitch black. The clocks are blinking. My fridge is a ticking time bomb.

I took a screenshot of the map at 7:45 a.m. It shows a green “Restored” badge covering my entire neighborhood. Meanwhile, I’m standing in my driveway with a flashlight, watching the utility truck drive *past* my house without stopping.

“How can the map say my power is back when I’m sitting here in the dark?” I asked.

The dispatcher told me: “The map updates automatically based on system status.”

It’s not just me. Neighbors up and down Woodward are posting similar stories: “Map shows green. House shows black.” “DTE says 99% restored. The ‘1%’ still waiting – we can see your map lying.”

I’m all for modern technology, but when a map becomes more fiction than fact – and you’re charging me a premium for “reliable service” – where’s the common sense?

DTE, if you can’t fix the grid, at least fix the map. Otherwise, stop pretending everything’s fine while my freezer is melting.
