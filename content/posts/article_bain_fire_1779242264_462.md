**Headline:** DID A MYSTERIOUS "BAIN FIRE" CONSUME A SECRET CACHE OF QUANTUM COMPUTERS? AUTHORITIES SAY NO.

**The Viral Claim:**
A viral TikTok video, now with 4.2 million views, claims that a "Bain Fire" – a new, uncontainable type of white-hot electric blaze – recently destroyed an off-the-grid research facility in rural Montana. According to the influencer "TechPrepper77," the fire was *not* ordinary. He alleged the facility was owned by a shell company linked to a former Google executive and contained stolen experimental quantum processors worth $2 billion. The video shows flickering, otherworldly white flames and a loudspeaker announcement saying, "Evacuate immediately. This is a Bain Fire event."

**The Reality Check:**
- **What is "Bain Fire"?** **NOT a real scientific term.** There is zero record of the phrase "Bain Fire" in any firefighting, engineering, or disaster management database. A search of the National Fire Protection Association (NFPA) and scientific journals yields nothing. The term appears to have been invented for this video.
