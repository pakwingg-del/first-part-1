**HEADLINE: "THE SHIRILLA EFFECT": HORROR DOCUMENTARY SPARKS MORAL PANIC OVER "DATING FOR DOPAMINE" CULTURE**

**Byline:** The Society Sentinel

**Toledo, OH** — The chilling new documentary *"The Last Text: The Mackenzie Shirilla Story"* is being pulled from streaming platforms in three states after a wave of protests, but not for the reasons you’d think. While the film chronicles the 19-year-old’s 2022 conviction for murdering her boyfriend, Dominic Russo, by driving her car into a wall at 100 mph, moral watchdogs are now pointing fingers at a far more insidious culprit: the "dopamine culture" that they say created her.

"This isn’t a story about a bad seed. It’s a story about a generation taught to use people as fuel for a thrill," says Dr. Helena Voss, a cultural ethicist featured in the documentary. "Mackenzie wasn't just 'crazy in love.' She was a product of a society that rewards the emotional highs of a relationship while discarding the ethical lows. She treated Dominic like a subscription she could cancel—permanently."

The viral controversy exploded after a leaked clip shows Shirilla, in a chilling prison interview, describing the fatal crash as "the most exciting moment I’ve ever felt." The clip has been split-screened with TikTok "couple goals" videos set to high-energy music, sparking a trending hashtag: **#RideOrDieRuin.**

Critics argue the documentary fails to prosecute the root cause: a "dating for dopamine" ethos where emotional intensity is prized over loyalty, and where "blocking" someone has replaced breaking up with them.
