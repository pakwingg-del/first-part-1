**CANYON HEIGHTS, CA** – As a fast-moving brush fire forced the evacuation of 2,000 homes Tuesday, veteran Fire Chief Mark Delgado stunned reporters by comparing the scene to a 2,000-year-old historical catastrophe.  

“I’ve seen a lot of fires,” Delgado said, gesturing to a wall of flame climbing the ridge. “But this pattern—walls of fire advancing from three directions as the wind shifts—reminds me of the Great Fire of Rome in 64 AD. Tacitus wrote that the flames ‘descended from the hills like a legion’ before consuming the Circus Maximus.”  

The comparison went viral instantly, with #NerosGhost trending as shocked residents posted photos of the blaze silhouetting a nearby strip mall. “Our Whole Foods is the new Circus Maximus,” one evacuee joked grimly.  

Historians are divided. Some note that while Nero allegedly fiddled, today’s culprit is likely a downed power line—but the parallels are eerie. “The Romans had no fire departments, no helicopters,” noted Dr. Lena Park of UCLA. “We do. Yet here we are, watching the same elemental fury that turned ancient Rome to ash. The question is: are we learning from history, or just repeating it?”  

As winds intensify, officials warn the fire may burn for days. Meanwhile, the internet has already crowned this the “Great Inferno of 2025”—with memes comparing the smoke plume to the eruption of Vesuvius.  

**Is history repeating itself, or is this just Mother Nature’s cruel rerun?**
