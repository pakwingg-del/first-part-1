**BREAKDOWN: CORTE SUPREMA RULING SPARKS NATIONAL DEBATE**

**DATELINE:** WASHINGTON, D.C. – The Supreme Court of the United States has issued a landmark ruling in the case of *Citizens for Fiscal Oversight v. Federal Energy Commission*. (5:00 PM EST)

**WHO:** The highest judicial authority in the nation, the Supreme Court, delivered a 6-3 decision authored by Chief Justice Elena Torres. The dissenting opinion was written by Justice Samuel Rothschild.

**WHAT:** The Court declared unconstitutional a 2023 federal statute that granted the Federal Energy Commission unprecedented authority to impose retroactive utility rate hikes without legislative approval. The majority opinion argues the law violates the separation of powers doctrine.

**WHERE:** The ruling originates from a dispute involving a consortium of residential utility customers in the Pacific Northwest. The case was appealed from the Ninth Circuit Court of Appeals.

**WHEN:** The opinion was released moments ago, immediately halting all pending retroactive rate adjustments currently under review by the Commission.

**WHY:** The central legal question was whether Congress can delegate retroactive economic regulatory power to an executive agency without a clear, post-enactment legislative review mechanism. The Court found that the "non-delegation doctrine," while typically flexible for future rulemaking, prohibits blanket retroactive authority.

**BREAKING ANALYSIS:** Legal experts say this decision will reshape the regulatory landscape, forcing Congress to re-draft energy policy legislation. The White House has yet to issue an official statement. We will continue to monitor this developing story.
