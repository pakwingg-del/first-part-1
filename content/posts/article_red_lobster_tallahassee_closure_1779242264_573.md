**Headline: Red Lobster’s Tallahassee Shutdown: The ‘Endless Shrimp’ Reckoning That Signals a Nation’s Moral Bankruptcy**

**Tallahassee, FL** — The abrupt closure of the Red Lobster on Apalachee Parkway is not just a corporate casualty—it is a **symptom of a society that has abandoned thrift, gratitude, and the sacred covenant of the bargain**. The chain’s infamous “Ultimate Endless Shrimp” promotion, meant to be a festive indulgence, became a symbol of our gluttonous undoing. We taught our children to demand limitless excess for a fixed price, then blamed the company for going under.

Local pastor and civic critic, Rev. Harold Meacham, called the closure a "parable of our times." "We mocked the shrimp as 'bottomless,' but we forgot that the ocean, the farmer, and the wage-earner are not," Meacham said outside the darkened dining room. "When we value quantity over quality, and demand the world on a platter for $20.99, we are not scoring a win—we are pulling the anchor out of our own ship."

Critics note that the restaurant’s collapse mirrors a broader cultural decay: the commodification of patience, the death of the family-style meal, and the entitlement of expecting a luxury to be infinite. "First the shrimp, then our dignity," wrote one furious online commentator. "What's next—welfare biscuits?"

As the last cheddar bay biscuit crumbles, the question remains: when did 'all-you-can-eat' become our national motto? And who will be left to pay the check?
