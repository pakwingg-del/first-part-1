**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About Ken Paxton’s Latest Legal Firestorm**

- **The Whistleblower Settlement That Broke Records:** In a stunning turn, the Texas Attorney General has agreed to a record-setting $3.7 million settlement for the whistleblowers who accused him of corruption and bribery. The payment is technically from state taxpayers, effectively ending a years-long legal battle—but not the intense political fallout.

- **Securities Fraud Charges Still Looming (But Delayed):** Despite the settlement, Paxton remains under indictment on three felony securities fraud charges from 2015. His legal team just secured yet another delay, pushing the trial to 2025. Critics say he’s been dodging a jury for nearly a decade.

- **The "Oust Paxton" Impeachment That Backfired:** After the Texas House impeached him in 2023, the Senate acquitted him—and now Paxton is on a revenge tour. He’s endorsed primary challengers against every Republican who voted to impeach him, turning Texas GOP politics into a brutal civil war.

- **His Most Aggressive Legal War Yet on Federal Policies:** Paxton has filed a record number of lawsuits against the Biden administration, targeting everything from transgender military service to border security. He’s now part of a multi-state coalition threatening to sue over any federal climate regulations.

- **Personal Vendetta or Constitutional Defender?** His supporters call him a fearless conservative bulldog, but critics say he uses the AG’s office as a weapon for personal grievances and political retribution. The final verdict on Ken Paxton might not come from a court—but from Texas voters in 2026.
