**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About Abigail Spanberger**

- **She’s a Former CIA Officer Turned Political Centrist:** Before entering Congress, Spanberger spent eight years as a covert CIA officer, tracking illicit finance networks. In the House, she positioned herself as a moderate Democrat, often breaking with party leadership on issues like immigration and energy policy to win in a competitive Virginia district.

- **The "No" Vote That Made Headlines:** In 2022, Spanberger famously told the Democratic Party, "Nobody in this district thinks we defunded the police – we *actually* didn't – but people *believe* we did." She was one of the few Democrats to publicly distance herself from the "Defund the Police" slogan, arguing it cost the party suburban voters.

- **She’s Betting on a Red-to-Blue Strategy for Governor:** Declining a run for a fourth term in Congress, Spanberger is aiming for Virginia’s governor's mansion in 2025. She’s framing her campaign around pragmatic, kitchen-table issues (education and infrastructure), hoping to replicate the winning formula of centrist governors like Glenn Youngkin—but from the left.

- **A Maverick on Immigration:** In a deeply polarized Congress, Spanberger helped lead a bipartisan border security bill in 2024. She pushed for restrictions on asylum claims, a move that angered progressive activists but won praise from her district's swing voters. She insists compromise is the "only path forward" on the crisis.

- **The "Purple District" Survival Manual:** Spanberger first flipped her seat in 2018 by unseating a Republican incumbent. She survived a 2020 red wave and a 2022 redistricting battle by focusing on local economic wins and avoiding national party chaos. Her political playbook is now being studied by Democrats trying to reclaim the political center.
