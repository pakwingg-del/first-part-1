**CLAIM:** *A viral social media post claims a new, unverified “DTE Outage Map Pro” app offers real-time, faster updates than DTE Energy’s official map, and that the company is “hiding the true number of outages” from customers.*

**VERDICT: ⚠️ MISLEADING / PARTIALLY FALSE**

**What’s True:**
- DTE Energy’s official outage map does sometimes refresh slower during major storms, which can frustrate customers.
- Several third-party apps do scrape public DTE data and present it in a different format.

**What’s False / Exaggerated:**
- There is **no** official “DTE Outage Map Pro” app endorsed by DTE Energy. The viral post is promoting an unaffiliated third-party app.
- DTE Energy **publicly publishes** its outage data via an API accessible to developers. The “real-time” claim is misleading—these third-party apps pull the *same* data DTE provides, with a similar delay (typically 5–15 minutes).
- The accusation that DTE is “hiding the true number” is **unsubstantiated**. DTE’s official numbers match state emergency management data in verified past events. During widespread outages, discrepancies occur due to restoration timing, not concealment.
- Privacy experts warn that unofficial apps may collect user location or contact info without clear disclosures. DTE has not authorized any third-party app for outage reporting.

**Bottom Line:** Stick with DTE’s official outage map at outage.dteenergy.com or the company’s mobile app for the most reliable, verified information. Be wary of “pro” apps claiming insider data—they’re often riding on the same public data with possible privacy risks.
