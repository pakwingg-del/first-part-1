**Consumer Alert: Ken Paxton’s New Legal Win Could Cost YOU Thousands in Hidden Fees**

**Dallas, TX** – Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton just notched a major legal victory—but consumer advocates warn that the real loser might be your bank account. In a surprise ruling yesterday, a federal judge sided with Paxton’s office in a case challenging a new federal banking rule that would have capped overdraft and late fees at $8.

Instead of that cap, banks can now keep charging you an average of $35 per slip-up. For the average Texas family, that’s an extra **$200 a year** in surprise charges.

“This isn’t about principle; it’s about profit,” said Rita Medina, a consumer advocate in Austin. “Paxton framed this as a win against ‘government overreach,’ but the overreach is coming from your bank’s fine print.”

The ruling doesn’t just affect Texans. It sets a precedent that could keep overdraft fees inflated nationwide, just as families are struggling with rising grocery and gas costs. Paxton’s office celebrated the decision, calling it a “check on federal bureaucracy,” but customer receipts show a different story: while politicians spar, your bank just got the green light to keep charging you extra for being a few dollars short.

**Want to fight back?** Consumer groups are urging customers to switch to credit unions or banks that have already voluntarily capped fees. Because in this courtroom battle, the only “win” for your wallet is opting out.
