**DATA ANOMALY DETECTED: GEORGIA BALLOT STREAM REVEALS 'GHOST VOTES' WITH PERFECT ZERO-ERROR RATE**

**ATLANTA, GA** – A routine audit of Georgia's election data logging has uncovered a statistical impossibility that has election integrity analysts and mathematicians alike calling it "a glitch in the matrix."

Independent data analyst Dr. Anya Petrova was cross-referencing timestamp logs from Fulton County’s optical scanners when she noticed a pattern that defies all natural probability. For a continuous 43-second window on Election Night, the system recorded exactly **6,000 ballots** being fed through 15 machines. The impossible part? **Each machine reported a final tally of exactly 400 votes, with zero under-votes, zero over-votes, and zero blank ballots.**

“In real-world voting, a machine sees at least a 1-3% error rate from human handling—creases, smudges, mis-feeds. A perfect zero-error batch on this scale has the same statistical probability as flipping a coin and getting heads 6,000 times in a row,” Petrova told *The Anomaly Report*.

But the strangest detail came when she cross-referenced the voter history logs. **Every single one of those 6,000 ballots came from voters who had been marked as 'Inactive' in the previous election cycle.**

Local election officials have dismissed the finding as a “data stitching error,” but Petrova remains unconvinced.

“The system didn't glitch. It corrected itself too perfectly. That’s not a bug—that’s a feature we weren’t supposed to see.”
