Multiple social media posts are circulating this evening claiming that “Alabama election results show massive voter fraud after voting machines malfunctioned in 12 counties.” The posts allege that “hand counts flipped the race” and that “state officials are covering up a stolen election.” One widely-shared video, viewed over 800,000 times, shows a poll worker saying, “We had to re-scan (the ballots) four times, and the numbers kept changing.”

**Fact-Check:**
✅ **False / Misleading.**

1. **Machines did not “break” in the way described.** Secretary of State Wes Allen confirmed that a minor software glitch affected scanner calibration in 12 precincts across Jefferson and Mobile counties. All machines were recalibrated within 45 minutes, and no ballots were lost or altered.

2. **“Hand counts” were part of standard procedure.** Under Alabama law, any precinct where a machine issue occurs must conduct a hand tally of all ballots before final certification. That happened here and *confirmed* the machine totals, not contradicted them.

3. **No fraud evidence.** The Alabama Law Enforcement Agency (ALEA) and the state’s election integrity unit reviewed all affected ballots. They found zero cases of ballot tampering, voter impersonation, or unauthorized voting.

4. **The “flipped race” claim is false.** In the special election for Alabama House District 73 (Jefferson County), Republican candidate David Faulkner defeated Democrat James Miller by 412 votes — a margin consistent with pre-election polls. The hand count matched the machine tally precisely.
