**Viral Claim:** Georgia Supreme Court candidate signs pledge to “ban transgender bathrooms” and “bring back prayer in schools” – then tries to claim the signature was a “joke.”

**Verdict: MISLEADING (Context Needed)**

**The Story:** A screenshot circulating on Facebook and X (formerly Twitter) shows a candidate for the Georgia Supreme Court, John “Justice Now” Rawlings, allegedly signing a “Pledge for Biblical Law” at a local GOP event. The pledge includes items like “Defend the Ten Commandments in courtrooms” and “Protect children by rejecting transgender rights in public facilities.”

**What’s Being Shared:** The image shows a signature on a line that says “Agreed and Accepted,” with the candidate’s name printed beneath it. Captions scream: “This man wants to be on Georgia’s highest court and thinks God should tell him the verdicts!”

**What We Found:**

*   **The Signature is Real:** The document exists. It was hosted by a fringe group called the “Georgia Christian Voter Alliance” and was presented at a candidate forum on November 3.
*   **The Candidate’s Response:** In a since-deleted tweet, Rawlings claimed he “signed it sarcastically” to “expose the radical left” and that he “doesn’t actually want to mix church and state.”
*   **The Reality Check:** Legal experts note that even if the signature was a “joke,” a candidate for a non-partisan, highest-level state court cannot credibly claim they were joking about the First Amendment. The Georgia Supreme Court decides cases on abortion, voting rights, and family law.
*   **The “Fake” Element:** The viral posts claim Rawlings *campaigns* on these issues. His actual campaign website mentions “judicial restraint” and “equal justice,” not bathrooms or prayer.
