**CBP Issues Urgent Memorial Day Travel Warning: “Worst Crossings Since the Trail of Tears”**  

In a press release that stunned historians and travelers alike, U.S. Customs and Border Protection issued a stark Memorial Day weekend travel alert, drawing a chilling parallel to one of America’s darkest chapters. “We are facing the worst congestion and humanitarian strain at our ports of entry since the Trail of Tears,” a senior CBP official stated, referencing the forced displacement of Native Americans in the 1830s that claimed thousands of lives.  

The agency warns that record-breaking vehicle lines, combined with extreme heat and limited resources, could create “life-threatening delays” for families attempting to cross at southern border checkpoints. “Just as in that tragic era, we see massive groups of people trapped in unimaginable conditions—except now it’s Americans stuck in cars and buses,” the official added.  

Critics are calling the comparison inflammatory, but CBP insists the historical lens is meant to shock the public into preparing for potential 12-hour waits, water shortages, and medical emergencies. Meanwhile, AAA predicts 43.8 million travelers this Memorial Day—and border states are bracing for a chaotic, history-tinged holiday weekend.
