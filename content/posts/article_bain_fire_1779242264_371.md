**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**LOCAL (BAIN): Widespread Fire Contained, Investigations Underway**

**BAIN, [Date] —** A significant fire incident that prompted a multi-agency emergency response in the Bain municipality has been fully contained as of early this morning, according to fire authorities.

**What:** A large structure and brush fire, which officials describe as "rapidly escalating," consumed a commercial warehouse and threatened adjacent residential properties. No civilian casualties have been reported; however, two firefighters sustained minor injuries and were treated at the scene.

**Where:** The epicenter of the blaze was located at the industrial complex on West Bain Road, near the intersection with Maple Avenue. A mandatory shelter-in-place order was issued for a quarter-mile radius, covering approximately 200 residences, which has since been lifted.

**When:** Emergency services received the initial call at 2:47 PM local time yesterday. The fire was declared fully under control at 6:52 AM today. The cause of ignition remains undetermined.

**Who:** The Bain Fire Department led suppression efforts, supported by three neighboring county fire districts and the State Bureau of Emergency Management.

**Why:** A joint investigation is now underway between the Bain Fire Marshal's Office and the State Fire Investigation Unit. While the cause is classified as "active and undetermined," authorities have confirmed there is no evidence of foul play at this time. Preliminary assessments suggest the fire may have originated from malfunctioning industrial machinery.

**Current Status:** All evacuation orders are rescinded. Residents are permitted to return to their homes. Road closures on West Bain Road remain in effect for structural assessments. A public information hotline has been established for displaced residents and those seeking property damage updates.
