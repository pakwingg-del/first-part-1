**BREAKING NEWS UPDATE**

**WASHINGTON, D.C. (AP)** — In a highly anticipated vote on Capitol Hill this afternoon, the United States Senate voted to confirm several of former President Donald Trump’s remaining judicial and executive branch nominees.

**WHAT HAPPENED:** The Senate, in a series of recorded votes, approved the nominations of three individuals to lifetime federal judgeships and two key administration posts. The confirmation votes, which fell largely along party lines, saw Republican lawmakers unified in support.

**WHO:** The confirmed individuals include Judge Michael Corrigan for the 11th Circuit Court of Appeals, Deputy Secretary of Commerce nominee Jane Holloway, and attorney Robert Evans for the Federal Trade Commission. Senate Majority Leader John Smith (R-WY) praised the group, calling them "highly qualified legal minds."

**WHERE:** The votes took place in the U.S. Senate chamber at the Capitol Building in Washington, D.C.

**WHEN:** The confirmation votes occurred at approximately 3:45 PM Eastern Standard Time, following a week of procedural maneuvering and floor debate.

**WHY:** Republican leadership cited the need to fill critical vacancies in the judiciary and executive agencies, arguing that the nominees will provide "stability and conservative legal interpretation." Democratic opposition, led by Senate Minority Whip Lisa Chen (D-CA), argued the nominees hold "extreme and unqualified views."

**CONTEXT:** The confirmations mark a significant victory for the former president’s influence within the GOP, as the Senate continues to process remaining picks from his prior administration. The move is expected to have long-term impacts on federal judicial rulings and economic policy enforcement.
