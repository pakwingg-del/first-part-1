**CLOSING SOON: Red Lobster Tallahassee Serves Its Last Biscuit**

After 30 years of cheddar Bay biscuits and endless shrimp, Red Lobster on Apalachee Parkway is shutting its doors for good. Effective immediately, the location will cease operations by end of month—citing "critical underperformance" and ongoing corporate restructuring.

This is not a one-off closure. The company has shuttered over 60 locations nationwide in the past 18 months, with Tallahassee joining a growing list of "zombie assets" being sold off by its private equity-backed parent. The local franchisee operated on razor-thin margins, with average daily traffic dropping 42% since 2020.

**Key impact**: 87 employees will be laid off, with severance capped at two weeks. The property, a 7,200 sq ft standalone building, now goes to auction next quarter—likely to be demolished for a mixed-use development.

**CEO takeaway**: This is a textbook case of legacy casual dining being cannibalized by shift-to-value and hybrid work. No bailout incoming.
