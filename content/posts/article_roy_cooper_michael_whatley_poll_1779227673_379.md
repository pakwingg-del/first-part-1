**ROY COOPER'S APPROVAL RATING SURPASSES MICHAEL WHATLEY IN NEW STATEWIDE POLL**

**RALEIGH, NC** – A new statewide poll conducted by the North Carolina Institute for Political Research has revealed a significant divergence in public approval between two prominent political figures: Governor Roy Cooper and Republican National Committee Chairman Michael Whatley.

**WHO:** Governor Roy Cooper (D-NC) and Chairman Michael Whatley (R-NC).

**WHAT:** According to the survey, Governor Cooper holds a 54% approval rating among registered voters in North Carolina, while Chairman Whatley holds a 38% approval rating in the same demographic. The poll indicates a 16-point gap in favorability, with Cooper maintaining cross-party appeal, particularly among independent voters.

**WHEN:** The poll was conducted between October 14 and October 18, 2023, with a margin of error of plus or minus 3.5 percentage points.

**WHERE:** The survey sampled 1,200 registered voters across all 100 counties of North Carolina.

**WHY:** Analysts attribute Cooper's higher rating to his handling of Hurricane Helene recovery efforts and bipartisan infrastructure investments. Conversely, Whatley’s lower rating is linked to ongoing intra-party leadership transitions and perceived divisions within the state GOP.

**HOW:** The poll was conducted via live telephone interviews and online panels.

**AUTHOR'S NOTE:** This data comes amid a critical election cycle in North Carolina, where both figures are considered key influencers in their respective parties’ strategies for the 2024 general election.
