🚨 **LOCAL RESIDENT ERUPTS OVER “BAIN FIRE” FIASCO: “WHERE’S THE COMMON SENSE?!”** 🚨

Just got back from the neighborhood watch meeting and I’m still shaking my head. So “Bain Fire” is trending in our group—and not for a good reason. 

Here’s the deal. Apparently, someone in the new development on Elm Street decided to “clean up” their yard by piling up a mountain of old leaves, broken pallets, and—get this—a full couch cushion. Then, they set the whole thing ablaze in their backyard last night. At 10 PM. In a drought.

Neighbors called the fire department because the smoke was rolling over the fence into three different yards. Kids’ trampolines melted. Mrs. Patterson’s cat hasn’t come home since. But when the fire crew showed up? Our “brave” burner was standing there with a garden hose, yelling, “I’m just taking care of yard waste! Nobody told me it was illegal!”

Oh, I’m sorry—did we forget how to use a trash bin? Did common sense just pack its bags and move to another county? 

The fire captain had to explain, on video, that open burning is banned in our township unless you have a permit. And you can’t just torch a couch cushion because it “makes good kindling.” 

Honestly, this is exactly why we can’t have nice things. If you live in a neighborhood with neighbors, you do not light a bonfire at bedtime. You do not burn trash. And you definitely do not melt plastic into the soil because you’re too lazy to bag your leaves.

Let’s get a grip, people. Use common sense, or next time the fire department is going to let the whole lot burn and write you a ticket for the cleanup.
