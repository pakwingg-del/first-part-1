**BREAKING VIRAL NEWS SNIPPET**

**Salesforce CEO Marc Benioff 'Forces' All 70,000 Employees to Live-Tweet Each Other’s Dreams During Company Retreat — Claims It's 'Essential for Cloud Synergy'**

**Rating:** 🔴 Fake

**Full Story:**
A post is going viral claiming that Salesforce CEO Marc Benioff mandated a radical new company policy at a recent retreat in Hawaii. The rumor alleges that Benioff required all 70,000 global employees to participate in a "Dream-Sharing Protocol," where each worker must live-tweet the dreams of two randomly assigned colleagues for one full week. The supposed policy states that failure to post a minimum of 15 dream summaries per day would result in forfeiting stock options to a "collective consciousness fund."

Posts include fabricated screenshots of a company-wide Slack message in which Benioff allegedly wrote: "Our data is the body, but our dreams are the soul of the org chart. To achieve true Cloud Synergy, we must know each other's sleeping anxieties."

**Verdict: Fake**
- Salesforce has confirmed to multiple fact-checking outlets that this policy does not exist.
- The screenshots show a Slack layout style that was updated last year, and the timestamp references a date in 2026.
- Benioff, while known for eccentric corporate retreats (including "Ohana" culture and floating meditation), has never implemented a hypnagogic content quota.
- The viral post likely stems from a satirical article on a business parody site that was shared out of context without its "Opinion" label.

⚠️ **Bottom line:** Your Salesforce stock options are safe from dream-based forfeiture. This is a fabricated rumor designed to critique tech CEO culture, not a real HR memo.
