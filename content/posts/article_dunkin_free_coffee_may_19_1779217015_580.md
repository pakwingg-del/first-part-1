**MORAL CRITIC CHIMES IN: "Dunkin’s Free Coffee Day on May 19 Isn’t Generosity—It’s a Spiritual Poison"**

In what many are calling a harmless promotion, Dunkin’ Donuts has announced it will give away free coffee on May 19. But as a moral critic who has watched the slow erosion of American virtue, I see the dark underbelly of this corporate "kindness." This isn’t a gift; it’s a calculated seduction to normalize gluttony, instant gratification, and the worship of cheap dopamine.

Consider the message: "You deserve something for nothing." This is the same cultural rot that has replaced hard work with entitlement. We no longer earn our morning ritual—we demand it as a dividend of our consumer loyalty. On May 19, millions will stand in line, not for community or charity, but for a fleeting fix of caffeinated self-indulgence. The coffee is free, but the cost to our character is immeasurable.

And what of the environmental toll? Thousands of single-use cups, plastic lids, and napkins will be tossed into landfills in this ritual of mass consumption. We are teaching our children that "free" is the highest good, while ignoring the mountains of waste left in our wake.

This is not a moment of generosity. This is the downfall of society, one free cup at a time. Wake up, America—before we’ve all sold our souls for a cold brew.
