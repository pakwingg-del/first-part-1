**Headline:** Calvin Klein’s “Parent Trap 2.0” Ad Sparks National Outcry: Critics Claim It Normalizes “Digital Diaper Days” for Gen Alpha

**Snippet:**

In a move that has social conservatives and child psychologists up in arms, Calvin Klein has dropped a new campaign featuring a 14-year-old influencer modeling a sheer mesh “Transition Top” alongside her mother. Branded as “Generation Unfiltered,” the ad shows the teen in a pose critics call “hyper-sexualized for the checkout aisle.” 

Moral watchdog group *The Family Front* is calling for a boycott, arguing the campaign blurs the line between childhood and adult performance. “We’ve moved from crop tops at 12 to sheer lingerie at 14,” said spokesperson Linda Graves. “This isn’t empowerment—it’s the systematic erasure of innocence. We are normalizing a culture where a teenager’s value is measured in engagement metrics and visibility of their silhouette. What’s next? Calvin Klein diapers with a ‘thirst trap’ print?”

The outrage went viral after a video of a mother in Dallas tearing down the billboard with a crowbar circulated on TikTok. “I’m not prude, I’m protective,” she said in the clip. “This is the downfall: we are putting the soft core of adult marketing onto the soft bones of children.” 

Calvin Klein has not commented, but the campaign has already been flagged for “review” by two state attorneys general.
