🤬 **"COMMON SENSE STRIKES OUT" - SUPREME COURT RULES COMMON SENSE ISN'T A LEGAL DEFENSE**

Just when you think the system can't get any more out of touch, the Supreme Court drops a bombshell that would make any regular person spit out their morning coffee.

In a move that has my blood absolutely boiling, the highest court in the land just ruled that "common sense" is **not** a valid legal defense in a property dispute case. That's right, folks. According to a 5-4 decision, a homeowner can't simply say "any reasonable person would know that was wrong" and expect a judge to agree.

Let me break this down for you because this screams ivory tower nonsense. The case involved a guy who removed a survey stake from his neighbor's property because he "knew" the survey was wrong. He said any normal person would see the corner was off by three feet. But the Court said his "common sense" belief doesn't matter if he didn't hire a second surveyor or get a court order first.

So now, if a contractor is clearly building a shed on your land, you can't just walk over and say "hey, that's my property" because that's "only common sense." You need a lawyer, a notary, and probably a sternly worded letter on fancy paper.

What's next? Do I need a legal degree to tell if my neighbor's dog is barking too loud? Do I need a court order to know that parking in my driveway is wrong?

This is why people are fed up. The courts are so far removed from reality that they think "common sense" is a legal loophole instead of the basic foundation of how decent society operates. Wake up, judges. The real world doesn't run on arcane technicalities. It runs on people knowing right from wrong without needing a law library.
