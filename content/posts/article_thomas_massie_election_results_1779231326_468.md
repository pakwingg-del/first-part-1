**VERDICT: MISLEADING**

**Headline:** *"Thomas Massie Election Results Show Landslide Victory After Being 'Dropped' by Vote-Tabulating Software in Kentucky"*

**The Viral Claim:**
A fast-spreading social media post claims that Representative Thomas Massie (R-KY) won his primary election by a "landslide" of 83% but that this result was hidden by major news outlets because early vote tallies "accidentally dropped him from the ballot" due to a software glitch. The post adds that local officials had to "manually add his votes back in."

**Why It’s Misleading:**

While it is **true** that Rep. Thomas Massie won his 2024 primary election handily (defeating challenger Eric Deters by a wide margin, with Massie receiving roughly 75-80% of the vote), the claim of a nefarious "vote-dropping software glitch" is **exaggerated and lacks evidence.**

- **The "Glitch" Reality:** In March 2024, a routine software update in Boone County, Kentucky, caused a brief display issue on the election night website—the tabulation software *showed* zero votes for a small number of precincts for a few minutes before populating correctly. This was a visual loading error, not a case of votes being "deleted." Local election officials confirmed the votes were always present in the secure tabulation hardware.
- **No "Cover-Up":** The election result (Massie winning by ~75%) was widely reported by local Kentucky media and validated by the Kentucky Board of Elections final certified count.

**Bottom Line:** The claim is **misleading**. The "landslide" win is factual. The suggestion that the software deliberately erased Massie's votes or that the media suppressed the result to undermine him is **false** and stems from a misinterpretation of a short-lived display error.
