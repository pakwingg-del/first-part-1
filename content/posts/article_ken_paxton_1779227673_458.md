**Breaking News: Viral Claim vs. Fact: Did Ken Paxton Declare Texas an "Independent Republic" After SCOTUS Ruling?**  

**The Claim (Viral TikTok/Telegram Post):**  
> "Ken Paxton just dropped a bombshell: Texas is now a 'Sovereign Independent Republic' following the Supreme Court's decision to decline Texas v. Pennsylvania. He's ordering all state agencies to ignore federal law starting Jan 15th. Multiple sources are confirming this is REAL."  

**Fact-Check Verdict: ❌ FAKE / MISLEADING**  

**The Reality:**  
✅ **No official proclamation exists.** On the date in question, Attorney General Ken Paxton issued a *statement* criticizing the U.S. Supreme Court for "refusing to uphold the Constitution" and reaffirmed Texas's "right to defend itself." However, he did not declare Texas an independent republic or order agencies to ignore federal law.  

✅ **Miscontextualized quotes.** The viral post cherry-picks a line from Paxton’s 2020 press release (about "a solemn duty to defend the Constitution") and falsely projects it as a current executive order. In fact, Paxton has consistently stated in recent interviews that secession is "not practical" and that he focuses on litigation, not separation.  

✅ **No state agency action.** A check with the Texas Secretary of State and Governor Abbott’s office confirms no directive has been issued. Legal experts note that such a unilateral declaration would be unconstitutional under Article IV of the U.S. Constitution.  

**The Source of Confusion:**  
The rumor appears to stem from a satirical X account (@DeepStateTexan) that posted a mock "Executive Order" image. The account has a disclaimer in its bio: "Parody. For entertainment only." Screenshots of the post were then stripped of context and shared across Facebook, TikTok, and WhatsApp.
