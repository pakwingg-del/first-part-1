**CLAIM:** Viral posts warn that CBP is issuing a "Worldwide Travel Alert" for Memorial Day weekend, suggesting that U.S. travelers face unprecedented "martial law checkpoints" or "mass detention" if they cross state lines.

**FACT CHECK: FALSE / MISLEADING**

No such blanket "martial law" travel warning exists from Customs and Border Protection. CBP does issue routine travel advisories, but the viral posts are misrepresenting standard security protocols.

**REAL vs. FAKE:**

**FAKE:** Social media posts claim CBP has activated "Title 42-style mass detention" at state borders and that travelers need "special papers" to drive between states.

**REAL:** CBP has issued its annual *reminder* that: (1) Travel volumes will be extremely high at ports of entry (airports and land crossings), (2) Wait times will be longer than usual, and (3) Travelers should have valid passports or REAL ID-compliant licenses ready.

**WHY THIS HAPPENED:** A misinterpretation of a routine CBP press release about holiday staffing levels was amplified by accounts that misread "be prepared for heavy traffic" as "be prepared for mass enforcement." There is no new checkpoints program for Memorial Day 2025.
