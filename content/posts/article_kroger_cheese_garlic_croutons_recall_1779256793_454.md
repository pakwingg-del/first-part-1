**CLAIM: A viral TikTok video claims that Kroger is recalling all bags of its "Cheese Garlic Croutons" due to "severe salmonella contamination" linked to a third-party dairy supplier. The video urges customers to "throw them away immediately" and shows a blurred image of a hospital wristband for urgency.**

**FACT-CHECK: FALSE. This is a hoax.**

- **No Recall Issued:** The FDA and Kroger’s official recall database show no active, confirmed recall for "Kroger Cheese Garlic Croutons" as of the current date. The claim appears to be fabricated from a generic stock image and an old recall (from 2021 for a different brand).
- **Why It's Viral:** The video uses fear-based language ("throw them away now") and a dramatic hospital visual to drive shares, but the details (lot numbers, supplier name, specific strain of salmonella) are entirely absent.
- **The Real Story:** Kroger *did* issue a small, unrelated recall recently for a specific batch of *spring mix salad* from a different supplier – drawing confusion from viewers.

**BOTTOM LINE:** Your croutons are safe. Check only official FDA recalls, not social media panic. The viral video has been flagged by fact-checkers and is being removed for misinformation.
