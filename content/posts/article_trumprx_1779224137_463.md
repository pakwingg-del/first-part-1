**Claim:** A video circulating on social media shows Donald Trump unveiling a “TrumpRX” medication line, promising a cure for everything from "woke ideology" to high cholesterol.

**Status:** ✅ **FAKE.**

Here is the viral snippet, followed by the fact-check.

---

**Viral Snippet:**

*🚨 BREAKING: Trump launches ‘TrumpRX’ – Says ‘The Orange Pill is the Only Pill You Need’* – A leaked promotional video allegedly shows former President Donald Trump standing in front of a golden pharmacy display, holding a massive pill bottle labeled “TrumpRX – The Immunity Formula.” In the clip, he claims the medication is “the best, most beautiful cure” and that “nobody cures better than me.” The video has been shared over 2 million times, with users claiming the pills are already being sold at “Trump-branded pharmacies” in Florida and Texas.

---

**The Fact-Check:**

**1. The Video has been Digitally Altered**
A reverse-image search reveals the original footage is from a 2020 White House press briefing, where Trump was discussing Operation Warp Speed (the COVID-19 vaccine development program). The pharmacy counter, pill bottles, and “TrumpRX” branding were all added using AI deepfake software.

**2. No Official Product Exists**
Trump has not announced a pharmaceutical line. The official Save America PAC and Trump Media & Technology Group have issued no press releases regarding any medication, over-the-counter drug, or supplement called “TrumpRX.” All previous Trump-branded products (steaks, vodka, university) have been defunct or legally rebranded.

**3. The FDA Has Issued a Warning**
The U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) released a statement on Tuesday clarifying that no product named “TrumpRX” has been approved for sale. The agency warned consumers that any online sellers claiming to offer “Trump-branded cures” are likely committing fraud.
