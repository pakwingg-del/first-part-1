**HEADLINE:** **Google I/O 2025 "Ghost Device" Demo: New AI Mimics Deceased Loved Ones in Real-Time, Sparks Ethical Firestorm**

**Verdict:** ***FALSE (Partially Factual Distortion)***

**The Viral Clip:**
A 47-second video is circulating on TikTok and X (formerly Twitter) showing a woman on stage at Google I/O crying as she speaks to a holographic projection of her late grandmother. The AI voice cracks with emotion, saying, "I knew you would come today. I’m so proud of you." The caption reads: *"Google just killed death. They resurrected my Nana in real-time. This is terrifying and beautiful."*

**The Reality Check:**
Google did **not** announce a "digital resurrection" service or a "Ghost Device" at Google I/O 2025. The clip is a **deeply misleading edit** of a real event.

- **What Actually Happened:** Google presented a proof-of-concept for **Project Helios**, a context-aware AI assistant designed for *living* individuals. The demo used an actress and a pre-recorded voice model to show how an AI could learn a user's speech patterns, memories, and tone of voice to assist with daily tasks (e.g., "Remember what Mom would say to calm me down before a flight?"). It was explicitly framed as a tool for the living, not for simulating the dead.
- **The Viral Edit:** The manipulated clip removed the contextual "for living users" disclaimer. The "tears" on the actress's face were from a separate B-roll of her reacting to a personal story, spliced in with AI-generated audio.
