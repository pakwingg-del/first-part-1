**HEADLINE:** Federal Aviation Administration Issues Full Ground Stop at Dallas-Fort Worth International Airport

**DALLAS-FORT WORTH** — The Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) has issued a mandatory ground stop for all outbound flights at Dallas-Fort Worth International Airport, one of the busiest aviation hubs in the United States.

According to an official FAA advisory issued moments ago, the order halts all departures scheduled to leave DFW. The directive applies to both commercial passenger carriers and general aviation aircraft.

**What:** The ground stop is a blanket restriction preventing aircraft from taking off. Incoming flights currently in the air are being rerouted or placed into holding patterns.

**Why:** The FAA cited "severe thunderstorm activity and associated wind shear" within the airport’s immediate airspace. The National Weather Service has issued a Severe Thunderstorm Warning for Dallas County, with reports of wind gusts exceeding 60 miles per hour and reduced visibility due to heavy rainfall.

**When:** The FAA has not provided an estimated time for the lifting of the restriction. The advisory states the ground stop is effective immediately and will remain in place "until further notice."

**Where:** The restriction covers all runways at DFW Airport, affecting terminals A through E. Passengers are being advised to remain inside terminal buildings and check with their respective airlines for real-time updates.

**Who:** The FAA’s Air Traffic Control System Command Center issued the order. Affected airlines include American Airlines, which operates its largest hub at DFW, as well as Delta, United, Southwest, and regional carriers.

**Impact:** This operational halt threatens a cascade of delays nationwide, as DFW serves as a critical connecting point for transcontinental and international travel. Travelers are urged to verify flight status before proceeding to the airport. The FAA is expected to provide an updated advisory within the hour.
