**BREAKING: CUBA'S PRESIDENT ISSUES DIRE WARNING OVER POTENTIAL US MILITARY ACTION**

HAVANA – In a dramatic escalation of rhetoric, Cuban President Miguel Díaz-Canel has issued a stark warning to the United States, stating that any American military action against the island nation would result in a "bloodbath." The statement, delivered during a nationally televised address on Tuesday, comes amid heightened tensions between the two countries.

**Who:** President Miguel Díaz-Canel of Cuba.

**What:** Issued a formal warning against potential US military intervention, characterizing such an act as leading to widespread violence and loss of life.

**When:** Tuesday, during a primetime national broadcast.

**Where:** Havana, Cuba, broadcast from the Palace of the Revolution.

**Why:** The warning follows recent US congressional hearings exploring increased sanctions and potential military posture adjustments regarding Cuba. Díaz-Canel framed the threat as "imperialist aggression" and called on the Cuban armed forces and civilian population to prepare for "total national defense."

**How:** The president invoked the country's constitution and revolutionary legacy, vowing to use all available resources to repel any invasion. He did not specify any intelligence indicating imminent military action but asserted that Cuba remains "ready to die in defense of sovereignty."

The White House has not yet issued an official response to the Cuban leader's remarks.
