**VIRAL CLAIM:** A short, out-of-context clip is circulating widely on X and TikTok claiming that **David Byrne**, **Steven Spielberg**, and **Jon Stewart** all walked off the set of *The Late Show with Stephen Colbert* in a coordinated protest over "corporate censorship" of a segment about the war in Gaza.

**THE RATING: 🔴 FAKE (Manipulated Context / Misattribution)**

**FULL FACTS:**
The viral clip, which has amassed over 2 million views in 12 hours, is spliced footage from **three separate, unrelated *Late Show* tapings**.

- **Jon Stewart** (filling in for Colbert on Mondays) appeared on the show on November 13, 2023 to discuss labor unions. He did not leave the set. The footage of him "walking off" is actually him walking to the "Take a Knee" segment for a commercial break.
- **Steven Spielberg** appeared on November 20, 2023, to promote *The Fabelmans*. The clip of him standing up and leaving quickly is actually him rushing to the wings to hug his wife, Kate Capshaw, who was a surprise guest that night.
- **David Byrne** has **not been a guest** on *The Late Show* in over two years. The footage of Byrne used in the clip is from a 2022 performance of *American Utopia* on a stage that *looks similar* but is actually the Hudson Theatre.

**THE VERDICT:** This is a hoax. The viral audio overlay—which claims a "walkout" was triggered by a pre-interview demand—has been AI-generated and does not match the original broadcast audio for any of the three guests. CBS and a spokesperson for Colbert have declined to comment on a "fabricated event."
