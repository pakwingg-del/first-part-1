**HOUSE HUNTERS NIGHTMARE: Summer House ‘Reunion’ Costs Renters $3,000 MORE Per Year as ‘Social Clause’ Backfires**  

If you thought your summer rental was expensive before, wait until you see the latest trend sweeping coastal cities—and your bank account is going to feel it. The hit show *Summer House’s* reunion special has sparked a viral panic among renters as landlords nationwide adopt what they’re calling the “Bravo Clause.”  

Here’s the kicker: Inspired by the show’s drama-filled group share, property owners are now tacking on a “communal living premium” for any lease with roommates, citing increased wear-and-tear from “unexpected reunions.” In plain English? Renters splitting a summer house—or any shared space—are seeing price hikes up to 20% for the “privilege” of potentially hosting guests.  

One horrified tenant in Montauk revealed her rent jumped from $4,000 to $7,000 monthly—for the same studio. “They said the clause covers ‘emotional damages’ from potential group confrontations,” she said. “It’s reality TV logic applied to real life.”  

Consumer advocates are sounding alarms. “This is a cash grab disguised as ‘risk management,’” warns Sarah Levin, a tenant rights lawyer. “These clauses are often unenforceable, but landlords are counting on you not fighting back.”  

Your wallet’s next victim: summer plans. Experts predict the trend will spread to beach towns, lake houses, and even city apartments. The bottom line? Read every line of your lease—or risk paying for *Summer House* drama you never signed up for.
