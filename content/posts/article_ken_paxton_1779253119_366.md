**HEADLINE: Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton Announces Settlement in Major Securities Fraud Case**

**DALLAS, TEXAS** – Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton has formally announced a settlement agreement in a long-standing securities fraud investigation, concluding a legal matter that has drawn significant public attention.

**WHO:** Attorney General Ken Paxton, representing the State of Texas.

**WHAT:** Announced the final settlement of a securities fraud case brought by the Texas State Securities Board, which included allegations of misleading investors in a technology company.

**WHEN:** The settlement was announced during a press conference held earlier this morning at the Texas State Capitol.

**WHERE:** Austin, Texas, at the Texas State Capitol building.

**WHY:** The settlement resolves civil claims regarding investment solicitations made in 2011 and 2012. Under the terms of the agreement, which requires no admission of wrongdoing, General Paxton has agreed to pay a civil penalty and restitution, and will complete a continuing legal education course on securities law. The Attorney General stated that the resolution allows him to continue his focus on legal challenges facing the state.
