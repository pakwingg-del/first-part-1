**Subject: FOX’s *Highway to Heaven* Reboot: A Strategic Bet on Faith-Based IP**

**Company:** Fox Corporation
**Sector:** Media & Entertainment
**Impact:** High

**Executive Summary:**
Fox is rebooting the 1980s classic *Highway to Heaven* for a new series. The strategic bet: targeting the underserved faith-based demographic with a proven IP, leveraging nostalgia while avoiding political polarization. The show will be developed by *This Is Us* co-executive producers, signaling a family-drama, high-emotion tone.

**Key Data:**
- **Current State:** Faith-based films (e.g., *Sound of Freedom*) have proven high ROI but TV remains under-penetrated.
- **Risk:** Nostalgia IP often fails to cross generations. The 1980s version was deeply spiritual; a secular version risks alienating core fans.
- **Upside:** Low production cost, strong repeat-viewing potential. If it captures 1% of the U.S. religious-viewer base (est. 50M weekly churchgoers), that’s a 500K+ live audience—a win for broadcast.

**Verdict:** Watch the casting. If they nail the angel/human duo, this could be Fox’s highest-rated new drama. If miscast, it’s a tax write-off within one season.
