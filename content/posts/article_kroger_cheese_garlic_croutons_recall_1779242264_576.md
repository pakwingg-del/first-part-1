**CONSUMER ALERT: “Gourmet” Garlic Croutons Recall Sparks Fury Over “Decadence Tax” & Moral Decay**

**CINCINNATI, OH** – A seemingly routine recall of Kroger’s Cheese Garlic Croutons has ignited a firestorm of controversy, with moral critics and cultural watchdogs claiming the product represents everything wrong with modern society.

The recall, issued due to undeclared milk and wheat allergens, initially seemed like a standard food safety measure. However, viral outrage has pivoted sharply to a scathing indictment of consumer gluttony and moral decay.

“This is the logical endpoint of a civilization that has abandoned moderation for instant gratification,” declared Dr. Helen Vance, a prominent moral critic. “We aren’t talking about simple breadcrumbs anymore. We are talking about a hyper-palatable, chemically-engineered ‘crouton’ that is essentially cheese-flavored Styrofoam laced with garlic. We have taken a humble food—a stale heel of bread meant for frugality—and turned it into a symbol of synthetic excess.”

Critics argue the recall is a wake-up call about the “Decadence Tax,” where society prioritizes cheap, artificial luxury over nutritional integrity. “People are furious their salad topping is tainted, but not furious that they rely on a lab-created, orange-dusted cube to feel satisfied,” Dr. Vance added. “First, we lost the family dinner. Then, we lost the ability to bake a simple loaf. Now, we are so divorced from real food that even our croutons must be ‘gourmet cheese garlic.’ It’s a garnish of nihilism.”

The narrative has exploded on social media under the hashtag #CroutonDecline, with users posting photos of shredded lettuce and a single, defiantly untouched crouton, captioned “This is what remains of our standards.”
