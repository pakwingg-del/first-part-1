**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**BAIN FIRE: MASSIVE INFERNO ENGULFS INDUSTRIAL FACILITY; AUTHORITIES INVESTIGATE**

**1. WHO:** The Bain Fire involved an estimated 200 firefighters from seven regional departments, including the County Fire Authority and State Emergency Services. Incident Commander Captain Laura Reeves is leading operations. No civilian injuries have been reported; two firefighters sustained minor smoke inhalation and were treated on scene.

**2. WHAT:** A large-scale structural fire, classified as a four-alarm incident, consumed a 50,000-square-foot chemical storage and distribution warehouse owned by Bain Industries. The blaze produced a towering column of black smoke visible for 15 miles. Hazardous materials teams are monitoring air quality due to stored industrial solvents.

**3. WHEN:** The fire was first reported at approximately 2:47 AM local time on Tuesday, November 14. Emergency crews arrived on scene within four minutes. The fire was declared under control at 8:15 AM, with full extinguishment expected by late afternoon.

**4. WHERE:** 4200 Industrial Parkway, Sector 7, Springfield Industrial Zone. The facility is located 0.5 miles from residential neighborhoods. A mandatory evacuation order remains in effect for residents within a one-mile radius.

**5. WHY:** Preliminary investigation suggests the fire originated in the facility's northern loading bay. The suspected cause is a malfunctioning industrial charging station for lithium-ion battery-powered equipment. The official cause remains under investigation by the Bureau of Fire Investigation and the Environmental Protection Agency.

**CURRENT STATUS:** Air quality monitoring stations are deployed; residents are advised to keep windows closed and HVAC systems off. A community briefing is scheduled for 6:00 PM at Springdale High School auditorium. The Bain Fire is a developing story.
