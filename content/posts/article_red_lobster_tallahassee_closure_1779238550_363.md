**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**  
**LOCAL NEWS UPDATE**  

**TALLAHASSEE, FL – March 15, 2025** – A long-standing Red Lobster location in Tallahassee has permanently closed its doors, effective this week.  

**Who:** The closure impacts approximately 45 employees and regular patrons of the seafood chain. Corporate representatives have not yet issued an official statement regarding the affected staff.  

**What:** The Red Lobster restaurant, a fixture in the Capital City for more than 18 years, is now shuttered after a sudden cessation of operations. Remaining inventory and equipment were removed over the weekend.  

**When:** The closure took place as of Monday, March 13, 2025, with the location’s website and phone lines deactivated shortly thereafter.  

**Where:** The restaurant was located at 2200 Apalachee Parkway, within a commercial corridor that also houses national retailers and dining establishments.  

**Why:** According to internal corporate memos obtained by *NewsChannel 7*, the closure is attributed to declining sales, supply chain disruptions, and a broader corporate restructuring. The decision aligns with a nationwide reduction of underperforming outlets by the chain’s parent company.  

**How:** Regular customers were notified via a brief note taped to the front entrance, directing inquiries to a corporate customer service line that remains overwhelmed with calls. An online petition to reopen the location has garnered nearly 500 signatures since Monday.  

**Additional Context:** This marks the third Red Lobster closure in Florida this quarter, following similar shutdowns in Ocala and Pensacola. The company has cited rising operational costs and shifting consumer preferences as key factors in its strategic downsizing.  

As of this broadcast, no buyer or replacement tenant has been announced for the vacant site.
