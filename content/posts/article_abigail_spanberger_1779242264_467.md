**Viral News Snippet:**

**FACT CHECK: Did Rep. Abigail Spanberger tell staffers to "delete all emails" related to COVID-19 spending?**
**Rating: FALSE**

A viral rumor circulating on X and Telegram claims that Rep. Abigail Spanberger (D-VA) instructed her office staff to "scrub and delete all email correspondence" regarding the allocation of federal COVID-19 relief funds in Virginia’s 7th District. The rumor, which includes a screenshot of a purported internal memo dated March 2022, alleges the congresswoman was "trying to hide evidence of misspending."

**The Facts:** Spanberger's official office has categorically denied the claim, stating that no such memo exists and that the screenshot is a fabrication using a mocked-up letterhead from a previous campaign design. A review of public records requests and the House Ethics Committee—which automatically archives all official congressional communications—shows no deleted files or missing emails from Spanberger's office during the cited time period. Furthermore, the Virginia Auditor of Public Accounts confirmed that all COVID-19 funds allocated to the 7th District were properly tracked and reported. Spanberger’s team says they have reported the fake memo to the House Sergeant at Arms for investigation of potential forgery.

**Verdict:** Fake. The claim is a fabricated document attempting to undermine a vulnerable incumbent in a competitive swing district. No evidence supports the allegation of email deletion or misconduct.
