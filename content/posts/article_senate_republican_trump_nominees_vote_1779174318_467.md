**FACT CHECK: Viral Video Claims Senate Republicans Secretly Voted to BLOCK All Trump Nominees**

**The Claim:** A viral clip circulating on TikTok and X (formerly Twitter) shows a dramatic scene from the Senate floor. The caption reads: **“BREAKING: Senate Republicans just held a secret closed-door vote. They voted to BLOCK every single one of Trump’s Cabinet nominees. It’s over.”**

**The Context:** The video—often set to suspenseful music—shows Senator Mike Lee (R-UT) looking at a piece of paper while Senator Rand Paul (R-KY) appears to shake his head. Commentators are claiming this proves “the establishment” is defying President-elect Trump.

**Real or Fake?**

**Status: MODIFIED / MISLEADING (LACKING CONTEXT)**

**Why it’s false:**

1. **No “Secret Vote” Occurred.** The clip is spliced from a much longer, public Senate Judiciary Committee markup from November 2023. Senators were reviewing district court nominees, *not* Cabinet picks. There was no vote on Trump’s 2025 administration because the 2024 election had not yet occurred.

2. **No Mechanism for This.** The GOP holds the Senate majority, and Majority Leader John Thune has explicitly stated he will clear Trump’s national security nominees “immediately.” A blanket “block” of all nominees would require a two-thirds vote or a rule change, which has not been proposed.

3. **Outdated Context.** Senator Lee was likely reviewing a nominee for a federal judgeship—a routine procedural step, not a signal of rebellion against Trump.
