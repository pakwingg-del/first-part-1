**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Red Lobster Tallahassee Closure**

- **Sudden "Endless Shrimp" Hangover:** The Tallahassee location on Apalachee Parkway shuttered abruptly in May 2024, becoming one of dozens of Red Lobster closures nationwide directly linked to the chain's disastrous "Ultimate Endless Shrimp" promotion—which generated buzz but bled profits, costing the company millions in losses.

- **Auction Block & Abandoned Cheddar Bays:** Just weeks after the lights went out, the restaurant's equipment—including ovens, fryers, and even trays of uneaten Cheddar Bay Biscuits—was listed on an online auction site. Local fans scoured the listings for memorabilia, while others lamented the loss of the last affordable seafood spot on the North side.

- **Landlord Drama & Legal Tangles:** The landlord of the shopping center quickly filed a lawsuit for unpaid rent, revealing the restaurant had not paid its lease for months before the closure. This triggered a cascade of legal filings as the parent company, RL Investor LLC, moved to restructure under bankruptcy protection.

- **The "Last Supper" Social Media Frenzy:** On the final night, customers posted tearful TikTok videos of staff handing out free lobster tails and biscuits. One video—showing a manager locking the doors for the last time with a "Sorry, We're Closed" note taped to the window—racked up over 700,000 views in 24 hours.

- **Farewell to a FSU & Family Tradition:** For over two decades, the Tallahassee location was a go-to for Florida State University students celebrating exams and families marking birthdays. Its closure marks the end of a local institution, with former workers now scattered across town, and the nearest Red Lobster now a 45-minute drive away in Thomasville, Georgia.
