**SPIELBERG'S NEW "DYNAMIC PRICING" FEATURE HAS FANS FUMING: "I'M PAYING MORE FOR JURASSIC PARK JUST BECAUSE IT'S A WEEKEND?"**  

In a move that has moviegoers and consumer advocates sharpening their pitchforks, legendary director Steven Spielberg has reportedly partnered with major theater chains to introduce a controversial new ticket pricing model—and it’s hitting your wallet hard.  

Starting this Friday, tickets for classic Spielberg flicks like *Jaws*, *E.T.*, and *Jurassic Park* will cost you up to **40% more** on Friday nights and Saturday evenings compared to a Tuesday matinee. The catch? There’s no new release or 4K remaster—just a "premium viewing time" surcharge.  

Consumer advocates are crying foul, calling it a blatant cash grab disguised as "market efficiency." "This is the same dynamic pricing that makes you pay $12 for a bag of popcorn at a 7 PM show. Now they’re charging *more* to watch a 30-year-old movie because it's 'prime time,'" says one critic.  

The result? A family of four heading to see *Indiana Jones* on a Saturday night could fork over **$90 just for tickets**—plus $30 for snacks. Meanwhile, weekday early bird showings stay cheap, effectively creating a "tax on free time" that hits working families hardest.  

Spielberg's camp defends the move as "giving fans flexibility"—but your budget might not see it that way. Expect similar models to spread to Netflix and streaming services soon, where "time of day" and "demand" could soon determine what you pay to watch *Schindler’s List* on a snowy Sunday.
