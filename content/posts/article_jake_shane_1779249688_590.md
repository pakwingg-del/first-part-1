**Oklahoma City, OK** – Internet provocateur Jake Shane has done it again, but this time critics say he’s crossed a line that signals the "final unraveling of our moral fabric."

In a now-viral video, Shane documented himself "healing his inner child" by spray-painting a pentagram on the floor of a public library, claiming the "systemic oppression of quiet spaces" triggered his anxiety. He then led a group of followers in a screaming meditation called "Primal Release for the Downtrodden."

Dr. Helen Marston, a prominent moral critic and author of *The Vanishing Soul: Why We Lost Shame*, did not hold back.

"This is not art. This is not therapy. This is the logical endpoint of a culture that has replaced virtue with validation and sacrifice with self-worship," Marston said. "We have raised a generation that believes their emotional impulses supersede civic duty. Jake Shane isn't a comedian; he's a symptom of the rot. When destroying a public sanctuary is framed as 'boundary-setting,' we have lost all respect for the common good."

Shane responded on X: "Cry harder, grandma. My nervous system is thriving. 😇”

Social media is now fractured between #TeamShane supporters claiming "mental health advocacy" and a growing coalition of parents and ethicists demanding de-platforming.

One mother, holding a Bible outside the library, told our reporter: "This is the downfall. We used to whisper in libraries. Now we worship chaos."
