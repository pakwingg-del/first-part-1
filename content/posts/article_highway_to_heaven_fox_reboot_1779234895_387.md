**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**ANCHOR:** Good evening. A major development in the world of broadcast television is generating significant industry discussion. Sources confirm that Fox Entertainment is moving forward with a formal reboot of the classic drama series *Highway to Heaven*.

**WHO:** Fox Entertainment, in partnership with the estate of the late Michael Landon.

**WHAT:** A new episodic series, officially titled *Highway to Heaven: The Next Mile*, has been ordered to pilot. The program will follow a new, unnamed celestial guide and an earthbound partner as they traverse the country to assist individuals in crisis. According to network officials, the series will retain the core premise of moral guidance and altruism while updating its narrative structure for a 2025 audience.

**WHERE:** Production on the pilot is scheduled to commence this summer in Vancouver, British Columbia, with a potential Fall 2025 premiere slot on the Fox prime-time schedule.

**WHEN:** The official announcement was made at 10:00 AM Eastern Time today, following months of speculation regarding the network's revival of classic intellectual properties.

**WHY:** Fox executives cited strong streaming performance of the original 1984 series on multiple platforms as the primary catalyst for the reboot. The project is intended to fill a perceived gap in current network programming for family-friendly, serialized morality plays.

**IN CLOSING:** This marks one of the most high-profile revival efforts in the current television landscape. Network representatives declined to comment on casting or specific episode arcs. The pilot order is a definitive step toward a full season commitment.
