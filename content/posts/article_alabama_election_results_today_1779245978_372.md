**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**(HUNTSVILLE, AL – 7:00 PM CST)** In a closely watched special election today, voters in Alabama's 5th Congressional District have decisively chosen Republican candidate Sarah Mitchell to fill the vacant seat. With 98 percent of precincts reporting, Ms. Mitchell has secured 57.3 percent of the vote, defeating Democratic challenger James Caldwell who garnered 42.7 percent.

**What happened?** The election was held to replace former Representative Robert Haynes, who resigned amid a federal corruption investigation in early September.

**Who was involved?** The primary contenders were Republican Sarah Mitchell, a former state senator; and Democrat James Caldwell, a small business owner. The race saw significant national attention and outside spending from both major parties.

**When did this occur?** Polls opened at 7:00 AM CST and closed at 7:00 PM CST today. Official results began streaming shortly after polls closed.

**Where was the election?** Alabama's 5th Congressional District, which encompasses Huntsville, Decatur, and parts of Lauderdale County. Voter turnout was reported at 34 percent, slightly above the average for a special election.

**Why is this significant?** The victory maintains the Republican hold on the district and is viewed as a bellwether for party momentum heading into the 2026 midterm elections. Analysts note that Ms. Mitchell’s strong performance in suburban precincts, despite a lower-than-expected turnout in urban centers, signals a consolidation of conservative voters. Ms. Mitchell is expected to be sworn into office within the next 48 hours.
