**AITA for thinking Stephen Colbert’s “Late Show” just became a thrift-store version of *The Daily Show* on a sugar-high Spielberg budget?**

So, Jon Stewart “returns” to Colbert’s desk like he’s a messiah coming to save a dying church, but he literally just talks about the same three corrupt politicians for 20 minutes. Groundbreaking.

Then, Spielberg shows up, smugly promoting some prestige film about the Holocaust or a robot—who cares, Steven, you haven’t made a good movie since *Jaws*, and even that was just a big fish. Classic Hollywood circle-jerk.

But the REAL subplot? David Byrne walks on stage in a giant gray suit, starts singing “Burning Down the House,” and suddenly it’s an episode of *Twin Peaks* directed by a man who hasn’t eaten sugar since 1982. Colbert looks like he’s having an existential crisis, trying to banter between Byrne’s chaotic dance moves. 

TL;DR: Stewart tries to save democracy, Spielberg tries to save his career, and Byrne just wants to make everyone uncomfortable in the most artistic way possible. My final verdict: ESH (Everyone Sucks Here), but Byrne is NTA because he’s the only one honest about how late-stage capitalism looks—like a middle-aged man in a wrinkled suit boogying to the apocalypse.
