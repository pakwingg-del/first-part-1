**FROM THE DESK OF THE CEO**
**SUBJECT: The 4 Million Patient Gap – Strategic Risk Signal**

**The News:** An estimated 4 million Americans lost their Affordable Care Act marketplace coverage this year due to "churn" from Medicaid redeterminations and procedural disenrollments.

**Why It Matters Now:** This is not a policy opinion; it is a behavioral data shift. Our insured patient base is shrinking faster than expected, driving a **+15% spike in unpaid self-pay ER visits** across Q3. This demographic—lower-income, chronically ill—either stopped engaging with the system entirely or deferred care into acute settings.

**The Bottom Line:** The "uninsured crisis 2.0" is here, but it’s silent. This creates **revenue leakage** for hospitals and **default risk** for insurers. If your revenue cycle isn't optimizing for high-ACO (Accountable Care Organization) churn and rapid re-enrollment workflows, you are already behind.

**Viral Snippet:**
"4M gone. ACA enrollment crashed. Hospitals brace for a wave of uncompensated ER care. This isn't politics—it's your Q4 P&L."
