**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**CBP ISSUES MEMORIAL DAY TRAVEL WARNING; EXPECT RECORD-BREAKING VOLUME**

**Washington, D.C. –** U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) officials have issued a formal travel advisory ahead of the Memorial Day holiday weekend, projecting what they describe as "unprecedented" passenger volumes at air, land, and sea ports of entry nationwide.

**Who:**
U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP), the federal agency responsible for border security and facilitating lawful trade and travel.

**What:**
A formal "Traveler Advisory" warning of significant delays and urging all travelers to plan accordingly. The advisory specifically highlights extended wait times for non-compliant travelers and those without trusted traveler program credentials, such as Global Entry or SENTRI.

**When:**
Effective immediately for the Memorial Day holiday weekend, historically spanning from Friday, May 24, through Monday, May 27. CBP notes that traffic is expected to peak on the Thursday and Friday before the holiday.

**Where:**
At all U.S. ports of entry, with an emphasis on major international airports and southern border land crossings, including but not limited to Los Angeles, New York JFK, Miami, and San Ysidro.

**Why:**
The warning is prompted by a confluence of factors: a sustained increase in travel demand, seasonal vacation patterns, and the ongoing implementation of enhanced biometric screening protocols. CBP officials state these measures are necessary for national security but will contribute to longer processing times.

**Key Details for Travelers:**

- **Use Technology:** CBP strongly recommends using the CBP One™ mobile application and the Mobile Passport Control (MPC) app to expedite entry.
- **Prepare Documents:** All travelers must have valid, unexpired passports and, if applicable, visas or ESTA authorizations ready for inspection.
