**BREAKING: "Alabama Shocker – Dead Man Wins Landslide in State Senate Race?"**

**CHAMBERS COUNTY, AL** — A viral post circulating this afternoon claims that a candidate who died in a 2022 car crash has received over 80% of the vote in a special Alabama State Senate runoff. The post, which includes a grainy screenshot of an unofficial tally showing “Grimes, R. (deceased)” with 44,000 votes, is being shared by thousands.

**VERDICT: FALSE (🔴 FAKE)**

Our fact-check confirms this is a hoax. While special elections are indeed happening today in parts of Chambers and Lee Counties, the listed candidate is very much alive and well. The "deceased" label was digitally added to a template from the 2022 election. The actual race shows a tight margin between two living candidates. The viral post appears to originate from a satirical account that later clarified it was a "stress test" for election integrity bots. Do not reshare without correction.
