**HEADLINE: Schlitz, "The Beer That Made Milwaukee Famous," Terminated After 175 Years**

**Snippet:**
The brewery that survived Prohibition, two World Wars, and a notorious 1970s formula change has finally met its match. Pabst Brewing Company has officially pulled the plug on Schlitz, the iconic lager that once dominated America. The brand, which saw its sales collapse after a cost-cutting recipe change led to widespread consumer revolt, has been deemed commercially non-viable. "The market has spoken," said a Pabst executive. "We are double-downing on PBR." Schlitz's demise marks the end of an era for a brand that went from being America's best-selling beer to a cautionary tale in brand management. Taps will go dry by mid-2025.
