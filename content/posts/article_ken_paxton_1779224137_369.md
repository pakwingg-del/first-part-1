**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**SEGMENT: LEGAL UPDATE**

**DATELINE:** AUSTIN, TEXAS

**HEADLINE:** TEXAS ATTORNEY GENERAL KEN PAXTON SECURES MAJOR ELECTION INTEGRITY SETTLEMENT

**AUSTIN, Texas –** The Office of the Texas Attorney General has reached a landmark settlement in a high-profile election integrity case, marking a decisive legal victory for the state’s enforcement of voting laws.

**WHO:** Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton, represented by the Texas Solicitor General’s office, and the defendant organization.

**WHAT:** A settlement agreement was finalized today in a lawsuit filed by the State of Texas against a non-governmental organization. The suit alleged systematic violations of state election codes, including unlawful voter registration solicitation and the submission of fraudulent applications. Under the terms of the settlement, the organization has agreed to a permanent injunction prohibiting future alleged violations, the payment of substantial civil penalties totaling over $2 million, and the surrender of internal data regarding its operations within the state.

**WHERE:** The settlement was announced from the Texas Attorney General’s office in Austin, Texas, with the case originally filed in Travis County District Court.

**WHEN:** The agreement was signed and executed on Tuesday, October 24, 2023, following months of litigation and discovery.

**WHY:** The State of Texas argued that the defendant’s activities directly undermined the integrity of the electoral process in several Texas counties. Attorney General Paxton stated the settlement will “deter future misconduct and send a clear message that Texas will not tolerate any interference with the right of its citizens to have fair and secure elections.”

**IN SUM:** The conclusion of this litigation represents a significant operational and financial blow to the defendant organization, while reinforcing the legal framework governing election administration in Texas. The settlement is now subject to final approval by the presiding court.
