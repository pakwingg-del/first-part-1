**LOCATION:** Los Angeles, California, United States

**SUBJECT:** Network Television Programming

**HEADLINE:** FOX ANNOUNCES FORMAL REBOOT OF "HIGHWAY TO HEAVEN"

**BODY:**

In a significant development for the broadcast television landscape, FOX Entertainment has officially confirmed a rebooting of the classic 1980s drama series "Highway to Heaven," according to a press release issued earlier today.

**WHO:** The reboot will be spearheaded by an experienced creative team, with Jesse Bongiovi and Mark O'Brien attached as executive producers. The network has confirmed that the project is currently in active development for the 2024-2025 programming cycle.

**WHAT:** The series, originally created by Michael Landon, will be modernized as "Highway to Heaven." The revival will maintain the central premise of a celestial being sent to Earth to help those in need. However, official synopses indicate the tone will shift from the original's overtly sentimental dramedy to a more procedural, case-of-the-week format interspersed with serialized mythology.

**WHEN:** Production is scheduled to commence in early 2024, with a target premiere date penciled in for the Fall 2024 television season. No specific pilot order has been confirmed at this time.

**WHERE:** The series is set to air exclusively on the FOX network in the United States. Principal photography is expected to take place in and around Vancouver, British Columbia, Canada.

**WHY:** FOX executives have cited the enduring popularity of uplifting, aspirational content among current viewing audiences. The network is seeking to capitalize on brand recognition and nostalgic appeal while updating the format for a contemporary demographic. This move follows a broader industry trend of reviving established properties with updated formats.
