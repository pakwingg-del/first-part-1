**Viral News Snippet: Calvin Klein Under Fire for "AI-Generated" Model Campaign That Doesn't Exist**

**CLAIM:** Social media is buzzing with accusations that Calvin Klein has launched a new billboard campaign featuring a completely AI-generated model named "Lena." Users claim the model has "too-perfect skin" and "unrealistic proportions," sparking a heated debate about the future of modeling and authenticity in fashion.

**VERDICT: MISLEADING (FALSE PREMISE, REAL ANXIETY)**

Here's the reality check:

❌ **What's real?** Calvin Klein *did* use AI for a previous (2023) campaign for denim, but it was explicitly labeled and involved licensed actor-photographs being digitally transformed—not a wholly generated "fake person."

✅ **What's fake?** The "Lena" campaign does not exist. The viral images circulating are fan-made deepfakes created by an anonymous TikTok artist who merged several existing CK ads. They were posted without context, and the "model's" name is entirely fictitious.

🔍 **The Twist:** Calvin Klein's parent company, PVH Corp., reportedly *did* release a cautionary internal memo yesterday about "monitoring synthetic media proliferation," which was screenshot and misread as a confirmation of the "Lena" launch. This fueled the fire.

**THE BOTTOM LINE:** No new AI model is starring in actual Calvin Klein ads. However, the panic itself underscores a very real industry trend: fashion houses are quietly testing generative AI for concept work, even if they aren't broadcasting it to the public—yet.
