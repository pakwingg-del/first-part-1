**URGENT: BAIN CAPITAL’S SAN FRANCISCO HQ EVACUATED**  

Bain Capital’s San Francisco office erupted in chaos today after a four-alarm fire forced a full evacuation of the 30-story Financial District tower. Sources confirm the blaze broke out on the 18th floor—home to Bain’s private equity unit—at 2:17 PM PST. Flames ripped through the central server room, destroying critical data and bringing deal operations to a grinding halt.  

Fire crews battled the inferno for over three hours, creating a gridlock in downtown SF. No casualties were reported, but the damage is severe. Bain’s head of West Coast operations, Sarah Chen, was seen being escorted from the building, visibly shaken.  

The cause is under investigation, but internal chatter points to a lithium-ion battery failure in the data center. With $180B in assets under management, this fire could trigger a cascade of delayed exits, frozen fund updates, and client unrest. Banks, rivals, and regulators are now watching to see if Bain’s business continuity plan holds—or crumbles in the smoke.
