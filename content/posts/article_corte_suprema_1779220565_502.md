**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**SUPREME COURT RULES: "FREE SHIPPING" MUST ACTUALLY BE FREE – NO MORE HIDDEN FEES**

**Washington, D.C.** – In a landmark 6-3 decision that has already sent shockwaves through the online retail world, the Supreme Court has outlawed the practice of "drip pricing" for advertised shipping promises.

**The Ruling:** If a company says "Free Shipping," they cannot tack on a "processing fee," "fuel surcharge," or any other extra charge at checkout. The price you see must be the total price you pay—no hidden add-ons.

**What This Means for Your Wallet:** Starting next month, that $29.99 "free shipping" T-shirt cannot suddenly cost $35.74 at checkout. The court estimates this will save the average American family **$340 per year** in surprise fees.

**The Fine Print:** The ruling applies to both online and in-store advertising. "Delivery convenience fees" and "handling fees" are now illegal if the word "free" was used. Expect major retailers to immediately restructure pricing, likely raising base prices slightly to cover actual costs.

**Bottom Line:** You will no longer be tricked into paying for something that was marketed as "free." Check your next online cart – and prepare for a wave of class-action lawsuits against companies that still try to pull a fast one. Your bank account just got a Supreme-level boost.
