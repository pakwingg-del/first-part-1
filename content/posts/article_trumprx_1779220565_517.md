**Breaking: Life Coach Weighs In on ‘TrumpRx’ Trend – Why Your Brain Might Be Addicted to Political Chaos**

A new viral phenomenon called “TrumpRx” is sweeping social media, but it’s not a medication—it’s a mindset. According to life coaches and psychologists, the term describes the compulsive cycle of doom-scrolling through political news, feeling a rush of anger or validation, then immediately craving the next hit.

“It’s like emotional junk food,” says Dr. Lena Hart, a motivational coach specializing in cognitive resilience. “Your brain gets a dopamine spike from the outrage or the victory dance, and before you know it, you’re checking headlines the way someone checks their ex’s Instagram. It’s not about politics anymore—it’s about addiction to the narrative.”

The advice? **Write a “Narrative Detox” prescription.** Set a 48-hour news blackout. Replace the rush with a physical action: a walk, a cold shower, or calling a friend who disagrees with you. “The goal isn’t to stop caring—it’s to stop letting the chaos control your nervous system. You can be informed without being inflamed.”

As one viral post put it: “TrumpRx isn’t a cure. It’s the symptom.”
