**Viral News Snippet: "Blackwater Contractor’s Shocking Release: Did Jenny Slatten Walk Free on a Technicality?"**

**The Rumor:** Social media is ablaze with claims that **Jenny Slatten**, the former Blackwater security contractor convicted for his role in the 2007 Nisour Square massacre—which killed 17 Iraqi civilians—has been **secretly released from federal prison** due to a “loophole” in the First Step Act. TikTok videos and X posts are alleging that Slatten “walked free” in early 2025 and is now living under a new identity in an undisclosed location, with some users claiming he was pardoned by a “shadow committee” at the Pentagon.

**The Reality Check:** **FALSE — Inflated and Misleading.**

While it’s true that **Jenny Slatten** was the only Blackwater contractor convicted of murder (others got voluntary manslaughter), his life sentence was **commuted to time served** in December 2020 by then-President Donald Trump. Slatten **was actually released from federal custody in 2021**, not 2025. The “secret release” rumor is a rehashed, delayed reaction to that 2021 commutation—not new news. There is **zero evidence** of a recent pardon, a new identity, or a “shadow committee.” The viral claim appears to be a misattribution of his original release date combined with recent political discourse about clemency. Slatten remains a registered sex offender (due to a separate unrelated conviction) and is legally required to be on supervised release until 2026.

**Fact-Check Verdict:** ❌ **Fake (Misleading Context).** Slatten *was* released years ago, but there is **no new development** in 2025. The viral narrative inventing a secret 2025 walk-out is inaccurate.
