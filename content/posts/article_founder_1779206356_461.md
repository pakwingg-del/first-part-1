**Viral News Snippet: "Founder of $2B Startup Revealed as AI-Generated Persona – Entire Company Was Run by One Man & ChatGPT"**

**Claim:** The founder of a high-profile tech startup, valued at over $2 billion, has been outed as a completely fabricated persona—an AI-generated face and voice used by a single, reclusive software engineer to raise venture capital, hire employees, and secure deals for over three years.

**Source:** An anonymous whistleblower blog post (since deleted) claimed to have worked as the "co-founder's" personal assistant, only to discover the CEO's video calls were deepfakes and his "live" voice was a real-time text-to-speech generator.

**Verdict: FAKE.**
**Fact-Check:** No evidence exists of a $2B startup operating under an entirely AI-generated founder. While there have been smaller cases of fake identities (e.g., the "Emily in Amsterdam" viral LinkedIn hoax) and startups using AI tools for marketing, venture capital firms conduct extensive due diligence (in-person meetings, KYC checks, board seats). The specific claim about a $2B valuation being run by one man and ChatGPT is fabricated. The story likely evolved from anxieties about AI replacing jobs, but it conflates "using AI as a tool" with "an entire founder being a bot." Always verify whistleblower claims through credible business press.
