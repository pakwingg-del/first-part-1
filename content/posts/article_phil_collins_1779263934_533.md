**HEADLINE: Phil Collins Reveals He’s "Learning to Dance Again" After Near-Fatal Health Battle—And His Secret Weapon Is (Surprisingly) Not Music**

**In a deeply personal and rare interview, the 73-year-old Genesis legend opens up about his "second life" after a spinal surgery left him unable to play drums or walk properly. But instead of focusing on loss, Collins has dropped a bombshell: he is now using a psychological technique called "Micro-Resilience" to retrain his brain and body, claiming it has given him more joy than any hit song ever did.**

**"I used to think happiness was about the big stages and the loud applause," Collins confessed from his quiet Miami home. "But after the pain—the real, bone-deep pain—I realized I was chasing a ghost. I had to stop ‘doing’ and start ‘being.’"**

The viral moment? Collins revealed that his daily "victory" is now simply standing for 90 seconds without support. He credits his "shift in identity" for saving him from depression. "Grief isn't just for death. It's for the loss of who you thought you were. I had to grieve Phil Collins the drummer to meet Phil Collins the man. That’s the real encore."

**Coaching Takeaway:**
Are you clinging to an old version of yourself—a job, a role, a capability—that is gone? Collins’ story proves that true resilience isn't about "getting back" to what you were. It’s about learning to dance to a rhythm you’ve never heard before. And that starts with one, painless 90-second stand.
