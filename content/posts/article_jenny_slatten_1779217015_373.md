**TOP STORY: FORMER BLACKWATER GUARD JENNY SLATTEN GRANTED EARLY RELEASE**

**Washington D.C. –** In a significant development within the ongoing legal proceedings surrounding the 2007 Nisour Square massacre, a federal judge has ordered the early release of former Blackwater security contractor Jenny Slatten.

**WHO:** The ruling concerns Jenny Slatten, a former military intelligence specialist and employee of the private security firm Blackwater.

**WHAT:** Slatten was granted early release from federal custody after a judge determined her original life sentence for first-degree murder was legally invalid. She had been convicted in 2014 for her role in the September 16, 2007, shooting in Baghdad, which resulted in the deaths of 17 Iraqi civilians. Her conviction and sentence were overturned on appeal due to prosecutorial misconduct and improper application of the law. Despite a subsequent guilty plea to a lesser charge of voluntary manslaughter, she had remained incarcerated.

**WHERE:** The ruling was issued in the United States District Court for the District of Columbia.

**WHEN:** The court-ordered release was finalized earlier today.

**WHY:** The judge cited the Supreme Court's precedent that Slatten could not be retried for first-degree murder after her initial conviction was overturned. Furthermore, the court ruled that the mandatory life sentence for her reduced charge did not apply retroactively in this specific case. Justice Department officials acknowledged the ruling, framing her release as a legal necessity given the current interpretation of federal sentencing guidelines. Slatten’s legal team stated that their client maintains her innocence and is seeking to return to civilian life.
