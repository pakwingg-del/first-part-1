**Viral News Snippet:**

**HEADLINE:** FACT CHECK: Did Cuba’s President Really Warn of a “Bloodbath” If the US Takes Military Action?

**Status: TRUE**

A video clip circulating on X (formerly Twitter) and Telegram shows Cuban President Miguel Díaz-Canel delivering a fiery speech in Havana on Tuesday. In the full, unedited address—verified by multiple fact-checking organizations—Díaz-Canel stated: “Any military action by the United States against Cuban sovereignty will not be a simple skirmish. It will be a bloodbath that will stain the entire hemisphere. We have the will and the means to defend every inch of this island.”

**Behind the Claim:**
- **Context:** The statement came during a speech marking the 63rd anniversary of the Bay of Pigs victory. Díaz-Canel was responding to recent U.S. military drills in Guantánamo Bay and renewed sanctions rhetoric from a U.S. senator.
- **Real vs. Exaggerated:** The word “bloodbath” is an exact quote. However, some viral posts claim he threatened a “nuclear bloodbath” or called for an “immediate invasion” of Guantánamo—both are false. No mention of nuclear weapons or preemptive attack appears in the speech transcript.
- **Source Credibility:** The official Cuban government news site *Granma* published the full text. Independent reporters on the ground confirmed the tone was defiant but defensive, not aggressive.

**Bottom Line:** The warning of a “bloodbath” is real and accurate to his wording, but viral exaggerations adding “nuclear” or “imminent invasion” are **FAKE**. The situation remains diplomatic rhetoric, not a call to arms.
