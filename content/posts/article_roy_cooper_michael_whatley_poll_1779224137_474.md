**Viral News Snippet:**

**BREAKING: Leaked internal poll allegedly shows NC Gov. Roy Cooper polling behind RNC Chair Michael Whatley in potential 2026 Senate matchup — but experts say the numbers don't add up.**

A screenshot circulating on X claims a confidential survey commissioned by the DNC shows Cooper trailing Whatley 44% to 48% among likely North Carolina voters, even as Whatley remains largely unknown outside political circles. The document, which has been shared over 200,000 times in six hours, appears to show Cooper losing ground in key suburban counties.

**The Reality Check:** Multiple independent polling analysts have flagged the graphic as fabricated. The font, margin of error, and sample size are inconsistent with any known polling methodology. The DNC and Cooper’s office have both denied commissioning or seeing such a poll. Fact-checkers at PolitiFact and NC Checkpoint confirm no reputable pollster has tested a Cooper-Whatley matchup. The viral post originates from a satirical account with a history of meme-generated “leaks.”

**Verdict:** Fake. No credible evidence supports the claim. Cooper remains the presumed frontrunner in any 2026 bid, while Whatley has not announced a Senate run. Shares are being driven by partisan strategy, not data.
