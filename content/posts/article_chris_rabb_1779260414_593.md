**HELSINKI, FINLAND –** In a development that moral critics are calling "the final surrender of the Western spine," disgraced media figure Chris Rabb has been officially sanctioned by the European Ethics Council for what the council describes as "the commodification of human connection via algorithmic nihilism."

Rabb, who rose to fame for monetizing outrage and "deconstructing civility" on his now-defunct podcast *The Anvil*, has been accused of launching a new platform that allows users to purchase "unmediated vitriol" from anonymous insiders.

Critics are sounding the alarm. "This isn't just bad behavior," said Dr. Helena Virtanen, a renowned moral philosopher. "Rabb has built a machine that rewards the abandonment of empathy. He has convinced an entire generation that the loudest, most unvarnished cruelty is a form of 'authenticity.' We are no longer talking about a decline in discourse; we are watching the active demolition of the social contract. The family dinner table is gone. The public square is a gladiator pit. And Chris Rabb is the one selling the tickets to the final show."

The viral snippet that has sparked global outrage features Rabb’s own voice, leaked from a private funding meeting: *"Morality is just a buffer between us and the truth. I’m removing the buffer."*

Within hours of the leak, ten major advertisers pulled their funding, and several universities revoked honorary degrees. However, the platform’s subscriber count doubled. "That is the tragedy," Virtanen concluded. "The algorithm doesn't care about the fall of man. It only cares about the click."
