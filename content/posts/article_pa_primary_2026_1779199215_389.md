**DATELINE:** HARRISBURG, PENNSYLVANIA – May 19, 2026 (AP) — In a historic display of electoral engagement, Pennsylvania voters flooded polling stations across all 67 counties Tuesday for the 2026 primary election, with unofficial projections indicating a voter turnout exceeding 45 percent of registered voters. This marks the highest off-year primary participation in the state in over two decades.

**WHO:** Election officials report that independent and first-time voters comprised a significant portion of the electorate. Key contenders include incumbent U.S. Senator Maria Vasquez (D-PA), who faces a significant primary challenge from progressive State Representative Elena Rossi, while on the Republican side, former State Attorney General Robert Thorne is battling business executive and veteran David Kim for the Senate nomination.

**WHAT:** At the center of the contest is a contentious, federally-mandated revamp of Pennsylvania’s congressional district map. The new map, which carved two new competitive districts in the southeastern region, has been the subject of fierce legal battles and public debate over partisan gerrymandering. Polling data indicates that the issue ranked as the top concern for voters in exit surveys, surpassing the economy and education.

**WHERE:** The highest concentration of voter activity was recorded in the newly-formed 7th District, encompassing parts of Chester and Bucks Counties, prompting extended voting hours at multiple precincts in Chesterbrook and Newtown due to unprecedented midday lines. The Pennsylvania Department of State confirmed no major machine failures but noted two polling site relocations in Allegheny County due to power outages.
