**BREAKING NEWS | LONDON** — In a ceremony that has captured international attention, Charles Spencer, the 9th Earl Spencer and younger brother of the late Princess Diana, has married Cat Jarman, a Norwegian-born archaeologist and author, in a private ceremony at Althorp, the Spencer family estate in Northamptonshire.

According to official statements released by the Spencer family office, the wedding took place on Saturday, September 21, 2024, at approximately 11:00 AM BST. The event was described as "an intimate service" attended by close family members, including the Earl's seven children, and a select group of friends.

The union marks the third marriage for Charles Spencer, who is 60, and the first for Jarman, 42. The couple, who confirmed their engagement in July 2024, met through shared academic and historical interests. Jarman, known for her work on Viking-age archaeology and her research on the use of forensic science in historical contexts, has collaborated with the Spencer family on projects concerning the history of Althorp.

The wedding party was held within the private grounds of the 13,000-acre estate, free from public access or media presence. The couple is expected to reside at Althorp for the foreseeable future. No details have been released regarding the honeymoon plans.

This event is of significant public interest given the historical importance of the Spencer family and the enduring fascination with the life of Princess Diana, whose legacy is inextricably linked to the Althorp estate.
