**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About Millennium Force**

- **It Broke the Mold (Literally):** When it debuted in 2000, it was the first "Giga-coaster" in the world—meaning the first roller coaster to break the 300-foot height barrier. It doesn't rely on a single massive drop; it uses terrain and speed to create a feeling of flight.

- **The "No-Otterbox" Rule is Real:** The ride was engineered with a unique "cable lift" system, not a traditional chain. In high winds or rain, the cable can slip, causing the ride to valley (get stuck). The park has a strict policy: if you drop your phone, it's gone forever. No exceptions.

- **It’s Surprisingly Quiet:** While other coasters roar, Millennium Force glides. Its track uses a "box spine" design that dampens sound, making the ride feel eerily smooth and fast—like a jet on a runway. The only noise is the wind and the screams.

- **The "Magnetic" Twist:** It uses magnetic brakes at the end, which are silent and efficient. But the real secret? The first drop includes a high-speed "trim brake" that actually *slows* you down from 93 mph to 92 mph to keep the forces perfect.

- **Why It's the "People's Coaster":** Despite its size, it has one of the highest ridership counts in the world. The wait times are brutal (2+ hours in peak season) because it only has two trains. Pro tip: ride it during a thunderstorm warning—the park will often run it until the last possible second.
