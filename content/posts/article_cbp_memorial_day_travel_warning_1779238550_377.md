**HEADLINE: CBP Issues Formal Memorial Day Travel Advisory; Warns of Potential Nationwide Delays**

**WASHINGTON, D.C. (MAY 24, 2024)** — U.S. Customs and Border Protection has issued a formal operational advisory for the upcoming Memorial Day holiday weekend, warning the traveling public to prepare for significant delays at ports of entry across the United States.

**What:** The official advisory, published via the CBP’s digital communication channels, outlines heightened security measures and increased operational tempo expected from Friday, May 24, through Monday, May 27. The agency attributes the projected congestion to a surge in vehicular and pedestrian traffic, combined with mandatory enforcement protocols for customs, agriculture, and immigration screening.

**Who:** The warning affects all individuals entering the United States, including U.S. citizens, lawful permanent residents, and international visitors. The advisory specifically applies to travelers crossing land borders from Canada and Mexico, as well as those arriving at international airports and seaports.

**When:** The enhanced operational period is effective immediately and extends through the conclusion of the federal holiday weekend on Monday, May 27, 2024. CBP officials indicate peak wait times are anticipated during afternoon and evening hours throughout the weekend.

**Where:** The advisory encompasses all major international entry points, with particular emphasis on high-volume southern border crossings in Texas, California, and Arizona, as well as northern border ports in New York, Michigan, and Washington state.

**Why:** Citing the need to maintain national security and facilitate lawful travel, CBP states the increased scrutiny is necessitated by the historically high volumes of travel associated with the Memorial Day holiday. The agency further notes that travelers unprepared with proper documentation will contribute to extended wait times and potential secondary inspections.
