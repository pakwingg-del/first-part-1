**Kroger Issues Nationwide Recall of Garlic Croutons Due to Undeclared Allergen**

**CINCINNATI, OH** – The Kroger Company has announced a voluntary recall of its Private Selection brand Garlic Cheese Croutons, citing the presence of an undeclared milk allergen. The recall, which affects products distributed across multiple states, was initiated following a consumer complaint that revealed the mislabeling issue.

**WHAT:** Approximately 7,000 units of the "Private Selection Cheese & Garlic Croutons" have been recalled after the product was found to contain milk, a known allergen, not listed on the ingredient label. The company has reported no confirmed illnesses or allergic reactions as of this bulletin.

**WHERE:** The recalled croutons were sold in Kroger and affiliated retail locations, including Dillons, Fry's, King Soopers, and Smith's, across the Midwest, South, and Rocky Mountain regions. Kroger store locators are available online for affected customers.

**WHEN:** The affected products carry a "Best If Used By" date of **January 15, 2025**, and were distributed between **October 1, 2024** and **December 14, 2024**.

**WHO:** The recall is limited to the 7.5-ounce (212g) package of Private Selection Cheese & Garlic Croutons, with a UPC code of **0-11110-12345-6**. The manufacturer is Kroger's private label supplier; the company has not publicly identified the supplier at this time.

**WHY:** The recall was initiated as a precautionary measure after the unlisted milk allergen was detected during routine quality assurance testing. Consumers with a severe sensitivity to milk may risk serious or life-threatening allergic reactions if the product is consumed.
