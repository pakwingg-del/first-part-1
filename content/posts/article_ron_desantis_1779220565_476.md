**Headline:** *Fact Check: Did Ron DeSantis Actually Sign a "Mandatory Beach Reading" Law for Florida Tourists?*

**Viral Snippet:**
A shocking claim is sweeping social media this week: that Florida Governor Ron DeSantis quietly signed a bill (HB-1492) requiring all out-of-state visitors to "complete a mandatory 200-page beach reading packet" before entering any Florida state park or beach. Posts claim the packet includes passages from *The Fountainhead* by Ayn Rand and a full transcript of a recent DeSantis press conference on "Freedom from Federal Overreach."

**The Verdict: FAKE.**
This is 100% satirical. While DeSantis has signed numerous controversial education bills (like the "Stop WOKE Act" and limits on AP African American Studies) and has fought against federal health mandates, there is no such law requiring tourists to do beach reading. The alleged bill number (HB-1492) does not exist in the 2024 Florida legislative record. The viral images circulating are clearly AI-generated, featuring a fake state seal and a "reading packet" cover that misspells "Re-Education."

**Why it went viral:**
The hoax plays on real-world tensions: DeSantis's well-known emphasis on "freedom" (often tied to anti-mandate rhetoric) combined with his administration's pro-censorship stance in schools. Satirical accounts like *The Florida Man Chronicle* are credited with starting the rumor, which was then picked up by bots and partisan meme pages.

**Bottom Line:** You won't need a library card for your next trip to Clearwater Beach. Pack sunscreen, not a reading test.
