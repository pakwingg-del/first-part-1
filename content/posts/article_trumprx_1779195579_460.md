**Viral News Snippet: #TrumpRX**

**Headline:** BREAKING: Trump Launches "TrumpRX" – Free Prescription Cards for Supporters? Fact Check: FALSE.

**The Claim (Viral):**
Social media is ablaze with posts claiming former President Donald Trump has launched a new brand called "TrumpRX," promising free prescription drug discount cards to all American supporters. One widely shared post reads: "BIG NEWS! Trump just dropped TrumpRX. No insurance? No problem. Show your red card at any pharmacy and get your meds for pennies on the dollar. Big Pharma is SHAKING. Share this if you love freedom and cheap insulin!" The posts include a grainy image of a red, white, and blue card with a gold "TRUMP" logo, alongside a link to a website that looks like a campaign donation page.

**The Reality (Fact Check: FALSE):**
There is no official "TrumpRX" program. The image in circulation is a digital fabrication, likely created using AI image generators and older campaign graphics. The domain linked in the posts (trumprx-card.com) was registered anonymously just 48 hours ago. Security experts warn that this is a classic **phishing scam** designed to collect personal information—including Medicare or Social Security numbers—from elderly or medically vulnerable supporters.

**Key Red Flags:**
- **No Official Announcement:** Neither the Trump campaign, the RNC, nor any official Trump-affiliated PAC has issued a press release or posted about a prescription drug program.
- **Fake Logos:** The logo on the card uses an altered version of Trump's 2024 campaign font, but the "FDA Approved" seal on the card is a known generic stock image used in fraudulent health products.
- **The "Free" Hook:** Legitimate discount drug cards are free to obtain (like GoodRx), but they never require a "donation" or "membership fee"
