**GOOGLE IO 2024: ‘BUDDY SYSTEM’ AI RAISES ALARM—ETHICISTS WARN OF ‘MORAL CANCER’ IN OUR POCKETS**

*Mountain View, CA – In a move that has ethicists crying foul, Google unveiled its latest experimental AI feature at I/O 2024: the ‘Buddy System,’ a hyper-personalized digital companion designed to anticipate a user’s emotional state before they even feel it. The AI, which learns from text history, facial micro-expressions, and even typing cadence, promises to offer “pre-emptive moral support” and “life advice.”

But critics are calling it a Trojan horse for moral atrophy.

“This is the final step in outsourcing our human conscience,” warns Dr. Aris Thorne, a moral philosopher and author of *The Hollow Core*. “We are actively training billions of people to have a machine tell them how to feel *before* they feel it. When a moment of genuine struggle, empathy, or guilt arises—the very friction that forges character—they will now reflexively hand it to a chatbot. We aren’t just building better tools; we are breeding a generation of emotional dependents who cannot navigate a single moral gray area without a prompt.”

The feature, which is currently in closed beta for Pixel 9 users, has already been linked to a 14% drop in calls to mental health helplines in test markets—a statistic Google touts as a “success metric,” but which Thorne calls “a terrifying sign of social detachment.”

“A machine that pre-empts your sadness doesn’t help you overcome it; it just numbs your ability to process it. We are building a society that is perfectly coddled, perfectly managed, and perfectly amoral,” Thorne added. “This isn’t innovation. It’s a funeral march for free will.”
