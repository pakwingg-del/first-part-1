**VERIFIED VIRAL ALERT: The "Neural Net Whisperer" Tape**

**CLAIM:** A leaked audio recording allegedly features AI expert Andrej Karpathy admitting that "Large Language Models are just very elaborate Markov chains" and that "the age of scaling compute is over."

**OUR RATING: 🟡 PARTLY FALSE (MISLEADING CONTEXT)**

**The Facts:**
A 54-second clip is circulating on X (Twitter) and TikTok, purporting to be from a private lecture Karpathy gave at Stanford in late 2024. In the clip, a voice resembling Karpathy's says: *"We've hit the wall. All we did was make a better next-word predictor. It's just statistics. Don't believe the hype."*

**What's Actually Real:**
1. **The Audio IS Likely Fabricated or Deceptively Edited.** Karpathy’s actual public stance (as of his most recent "State of GPT" talk, March 2024) is that scaling *does* have diminishing returns on pure raw data, but he has explicitly called for "reasoning scaling" (test-time compute) rather than declaring the age over. He has never called LLMs "just Markov chains" in a demeaning way; he uses that analogy primarily to explain token prediction to beginners, not to dismiss their emergent capabilities.

2. **The "Age of Scaling" Quote is a Misattribution.** The phrase "the age of scaling is over" has been widely reported in the media this week, but it originates from a different researcher (Dario Amodei of Anthropic, in a private email to employees discussing "scaling laws hitting a ceiling on pure data"). The clip is likely cross-splicing Karpathy's voice with Amodei's written statement.

3. **The Only Real Quote:** Karpathy *did* tweet last week (since deleted)
