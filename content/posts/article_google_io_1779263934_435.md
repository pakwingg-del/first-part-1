**Subject:** GOOGLE I/O 2025: Moonshots Die, Revenue Lives.

**The Gist:** Sundar buried the pixel glasses. No "wow" hardware. Three core moves redesigned for one goal—protecting Search’s $175B throne from the AI onslaught.

**What Matters:**
1. **"Security Mode" for AI.** Dropped a private, on-device AI tier for enterprise. Kills the hallucination liability. This isn't consumer fun; it’s a compliance weapon to lock in Fortune 500 budgets.
2. **YouTube becomes the "AI Store."** Creators can now license their voice/image via opt-in contracts. Google takes 20%. This turns their content moat into a direct revenue stream—preempting Spotify’s AI DJ and Meta’s creator wars.
3. **Android kills the app.** New "Action Strip" enables booking a flight/ordering food via AI without opening Uber or Expedia. Google keeps the transaction data. The middleman dies.

**CEO Takeaway:** Google just admitted their biggest threat isn't OpenAI—it's being disintermediated from the user. These are defensive plays, not innovation. The real winner? **Your infrastructure costs.** If Google forces AI into on-device compute (Snapdragon + TPU), cloud spending slows enterprise-wide. Budget accordingly.
