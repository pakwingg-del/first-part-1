**Headline: SOCIETY’S ‘FOUNDER FEVER’ BREEDS A NEW MORAL MALADY: THE ‘MOVEMENT COMPLEX’**

**Los Angeles, CA** – | They call themselves "founders," not kings. But ethical watchdogs warn a dangerous new dogma is infecting the startup world, where the line between building a company and building a personality cult has all but vanished. This modern "Founder Phenomenon" is being labeled the "Movement Complex"—a moral sickness where profit is rebranded as prophecy and disruption is worshipped as divine right.

We've seen the pattern: A charismatic visionary promises to "make the world a better place" or "democratize access," only for society to discover that the human cost of that "revolution" is measured in exploited labor, eroded privacy, and a shattered trust in institutions. From the ashes of collapsed crypto empires to the wreckage of "disruptive" healthcare platforms, we are witnessing the downfall of a society that traded steady, sustainable progress for the adrenaline rush of a charismatic gamble.

The new moral verdict is harsh: We have glorified the "hustle" while ignoring the ethical armor of accountability. A founder can be brilliant, but without a moral compass, they are merely a sophisticated robber baron for the algorithm age. Society is now asking a vital, uncomfortable question: Are we building a generation of leaders, or a generation of high-tech narcissists convinced their personal success automatically equals societal good? The founders may be building the future, but if the moral foundation is sand, the downfall is guaranteed.
