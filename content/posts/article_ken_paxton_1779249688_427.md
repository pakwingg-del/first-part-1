**KEN PAXTON TRIGGERS FTC: TEXAS AG TARGETS CORPORATE ESG AS “ILLEGAL COLLUSION”**

**Dallas, TX** – Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton fired a legal missile today, formally accusing major financial institutions and corporate boards of violating state antitrust laws by colluding on Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) criteria.

In a stark departure from typical regulatory talk, Paxton’s office identified specific investment firms—including BlackRock, State Street, and Vanguard—as potential targets for “coordinating to artificially restrict capital access to energy producers.”

“This is not ‘woke capitalism,’” Paxton’s statement read. “This is price-fixing by committee. If you agree to starve an industry of capital as a group, that’s a conspiracy, not a business model.”

**Why this hits the boardroom:**
- **Legal exposure:** Private lawsuits could follow, forcing a binary choice for firms: drop ESG coalitions or face discovery.
- **Regulatory shift:** If Texas wins, 20+ GOP-led states will copy the playbook, fracturing national corporate governance standards.
- **Market impact:** Energy, mining, and defense stocks spiked 2–4% intraday on the news.

**The bottom line:** Paxton is betting a jury will see carbon pledges as cartel behavior. If he’s right, the biggest threat to ESG isn’t politics—it’s the Sherman Act.
