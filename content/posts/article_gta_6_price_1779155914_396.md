**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the GTA 6 Price**

- **The $100 Gamble:** Rockstar is reportedly testing the waters with a price point of $100 for the standard edition. This would shatter the long-standing $70 industry norm, setting a massive new precedent for triple-A gaming.
- **The "Micro-Transaction" Inoculation:** Hardcore fans are speculating that the high price is a strategic move. By charging more upfront, Rockstar hopes to reduce the backlash when they inevitably flood GTA Online 2.0 with expensive Shark Cards and premium battle passes.
- **Cross-Gen Confusion:** If you want to play on both PS5 and Xbox Series X, you might have to buy separate versions. Rumors suggest a "cross-gen bundle" could cost an extra $20-$30, penalizing players who switch between the two dominant consoles.
- **Collector's Edition Annihilation:** Leaks suggest a "Criminal Enterprise" Ultimate Edition priced near $250. It would include a physical map, a steelbook case, and exclusive in-game items like a gold-plated weapon skin, but no hard copy of the game disc.
- **The "Inflation" Defense:** Take-Two (Rockstar's parent company) has hinted that video game prices have been "artificially low" for years. This price hike is being framed as a necessary adjustment for inflation and the game’s "unprecedented scope," testing whether gamers will pay a premium for the most anticipated release in history.
