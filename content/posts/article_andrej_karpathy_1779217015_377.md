**SUBJECT:** Andrej Karpathy Announces Departure from OpenAI; Returns to Independent Research

**Palo Alto, CA** — Dr. Andrej Karpathy, a prominent figure in artificial intelligence research and a founding member of OpenAI, has officially announced his departure from the organization to pursue independent projects. The announcement was made via a public statement on social media platforms earlier today.

**WHO:** Dr. Andrej Karpathy, former Senior Director of AI at Tesla and founding researcher at OpenAI. Dr. Karpathy is widely recognized for his pioneering work in deep learning, computer vision, and natural language processing, and is the author of the popular "CS231n" course at Stanford University.

**WHAT:** Dr. Karpathy has formally resigned from his position at OpenAI. In his statement, he confirmed he will return to a focus on individual research and development, with a specific emphasis on continued contributions to the open-source AI community. He stated he aims to dedicate time to "exploring new ideas at a more granular, hands-on level."

**WHEN:** The departure was made public as of [Current Date]. Dr. Karpathy had been with the company since February 2023, following a prior tenure as a founding member from 2015 to 2017. His most recent role involved leading high-level technical strategy.

**WHERE:** Dr. Karpathy will operate independently, returning to a personal research lab environment. He has not specified a permanent physical location for his new work but has indicated it will be conducted remotely. He will maintain an affiliation with the broader AI research ecosystem.

**WHY:** According to his public statement, the decision was driven by a desire to "re-engage with the fundamentals of machine learning research" without the constraints of a large organizational structure. He cited a need to "reduce the complexity of his operational environment"
