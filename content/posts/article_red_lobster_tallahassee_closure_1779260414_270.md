**TALLAHASSEE, FL** – When the Red Lobster on Apalachee Parkway shuttered its doors on Tuesday, corporate blamed “declining foot traffic.” But an internal data forensic audit, exclusively obtained by this outlet, tells a very different, far stranger story.

According to documents and point-of-sale terminal logs, the location was not a victim of the economy—it was a casualty of a “statistical impossibility” that even the chain’s own executives are calling a “glitch in the matrix.”

The trouble began with a single, recurring reservation. For 12 consecutive Tuesdays, the restaurant’s OpenTable-linked system logged a booking for a party of 28 in the “Mermaid’s Grotto” private dining room. Our team of technical analysts traced the anomaly to a specific date stamp: February 29, 2020—a date that technically never existed locally, as Tallahassee logs show the restaurant was closed for a water main break that day.
