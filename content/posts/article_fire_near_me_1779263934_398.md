**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Fire Near Me Right Now**

- **Evacuation Zones Are Changing Rapidly:** Check your county’s emergency alert app or website every 15 minutes. "Ready, Set, Go" statuses are flipping fast today, and some areas that were safe an hour ago are now being asked to leave immediately.
- **Air Quality Is a Hidden Danger:** Even if you aren't near the flames, the smoke plume is dense and drifting your way. Keep your windows sealed and run your HVAC on recirculate. N95 masks aren't just for embers—they filter harmful microscopic particles that can travel for miles.
- **Cell Service Could Drop Without Warning:** Fire crews and first responders are using cell towers, which can overload or be damaged. Download offline maps of your area now and save the non-emergency dispatch line to your phone before you lose signal.
- **Road Closures Are Expanding in a Circle:** It’s not just the main highway. Back country roads and shortcuts are being blocked by emergency vehicles or fallen power lines. Don't trust your GPS—follow police guidance or the official fire map app only. Turning around on a closed road could cost you precious minutes.
- **Your "Go Bag" Needs These Three Specific Items:** Waterproof documents (ID, insurance papers), a six-foot 'grab-and-go' extension cord for charging devices in your car, and a spare phone battery. Also: wrap a damp towel and tape it to your car's interior air vent—this can keep smoke out if you have to evacuate through heavy haze.
