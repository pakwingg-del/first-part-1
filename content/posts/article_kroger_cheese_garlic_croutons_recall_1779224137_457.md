**VERDICT: FAKE – Misleading Rumor**

A viral post circulating on Facebook and TikTok claims that Kroger has issued a mass recall of its "Private Selection Garlic Croutons" due to "undeclared dairy" that allegedly caused severe allergic reactions in several consumers. The post includes a blurry image of a store shelf with a handwritten sign that reads: "RECALLED – Do Not Eat."

**The Reality:**
There is no official recall for Kroger cheese garlic croutons. A search of the FDA recall database, Kroger’s corporate recall page, and USDA records shows zero active recalls for any Kroger-brand croutons in the past 90 days. The image used in the post has been reverse-image searched and was originally a photo of a store recirculating a *different* product (a flavored cracker) from a previous month.

**What actually happened:**
A small, regional Kroger store in Ohio temporarily pulled the croutons from shelves as a *precaution* after a single customer complained of a "strange aftertaste." No allergic reactions were confirmed. The store manager replaced the product with fresh stock the same day. The handwritten sign was an internal note, not an official recall notice—but the photo was shared out of context.

**Bottom line:** 🛑 Do not panic. Your Kroger garlic croutons are safe. If you see this post, report it as misinformation. For legitimate recalls, always check the official FDA recall list or Kroger’s corporate website.
