**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**DATELINE: TALLAHASSEE, FL**

**SUBJECT:** Red Lobster Tallahassee Permanently Closes All Locations Following Corporate Restructuring.

**TALLAHASSEE, FL –** Effective immediately, all three Red Lobster restaurant locations within the Tallahassee city limits have been permanently shuttered. This action was confirmed by corporate headquarters at 09:00 hours Eastern Time today.

**WHO:** Red Lobster Management, LLC. Approximately 140 local employees have been affected.

**WHAT:** The complete and immediate cessation of operations at all Tallahassee-area Red Lobster restaurants. All dining rooms, carry-out services, and delivery options are indefinitely suspended. A notice posted on the front door of the North Monroe Street location simply reads, “This location is now closed.”

**WHERE:** All three franchised establishments in Leon County. Specifically: the location at 2111 North Monroe Street, the site at 3525 Apalachee Parkway, and the venue at 1370 West Tennessee Street.

**WHEN:** The closures were enforced as of 22:00 hours on October 26, 2023. The first confirmed reports of locked doors and barricaded entrances were filed by local law enforcement this morning.

**WHY:** According to a brief statement issued by Red Lobster’s corporate communications division, the decision is part of a broader, “comprehensive strategic realignment and cost-reduction initiative” affecting underperforming markets. Economic data indicates a sustained 18% decline in foot traffic at these specific locations over the past three fiscal quarters, coupled with rising operational costs and supply chain disruptions.

**FOR FURTHER INFORMATION:** The parent company has stated that all affected employees will be provided with severance packages and priority consideration for positions at remaining Florida locations, primarily in the Jacksonville and Orlando metropolitan areas.
