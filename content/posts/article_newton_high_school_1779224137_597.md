**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**NEWTON, KS –** In a move that has ignited a firestorm of controversy across the Sunflower State, Newton High School has officially replaced its traditional English literature curriculum with a mandatory, semester-long "Social Media Storytelling and Viral Branding" course.

School board officials defended the decision as "career-ready education," arguing that by 2026, the analytics of a TikTok algorithm will be more relevant to students than the themes of *The Scarlet Letter*. However, critics are sounding the alarm over what they call the "Systematic Liquidation of the Moral Imagination."

"This is the final curtain call for critical thinking," said Dr. Harold Vance, a retired ethics professor and local parent. "We are telling teenagers that a 'like' is worth more than a lesson, that a trending audio clip holds more value than understanding the tragedy of *Romeo and Juliet*. We are not just cutting a class; we are amputating the soul’s ability to process consequence, nuance, and regret."

The outrage reached a fever pitch after an assignment went viral: students were tasked with "gatekeeping" a fictional product, using "Call-Out Culture" rhetoric to destroy a competitor's reputation. The winning project, a video titled "Destroying the Honor Roll," featured students aggressively shaming peers who studied for a physics test.

"These kids are learning how to perform moral outrage without ever feeling true moral injury," Vance continued. "They are becoming brilliant puppets of synthetic conflict. Parents woke up this morning thinking their children were learning about iambic pentameter. They’re now teaching them how to wield pitchforks for brand metrics."

The district’s superintendent responded simply: "Transparency. Engagement. Relevance."

For the students of Newton, the lesson is clear. The path to success no longer lies in understanding human nature—but in performing it for an audience.
