**BREAKING NEWS | LEGAL AFFAIRS**

**HOUSTON, TX – June 18, 2025 (5:00 PM CT)** – In a significant development within the Texas judicial system, suspended Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton has been formally indicted by a state grand jury in Harris County.

**WHO:** The indictment was issued against Ken Paxton, the state's highest-ranking law enforcement official who has been suspended from duties pending the outcome of these proceedings. The investigation was led by the Harris County District Attorney's Office.

**WHAT:** Paxton has been charged with three first-degree felony counts of securities fraud and one count of failing to register as an investment adviser. These charges stem from alleged activities conducted between 2011 and 2014, prior to his election as Attorney General.

**WHERE:** The indictment was returned by a grand jury convened in Harris County District Court in Houston, Texas.

**WHEN:** The formal charges were unsealed earlier today, following a months-long investigation by a special prosecutor.

**WHY:** Prosecutors allege that Paxton solicited investors in a technology company, Servergy Inc., without disclosing that he was being compensated for those solicitations. This constitutes a violation of state securities laws designed to protect investors from fraudulent and deceptive practices.

**HOW:** Authorities allege that Paxton acted through intermediaries to recruit investors, failing to register as an investment adviser representative as required by Texas law. The securities fraud charges each carry a potential sentence of five to 99 years in prison.

Ken Paxton has previously denied any wrongdoing. His legal team is expected to issue a formal statement following his arraignment, which is scheduled for next week. We will continue to follow this developing story.
