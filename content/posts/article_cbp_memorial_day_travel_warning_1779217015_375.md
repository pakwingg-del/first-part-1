**HEADLINE: CBP ISSUES MEMORIAL DAY TRAVEL ADVISORY: EXPECT RECORD VOLUME, ENHANCED SECURITY MEASURES**

**WASHINGTON, D.C. – May 23, 2025 (CBS News) –** In a formal announcement released Wednesday, U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) issued a comprehensive travel warning for the upcoming Memorial Day holiday weekend, citing anticipated record-high traveler volume and the implementation of heightened security protocols at all ports of entry.

**WHO:** The warning is directed at all U.S. citizens, lawful permanent residents, and international visitors planning to cross U.S. land borders or arrive at international airports between Friday, May 23, and Monday, May 26.

**WHAT:** The agency is advising travelers to prepare for significant delays, extended processing times, and increased scrutiny during customs and border inspections. CBP specifically warned against the attempted importation of prohibited agricultural items, fireworks, and certain commercial goods without proper documentation.

**WHEN:** The advisory is effective immediately and spans the entire Memorial Day weekend, with peak congestion anticipated on Sunday, May 25, and Monday, May 26, during the return travel period.

**WHERE:** The warning applies to all U.S. ports of entry, with particular emphasis on the busiest land crossings, including San Ysidro in California, El Paso in Texas, and the Peace Bridge in New York. International airports in major hubs such as Atlanta, Chicago, and New York are also included.

**WHY:** The advisory was prompted by a projected surge in cross-border travel exceeding pre-pandemic levels by 15 percent, combined with the federal requirement for all non-U.S. citizens to present valid travel documents. CBP officials cited the need to maintain national security and enforce immigration laws as primary reasons for the enhanced vigilance.
