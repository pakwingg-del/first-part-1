**Lead Anchor:** This is a breaking news update from the National News Desk.

**Location:** San Antonio, Texas

**Headline:** Former Army Medic Jenny Slatten Sentenced in Connection with Deaths of Two Fellow Soldiers.

**Who:** Jenny L. Slatten, a former combat medic with the United States Army.

**What:** Following a military court-martial, Slatten has been found guilty of involuntary manslaughter and dereliction of duty. The charges stem from a medical negligence investigation concerning the administration of a lethal dose of an opiate-based anesthetic.

**When:** The sentencing was delivered today, March 11, 2025, following a week-long deliberation by the court-martial panel.

**Where:** The incident occurred in the intensive care unit of the 47th Combat Support Hospital in Tikrit, Iraq, in 2023. The sentencing hearing was held at Fort Sam Houston, Texas.

**Why:** The court determined that Slatten, against established medical protocols, administered a high-concentration dosage of fentanyl to two separate patients, resulting in respiratory failure. The prosecution argued that Slatten's actions demonstrated a grossly negligent disregard for standard operating procedures, citing a pattern of recklessness. The defense maintained the incident was a tragic but accidental medication error. Slatten has been dishonorably discharged and sentenced to 15 years of confinement in a military correctional facility. A formal appeal is expected.
