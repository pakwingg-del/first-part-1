**Dateline: Washington, D.C. | September 4, 2024**

**Who:** Former Federal Trade Commission (FTC) Chief Technology Officer Cain Clark, along with two unnamed senior staff members.

**What:** The Department of Justice (DOJ) has confirmed the formal indictment of Clark on three counts of wire fraud, one count of conspiracy to commit computer intrusion, and two counts of making false statements to federal investigators. The charges stem from an alleged scheme involving the unauthorized access of proprietary market data from 14 Silicon Valley competitors.

**When:** The indictment was unsealed earlier this morning following Clark’s arrest at his residence in Arlington, Virginia. The alleged activities are believed to have occurred between March 2022 and November 2023.

**Where:** Clark’s office within the FTC’s Bureau of Competition in Washington, D.C., as well as a private co-working space in San Francisco, California.

**Why:** According to the indictment, Clark is accused of exploiting his access to confidential internal FTC investigation files to steal trade secrets regarding algorithmic pricing strategies. The DOJ contends Clark then utilized this sensitive data to advise a private equity firm on leveraged buyouts, netting an estimated $2.1 million in consulting fees. The information was allegedly accessed via a compromised virtual private network (VPN) to obscure the digital footprint.

**Conclusion:** Clark has entered a plea of not guilty. If convicted on all counts, he faces a statutory maximum of 45 years in federal prison. A pre-trial hearing has been scheduled for the first week of October.
