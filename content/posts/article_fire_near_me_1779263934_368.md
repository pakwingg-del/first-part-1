**BREAKING NEWS: MAJOR STRUCTURE FIRE NEAR DOWNTOWN METROPOLITAN AREA**

**Date:** October 27, 2023
**Time:** 14:30 EST

**Location:** 400 block of West Adams Avenue, within two blocks of the Central Business District.

**WHAT:** A multi-alarm fire has engulfed a five-story commercial building, prompting a massive emergency response and a mandatory evacuation of the immediate vicinity.

**WHO:** Local fire department officials, including Chief Allen Park, are commanding the scene. Response includes Engine Co. 7, 12, and 14, as well as the Hazardous Materials Unit. The American Red Cross is on standby.

**WHEN:** First reports of smoke and flames were received by dispatchers at 13:58 EST.

**WHY:** The cause of the fire is currently under active investigation. Preliminary reports suggest the blaze may have originated in the basement mechanical room, though arson has not been ruled out.

**HOW:** Authorities have established a 500-foot perimeter, closing all roads between First and Fifth Avenues. Commuters are advised to avoid the area. Two civilians and one firefighter have been transported to City General with non-life-threatening injuries. A direct cause has not yet been determined.

**ADVISORY:** Residents within a four-block radius should close all windows and doors. Air quality monitors are currently deployed. This is a developing situation.
