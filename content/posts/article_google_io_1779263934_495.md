**The Price of Convenience: Google’s New AI ‘Help Me Shop’ Feature is Designed to Empty Your Wallet Faster**

**MOUNTAIN VIEW, CA** – Google just announced a suite of new AI tools at its I/O conference, and while they promise to make your life easier, consumer advocates are sounding the alarm over a feature that could cost you thousands.

The headline grabber is "Help Me Shop," a new AI-powered search tool that will act as your personal shopper. You type in a need, like "outdoor dinner set for a dinner party," and the AI doesn't just show you links. It creates a curated, shoppable visual guide, comparing prices, reviews, and even suggesting "complete the look" items—spinning a simple search into a full-blown cart frenzy.

**The Consumer Trap:**
Here’s where your wallet takes a hit. While Google claims this saves time, experts point out the algorithm is engineered to maximize *average order value*. The AI is programmed to steer you toward higher-margin items. If you ask for a "budget-friendly grill," the AI might show you a $300 model, but then subtly rank a $600 "top-rated" version higher, using sponsored links and real-time price comparisons that make the splurge seem like a "deal."

“This isn’t a shopping assistant; it’s a high-speed checkout machine,” says Maria Lopez, a digital consumer rights advocate. “The AI doesn’t know your actual budget. It knows the retailer's profit margins. The ‘best value’ tag is going to be given to the product with the best advertising spend, not the best price.”
