**Alabama Man Shows Up To Vote, Finds Out His ‘Common Sense’ Wasn’t Registered**

MOBILE, AL — In a viral Facebook rant that has locals nodding in weary agreement, an angry resident of Baldwin County is demanding answers after a bewildering trip to the polls for the 2026 Alabama Primary.

“I walked in there ready to do my civic duty. I had my ID. I had my opinions. I figured that was enough,” wrote Frank “Skeeter” Thompson on the *Coastal Alabama Neighbors & Nonsense* community page. “But the poll worker tells me my ‘common sense’ isn’t listed in the precinct book. She said I need a voter registration card. I said, ‘Ma’am, I’ve been living here 40 years. I watch the news. I know who I don’t want in office. That’s common sense.’”

Thompson insists he did what any reasonable person would do: he showed up, saw a long line, and “assumed the system worked.” He is now calling for a statewide audit, arguing that “common sense should be an acceptable form of identification in this state.”

Several commenters quickly chimed in: “This is why we can’t have nice primaries. If you didn’t register by the deadline, you didn’t register. It’s not the poll workers’ fault you’re living in 2024.” Others, however, roundly supported Thompson, with one user writing, “He’s got a point. I don’t need a card to tell me which candidate has the worst hair.”

The post has since been shared over 2,000 times, with many urging the Alabama Secretary of State to clarify whether “just being fed up with everything” qualifies as a valid voter credential.
