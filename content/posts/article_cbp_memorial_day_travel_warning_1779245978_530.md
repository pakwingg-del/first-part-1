**CBP Issues Stark Memorial Day Travel Warning: "Your Road Trip Could Be Your Last"**

**Washington, D.C.** – In an unprecedented and chilling advisory just ahead of the Memorial Day weekend, U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) has issued a travel warning that goes far beyond typical traffic delays or airport security lines. Citing a "convergence of stressors," the agency is urging Americans to treat their holiday travel plans as a matter of "psychological survival."

The viral warning, titled *"Operation Safe Return: The Mental Toll of the Road,"* states that the combination of record-high gas prices, post-pandemic social anxiety, and a fractured national mood has created a "perfect storm of road rage, decision fatigue, and emotional volatility." CBP officials are advising travelers to not only pack sunscreen and snacks, but also a "5-minute decompression ritual" before entering a vehicle.

“You are not just driving to the lake house; you are piloting a vessel through a sea of collective trauma,” the advisory reads, in a rare switch from traffic law to life coaching. “Before you merge onto the highway, ask yourself: ‘Am I running away from something, or toward something?’ If the answer is ‘away,’ consider staying home.”

The psychological twist has sparked a firestorm on social media. Critics accuse the agency of fear-mongering, while supporters say it is the most honest government communication in years. One user posted, “CBP finally admitting the real border is in our own minds.”

As millions prepare to hit the road, the message is clear: this Memorial Day, the most dangerous border you cross might be the one between how you feel and how you drive.
