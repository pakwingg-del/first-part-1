**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Kroger Cheese Garlic Croutons Recall**

- **Undeclared Allergy Alert:** The recall was issued because these croutons may contain **milk**, which is not listed on the ingredient label. This poses a serious health risk to anyone with a milk allergy or severe lactose intolerance.

- **Affected Products:** The recall applies specifically to **Kroger-brand Cheese Garlic Croutons** sold in select states. Look for a **6 oz bag** with the UPC code **11110 82775** and a **Best if Used By date of MAR 27 2025**.

- **Where They Were Sold:** These croutons were distributed to Kroger stores and its family of retailers (including Fry's, Ralphs, Dillons, Smith's, and others) across the Midwest, South, and West Coast. If you bought them anytime recently, check your pantry.

- **No Illnesses Reported Yet:** As of the recall announcement, the FDA and Kroger have not received any confirmed reports of allergic reactions or illnesses linked to this batch. However, the risk remains until the product is removed from shelves.

- **What You Should Do:** If you have this recalled product, **do not eat it**. Immediately dispose of it or return it to your nearest Kroger store for a **full refund**. If you have a milk allergy and have consumed these croutons, monitor for symptoms and contact your doctor. For questions, call Kroger's toll-free customer service at **800-576-4377**.
