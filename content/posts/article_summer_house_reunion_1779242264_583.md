**SUMMER HOUSE REUNION DEVOLVES INTO "MORAL PESTILENCE," SAYS ETHICS OBSERVER**

**BOSTON, MA** – The highly anticipated *Summer House* reunion has sparked more than just feuds over kitchen counters and romantic entanglements—it has ignited a firestorm of moral outrage. Dr. Helena Vance, a prominent cultural ethicist and author of *The Hollow Feast: How Reality TV Sacrifices Soul for Spectacle*, has labeled the special a "three-hour masterclass in ethical decay."

In a statement that has since gone viral, Vance claimed the show has crossed an invisible line that signals "the final, shameful surrender of one of society's last bastions of virtue."

"It is no longer about bad decisions in one's youth," Vance said in a press release. "This reunion was a celebration of weaponized vulnerability. We watched cast members parse trauma for screen time and monetize betrayals that, a decade ago, would have been handled in private, with a therapist. This is not entertainment. It is a moral pestilence. We are teaching an entire generation that emotional disrobing is a competitive sport. The 'downfall of society' is not coming—it is here, sitting on a couch, holding a martini, and arguing about who hooked up with whom in the Hamptons."

Critics of Vance argue the show is a harmless guilty pleasure. But the ethicist counters that the "guilt" is now the point. "When we cheer for people weaponizing their lowest moments for a viral moment, we become complicit in our own coarsening. We have traded wisdom for clicks, and the price is our ability to discern integrity from charisma."

The comment has split fans online, with hashtags #SummerHouseDownfall and #LetThemLive trending simultaneously.
