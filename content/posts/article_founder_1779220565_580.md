**HEADLINE: “The Founder’s Fallacy: How Silicon Valley’s ‘Gifted Genius’ Myth Is Breeding a New Generation of Moral Monsters”**

**City, State** – In a scathing op-ed that has set tech Twitter ablaze, cultural critic Dr. Eileen Vasquez argues that our society’s fetishization of the "Founder Archetype" is tearing apart the very fabric of civic ethics. “We have created a modern-day cult of personality that equates financial risk-taking with moral authority,” writes Vasquez. “A 27-year-old who disrupts the taxi industry is now lauded as a visionary, even when his business model is a pedagogical textbook on wage theft and algorithmic serfdom.”

The piece dissects the recent downfall of a prominent AI-startup CEO, who was found to have secretly siphoned user data to train a predatory loan algorithm. While the public has focused on the legal breach, Vasquez argues the true crime is the “moral rot” enabled by a culture that rewards speed over scruples.

“We gave founders the keys to the kingdom because they promised us convenience and a 10% annual return,” Vasquez continues. “In return, we got gig-economy indentured servitude, a hollowed-out middle class, and now, algorithms designed to exploit human weakness for profit. The ‘founder’ is not a hero; he is the high priest of a religion that worships disruption while ignoring the wreckage it leaves behind. This isn’t a bug in the system—it is the system. We are not witnessing a scandal; we are witnessing the final, logical conclusion of an empire built on the backs of the desperate and the distracted. Wake up, or the next ‘disruption’ might be your own moral compass.”
