**Top 5 things you need to know about the Kroger cheese garlic croutons recall:**

- **The Big Danger:** The recall was issued due to **undeclared milk** on the label. For shoppers with severe dairy allergies or lactose intolerance, these croutons pose a serious health risk that could trigger anaphylaxis.

- **What to look for:** Only specific **Kroger Cheese Garlic Croutons** are affected. Check the package for **UPC 11110 09235** and a **"Best By" date of October 22, 2024**—these are the items you need to toss.

- **Why it happened:** A **packaging error** caused the wrong label to be placed on some batches. The croutons inside contain milk, but the outside says "milk-free," making the allergy warning invisible.

- **Where they were sold:** These croutons were distributed exclusively to **Kroger** and its sister stores, including **Fred Meyer, Ralphs, Smith's, King Soopers, Fry's, and QFC**. If you bought them recently, check your pantry.

- **The free replacement:** Do NOT eat them. Kroger is offering a **full refund or free replacement** with no receipt required. Just return the product (or take a photo of the bag) to customer service.
