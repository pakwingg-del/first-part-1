**VERIFIED: FAKE NEWS ALERT**

**The Claim:** A viral social media post warns that "CBP (Customs and Border Protection) has issued a 'Level 4: Do Not Travel' advisory for all U.S. roads, bridges, and airports from May 24–27 due to 'credible threats of coordinated cartel activity at all ports of entry' and advises Americans to 'stay home' to avoid 'imminent mass detention operations.'"

**Status: FALSE.** This is a fabricated and dangerous hoax.

**The Facts:**
- CBP has **NOT** issued any "Level 4" travel advisory. The U.S. Department of State issues travel advisories for international destinations, not domestic travel. CBP does not have the authority to issue "do not travel" warnings for interstate highways or domestic airports.
- CBP has issued its routine **Memorial Day Travel Reminder** (posted May 22, 2025), which simply reminds travelers to expect heavy traffic at land ports of entry, ensure they have proper documents (like a passport or Global Entry card), and declare all agricultural items. The agency encourages early arrival but **explicitly says no unusual threats have been detected.**
- There is **zero credible evidence** of a coordinated cartel plot targeting American civilians at all ports of entry simultaneously. Such a claim would require a specific, nationwide threat matrix shared by multiple federal agencies (FBI, DHS, TSA) — none of which have issued any such warning.
- The "mass detention operations" line is a recycled talking point from earlier disinformation campaigns. CBP conducts lawful immigration enforcement; it does not run mass-roundup operations targeting Memorial Day road trippers.

**The Real Tip:** A savvy traveler this weekend should: check real-time border wait times at [bwt.cbp.gov](https://bwt.cbp.gov), ensure your passport is valid, and leave your fruit and plants at home
