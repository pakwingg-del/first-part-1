**BREAKING NEWS: MILLIONS FACE LOSS OF AFFORDABLE CARE ACT COVERAGE**

The United States Department of Health and Human Services has confirmed a significant reduction in enrollment under the Affordable Care Act, commonly known as Obamacare.

**What:** The agency reports that an estimated 3.2 million Americans are set to lose their health insurance coverage due to systemic glitches in eligibility verification systems and the expiration of temporary pandemic-era protections.

**Who:** The affected individuals are primarily low-to-middle-income enrollees in states that use the federally facilitated HealthCare.gov marketplace.

**When:** The coverage terminations are slated to take effect over the next 30 days, with final implementation no later than October 1st.

**Where:** This impact is concentrated in 33 states that rely on the federal exchange, including Texas, Florida, Georgia, and Ohio.

**Why:** Federal officials cite a combination of factors: a surge in automated system errors, failure of enrollees to re-verify income documentation, and the formal end of the continuous enrollment provision tied to the COVID-19 public health emergency.

The Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services has urged affected parties to check their accounts immediately or face a gap in medical coverage.
