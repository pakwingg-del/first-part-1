# Did Thomas Massie Lose His Primary? The Viral “Election Results” Claim, Fact-Checked

**The Claim:** A wave of social media posts, primarily on X (formerly Twitter) and Facebook, are claiming that incumbent U.S. Representative Thomas Massie (R-KY) was defeated in the Kentucky primary election. The posts often include a screenshot of a headline reading, “Massie Ousted by Pro-Trump Challenger” accompanied by celebratory or dismayed comments about the “establishment” losing.

**The Reality:** **FALSE. Thomas Massie did not lose. The election has not yet occurred.**

As of the current date, the Kentucky primary elections for U.S. House seats are scheduled for **May 20, 2025**. The viral posts are circulating an outdated or fabricated result.

**The Origin of the Confusion:**
1.  **Old News, Recycled:** In **2022**, a pro-Trump challenger did run against Massie. Massie won that primary decisively with over 70% of the vote. The viral posts are likely using graphics from that past election cycle.
2.  **The Current Race:** In 2024 and 2025, Massie is facing a primary challenge from Eric Deters. While Deters has been vocal and the race has garnered attention, no votes have been counted. Massie is widely expected to win.
3.  **Malicious “Predictions”:** Some accounts are posting AI-generated “results” or using misleading language like “*In the battle for the soul of the GOP, Massie was defeated…*” without a date, tricking algorithms.
