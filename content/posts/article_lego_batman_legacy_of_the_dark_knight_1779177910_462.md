**VIRAL RUMOR: "LEGO BATMAN: LEGACY OF THE DARK KNIGHT" – A FULL FAN MOVIE OR A MAJOR STUDIO LEAK?**  

**The Claim:** Social media is buzzing with posts claiming that Warner Bros. has quietly greenlit a new, dark, mature-themed LEGO Batman movie titled *Legacy of the Dark Knight*—set to be released exclusively on streaming in 2025. Supposed stills show a gothic-looking, brick-built Bruce Wayne staring out over a rain-soaked Gotham, captioned: *"Every hero needs a villain. But what if the villain is you?"* Fans are also sharing a fake trailer description that includes a gritty monologue from a LEGO Joker, complete with "deeply philosophical" voice work by Mark Hamill.  

**Judgment: FAKE.**  

**What’s Really Happening:**  
- No such project has been announced by Warner Bros., The LEGO Group, or any official studio. The stills are fan-made renders, likely created using Unreal Engine 5 or Blender, circulating from a popular LEGO fan art account on DeviantArt.  
- The “trailer description” is a repurposed script from a cancelled fan film project from 2022—originally titled *LEGO Batman: The Last Laugh*—which never received any studio backing.  
- While LEGO has released darker, more mature-themed sets tied to *The Batman* (2022) and *Batman: The Animated Series*, they have explicitly stated that all LEGO media remains family-friendly and will not pivot to an R-rated or deep psychological narrative.  

**Why It Went Viral:** A mix of nostalgia, high-quality AI-generated images, and the internet’s obsession with a “dark take” on the beloved LEGO Batman franchise. Plus, the title *Legacy of the Dark Knight*
