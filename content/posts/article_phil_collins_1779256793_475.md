**VERDICT: FAKE** (Satirical origins confirmed, widely shared out of context)

**The Viral Snippet:**
> **Phil Collins Announces “Final Goodbye” AI-Generated Hologram Tour – Will “Perform” From His Living Room Couch**

**What’s being shared:**
Social media posts claim that legendary Genesis frontman Phil Collins, who has been battling significant health issues including nerve damage and limited mobility in recent years, has officially retired from live performance – but will “return” via a lifelike AI hologram. The posts include a fabricated quote: *“I can’t move my hands or feet, but my digital twin can still sell out arenas.”*

**Why it’s FAKE:**
- The story originated from a well-known satire/news-parody account (@TheOnionNewsNetworkAdLib) and was never published by any legitimate music outlet.
- Collins’ actual management confirmed in 2022 that he was “no longer able to perform live” but explicitly stated he has **no plans** for a hologram tour. No deal with any AI company has been announced.
- The accompanying “video screenshot” is a deepfake edit of a 1980s concert still, overlayed with a generic 3D model.

**Real Context:**
Phil Collins is indeed retired due to severe nerve damage and spinal issues. In 2022, he told *Mojo* magazine: “I literally can’t hold a drumstick. I could probably still sing, but it wouldn’t be fair to the fans.” Genesis did perform a final farewell tour in 2022 (with his son Nic on drums), and Collins has repeatedly stated he has no interest in hologram performances.

**Bottom line:** A parody post gone viral. Collins is done performing – but he’s staying on his couch, not on a stage or a screen.
