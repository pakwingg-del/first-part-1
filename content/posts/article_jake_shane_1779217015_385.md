**DATELINE: NEW YORK – MAY 24, 2025 (Breaking News)**  

**Who:** Jake Shane, 34, former chief technology officer of Nexus Dynamics.  
**What:** Shane has been charged with 17 counts of corporate espionage and wire fraud following a two-year federal investigation into the theft of proprietary artificial intelligence algorithms.  
**Where:** Charges were unsealed this morning in the Southern District of New York federal courthouse.  
**When:** The alleged crimes occurred between January 2023 and March 2025, with Shane apprehended yesterday at John F. Kennedy International Airport upon returning from an undisclosed foreign destination.  
**Why:** According to the 42-page indictment, Shane is accused of exfiltrating sensitive source code from Nexus Dynamics’ secure servers and transferring it to a rival firm, Quantix Innovations, in exchange for $4.2 million in offshore payments.  

“This case represents a grave betrayal of corporate trust and a direct assault on American technological leadership,” said U.S. Attorney Maria Espinoza in a statement. “The defendant allegedly exploited his position to sabotage his employer and enrich himself at the expense of national security.”  

Shane, who has pleaded not guilty, is being held without bail pending a detention hearing scheduled for Friday. If convicted, he faces a maximum sentence of 45 years in federal prison. The trial is expected to draw significant attention due to the classified nature of the stolen algorithms, which federal prosecutors claim had direct applications in defense and communications systems.
