**BREAKING: Fact-Check: "NASA Confirms Rare 'Double Eclipse' Will Plunge Earth Into 3 Days of Darkness" — Claimed by Viral TikTok Account**  

**Our Verdict: FALSE — This is a hoax. No such event is predicted.**  

A viral video circulating on TikTok and Instagram claims that the upcoming April 8, 2024 solar eclipse will be "unlike any in history," creating a "double shadow" that will cause a "3-day global blackout." The account, "CosmicRevelations_777," says NASA has issued a "silent alert" and that governments are "prepping for worst-case power grid failure."  

**What we found:**  
- **Dr. Sarah Jenkins, NASA Astrophysicist:** "There is absolutely no scientific basis for a 'double eclipse' or three days of darkness. The April 8 eclipse will be a normal total solar eclipse with a maximum totality of about 4 minutes 28 seconds, depending on location."  
- **FEMA Statement:** FEMA confirmed no "power grid precaution letters" have been sent; the viral post is using a real 2019 grid security memo out of context.  
- **Image Analysis:** The "double shadow" photo in the video is actually a composite image from the 2017 eclipse, with a second moon added via AI.  
- **Counter-Check:** No major observatory (including the Royal Greenwich or Paris Observatory) has any record of a "double eclipse" event. The term does not exist in astronomy.  

**Why this matters:**  
With 30 million people expected to travel for the eclipse, false claims about extended darkness could cause unnecessary panic and dangerous behavior (hoarding supplies, stockpiling generators). The actual eclipse will be a safe, awe-inspiring event if you follow standard eye safety (use ISO 12312-2 glasses).
