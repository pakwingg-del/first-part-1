**Executive Summary: Schlitz Beer's Return**

**Headline:** The Comeback of the Beer That Made Milwaukee Famous

**The Bite:** Schlitz is back—and this time, it’s not a nostalgia play. After decades of dormancy following the 1970s formula change that tanked the brand, parent company Pabst is relaunching Schlitz as a "blue-collar cult" icon. The strategy? Zero marketing. No TV spots. Instead, they’re leaning into organic demand from Gen Z and millennial drinkers rediscovering the pre-1975 recipe.

**The Strategic Angle:** This isn’t about volume; it’s about margin. Schlitz is being positioned as a premium alternative within the value segment—higher ABV (5.5%), all-malt, and priced just below craft. Pabst is betting that the "revenant brand" narrative (ruined by corporate greed, revived by authenticity) will generate earned media on social and trade.

**The Catch:** Supply is limited. This is a deliberate scarcity play to test pricing power. If it works, Schlitz becomes a high-margin cash cow. If not, it’s a controlled burn—no ad spend wasted.

**Bottom line:** Schlitz is a case study in leveraging brand trauma as a launchpad. Watch for it to outperform spreadsheets—but not to reclaim volume share. This is about dollars, not cans.
