**HISTORY REPEATS: Trump’s Banking Order Mirrors FDR’s Gold Confiscation—But Targets Immigrants**

In a move that constitutional historians are calling “the most radical economic de-platforming since the New Deal,” President Trump’s latest executive order instructs banks to flag accounts tied to undocumented immigration status.

Experts note a striking parallel to FDR’s 1933 Executive Order 6102, which forced citizens to surrender gold. Back then, the government tracked reserves. Now, banks are being forced to track residency.

“This isn’t just policy—it’s a financial Sedition Act,” says Yale historian Dr. Lena Voss. “In 1798, we criminalized speech. In 2025, we’re criminalizing the ability to hold a checking account.”

The order, which uses dormant banking protocols originally designed to catch terrorists, now categorizes “undocumented financial behavior” as a red flag—transforming every bank deposit into a potential deportation trigger.

Critics call it “financial McCarthyism.” Supporters say it is long-overdue enforcement.

But as one former FDIC official noted: “The last time we weaponized banking data this broadly, it was to freeze the assets of Japanese Americans in 1942.”

The White House has not commented on the historical comparison.
