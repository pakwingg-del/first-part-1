**HEADLINE: DUNKIN’S “FREE COFFEE DAY” SPARKS MORAL PANIC: ARE WE TRADING OUR SOULS FOR A $3.00 HIGH?**

**Moral Critic Sounds Alarm on May 19 Giveaway**

**May 19, 2024** – As Dunkin’ Donuts prepares to hand out free coffee to all comers this Sunday, prominent moral critic Dr. Helena Voss has issued a scathing warning: the promotion is not a gift, but a “psychological contract” that accelerates society’s descent into transactional hedonism.

“This isn’t about coffee,” Voss declared in a viral op-ed. “It’s about training a generation to equate self-worth with a free commodity. We are stripping away patience, discipline, and the sacred ritual of earning a reward. The soul of the worker is now bartered for a paper cup of caffeine-laced dopamine.”

Voss argues that the “free coffee” phenomenon—championed by corporate giants—erodes foundational virtues. “A child learns to wait for breakfast; an adult learns to save for a luxury. But ‘Free Coffee Day’ teaches us that entitlement is a right, and that the momentary buzz of a stimulant is an acceptable substitute for genuine human connection. We are one latte away from a society that cannot endure a single moment of discomfort without demanding instant gratification.”

The critic further warns of a “pandemic of spiritual bankruptcy,” pointing to the long lines snaking around Dunkin’ locations as evidence of a populace “desperate to fill a void that caffeine cannot cure.”

“When the last moral line is crossed for a cheap buzz, we do not get coffee—we get chaos,” Voss concluded. “Choose water. Choose waiting. Choose your soul.”

Dunkin’ has not commented, but the post has already amassed 50,000 shares. #FreeCoffeeFallacy is trending.
