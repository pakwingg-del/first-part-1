**BREAKING NEWS: Berkshire Hathaway Reports Record Cash Reserves, Executes Major Portfolio Shift**

**WHO:** Berkshire Hathaway Inc., led by Chairman and CEO Warren Buffett.

**WHAT:** The conglomerate announced a record cash reserve of approximately $325 billion as of the close of the third quarter. Simultaneously, filings reveal a significant reduction in its stake in major tech holdings, including selling roughly half of its shares in Apple Inc., and a net sale of $75 billion in equities overall.

**WHEN:** The financial data and trading activity were disclosed in the company's quarterly earnings report released after market close on Saturday.

**WHERE:** Corporate headquarters in Omaha, Nebraska; portfolio adjustments executed across U.S. stock exchanges.

**WHY:** In his annual shareholder letter, Warren Buffett cited current market valuations as "extremely high" and stated that "opportunities for deploying capital in equity markets are not as attractive as holding cash or short-term Treasuries at current yields." He further noted a lack of "transformative acquisition targets" suitable for the company's size.

**HOW:** The sales were conducted through a systematic program of divestiture over the past two fiscal quarters. The proceeds have been allocated to cash equivalents, specifically U.S. Treasury bills, yielding a risk-free return of over 5%. The move effectively positions Berkshire as a significant creditor to the U.S. government.

**KEY IMPACT:** The announcement sent immediate shockwaves through global markets. S&P 500 futures fell 0.4% in overnight trading as analysts interpret the move as a strong signal that the world's most famous value investor sees limited near-term upside for equities. This marks the highest cash position in Berkshire's history, surpassing the previous record set during the 2008 financial crisis.
