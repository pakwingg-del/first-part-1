**Viral News Snippet: Mackenzie Shirilla Documentary – Real or Fake?**

**The Rumor:** A new documentary titled *"The Speed of Grief: The Mackenzie Shirilla Story"* is circulating on TikTok and YouTube, claiming to feature never-before-seen interrogation footage and a "confession from beyond the grave" from the Ohio teen convicted of murdering her boyfriend.

**The Verdict: FAKE (with a grain of truth)**  
While Mackenzie Shirilla—the 19-year-old sentenced to life in 2023 for deliberately crashing her car at 100 mph, killing her boyfriend Dominic Russo—is a real case, **no verified documentary matching this description exists.** The clips going viral are actually AI-generated deepfakes or heavily edited snippets from her actual trial footage, misleadingly narrated to suggest supernatural evidence. A user with the handle @TrueCrimeSphere admitted in a deleted comment that the "confession" was fabricated using a voice-cloning app.

**Reality Check:** The only legitimate long-form coverage to date is a 2023 episode of *20/20* and a forthcoming Netflix project, *"Cruel Intentions: The Mackenzie Shirilla Files,"* scheduled for late 2024. **Do not fall for the bait** — and report any suspicious clips claiming to reveal "new secrets."
