**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About Trump’s Banking Immigration Status Order**

- **The "Financial Vetting" Mandate:** This order *revives and expands* a hardline policy that forces banks and credit unions to scrutinize a customer's immigration status before opening or maintaining accounts. It targets transactions linked to possible illegal entry or overstayed visas.
- **From ID Check to Data Mining:** It’s not just about showing a driver's license. The order instructs Treasury and Homeland Security to cross-reference immigration databases with financial transaction patterns, flagging anyone who sends money abroad to countries or regions on a "high-risk" list.
- **Remittance Red Flag:** Expect major delays for routine money transfers (remittances). The order specifically targets money sent from the U.S. to family in Latin America and Southeast Asia, requiring banks to certify the sender's legal status or halt the transaction.
- **The "Know Your Customer" Nightmare for Undocumented Immigrants:** This effectively makes it impossible for an undocumented person to access a standard bank account, debit card, or mortgage. It forces a return to a cash-only economy for millions, raising risks of theft and loss.
- **Legal & Banking Whiplash:** Major institutions like JPMorgan Chase and Bank of America are already bracing for lawsuits, arguing the order violates privacy laws (Right to Financial Privacy Act). Meanwhile, state-level "sanctuary banking" laws are being drafted to block compliance, setting up a massive state vs. federal showdown.
