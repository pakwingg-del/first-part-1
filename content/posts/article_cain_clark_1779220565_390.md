**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Cain Clark Verdict**

- **Guilty on All Counts:** After a swift deliberation, the jury found Cain Clark guilty of first-degree murder and child endangerment for the brutal death of his girlfriend’s 3-year-old son, Darnell Taylor. The prosecution argued Clark shook and struck the toddler in a fit of rage over a spilled drink.

- **The 72-Hour Gap:** The most damning evidence was the timeline. Clark didn't call 911 until nearly three days after Darnell stopped breathing. Instead, he allegedly cleaned the apartment and coached the child's mother on what to tell paramedics, calling it a “fall down the stairs.”

- **The "Anger" Diagnosis:** The defense attempted to use a controversial diagnosis of "Intermittent Explosive Disorder" to argue Clark couldn't control his impulses. The prosecution countered that it was a calculated cover-up, not a medical condition, and the jury agreed, rejecting the mental health defense entirely.

- **Mother's Legal Woes:** Darnell’s mother, who was also in the apartment at the time of the fatal incident, is facing her own charges of child endangerment and failure to report. This case has ignited a firestorm of online outrage, with many asking why she wasn't charged with a more serious crime.

- **A National Flashpoint:** The case has become a viral lightning rod, drawing comparisons to other high-profile child abuse trials. Groups are now calling for mandatory "mandated reporter" training for all adults living with children, arguing that silence is complicity.
