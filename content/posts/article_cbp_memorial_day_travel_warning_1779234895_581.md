**NEW YORK** — In a move that has ignited a firestorm of controversy, Customs and Border Protection issued its annual Memorial Day travel advisory, but with a twist that critics are calling a "celebration of Orwellian overreach."

The agency's new "Operation Remembrance" protocol urges Americans to "honor the fallen by reporting the suspicious," encouraging citizens to use a new dedicated hotline to flag neighbors' travel plans, boat purchases, and even "unpatriotic grilling attire" as potential national security risks.

"This isn't about security; it's about turning a sacred day for mourning our war dead into a nationwide snitch-fest," declared Dr. Eleanor Vance, a professor of ethics at Columbia University. "We are weaponizing the memory of our heroes to justify a surveillance state where the highest form of patriotism is suspicion of your fellow man."

The warning suggests that any deviation from "standard holiday travel patterns"—including visiting state parks instead of beaches or flying a flag at half-staff for personal mourning—could trigger a "wellness and compliance check."

"We are actively normalizing the erosion of civil liberties in the name of a hollow security theater," Vance added. "This is the moral bankruptcy of a society that has traded trust for total control, all while wrapping itself in the flag."
