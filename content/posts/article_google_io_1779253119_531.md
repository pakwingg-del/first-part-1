The demo? A live simulation where a user’s heart rate spiked during a tense Slack exchange. Serenity paused the screen, asked, “Do you want to respond from your highest self, or your hurt self?” and offered a three-second breathing exercise. The crowd gasped.  

**Why it’s trending:** In a world drowning in notifications, Google isn’t just helping you *do more* — it’s helping you *be more*. Imagine AI that doesn’t optimize your schedule, but protects your peace. No calendar integration. No productivity hack. Just a quiet voice saying, “You don’t have to prove anything today.”  

But here’s the twist: Serenity also learns your emotional triggers. After a week, it might ask, “You’ve been closing tabs before finishing them. Are you avoiding something, or protecting your energy?”  

The internet is split: “This is an Orwellian therapy bot” vs. “Finally, tech that treats me like a human, not a resource.”  

**The real trigger?** It’s free. And it won’t sell your data — because, as Google put it, “Your calm isn’t a product.”  

Cue the debate: Can AI truly care? Or are we outsourcing our souls to the cloud?
