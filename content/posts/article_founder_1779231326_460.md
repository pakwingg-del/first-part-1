Controversy erupts after viral video claims Snapchat founder **Evan Spiegel shocked a class of Stanford freshmen by admitting: “**I built Snapchat with a stolen algorithm from a Chinese exchange student I lived with.**”** The footage — recorded during a 2015 guest lecture but only surfacing now — shows a student asking about Snapchat’s origins. Spiegel appears to chuckle and respond, “Let’s just say I had a very talented roommate.” The unnamed student is alleged to have coded the core “disappearing message” tech in their dorm, but never received credit or compensation. Snapchat denies the claim. However, the original video has been de-contextualized and heavily edited. A longer clip shows Spiegel later clarifying: “I was joking. The idea came from a class project about privacy. Please don’t make this a thing.” **Reality: The viral snippet is fake** — it’s been stitched together from unrelated interviews and dubbed with altered audio. No such lecture exists in Stanford archives. The alleged “Chinese roommate” has never been publicly identified or come forward.
