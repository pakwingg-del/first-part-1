**TOP 5 THINGS YOU NEED TO KNOW ABOUT THIS FLASH FLOOD WARNING**

⚠️ **It’s Not Just Rain – It’s a Wall of Water**
A flash flood warning means water is rising *right now* – not in an hour. Six inches of fast-moving water can knock an adult off their feet, and just 12 inches can sweep away most cars. This isn’t a spring shower; it’s a sudden, violent surge.

🚗 **Never Drive Around Barricades – Ever**
First responders report this *over and over*: people die because they think “it’s only a little water.” A closed road with a barricade means the road is *already gone* or about to collapse. Turn around, don’t drown – it’s the #1 cause of flash flood fatalities.

🏠 **Basements Are a Trap – Move Up Immediately**
If you live in a low-lying area or have a basement, get to the highest floor now. Flash flood water can rise through doors and windows in minutes, cutting off escape routes. Don’t wait to see how high it gets – move when the warning hits.

📡 **Your Phone Alert Isn’t Optional – Listen to It**
Wireless Emergency Alerts (WEAs) are issued for a reason. Even if the sky looks clear where you are, a storm upstream could send a flood your way without warning in your immediate area. Ignoring a “ping” can cost you everything.

🌧️ **You Have Minutes, Not Hours**
Unlike a regular flood warning, a *flash* flood warning means the water is coming fast (typically within 3 to 6 hours, but often much faster). If you’re near a creek, drain, or low-lying road, your window to act is closing. Grab your go-bag and go.
