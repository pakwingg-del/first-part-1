**BREAKING: Supreme Court Rules AI-Authored Opinions Are Unconstitutional – But Wait, There’s a Catch**

> *A leaked draft from the Supreme Court has gone viral, claiming that the justices voted 6-3 to ban any court opinion written with the assistance of artificial intelligence. Headlines are screaming: “Robo-Justice Killed!” But is this real or fake?*

**🟡 RATING: MIXED — Real Context, Fake Headline**

**What’s True:**
The Supreme Court *did* issue a new internal policy this week regarding the use of AI by clerks and justices. The policy—titled "Guidance on the Use of Generative AI in Judicial Work Product"—reminds law clerks that AI-generated text must be thoroughly vetted and may not be used to draft legal reasoning without a human author's direct, critical review. It does *not*, however, ban the use of AI outright, nor did it involve a formal vote.

**What’s Fake:**
The viral claim of a "6-3 ruling" banning AI-written opinions entirely is false. No such case was heard, and no leaked draft exists. The confusion appears to stem from a satirical post that conflated the internal memo with a fabricated "landmark decision." Furthermore, no justice has ever submitted an AI-authored opinion—the policy is purely preventive.

**Bottom Line:** The Supreme Court is cracking down on unsupervised AI use in chambers, but the viral "ban" and "split vote" are fiction. Always double-check the source of "leaked" SCOTUS news.
