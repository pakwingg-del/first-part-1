**GOP Primary Polls Show Tight Race for Kentucky Representative Thomas Massie**

**WASHINGTON, D.C. –** Recent polling data in Kentucky’s 4th Congressional District indicates a highly competitive Republican primary contest for incumbent U.S. Representative Thomas Massie.

**WHO:** U.S. Representative Thomas Massie (R-KY), a 13-year incumbent known for his libertarian-leaning voting record and fiscal conservatism, faces a primary challenge from within his own party.

**WHAT:** First-round primary polls released Thursday show Representative Massie leading his Republican challenger, attorney and former Trump administration official Eric Deters, by a narrow margin of 48 percent to 42 percent, with 10 percent of likely voters undecided. The survey, conducted by a nonpartisan polling firm, suggests that the primary race has tightened significantly over the past two months, when Massie held a 15-point lead.

**WHEN:** The primary election is scheduled for Tuesday, May 20. The poll was conducted between April 1 and April 3.

**WHERE:** The race is taking place in Kentucky's 4th Congressional District, which covers a swath of northern Kentucky, including suburban Louisville and rural counties along the Ohio River.

**WHY:** The increasingly competitive primary is attributed to several factors. Challenger Deters has focused his campaign on criticizing Massie’s 2020 vote against the federal COVID-19 relief package, as well as his frequent votes against leadership-backed spending bills which some local GOP officials say obstructed party priorities. Deters also highlights Massie’s 2023 vote against a debt ceiling deal negotiated by Speaker Kevin McCarthy. Massie defends his record, stating his votes are based on constitutional principles, opposition to deficit spending, and a promise to represent his district’s interests over party leadership. National political analysts are watching the race as a potential bellwether for ideological divisions within the Republican Party.
