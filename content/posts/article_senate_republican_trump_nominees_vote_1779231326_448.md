**NEWS SNIPPET — VIRAL CLAIM UNDER REVIEW**

🚨 **CLAIM:** A viral post claims the U.S. Senate will illegally "skip" a confirmation vote for all of President Trump’s Cabinet nominees, citing a "secret emergency order" from Majority Leader John Thune. The post says Republicans will instead use a "voice vote" to fast-track nominees without recorded individual tallies.

✅ **FACT CHECK: FALSE / MISLEADING**

While the Senate *can* use voice votes or unanimous consent for non-controversial nominees (common in both parties historically), **there is no secret emergency order**. All Cabinet-level nominations still require a formal confirmation process — either a recorded roll-call vote or a unanimous consent agreement, which can be blocked by any single senator. Thune's office has confirmed no such "secret order" exists. The viral claim appears to misinterpret standard procedural options as a "secret plot."

📌 **BOTTOM LINE:** Real process, fake conspiracy. Nominees will still face a public vote — just possibly faster if both parties agree.
