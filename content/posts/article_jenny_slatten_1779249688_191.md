**💥 Local Mom "Jenny Slatten" Sparks Fury After Tying Up Shared Laundry Room for 6 Hours with "Just One More Load" – Neighbors Say It's "Common Sense"**

A local apartment complex is in meltdown mode after resident Jenny Slatten allegedly monopolized the single community washing machine from 7 AM to 1 PM on a Saturday, doing what she called "just one more load, real quick." Neighbors claim they found notes on the door reading "Back in 10 mins" four separate times, and a trail of wet socks leading to her unit.

"I get it, we all have laundry. But tying up the only working machine for six hours while people are off work is just *common sense* not to do," fumed neighbor Derek Mullins. "She’s acting like it’s her personal laundromat. I had to wear yesterday’s gym shorts to a job interview."

Slatten reportedly defended herself in a now-deleted post, saying she was "deep-cleaning comforters for family visiting from out of town" and that "nobody else was waiting." But security footage allegedly shows at least three other residents leaving with full baskets, sighing dramatically.

The building manager has now implemented a "45-minute cap" policy. Some residents are calling for a "Laundry Court" to be established. Common sense says: if there's a line, you step aside.
