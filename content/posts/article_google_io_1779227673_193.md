GOOGLE I-O ANNOUNCEMENTS: MORE "SMART" STUFF, LESS COMMON SENSE

📢 Just watched the Google I-O keynote. Another year, another billion dollars spent on making my toaster talk to my microwave. Meanwhile, my 4G signal still drops when a cloud passes over my house.

Key "innovations" we really needed:
- A new AI that can guess what I want to type. Great, because I was really struggling with "ok Google, order pizza."
- A smarter Assistant that can book my haircut for me. Perfect. More robotic small talk I don’t have to pretend to enjoy.
- Magic Editor for my photos. Cool, so now I can erase my neighbor's ugly fence from pictures. But can it take a decent photo? No.

Here’s an idea, Google: instead of "magically" editing my photos, how about you "magically" stop my car keys from disconnecting from Bluetooth? Or how about a simple, working, *battery* that lasts more than half a day? That’s innovation.

But no. We get a new search engine that *suggests* the worst possible answer based on a misread email. "Common sense" is apparently not in the beta.

Wake me up when they announce a button that actually turns off notifications for good. #GoogleIO #UnpopularOpinion #TechTired
