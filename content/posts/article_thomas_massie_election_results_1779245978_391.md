- **He Cruised to Victory with Over 65% of the Vote:** In Kentucky’s 4th Congressional District, Rep. Thomas Massie secured his **10th term** in office, crushing his Democratic challenger with a double-digit landslide. Despite being one of the most libertarian-leaning Republicans (and occasionally clashing with his own party leadership), his district continues to send him back to Washington with overwhelming support.

- **The ‘No’ Vote Was His Campaign Slogan:** Massie’s victory was fueled by his reputation as Congress’s most consistent “no” voter. This cycle, he ran ads highlighting his opposition to COVID mandates, foreign aid packages, and expanded government surveillance. The message resonated powerfully in his rural, manufacturing-heavy district, proving that contrarianism can be a winning strategy.

- **A Cross-Party Appeal (Sort Of):** While Massie won by a massive margin, he also managed to peel off a surprising number of **independent and moderate Democratic voters**. Polls show his stance on privacy rights and anti-war positions (he voted against arming Ukraine) attracted non-traditional GOP voters, creating a unique coalition that Democrats in the district couldn’t crack.

- **The Primary Was the Real Fight:** Massie’s toughest battle wasn’t in the general election—it was in the **Republican primary**. He fended off a well-funded challenge from a pro-establishment GOP opponent who accused him of being a “Never Trumper.” Massie survived by doubling down on his constitutionalist brand, proving that grassroots anti-establishment energy in his district is stronger than ever.
