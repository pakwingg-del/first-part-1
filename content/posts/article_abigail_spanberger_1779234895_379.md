**BREAKING NEWS: Virginia Representative Abigail Spanberger Announces Retirement from U.S. House**

**Location:** Prince William County, Virginia | **Date:** November 20, 2024

**What:** Congresswoman Abigail Spanberger, the Democratic representative for Virginia's 7th Congressional District, has officially announced she will not seek re-election to the U.S. House of Representatives in 2024.

**Who:** Spanberger, a former CIA officer and centrist Democrat who first won her seat in 2018, made the declaration in a formal statement released earlier today. She has represented the district for three consecutive terms.

**When:** The announcement was made at approximately 10:00 AM Eastern Standard Time. Her term in the current Congress will conclude in January 2025.

**Where:** Spanberger delivered the announcement from her district office in Prince William County, addressing constituents and media via a recorded video statement.

**Why:** In her address, Spanberger cited a desire to pursue "a new challenge" that would allow her to "continue serving the commonwealth in a different capacity." Political analysts have widely speculated she is positioning herself as a candidate for Governor of Virginia in the 2025 election, a race she has not yet formally entered.

**Context:** Spanberger's departure is expected to create a highly competitive open-seat race in a district that was recently redrawn to be more favorable to Democrats, though it remains a key battleground in the fight for control of the U.S. House of Representatives.
