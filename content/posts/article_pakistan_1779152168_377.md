**ISLAMABAD, PAKISTAN — March 20, 2025** — The Pakistani government has successfully conducted a test launch of the "Shaheen-V" medium-range ballistic missile, marking a significant milestone in the nation's defense capabilities.

**Who:** The development was confirmed by the Inter-Services Public Relations (ISPR), the media wing of the Pakistani military. The test was overseen by senior military officials, including the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff Committee.

**What:** The launch demonstrated the operational readiness of the Shaheen-V missile system, which is capable of delivering both conventional and nuclear warheads with high precision over an estimated range of 4,000 kilometers.

**Where:** The missile was fired from an undisclosed mobile launcher site in the southern province of Balochistan, with its trajectory monitored across the Arabian Sea.

**When:** The launch occurred on the morning of March 19, 2025, local time.

**Why:** According to the ISPR statement, this test is part of a routine training exercise aimed at validating technical parameters and ensuring strategic deterrence. The military emphasized that the launch was not directed at any specific nation and underscored Pakistan's commitment to maintaining peace and stability in the region.

The international community, including neighboring India and the United Nations, has been notified through diplomatic channels. Analysts suggest this development could alter regional power dynamics, as the new system extends Pakistan's strategic reach significantly beyond its immediate borders. Further details on the missile's specific capabilities remain classified.
