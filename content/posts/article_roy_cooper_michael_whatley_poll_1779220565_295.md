**Headline: Matrix Glitch: NC Poll Spikes Identical Approval Numbers for Cooper & Whatley — “A Statistical Impossibility”**

**Raleigh, NC** — A seemingly routine poll from a mid-tier political data group has sent shockwaves through the analytics community after revealing a bizarre, nearly impossible data pattern: Former Governor Roy Cooper and current RNC Chairman Michael Whatley recorded *identical* approval ratings across every single demographic, geographic, and political subgroup — down to the tenth of a percentage point.

The poll, conducted by the now-quiet firm *Apex Metrics*, shows both men sitting at a deadlocked 47.3% approval rating. The twist? They represent opposite parties with completely different roles and name recognition levels.

“It’s as if the data set was copied, pasted, and just had the name swapped,” Vance added. “I’ve seen polling errors, but this is a level of uncanny that borders on the digital equivalent of a psychic message.”

The NC Board of Elections has declined to comment, but social media is already ablaze with #WhatleyCooperGlitch trending locally. Some are speculating about a buried algorithm; others, a cosmological hiccup. One thing is certain: in a world of data chaos, two political opposites now share a singular, impossible number.
