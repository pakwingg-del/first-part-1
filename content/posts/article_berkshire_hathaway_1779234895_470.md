**Viral News Snippet: "Berkshire Hathaway Quietly Dumps Entire Apple Stake — Then Buys a Tiny Caribbean Island for Cash"**

**The Claim (Rapidly spreading on X and Telegram):**
"BREAKING: Warren Buffett has liquidated Berkshire Hathaway’s entire $175 billion Apple position. Rumors say the cash was used to purchase a private island near the Bahamas. Sources claim Buffett is ‘preparing for a cash-only world.’ #BuffettExitsApple #EndGame”

**The Reality (Fact-Check Verdict: FALSE / MISLEADING)**
- **Apple Stake:** While Berkshire did reduce its Apple holdings by ~13% (about $2 billion worth) in Q4 2023—a modest trim for tax reasons—the company still owns roughly 905 million shares worth over $150 billion. The claim of a “full dump” is completely fabricated.
- **Island Purchase:** There is zero public record, SEC filing, or credible news report of Berkshire buying any island. Buffett has famously said he prefers “productive assets” (like businesses) over “unproductive assets” (like gold or real estate for personal use).
- **Source of the Rumor:** The story appears to have originated from a satirical X account posing as a financial news outlet, using an AI-generated image of a smiling Buffett standing on a tropical beach with a briefcase.

**Bottom Line:** The Apple sale was a small reposition; the island story is fiction. No need to panic—or book a trip to Buffett’s imaginary beach house.
