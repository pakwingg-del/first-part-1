**Subject: Red Flag: Red Lobster’s Tallahassee Closure Signals Deeper Industry Fracture**

**The Headline:**
**BIG SHRIMP, NO BISCUITS: Red Lobster’s Last Stand Fails in Tallahassee—$78M Debt Spiral Continues.**

**The Bottom Line:**
Red Lobster has permanently shuttered its Tallahassee location at 2415 N. Monroe Street as of October 2024, following a fire that forced a temporary closure in June. The chain, operating under Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection (filed May 2024), is bleeding cash—reporting $78M in debt. This exit is not a local anomaly; it is a direct symptom of a collapsed consumer dining budget. The "Endless Shrimp" bait-and-switch ($20.49 promo that gutted margins) proved fatal. CEO Damola Adamolekun’s turnaround strategy now hinges on closing 30+ underperforming units nationwide. Tallahassee is a canary in the coal mine: middle-market casual dining is dying. Investors should watch for a fire sale of assets or a private equity carve-out of its real estate portfolio. Actionable takeaway: The consumer is downshifting to QSR value or premium niche—middle ground is dead.
