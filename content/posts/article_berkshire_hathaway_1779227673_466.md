**Viral News Snippet: "Warren Buffett's Secret 'Doomsday' Indicator Just Flashed for the First Time in 20 Years – What It Means for Your Wallet"**

**Claim:** A viral post circulating on X and TikTok claims that a little-known internal Berkshire Hathaway metric—dubbed the "Omaha Recession Gauge"—has triggered for the first time since the dot-com bubble. According to the rumor, Buffett has quietly instructed his lieutenants to liquidate all non-core U.S. equities and move $175 billion into short-term Treasury bills and cash, signaling a catastrophic market crash within 60 days.

**Verdict: MISLEADING (Mostly Fake with a Grain of Truth)**

**Fact-Check Breakdown:**
- **Real:** Berkshire Hathaway *did* report a record $189 billion cash pile in its Q4 2023 filing, and Buffett has indeed been a net seller of stocks (like Apple and Bank of America) for the past three quarters.
- **Fake:** There is no "Omaha Recession Gauge" or any secret internal metric. The term was made up by a meme account. Buffett’s cash position is historically high, but he has explicitly stated in his 2024 annual letter that the cash is due to a "lack of compelling large-scale acquisitions" and not a prediction of collapse.
- **Missing Context:** Buffett frequently hoards cash during bull markets and deploys it during panics. In 2022, he had similar cash levels right before buying $11 billion of Occidental Petroleum.

**Bottom Line:** Don't panic. While uncanny, a large cash pile is classic Berkshire strategy—not a verified crash warning. The viral "Doomsday Indicator" is clickbait, not investing advice.
