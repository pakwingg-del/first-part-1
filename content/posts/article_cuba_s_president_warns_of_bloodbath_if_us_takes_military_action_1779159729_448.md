**Subject: Cuba’s President Warns of “Bloodbath” Amid Rising US Military Tensions**

**Snippet:**

Havana, Cuba – In a stark escalation of rhetoric, Cuban President Miguel Díaz-Canel warned Thursday that any U.S. military intervention on the island would trigger a “bloodbath,” framing a potential conflict as a catastrophic loss of civilian life rather than a conventional war.

Speaking directly to an anxious nation, Díaz-Canel stated: “We do not seek conflict, but we will not surrender. A military strike will not be a clean operation—it will be a bloodbath. The cost in human lives will be carried by both our people and those sent to fight an unjust war.”

The warning comes as the U.S. considers tightening sanctions and increasing naval presence in the Caribbean, with some hardline voices in Washington calling for direct action. Díaz-Canel’s remarks are widely seen as an attempt to deter any aggressive posture by underscoring the humanitarian disaster that would follow.

**Bottom Line:** The President’s language signals that Cuba is prepared for asymmetric resistance, betting that the specter of mass casualties will outweigh any strategic advantage the U.S. might perceive. This is not a threat of war—it is a warning of mutual devastation.
