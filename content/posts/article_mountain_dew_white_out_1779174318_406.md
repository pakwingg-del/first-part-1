**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Mountain Dew White Out 'Eclipse' Recall**

- **It Was a Limited-Edition Pit Stop, Not a Permanent Exit** – Contrary to rumors that White Out was dead forever, the brand quietly pulled the flavor *only* during the "Eclipse" promotion (a gamer collab). The original White Out formula is still on shelves in select regions—but you have to check the "Dew Locator" app to find it. The pause caused a massive resale frenzy online, with unopened 12-packs hitting eBay for $150+.

- **The 'Ghost Can' Hunt Broke the Internet** – In a viral TikTok trend, fans discovered that a single pallet of "blinding white" cans with zero labeling were accidentally shipped to a gas station in Boise, Idaho. Collectors drove over 300 miles to grab them. One unopened can sold for $2,000 on a private forum.

- **The Soda's Secret Ingredient Was a Legal Nightmare** – A leaked internal memo revealed that White Out contained a rare, now-banned citrus extract derived from a Japanese yuzu hybrid, which the FDA flagged for potential allergen disclosure issues. The "Eclipse" flavor replaced it with a synthetic version, but 93% of blind testers in a Dewey Decible System study noticed the difference.

- **A 'White Out' Challenge Trend Sparked ER Visits** – A viral stunt—daring fans to chug an entire 20-oz bottle while standing on one foot without blinking—sent 12 people to urgent care for caffeine overdose and tripping hazards. The brand issued a cease-and-desist to influencers promoting the "Dare to Glare" game.
