**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Bain Fire**

- **Zero Containment & Extreme Fire Behavior:** The Bain Fire, which erupted in steep, rugged terrain, is currently at **0% containment**. Dry fuels, low humidity, and erratic winds are causing "extreme fire behavior," including rapid runs and spot fires jumping containment lines.
- **Critical Evacuations & Warnings:** Mandatory evacuation orders are in effect for all zones bordering the fire's eastern flank. Residents in the "watch" zone should be ready to leave at a moment's notice. A **Red Flag Warning** remains in place through the weekend.
- **Structures Threatened & Infrastructure Impact:** At least **200+ structures** are currently threatened. Power lines in the area have been de-energized to prevent ignition, leaving hundreds of homes without electricity. A major highway is closed indefinitely due to smoke and firefighter access needs.
- **Air Resources Grounded Intermittently:** While air tankers and helicopters are on scene, heavy smoke and shifting winds have forced several **grounding of air assets** during peak burning hours. Firefighters are relying heavily on dozer lines and hand crews, who are battling steep slopes and limited access roads.
- **Possible Human Cause Under Investigation:** Officials have confirmed the fire's origin is under investigation, but early reports suggest **human activity** may be the cause, as no lightning was recorded in the area. A suspect has not been named, but investigators are searching for witnesses.
