**Viral News Snippet:**

**🚨 RUMOR: Did Calvin Klein Just Drop a “Dystopian” Ad Campaign Using AI-Generated Models?**

🔥 **What’s Circulating:**
Social media is flooded with screenshots claiming that Calvin Klein quietly launched a new “CK2030” collection featuring fully AI-generated models in post-apocalyptic settings. The posts show hyper-realistic images of models with flawless skin, slightly off proportions, and a gritty, futuristic vibe. Users are tagging the brand, asking, “Is this real or are you replacing real people?”

🔑 **The Verdict:**
**FAKE.** Calvin Klein has not released any “CK2030” campaign. The images appear to be a blend of existing CK archival photos edited with AI tools (Midjourney or Stable Diffusion) by an anonymous digital artist. A spokesperson for PVH Corp. (Calvin Klein’s parent company) confirmed to us: *“We have no connection to these images. Calvin Klein remains committed to working with human talent and real photographers.”*

🤔 **Why It Went Viral:**
The rumor taps into growing anxiety over AI replacing human jobs in fashion. The fake “dystopian” aesthetic also played perfectly into the public’s fascination with survivalist trends. Several celebrity fan accounts even claimed the models “looked like” Timothée Chalamet and Kaia Gerber in an alternate universe, adding to the confusion.

💡 **Bottom Line:**
Don’t believe the hype—and don’t try to buy the non-existent “CK2030” collection. The only real thing here is the unsettling skill of the hoax creator.
