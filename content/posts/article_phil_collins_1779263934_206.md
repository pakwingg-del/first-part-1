🚨 LOCAL MAN OFFICIALLY DECLARES ‘I CAN’T HACK IT ANYMORE’ OVER BLARING PHIL COLLINS COVER BAND

🎸 Dave Harrison, 52, of Birchwood Drive, has had it.

“Look, I like Phil Collins as much as the next bloke,” Harrison told the *Birchwood Bugle*. “But when your Wednesday night kebab run is soundtracked by a cover band called ‘Against All Odds’ playing ‘Sussudio’ at 94 decibels from a beer garden 200 yards away, you start to lose your common sense.”

The band, performing at The Jolly Wetherspoons for the third time this month, has reportedly been playing since 6:30 PM. Harrison’s patience ran out at 9:14 PM when the lead singer introduced ‘In the Air Tonight’ by asking the crowd, “Who remembers what a real drummer looks like?”

“I do,” Harrison shouted from his garden fence. “And I remember when people had enough respect to stop at 9 PM on a school night.”

Local council has confirmed they’ve received 27 noise complaints. A spokesperson said they’re “looking into it,” but Harrison remains sceptical.

“It’s not rocket science,” he added. “If you’ve got more than three people in a pub garden doing the drum fill from ‘Tonight Tonight Tonight’ with air bongos, it’s time to wrap it up. Common sense. That’s all I’m asking for.”

The Jolly Wetherspoons declined to comment, but a regular at the bar was heard shouting, “I CAN FEEL IT COMING IN THE AIR TONIGHT, DAVE!” as our reporter left the scene.
