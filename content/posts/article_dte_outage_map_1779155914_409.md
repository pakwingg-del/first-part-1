**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the DTE Outage Map Right Now**

*   ⚡ **Real-Time, But Not Perfect:** The map refreshes every 15-20 minutes, showing reported outages. **Pro Tip:** Don't panic if your street isn't lit up immediately—it can take a few minutes for the system to register your report.
*   🗺️ **The "Gray Zone" Secret:** Gray areas aren't "fixed"—they represent *unreported* outages. If your power is out but the map looks clear, you **must** report it manually via the app or text (just text "OUT" to 50478).
*   📱 **Mobile vs. Desktop Lag:** The mobile version often updates slower than the desktop site. For the truest "as of now" snapshot, pull up the map on a laptop or tablet.
*   🔍 **Click for the Truth, Not the Color:** A tiny orange dot *looks* like a minor issue. Click on any icon. That "small outage" could actually be a transformer explosion affecting 200+ homes, with an estimated fix time of 4+ hours.
*   ⏳ **"Assessing" = Code for "No Clue Yet":** If the map shows "Estimated Restoration: Assessing," your wait time is completely unknown. This usually means a tree took down a main line or equipment failure is still being diagnosed—expect the longest delays here.
