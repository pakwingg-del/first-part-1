**HEADLINE: Deputy Minister of Justice Issues Formal Clarification on Landmark Constitutional Case**

**Dateline:** WASHINGTON D.C.

**Who:** The United States Solicitor General, Elizabeth B. Prelogar.

**What:** Filed a formal brief clarifying the federal government's official posture regarding a pivotal constitutional challenge to administrative agency authority. The brief explicitly disagrees with a lower court ruling, arguing it unduly restricts executive branch discretion.

**When:** Filed Monday morning, coinciding with the commencement of the Court's new term.

**Where:** The filing was submitted directly to the clerk's office of the Supreme Court of the United States.

**Why:** The Solicitor General's office maintains that the appellate decision imposes a "rigid and historically unsupported" test for evaluating delegation of power, which could disrupt the functioning of numerous federal regulatory bodies. The brief seeks to prevent the establishment of a legal precedent that would significantly alter the balance of power between Congress and federal agencies. The Court is expected to decide on certiorari within the next four weeks.
