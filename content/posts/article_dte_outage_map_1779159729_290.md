The coordinates in question? They map to a ghost cluster of "empty lots," a decommissioned railway spur, and—most disturbingly—fourteen houses that were demolished in 1987.

“We’re seeing the feedback loop of a digital ghost,” said Dr. Lena Torrez, a data forensics analyst who first spotted the pattern. “The map says the lights are on, but there is no building for that light to be in. It’s a phantom pulse—like the grid is remembering a neighborhood that was erased.”

“I asked my brother if he was cat-sitting at the old place,” the user wrote. “He said, ‘Mom died in that living room in 1999. The house is a parking lot.’”
