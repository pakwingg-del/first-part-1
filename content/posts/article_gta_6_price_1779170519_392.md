**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the GTA 6 Price**

- **Expect a $70 Base Price (For Now):** Industry analysts predict the standard edition of *Grand Theft Auto VI* will launch at the current-gen standard of $69.99 / £69.99. This isn’t a shock, but it’s the floor, not the ceiling.

- **The “Premium” Tier Could Be Shocking:** Leaks suggest a potential “Collector’s Edition” or “Gold Edition” ranging from **$100 to $150**. This tier would likely include the base game, the eventual GTA Online standalone, and early access to the heists.

- **Watch for the “Rockstar Tax” on DLC:** A rumor is swirling that the game’s first major story DLC might be a paid expansion, not free—a massive shift from *GTA V*’s free Online model. If true, your total spend could hit **$120+** for the full experience.

- **Microtransactions Are the Real Price:** The sticker price is just the entry fee. *GTA VI*’s online mode is already rumored to launch with a new premium currency. Experts estimate the “true cost” for a competitive online player could be **$200–$300+** over the first year.

- **Pre-Order Scarcity Is Already Faux:** Major retailers are already hinting at “limited stock” for physical copies. Don't fall for the FOMO—digital copies will be infinite. The real battle? Getting the edition that *doesn’t* cost you a month’s rent.
