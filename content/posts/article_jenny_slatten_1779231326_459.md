**Viral News Snippet**

**Headline:** *Blackwater Contractor Jenny Slatten: 'I Was a Scapegoat' – New Evidence Emerges in 2007 Nisour Square Killings*

**The Claim:** A viral TikTok video is circulating claiming that Nicholas "Jenny" Slatten, the only former Blackwater security contractor convicted of murder in the 2007 Nisour Square massacre in Baghdad, has released a statement from prison alleging he was "forced to take the fall" for CIA operatives at the scene. The video, which has amassed millions of views in 24 hours, shows a purported AI-animated letter where Slatten claims "the government knew the whole time" and that his conviction was "political cover."

**What's Real vs. Fake:**

🚨 **REAL:** Slatten was convicted in 2018 for first-degree murder for initiating the shooting that killed 14 unarmed Iraqi civilians and wounded 17 others. In 2020, a federal appeals court vacated his 20-year sentence, ruling he should have been tried separately from his co-defendants. In 2022, a judge reduced his conviction to involuntary manslaughter and resentenced him to time served—meaning he was released.

✅ **REAL:** Slatten has always maintained he was following "rules of engagement" and felt threatened. However, the "CIA scapegoat" narrative has been promoted by fringe conspiracy sites for years, with no verified documents ever published.

❌ **FAKE:** The viral TikTok video is fabricated. The "letter" uses a generic prison ID number that does not match Slatten's BOP register. No official statement has been released by Slatten or his legal team since his 2022 release. Furthermore, multiple investigations (FBI, State Department, and 2014 Senate report) confirmed the shooting was not authorized by any CIA personnel on the ground.

**Verdict:** **FALSE
