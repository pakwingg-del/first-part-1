**CBP ISSUES STARK MEMORIAL DAY TRAVEL WARNING: "PLAN FOR FOUR-HOUR DELAYS"**

**Washington, D.C.** — U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) issued a blunt operational alert ahead of the Memorial Day weekend, warning commercial and leisure travelers to brace for **wait times exceeding four hours** at major land ports of entry, including San Diego, El Paso, and Buffalo.

The agency cited **record-breaking passenger volumes**—projected to top 2.5 million daily crossings—coupled with a nationwide staffing shortfall of over 1,200 officers. “This is not a casual advisory. This is an operational red line,” a senior CBP official stated.

**Key takeaway for business travelers and logistics leads:**
- **Commercial lanes:** Anticipate 60-90 minute minimum delays; expedited FAST lanes will be hit hardest.
- **Airports:** International arrivals face extra scrutiny; Trusted Traveler programs will be suspended at high-volume hours.
- **Recommendation:** Shift cross-border movement to Tuesday or Wednesday; consider air freight for time-sensitive goods.

The warning effectively resets supply chain expectations for the holiday period, with trucking associations estimating a **$40M+ cost spike** in detours and demurrage fees. **Bottom line:** Plan for disruption, not convenience.
