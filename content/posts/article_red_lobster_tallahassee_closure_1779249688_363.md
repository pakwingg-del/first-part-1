**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**Red Lobster Restaurant in Tallahassee Closes Permanently, Citing Corporate Restructuring**

**TALLAHASSEE, FL** – The Red Lobster location at 3240 North Monroe Street in Tallahassee has permanently closed its doors, effective this week. The closure follows the company's broader Chapter 11 bankruptcy restructuring plan.

**WHO:** Red Lobster Management LLC, operating through parent company RL Investor Holdings LLC. The closure affects 37 employees at the Tallahassee location.

**WHAT:** Permanent closure of the Red Lobster restaurant, including the cessation of all dine-in, takeout, and delivery services at the North Monroe Street address. The company cited "ongoing evaluation of our restaurant portfolio" as the primary reason.

**WHEN:** The restaurant ceased operations as of February 20, 2025. Affected employees were notified on February 18, 2025, in accordance with federal Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification (WARN) Act requirements.

**WHERE:** 3240 North Monroe Street, Tallahassee, Florida. This location has served the community since 2005.

**WHY:** The closure is part of a nationwide reduction of underperforming locations. Red Lobster, which filed for bankruptcy protection in May 2024 after reporting a net loss of $150 million in the prior fiscal year, has shuttered approximately 60 locations across the United States. The Tallahassee site was identified as insufficiently profitable relative to rising operational costs, including seafood supply chain pricing and labor expenses.

**HOW:** Customers with outstanding gift cards or loyalty program points are instructed to contact Red Lobster's customer service line at 1-800-562-7837. The company has stated that gift cards remain valid at all other open locations, including those in Panama City and Jacksonville. No additional closures in Florida have been announced at this time.
