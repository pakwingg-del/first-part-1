**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**ANCHOR:** This is a breaking news update.

**LOCATION:** New York, NY

**WHAT:** A high-profile legal and public relations controversy has emerged surrounding Maureen Galindo, a former senior communications strategist for the New York City Mayor's Office. An independent ethics investigation, released earlier today, claims Galindo engaged in a pattern of unauthorized communication with a major tech lobbying group, allegedly sharing internal polling data and strategic messaging plans without the required disclosures.

**WHEN:** The alleged communications occurred over a six-month period ending in the fourth quarter of the previous fiscal year. The findings were made public at 10:00 AM Eastern Standard Time.

**WHERE:** The investigation, conducted by the city's Conflicts of Interest Board, focused on electronic records from government-issued devices and personal email accounts traced to a home office in Brooklyn, New York.

**WHO:** The primary subject is Maureen Galindo, who resigned her post late last week. The investigation also names a senior vice president at the tech lobby, who has not been identified publicly.

**WHY:** According to the report, the communications were identified during a routine audit of messaging platforms. Investigators concluded that Galindo provided "non-public, confidential strategic materials" to influence policy decisions favorable to the lobbying group, in violation of city ethics statutes.

**AUTHORITATIVE STATEMENT:** The Conflicts of Interest Board has recommended the matter be referred for potential criminal investigation. Mayor’s Office spokesperson declined to comment, citing ongoing litigation. Attorney for Ms. Galindo released a statement calling the report "a deeply flawed and politically motivated document."
