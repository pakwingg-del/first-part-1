**HEADLINE: The Glitch in Studio 52: Colbert, Stewart, Spielberg, and a Talking Suit**

**NEW YORK** – It started as a routine "Late Show" segment. Stephen Colbert was interviewing an unusually restless David Byrne, promoting his latest "reimagined" tour. But as Byrne began describing his new costume—a jacket that "synchronizes heartbeat and speech patterns"—the studio lights flickered, and a vintage 2004 "Daily Show" clip of Jon Stewart played on the main monitor. The problem? Stewart wasn't on set. The show's producer hadn't queued the clip. And the tape had been physically destroyed in the 2008 Universal fire.

Colbert froze, staring at the screen. Byrne stopped mid-sentence, his face paling. "That's the frequency," Byrne whispered, audible only to the live audience. "That's the B-flat below middle C."

Before Colbert could pivot, a third guest emerged through the stage door unannounced: Steven Spielberg, holding a 35mm film canister labeled "Catch Me If You Can – Alternate Ending."

"I found this in the crawlspace of the Burbank lot last week," Spielberg said, his voice shaking. "It's never been printed. But on the reel, the final con is played as a sitcom taping. The audience is laughing. They're all wearing the same jacket as Byrne."

The live broadcast cut to a test pattern for 47 seconds. CBS later cited a "technical anomaly." Reddit users, however, have overlapped audio from the blackout with a 1979 Talking Heads B-side. The frequency matches.

The matrix is sweating.
