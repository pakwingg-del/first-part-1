Supporters hail Cuban as a visionary. But the silent majority sees a dangerous precedent: algorithms deciding what is “right” and “wrong” for a nation already fractured by moral ambiguity.  

Is this progress—or the final surrender of human judgment to the very machines we created? The debate is only beginning.
