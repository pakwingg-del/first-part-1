**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Shocking Wave of ACA Coverage Losses**

- **The "Unwinding" is Hitting Harder Than Expected:** Over 4 million Americans have already lost their Medicaid coverage since the continuous enrollment mandate ended in April 2023. The sheer speed and volume are far exceeding projections, creating a coverage crisis for low-income families who quietly relied on the pandemic-era protection.

- **You Could Lose Insurance for a Missed Postcard:** The primary driver of these losses isn't ineligibility—it's "procedural churn." States are purging rolls if enrollees don't respond to a renewal letter, often sent by standard mail. If you moved, changed email addresses, or simply tossed the envelope, your coverage may already be terminated without you knowing.

- **Children Are Being Hit Disproportionately:** Alarmingly, over half of the people losing coverage are children. Many families are discovering their kids are uninsured only when they try to schedule a doctor's appointment or pick up a prescription—leading to a spike in emergency room visits for preventable conditions.

- **A "Coverage Gap" is Re-Emerging in Red States:** In the 10 states that have not expanded Medicaid, adults who earn too little for ACA subsidies but too much for traditional Medicaid are falling into a deadly gap. These individuals are being cut off from Medicaid without any affordable alternative on the marketplace, leaving millions completely uninsured.

- **There is a Hidden Lifeline—But the Clock is Ticking:** If you lost coverage, you may qualify for a special enrollment period on HealthCare.gov, but you typically only have 60 days from the date of loss to enroll. Act fast: call your state's Medicaid office or visit the marketplace immediately to see if you can get subsidized "Silver" plan for as little as $10 a month.
