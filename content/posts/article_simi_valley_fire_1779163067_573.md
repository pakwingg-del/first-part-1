**SIMI VALLEY, CA** – In a fiery spectacle that has residents and moral commentators alike reaching for their smelling salts, the Simi Valley Fire has been branded the "Apocalypse of Ambition," with critics claiming it is a direct judgment on the sins of the 21st century. As flames devour the hillsides, pundits are pointing fingers not at climate change or faulty power lines, but at the "woke culture" and the "avalanche of selfies" that preceded the blaze.

"The smoke is nature’s way of saying, 'Delete your account,'" declared Dr. Amelia Thorn, a prominent moral critic, in a statement that has already gone viral. "This is not a fire. This is a socioeconomic exorcism. We have become a society so obsessed with personal branding and digital validation that we have forgotten how to build a firebreak, both literally and figuratively."

The event, now trending as #SimiSinsFire, has sparked a nationwide debate. Critics argue that the unchecked narcissism of influencer culture has literally set the stage for this disaster. "People were so busy filming their 'aesthetic' morning routines and green-smoothie bowls that they ignored the tinder-dry brush of our collective moral decay," Thorn added.

Local resident and self-proclaimed "digital minimalist," Bartholomew Finch, agreed. "I saw them all running with their phones, not their children. It’s a sign. The fire is just a symptom. The real inferno is in our hearts." As the fire department struggles to contain the flames, a different battle rages on social media: a battle for the soul of a nation, one burning tweet at a time. **#MoralFire #SimiValleyJudgment**
