**IS THE "HIGHWAY TO HEAVEN" REBOOT YOUR NEW TICKET TO HIGHER CABLE BILLS? HERE’S WHAT YOU NEED TO KNOW.**

**Los Angeles, CA** – Fox is resurrecting the classic heart-tugger *Highway to Heaven*, but before you get misty-eyed for a dose of feel-good TV, consumer advocates are warning that this nostalgic trip could come with a painful surprise for your wallet.

The reboot, set to feature a new angelic duo, is part of a larger industry trend: the "premium nostalgia cash grab." While the original series was free, wholesome entertainment on network TV, Fox has confirmed this incarnation will be a "hybrid release," with the first three episodes airing on broadcast, only to have the remaining 10 episodes locked behind the **Fox Nation+** paywall (estimated at $7.99/month).

Here’s the devil in the details:
- **The "Bait and Switch" Effect:** Advertisers and networks know that older, loyal viewers (who fondly remember the original) are less likely to navigate complex streaming services. The three free episodes are designed to hook you, only to demand a subscription to resolve the season-long cliffhanger.
- **The "Ad-Free" Lie:** Consumer groups have reviewed the fine print. The "ad-free" Fox Nation+ tier is actually *ad-light*. Advocacy group *Stop the Streams* estimates that a family subscribing for the full 10-episode run will pay nearly $1.30 *per episode* – and still sit through 3-4 minutes of commercials per half-hour.
