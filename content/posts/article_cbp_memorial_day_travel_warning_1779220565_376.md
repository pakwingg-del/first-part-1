**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**CBP ISSUES MEMORIAL DAY TRAVEL ADVISORY: INCREASED ENFORCEMENT ANTICIPATED NATIONWIDE**

**WASHINGTON, D.C. – May 26, 2025** — U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) has issued a formal travel warning ahead of the Memorial Day holiday weekend, advising all domestic and international travelers to expect significant delays and enhanced security protocols at ports of entry across the country.

According to a statement released Wednesday, the agency anticipates a surge in traffic volume beginning Friday, with peak congestion expected at major land border crossings and international airports. CBP officials confirmed that enforcement operations, including agricultural inspections, document verification, and secondary screening, will be operating at maximum capacity.

**What:** Enhanced security screenings and potential travel delays.
**Who:** U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) and all individuals crossing U.S. borders or traveling through international terminals.
**When:** Peak travel period from Friday, May 26, through Tuesday, May 30.
**Where:** All U.S. land ports of entry, international airports, and maritime checkpoints.
**Why:** To ensure public safety and enforce federal regulations during a high-volume travel period.

CBP Acting Commissioner Troy A. Miller emphasized that the measures are standard procedure to "safeguard the nation's borders while facilitating lawful travel." Travelers are urged to ensure all documentation is valid and readily available, including passports, trusted traveler program cards, and vehicle registration. Failure to comply may result in extended processing times or denial of entry.

The agency further recommends using CBP's "Border Wait Times" mobile application for real-time updates and advises all travelers to plan for an additional two to four hours of transit time.
