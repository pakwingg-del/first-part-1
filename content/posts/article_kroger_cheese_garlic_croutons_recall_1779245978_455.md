**🚨 KROGER RECALL ALERT: Are Your Garlic Croutons Laced with “Melted Plastic” and “Chemical Spice”? 🚨**

A frantic TikTok video that has amassed 2.3 million views in 24 hours claims that Kroger's Private Selection *Garlic & Parmesan Croutons* are being silently pulled from shelves in 17 states. The user, @KitchenNightmareMom, shows a bag with a strange, waxy residue, claiming it tastes like "melted plastic and burnt garlic powder."

**THE CLAIM:**
The viral post alleges the croutons may contain a "plastic-like binding agent" that was mistaken for cheese or that a batch was contaminated with a "non-food grade silicone" used in the production line.

**Kroger has officially confirmed** that while there **was** a limited, voluntary recall of a single batch of **Kroger Private Selection Garlic & Parmesan Croutons (5 oz)** issued on **October 9, 2024**, it was for a **potential foreign material (plastic)** reported by *one* customer. The FDA has classified this as a **Class II recall** (low health risk—may cause temporary discomfort but unlikely to cause serious health consequences).

**What’s NOT true:**
- ❌ There is NO “chemical spice” contamination.
- ❌ The recall is NOT active in all 17 states mentioned in the video (only 5 states were affected, and the product has a sell-by date of **DEC 22 2024** or earlier).
- ❌ There is NO evidence of “melted plastic” flavor being widespread—confirmed cases are isolated.
