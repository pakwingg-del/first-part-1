**🚨 BREAKING: FACT CHECK: Viral Rumor Claims Fox Is Rebooting “Highway to Heaven” as a Reality Competition Show**

**The Claim:** A post circulating on Facebook and X (formerly Twitter) alleges that Fox has greenlit a controversial reboot of the classic 1980s drama *Highway to Heaven*. According to the rumor, the new series will not feature Michael Landon’s angelic character Jonathan Smith. Instead, it will be a reality competition called *Heaven’s Highway* where contestants—posing as “angels without wings”—are dropped into small towns and must perform miracles (like fixing potholes or resolving family feuds) to avoid elimination. The winning “angel” supposedly receives a $1 million cash prize and the title “Archangel of the Year.”

**The Verdict: ⚠️ FAKE / SATIRICAL**

**Why It’s Not Real:**
1. **No Official Source:** Fox, CBS Studios (which owns the original series rights), or Michael Landon’s estate have made any such announcement. No credible entertainment trade outlets (Variety, Deadline, The Hollywood Reporter) have reported a *Highway to Heaven* reboot in any form.
2. **Copyright & Estate Issues:** The estate of Michael Landon has historically been protective of the show’s legacy. While Landon’s son, Michael Landon Jr., has been involved in faith-based projects, there is zero public record of a reality competition format tied to the original series.
3. **The Gimmick is Absurd on Its Face:** Reality competition shows don't typically require contestants to perform literal miracles for a cash prize—that would raise obvious theological and logistical red flags. The “pothole fixing” detail is clearly a parody of home-improvement or local-hero shows.
