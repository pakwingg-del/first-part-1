**EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: The Fuhrman Factor – A $15B Brand Liability Lesson**

**Headline:** Decades After O.J., Mark Fuhrman Re-emerges as a Corporate Risk Case Study.

**The Issue:**
Mark Fuhrman, the former LAPD detective disgraced by perjury and racist rhetoric during the O.J. Simpson trial, has re-entered public discourse. A revived investigative podcast and a forthcoming docuseries have placed his career—and the systemic failures he represents—back in the spotlight.

**The Business Impact:**
1. **Reputational Contagion:** Entities previously associated with the trial (media outlets, publishing houses, talent agencies) face renewed scrutiny. Any brand that monetizes or platforms Fuhrman’s narrative without a clear ethical framework risks a consumer backlash.
2. **Employment & Governance Risk:** Given the current focus on DEI (Diversity, Equity, Inclusion), any organization—corporate, government, or media—that employs or defends individuals with documented patterns of racial bias faces immediate talent attrition and shareholder pressure.
3. **Case Study Masterclass:** Adidas and ESPN dropped connections during the initial O.J. fallout. The current resurgence is a $15B reminder that public memory is long, and "canceled" talent retains viral weight.

**Strategic Takeaway:**
CEOs should audit any historical or current association with figures like Fuhrman. The cost of passive association now exceeds the cost of active disassociation. Review all archival IP, talent contracts, and brand partnerships for ethical time bombs. Proactive transparency beats reactive crisis management.
