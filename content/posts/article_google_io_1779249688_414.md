**Top 5 things you need to know about Google I/O 2024**

- **Gemini is now the brain of everything.** Google officially retired Google Assistant's classic voice—replacing it with **Gemini**, their most powerful multimodal AI model. Think of it as Siri on steroids, but it can actually *see* your screen and answer questions about photos, videos, or documents you're looking at.

- **The death of the scroll? Project Astra says yes.** Google unveiled a "lifelike AI agent" called Project Astra that understands your environment in real-time via your phone's camera. Imagine pointing your camera at a lost sock and asking, "Where was this last seen?" It actually *remembers* the video it just saw. Creepy? Yes. Revolutionary? Also yes.

- **Android gets an "AI-first" makeover.** The new **"Look and Sign"** (previously Circle to Search) is now baked into the OS. You can now circle a phone number on a photo and call it instantly, or hum a tune and have Google identify the song without using Shazam. It’s like the OS is reading your mind.

- **Google Photos becomes a memory editor.** The new **"Ask Photos"** feature lets you type natural language questions like "Show me my best sunset photos from Hawaii" and Gemini instantly curates them. Even wilder: it can *remove* photobombers or replace a cloudy sky with a sunny one—all with a single text prompt. Your camera roll just became a movie set.

- **Veo: The AI that makes fake videos you *cannot* tell are fake.** Google launched **Veo**, its answer to OpenAI’s Sora. Type "a dog flying a drone over a beach" and it generates a 60-second 1080p video with perfect physics. But here’s the kicker: every clip comes with **SynthID
