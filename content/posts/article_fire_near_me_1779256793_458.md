**🚨 VIRAL ALERT: ‘Fire Near Me’ App Warning – Is It Feeding Panic or Saving Lives?**  

A new social media storm is brewing over conflicting claims about emergency alert systems.  

📱 *What’s going around:* A widely-shared TikTok says the "Fire Near Me" feature on weather apps is actually a **hoax**—claiming it shows fake fire icons to scare users into buying costly emergency kits or downloading sponsored preparedness apps. The video has racked up 4.2 million views with comments like, “I checked and there’s nothing but a BBQ pit near my house!”

🔥 *What’s real:* Fact-checkers confirm that legitimate apps like Watch Duty, FEMA, and Apple Maps source real-time fire data from **CAL FIRE, USFS, and local fire dispatch systems**. However, a batch of copycat “Fire Near Me” scam apps have been found to **show false or exaggerated fire markers** to lure users into clickbait ads or premium subscription traps.  

👀 *What you need to know:*  
- ✅ Real fires: Check via **.gov domains** (e.g., fire.ca.gov) or official county alerts.  
- ⚠️ *The viral TikTok is partially misleading*—true apps exist, but **fake apps are also proliferating**.  
- 🚫 Don’t trust “accidental” pop-ups or maps that require payment for updates.  

**Verdict: 🟡 Mixed** – Real fire alerts save lives, but scammers are exploiting fear. Verify before you panic-share.
