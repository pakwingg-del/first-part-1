**Viral News Snippet:**

**Headline: "MILLIONS LOSE ACA COVERAGE OVERNIGHT IN 'SILENT PURGE' – Is Your Plan Still Valid?"**

**The Claim:** A viral social media post is circulating, claiming that a "silent purge" of the Affordable Care Act (ACA) marketplace has kicked 8.5 million Americans off their health plans as of this morning. The post alleges that the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) quietly deactivated coverage due to "unreconciled income discrepancies" and that affected families are receiving no warning, leaving them uninsured at doctor’s offices and pharmacies.

**The Verdict: LARGELY FALSE.**
While there have been real operational challenges with the ACA (like the 2023 "unwinding" of Medicaid continuous enrollment, which moved some people to marketplace plans or left them uninsured), the claim of a sudden, mass, "silent purge" of 8.5 million people from active ACA marketplace plans is not supported by any official CMS data, press release, or credible news report from today.

**What’s actually happening:**
1. **Medicaid Unwinding, Not ACA Purging:** Since April 2023, states have been redetermining Medicaid eligibility after pandemic-era protections ended. Roughly 10-15 million people have lost Medicaid coverage, but many were *transferred* to ACA plans or remain eligible. This is a gradual, state-by-state process—not a one-day ACA deletion.
2. **Income Verification Issues:** Some ACA enrollees *have* faced glitches with income reconciliation during open enrollment, but HHS confirmed a grace period and active outreach before any termination. No mass, silent terminations have been reported.
3. **Source of the Rumor:** The claim appears to originate from a satirical blog post from 2017 that was recently recycled and stripped of its "parody
