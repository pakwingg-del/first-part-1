**Texas AG Ken Paxton says local noise ordinances are “unconstitutional” if they stop people from honking in support of Trump.**

DENTON, TX — In a fiery new opinion letter, Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton fired off a warning to local municipalities on Tuesday, declaring that any city ordinance that bans or fines drivers for honking in political support is a “direct violation of the First Amendment.”

“This is just common sense,” Paxton wrote. “We cannot have local bureaucrats using noise complaints to silence political expression. If you can honk for a local barbecue joint, you can honk for the President.”

The letter came in response to an incident in Denton, where a driver was cited $175 for “excessive horn use” while participating in a “Trump Train” rally through downtown. The driver, local HVAC technician Mike Roybal, told the Fort Worth Star-Telegram he was “just showing support” and that the police were “playing politics.”

Paxton’s office stopped short of issuing a formal injunction but stated that any ticket issued “solely for political honking” should be dismissed and that offending officers may be subject to civil liability.

Not everyone is honking with joy. The Denton City Council, which passed the noise ordinance last year citing an increase in “aggressive road rage honking,” fired back Wednesday morning.

“We didn’t ban free speech; we banned drivers from using their vehicles as noisemakers at 2 a.m. outside a library,” said Councilwoman Lisa Hernandez in a statement. “If Paxton wants to talk about common sense, he should tell his supporters to respect quiet hours like the rest of us.”

The Attorney General’s office has not yet responded to that comment.
