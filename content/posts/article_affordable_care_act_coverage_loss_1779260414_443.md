**HEADLINE: ACA Enrollment Plummets: 15M Americans Lose Subsidies After Expiration of Enhanced Tax Credits**

**The Situation:**
The Affordable Care Act marketplace is facing its largest coverage disruption since launch. Enhanced premium tax credits—enacted under the American Rescue Plan and extended through 2025—expired on December 31. Without Congressional action, an estimated 15 million Americans will face average premium increases of 70% to 90% in 2026.

**By the Numbers:**
- 15 million: Estimated enrollees losing premium subsidies
- ~$2,500/year: Average subsidy lost per household
- 4 states: Projected to see more than 30% of marketplace enrollees become uninsured (Texas, Florida, Georgia, North Carolina)

**The Business Impact:**
- **Insurers:** Expect disproportionate risk pool deterioration. Younger, healthier subscribers will drop coverage first, driving up claims costs for remaining members. Expect 2026 rate filings to spike 15–25% above medical trend.
- **Hospitals & Providers:** Uncompensated care costs will rise sharply in states that did not expand Medicaid. Emergency department utilization will increase as the newly uninsured delay primary care.
- **Employers:** No direct mandate impact, but expect talent mobility disruption. Employees with chronic conditions in non-expansion states may face coverage gaps during job transitions.

**Strategic Takeaway:**
This is a top-5 operational risk for health insurers in 2026. The tail is fast—rate filings for 2026 products are due Q2 2025. Immediate action: model lapse assumptions conservatively; consider product redesign for subsidy-eligible populations. political solution remains unlikely before midterms.
