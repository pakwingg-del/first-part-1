**Local Man "Just Asking Questions" About Why Counting Ballots By Candlelight Isn't "Common Sense"**

🚨 **GEORGIA ELECTION UPDATE** 🚨

**ATLANTA, GA** – As the dust settles on another nail-biter election in Georgia, local resident and self-proclaimed "common sense" enthusiast Kevin Miller, 47, took to the "Gwinnett County Moms & Dads Unite" Facebook group to express his very reasonable concerns.

"Look, I'm not a conspiracy theorist, but can we all agree that basic common sense is dead?" Miller wrote in a now-viral post. "We have billions of dollars for Ukraine, but we can't afford enough lightbulbs and working voting machines to count ballots before midnight? My grandfather fought in WWII for 'see something, say something,' not 'count something in the dark until 3 AM.'"

The post, which has been shared over 1,200 times, goes on to demand that all future elections be "held on a Tuesday in June, in a well-lit Waffle House, with a hand count overseen by three off-duty sheriffs and the cashier from the 7-Eleven." Miller insists this is the only "logical, commonsense" solution.

Commenters quickly rallied. "PREACH! Common sense isn't so common anymore," wrote one user. Another added, "Why can't we just vote on paper like my grandma's church potluck voting? It worked for the best casserole."

Miller concluded his rant with a plea: "I'm just asking questions. And why are we letting computers decide our leaders? My 2003 Dodge Ram doesn't have a computer problem, and it still runs fine."
