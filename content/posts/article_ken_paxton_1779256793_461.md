**KEN PAXTON RUSHES TO DALLAS TO “RESTORE ORDER” AFTER MYSTERIOUS “GHOST SHUTDOWN” – City Hall Gone Dark, Officials Silent**

**Rating: PARTIALLY TRUE / MISLEADING CONTEXT**

**The Viral Claim:** A widely-shared social media post alleges that Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton personally led a tactical operation in Dallas this morning after the city’s municipal government suddenly went “off the grid.” The post claims City Hall was locked down, all official communications ceased for three hours, and Paxton arrived with a “rapid response legal team” to investigate what he called an “unauthorized, unconstitutional shutdown” by rogue city employees.

**What’s Actually Happening:**
✅ **REAL:** Ken Paxton’s office did issue a press release today stating he is deploying a team to Dallas to investigate complaints of **voter roll irregularities** and potential “ghost voting” in the upcoming May elections. He specifically cited a concern over non-citizen registration attempts. Paxton made a brief, unannounced appearance outside the Dallas County Elections office at 10:15 AM.

❌ **FAKE:** The claim that City Hall “went dark” or that there was a “ghost shutdown” is **completely fabricated**. Dallas City Hall remained open and fully operational. The 3-hour “silence” in communications was actually a scheduled internal server upgrade for the city’s email system, which caused a minor, routine outage of the public-facing press release page. No phones were down. No doors were locked.

⚠️ **MISLEADING:** The viral photo circulating shows Paxton standing in front of a blank digital sign. The photo is real—but the sign was simply being serviced by a city technician. Paxton was there for the voter roll issue, not a “city-wide shutdown.”
