**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About Schlitz Beer Being Discontinued**

- **The "Beer That Made Milwaukee Famous" is officially gone** – After a long struggle to reclaim its glory days, the iconic Schlitz brand has been quietly discontinued by current owner Pabst Brewing Company, ending a legacy that began in 1849.

- **A tragic fall from #1** – Schlitz was once America's best-selling beer in the 1950s and '60s, but a disastrous recipe change in the 1970s (designed to speed up brewing) ruined the taste and created a "skunky" reputation that drove drinkers to Budweiser and Miller. It never recovered.

- **Nostalgia couldn't save it** – Despite a 2010s revival attempt with the "Old Style" recipe, younger drinkers never embraced the brand. Sales plummeted to less than 1% of its peak, making it unsustainable for Pabst to keep the dusty cans on shelves.

- **Collectors are going wild** – Empty Schlitz cans and vintage signs are selling for triple the price on eBay and Mercari. A full, unopened 6-pack of pre-1970s Schlitz recently went for $250. If you have a case in your basement, you may be sitting on a goldmine.

- **Final verdict: A cautionary tale** – Industry experts say Schlitz's death is a textbook case of how one wrong business decision can kill a 150-year-old brand. It’s not just about beer—any brand that betrays its loyal core will lose its soul—and eventually, its life.
