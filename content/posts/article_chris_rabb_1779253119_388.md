**BREAKING NEWS**

**DATELINE:** Washington, D.C. – April 9, 2025 (12:30 PM EST)

**SUBJECT:** New Developments in the Chris Rabb Inquiry

**LOCATION:** United States Capitol, Washington, D.C.

**WHO:** State Representative Chris Rabb (D-Pennsylvania) and the Pennsylvania House of Representatives.

**WHAT:** Representative Chris Rabb has formally introduced a new legislative package known as the "Transparency in Digital Lending Act." The proposed legislation aims to impose strict disclosure requirements on financial technology companies and alternative lenders operating within Pennsylvania. The bills specifically target the use of algorithmic credit scoring and short-term loan products, which Rabb argues disproportionately affect low-income and minority communities.

**WHEN:** The legislation was officially tabled during a scheduled session of the Pennsylvania House on the morning of April 9, 2025, at 10:15 AM EST.

**WHERE:** The announcement was made on the floor of the Pennsylvania House of Representatives in Harrisburg, followed by a press conference in Room 140 of the Main Capitol Building.

**WHY:** Representative Rabb cited a recent increase in consumer complaints regarding hidden fees and predatory lending practices by digital platforms. He stated the bills are necessary to "ensure that technological advancement in lending does not come at the expense of fair access and financial security for Pennsylvania families."

**AUTHORITY:** The full text of the “Transparency in Digital Lending Act” (House Bills 4521 and 4522) is now available for public review via the Pennsylvania General Assembly portal. The bills have been referred to the House Commerce Committee for initial review.
