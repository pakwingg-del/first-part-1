**Headline: Mark Cuban Drops a Money Bomb: “Stop Buying These 3 Things If You Want To Get Rich”**

In a new interview that’s sending shockwaves through personal finance circles, billionaire “Shark Tank” star Mark Cuban just gave the middle class a brutally honest wake-up call. His blunt advice? If you’re buying extended warranties, leasing a new car, or paying for premium cable packages, you’re bleeding cash—and keeping yourself broke.

“The average American is paying for the *fear* of a broken gadget, not the fix,” Cuban told a crowd of entrepreneurs. He slammed extended warranties as a “pure profit center for billion-dollar companies,” noting that most credit cards already double your manufacturer’s warranty for free. On cars, he warned that leasing is a “wealth killer” disguised as a lower monthly payment. “You’re paying for the depreciation of an asset you don’t even own. It’s rent—on something that loses value.”

But he saved his harshest words for cable TV. Cuban, who once owned a major stake in a cable network, admitted the industry is a racket. “If you’re paying $200 a month for channels you watch maybe five shows on, you’re funding your own financial stagnation. That’s $2,400 a year. Invest that for a decade, and you’re looking at serious money.”

The viral takeaway? The path to financial freedom isn’t about earning more. It’s about stopping the leaks. Consumers are already flooding social media with screenshots of canceled subscriptions and canceled car leases. The question isn’t *if* you should do it—it’s whether you can afford *not* to.
