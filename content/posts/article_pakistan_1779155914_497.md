**Consumer Alert: “P’Hire Crisis” Will Hit Your Wallet This Month — Why You’re Suddenly Paying More For Essentials**

**Breaking**: Forget inflation. Pakistan’s new “consumer tax” is whether you find ANY spare change left.

The trend: **The 80-20 Split is Dead.** A viral TikTik chart shows that since the February fuel price adjustment, 80% of your monthly salary now goes to *actual survival* (bread, power, water, transport) — leaving just 20% for everything else. But this week, that 20% is being *wiped out* by a sudden, silent **“P’Hire Crisis.”**

**Why Your Wallet is Bleeding:**
- Every household task (fixing a leaky pipe, wiring a light, painting a wall) now carries a **“survival surcharge.”** Local mechanics and electricians have started charging a *flat minimum fee* of PKR 800 just to *show up* — because their own costs (riding rickshaws, buying a chai, staying afloat) have tripled.
- **The result**: You’re paying more for *nothing*. A simple 10-minute light bulb replacement now costs PKR 1,200 (including the “show up” fee plus markup on a bulb that costs PKR 70 at market).
- **Worst part**: It’s *legal* but invisible. These service providers are now “the new landlords” of your daily life — and they don’t give receipts.

**How This Ruins Your Month:**
1. **Hidden Budget Blowout**: That “spare PKR 5,000” you thought you had? Gone by Monday if one appliance breaks.
2. **The “No Cash” Trap**: As ATMs run dry faster this week, these professionals demand cash *upfront* — so your digital wallet is useless.
