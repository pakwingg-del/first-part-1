**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**WASHINGTON, D.C. — U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) issued a formal travel advisory Wednesday, warning of significant delays and heightened security protocols at all ports of entry ahead of the Memorial Day holiday weekend.**

**Who:** The U.S. Customs and Border Protection agency, in coordination with the Department of Homeland Security.

**What:** An official travel warning citing "exponentially increased wait times" and a "zero-tolerance posture" for prohibited agricultural items, undeclared goods, and non-compliant travel documentation.

**When:** Effective from the evening of Thursday, May 22, through the conclusion of the Memorial Day federal holiday on Monday, May 26.

**Where:** All international land borders, including pedestrian and vehicle lanes at the northern and southern borders, as well as all international airports and seaports nationwide.

**Why:** The advisory states the measures are necessary to manage an anticipated surge of more than 2.5 million travelers crossing into the United States. CBP Commissioner Troy A. Miller stated the agency is prioritizing "operational security and traveler safety" during the "historically busiest travel period of the calendar year."

**Key Details:**

- Maximum staffing levels will be deployed, but officials warn against "complacency" regarding travel times.
- Travelers are strongly advised to enroll in biometric entry programs, such as Global Entry or SENTRI, to expedite processing.
- A strict enforcement policy will be in effect for agricultural products; CBP agriculture specialists will conduct 100% inspections on select cargo and passenger vehicles.
- Travelers failing to declare cash or monetary instruments exceeding $10,000 face immediate seizure and potential civil penalties.
- The advisory concludes with a direct order: "Plan for extended delays. Prepare your documents. Declare all items. Non-compliance will result in significant penalties and denial of entry."
