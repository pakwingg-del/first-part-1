**BREAKING NEWS UPDATE**

**LOUISVILLE, KY – November 6, 2024 (AP)** – Congressman Thomas Massie has secured re-election to the United States House of Representatives for Kentucky’s 4th Congressional District, according to official returns released by the Kentucky State Board of Elections.

**WHAT:** The incumbent Republican representative defeated Democratic challenger Calvin A. Jones in the general election. Final unofficial tallies show Massie receiving 72.4 percent of the vote, compared to Jones’ 27.6 percent, with 98 percent of precincts reporting.

**WHO:** Thomas Massie, a five-term incumbent known for his libertarian-leaning fiscal conservatism and independent voting record, will serve a sixth term representing the district that encompasses 11 counties in northern Kentucky.

**WHERE:** The 4th District includes all or parts of Boone, Campbell, Kenton, Grant, Pendleton, Gallatin, Carroll, Trimble, Oldham, Henry, and Shelby counties.

**WHEN:** The election was held on Tuesday, November 5, 2024. Massie delivered his victory speech at 10:15 p.m. Eastern Time from his campaign headquarters in Villa Hills.

**WHY:** Political analysts attribute Massie’s decisive margin to strong name recognition, a favorable district map, and a campaign platform emphasizing reduced federal spending, Second Amendment protections, and opposition to foreign aid packages. Jones, a first-time candidate, was unable to overcome the district’s registered Republican majority of 58 percent.

**NEXT STEPS:** Massie is expected to be sworn in for his new term on January 3, 2025. In a brief statement following the announcement, Massie thanked constituents for their trust and reiterated his commitment to auditing the Federal Reserve and opposing legislation he deems unconstitutional. Democratic Party officials have not yet commented on the results.
