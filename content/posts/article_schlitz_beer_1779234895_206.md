**MAN, I CAN'T BELIEVE WHAT THEY'VE DONE TO THE SCHLITZ**

I live two blocks from the old Milwaukee brewery on the south side. Grandpa worked there for 38 years. That smell—stale barley and yeast—was the smell of a paycheck. It was the smell of a Friday night.

Now? They're tearing down the grain silos to build "luxury lofts" for people who drink IPAs that taste like a pine tree exploded.

But the real kicker? I saw a can of the "new" Schlitz at the gas station yesterday. It's not even beer anymore. It's a "Hard Seltzer." Schlitz. Hard. Seltzer.

**Common sense:** You don't fix a legend. You don't take a beer that won two World Wars and turn it into La Croix for people in bike shorts.

My nephew bought a 12-pack of the old stuff last week. Ten cans. Drank one. Said it "tasted like a melted penny." Left the rest in my fridge.

You know what? You leave a genuine Schlitz in a fridge for six months? It still tastes like victory.

But these suits in boardrooms? They wouldn't know common sense if it hit them in the face with a glass bottle.

RIP, Milwaukee's Finest. You were too honest for this generation.
