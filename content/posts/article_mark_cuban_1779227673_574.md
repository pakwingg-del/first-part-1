**"MARK CUBAN SAYS CRITICAL THINKING IS 'MORE IMPORTANT THAN PATRIOTISM' — MORAL CRITICS SAY WE'VE LOST OUR SOULS"**

In a fiery town hall exchange, billionaire entrepreneur Mark Cuban declared that "teaching kids to question authority is more important than teaching them to stand for the anthem," claiming blind patriotism leads to "obedience over ethics."

But moral critics are sounding the alarm. "This is the final nail in the coffin of a shared moral fabric," says Dr. Elaine Harper, a cultural ethics scholar. "He's essentially telling a generation that loyalty, sacrifice, and national unity are optional—even suspect. We're raising children who will deconstruct every institution but build nothing. That's not progress. That's the death of civic virtue." 

The backlash is spreading: school boards are debating whether Cuban's comments signal a dangerous shift toward "moral relativism as the only absolute," while parents worry that the 'question everything' ethos erodes respect for family, faith, and flag. 

Is this the dawn of a more thoughtful society—or the unraveling of the ties that bind us?
