**EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: The Unwinding Crisis**

**Headline:** 20 Million Americans Losing ACA Coverage: The Next Corporate Headwind

**The Event:** The federal “continuous enrollment” provision has ended, triggering the largest healthcare coverage unwinding in U.S. history. An estimated 15–20 million Americans are being disenrolled from Medicaid in the next 12 months.

**Business Impact:**

- **Labor Disruption:** A sudden loss of coverage forces millions into the individual market or uninsured status. Expect increased absenteeism, reduced workforce mobility, and pressure to expand employer-sponsored insurance.
- **Healthcare Sector Volatility:** Hospitals and insurers face a payer mix shift from government to self-pay. Bad debt and uncompensated care costs are projected to rise 18-22% in 2024.
- **Consumer Spending Drag:** Households absorbing higher premiums or going uninsured will cut discretionary spending. We project a 0.3–0.5% drag on consumer spending in affected demographics.

**Strategic Imperative (Next 90 Days):**
- Audit your workforce for coverage gaps.
- Model bad-debt exposure for healthcare portfolios.
- Prepare for a “coverage cliff” in Q3 as redeterminations peak.

**Bottom Line:** This is not a political event. It is a market shift. Executives must treat the coverage unwinding as a macroeconomic headwind, not a policy debate.
