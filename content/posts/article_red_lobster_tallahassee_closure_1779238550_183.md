**🚨 COMMON SENSE CHECK: Red Lobster in Tallahassee CLOSES, and we’re supposed to be surprised?**  

Just drove past the Red Lobster on Apalachee Parkway and saw the padlocks. Place is dark. Out of business. Another one bites the dust.  

Now, I’m no economist, but let’s use some common sense: You can’t run an “all-you-can-eat” shrimp deal for $20.99 and expect to stay open. That’s not a business model, that’s a math problem where you lose money on every plate. Then you wonder why the lights are off?  

And don’t even get me started on the “cheddar bay biscuits” packed with preservatives. We’ve got perfectly good local seafood spots—Shell Oyster Bar, Harry’s—that actually know what fresh fish looks like.  

Sure, blame “corporate mismanagement” or “inflation.” But maybe, just maybe, we should’ve seen this coming when every visit felt like a gamble between decent service and a 40-minute wait for cold food.  

Common sense says: If you keep treating customers like they’re doing you a favor, that door swings both ways.
