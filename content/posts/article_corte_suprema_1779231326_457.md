**Viral News Snippet**
**Headline:** BREAKING: Supreme Court Rules "Silence is Not Consent" — But Not the Way You Think
**Source:** TheLastFactCheck.com

**The Rumor:**
Social media exploded this morning with claims that the **U.S. Supreme Court** had issued a landmark 9-0 ruling stating that "silence in the face of an accusation automatically implies guilt and waives your right to remain silent." Thousands of shares and angry comments flooded X (formerly Twitter), with users declaring the Fifth Amendment "dead."

**The Reality (Fact-Check): False.**
This is a classic **misattribution hoax**. The viral quote originates from a fictionalized TV drama script (the finale of a legal thriller on Netflix) and was paired with an AI-generated image of the Court building and a fake docket number.

- **The Truth:** The Supreme Court has issued no such ruling. The Fifth Amendment remains intact. If you watch the video clips being shared, the "Justice" speaking is actually an actor in a robe.
- **The Danger:** This rumor is designed to erode public trust in the judiciary by making people believe their constitutional rights have been erased overnight.

**Verdict:** 🚫 **ALERT: FALSE.** Scroll past this one. The real Supreme Court did not take away your right to remain silent. That is a core protection that has not changed.
