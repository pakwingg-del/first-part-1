**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About CBP’s Memorial Day Travel Warning**

- **Record-Breaking Rush:** CBP is bracing for the highest Memorial Day weekend traffic in years, projecting over 3 million travelers at ports of entry. Wait times could exceed 3 hours at peak hours, especially at major crossings like San Ysidro, El Paso, and Buffalo.
- **Zero Tolerance for Backseat Balloons:** Border officers are cracking down on prohibited agricultural items—specifically, helium balloons and decorative confetti—which can be mistaken for contraband by K-9 units. Expect random secondary inspections for any celebratory decorations.
- **New "Fast Lane" Mandate:** Travelers with Global Entry or SENTRI must have a **physical card** (not just mobile app) to use dedicated lanes this year. CBP warns that digital-only credentials will result in immediate diversion to regular lines, adding 45+ minutes to your trip.
- **"Digital Re-entry" Scrutiny:** For the first time, CBP agents will randomly check social media and messaging apps at secondary inspection. If you’ve posted about "border runs" or "importing festival gear," expect extended questioning. Tip: Clear your browser history *before* crossing.
- **Friday 4 PM Curfew:** CBP is recommending travelers avoid crossing between **4:00 PM and 9:00 PM on Friday**, historically the peak surge. Instead, schedule crossings for early morning (6–9 AM) or after 10 PM to bypass the largest crowds.
