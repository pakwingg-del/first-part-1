**Subject:** Dunkin’s Breaks Marketing Mold: Free Coffee on May 19 – No Purchase Necessary

**The Headline:** *Dunkin’ bets on pure customer acquisition, not upsells.*

**The Viral Snippet:**

Forget "Buy one, get one." On May 19, Dunkin’ is offering a medium hot or iced coffee **completely free** — no purchase required. No app download. No loyalty card scan. Just walk in, get the coffee, leave.

**Why this matters to you (the CEO):**

It’s a bold, zero-friction acquisition play in a market saturated with conditional offers. By removing all barriers, Dunkin’ converts a one-day giveaway into high-velocity sampling, likely aimed at luring cold customers away from Starbucks and McDonald’s during a low-traffic window (post-Mother’s Day).

**The business takeaway:** This isn’t a charity play. It’s a high-leverage, short-burst strategy that prioritizes *share of mind and foot traffic* over immediate margin. Expect heavy upselling via point-of-sale signage, but the absence of a hard gate suggests the real KPI is new-store visits, not average ticket.

**Risk:** Dilution of brand value if executed without strong take-home or conversion follow-up. **Reward:** One of the lowest CPAs in quick-service history if even 10% of recipients convert to repeat buyers.

**Verdict:** Smart, aggressive, and deceptively simple. This will set a pricing precedent others will be forced to match — or lose share.
