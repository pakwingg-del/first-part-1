**The Fine Print He Forgot to Read: Mitch McConnell’s $230 Million Mistake That’s About to Hit Your Wallet**

When Senator Mitch McConnell recently warned that the U.S. could default on its debt under a potential Harris administration, he probably didn’t expect the internet to do its homework.

Turns out, buried in the bipartisan budget deal McConnell helped craft in 2023 is a ticking time bomb: an automatic 4% across-the-board spending cut—dubbed “McCut”—that will activate the very moment the next President takes office. The Congressional Budget Office (CBO) now projects this will slash $230 million in critical consumer protections, food safety inspections, and the IRS’s ability to refund your taxes on time.

So, what does this mean for you?

• **Slower refunds.** The IRS is already struggling. If this cut kicks in, expect your 2025 tax refund to be held hostage until summer.
• **Higher grocery bills.** Fewer USDA inspectors means more recalls—and more expensive imports when domestic safety lapses happen.
• **Your credit score could take a hit.** The cut targets the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau’s funding for error correction—meaning billing mistakes on your mortgage or student loans might go unchallenged for months.

The irony? McConnell himself voted for the very mechanism that triggers these cuts. While he points fingers at hypothetical future leaders, your real-world finances are about to take the hit *because of a deadline he helped write into law.*

The clock is ticking. And the price tag? It’s coming out of your pocket.
