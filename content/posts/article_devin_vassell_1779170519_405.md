**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About Devin Vassell’s Resurgence with the Spurs**

After a slow start following his return from a foot injury, he is now averaging a career-high 2.8 steals per game over his last 10 outings while shooting 40% from three. He’s leading the league in deflections during that stretch.
- **He’s the Closer Wemby Needs:** While Victor Wembanyama is the unstoppable force, Vassell has quietly become San Antonio’s go-to isolation scorer in the clutch. His mid-range pull-up jumper—the "Vassell Special"—is the Spurs' most reliable bail-out when the shot clock is winding down.
- **The "Off-Ball" Threat is Breaking Defenses:** Teams are now sending two defenders at Wembanyama, leaving Vassell as the primary release valve. His elite ability to relocate off screens and catch-and-shoot in motion has spiked the Spurs’ offensive rating by 12 points when he is on the floor.
- **He’s Becoming a Vocal Leader:** The quiet kid from Florida State is now the loudest voice in the huddle. With the Spurs rebuilding their culture, Vassell has taken ownership of defensive assignments and is regularly calling out rotations—a massive leap from his previous "lead by example" approach.
- **The Fourth-Year Leap is Real for a "Late Bloomer":** Drafted 11th overall in 2020, Vassell is proving that development timelines aren't linear. His player efficiency rating (PER) has jumped from 16.2 to 21.4 this season, a mark that puts him in the same statistical tier as rising stars like Jalen Brunson and Tyrese Maxey in their breakout years.
