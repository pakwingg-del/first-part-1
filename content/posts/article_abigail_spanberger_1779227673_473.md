**Fact Check: Viral Rumor Claims Rep. Abigail Spanberger Left Congress to "Profit Off Parental Rights" – Here’s the Truth**

**The Rumor:** A viral post circulating on X and Facebook claims that outgoing Virginia Congresswoman Abigail Spanberger (D-VA) is "abandoning her district early" to join a "highly paid D.C. lobbying firm focused on undermining parental rights in schools." The post includes a fabricated quote: *"I’ve done my time in a dead-end job; now it’s time to make real money defending woke agendas."*

**Verdict: FALSE.**

**The Facts:**
1. **She is not leaving Congress early.** Spanberger has repeatedly confirmed she will serve out her full term in the U.S. House, which ends in January 2025. She announced in November 2023 that she would not seek re-election to the House in 2024 because she is running for Governor of Virginia in the 2025 election.
2. **No lobbying job exists.** There is zero record of Spanberger taking a lobbying job. A search of public disclosures, FEC filings, and statements from her office shows she is actively campaigning for Governor. Her campaign has raised over $5 million to date.
3. **The quote is fabricated.** The quote attributed to her does not appear in any transcript, interview, or press release. Her office has denied the statement, calling it “a transparent smear designed to distract from her gubernatorial campaign.”

**The Origin:** The rumor appears to have originated from a parody account that was later reshared by bad actors. It gained traction after being picked up by a hyper-partisan blog that often publishes hoaxes as news.

**Bottom line:** Don’t fall for it. Rep. Spanberger is still in office, serving her district, and actively running for Virginia Governor. No lobbying gig. No early exit. Just a standard political campaign.
