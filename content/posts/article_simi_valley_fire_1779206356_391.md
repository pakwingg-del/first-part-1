**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Simi Valley Fire**

🔥 **It’s Fueled by a Rare "Santa Ana on Steroids"**
The blaze is being driven by unprecedented 80-100 mph wind gusts—combining dry Santa Ana conditions with a high-pressure jet stream effect. This is creating "fire tornado" risks and making aerial suppression nearly impossible until winds subside.

🚁 **Mandatory Evacuations for 12,000+ Residents**
Authorities have declared a Local Emergency, ordering evacuations for everything north of the 118 Freeway between Kuehner Drive and First Street. The Rancho Simi Recreation and Park District has opened a shelter at the Simi Valley Senior Center.

🛑 **Critical Infrastructure at High Risk**
The fire is currently threatening the key communication towers atop Rocky Peak, as well as the Santa Susana Field Laboratory—the former nuclear research site. Officials are actively sandbagging and clearing firebreaks to prevent toxic material exposure.

📸 **Viral "Wall of Fire" Footage From High School**
Social media is exploding with drone and cell phone footage from Royal High School, showing flames climbing a canyon wall in under 8 minutes. One clip has already surpassed 2 million views, with witnesses describing the sound as a "freight train of fire."

⏰ **Air Quality Emergency & Health Warning**
The smoke plume is drifting toward Thousand Oaks and Ventura. The AQI in affected zones has hit 488 (Hazardous). Health officials are urging everyone within 10 miles to wear N95 masks, seal windows, and avoid outdoor activity as particulate matter spikes.
