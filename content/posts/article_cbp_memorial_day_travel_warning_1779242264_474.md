**CLAIM:** U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) has issued a nationwide "Level 4: Do Not Travel" warning for all major U.S. highways and airports for Memorial Day weekend, citing "imminent terror threats" and "overwhelming gridlock" at ports of entry.

**VERDICT: ⚠️ MISLEADING**

- **The Reality:** CBP did issue a travel advisory for Memorial Day weekend, but it was a standard, routine operational warning about *increased wait times* at land borders and airports due to the holiday surge. The agency specifically urged travelers to have their documents ready and use apps like "CBP One" for faster processing. No "Level 4" threat classification exists in CBP’s public messaging system.
- **The Origin:** The viral warning appears to originate from a satirical website that fabricated a "leaked memo" claiming CBP was warning of "coordinated attacks" on interstate rest stops. This was picked up by a clickbait news aggregator that stripped the satire label.
- **The Verdict:** **FAKE.** Do not change your Memorial Day plans based on this. The only real warning from CBP is to plan for heavy traffic. The rest is misinformation designed to cause panic.
