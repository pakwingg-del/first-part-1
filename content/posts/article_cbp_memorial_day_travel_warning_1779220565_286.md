**BREAKING: CBP Issues ‘Memorial Day Matrix Alert’ After Officers Spot Same SUV at 7 Different Border Crossings Simultaneously**

**TUCSON, AZ** – U.S. Customs and Border Protection has issued an urgent travel advisory for Memorial Day weekend, but it’s not about wait times or bridge closures.

Sources confirm that internal CBP systems flagged a “statistical anomaly” early Monday morning: a single, silver 2018 Ford Explorer with Texas plates was simultaneously scanned entering the U.S. at seven different ports of entry—from San Diego to Brownsville. Identical driver, identical passenger, identical bumper sticker reading “Lake Tahoe or Bust.”

“We thought it was a database glitch. Then we looked at the biometric data,” a senior CBP analyst told reporters under condition of anonymity. “The facial recognition matched at every location. We’re not saying it’s a glitch in the matrix, but we’re not *not* saying it.”

The agency has now activated an emergency “Temporal Overlap Protocol,” typically reserved for major system failures. Travelers are advised that if you see the same Ford Explorer with the “Lake Tahoe or Bust” sticker in the parking lot of a rest stop, a gas station, and your own rearview mirror simultaneously—do not approach. Report it to CBP’s new “Anomaly Response Hotline.”

When reached for comment, the man on the Explorer’s registration denied all knowledge, stating he was “just trying to beat the holiday traffic.”

The warning is in effect through Monday midnight, or until the pattern stops repeating.
