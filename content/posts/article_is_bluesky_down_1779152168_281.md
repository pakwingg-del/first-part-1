**Snippet: “Glitch in the Sky: BlueSky Users Report ‘Ghost Scroll’ as Platform Appears to Self-Destruct—Then Snaps Back Exactly 47 Minutes Later”**

In what users are calling “the most on-brand outage yet,” BlueSky experienced a bizarre 47-minute glitch this morning that left tech analysts scratching their heads. At exactly 8:14 AM GMT, the decentralized social network began displaying posts from the future—timestamps from 9:01 AM, hours before they were written. Then, the feed suddenly reversed, scrolling backwards through deleted drafts and forgotten replies. One user reported seeing a message they had typed and deleted *five years ago on a different platform*.

The matrix-level anomaly ends with an even weirder coincidence: when service returned at 9:01 AM, every single user who had been active during the glitch found their profile bio replaced with the same cryptic string: “IP: 47.0.0.1.”

BlueSky has not commented, but one developer noted that 47 minutes is exactly the time it takes light from the Sun to reach Jupiter—and that BlueSky’s servers are allegedly hosted in an old bunker that was once a SETI listening post.
