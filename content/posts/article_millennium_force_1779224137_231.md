**Headline:** Millennium Force Track Found on Facebook Marketplace; Buyer Says It’s ‘Perfect for a Backyard Zipline’

**Sandusky, OH** — In a move that has left coaster enthusiasts both horrified and cackling with glee, a full 400-foot section of steel track from Cedar Point’s legendary *Millennium Force* has allegedly appeared for sale on Facebook Marketplace. The listing, posted by a user named “Craig ‘The Scrapper’ Jenkins,” features a grainy photo of the iconic yellow rail propped against a rusted minivan. The description reads: “Great condition. Some scratches. Used for fast boi rides. $400 OBO. You haul. No lowballers, I know what I have.”

The irony is as steep as the coaster’s first drop. Millennium Force—the trailblazing giga-coaster that shattered records in 2000—is now being hawked alongside a broken treadmill and a collection of beanie babies. Commenters on the subreddit r/rollercoasterjerk were quick to roast the deal, pointing out that the “track” is likely a piece of decorative fence or a discarded handrail from a local skate park.

“We spent years trying to avoid the rattle in the back row, and now this guy wants me to haul it home in a pickup truck?” wrote user *DropZoneDad69*. “I’ll wait for the Top Thrill Dragster launch cable to show up on OfferUp.”

Cedar Point has yet to comment, but a park spokesperson was reportedly seen muttering, “For $400, it’s definitely a steal—just don't expect the chain lift to be included.”
