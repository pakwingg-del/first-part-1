**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About Flash Flood Warnings**

Here is a viral news snippet on the current Flash Flood Warning.

🚨 **BREAKING: "Turn Around, Don't Drown" is NOT a Suggestion**
A Flash Flood Warning is currently in effect for [Insert County/City Here] until [Insert Time]. This is the most urgent of flood alerts—it means flooding is *imminent or already happening*. Here are the five critical details you need to survive this rapid weather event.

1.  **The "Wall of Water" Factor:** This isn't a slow river rise. We are talking about a sudden, violent surge of water that can turn a dry street into a raging river in under 60 seconds. Six inches of moving water can knock you off your feet. One foot can sweep away your car.

2.  **Meteorologist Phrase: "Training Thunderstorms."** The term "training" refers to a series of powerful storms moving over the same exact spot like boxcars on a track. This "stall" is what causes the catastrophic, unexpected deluge. *If you hear this on the weather radio, the risk just doubled.*

3.  **The Highway is the Most Dangerous Place:** **Rule 10/10:** Any vehicle, including an SUV, is almost completely invisible in 10 inches of water. **Rule 1/3:** Watch for water on the road at 1/3 of a mile away. If you can't see the pavement, **Do NOT enter**. Most flood deaths happen in cars, and it only takes 12 inches to float a compact car.

4.  **"Watch the Basement, Check the Overpass":** The most vulnerable spots right now are:
    - **Underground areas:** Basements and underpasses become death traps in seconds.
