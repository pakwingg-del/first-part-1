**BREAKING NEWS – 5:00 PM ET**

**ATLANTA** – In a landmark proceeding that legal experts are calling a “referendum on judicial independence,” the Georgia Supreme Court election for the Division 6 seat has concluded with a decisive result.

**WHO:** Andrew Pinson, the incumbent justice appointed by Governor Brian Kemp, has retained his seat on the state’s highest court.

**WHAT:** The election, officially nonpartisan but widely viewed through a partisan lens, was the most expensive judicial race in Georgia history. Pinson defeated challenger John Barrow, a former Democratic U.S. Representative.

**WHEN:** The race officially concluded Tuesday evening, with Pinson securing approximately 53.4% of the vote against Barrow’s 46.6%.

**WHERE:** The results were certified by the Georgia Secretary of State’s office, with precincts reporting across all 159 counties.

**WHY:** The election has drawn national attention due to its implications for abortion access and voting rights. Justice Pinson was part of the majority that upheld Georgia’s six-week abortion ban in 2022. His victory is being characterized by analysts as a consolidation of conservative jurisprudence on the bench, while opponents framed it as a loss for the political left’s strategy of using state supreme court races to influence social policy.

Justice Pinson is scheduled to swear in for a full six-year term next week.
