**Viral News Snippet: "Andrej Karpathy's 'AI Farming' Bombshell: He Says GPT-6 Will Be 'Grown, Not Coded' — and That We're Already Too Late?"**

**Status:** Partially Real / Mostly Misinterpreted

**Real:** Former OpenAI and Tesla AI leader Andrej Karpathy recently gave a private lecture at Stanford where he expanded on his "Software 2.0" theory. He did argue that future AI models (post-GPT-4) will require less manual coding and more "data engineering" and "compute farming" — essentially, growing neural networks like crops rather than building them like bridges.

**Fake or Exaggerated:** The viral clips circulating on TikTok and X (formerly Twitter) claim Karpathy said:
> *"GPT-6 will be planted like a seed, and we're already fighting the last war by teaching humans to code."*

This specific quote **does not exist** in any verified recording. Karpathy did reference the concept of "computer" (computational agriculture) and stated that **"we are underestimating how much the bottleneck is data, not code,"** but he did not predict a specific model name (GPT-6) or claim "we're already too late." The dramatic language appears to be stitched together from a 2022 tweet about "Software 2.0" and a 2024 throwaway comment about AI scaling laws.

**Verdict:** The snippet is **misleading** — the core idea (that future AI will be grown, not hand-coded) is a real argument he has made, but the specific "GPT-6 planting like a seed" quote and the "too late" panic are fabricated for clicks.
