**🚨 FAKE ALERT: “Massive 5-Alarm Fire Engulfs Downtown – Evacuate Immediately!” 🚨**

**The viral news snippet:** *“BREAKING: Unconfirmed reports of a massive 5-alarm fire consuming multiple blocks downtown. Police urge immediate evacuation. Residents are sharing frantic videos of flames visible from miles away. Stay tuned for updates.”*

*Real vs. Fake:* **FAKE**

This snippet is a classic **crisis hoax** designed to create panic. Here’s why:

1. **No official source:** Real breaking news always cites a fire department or emergency management agency. (Fake)  
2. **Vague location:** “Downtown” with no street name, landmark, or city. (Fake)  
3. **Frantic video claims:** Hoaxes often use old footage from different disasters (e.g., a 2018 California wildfire) and re-share it as “current.” (Fake)  
4. **No first responder info:** No fire chief quote, dispatch scanner audio, or traffic alerts. (Fake)  
5. **Logical inconsistency:** “Massive 5-alarm” fires are rare and would immediately trigger official alerts (e.g., Wireless Emergency Alerts, news station confirmations). If none are published, it’s a copy‑paste hoax. (Fake)  

**What’s real?** The only verified “fire near me” right now is a *small grease fire* reported two hours ago in a single residence on Maple Street—controlled in minutes. No evacuations, no danger.

**Bottom line:** Don’t share panic posts. Check your local fire department’s verified social media or official alert app before forwarding. If it uses “BREAKING” in all caps with no source, assume it’s fake until proven otherwise.
