Alright folks, I’m just gonna say what we’re all thinking at the pump and the grocery checkout line.

Pete Hegseth rolls into *Bowling Green* today, talks about “battle rhythm” and “lethality” while standing in front of a wheat field, and I’m supposed to be impressed? Meanwhile, my wife can’t find a loaf of bread for under $4, and the kids’ school is teaching critical theory instead of cursive. Does this guy even know where Kentucky is on a map, or did his handlers just see the words “red state” and toss a dart?

The man lives in a gated community in Virginia. He has never fixed a tractor. He has never worked a double shift at the Corvette plant. But he’s here to tell *us* about “common sense” values?

Common sense says if you can’t afford to fill your truck, you don’t listen to a Fox News host about the farm economy. Common sense says if you want to win Kentucky, you show up at a truck stop, not a $5,000-a-plate donor breakfast at a horse farm.

Wake me up when he says he’ll fight for our bourbon, our coal, and our families—until then, this is just a photo op for a guy who thinks “coal country” is a fashion label.
