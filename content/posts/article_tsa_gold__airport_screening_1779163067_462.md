**Viral News Snippet:**

**BREAKING: TSA Secretly Rolling Out "Gold+" PreCheck Program for Ultra-Rich — But It's Not What You Think**

🚨 **Rumor Level:** 🔥🔥🔥🔥🔥 (Viral on X, TikTok, and fringe news sites)

**Headline:** *"TSA 'Gold+' Screening Lets Billionaires Skip Body Scanners — Is This Real or a Hoax?"*

**The Claim:** A leaked internal memo suggests the TSA is piloting a new, invite-only tier called "TSA Gold+" at major airports (JFK, LAX, DFW). The alleged program allows members to bypass all physical screening, including metal detectors and body scanners, in exchange for a "digital biometric profile" and a staggering $1,200 annual fee — plus a one-time "concierge background check" costing $5,000. Videos show private suites with leather chairs and champagne being served before passengers are fast-tracked to the plane.

**Real vs. Fake:**

✅ **REAL Elements:**
- TSA has been testing **biometric screening** (facial recognition, iris scans) at select airports since 2022 as part of the "TSA PreCheck Touchless Identity" project.
- A **"premium" tier** was tangentially discussed in DHS budget documents in 2023, but it was focused on advanced biometric enrollment, not luxury perks.
- The $1,200 figure is plausible — current PreCheck is $78/year; Clear (private biometric service) charges $189/year.

❌ **FAKE/Exaggerated:**
- **"Skipping all screening"** is false. TSA *must* maintain some physical screening for all passengers per federal law (49 CFR § 1520). The "leaked video" of champagne and private suites is stock footage from a Dubai Emirates first-class lounge, not a TSA area
