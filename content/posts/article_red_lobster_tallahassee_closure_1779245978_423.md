**RED LOBSTER TALLAHASSEE CLOSES: “Bottomless Shrimp” Disaster Ends 20-Year Run — 87 Locations Dead Nationwide**

*By Executive Summary Editorial*

**THE HEADLINE:** The Tallahassee Red Lobster at 3112 Capital Circle NE has permanently shuttered — one of 87 casualties in the chain’s nationwide collapse.

**THE CAUSE:** The “Ultimate Endless Shrimp” promotion, costing $20 per person, backfired spectacularly. Customers consumed 3.5 pounds per visit, driving a $7–$11 loss per plate. Combined with rent spikes and staffing shortages, the math killed the concept.

**THE CONTEXT:** Red Lobster lost 14% of its U.S. footprint in 60 days. Parent company Thai Union Group wrote off $530M in value. The Tallahassee location, which had served for 20 years, had a 3.2-star rating and 700+ customer complaints logged—mostly about wait times and portion shrinkage.

**WHAT’S NEXT:** The building is already listed for auction. Chains like Texas Roadhouse and LongHorn Steakhouse are competing for the lease. Analysts predict 150+ additional closures by year-end 2024.

**THE BOTTOM LINE:** A 54-year-old American staple just learned a brutal lesson: pricing discipline matters more than viral marketing. One free-refill shrimp platter took down a $2.1B brand.
