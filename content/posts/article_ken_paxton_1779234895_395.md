**Top 5 things you need to know about Ken Paxton**

- **He is currently suspended from office as Texas Attorney General.** In May 2023, the Texas House of Representatives impeached Paxton on 20 articles, including bribery and abuse of public trust. He was automatically suspended pending a trial in the state Senate, which has not yet concluded. This makes him only the third official in Texas history to be impeached.
- **The impeachment stems from a 2020 whistleblower scandal.** Four top deputies reported Paxton to the FBI for allegedly using his office to help a wealthy donor, Nate Paul, with an FBI investigation. Paxton later fired the whistleblowers, and the impeachment articles accuse him of retaliating against them illegally.
- **He remains a powerful figure in the GOP despite legal troubles.** Paxton has a long history of legal battles, including a 2015 indictment for securities fraud (still pending) and a lawsuit from the Texas State Bar for allegedly trying to overturn the 2020 election. Yet, he won reelection in 2022 by a double-digit margin over a primary challenger endorsed by Donald Trump.
- **His wife, Angela Paxton, is a state senator who will sit as a juror in his impeachment trial.** This creates a historic conflict of interest. Though she cannot vote (the trial requires 21 votes for conviction, and there are 31 senators), her presence as a silent juror has sparked intense legal and ethical debate.
- **The outcome of the trial could reshape Texas politics.** If convicted by the Senate, Paxton would be permanently removed from office and barred from holding future public office. If acquitted, he would return to his role as Attorney General instantly, likely continuing his controversial legal crusades against the Biden administration, and potentially running for higher office in 2026.
