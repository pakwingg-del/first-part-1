**Top 5 Things You Need To Know About the FAA’s Emergency DFW Ground Stop**

- **A Full Stop for All Departures:** The Federal Aviation Administration issued a nationwide Ground Stop for all aircraft destined for Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport (DFW). Flights already in the air are being held at their origin gates or diverted to alternate airports like Love Field or Austin.

- **The Suspected Culprit: Air Traffic Control Tech Failure:** Early reports indicate the halt was triggered by a critical IT failure at the Fort Worth Air Route Traffic Control Center (ZFW). Controllers lost the ability to see aircraft transponder data on their scopes, forcing the FAA to switch to a slower, paper-based backup system for safety.

- **This is NOT a Security Incident:** The FAA and TSA have not activated a security lockdown. The disruption is being categorized as a severe technology/systems outage—not a drone sighting, not a runway incursion, and not a homeland security threat.

- **Massive Cascading Delays for the Whole U.S.:** Because DFW is American Airlines’ largest hub (operating over 800 daily departures), this Ground Stop is causing a ripple effect. Planes at DFW are not turning around for their next flights. Expect delays and cancellations to expand from Texas to Chicago, Miami, and Los Angeles for the rest of the day.

- **The Holding Pen is at 35,000 Feet:** You might see a “traffic jam in the sky” on FlightRadar24. Planes already inbound are being put into massive holding patterns over West Texas, Oklahoma, and Arkansas. If you are on a plane circling above DFW right now, you are likely in a stack of aircraft waiting to land as soon as the tech is rebooted.
