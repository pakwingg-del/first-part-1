**Consumer Alert: The Price You Pay for Online “Perfection” – Maureen Galindo Trend Could Be Costing You Big**

A new viral trend is sweeping social media under the hashtag #MaureenGalindoEffect, and consumer watchdogs say it’s quietly draining your wallet. Creators are pushing followers to purchase expensive, single-use aesthetic products—from custom packaging to branded "compliment cards"—all to mimic the "effortless luxury" look popularized by influencer Maureen Galindo.

But here’s the bite: That curated, glossy image is costing the average fan at least **$85 extra per month** on items you don’t actually need.

Experts warn this "aspirational spending" is a major driver of credit card debt for Gen Z and Millennials. The trap? You’re not just buying a product; you’re buying the *feeling* of having a perfectly organized life. The FTC is now quietly investigating whether undisclosed affiliate kickbacks are fueling the trend.

**Your Wallet Move:** Before you click “buy” on that aesthetic throw pillow or set of gold-rimmed notecards, ask yourself: Is this really for *my* life, or just for the photo? The trend’s price tag is real—but the happiness it sells is often retouched.
