**BREAKING: Fox’s ‘Highway to Heaven’ Reboot Sparks Heated Debate on Divine Intervention & Self-Worth**

LOS ANGELES — In a move that’s dividing spiritual circles and self-help communities alike, Fox has officially greenlit a reboot of the classic ’80s series *Highway to Heaven*. But this time, the angelic road trip is taking a hard turn into modern therapy culture.

The new series, tentatively titled *Highway: Reborn*, will reportedly swap Jonathan Smith’s celestial mission for a team of “spiritual life coaches” who travel the country helping people overcome trauma, addiction, and loneliness—without a single flash of miraculous light. Instead of angels performing literal miracles, the protagonists will guide humans to realize their own “inner power.”

The internet is exploding. Critics are calling it “toxic positivity dressed in a trench coat,” while fans of the original series say it strips the show of its divine premise. But one life coach, Dr. Elena Reyes, sees a deeper psychological goldmine.

“The original show was about **external rescue**—someone comes down from heaven to fix you. The reboot is tapping into a huge cultural shift: people are tired of waiting for a miracle to change their lives,” says Reyes. “But there’s a dangerous flip side. If you’re told you alone are responsible for your healing, and you can’t ‘manifest’ your way out of depression or financial ruin, you internalize that as failure.

“The real ‘highway to heaven’ isn’t about either/or—it’s about learning to accept help from both the seen and the unseen without losing your sense of agency.”
