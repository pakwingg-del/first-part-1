**Breaking: Ken Paxton Files Emergency Injunction to Block "AI-Generated Evidence" in Upcoming Criminal Trial**

**Claim:** Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton has filed an emergency legal motion Tuesday, arguing that prosecutors cannot use evidence derived from a commercial AI analysis tool because the software's algorithms are "proprietary, unverifiable, and violate due process under the 14th Amendment."

**What's True:** Paxton's office did file a formal, 47-page motion in the ongoing criminal securities fraud case (State of Texas v. Paxton, Case No. 16-0948) challenging the admissibility of digital forensic evidence. The motion specifically targets data extracted from cellphones using a software program known as "Cerebral Lens 2.0" – a tool that uses machine learning to reconstruct deleted text messages and geolocation data. The defense argues that the prosecution cannot produce the source code for independent verification, making the evidence "unconstitutional hearsay."

**What's Fake:** Social media posts are claiming the motion contains the phrase "rogue algorithm run by Silicon Valley elites" and that Paxton is simultaneously suing AI companies for "election interference" in this case. These dramatic quotes are fabricated. The actual filing uses standard legal language regarding admissibility standards under *Daubert v. Merrell Dow Pharmaceuticals*.

**Bottom Line:** The motion is real, but the inflammatory quotes circulating on X (formerly Twitter) are fake. No ruling has been issued yet. Paxton is currently suspended from office pending the felony trial, which includes charges of securities fraud for allegedly misleading investors in a tech startup. The AI evidence angle is novel but a long-shot legal strategy.
