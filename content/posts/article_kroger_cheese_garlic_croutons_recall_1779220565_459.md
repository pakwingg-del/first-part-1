**Viral News Snippet: "Did Kroger Recall ALL Garlic Croutons Due to 'Exploding Cheese Pods'?"**

**The Claim:** A video is circulating on TikTok and X (formerly Twitter) claiming that Kroger has issued an "Urgent, Nationwide Recall" on its "Private Selection Garlic Cheese Croutons" because "micro-pressurized cheese powder capsules" are causing the crouton bags to spontaneously explode in pantries. The video shows a user shaking a bag that makes a "hissing" sound, claiming it's a "chemical leak."

**The Reality: ❌ FAKE (With a Grain of Truth)**

**Fact-Check:** This claim is **false** and appears to be a satirical prank video that got amplified out of context.

1.  **No Official Recall:** A search of the **FDA recall database** and **Kroger's official website** shows *zero* active or recent recalls for any Kroger-branded croutons, garlic or otherwise.
2.  **The "Cheese Pod" Myth:** There is no such thing as a "micro-pressurized cheese powder capsule" in croutons. The hissing sound in the viral video is most likely caused by air escaping the bag due to a small tear or the bag being opened and resealed incorrectly.
3.  **Mistaken Identity:** The viral video likely took a snippet of an older, unrelated recall. In **February 2024**, a separate supplier (not Kroger) recalled a *different* product (chicken salad) due to hard plastic pieces. The internet has falsely merged that story with a generic bag of croutons.
