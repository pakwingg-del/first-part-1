**FACT CHECK: Viral Claim of "Massive Voter Fraud" in Alabama Special Election is FALSE**

A viral post circulating on X/Twitter claims that "thousands of illegal ballots" were cast in today's Alabama special election for State Senate District 14, citing a "spike in turnout" in Jefferson County as proof of fraud.

**Verdict: FALSE**

Here are the verified facts:

1. **The "Spike":** The initial precinct-level data showing a 42% turnout increase over the 2022 midterms is accurate. However, this is due to a **highly competitive open-seat race** and a major **local ballot initiative** to fund a new county library system, not fraud.
2. **The Source:** The viral post originates from an account known for sharing "parody" election content and has a history of posting debunked claims. No official election observer or law enforcement agency has reported irregularities.
3. **Chain of Custody:** Alabama Secretary of State Wes Allen confirmed in a press release that all 127,000 ballots were accounted for by bipartisan poll workers. The state's new voter ID law was enforced in every precinct.
4. **The Result:** Republican candidate John Parker defeated Democrat Linda Hartwell by 4,213 votes (51.8% to 48.2%). Hartwell conceded at 9:15 PM CT, thanking poll workers for a "smooth and secure" process.

**Bottom Line:** This is a classic "false flag" disinformation narrative designed to sow doubt in a routine, well-observed election. The only "spike" today was in civic engagement.
