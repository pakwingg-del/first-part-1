Just saw the Google IO keynote. What an absolute joke.

So let me get this straight: they spent two hours showing off an AI that can *write poetry for your emails* and a pair of smart glasses that *live-translate a conversation in real-time*?

Meanwhile, my neighbor’s street hasn’t had a single pothole fixed in three years, the local library just cut its hours again because of “budget shortfalls,” and my kid’s school can’t afford new textbooks.

But hey, at least some tech bro in Mountain View can now ask his refrigerator to generate a sonnet about his leftover pizza. Where’s the *common sense*? How about an AI that tells the city council to fix the roads?

“Hey Google, patch the hole on Elm Street.” “Sorry, I can only help you schedule a reminder to *complain* about the hole on Elm Street.”

We’re paying for this progress with our taxes and our patience. Wake me up when they invent an AI that can file a noise complaint against a leaf blower at 7 AM on a Saturday. Until then, keep your digital assistants and give us back our common sense.
