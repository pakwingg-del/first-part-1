Karpathy, who left OpenAI in 2024 to “work on pure, open AI research,” has been praised as a visionary. Yet his latest pronouncement—that large language models have “transcended” the need for curated human knowledge—has raised alarm. Critics note that his current venture, a cryptic education project called “Eureka Labs,” is built on the premise that AI can replace *teachers*.

Meanwhile, as governments scramble to regulate, Karpathy’s “we don’t need human data” mantra conveniently aligns with calls to scrap transparency rules.
