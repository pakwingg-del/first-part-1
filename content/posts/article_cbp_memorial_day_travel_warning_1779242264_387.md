**CBP ISSUES MEMORIAL DAY TRAVEL ADVISORY, EXPECT RECORD BORDER VOLUME**

**WASHINGTON, D.C. (May 23, 2025)** — U.S. Customs and Border Protection is warning travelers of significant delays expected at ports of entry nationwide during the Memorial Day holiday weekend.

According to a formal statement released by the agency, "Millions of travelers are expected to cross through U.S. ports of entry, with peak wait times anticipated between Friday afternoon and Monday evening." Citing operational security and processing requirements, CBP officials are urging all individuals—including U.S. citizens, lawful permanent residents, and foreign nationals—to prepare for extended wait times at land borders, airports, and seaports.

To mitigate delays, the agency recommends that all travelers ensure they possess valid, unexpired travel documents. Additionally, those enrolled in expedited processing programs, such as Global Entry, SENTRI, or FAST, are reminded to verify their membership status and ensure biometric data is current.

CBP Public Affairs noted that primary inspection lanes are expected to see the most congestion. The agency is deploying additional personnel and technology to facilitate lawful travel while maintaining national security protocols. Travelers are advised to consult the official CBP Wait Times website or mobile application for real-time updates before departing for a port of entry.

"Patience and preparation are critical to crossing efficiently during this high-volume period," the statement concluded. The Memorial Day holiday, observed on Monday, May 26, is widely considered the unofficial start of the summer travel season.
