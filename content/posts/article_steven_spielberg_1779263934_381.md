**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**HOLLYWOOD —** Acclaimed filmmaker Steven Spielberg has announced a landmark partnership with an international consortium of historians and archivists to launch a global digitization initiative for endangered film reels.

**Who:** Academy Award-winning director Steven Spielberg, in collaboration with the World Film Heritage Project (WFHP).

**What:** A multi-year, multi-million dollar initiative titled "The Last Reel," which aims to identify, restore, and digitize approximately 50,000 decaying nitrate film reels from developing nations, many of which are the only surviving copies of early 20th-century local cinema.

**When:** The official announcement was made during a press conference at the University of Southern California School of Cinematic Arts on Tuesday, October 24, 2023.

**Where:** The first phase of the project will focus on archives in Southeast Asia, Sub-Saharan Africa, and the South Pacific, with immediate operations commencing in Jakarta, Indonesia, and Lagos, Nigeria.

**Why:** Spielberg stated the initiative is a direct response to a recent UNESCO report warning that up to 70% of silent-era film assets outside of Europe and North America have already been lost due to heat, humidity, and a lack of funding. The director warned that without immediate intervention, "an entire chapter of human storytelling will vanish without a trace."

**Key detail:** "The Last Reel" will be housed at a newly constructed, climate-controlled facility in Los Angeles, with digital masters to be made freely available to scholars and filmmakers in the countries of origin within five years.
