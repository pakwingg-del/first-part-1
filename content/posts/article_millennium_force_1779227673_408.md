**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About Millennium Force – The Coaster That Rewrote Physics**

- **It was the first "Giga-Coaster" on Earth.** When it opened in 2000 at Cedar Point, it became the world’s first roller coaster to break the **300-foot vertical height barrier** (technically 310 feet). It didn’t just go high—it used the terrain’s natural ravine to drop you *into a tunnel*, making the speed feel truly apocalyptic.

- **The "Flight Deck" is a lawsuit magnet.** The ride’s unique, open, waist-high restraint system creates a terrifying "floating" sensation—you feel like you could literally fly out of the train. This design is so aggressive that riders have filed lawsuits claiming the ride caused *whiplash from sheer wind pressure*, though the park stands by its safety record. Spoiler: You’re safe, but your brain doesn’t know that.

- **It holds a secret "ghost track" record.** For 13 years, Millennium Force held the record for the longest roller coaster drop (300 feet) at a 80-degree angle. That drop is so famous that coaster enthusiasts call it "The Big One"—but the ride’s true terror comes from the **over-banked, 150-foot-tall "Overbanked Turn"** where you feel like you’re being peeled off the track.

- **There’s a "Zero-G" moment that isn’t in the brochure.** Engineers designed the ride to hit exactly **93 mph** at the bottom of the first drop. But in the final helix before the brake run, the train actually experiences **negative G-forces for a split second**—meaning your stomach floats upward. The park never advertises this, but it’s why riders often scream during the otherwise "calm" ending.
