**Headline:** *BREAKING: DFW Airport Issues Full Ground Stop After Mysterious Hovering Object Spotted Over Runway*

**The Viral Snippet:** A screenshot of what appears to be an FAA NOTAM (Notice to Air Missions) is circulating widely on X (formerly Twitter), claiming the FAA has issued a full ground stop at Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport due to an “unidentified hovering object” detected near Runway 18L. The post has amassed over 200,000 views in 45 minutes, with users speculating about drones, weather balloons, or even a “UAP event.”

**🔍 REAL vs FAKE :**

**FAKE** — The screenshot is **doctored**. The legitimate NOTAM text was pulled from a standard *weather-related* ground stop issued for DFW on October 24, 2024, due to severe thunderstorms. The viral version replaced “weather conditions / lightning in vicinity” with “unidentified hovering object near runway.” The FAA confirms no such ground stop is in effect.

**What’s Actually Happening:**
- DFW is currently operating normally.
- The viral post’s image shows a timestamp from 2023, recycled and edited with new text.
- No official law enforcement or military activity reports match the claim.

**Bottom line:** A creative hoax, not a real aviation incident. The “hovering object” exists only in a photo editor.
