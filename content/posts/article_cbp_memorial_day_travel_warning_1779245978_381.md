**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**CBP ISSUES NATIONWIDE MEMORIAL DAY TRAVEL ADVISORY**

**WASHINGTON, D.C. —** U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) has issued a formal travel advisory for the upcoming Memorial Day holiday weekend, warning of significant congestion and increased processing times at all ports of entry.

**WHO:** U.S. Customs and Border Protection.
**WHAT:** Issuance of a nationwide travel warning.
**WHEN:** Effective for the Memorial Day holiday weekend, beginning Friday, May 24, and concluding Monday, May 27.
**WHERE:** All air, land, and sea ports of entry across the United States, with particular emphasis on the Southwest border crossings and major international airports.
**WHY:** To alert travelers to anticipated heavy volume expected to create record-level wait times. CBP attributes the surge to high seasonal demand and increased summer travel.

**DETAILS:** Commissioner Troy A. Miller stated the agency anticipates a 20% increase in traveler volume compared to the same period last year. Officials advise all travelers to prepare for delays lasting multiple hours, particularly during peak periods of 10:00 AM to 6:00 PM.

**GUIDANCE:** CBP urges all U.S. citizens to ensure their passport or Trusted Traveler Program card, such as Global Entry or SENTRI, is valid. International visitors are reminded to check visa expiration dates.

**STATEMENT:** "We are committed to facilitating lawful travel and trade, but we must also prioritize security," said Commissioner Miller. "We strongly advise travelers to plan extensively, utilize the CBP One app for real-time wait times, and consider alternative travel times to mitigate significant disruptions."
