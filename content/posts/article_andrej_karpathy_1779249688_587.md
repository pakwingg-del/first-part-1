**Palo Alto, CA** — In a manifesto that has sent shockwaves through Silicon Valley’s moral conscience, AI researcher Andrej Karpathy—often hailed as the father of modern neural networks—dropped a truth bomb that reads less like a technical memo and more like a dystopian sermon. In a viral post late Tuesday, Karpathy did not praise the latest GPT release. Instead, he declared that the tech industry has become a “cargo cult of optimization,” chasing metrics while hollowing out human meaning.

“We have optimized for engagement at the expense of enlightenment,” Karpathy wrote. “We have built gods of code that we no longer understand, and we bow to them with every click. The scaffold of society—trust, nuance, literacy—is being dismantled one algorithmic feed at a time.”

The moral critics have seized on this as a long-overdue confession. Karpathy, once the golden boy of artificial intelligence, is now warning that the very tool he helped forge is eroding empathy and critical thought. “He’s like a nuclear physicist suddenly screaming that the bomb shouldn’t be built,” said Dr. Eleanor Vance, a media ethicist at Stanford. “But the bomb is already *here*, and it’s rewriting our children’s brains.”

The “downfall of society” angle is sharp, and it’s sticking. Karpathy didn’t just critique the software; he indicted the *spirit* of the age. “We have traded the messy, beautiful chaos of human discourse for clean, profitable silence,” he wrote. “We have automated loneliness.”
