**HEADLINE: 🚨 RED LOBSTER ABANDONS TALLAHASSEE: "Endless Shrimp" Deal Blamed for Your Empty Wallet & Closed Doors**

**TALLAHASSEE, FL** – Your go-to spot for buttery biscuits and that "Endless Shrimp" trap is officially shuttered. Red Lobster has locked the doors on its Apalachee Parkway location, and consumer advocates say your pocketbook is the real victim.

**The Bait-and-Switch Reality:**
Red Lobster’s parent company, Thai Union, cited rising food costs, but consumer watchdogs are calling foul. The real culprit? That "$20 Ultimate Endless Shrimp" deal. While it felt like a steal, advocates warn promotions like this are a classic **"financial bait-and-switch."** The chain loses money on the deal, forcing other menu prices to skyrocket—and eventually, they close the entire restaurant.

**How This Hits Your Wallet:**
- **Unpaid Unemployment:** 150 local employees are now jobless. Many are owed final paychecks, and the state is already fielding calls about wage theft. If you were a regular, your tip money is gone.
- **Food Desert Danger:** This closure creates a "budget dining vacuum." Fewer affordable seafood options mean competitors (like LongHorn or Bonefish) can raise prices with no pushback. Expect to pay **$4 more** for a similar fish plate citywide within 90 days.
- **Gift Card Ghosts:** Did you get a $50 gift card for the holidays? **It’s now worthless.** The company will not honor it, and Florida law does not require it. Check your wallet—that cash is gone.
