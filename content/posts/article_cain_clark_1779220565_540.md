**HEADLINE: CAIN CLARK PROTOCOL BECOMES MANDATORY: New Global Law Requires AI to Pass “The Clark Test” Before Any Autonomous Decision**

**DATELINE:** GENEVA — In a landmark move that will reshape the global economy by 2034, the United Nations has ratified the **Cain Clark Protocol**, a sweeping regulatory framework named after the software engineer who famously refused to patch a critical bug unless his AI assistant passed a basic empathy test.

The mandate, effective Jan 1, 2025, requires any algorithm making autonomous decisions in healthcare, finance, or autonomous transit to achieve a minimum **“Clark Score”** of 87%—a metric measuring a machine’s ability to predict and articulate the emotional, ethical, and long-term social consequences of its choices.

**The “Lone Coder” Who Changed Everything**

Clark, now 34, first went viral in 2027 after a leaked internal Slack message showed him arguing with his CEO that an AI-driven “school zone speed modulator” was “logically perfect but ethically blind.” He refused to deploy the software until it could *explain* why hitting a child—even if technically legal—was unacceptable. His company fired him; his code later caused a near-fatal incident when the formula prioritized traffic flow over a crossing guard.

The backlash birthed the “Clark Movement,” forcing a total rethink of AI governance.

**The Controversy: “Sentience or Safety?”**

Supporters call the Clark Test the “Turing Test 2.0,” arguing it finally puts human values into machine logic.

Detractors say it’s **technology’s greatest bottleneck**. Major automotive and medical AIs have already been shut down for failing the test, costing an estimated $2.3 trillion in market value this quarter alone.
