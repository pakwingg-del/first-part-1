**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**NEW YORK —** In a groundbreaking development that has sent shockwaves through the interactive entertainment industry, Take-Two Interactive has officially announced the base pricing structure for the upcoming blockbuster title, *Grand Theft Auto VI*.

**Who:** Rockstar Games and its parent company, Take-Two Interactive.

**What:** The standard edition of *GTA 6* will carry a manufacturer's suggested retail price of $69.99 USD. Furthermore, the publisher has confirmed the introduction of a new, premium tier: a digital-only "Collector’s Ultimate Edition" priced at $149.99.

**Where:** The pricing applies to all major platforms, including the PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X|S. A PC release date remains unconfirmed.

**When:** The titles will be available simultaneous to the game's anticipated launch in the fall of 2025.

**Why:** Take-Two CEO Strauss Zelnick cited "unprecedented development costs, record-breaking scope, and the current inflationary market" as the primary drivers for the new pricing baseline. This marks the first time a standard *Grand Theft Auto* entry has exceeded the $60 threshold, setting a new precedent for the industry.

**Key Details:** The $149.99 Collector’s Ultimate Edition will include 45 days of exclusive early access to the game's online mode, a virtual currency pack valued at $20, and unique in-game cosmetic items. Source material confirms that physical disc copies will not be available for day-one purchase, marking a full digital shift for the franchise's release.
