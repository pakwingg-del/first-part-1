**🚨 VIRAL NEWS SNIPPET: KROGER CHEESE GARLIC CROUTONS RECALL 🚨**

⚠️ **CLAIM:** Social media posts are warning that a batch of Kroger-brand "Cheese & Garlic Croutons" is being pulled from shelves nationwide because they allegedly contain **undeclared peanuts**, posing a severe risk to those with allergies. One post claims a child was hospitalized after eating them.

✅ **FACT-CHECK: PARTIALLY TRUE**

🔍 **What’s real:** Kroger *did* issue a voluntary recall on select 6 oz. bags of their **"Cheese & Garlic Croutons"** (UPC: 11110-99084; Best By dates: 03/09/2026). The recall was triggered after a supplier notified Kroger that the croutons may have been cross-contaminated with peanuts during production. The FDA has listed this as a Class II recall (potential for temporary health issues).

❌ **What’s fake:** The viral post claiming a **child was hospitalized** is **unverified** and has not been confirmed by Kroger, the FDA, or any news outlet. No known reports of severe reactions exist yet. Additionally, the recall is **regional** (affecting only stores in the Midwest and South), not nationwide.

⚠️ **Bottom line:** Check your pantry. If you bought these croutons, **do not eat them**—return to store for a refund. But the dramatic "child hospitalized" story is **false** as of now. Always verify with official FDA recall lists before sharing.
