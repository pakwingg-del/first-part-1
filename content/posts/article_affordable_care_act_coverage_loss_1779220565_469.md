**Viral News Snippet:**

**BREAKING: Millions of Americans to Lose ACA Coverage as "Secret Clause" Kicks In – Fact or Fake?**

🚨 **Rumor:** A viral Facebook post claims that a "little-known secret clause" in the Affordable Care Act (ACA) will automatically kick in next month, stripping health insurance from anyone who earned over $30,000 last year. The post warns, "If you're middle class, you're about to be thrown off your plan without warning. The government wants you on expensive private insurance." It has been shared over 50,000 times in 24 hours.

**Fact Check: FAKE.**
There is no such "secret clause." The ACA (Obamacare) uses income-based subsidies, but coverage is not automatically terminated based on a single income threshold. The rumor appears to distort legitimate changes to "subsidy clawback" rules from the 2024-2025 enrollment period—where some families may owe a partial repayment if they overestimated their income. However, this is a tax adjustment, not a mass cancellation of policies. Actual coverage loss under the ACA has been driven by state policy changes (e.g., redeterminations in Medicaid unwinding) or premium hikes—not a single federal income cutoff. The $30,000 figure cited in the rumor is arbitrary and incorrect.

**Verdict:** 🛑 Stop sharing. This is a misrepresentation of routine tax processes. Always verify with the official healthcare.gov (U.S.) or your local exchange before panicking.
