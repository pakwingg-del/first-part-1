⚠️ YOUR AIR CONDITIONER IS ABOUT TO COST YOU A FORTUNE.

An excessive heat advisory is baking half the country today, with "feels-like" temps soaring past 105°F. But here’s the wallet-jolt you need to hear: Your AC is working 3x harder than normal to keep your home at 72°F. That means your electric bill is about to look like a mortgage payment.

🏠 **The "Cool Down" Tax:** Experts warn that running central air during this spike could cost homeowners an extra **$175-$300** this month alone. Desperate for relief? Forget plugging in a space heater — a single window unit running full blast costs about 50 cents an hour, which adds up fast.

🌳 **The $5 Hack That Might Save Your Wallet:** Before you crank the thermostat, go outside. Check your air conditioner’s outdoor unit. Is it clogged with leaves, dirt, or overgrown weeds? A dirty condenser can make your system work **20% harder**. Cleaning it with a garden hose and a soft brush is a $5 fix that could save you **$40 a month**.

🛑 **Don’t Fall for The "Emergency Fix" Scam:** Beware of "emergency HVAC techs" charging $300 just to walk through your door. If your system breaks down this weekend, try cleaning the filters and checking your thermostat batteries first. A simple $2 battery swap can reboot a confused unit.

❄️ **The Muggy Truth:** High humidity is the real enemy. Your AC is fighting to remove moisture, not just cool air. Use a **dehumidifier** in the dampest room (basement or laundry room). It lets your AC rest, slashing your bill by up to 15%.

**The Bottom Line:** Stay hydrated, check your outdoor unit, and don't let the heatwave burn a hole in your wallet. Live
