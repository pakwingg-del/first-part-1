**Viral News Snippet: "Millennium Force’s ‘Ghost Train’ Glitch Has Riders Seeing Double"**

**Cedar Point, OH** – A viral video circulating on TikTok and X claims that a "secret glitch" in the Millennium Force roller coaster’s control system causes its trains to briefly overlap in mid-air, creating a terrifying "ghost train" effect during afternoon heat waves. The clip, viewed 8 million times, shows two trains appearing to occupy the same spot on the track for a split second, with riders screaming "That’s not real!"

**Status: ✘ FAKE**

Fact-checkers confirm the video is CGI, manipulated using rotoscoping and after-effects. Cedar Point’s ride maintenance team states: "Millennium Force’s block-brake system is physically incapable of allowing two trains to interact or overlap. The 'ghost train' is a clever visual illusion—no heat wave can bend physics." The trend has, however, sparked a renewed interest in the coaster's 25-year-old engineering, with enthusiast forums now debating whether the park should add "virtual ghost trains" for Halloween.
