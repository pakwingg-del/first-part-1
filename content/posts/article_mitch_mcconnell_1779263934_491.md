**WASHINGTON, D.C. — In a move that has left millions of Americans suddenly checking their bank accounts, Senate Minority Leader Mitch McConnell has reportedly brokered a deal to fast-track a new tax on your *digital* pocket change.** 

The new provision, buried deep in a must-pass spending bill, would slap a 2% "Digital Transaction Fee" on every Venmo, CashApp, and PayPal transfer over $20. That means sending your kid $50 for lunch could cost you an extra $1. However, the bill conveniently exempts cryptocurrencies and high-frequency stock trades—activities critics note are favored by wealthy donors.

**Why this hits your wallet:** If you split rent with roommates or pay the babysitter via app, you’re about to get nickeled-and-dimed. Consumer advocates estimate this could cost the average American household $600 per year in hidden fees.

**The kicker:** The fee is framed as "closing the tax gap" on gig workers, but it collects from everyone—even if you’re just paying your sister back for pizza. The bill is expected to pass with bipartisan support, but only after a 3 a.m. amendment no one read.

**What you can do:** Check your app settings—some banks are already testing "offline cash" features to dodge the fee, but experts warn that could lead to even more security risks.
