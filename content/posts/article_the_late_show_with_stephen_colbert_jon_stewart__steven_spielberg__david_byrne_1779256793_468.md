**Headline:** *FACT CHECK: Did Stephen Colbert, Jon Stewart, Steven Spielberg, and David Byrne Secretly Film a Joint ‘Final Call’ Episode Before a Government Shutdown?*

**Rumor:** A viral thread on X and TikTok claims that the four cultural icons gathered in a soundproof bunker beneath the Ed Sullivan Theater to film a “never-to-be-aired” unity episode titled *The Late Show: The Last Broadcast*. The rumor alleges that Spielberg directed the episode, Stewart played the role of “Truth Commissioner,” and Byrne composed a soundtrack using only vacuum cleaners and Congressional transcripts. The post claims the footage is “locked in a safe until the government collapses.”

**Verdict: FAKE.** 

**What’s Actually Happening:**

- **Spielberg** is currently on location in New Zealand shooting a sci-fi epic—not directing late-night TV.
- **David Byrne** is in the middle of a sold-out tour for his *Theater of the Mind* immersive audio show in New York, which wraps in December. His touring calendar confirms he was 300 miles away during the alleged shoot date (Oct. 11).
- **Jon Stewart** openly mocked the “bunker” rumor on his own podcast, saying: “If I’m in a bunker with Colbert and Byrne, I’m not making TV. I’m starting a very anxious, harmonizing cult.”
- **Stephen Colbert’s** team confirmed to PolitiFact that the episode in question—a remote “solidarity” segment with Stewart—was filmed via satellite and aired on Tuesday. No Spielberg. No Byrne. No vacuum cleaners.

**Why It Spread:** The rumor includes just enough verifiable elements (Colbert and Stewart *did* film a special segment; Byrne *has* written political music before) to feel plausible, and taps into audience anxiety about media censorship.

**Bottom Line:** The bunker is a fiction
