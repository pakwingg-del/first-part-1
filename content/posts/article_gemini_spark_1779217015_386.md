**HEADLINE: BREAKING: Google Unveils "Gemini Spark," Next-Generation AI Architecture, Claims Unprecedented Reasoning Capabilities**

**MOUNTAIN VIEW, CA** – In a formal announcement made earlier this morning at its headquarters, Google DeepMind officially unveiled "Gemini Spark," a new AI architecture that the company states represents a "generational leap" in autonomous reasoning and contextual memory.

**WHAT?** The tool, designated as a successor to the Gemini family, is not a standard chatbot but rather a core reasoning engine. According to the official press release, Gemini Spark is designed to process complex, multi-step problems with a "99.7% reduction in hallucination rates" compared to its predecessors. The key feature is its ability to maintain a dynamic "working memory" that tracks logical threads over extended dialogues.

**WHO?** The announcement was made by Demis Hassabis, CEO of DeepMind, alongside Google CEO Sundar Pichai. The project, internally code-named "Project Ember," has been in development for 18 months with a dedicated team of 200 researchers.

**WHEN?** The news was published at 10:00 AM Pacific Time. Developer access via a private API is scheduled to begin in Q4 of this year, with a limited public beta expected in early next year.

**WHERE?** The launch is exclusive to Google Cloud’s Vertex AI platform for enterprise clients initially, with expansion to consumer products—including a potential integration with Google Search and Bard—expected in mid-next year.

**WHY?** Hassabis stated the development was driven by a need to solve "the reasoning collapse problem" observed in large language models, where performance degrades on deeper logical tasks. Gemini Spark utilizes a novel "Self-Correction via First-Principles" (SCFP) algorithm.
