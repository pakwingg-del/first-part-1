**Claim:** A leaked internal memo from U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) is circulating online, warning travelers that **all vehicles crossing land borders or entering airports** will face **enhanced biometric screening** starting Memorial Day weekend. The memo allegedly states that even U.S. citizens must provide “digital proof of citizenship” or risk detention, with a **48-hour delay for verification** if documents are missing.

**Verdict: ⚠️ PARTIALLY TRUE – MISLEADING**

- **Real:** CBP *has* increased staffing and upgraded biometric scanners (facial recognition) at 30+ ports of entry ahead of the holiday weekend. They also remind travelers that **REAL ID enforcement begins May 7, 2025** for domestic flights over 18.
- **Fake:** The “mandatory digital proof” and “48-hour detention” claims are **fabricated**. No internal memo exists. CBP’s official statement says processing delays will only apply to **non-compliant foreign nationals** with expired visas or pending removal orders. U.S. citizens cannot be denied entry solely for lacking a digital ID.

**Bottom line:** Don’t panic, but **double-check your ID**. The viral warning is fact-checked as **misleading hype** mixed with real policy changes.
