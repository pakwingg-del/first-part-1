**TEEN DATING APP “MASSIE PRIMARY” CONDEMNED BY MORAL CRITICS: “A DOWNLOADABLE LICENSE TO DESTROY CHILDHOOD”**

**Washington, D.C.** – A firestorm of outrage erupted this week following the viral spread of “Massie Primary,” a new teen-focused dating app that allegedly gamifies social hierarchy and rewards users with “status points” for securing dates with younger, less experienced partners. Moral critics are calling it a “digital grooming platform in sheep’s clothing,” warning that the app’s core algorithm actively incentivizes emotional manipulation and age-gap predation among minors.

Dr. Helen Vancroft, a noted family ethicist, declared the app a “cultural cancer,” stating: *“This is not innovation; this is a downloadable license to destroy childhood. By turning courtship into a quantified competition, we are teaching an entire generation that human worth is a scoreboard and that the ‘prize’ is access to someone more vulnerable. This is the logical, terrifying endpoint of a society that replaced empathy with engagement metrics.”*

The app, which requires users to verify their school ID, has been banned by several school districts already, yet it remains available in app stores. Parent groups are now demanding immediate federal intervention, claiming that “Massie Primary” does not merely reflect a moral decline—it actively engineers one. The question remains: in a world already fractured by screens, will we let this be the final lesson we teach our children about love?
