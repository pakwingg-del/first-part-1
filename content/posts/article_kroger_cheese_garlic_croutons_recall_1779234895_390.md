**ALERT: Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Kroger Cheese Garlic Croutons Recall**

- **🛑 The Recall Involves a Specific "Best By" Date**  
  Kroger has issued a voluntary recall for its *Private Selection* brand Cheese & Garlic Croutons (6 oz.) with a **"Best By" date of July 18, 2025**. Check the back of your bag immediately—any package outside this window is not affected.

- **🧀 Why It Got Pulled: Undeclared Allergen Danger**  
  The croutons contain **milk (dairy)**, but the ingredient label failed to list milk as an allergen. This poses a serious risk for anyone with milk allergies or severe lactose intolerance—consumption could trigger anaphylaxis or other allergic reactions.

- **📍 Where They Were Sold**  
  These croutons were distributed exclusively to **Kroger stores** (including affiliates like Ralphs, Food 4 Less, Fry's, and Smith's) across the **Midwest, South, and West Coast** of the U.S. Not sold at other retailers like Walmart or Target.

- **💰 What You Should Do: Full Refund Offered**  
  Do not eat them—return the unopened (or even opened) bag to any Kroger store for a **full refund**. No receipt is needed. You can also call Kroger's customer service at 1-800-KRO-GERS for additional support.

- **📢 No Injuries Reported Yet—But Act Fast**  
  As of the recall date, **no illnesses or allergic reactions** have been confirmed. However, the FDA and Kroger urge customers to check pantries immediately and discard or return product to prevent potential harm.
