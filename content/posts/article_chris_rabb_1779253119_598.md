**Headline: Moral Outrage Erupts: Chris Rabb's "Ethical Fluidity" Seminars Linked to Club Kid Culture Collapse**

“He’s glorifying a culture that was defined by unchecked excess, chemical dependency, and the commodification of rebellion,” declared Dr. Helen Vance, a cultural ethics professor at Liberty University. “This isn’t just bad advice. This is the philosophical grease for the next moral collapse.”

Outrage has focused on Rabb’s alleged assertion that “authenticity” must come before social contract, with opponents claiming this undermines the basic trust structures of family and civic life. Hashtags like #RabbTheRot and #SaveTheSocialContract are trending, as parents and religious leaders call for the cancellation of community partnerships with his initiatives.

Critics argue this is a dangerous step backward, drawing a direct line from Rabb’s rhetoric to the social isolation and nihilism they say now plagues Generation Z. “We are literally teaching a generation that their impulses are their only gods,” one local pastor thundered. “And Chris Rabb is the high priest.”
