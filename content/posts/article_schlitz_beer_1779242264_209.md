🚨 **LOCAL MAN DECLARES SCHLITZ BEER “THE ULTIMATE COMMON SENSE SOLUTION” AFTER PRICE HIKE ON CRAFT BREWS** 🚨

Wilkes-Barre, PA – In a now-viral rant on the “Honesdale Happenings” Facebook page, resident Gary Kowalski, 58, let loose on local yuppies after discovering a four-pack of hazy IPA costs more than a case of Schlitz.

“It’s just common sense,” Gary wrote. “You walk into the beer distributor, see a can with a painting of a guy holding a pine tree for $14, and then you see a 12-pack of the Schlitz for $6.99. What are we even talking about? The Schlitz has been keeping America running since before your grandpa got his first mullet. It’s the official beer of ‘we have groceries to buy.’”

Gary’s post, which includes a photo of him holding a can next to his ‘08 F-150, has racked up 400 shares and over 2,000 laughing emojis. “This is the kind of straight talk we need,” commented Linda from Hawley. “I tried a ‘sour ale’ once. Tasted like lemon Pledge and regret. Schlitz just tastes like a Friday night.”

Local brewery owners are reportedly “shaking their heads,” but Gary insists: “Schlitz is proof that you don’t need to be fancy to be good. It’s the beer of getting the job done. Common sense says: don’t pay for hype when you can pay for history.”

Cheers to common sense, apparently. 🍺
