**HEADLINE: "SHATTERED GUARDRAILS: Supreme Court Ruling Opens Floodgates for 'Moral Hazard' as Pundits Declare the Death of Personal Responsibility"**

**Viña del Mar, Chile** – In a decision that has ignited a firestorm of ethical debate, the Corte Suprema has ruled that a national corporation is not liable for damages caused by its product when a consumer "knowingly misused" the item in a manner that violated established safety codes and common decency.

The case has become a lightning rod for moral critics who claim the court has effectively given the green light to a "culture of victimhood."

"We are watching the slow, deliberate dismantling of the social contract," declared Dr. Alistair Finch, a prominent cultural critic. "This ruling doesn't just forgive a corporation's lack of oversight; it actively penalizes the concept of personal foresight. We are now teaching a generation that there is no action too reckless, no judgment too impaired, that cannot be outsourced to a faceless entity. The 'fallback' has become the primary plan. When the highest court in the land validates the abdication of personal responsibility, we do not get a more just society. We get a more careless, entitled, and ultimately, a more fragile one. The guardrails of conscience have been removed."

The verdict has sparked protests outside the Palacio de los Tribunales, with families arguing it sets a precedent where "the price of a broken moral compass is now billed to the common good."
