**🚨 ALERT: YOUR DAD’S FAVORITE BEER IS GONE FOR GOOD 🚨**

**"The Beer That Made Milwaukee Famous" Has Officially Retired — And It’s Hitting Your Wallet Where It Hurts**

In a move that has beer lovers and budget-conscious drinkers reeling, **Schlitz** — the iconic, no-frills brew that survived Prohibition, two World Wars, and decades of clunky advertising — is being **permanently discontinued**.

**Why You Should Care:**
💰 **Your Wallet:** Schlitz was the ultimate “utility beer.” At a time when a six-pack of craft IPA costs you $14, Schlitz was still clocking in under $7. That safety net for cheap beer night? Gone.
🏷️ **The Price Spike Alert:** With Schlitz out of the picture, Pabst, Miller High Life, and Keystone just got a little more expensive. Supply and demand 101: one less cheap option means the others can charge more. Expect to see your go-to “cheap” sixer creep up 50 cents to a dollar this summer.
🍺 **Your Dad’s Legacy:** If your dad or grandpa swore by “the Schlitz,” you now have to explain to him that his go-to “I’m not fancy, I’m smart” beer is extinct. (Cue the emotional damage and a grumpy trip to the liquor store.)

**The Nasty Truth:** The “Gusto” is dead. After a fire destroyed a key production facility in 2023, the company quietly pulled the plug. They’re not even making a “limited edition” run. It’s over.
