**DATELINE: LONDON, UNITED KINGDOM**

**Anchor:** This is a special bulletin from NewsChannel 7.

**HEADLINE:** Phil Collins Announces Final Retirement from Music, Citing Debilitating Health Complications.

**WHO:** Phil Collins, the 74-year-old Grammy-winning, multi-platinum recording artist, drummer, and former lead vocalist of the progressive rock band Genesis.

**WHAT:** In a formal statement released through his management earlier today, Collins has announced his definitive and permanent retirement from all public musical performances and studio recording. The announcement includes the cancellation of any pending archival projects and a definitive end to all public appearances related to his musical career.

**WHEN:** The announcement was made today, at 10:00 AM GMT. The decision is effective immediately. Collins has not performed a full concert since his 2022 "Not Dead Yet" tour concluded, but this statement ends any speculation of future activity.

**WHERE:** The announcement was released from Collins’ private residence in Switzerland, where he has been undergoing treatment and rehabilitation for multiple chronic conditions.

**WHY:** Collins cites an irreversible and significant deterioration in his overall health as the primary reason. Key factors include the continued complications from a 2009 spinal surgery that has left him with severe nerve damage, known as "drop foot," rendering him unable to play the drums—his primary instrument. Additionally, he has suffered from persistent mobility issues, requiring the use of a cane or walker, and has experienced a significant loss of stamina, which medical experts confirm is a direct result of his prolonged physical ailments. Collins stated, “The physical toll is too great. My body is telling me to stop. The tank is empty.”
