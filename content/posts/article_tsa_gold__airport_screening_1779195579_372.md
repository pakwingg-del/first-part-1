**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**TSA Introduces "TSA Gold+" Service for Enhanced Airport Screening**

**Date:** [Insert Date]

**WASHINGTON, D.C.** – The Transportation Security Administration (TSA) has announced the nationwide launch of a new premium security screening program, "TSA Gold+," which introduces a tiered system aimed at expediting passenger flow while maintaining rigorous security standards.

**WHO:**
The "TSA Gold+" program is available to all U.S. adult citizens and lawful permanent residents who voluntarily enroll and meet specific eligibility criteria, including a background check and payment of an annual fee. The program specifically targets frequent flyers and high-volume travelers.

**WHAT:**
TSA Gold+ is an expedited screening program that allows enrolled passengers to keep on shoes, light outerwear, belts, and laptops in their carry-on bags during standard physical screening. In addition to existing TSA PreCheck benefits, Gold+ offers a dedicated, single-file security lane with dedicated personnel, as well as a secondary, non-invasive ID verification kiosk that reduces wait times. The program also includes a "fast-track" option for connecting flights.

**WHEN:**
The TSA Gold+ program officially launched nationwide on [Date]. Enrollment centers are now open at all major U.S. hub airports and at select TSA enrollment locations.

**WHERE:**
The program is available at all domestic commercial airports with TSA security checkpoints. International departures are not included at this time.

**WHY:**
The TSA introduced the program to address increasing passenger volumes and public demand for faster, more predictable airport experiences. The agency states that the enhanced screening process will maintain critical threat detection capabilities.

**HOW:**
Enrolled passengers must present their TSA Gold+ digital or physical identification card at a designated Gold+ checkpoint. The lane features advanced imaging technology and behavior detection officers. The annual fee is $120, with a $100 renewal fee.
