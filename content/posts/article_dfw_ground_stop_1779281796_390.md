# ✈️ Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the DFW Ground Stop

- **What Happened:** The Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) issued a full ground stop for all flights bound for Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport (DFW) due to severe thunderstorms and lightning within 5 miles of the runways. Over 1,200 flights were delayed or canceled within the first hour.

- **Why It's a Big Deal:** DFW is the third busiest airport in the world. A sudden ground stop doesn't just affect Texas—it creates a ripple effect nationwide, delaying planes, crews, and connecting passengers from coast to coast.

- **What Travelers Are Experiencing:** Viral videos this morning show passengers stuck on tarmacs in Chicago, Denver, and Los Angeles, watching their DFW-bound flights get scratched in real time. Some have been waiting on the runway for over 90 minutes.

- **How Long Will It Last?:** The FAA says the stop will remain in effect until at least 2 p.m. CST, but reports of "pop-up supercells" forming every 15 minutes have officials bracing for an extended hold. Check your airline app—many are automatically rebooking.

- **The Silver Lining:** If your flight is canceled, airlines are waiving change fees for DFW itineraries today. Travelers are being encouraged to rebook for tomorrow morning, when forecasters predict clear skies and normal operations.
