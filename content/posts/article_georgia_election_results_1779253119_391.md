Here are the **Top 5 Things You Need to Know** about the Georgia election results:

- **Runoff Rematch Decides the Senate:** The race came down to a high-stakes runoff between incumbent Democrat Raphael Warnock and Republican challenger Herschel Walker. Warnock secured a narrow victory, giving Democrats an outright 51-seat majority in the Senate (a crucial extra seat that reduces the influence of party holdouts like Joe Manchin).

- **Record Early Voting & Surprise Turnout:** Over 1.9 million Georgians cast early ballots, shattering previous runoff records. The surge was driven by heavy turnout from Black voters and suburban women in metro Atlanta, a demographic that analysts say tilted the race decisively blue despite a statewide Republican trend.

- **The "On The Ballot" Factor:** The most viral subplot was the direct targeting of Governor Brian Kemp by Democratic groups. Kemp won his own re-election in November, but because he certified the election (refusing Trump’s pressure to overturn 2020 results), Warnock’s ads relentlessly tied Walker to Kemp. Result? Kemp voters split their ticket and backed Warnock.

- **The "Still Feel the Sting" Effect:** Georgia Democrats successfully weaponized the 2021 voting law SB 202. By framing the runoff as a battle for "voting rights" and "democracy itself," they turned what was a procedural election into a national referendum on access, driving massive turnout among college students and civil rights groups.

- **Money, Money, Money:** This became the most expensive Senate race in U.S. history, with over $400 million spent. Warnock outspent Walker 3-to-1, largely on digital ads targeting independent women, while Walker was hampered by a series of damaging personal scandals that even his base struggled to defend.
