**BERKSHIRE HATHAWAY ANNOUNCES RECORD QUARTERLY PROFIT, CASH RESERVES SURPASS $200 BILLION**

**OMAHA, NE – August 7, 2024** – In a filing with the Securities and Exchange Commission released after market close Wednesday, Berkshire Hathaway Inc. reported a record quarterly operating profit of $11.6 billion for the period ending June 30, 2024. This represents a 15% increase year-over-year, driven primarily by strong performance in the conglomerate's insurance and railroad divisions.

**What:** The disclosure of second-quarter earnings revealed a surge in net income to $30.3 billion, buoyed by substantial gains from the company's massive equity portfolio. However, the most significant financial metric highlighted in the report was the accumulation of cash and Treasury bill holdings, which now total a historic $207.3 billion.

**Who:** The announcement was made by Berkshire Hathaway, led by its CEO, Warren Buffett, who has historically used such cash reserves to execute major acquisitions during market downturns.

**Where:** The reported figures are based on the company’s consolidated operations, headquartered in Omaha, Nebraska.

**When:** The earnings report was filed on the afternoon of Wednesday, August 7, 2024, covering the fiscal second quarter.

**Why:** Analysts attribute the massive cash pile to a lack of compelling, large-scale acquisition targets in the current elevated market. Furthermore, the report confirmed that Chairman Buffett continued to reduce the company’s stake in Apple Inc., selling approximately 390 million shares in the second quarter.

**How:** The cash reserve ballooned partly through asset sales and the retention of earnings from subsidiaries.
