**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Kroger Garlic Cheese Croutons Recall**

**1. The "Hidden Allergen" Nightmare**
Kroger issued a voluntary recall for its **Private Selection Garlic and Parmesan Croutons** (5 oz. bags) because they may contain **undeclared milk**. This is a critical issue for anyone with a dairy allergy or lactose intolerance, as the allergen isn’t listed on the label.

**2. Which Specific Bags Are Affected?**
Only croutons with a **"Best If Used By" date of January 27, 2025** are impacted. The UPC is **0-11110-85562-4**. If your bag matches that date, do not eat it—even if you usually tolerate dairy, cross-contamination risk is high.

**3. The Scary "Mistake" That Triggered the Recall**
The mislabeling was discovered after **one customer reported a severe allergic reaction**. This isn’t a small packaging error—it’s a life-threatening oversight for the estimated **32 million Americans** with food allergies.

**4. Full Refund—No Receipt Required**
Kroger is offering a **full refund** on the affected croutons. You don’t need a receipt; just return the bag to any Kroger-owned store (including Fry’s, Ralphs, or Smith’s) for a refund. If you’ve already eaten them and had a reaction, contact your doctor immediately.

**5. What to Do If You Bought Them**
- **Check your pantry** right now.
- **Throw away** or return the croutons.
- **Call Kroger’s customer service** at 1-800-576-4377 if you have questions.
- **Watch for symptoms** like hives, swelling, or difficulty breathing if you or a family member has a milk allergy.
