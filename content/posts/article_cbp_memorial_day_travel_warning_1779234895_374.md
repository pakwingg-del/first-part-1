**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**ANCHOR DESK – CBP ISSUES NATIONWIDE TRAVEL WARNING AHEAD OF MEMORIAL DAY WEEKEND**

**WASHINGTON, D.C.** — U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) officials issued a formal travel advisory today, warning of significant delays and heightened security protocols at all air, land, and sea entry points across the nation.

**What:** The CBP is implementing Operation Alert Secure, a nationwide enforcement surge targeting commercial and passenger traffic. The agency projects processing times to increase by as much as 300% during peak hours.

**Who:** The warning applies to all U.S. citizens, lawful permanent residents, and international visitors re-entering the United States. Additionally, commercial carriers and importers should expect extended inspections.

**When:** The advisory takes effect at 0001 hours on Thursday, May 26, and will remain in force through midnight on Tuesday, May 31.

**Where:** All 328 official ports of entry, including major international airports such as JFK, LAX, and O’Hare, as well as the busiest land border crossings in Texas, California, Arizona, and New York.

**Why:** According to CBP Acting Commissioner Troy A. Miller, the operation is a proactive measure to address an anticipated record volume of travelers—expected to exceed 2.7 million daily crossings—and to deter illegal activity often associated with holiday periods.

**Key Details:**

- Travelers are urged to enroll in or verify their membership in the Trusted Traveler Programs (Global Entry, NEXUS, SENTRI) at least 72 hours prior to departure.
- Non-compliant documentation, including expired Real ID credentials, will result in mandatory secondary screening.
- Agricultural and biological commodity declarations will face zero-tolerance enforcement.
