**VERDICT: MISLEADING / OUT OF CONTEXT**

**Viral News Snippet:**

**Headline:** “CBP Issues ‘Active Shooter’ Travel Warning for All Major Highways This Memorial Day Weekend”

**The Claim:**

A viral social media post warns that U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) has issued a "Level 4: Do Not Travel" advisory for Memorial Day weekend due to “credible threats of coordinated active shooter events” along interstates near the U.S.-Mexico and Canada borders. The post includes a pixelated image of a CBP logo with red “ALERT” banners and claims agents will be “conducting emergency roadblocks” starting Friday.

**What’s True:** CBP **did** issue a standard seasonal travel advisory reminding the public that heavy Memorial Day traffic could lead to delays at ports of entry (bridges, border crossings, and airports). The agency routinely warns travelers to plan ahead, expect longer wait times, and ensure proper documentation.

**What’s False/Misleading:**
- **No “Active Shooter” or “Level 4” warning exists.** CBP has **not** issued any credible-threat bulletin regarding active shooters targeting highways. The “Level 4” language is fabricated and mimics old, debunked FEMA hoaxes.
- **“Emergency roadblocks”** are not part of any current operational plan. CBP can conduct immigration checkpoints within 100 miles of the border, but not under the guise of an active-shooter alert.
- The viral image uses a **generic, outdated CBP logo** (pre-2023 redesign) and **lacks official document numbers or a .gov source link**, hallmarks of a cut-and-paste hoax.
