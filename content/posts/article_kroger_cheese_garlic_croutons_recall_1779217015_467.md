**UPDATE: KROGER ISSUES URGENT RECALL ON “CHEESE GARLIC CROUTONS” – BUT IS THE WARNING ABOUT A “TINY SPY CAMERA” REAL?**

*Viral News Snippet*

**The Claim:** A frantic TikTok user, @CheeseGuy911, posted a video claiming that Kroger has recalled its "Cheese Garlic Croutons" (14 oz. bag) after a consumer found a "miniature, fully functional spy camera" embedded inside a single crouton. The video, which has amassed 1.2 million views, alleges the device was actively recording from the salad aisle. The user warns: "Do not eat these. They are watching you."

**The Reality:** **FALSE – But with a twist of confusion.**

A Kroger spokesperson confirmed to *Fact-Check Weekly* that a **limited recall** was indeed issued for a specific batch (Lot # K2430A7, exp. 2025) of their *Store Brand* Cheese Garlic Croutons, but **not because of a camera**. The real recall is due to an undeclared **milk allergen** in the "Garlic" variant—a mislabeling error affecting only 4,000 bags sold in the Midwest. 

The "spy camera" claim has been traced back to a viral prank by a satirical news site, *OnionFields.com*, which posted a photoshopped image of a crouton with a lens. No actual devices were found. Kroger urges customers to ignore the camera rumor and instead check their pantries for the *allergen recall* if they purchased croutons between Jan. 3 and Jan. 10.

**Bottom line:** **Partially real** (there is a recall), **mostly fake** (no secret cameras). Check your bag for the lot number—not for a lens.
