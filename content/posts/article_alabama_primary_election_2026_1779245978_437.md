**HEADLINE:** *Alabama’s 2026 Primary Shocks the South: A Two-Front War for the Soul of the GOP*

**SNIPPET:**

Alabama’s Republican primary is no longer just a warm-up act for November. With a sitting governor facing an aggressive challenge from a Trump-endorsed insurgent—and a bitter open seat battle for Jeff Sessions’ old vacancy—the race has become a high-stakes proxy war between the old guard and the populist wave.

On one side, Governor Kay Ivey (82) is fighting for her legacy against a candidate who promises to “drain the Swamp” from Montgomery. On the other, a crowded primary for the Senate seat is pitting a Trump-aligned former football star against a seasoned state attorney, each claiming to be the only one who can flip the seat back red.

**Why it matters:** This isn’t just about Alabama. National donors are watching to see if the GOP can hold the Senate—and if the party’s future is defined by institutional experience or raw populist energy. Expect heavy spending, attack ads, and a turnout that could redefine the southern political map for 2028. The outcome will be a bellwether for every swing state Republican strategy going forward.
