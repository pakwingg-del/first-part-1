**🚨 BREAKING: Red Lobster in Tallahassee Closes – What Your Brain Is Really Craving (Hint: It’s Not Cheddar Bay Biscuits)**

The iconic Red Lobster in Tallahassee has officially shuttered its doors, leaving locals mourning endless shrimp and buttery biscuits. But while headlines scream “supply chain” or “declining sales,” here’s the real psychological bite: our grief over a restaurant closure is rarely about the food—it’s about the ritual.

That booth where you celebrated a promotion. The corner table for a first date. The familiar menu that asked nothing of you. When a place like this closes, it’s a tiny death of predictability. And in an era where everything feels unstable (prices, politics, the weather), losing a steady spot to escape can feel heavier than we admit.

**The coaching takeaway:** Don’t ignore the pang. Your brain isn’t mourning lobster—it’s mourning the *anchor*. The closure is a loudspeaker reminding you: “What else in your life feels unstable right now?”

**Action step:** Name one ritual—big or small—you can consciously protect today. Could be a morning coffee spot, a weekly walk, or 10 minutes of silence. Anchors aren’t just places; they’re *practices*. Build one before you lose another.

*Because the emptiness after a closed restaurant isn’t a menu void. It’s a soul-nudge to re-anchor yourself—biscuits optional.*
