**Viral News Snippet: Mark Cuban's 'Random Act of Crypto' Stuns Town, Sparks IRS Probe**

**FAIRBANK, IA —** A viral rumor sweeping social media claims that billionaire "Shark Tank" investor Mark Cuban paid off the entire property tax debt for the rural town of Fairbank, Iowa (population 1,100) by distributing a newly created cryptocurrency called "FairbankCoin" directly to residents' digital wallets.

**The Claim:** The video, which has amassed over 4 million views on TikTok, shows a woman claiming to be the town clerk announcing that "Mr. Cuban's charity called 'The Cyber Samaritan Fund' has liquidated a digital asset to cover every delinquent tax bill, no strings attached." The post states the total was $1.8 million.

**The Reality (Fact-Check):**
- **Is this real?** **NO.** This is a deepfake. The audio was generated by an AI voice cloning tool, and the video uses a 2022 town hall meeting clip from a different Iowa county.
- **The IRS & SEC Response:** Both agencies have launched investigations into the "FairbankCoin" smart contract, which was created on a testnet two days before the video went viral. Experts warn the coin is a "honeypot" designed to drain wallets of anyone who tries to claim the supposed "tax relief."
- **The Real Mark Cuban:** His official spokesperson released a statement: "Mr. Cuban has not engaged in any crypto charity in Iowa. He does not know where Fairbank is. Anyone clicking on a link in these comments will lose their money. This is a scam."
- **Local Impact:** The Town Hall of Fairbank had to shut down its phone lines due to hate mail and angry calls from residents saying they "missed the drop." The town's actual tax delinquency is $47,000—not $1.8 million.
