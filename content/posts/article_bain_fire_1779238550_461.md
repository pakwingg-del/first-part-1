**URGENT FAKE ALERT: “Bain Capital CEO Allegedly Admits to Starting Wildfires for Insurance Payouts”**

**Rating: ❌ FAKE / MISLEADING**

**What’s going viral:**
A screengrab of a fake CNN alert is circulating on X (formerly Twitter) and TikTok, claiming that Bain Capital CEO John Connaughton has “confessed” to deliberately igniting the recent wildfires in Lahaina, Maui, and parts of Northern California in order to collect massive insurance premiums on tech investments. The fabricated quote reads: *“We had to clean the slate to reset the valuation of coastal real estate. It’s just business.”*

**Why it’s fake:**
- No major news outlet — including CNN, NBC, or AP — has reported any such confession or legal filing.
- Bain Capital released a statement Tuesday calling the claim “a completely baseless fabrication” and confirming Connaughton was in Boston at a board meeting on the dates of the hottest fire days.
- Reverse-image searches show the CNN logo and lower-third graphic were spliced from a 2021 segment about stock market volatility.
- The original video source is a satirical account known for parody “confessions.”

**The real story:**
Investigations into the Maui fires (August 2023) and the Park Fire (July 2024) both remain focused on downed power lines and dry vegetation, not arson-for-profit. Insurance experts note that intentionally starting a wildfire would void virtually every policy, making a “payout scheme” financially nonsensical.

**Bottom line:** This is a hoax designed to inflame distrust in private equity. Share responsibly — and always check local fire authority reports before hitting repost. 🔥🚫📰
