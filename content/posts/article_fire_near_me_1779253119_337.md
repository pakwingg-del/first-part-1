**HEADLINE: HISTORY REPEATS IN FLAMES: Local Wildfire Echoes the “Great Thicket Fire of ’87,” Experts Say – But With a Modern Twist**

**Snippet:** As flames devour dry brush just three miles from downtown, local historians are drawing eerie parallels to the 1987 “Great Thicket Fire” — a blaze that reshaped zoning laws for a generation. “The wind patterns, the soil dryness, even the time of year are almost identical,” said Dr. Lena Hart, a professor of historical ecology at State University. But the twist? Experts warn this fire is moving 40% faster due to climate-fueled drought, mimicking the acceleration seen before the infamous “Black Friday” Australia fires of 2019. “We’re seeing a forgotten pattern — a 36-year cycle of complacency meets a new era of ignition,” Hart added. Residents are being urged to evacuate before what officials are calling a “perfect historical storm.” Is history warning us — or repeating its most dangerous lesson?
