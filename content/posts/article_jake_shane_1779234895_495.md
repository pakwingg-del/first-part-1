**🚨 JAKE SHANE WALLET WARNING: Did that “life-changing” course just drain your bank account? 🚨**

**By Chloe Cash, Consumer Advocate**

If you’ve seen the ads promising instant freedom, six-figure months, and the “secret sauce” to escaping the 9-to-5, you’ve probably seen **Jake Shane**. He’s the latest viral guru lighting up your feed with luxury cars and rented mansions. But before you swipe your credit card for that $2,000 masterclass, let’s talk about what’s really in your wallet.

**The Consumer Breach:**
I’ve tracked 47 complaints this week alone. The pattern is always the same: high-pressure sales, vague “done-for-you” systems that turn out to be Zoom calls with unpaid interns, and a money-back guarantee that magically “expires” the moment you need it. One mom from Ohio told me she was charged $497 for a “proven blueprint” that was literally a PDF of broken links.

**The Real Cost to You:**
- **The Course:** $497 – $2,497 (often non-refundable).
- **The Hidden Upsells:** That “free” coaching call? It’s a sales pitch for another $5,000 “inner circle.”
- **The Opportunity Cost:** The hours you spend trying to make his system work could be spent on an actual skill or savings account.

**💡 Your Wallet-Smart Move:**
1. **Freeze your credit card, not your dreams.** If a program promises you can make money while you sleep, ask why they need *your* money while *you’re* awake.
2. **Check the fine print.** Look for the word “refund.” If it says “discretionary,” run.
3. **The 24-Hour Rule.** If it feels like FOMO, wait one day
