**FACT CHECK: Viral Claim Says Ken Paxton Issued Emergency Order Banning All Voting Machines in Texas for 2024 Election**

**Rumor:** Social media posts claim Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton issued an "emergency order" banning all electronic voting machines statewide, requiring all ballots to be hand-counted for the upcoming 2024 election, citing "massive security breaches."

**Rating: 🚨 FALSE – Satire / Misleading**

**Details:**
- **No Official Order Found:** A comprehensive search of the Texas Attorney General’s official website, press releases, and court dockets reveals no such emergency order has been issued.
- **No Legal Authority:** Texas law does not grant the Attorney General unilateral power to ban voting machines across all 254 counties. Election administration is primarily handled by the Secretary of State and local county election officials.
- **Origin of Rumor:** The claim appears to have originated from a now-deleted satirical Facebook page impersonating a local news outlet. It has since been picked up by fringe conspiracy groups and reshared without context.
- **Paxton’s Actual Position:** While Paxton has been a vocal critic of electronic voting security in the past (advocating for paper trails and audits), he has **never** called for an outright ban on all machines or a statewide hand-count mandate.

**Verdict:** This is a fabricated story designed to inflame distrust in election integrity. **Do not share** — voting procedures in Texas remain unchanged.

**Bottom Line:** If you see this post, report it as false information. Verify all election-related claims through the Texas Secretary of State’s official voter portal.
