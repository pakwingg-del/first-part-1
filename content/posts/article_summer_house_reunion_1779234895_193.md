This house has been an absolute nightmare for our street. We've had to endure it all summer—bass so loud our windows shake, drunk people relieving themselves in our gardens, and parking that makes getting an ambulance down the road impossible. We have three young kids and an elderly neighbour with a heart condition.

Now the 'Summer House Reunion'? It’s just an excuse for another three-day bender. They brought in a literal marquee yesterday. A marquee! In a residential area! And don't get me started on the smoke machines. My house smells like a nightclub. The organisers were laughing when we asked them to move their generator away from our fence. 'It's just a bit of fun,' they said. Fun for who? Not the people paying the mortgage here.

I don't care who is famous. I don't care how much they spent on the catering. Common sense says if you want to party like a rockstar, buy a field in the middle of nowhere, not the house next door to families trying to sleep on a Tuesday night. This isn't jealousy, it's about basic respect and the right to a quiet life in your own home. I've already got the council on speed dial. #WakeUpNeighbours
