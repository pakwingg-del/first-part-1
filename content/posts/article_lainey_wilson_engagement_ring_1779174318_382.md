**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**NASHVILLE, Tenn. —** Country music artist Lainey Wilson has announced her engagement to former NFL player Devlin "Duck" Hodges, revealing a custom-designed diamond engagement ring estimated to be valued at over $200,000, according to industry experts.

**WHAT:** The ring, a brilliant-cut oval diamond set on a platinum band with a hidden halo of smaller diamonds, was presented to Wilson during a private ceremony at her family farm in Louisiana earlier this week. The gem, weighing approximately four carats, is described by jeweler sources as having "exceptional clarity and color," consistent with the artist's stated preference for "timeless elegance."

**WHO:** Lainey Wilson, 32, the five-time Academy of Country Music Award winner, and Devlin Hodges, 28, a former Pittsburgh Steelers quarterback who now works in real estate development in Alabama.

**WHEN:** The proposal occurred on Tuesday, May 14, 2024, during the couple's first trip to the property where Wilson was raised.

**WHERE:** The engagement took place at the Wilson family homestead in Baskin, Louisiana, a location the singer has frequently cited as her "creative ground zero."

**WHY:** Neither party has issued an official statement beyond a joint social media post captioned "Louisiana Love," but sources close to the couple indicate they have been planning the milestone for several months. The ring's design reportedly incorporates a small engraving of the Louisiana state flower — a magnolia — inside the band, further symbolizing the couple's shared roots in the South.

**FOR FURTHER INQUIRIES:**
Representatives for Ms. Wilson and Mr. Hodges have requested privacy regarding the specific details of the gem's provenance and final purchase price.
