**URGENT: "Satellite Image Shows Entire Mall Submerged Like a Ghost Ship" — FACT CHECK: Most viral clips are FAKE**

**The Viral Post:** A 30-second drone clip is racing across X (Twitter), TikTok, and Facebook, claiming to show the parking lot of a suburban shopping mall completely swallowed by murky floodwater, with submerged cars and a family stranded on the roof of the food court.

**The Claim:** "This is the flash flood at [Name of Mall] an hour ago. The entire food court is 20 feet underwater. Authorities confirm this is the worst disaster in a decade. Pray for [City]."

**🚨 The Reality (FACT-CHECK RESULT): FAKE & MISLEADING**

The footage in the viral post is **real flood footage** — but it is **misidentified and misdated**.

1.  **Wrong Location:** The mall shown is not the one named in the post. Forensic reverse-image searches reveal the drone footage is from **Hurricane Harvey in Houston (2017)** , or from a severe monsoon flood in **Bangkok (2022)** , not a recent "flash flood" event.
2.  **False Timeframe:** The rainfall that *did* trigger a legitimate flash flood warning in the area mentioned was only a **3-5 inch deluge** over two hours. While dangerous, it caused localized street flooding and basement water entry — *not* the apocalyptic submersion of a multi-story mall.
3.  **Authorities Did Not Say That:** The National Weather Service (NWS) and local emergency management have issued an official statement: "No major structural flooding of commercial buildings has been reported. The viral video is from an unrelated event."
