**BROKEN CODE: Karpathy's "Neural Network" Trained on Shakespeare Spits Out a CURSE that Matches a Forgotten Diary from 1598**

**SILICON VALLEY, CA** — In what researchers are calling the "most unsettling statistical anomaly since the invention of the hash function," a routine test by AI luminary **Andrej Karpathy** has produced a "glitch in the matrix" that has the open-source community both baffled and spooked.

Karpathy, known for his minimalist educational code (the famous "makemore" and "minGPT" repos), published the weights of a tiny, toy neural network trained exclusively on the complete works of William Shakespeare. It was a standard exercise in character-level language modeling.

But when a user on a niche Hacker News thread fed the model the prompt "What lies beneath the stage?", the network—which has no concept of time, reality, or authorship—output a single, cryptic line in perfect iambic pentameter:

*"The dirt be cold where John lies still and mute."*

The immediate shock? The name "John" appears exactly **once** in the entire Shakespeare canon (in *Henry IV*). The model had no reason to output it.

**The Matrix Break:**
Hours later, an anonymous user claimed a match. They uploaded a photo of a digitized, obscure Elizabethan diary entry from 1598—a record of a stagehand named **John Drayton** who died when a trapdoor latch failed during a performance of *Hamlet*. His body “fell to the cold dirt beneath the stage.”

The diary was scanned in 2021, but the file is a JPEG. The text is an image, not searchable text. The neural network **never** ingested this data.
