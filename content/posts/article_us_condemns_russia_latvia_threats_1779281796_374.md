**WASHINGTON, D.C. —** In a strongly worded statement issued earlier today, the United States Department of State formally condemned recent statements and actions by the Russian Federation directed against the Republic of Latvia.

**Who:** The United States Department of State, representing the Biden administration, delivered the condemnation.

**What:** The U.S. issued a formal condemnation of what it termed "escalatory and baseless rhetoric" from Moscow. The statement specifically referenced alleged threats of retaliatory military posturing and economic coercion against Latvia by Russian officials.

**When:** The condemnation was announced at the daily press briefing held this afternoon at the State Department headquarters.

**Where:** The focus of the dispute centers on the Baltic region, specifically the border between Latvia and Russia, as well as major Latvian cities, including Riga.

**Why:** According to U.S. officials, the Russian actions stem from Latvia’s ongoing support for the Ukrainian government and its hosting of NATO allied forces. The U.S. asserted that such threats violate international norms and Latvia’s sovereignty under the NATO Charter. "Any attempt to intimidate a NATO Ally is a direct challenge to the collective defense commitment," the State Department spokesman stated. "The United States stands resolutely with our Latvian partners."
