**DFW AIRPORT GROUND STOP ISSUED DUE TO SEVERE WEATHER DISRUPTION**

**DALLAS-FORT WORTH, TX —** The Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) has issued a full ground stop for all departures and arrivals at Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport (DFW) effective as of 14:30 Central Standard Time.

**WHAT:** A complete ground stop has been enacted at DFW, halting all inbound and outbound commercial and general aviation traffic. No aircraft are permitted to push back from gates or land at the facility until further notice.

**WHO:** The order was issued by the FAA's Air Traffic Control System Command Center (ATCSCC) due to a confluence of hazardous weather conditions and airspace congestion. The directive impacts all carriers operating out of DFW, including American Airlines, which maintains its largest hub at the airport.

**WHEN:** The ground stop was implemented at approximately 14:30 CST. According to FAA advisories, the initial estimated duration of the ground stop is two hours, with a re-evaluation scheduled for 16:30 CST. However, officials warn that the timeline is subject to change based on the weather's trajectory.

**WHERE:** The disruption is localized to DFW International Airport and its immediate terminal radar approach control (TRACON) airspace. Regional airports such as Dallas Love Field (DAL) and Fort Worth Meacham International (FTW) are not currently under a ground stop, though travelers are advised to monitor for potential ripple effects.

**WHY:** Official FAA statements cite a severe line of thunderstorms and associated wind shear moving through the North Texas corridor as the primary cause. The National Weather Service has issued a Severe Thunderstorm Warning for Dallas County, indicating the potential for 60 mph winds, quarter-sized hail, and frequent lightning, which poses a direct hazard to ground operations and aircraft movement.
