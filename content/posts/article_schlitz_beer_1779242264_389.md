**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**Anchor:** In a move that has sent ripples through the beverage industry, the Joseph Schlitz Brewing Company has announced a strategic corporate restructure, pivoting from its historic mass-market identity to a limited-release craft premium portfolio. Effective immediately, the company’s flagship 12-ounce can will be discontinued in favor of an aged, barrel-fermented variant.

**What:** The complete cessation of standard Schlitz production is scheduled for Q3 of this fiscal year. The new product line, designated “Schlitz Heritage Series,” will be produced in batches not exceeding 10,000 cases.

**Who:** The decision was confirmed by CEO Marcus Thorne in a press release issued from the company’s Milwaukee headquarters. The new portfolio will be distributed exclusively through independent retailers and high-end gastropubs.

**When:** The final shipment of legacy Schlitz will leave warehouses on September 30, 2025. The Heritage Series debut is slated for November 1, 2025.

**Where:** Distribution of the new line is initially limited to the states of Wisconsin, Illinois, and Michigan, with a nationwide rollout contingent on consumer demand metrics.

**Why:** According to internal documents obtained by this network, the decision is driven by a 73% decline in legacy sales over the past decade. The company has stated that market research indicates a demand for “authenticity and terroir” among drinkers aged 25 to 40, a demographic that has historically rejected the brand’s mass-market image.

**Analysis:** Analysts on Wall Street have responded with cautious neutrality. Standard & Poor’s has placed Schlitz’s credit rating on watch pending the success of the first Heritage Series batch. The move is widely seen as a high-stakes gamble to avoid the brand’s complete obsolescence in a market dominated by international conglomerates and hyper-local microbreweries.
