**WARNING: CBP Issues ‘Memorial Day Moral Hazard’ Alert – Flags Over Funerals?**  

**Washington, D.C.** – In a stunning departure from traditional travel advisories, U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) issued a “Moral Integrity Warning” ahead of Memorial Day weekend, urging Americans to consider the *spiritual consequences* of their holiday plans. The alert, which has since gone viral, claims that “heedless leisure travel on a day of military mourning” is contributing to the “rapid erosion of national conscience.”  

“Memorial Day is not a beach day,” the statement reads in part. “It is a day to stand in silence for the fallen, not to stand in line at airport TSA checkpoints while flag-draped coffins are still being unloaded at Dover.” The agency cites a “disturbing 400% spike” in social media posts showing BBQs, pool parties, and shopping sprees on the same day families are laying soldiers to rest.  

Critics are calling the warning “grotesque bureaucratic overreach,” while supporters insist it’s a necessary gut-check. “We have become a nation that treats its martyrs as an inconvenience to our vacation schedule,” said one retired Army chaplain, who praised CBP for “finally naming the moral rot.”  

The travel industry is in turmoil, with some airlines offering “reflection refunds” and others doubling down on patriotic promotions. Meanwhile, viral hashtags like #HonorBeforeFun and #NoBeachForBlood are splitting the nation along a new fault line: is this a sincere call to conscience, or a government-sponsored guilt trip that commercializes the very grief it claims to protect?  

One thing is certain: this Memorial Day, the only thing longer than the lines at the beach may be the shadow of a question no one wants to answer.
