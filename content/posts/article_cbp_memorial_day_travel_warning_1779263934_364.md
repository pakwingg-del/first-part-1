**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**CBP Issues Urgent Travel Advisory Ahead of Memorial Day Weekend**

**WASHINGTON, D.C. –** U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) has issued a formal travel warning for the upcoming Memorial Day holiday period, anticipated to be one of the busiest travel weekends of the year.

**WHO:** U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) officials.

**WHAT:** An official warning regarding significant delays and increased security measures at all ports of entry. CBP is advising travelers to prepare for extended wait times, particularly at the nation's busiest land border crossings and international airports. The agency has deployed additional resources to facilitate processing, but warns that volumes will exceed standard capacity.

**WHEN:** The advisory is in effect from Thursday, May 25, through Monday, May 29, encompassing the entire Memorial Day weekend.

**WHERE:** All U.S. ports of entry, including land borders with Canada and Mexico, and international airports.

**WHY:** The warning is based on projections of a record number of travelers returning from abroad, combined with the enforcement of federal immigration laws and security screening protocols. CBP cited the need to maintain "operational integrity and public safety" as the primary reason.

**HOW:** Travelers are advised to utilize CBP’s "CBP One" mobile application for real-time wait times at land ports. All travelers must ensure they possess valid, compliant travel documents, including a U.S. passport or Trusted Traveler Program card (Global Entry, NEXUS, SENTRI). Failure to produce proper documentation will result in significant processing delays. Additionally, CBP reminds all travelers that agricultural items and certain goods remain strictly prohibited.
