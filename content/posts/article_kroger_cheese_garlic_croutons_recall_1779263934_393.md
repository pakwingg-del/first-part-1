**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Kroger Cheese Garlic Croutons Recall**

- **Undeclared Allergen Crisis:** The recall was triggered because the croutons may contain *milk* (cheese) that isn't listed on the "Garlic" flavor label. This poses a serious risk for anyone with dairy allergies or lactose intolerance.

- **Affected Products Only:** The recall is specifically for **Private Selection** brand *Cheese Garlic* croutons (sold at Kroger, Fry's, Smith's, and other Kroger-owned stores). Other flavors like plain or seasoned are **not** affected—check the bag, not the bin.

- **Best-By Date Clue:** Look for the **"Best By" date of July 2, 2025** printed on the back of the bag. Only croutons with this exact date are involved; other dates are safe to eat.

- **Do Not Eat – Return or Toss:** If you have the recalled croutons, stop using them immediately. You can return them to any Kroger store for a full refund. If you've already eaten them and experience symptoms like hives, stomach cramps, or difficulty breathing, see a doctor immediately.

- **Milk Allergy Alert:** This recall is a life-or-death warning for milk-allergic individuals. Even trace amounts of cheese (from the label mix-up) can trigger severe, life-threatening reactions. Spread the word to anyone who shops Kroger—it's not just a texture issue, it's a health emergency.
