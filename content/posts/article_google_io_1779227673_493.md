**Consumer Alert: Google Just Made Your Charging Cables Obsolete – And It’s Going to Cost You.**

At Google I/O, the tech giant unveiled its new charging standard, **Google Fast Pair 2.0 Power Delivery** – and while it sounds fancy, for your wallet, it’s a nightmare.

Here’s what you need to know: **All your existing USB-C cables, including expensive ones you bought for your laptop, are now "legacy."** The new protocol requires a special, thinner chip inside the cable to prevent overheating and support the new 240W charging speed. That means the $20 cable you bought last month? Useless for the next Pixel or Galaxy phone.

**The Wallet Hit:** Expect to pay **$35 to $50 per cable** for the next year as manufacturers scramble to retool. Worse, Google confirmed old power bricks won't fast-charge the new devices, forcing you to buy new bricks, too.

**The Catch:** If you plug a new phone into an old cable to charge quickly, it will *limit* the charging speed to 15W – turning your "fast charger" into a slow trickle. You won't get the advertised speed unless you buy new gear.

**Action Step:** **Panic buy? No.** Wait 6 months. But do NOT throw away your old cables – use them for slow overnight charging. If you need a new cable right now for your current device, buy the cheapest one possible. Prices are about to crash on old stock, and the new standard isn't worth the premium until 2026.

**Bottom Line:** Google just built in planned obsolescence for your accessories. Your wallet just took a direct hit – and they didn't even warn you. 📉
