The goal is to lower U.S. prices by 40-60% while making domestic production profitable.

Any pill or injection without a verifiable domestic or allied supply chain signature would be banned from interstate commerce, with penalties of up to 5% of a company's global revenue.

Big Pharma’s reaction is immediate, with lawsuits claiming it violates WTO trade agreements, though supporters argue national security overrides trade law.
