**HEADLINE:** Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton Files Emergency Motion to Halt Federal Agency Rule

**DATE:** February 20, 2025

**LOCATION:** Austin, Texas

**REPORTER:** [Your Name], National News Desk

**SUMMARY:**

Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton has filed an emergency motion in the United States District Court for the Eastern District of Texas, seeking an immediate injunction to block a newly enacted federal agency rule.

**WHO:** The legal action was filed by Attorney General Ken Paxton, representing the State of Texas. The motion names the federal agency, the United States Department of Labor, as the defendant.

**WHAT:** The emergency motion requests a Temporary Restraining Order and Preliminary Injunction to halt the implementation of the Department of Labor’s "Fairness in Retirement Planning" rule. The State of Texas alleges the rule imposes unconstitutional fiduciary requirements on financial advisors, violating the First Amendment by restricting their ability to provide objective financial advice.

**WHEN:** The motion was filed late Tuesday evening. The rule is scheduled to take effect on March 1, 2025.

**WHERE:** The emergency action was taken at the U.S. District Court for the Eastern District of Texas, Tyler Division.

**WHY:** Attorney General Paxton argues the rule exceeds the Department of Labor's statutory authority and would cause immediate, irreparable harm to millions of Texas retirees and small business owners by reducing access to affordable financial guidance.

**OUTCOME:** The court has not yet issued a ruling on the motion. A hearing is expected within the next 48 hours.
