**HEADLINE: MORAL ABYSS: Cedar Point’s “Millennium Force” Branded a “Satanic Seducer of Youth” by Watchdog Group**

**CLEVELAND, OH** — In a fiery press conference held outside the gates of Cedar Point amusement park, the newly formed watchdog coalition *American Moral Fortitude* (AMF) declared the park’s flagship roller coaster, Millennium Force, a "direct assault on the moral fabric of the republic."

Citing a 243-page report, AMF Chairwoman Dr. Esther Holloway argued that the coaster’s 300-foot drop is not a marvel of engineering, but a "planned descent into hedonistic chaos." She pointed to the ride’s name, which she claims is a reference to the "false millennium" of the Book of Revelation—a "temptation engineered to lure the faithful into a state of artificial euphoria."

"Our youth are being taught that the pinnacle of human experience is a 90-second dopamine spike achieved through a mechanical demon," Dr. Holloway told reporters, clutching an oversized, illustrated diagram of the ride’s queue. "Gravity is a gift from God, not a tool for man-made rapture. We are stitching a culture of instant gratification and thrill-seeking into the souls of tomorrow's leaders."

The group, backed by several prominent local pastors, has called for an immediate "moral audit" of all "hyper-coaster technology," and has demanded that Cedar Point install reflective prayer booths at the exit ramp to allow riders to "atone for their reckless pursuit of G-force." Supporters are already circulating a petition to rename the ride "Millennial Forbearance," though Cedar Point has not commented on the request.
