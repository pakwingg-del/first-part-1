**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**TALLAHASSEE, FL — August 12, 2025** — A prominent seafood restaurant chain location has permanently closed its doors, leaving dozens of employees and loyal patrons in search of answers.

**What:** The Red Lobster restaurant at 2625 North Monroe Street has officially ceased operations, with the location now shuttered and signs removed from the building.

**Who:** The casual dining chain, owned by Thai Union Group as of the most recent financial restructuring, operated this specific franchise location for over two decades. The closure impacts an estimated 45 to 60 hourly and salaried employees.

**When:** The restaurant served its last meal on the evening of Sunday, August 10, 2025. Employees received notice of the immediate closure via a recorded message and posted notice at the venue earlier this week.

**Where:** Located at the corner of North Monroe Street and Tharpe Street, the restaurant was a fixture in the North Tallahassee commercial corridor, near the Tallahassee Mall and Interstate 10 interchange.

**Why:** According to corporate communications reviewed by this station, the closure is attributed to a combination of factors including unsustainable lease renegotiations, ongoing supply chain cost pressures, and a dramatic decline in foot traffic following recent road construction in the area. The chain has not announced plans for a new Tallahassee location, leaving the state capital without a corporate Red Lobster outlet.

The affected employees have been directed to the company's human resources hotline for information on severance and transfer opportunities. The closed property is currently being listed for commercial lease by its landlord, North Monroe Properties LLC.
