**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About Google I/O 2024**

A look at the biggest announcements from this year's developer conference that are already changing how we interact with search, memory, and the world around us.

- **Google Search Gets a "Circle to Search" Upgrade for Homework:** Beyond just identifying objects, you can now circle complex math problems or physics diagrams directly on your Android screen. Google will not just answer the question, but provide step-by-step explanations, making your phone a full-blown tutor.

- **Gemini Nano is Here to Answer Your Voicemail:** The on-device AI model is rolling out to the Pixel 8 line. You can now have it listen to voicemails and provide a full, detailed summary or even offer a suggested "smart reply" without your phone ever connecting to the cloud.

- **Project Astra: A Real-Time AI Assistant That "Sees":** Google demoed a terrifyingly smart new AI prototype that can see and understand your environment through your phone’s camera. Ask "Where did I leave my keys?" while you walk around a messy room, and it will memorize the last location it saw them. It’s like having a photographic memory for your AI.

- **Voice Engine is Now Live for Google Maps:** The new, hyper-realistic voice assistant (dubbed "Lens Live") is rolling out to everyone in Google Maps. It uses generative AI to listen to your surroundings and tell you, "Turn left at the blue awning" instead of a cold street name, making navigation feel like a conversation.

- **Photos Can Now "Magically Edit" Your Memories Straight:** The "Ask Photos" feature is a game-changer. You can type, "What was the name of that restaurant in Paris where we had the crème brûlée?" and Google Photos will analyze your trip, find the specific photo, and answer your question based on the image’s metadata.
