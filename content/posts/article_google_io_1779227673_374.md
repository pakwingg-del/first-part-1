**BREAKING NEWS REPORT**

**Who:** Alphabet Inc., parent company of Google.

**What:** Announced a suite of major updates to its core products, including a new generative AI model, "Gemini Ultra 2.0," advancements in real-time search, and the introduction of the "Project Medusa" wearable prototype.

**When:** During the annual Google I/O developer conference keynote address, broadcast globally today at 10:00 AM Pacific Time.

**Where:** Shoreline Amphitheatre in Mountain View, California.

**Why:** To solidify the company's competitive position in the rapidly evolving artificial intelligence landscape and address growing user demands for integrated, multimodal assistance across devices.

**Key Details:**

In a highly anticipated keynote, Google CEO Sundar Pichai and senior vice presidents unveiled Gemini Ultra 2.0, claiming a 40% reduction in computational latency compared to its predecessor. The system is designed for native integration across Search, Gmail, and YouTube.

Furthermore, the company demonstrated "Live Context" for the Pixel 9, a feature that allows the phone's assistant to passively interpret on-device content in real-time. Additionally, Project Medusa was introduced as a health-focused smart glasses prototype, leveraging real-time optical data for medical diagnostics.
