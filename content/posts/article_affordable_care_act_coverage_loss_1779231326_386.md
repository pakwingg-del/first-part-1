**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**ANCHOR:** "Good evening. A new federal report reveals a significant decline in enrollment under the Affordable Care Act, with more than 500,000 Americans losing their health coverage in the first quarter of 2025 alone. Here is our report."

**(GRAPHIC: "ACA COVERAGE LOSS: 500,000+ GONE IN Q1")**

**REPORT:**

In a development raising concerns among healthcare analysts, the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services has confirmed a sharp contraction in marketplace plan enrollment. According to the data, the net loss of approximately 520,000 covered lives occurred between January and March of this year.

**Who:** The affected population consists primarily of individuals who previously qualified for subsidized private plans through the federal exchange, healthcare.gov, as well as state-based marketplaces.

**What:** These individuals have experienced a termination or non-renewal of their health insurance policies under the Affordable Care Act, commonly known as Obamacare. The primary driver cited by officials is procedural, involving a large-scale eligibility redetermination process.

**Where:** The losses are concentrated in states that rely on the federal platform for enrollment, though significant reductions have also been recorded in state-run marketplaces. No single demographic or geographic area has been spared, but initial analysis indicates a disproportionate impact on lower-income households.

**When:** The coverage losses became effective throughout the period from January 1, 2025, to March 31, 2025. Reports indicate this is a continuation of a trend observed in the final quarter of 2024.

**Why:** Federal officials attribute the decline to the resumption of strict eligibility verification procedures. During the public health emergency, continuous enrollment was mandated. With those rules expired, insurers and marketplaces are now required to verify income and citizenship status annually.
