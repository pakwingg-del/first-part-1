**CLAIM: A viral Facebook post claims Red Lobster’s Tallahassee location on Apalachee Parkway has permanently closed because “the only thing left on the menu was endless shrimp and bad credit.”**  

**FACT CHECK: FALSE (with a twist of marketing hype).**  

The post, which has been shared over 4,000 times, includes a grainy photo of a “Closed” sign taped to the restaurant’s door and a caption blaming the closure on the chain’s infamous “Endless Shrimp” promotion driving it into bankruptcy. However, a check with the Florida Department of Business Regulation shows the Tallahassee location is still operating under a valid license. The photo is actually from a 2022 temporary closure during a kitchen renovation.  

**What’s really happening:**  
Red Lobster’s parent company, Thai Union, did file for Chapter 11 bankruptcy in May 2024, citing $1.2 billion in debt partially tied to the “Endless Shrimp” loss leader. But the Tallahassee restaurant remains open—and is currently running a “Surf & Turf Revival” promotion to boost sales.  

**The verdict:**  
The post is misleading. While Red Lobster’s financial struggles are real, the Tallahassee closure rumor is a recycled image with a fake backstory. **Verdict: Fake news, but it points to a real corporate crisis.** Share responsibly.
