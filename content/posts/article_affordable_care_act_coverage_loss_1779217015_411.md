**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the ACA 'Coverage Cliff' Crisis**

As millions brace for the end of enhanced subsidies, here is what you need to know right now:

- **The "Coverage Cliff" is Real.** Enhanced subsidies (put in place by the Biden administration and the Inflation Reduction Act) are set to expire at the end of 2025. If Congress does not act, premiums for middle-income families could skyrocket overnight—in some cases, jumping from $0 to over $1,200 per month. This is the largest disruption to ACA enrollment since the 2017 tax bill.

- **13 Million People Could Lose Everything.** The Congressional Budget Office (CBO) projects that 13 million Americans will lose their health insurance if the subsidies lapse. This is not just a "spike in costs"—it's a full-on expulsion from coverage for those just above the poverty line.

- **The "Subsidy Cliff" Hits the Middle Class Hardest.** The current system is designed to cap premiums at 8.5% of income. Without the extension, families earning just $45,000 a year could see their monthly bills triple, forcing them to drop coverage entirely. This is the demographic that voted on this issue in swing states.

- **Insurance Companies Are Already Sounding the Alarm.** Major carriers are warning that a sudden drop in enrollment will force them to raise premiums for everyone *else* in the individual market by 10-15% in 2026. This creates a "death spiral" scenario where only the sickest (and most expensive) patients stay insured.

- **The Clock is Ticking on a Bipartisan Fix.** A group of moderate Democrats and Republicans have introduced the "Inflation Reduction Act Extension Act," but it faces a steep uphill battle in a polarized Congress.
