**BREAKING NEWS: HBO ANNOUNCES GLOBAL RECASTING FOR HARRY POTTER TELEVISION SERIES**

**LONDON, UK — December 12, 2024** — In a major development for the entertainment industry, HBO has officially confirmed a comprehensive recasting of the principal characters for its upcoming television adaptation of the Harry Potter book series. The announcement was made earlier today by network executives during a press conference at Warner Bros. Studios.

**Who:** HBO and Warner Bros. Discovery have announced a complete recasting of the iconic role of Harry Potter, alongside the characters of Hermione Granger and Ron Weasley. No casting decisions have been made regarding adult supporting roles, including Professor Albus Dumbledore, Severus Snape, or Lord Voldemort.

**What:** The production will reboot the entire seven-book saga over a projected decade-long series, featuring an entirely new ensemble cast. A global open casting call has been initiated specifically for the three lead roles, seeking actors aged between 9 and 11 years old. The show is described as a "faithful, book-accurate adaptation" intended to explore deeper narrative arcs and character development limited by the original film runtime.

**When:** Filming is scheduled to commence in early 2026 at Warner Bros. Studios, Leavesden, in the United Kingdom. The series is expected to debut on HBO and its streaming platform, Max, in late 2026 or early 2027, with each season covering one book.

**Where:** The production will be based at the original Harry Potter film studios in Leavesden, England. The global casting search will take place across the United Kingdom and Ireland, with open submissions beginning next month.
