**ANCHOR:** A new era of artificial intelligence has been unveiled. Google has officially launched its next-generation AI model at its annual developer conference, Google I/O. The question now is not *if* AI will change the way we live, but *how fast*.

**REPORT:**

**GOOGLE I/O 2024: GEMINI ERA BEGINS WITH AMBITIOUS AI INTEGRATION**

**MOUNTAIN VIEW, CA** – At the Shoreline Amphitheatre on Wednesday, Google CEO Sundar Pichai took the stage to announce a comprehensive overhaul of the company's product ecosystem, powered by the full deployment of its latest AI model, Gemini.

**What happened?**
Google introduced "Gemini 1.5 Pro" with a context window capable of processing up to 10 million tokens, alongside "Project Astra," a prototype for a universal AI assistant designed to understand and interact with the real world in real-time via a smartphone camera. The company also unveiled "Veo," a new video generation model that competes directly with OpenAI's Sora, and "Trillium," its sixth-generation TPU chip designed to accelerate AI training.

**Who is affected?**
Every user of Google’s core services—including Search, Gmail, Photos, and Android—will see immediate changes. The "AI Overviews" feature, which provides summarized answers at the top of search results, is now rolling out to all U.S. users. Android users will gain the "Circle to Search" capability, allowing them to solve complex math equations or identify products with a simple gesture.

**When did this occur?**
The announcements were made during the keynote address on the first day of Google I/O, which commenced at 10:00 AM Pacific Time on Wednesday, May 15, 2024.
