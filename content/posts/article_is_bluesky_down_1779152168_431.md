**Subject: BlueSky Outage**

**Headline:** BlueSky Down: The Decentralization Dilemma Strikes

**Snippet:** BlueSky, the much-touted decentralized alternative to X, suffered a significant outage today, raising critical questions about the reliability of the AT Protocol. For nearly two hours, users were unable to post or access feeds. While the platform promotes independence from centralized control, this incident highlights a hard reality for business users: decentralization does not eliminate single points of failure, it just relocates them.

**Key Takeaways for Leadership:**
- **Reliability Risk:** For brands and executives building communities on BlueSky, downtime is a direct revenue and engagement threat.
- **Portability vs. Performance:** The AT Protocol’s promise of account portability is useless if the network is unavailable. This is a multi-million dollar lesson in infrastructure maturity.
- **Market Signal:** Watch for a user exodus back to X or Threads if reliability does not improve. This is a critical week for BlueSky’s enterprise adoption narrative.

**Verdict:** BlueSky is back up, but the damage to its credibility as a business-grade platform is very real. Do not bet your comms strategy on a fragile stack.
