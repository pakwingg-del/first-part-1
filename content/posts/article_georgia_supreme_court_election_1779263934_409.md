**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Georgia Supreme Court Election**

- **A Strangely High-Stakes Down-Ballot Race:** While often overlooked, Georgia’s Supreme Court elections have become proxies for the state’s massive political divide. The current seat up for grabs is nonpartisan on paper, but millions of dollars in dark money are pouring in from national groups looking to shape the court’s future rulings on abortion, voting access, and redistricting.
- **The Incumbent is a Major Target:** Justice Andrew Pinson, appointed by Governor Brian Kemp, is facing a fierce challenge from former prosecutor John Barrow. Barrow is running as a centrist focused on judicial independence, but his campaign has been heavily funded by progressive PACs attacking Pinson as a “rubber stamp” for the GOP-led legislature.
- **The “Swing Vote” Nightmare:** Georgia’s Supreme Court has a clear 6-3 conservative majority. However, recent high-profile cases have hinged on a single justice’s defection. This election could determine whether the court shifts even further right or creates a new, unpredictable swing vote on abortion and election law.
- **The Issue Nobody Talks About: Recusals:** A huge flashpoint is whether justices should step aside from cases involving their biggest donors. Barrow is making “judicial transparency” his signature issue, arguing that Pinson should recuse himself from cases argued by the state’s Republican Attorney General, who helped get him elected.
- **The Polls Are a Nightmare:** This is a low-turnout, unpredictable election. Neither candidate is a household name, and the outcome will likely be decided by which party’s base is more motivated to show up. Expect a photo finish—and a likely call for a recount before the results are certified.
