**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Georgia Supreme Court Election**

- **The Make-or-Break Majority:** This isn't just any judicial race—it’s the final battlefield for control of the state's highest court. A win for the conservative-leaning incumbent would preserve a 6-3 ideological majority, while a victory for the Democratic-backed challenger would flip the court to a 5-4 liberal tilt, reshaping appellate law for a generation.

- **The "Spoiler" Effect is Real:** A third-party candidate, a former prosecutor running as an independent, is polling at 7%. In a state decided by 11,779 votes in 2020, every ballot could be the difference. The independent has explicitly stated they plan to rule against traditional partisan lines, but their mere presence could siphon votes from the challenger.

- **The Dark Money Firestorm:** Over $4.2 million has been poured into this race by outside groups, more than any prior Georgia Supreme Court contest. The ads are gnarly: one spot accuses the incumbent of "letting polluters off the hook" in a toxic waste case, while another claims the challenger has "never defended a child victim." Fact-checkers have flagged both as misleading.

- **Abortion & Voting Rights are Collateral Damage:** This court will likely rule on the state’s six-week "heartbeat" abortion ban within 18 months. The challenger has publicly called the law "devastating," while the incumbent refuses to comment on "future litigation." Both are signaling how they'd handle a contentious gerrymander case that could decide Georgia's 2026 congressional map.
