**Breaking: Viral Rumor or Real Alert? CBP “Memorial Day Travel Warning” Sparks Confusion**

**The Claim:** A post circulating widely on social media warns that U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) has issued an “official Memorial Day travel warning” advising Americans to avoid all non-essential travel on highways within 50 miles of the U.S.-Mexico and U.S.-Canada borders due to “elevated terror threats” and “mass migration events.”

**VERDICT: FALSE (Misleading / Manufactured Alert)**

**The Facts:**
1. **No Official CBP Warning:** As of this morning, CBP has not issued any such generalized travel warning. Their official holiday advisories focus on normal operational tips: expect delays at ports of entry, ensure you have proper ID (like a passport or Global Entry card), and declare agricultural items.
2. **The “50-Mile Zone” Myth:** While CBP does have special statutory authority within 100 miles of the border, this does *not* translate to a blanket public travel ban or warning for holiday travelers.
3. **Origins of the Hoax:** The rumor appears to be a remix of an old, debunked 2022 post that claimed CBP was setting up “checkpoints” on major highways. That post was shared to cause panic and disrupt holiday travel.
4. **What IS Real:** CBP has warned that heavy traffic is expected at the San Ysidro and Otay Mesa ports of entry. Wait times could exceed 3 hours. This is standard seasonal information, not a “warning to stay away.”

**Bottom Line:** Ignore the doomsday travel alerts. The only CBP-related “warning” you need is to check border wait times before you drive. The rest is fiction designed to go viral.
