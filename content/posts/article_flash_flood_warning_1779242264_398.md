**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About This Flash Flood Warning**

- **It's a "Turn Around, Don't Drown" Situation** – Six inches of fast-moving water can knock over an adult. A foot of water can sweep away a small car. The number one cause of flash flood deaths is people driving into flooded roads. Do not attempt to cross barriers—they are there for a reason.
- **Hospitals and Emergency Shelters on High Alert** – Local authorities are pre-positioning swift-water rescue teams and opening temporary shelters in high-risk zones. If your home is in a low-lying area, charge your devices, move valuables to upper floors, and be ready to evacuate at a moment's notice.
- **Expect Sudden Road Closures and Hail** – The National Weather Service warns this storm system is dropping rain at rates of 2 inches per hour. Roads that seem clear now may be impassable in 15 minutes. Some areas are also reporting penny-sized hail, which can cause slippery, hazardous conditions.
- **Power Outages Are Likely** – High winds are accompanying the torrential rain. Downed power lines and transformer explosions have already been reported in neighboring counties. Have flashlights, batteries, and a battery-powered radio on hand—cell service may be spotty during the worst of the storm.
- **This Warning Expires at [Time/Date]** – Flash flood warnings are typically short-duration (a few hours). Stay tuned to local NOAA weather radio or your phone's emergency alerts for updates. The danger doesn't end instantly when the rain stops—water levels can take hours to recede, and hidden hazards like washed-out bridges may remain.
