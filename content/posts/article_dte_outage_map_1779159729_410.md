**TOP 5 THINGS YOU NEED TO KNOW ABOUT THE DTE OUTAGE MAP CRISIS**

- **The Map is Down, But the Anger is Up:** DTE's real-time outage map is currently glitching or completely offline for thousands of users, leaving frustrated customers in the dark—literally. Instead of showing where crews are, users are seeing blank screens or "no data available," fueling a wave of panic and misinformation on social media.

- **Unprecedented Storm Surge Blamed:** DTE cites a "historic" combo of severe thunderstorms and high winds (gusts up to 70 mph) as the cause, but critics say the utility's aging infrastructure is the real culprit. Insiders claim the map's server farm was overwhelmed by a record 300,000+ simultaneous outage reports—something it wasn't built to handle.

- **The "Ghost Outage" Phenomenon is Real:** Dozens of neighborhoods are reporting restored power on the map, but their lights remain off. Conversely, some areas still showing red "outage zones" have had power for hours. This data mismatch has led to confused residents skipping hot meals or driving to lit gas stations only to find them dark.

- **Hackers or Glitch? DTE Won't Say:** A cybersecurity expert tweeted that the outage map downtime might be linked to a "distributed denial-of-service attack" (DDoS) aimed at overloading the system. DTE has not confirmed, but tech influencers are urging customers to "not trust the map until further notice."

- **What You Must Do Right Now:** Ignore the map entirely. Call DTE's backup hotline (1-800-477-4747) or log into your online account to report an outage manually. Download a flashlight app and check on neighbors—especially elderly or medically dependent. DTE says a "simplified text-based map" might be live by 8 PM, but no promises.
