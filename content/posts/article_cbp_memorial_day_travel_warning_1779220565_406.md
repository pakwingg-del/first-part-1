**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About CBP's Urgent Memorial Day Travel Warning**

- **Unprecedented Wait Times at Southern Border Crossings:** CBP warns of delays up to 2-3 hours or more at major ports of entry (e.g., San Ysidro, El Paso, Nogales) as staffing shortages collide with the holiday surge. Officials are urging travelers to avoid peak hours (10 AM–4 PM) or consider alternative crossings.

- **New "Digital Passport" Requirement for Non-U.S. Citizens:** Starting May 27, all foreign nationals entering the U.S. via land or sea must present a verified digital travel authorization (via the CBP One app) or face immediate denial. The rule is already causing confusion and last-minute scrambles.

- **Zero-Tolerance Enforcement for Agricultural Violations:** CBP is deploying K-9 units and X-ray scanners at all checkpoints—not just for drugs, but for fresh fruits, meats, and plants. Travelers bringing unauthorized food or souvenirs from abroad face fines up to $10,000 or criminal charges.

- **Drone Surveillance Over Popular Memorial Day Destinations:** In a controversial move, CBP will deploy drones over high-traffic areas (beaches, national parks, festivals) to monitor "suspicious activity." Privacy advocates are up in arms, but CBP claims it's for safety and counter-terrorism.

- **Emergency "Fast Lane" Pass for Returning U.S. Citizens:** To ease congestion, CBP is launching a temporary program allowing U.S. citizens to pre-register for a "Memorial Day Fast Lane" via the CBP One app, waiving standard procedures. However, the system has already crashed twice due to overwhelming demand—expect glitches.
