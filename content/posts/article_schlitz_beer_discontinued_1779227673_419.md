**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About Schlitz Beer’s Final Tap**

- **The "Beer That Made Milwaukee Famous" Is No More**  
  Schlitz, a legendary American lager that dominated the 1950s as the top-selling beer in the U.S., has been officially discontinued after a slow, decades-long decline. The brand's iconic brown bottle and slogan are now relics of a bygone era.

- **A Perfect Storm of Bad Decisions Did It In**  
  The downfall started in the 1970s when Schlitz cut costs by switching to cheaper ingredients and a high-speed brewing process, sparking a scandal when bottles turned cloudy or fizzed over like exploding volcanoes. Consumer trust never recovered.

- **The Brand Secretly Lived On as a "Ghost Beer"**  
  After production ceased in the U.S., Schlitz continued as a budget brew under contract brewers—until now. The final batches were quietly brewed for regional distributors, but those taps have run dry.

- **Collectors Are Freaking Out Over Vintage Cans**  
  In a bizarre twist, empty Schlitz cans from the 1960s and '70s are now selling for hundreds on eBay, turning a failed brand into a lucrative nostalgia artifact. Some bars are even hosting "Schlitz wake" parties with remaining kegs.

- **Why This Matters In 2025**  
  Schlitz’s extinction signals a cultural shift: craft beer, seltzers, and nostalgia for small-batch, not mass-produced, brews have officially killed the "king of beers" from America’s industrial heyday. The only thing left? The slogan.
