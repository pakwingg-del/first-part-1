**Viral News Snippet: Calvin Klein’s “AI Model” Ad Sparks Outrage – Real or Fake?**  

**Headline:** *“Calvin Klein Replaces Human Models with AI-Generated Influencers – But Is It Real?”*  

**The Claim:** A series of hyper-realistic ads circulating on Instagram and TikTok show Calvin Klein’s latest campaign featuring a flawless, ethereal model with impossibly smooth skin and a single, eerily consistent shadow. The caption reads: “The future of fashion is here. No humans were harmed (or paid) in the making of this shoot.” The posts have racked up millions of views, with users accusing the brand of “killing the modeling industry” and “promoting unrealistic beauty standards from a machine.” Some even claim the model’s eye color changes between frames—a telltale sign of AI generation.  

**What We Found:**  
- **Fake.** Calvin Klein has not officially launched an AI-only campaign. The viral images are from a verified parody account (@futurefashion_fail) that uses deepfake technology and text-to-image tools like Midjourney to mock luxury brands.  
- **Real context:** A separate, real Calvin Klein campaign (for their new “Reclaim” denim line) does feature one digital avatar, but it’s clearly labeled as a “virtual ambassador” and was created with consent from a real human model whose likeness was scanned.  
- **Why it spread:** The fake ads tapped into genuine anxiety about AI replacing jobs, with users reposting without checking the source. Calvin Klein’s silence on social media fueled the fire—though the brand later confirmed the viral posts were “unaffiliated and unauthorized.”
