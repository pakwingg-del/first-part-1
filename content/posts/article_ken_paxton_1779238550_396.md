**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Ken Paxton Impeachment Fallout**

- **The Historic Impeachment:** In May 2023, Ken Paxton became only the third sitting official in Texas history to be impeached. The Republican-controlled Texas House voted 121-23 to suspend him, citing bribery, abuse of office, and obstruction of justice. This wasn't a partisan hit—it was his own party turning on him.

- **The Alleged "Love Nest" Scandal:** The accusations center around a $3.3 million whistleblower settlement Paxton allegedly tried to hide. Prosecutors claim he used his office to help a wealthy donor, Nate Paul, in exchange for a job for a woman Paxton was reportedly having an extramarital affair with. Think: a real estate empire, a secret romance, and a corrupt cover-up.

- **The Senate Acquittal (But He's Not Out Yet):** In September 2023, the Texas Senate voted to acquit Paxton on all 16 articles of impeachment. However, the drama didn't end there. He was reinstated as Attorney General, but he immediately faced renewed calls for a federal investigation into his campaign finance violations and potential witness tampering.

- **The Ongoing Legal Quagmire:** Paxton is still under active investigation by the FBI for the same allegations that led to his impeachment. He also faces a separate SEC lawsuit for allegedly defrauding investors in a tech company. His legal tab is already in the millions, and his own staff testified against him under oath.

- **The Political Earthquake for Texas GOP:** The case exposed a brutal intraparty war between Texas' far-right base (which supports Paxton) and more traditional conservatives (who led the impeachment).
