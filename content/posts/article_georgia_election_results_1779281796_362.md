**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**GEORGIA SECRETARY OF STATE CONFIRMS FINAL ELECTION TALLY; RAFFENSPERGER DECLARES RESULTS CERTIFIED**

**ATLANTA, GA – November 7, 2023** – Georgia Secretary of State Brad Raffensperger has officially certified the state’s general election results, concluding a highly scrutinized electoral process.

**WHO:** Secretary of State Brad Raffensperger, along with county election officials across Georgia’s 159 counties.

**WHAT:** The official certification of the 2023 general election results, finalizing vote tallies and confirming the winners for statewide and local contests.

**WHEN:** The certification occurred late Tuesday evening, following the completion of a statewide audit and risk-limiting procedures mandated by state law. The deadline for certification was midnight.

**WHERE:** The certification process culminated at the Georgia State Capitol in Atlanta, with electronic transmission of certified results from all counties to the Secretary of State’s office.

**WHY:** The certification fulfills the statutory requirement to finalize election outcomes, ensuring the integrity of the democratic process. According to Secretary Raffensperger, the process was "smooth, secure, and transparent," and a "model for the nation."

**KEY DETAILS:**
- Official turnout was recorded at 67% of registered voters, a 2% increase from the previous comparable election cycle.
- No significant irregularities or widespread machine errors were reported in any of the state’s 159 counties.
- The certified results will now be transmitted to the Governor’s office for final ratification.
- Legal challenges to specific race outcomes are currently limited to two local judicial contests, with hearings scheduled for next week.

This concludes the official reporting on the 2023 Georgia general election results.
