**HEADLINE: Google IO 2025: Sundar Pichai Unveils “Project Infinity” – The End of the App as We Know It**

**THE BOTTOM LINE:** Google just rendered your smartphone’s homepage obsolete. At IO 2025, Sundar Pichai announced **Project Infinity**, a unified AI agent that lives across devices, bypassing apps entirely. Instead of opening Uber, you tell Infinity “pick me up.” Instead of searching Gmail, you ask “what’s the cheapest flight to Tokyo in June?” The agent performs multi-step tasks, books reservations, and executes payments autonomously.

**WHY IT MATTERS:** Google is betting the moat isn’t search—it’s **action**. The core shift: Google assistant is no longer a chatbot; it’s a transactional layer. This directly challenges Apple’s privacy-first Siri, Meta’s social graph, and every SaaS company reliant on UI interaction. Early adopter: Spotify, of all companies, announced “Music by Intent,” removing browse UX entirely.

**REVENUE IMPLICATIONS:** Infinity is free for consumers. Monetization lives in **“Commerce as a Service”** —Google takes a 5% cut on any action completed (rides, bookings, purchases). CEO implications: If your business model depends on a user *seeing* your interface, your interface is dead. The new UI is natural language + one click.

**WATCH:** Stock held flat during the keynote. Bear case: Privacy backlash is immediate. EU regulators already signaled intent to investigate. Bull case: Google just killed the app store funnel. Shareholders should ask: *Is your company prepared to be a silent backend for Infinity?*
