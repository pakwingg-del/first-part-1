**Headline:** **Taxpayer Revolt? Massie Primary Win Means Your IRS Audit Could Get a Lot More Expensive**

**The Scoop:** Rep. Thomas Massie survived a primary challenge, and consumer advocates are sounding the alarm. While Massie is celebrated by some for slashing federal spending, his victory directly threatens the funding for the IRS's new "Direct File" program—the free tax filing system that saves you the $250+ average cost of TurboTax or H&R Block.

**Your Wallet Impact:** This isn't about politics; it's about your bottom line. If Massie’s anti-IRS stance blocks Direct File, you’re back to paying a middleman to file. But here’s the kicker: his win also puts a target on the expanded Child Tax Credit. If his allies push for "spending offsets," that monthly check that helps with groceries and daycare could vanish. Meanwhile, a defunded IRS means fewer audits on wealthy tax cheats—and more burden on you to make up the difference via higher taxes on your paycheck.

**The Bottom Line:** Before you cheer or boo Massie’s win, check your last tax bill. If you’re a middle-class family, you just lost a little more of your refund.
