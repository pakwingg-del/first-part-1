**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About Thomas Massie’s Polling Freefall**

- **The Stunning Drop:** Representative Thomas Massie (R-KY) has seen a dramatic 22-point dive in his approval rating among Kentucky’s 4th District Republicans over the past six weeks. Once a safe-seat incumbent, he now holds a razor-thin 48% approval—down from a commanding 70% in early January.
- **The Foreign Aid Catalyst:** The breaking point appears to be Massie’s high-profile opposition to the latest defense appropriations bill, which included billions in Ukraine aid. Internal GOP polling shows 68% of his district’s primary voters now view his anti-aid stance as “out of step” with party values—a massive swing from the 40% support he had on that issue just last year.
- **The Primary Threat Is Real:** For the first time in a decade, a credible primary challenger is emerging. State Rep. Savannah Maddox, a more pro-defense conservative, is reportedly polling at 39% in a hypothetical head-to-head, with 13% undecided. The Massie campaign has not yet commented on these numbers.
- **The “Freedom Caucus” Paradox:** While Massie remains a founding member of the hardline House Freedom Caucus, his polling suggests a growing disconnect between his libertarian-leaning foreign policy and the increasingly hawkish, pro-NATO sentiment among his own base. Insiders call it the “Ukraine Wedge.” 
- **What Happens Next:** The NRCC has already signaled it will not endorse in the primary, but campaign finance records show Maddox raised $340,000 in the last quarter alone—more than double Massie’s haul. If this trend holds, Kentucky’s 4th District could become the most-watched Republican primary of the 2026 cycle.
