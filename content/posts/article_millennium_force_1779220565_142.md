**AITA for thinking Millennium Force is just a slightly taller, slightly faster version of a mid-tier coaster that’s been coasting (pun intended) on nostalgia for 25 years?**

Look, I know I’m going to get downvoted into oblivion by the Cedar Point fanboys who still think 93 mph is "thrilling" in an era where we have Top Thrill 2, but I said what I said.

So I finally got on Millennium Force last weekend after hearing my entire life that it’s "the one." The one what? The one ride that gives you back pain before you even hit the first overbank? TL;DR: It’s a 310-foot, 93 mph lift hill followed by 2 minutes of "wheeeee" and then a sudden stop. No inversions, no airtime beyond a mild "uh oh," and the restraints are so loose I felt more like a passenger in a Prius going 90 on the highway.

I asked my buddy if he felt that "mind-blowing force" everyone talks about. He said he felt his phone almost fly out of his pocket and that was the highlight. I told him this ride is the Starbucks Pumpkin Spice Latte of coasters — overhyped, basic, and only impressive if you’ve never had anything better. Now the group is calling me a gatekeeping coaster snob.

AITA? I genuinely think we need to have a conversation about how this ride peaked in 2000 and hasn’t evolved.

**UPDATE:** I got roasted so hard in the comments that I went back and rode it again just to be sure. Still mid. Save your fast pass money for SteVe or just go ride Fury 325 like a civilized human.
