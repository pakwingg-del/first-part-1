**VERDICT: FAKE (Satire)**

**Headline:** “Mountain Dew White Out Will Be Rebranded as ‘Coke Zero Tundra’ in Major 2025 Restructure; Fans in Uproar”

**The Rumor:**
A viral Facebook post from a parody page called *Soda Industry Insider* claims that PepsiCo has sold the rights to Mountain Dew White Out to Coca-Cola, and that the drink will be rebranded as “Coke Zero Tundra” and sold exclusively at Arctic Circle drive-ins starting in spring 2025. The post includes a doctored image of a white can with a polar bear drinking from a straw.

**The Reality:**
- The post originated from a known satire account with the disclaimer “100% not true, relax, Dew Nation.”
- The image of the “Coke Zero Tundra” can was created with an AI tool and clearly mismatches the current White Out packaging.
- PepsiCo and Coca-Cola have no cross-brand licensing deals for carbonated soft drinks.
- Mountain Dew White Out was discontinued in most U.S. markets in 2022, but has not been sold or rebranded to any competitor.

**Bottom Line:** If you see a friend sharing “Coke Zero Tundra,” just remind them it’s a joke. There is no such product, and White Out remains a memory—not a Coca-Cola future.
