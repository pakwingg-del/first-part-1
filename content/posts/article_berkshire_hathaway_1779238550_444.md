**Berkshire’s Quiet Sell-Off: Is Buffett Signaling a Market Top?**

In a move that has Wall Street whispering, Berkshire Hathaway has trimmed its massive Apple stake and let its cash pile swell to a record $325 billion. CEO Warren Buffett isn’t buying. He’s stockpiling dry powder.

The message is clear: valuations are stretched, and the Oracle of Omaha sees more risk than reward right now. For institutional investors, this is the signal to watch. When the world’s most patient buyer stops buying, the market’s next chapter may be brutal.
