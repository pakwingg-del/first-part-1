**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the San Diego Shooting**

- **The Scene:** A targeted shooting erupted outside a crowded gas station and fast-food plaza in the Southcrest neighborhood, not far from downtown San Diego. Witnesses reported hearing a burst of rapid gunfire, sending customers and workers scrambling for cover behind vehicles and storefronts.

- **The Toll:** At least 4 people were rushed to local hospitals with what are now being described as **critical, life-threatening injuries**. Multiple victims were found at separate locations along the strip, suggesting the suspect moved or opened fire from a moving vehicle. No fatalities have been confirmed at this hour.

- **The Suspect:** Police have issued a "Be On the Lookout" (BOLO) for a dark-colored sedan, possibly a Honda Accord, last seen speeding south on I-5. The suspect is described as a male in dark clothing, but a clear motive remains "under active investigation," with no terror links immediately flagged.

- **The Chaos:** The incident triggered a massive lockdown—nearby schools, including Millennial Tech Middle School, went into "secure mode" and bus routes were halted. A several-block radius around the 400 block of Market Street is currently sealed off as evidence markers litter the asphalt.

- **The Warning:** Authorities are pleading with the public to **avoid the area** and not to share unconfirmed videos on social media, as it could compromise witnesses' safety. "We believe this was not a random act of violence," said a San Diego PD spokesperson, "but we are treating every possibility with maximum caution."
