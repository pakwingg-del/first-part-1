**AITA for putting my dad in a retirement home because he wouldn’t stop playing Phil Collins’ *Tarzan* soundtrack on repeat?**

Look, I’m not heartless. My dad (78M) has been a massive Phil Collins simp since the 80s. I’m talking full-on, “I-can’t-hear-you-over-*In the Air Tonight*” levels of obsession. He’s got the leather jacket. The bald head. The tragic divorced-dad energy. It’s almost poetic.

But the breaking point came last Tuesday. I walk into his house, and he’s just sitting there, staring at a VHS copy of *Tarzan*, sobbing. Not crying. *Sobbing*. He’s got the soundbar cranked to 11 because his hearing is shot (gee, wonder why), and *“Son of Man”* is playing on a loop. For **six hours**. He’s got a notepad where he’s been tallying every time Phil sings about being “strong like a jungle cat.” The tally is in the thousands.

I tried to put on literally anything else – a podcast, white noise, emergency sirens – and he starts screaming that I’m “suppressing his emotional journey with Phil.” I asked him if he wanted to watch *No Jacket Required* for some variety, and he said, “That album is about a time when I *had* hair. It’s too painful.”

So I called a home. They gave him a single room with a window that faces a brick wall. Yesterday, the nurse called and said he’s started a one-man drum solo on the cafeteria tables using his dentures as drumsticks, and he’s formed a cult of five other residents who now call him “Phil-llins.”

TL;DR: My dad is Phil Collins from Wish, and I’m tired of being his Jane. A
