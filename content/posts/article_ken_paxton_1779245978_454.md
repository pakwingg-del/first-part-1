**HEADLINE: FACT CHECK: Did Ken Paxton Secretly Fund a "Patriot Crime Lab" to Test Election Fraud Claims?**

**Rating:** **MIXED (Partially True, Partially False)**

**Viral Claim:** A viral social media post claims that Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton secretly funneled $3.2 million in public funds to a private, unaccredited "Patriot Crime Lab" operating out of a strip mall in suburban Dallas. The post alleges the lab is using burned ballots and "tainted" voting machines to manufacture evidence for a sweeping lawsuit targeting the 2024 election results in Harris County.

**The Facts:**

**What’s Real:**
*   **The Lab Exists:** A private entity calling itself the *American Election Integrity Consortium* (AEIC) *does* operate from a leased space in a strip mall in Plano, TX. Records show the AEIC received a $3.2 million grant from the Texas Office of the Attorney General (OAG) in late 2023.
*   **The Topic:** The lab *is* focused on analyzing voting equipment and ballots for "irregularities," specifically regarding chain of custody and software manipulation. They have published a preliminary report.
*   **The Connection:** A former senior advisor to Paxton is listed as a board member for the AEIC.

**What’s Fake:**
*   **The "Burned Ballots" Narrative:** While the viral post shows a photo of charred paper, the OAG and the AEIC confirm that **no burned ballots were used.** The lab is analyzing *scanned* digital images of ballot envelopes and independent audit logs provided by a whistleblower. The "fire" in the image is from a stock photo used for dramatic effect.
