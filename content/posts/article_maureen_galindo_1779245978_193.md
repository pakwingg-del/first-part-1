**Maureen Galindo's "Common Sense" Lease Rule Sparks Fury on Facebook**

Maureen Galindo, a local landlord and self-proclaimed "common sense enforcer," has gone viral for all the wrong reasons after posting a new lease rule on a community Facebook page. The rule, titled "The 10-Minute Guest Clause," states that any visitor who stays longer than 600 seconds is automatically classified as a "subtenant" and subject to a $50 fee per minute after that, retroactively. When questioned by a tenant, Galindo wrote, "If common sense existed, you wouldn't need a welcome mat. I do."

The post has exploded with comments from local residents. "Common sense says you don't charge people for talking to their neighbor on the sidewalk," fumed one user. Another added, "Maureen's version of common sense is the same as a trap for renters and basic human decency." The post has been shared over 4,000 times, with many calling for a boycott of her properties. Galindo has yet to respond to requests for comment.
