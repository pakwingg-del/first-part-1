**NEWS SNIPPET: "Marc Benioff Bans All AI-Generated Code at Salesforce – Order to Be Enforced by 'Human Empathy Auditors'"**

🔍 **REAL OR FAKE?**

**The Claim:** A viral social media post claims that Salesforce CEO Marc Benioff has issued an internal memo banning all AI-generated code from being deployed in any Salesforce product—effective immediately. The post says Benioff is creating a new "Office of Human Empathy" staffed by auditors who will review all code commits for signs of AI authorship and punish violators with immediate termination.

**Reality Check:** **FAKE.** This is entirely fabricated. There is no internal memo, no "Office of Human Empathy," and no ban on AI-generated code at Salesforce. In fact, Salesforce has been aggressively investing in AI tools, including its Einstein AI platform and the recently launched "Agentforce" suite for autonomous AI agents. Benioff himself has publicly stated that AI is "the most important technology of our generation" for Salesforce customers. While Benioff is a vocal advocate for "human-centered leadership," no such draconian anti-AI code policy exists. The rumor appears to have originated from a parody account satirizing fears of AI replacing human programmers. Always verify corporate announcements via official channels (e.g., Salesforce's press room or Benioff's verified social media).

**Verdict:** 🚫 False
