**Georgia Supreme Court Candidate Suggests ‘Common Sense’ Could Replace Law Degree — Locals Lose It**

Heard this morning that one of the Georgia Supreme Court candidates actually told a reporter that “judges overthink things and regular folks with common sense could solve cases faster.” Excuse me? We’re talking about the highest court in the state — not a HOA dispute over someone’s trash cans.

My neighbor’s cousin lives in Cobb County and said the candidate was pushing this “let the people decide” nonsense at a town hall. Since when did years of legal training become optional? Next thing you know, they’ll be asking my mechanic to rule on constitutional amendments because he’s good with a wrench.

I don’t care what party you’re in — we need judges who actually studied the law, not folks who think “common sense” is a substitute for precedent. This is how you end up with rulings that sound good over a fence but collapse in a courtroom. Some things just can’t be voted on by people who’ve never opened a law book. Georgia deserves better than a court run on gut feelings.
