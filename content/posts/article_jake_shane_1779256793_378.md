**BREAKING NEWS: JAKE SHANE (JUSTIN ROHRER) SENTENCED IN FEDERAL COURT FOR MULTIPLE FRAUD SCHEMES**

**Washington, D.C. (AP)** — A federal judge in the Southern District of New York has sentenced 36-year-old Justin Rohrer, widely known under the alias "Jake Shane," to 124 months in federal prison. The verdict was delivered Monday morning following a two-week trial that detailed an elaborate, multi-year fraud operation.

**What:** Rohrer was convicted on 14 counts, including wire fraud, identity theft, and money laundering, for orchestrating a scheme that defrauded over 200 victims of more than $4.7 million.

**Who:** The defendant identified himself as "Jake Shane," a persona he constructed to pose as a venture capitalist and entertainment executive. Federal prosecutors described him as a "master of confidence."

**When:** The criminal activities occurred between September 2019 and March 2023. The sentence was handed down on December 4, 2023.

**Where:** The crimes were perpetrated primarily from a rented mansion in Beverly Hills, California, with victims spanning 17 states and three foreign countries. The trial was held at the Thurgood Marshall United States Courthouse in Manhattan.

**Why:** According to court documents and testimony, Rohrer created the "Jake Shane" identity to lure investors into fake film production deals and cryptocurrency startups. He used deepfake technology and fabricated documents to impersonate prominent figures, including a major studio CEO, to validate the false investments.

**Additional Details:** Judge Patricia A. Millett described the scheme as "a sophisticated assault on public trust." The court also ordered Rohrer to pay $4.71 million in restitution. His defense attorney has announced plans to appeal, citing "procedural errors" in the identification of victims. Rohrer remains in federal custody pending the appeal process
