**🚨 FACT CHECK: Did Millions Lose ObamaCare This Year? 🚨**

**The Claim:** A viral text chain circulating on social media alleges: "BREAKING: Over 10 million Americans have been kicked off their Affordable Care Act plans since January 2024. The Biden administration is hiding the numbers. You're next."

**The Reality:**

❌ **Headline Rating: MISLEADING.** This is a classic case of mixing real policy changes with a false "apocalypse" narrative.

✅ **What actually happened (the real data):**
The Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) reported a *record* 21.3 million people signed up for ACA plans for 2024 – the highest ever.

🔍 **The source of the confusion:**
The "10 million" figure comes from a different program: **Medicaid**. Since April 2023, states have restarted the annual "redetermination" process (paused during the pandemic). This has resulted in *roughly 10 million* people being disenrolled from Medicaid — often for procedural reasons like not returning renewal paperwork, not because they are suddenly uninsured.

⚡ **Verdict:** The ACA (ObamaCare) marketplace *is not* losing people. It's more popular than ever. The "loss" is a bureaucratic unwinding of a temporary pandemic policy for a **different** insurance program (Medicaid). Most of the 10 million who lost Medicaid either qualified for other coverage (including ACA subsidies) or were no longer eligible.

**The Takeaway:**
Don't fall for panic-spam. While the Medicaid redeterminations are a real administrative crisis (especially for paperwork glitches), the claim that "ObamaCare coverage is collapsing" is demonstrably **false.**
