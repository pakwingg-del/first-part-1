**Subject: Kroger Pulls Cheese Garlic Croutons – Supplier-Failure Risk to Private-Label Trust**

To the CEO:

Kroger has issued a voluntary recall for its *Private Selection* Cheese Garlic Croutons due to undeclared wheat and soy allergens—a direct supply-chain quality failure.

**The Numbers:** The recall impacts product sold in 20+ states with a "Best If Used By" date of July 3, 2025. No illnesses reported to date, but the risk of anaphylaxis is present.

**The Real Cost:** This is not a food safety catastrophe—it’s a **brand-trust erosion event**. Private label margins are built on consumer confidence. One mislabeled allergen in a tier-one house brand creates liability, consumer complaints, and retail shelf disruption.

**Move:** Immediately enforce stricter supplier label verification and consider an accelerated shelf-audit protocol for all cheese-topped SKUs.

**Bottom line:** This recall costs money. The real loss is if your customer starts checking the label on *every* Kroger brand item again.
