**DALLAS-FORT WORTH (AP) — A ground stop has been issued for all flights inbound to Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport (DFW).**

* **Who:** The Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) issued the air traffic control ground stop, affecting all commercial and general aviation aircraft scheduled to arrive at DFW.
* **What:** A complete halt to all inbound traffic. Flights currently en route to DFW are being held at their origin airports or diverted to alternate facilities. Departures from the airport are currently unaffected, though significant delays are expected.
* **Where:** Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport (KDFW) and all related airspace sectors within the North Texas region.
* **When:** The ground stop was implemented at 14:30 Central Standard Time. An estimated duration has not yet been provided by FAA officials.
* **Why:** According to a preliminary FAA advisory, the stop is due to a "staffing shortage" within the DFW Terminal Radar Approach Control (TRACON) facility. The specific nature of the staffing issue has not been detailed, but operations are reported as "limited."

**Impact:** Travelers are urged to check with their respective airlines for individual flight statuses. Ground stops of this nature typically result in a cascading effect of delays for multiple hours following the resumption of normal traffic flow. Authorities are working to resolve the issue and restore standard operations.
