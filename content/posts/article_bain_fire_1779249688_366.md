**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**BAIN, INDIA – A significant structural fire has been reported at a commercial facility in Bain, resulting in a coordinated multi-agency emergency response. According to preliminary reports from the Bain District Fire Department, the blaze was first detected at approximately 14:30 hours local time at an industrial unit located on the eastern periphery of the city.**

**Who:** The Bain Fire Department, supported by three additional municipal fire brigades and units from the National Disaster Response Force (NDRF), has been deployed to the scene. All personnel are operating under the unified command of Chief Fire Officer Arjun Mehta.

**What:** The incident involves a large-scale fire that has consumed the primary processing wing of the facility. The specific nature of the materials stored at the site has not yet been officially confirmed by environmental safety officers, though air quality monitoring units are currently stationed downwind.

**Where:** The incident is confined to Plot 47, Sector 9, of the Bain Industrial Corridor. A 500-meter exclusion zone has been established, and authorities have diverted all vehicular traffic on National Highway 16.

**When:** The emergency services received the initial distress call at 14:28 hours. The fire reportedly escalated rapidly within the first hour of ignition. As of the latest update, suppression efforts have entered their fourth hour, with crews focusing on containment.

**Why:** The origin and cause of the fire remain under active investigation by the Bureau of Fire Safety and the local police. While a preliminary assessment is pending, officials have stated that there is currently no evidence to suggest an act of criminal intent. A full forensic audit of the building's electrical and safety systems will commence once the fire is fully extinguished.

**Current Status:** Officials confirm that all 47 personnel inside the facility at the time of the fire have been safely evacuated.
