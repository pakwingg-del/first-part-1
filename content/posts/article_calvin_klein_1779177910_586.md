**Headline: Calvin Klein’s Latest Campaign: “Unzip Your Soul” Sparks Fury—Critics Say It’s Selling Childhood to the Woke Agenda**  

**Body:**  
In a move that has parents, pastors, and cultural watchdogs seeing red, Calvin Klein has dropped its newest ad campaign, “Unzip Your Soul,” and it’s being branded as the final nail in the coffin of decency. The campaign features androgynous minors in sheer, deconstructed undergarments, posed in provocative, soft-core tableaux that blur the line between fashion and fetish.  

“We’re celebrating the freedom to explore identity without boundaries,” a CK spokesperson said. But moral critics aren’t buying it. “This isn’t freedom—it’s the systematic destruction of innocence,” fumed Dr. Elaine Hartwood, a family ethics advocate. “They’re training a generation to see their bodies as commodities before they can even vote. We’ve traded modesty for marketing, and now we’re stripping the soul out of childhood.”  

The backlash is spreading like wildfire, with calls for boycotts and a federal review of advertising standards. “This is the logical endpoint of a culture that worships shock over substance,” Hartwood added. “If we can’t protect the boundaries of youth, what’s left to save?”  

Is this edgy art’s final frontier—or a sign that society has finally lost its moral compass? One thing’s for sure: Calvin Klein just turned the heat up, and the culture war is about to boil over.
