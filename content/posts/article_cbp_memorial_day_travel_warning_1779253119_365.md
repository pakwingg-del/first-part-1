**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**DATELINE: WASHINGTON, D.C.**

**SUMMARY**

U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) has issued an official operational advisory regarding anticipated travel volumes during the upcoming Memorial Day holiday weekend.

**WHO:** U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) officials and field operations personnel.

**WHAT:** A formal travel warning and operational advisory, citing "significantly elevated" wait times at ports of entry across the southern and northern borders, as well as at international airports.

**WHERE:** All major U.S. land border crossings, including but not limited to San Diego, El Paso, and Buffalo, as well as major international terminals such as John F. Kennedy International Airport, Los Angeles International Airport, and Miami International Airport.

**WHEN:** Valid for the Memorial Day holiday period, specifically from Thursday, May 23, through Monday, May 27.

**WHY:** The advisory is attributed to a confluence of factors, including the highest projected travel volume since before the pandemic, enhanced security screening protocols, and seasonal spikes in agricultural inspections. CBP Commissioner Troy A. Miller, in a prepared statement, emphasized that the agency is prioritizing "national security and facilitation of legitimate trade and travel."

**DETAILS:**
CBP is urging all travelers to enroll in Trusted Traveler Programs, verify travel documents 72 hours in advance, and utilize the CBP One mobile application for real-time border wait estimates. The agency further warns of potential extended delays for non-compliant travelers and advises planning for an additional two to three hours of processing time.

**CLOSING STATEMENT:**
"This is a period of high operational tempo," the CBP advisory states. "We ask for the public’s patience and remind all parties that compliance with entry requirements is the most effective way to minimize disruption."
