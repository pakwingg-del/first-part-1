**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**News Anchor Desk – National Health Alert**

**What:** A significant reduction in health insurance coverage under the Affordable Care Act (ACA) has been officially documented.

**Who:** The affected population includes approximately 3.7 million Americans, primarily in the states of Texas, Florida, and Georgia.

**When:** This coverage loss was recorded during the most recent federal open enrollment period, which concluded on January 16, 2025.

**Where:** The decline is concentrated in states that have not expanded Medicaid, as well as those with documented policy churn regarding plan renewals and income verification.

**Why:** Federal officials attribute the contraction to the expiration of enhanced premium subsidies, combined with stricter eligibility redeterminations and administrative processing delays within state-level marketplaces.

**How:** The Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) confirmed that a combination of automated disenrollment for non-payment and a reduction in auto-renewals contributed to the net loss, marking the first major coverage decline in the program's history since the pandemic-era protections were lifted.
