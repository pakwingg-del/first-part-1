**Tom Kane’s “Common Sense” Lawncare Proposal Sparks Fury After Neighbor’s Hedge Trimming Lands Him in Hospital**

**LOCAL –** In a move that has the entire Maplewood Estates Facebook group divided, local resident Tom Kane is now under fire after his aggressive “common sense” approach to a neighbor’s overgrown hedge landed the retiree, Harold Miller, 72, in the ER with a suspected concussion.

According to the viral post, Kane allegedly took matters into his own hands after Miller’s Leylandii hedge crept two feet over the property line. Instead of talking it out, Kane claims he “exercised common sense” by firing up his own hedge trimmer and tackling the overgrowth—while Miller was still watering his petunias on the other side.

“I don’t need a letter or a HOA meeting. Common sense says if it’s on my side, I cut it,” Kane wrote in a now-deleted comment. “If you can’t maintain a 4-foot trim, you deserve what you get.”

Witnesses say the incident escalated when Miller, startled by the sudden whirring, tripped over his own hose and struck his head on a birdbath. Kane, in a subsequent post, doubled down: “If he had common sense, he wouldn’t have been standing there. This is personal responsibility 101.”

The group is now ablaze with comments, ranging from “Absolute lunatic” to “He’s not wrong—trim your own junk.” One admin has closed comments after Kane’s post was shared over 400 times, with users demanding a “common sense” ban.

Police were not called, but a GoFundMe for Miller’s medical bills has already raised $2,000. Kane’s final word? “I’ll trim anything that crosses my line. Try me, Harold.”
