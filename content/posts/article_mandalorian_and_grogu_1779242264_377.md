**WASHINGTON D.C. – In a move that has sent shockwaves through the galactic entertainment industry, Lucasfilm officially confirmed yesterday that the highly anticipated feature film, *"The Mandalorian & Grogu,"* has entered full pre-production.**

According to a statement released from Lucasfilm headquarters in San Francisco, California, filming is scheduled to commence in November of this year. What is the nature of this production? It is a major motion picture, not a television event, designed to continue the narrative of the bounty hunter Din Djarin and the Force-sensitive child known as Grogu.

Who is leading this project? Director Jon Favreau, the creator of the Emmy-award-winning series, will return to direct the film, with executive producer Kathleen Kennedy overseeing the production. While specifics regarding the plot remain classified, sources indicate the story will pick up directly following the events seen in *"The Mandalorian "* Season 3, which aired on Disney+.

Where will this story take place, and when? While specific star systems remain undisclosed, location scouts have been reported across various soundstages in Los Angeles and the United Kingdom. The expected release date is currently projected for late 2026.

Why this decision was made is clear to analysts: after generating over 70 million initial viewers for its television debut, the demand for a theatrical experience featuring the duo was described by Lucasfilm insiders as "undeniable." Pedro Pascal’s Din Djarin and the puppet and CGI hybrid of Grogu will now headline a standalone cinematic event. A formal trailer and official title are expected to debut at next month's Star Wars Celebration in London.
