**Viral News Snippet: “Google IO 2025: A Holographic Stage Shows You Your OWN Dreams – But There’s a Catch”**

**The Claim:** Reports are flooding social media claiming Google unveiled a feature at their 2024 IO Developer Conference called **“Project Daydream 2.0.”** The rumor alleges the tech giant demonstrated a mobile app that uses your Google Photos and search history to generate a real-time, AI-powered holographic dreamscape on the stage—showing an attendee’s own personal memories and future hopes projected onto a life-size screen.

**What’s Real/Fake:**

**Fake – The specific viral story is false.** Google did not launch a “Daydream 2.0” or a “personalized holographic dreamscape” feature. This appears to be an AI-generated hoax or a misinterpretation of “Project Starline,” a Google project (announced years ago) for 3D holographic video calls, which is a very different technology.

**Real-but-Misleading – The "holographic stage" part is a mix-up.** Google IO did showcase **Immersive Stream for XR**, a cloud-based tool for developers to build 3D environments. But it didn’t read anyone’s personal data.

**The Actual Story:** Google IO 2024’s biggest reveal was **Gemini Live**, a multimodal AI assistant that can see your camera feed and interact in real-time. No mind-reading holograms. The viral snippet is a perfect example of how people conflate real AI advancements (“Gemini sees your screen”) with sci-fi fantasy (“it sees your soul”).

**Verdict: Fake. Elon Musk didn’t tweet about it either.**
