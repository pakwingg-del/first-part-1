**HEADLINE: GENESIS FILES FOR BANKRUPTCY – PHIL COLLINS’ SILENCE COSTS SHAREHOLDERS $400M**

**NEW YORK –** The music world is reeling after Genesis, the iconic rock band, filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection this morning. The catalyst? A brutal accounting of Phil Collins’ recent health decline and the catastrophic cancellation of their 2024 reunion tour.

Internal documents obtained by *The Street* reveal that Collins’ “severe vocal cord paralysis and spinal issues” rendered the tour uninsurable. The band’s holding company, Genesis IP Ltd., is now $380M in debt after failing to secure a $200M Lloyds of London policy. Shareholders – including major pension funds – are wiped out.

Analysts are calling it the most expensive “silent partner” in music history. The takeaway for CEOs: **If your key talent is a single point of failure, you are not an asset; you are a liability.** The board is now facing a class-action suit for failing to force a "digital double" clause into Collins’ contract.

**Bottom line:** The band that defined stadium rock just became a case study in catastrophic risk management.
