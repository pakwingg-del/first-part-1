🔥 **VIRAL ALERT: “3-Alarm Fire Near Me” Sparks Evacuation Confusion – Here’s What’s Real vs. Fake** 🔥

**🚨 The Claim:** A dramatic video is circulating on TikTok and X claiming a “massive 3-alarm fire” is currently raging in a residential neighborhood near downtown, with helicopters dumping water and mandatory evacuations in effect.

**✅ What’s Real:**
- A **small brush fire** did start near the intersection of Elm and 5th Street around 2:30 PM local time, fueled by dry grass and gusty winds.
- Local fire crews responded quickly. The fire was contained to approximately 2 acres within 45 minutes.
- **One home** was temporarily evacuated as a precaution, but residents have since been allowed to return.

**❌ What’s Fake:**
- The viral video shows **old footage** from a 2021 wildfire in a different county, not today’s incident. The audio claiming “3-alarm fire” and “air support” is fabricated.
- **No helicopters** were deployed. The only aircraft overhead was a news helicopter.
- Evacuations were **not mandatory** and affected only 4 homes at most, not the “entire block” as stated in the clip.

**📊 The Fact:** This is a classic case of “scare-bait.” A real, small emergency is being exaggerated with recycled disaster footage to drive panic and clicks.

**🔍 How to check next time:** Open your local fire department’s official social media or website. If they haven’t posted about a major evacuation or a 3-alarm response, the viral claims are likely fake.

**🚒 Bottom line:** A little fire. A lot of misinformation. Stay calm, check local sources, and don’t share that video.
