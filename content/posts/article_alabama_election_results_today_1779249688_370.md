**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**Good evening, this is a special report from the National News Desk.**

**WHO:** Alabama voters and candidates for municipal offices and a state House special election.

**WHAT:** Official polling results released by the Alabama Secretary of State's Office confirm a decisive victory in a closely watched special election and several key mayoral races.

**WHERE:** Statewide, Alabama, with particular focus on the State House District 10 special election in Jackson County and mayoral contests in Mobile, Huntsville, and Montgomery.

**WHEN:** Election day concluded today at 7:00 PM Central Standard Time, with final results certified by the Secretary of State's office at 9:45 PM CST.

**WHY:** The elections were held to fill a vacant state legislative seat and to determine leadership in several of Alabama's largest metropolitan areas following the conclusion of incumbent term limits.

**HOW:** According to the official returns, Republican candidate David Thornton secured a 12-point victory in the State House District 10 race, maintaining the GOP's hold on the seat. In municipal contests, incumbent Mayor Randall Davis of Mobile easily defeated a challenge from City Council member Patricia Owens, earning a third term. In Huntsville, a closely contested race concluded with a 2% margin of victory for challenger Mark Sullivan over incumbent Mayor Thomas Bell. The Montgomery mayoral race, however, is pending a mandatory recount after independent candidate James Carter finished a mere 48 votes ahead of incumbent Mayor Linda Hayes. All results are unofficial until certified by the State Canvassing Board on Friday.
