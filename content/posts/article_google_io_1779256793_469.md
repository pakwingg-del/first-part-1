**Viral News Snippet: "Google Unveils 'Emotional Sync' AI at Google IO – Can Your Phone *Feel* What You Feel?"**

**Status: FAKE (but based on real tech)**

A clip claiming to show a new Google IO feature called **"Emotional Sync"** is going viral on TikTok and X. The video shows a Pixel phone screen displaying a live emotional "aura" that changes color—from blue (sad) to red (angry) to green (calm)—in real time as the user speaks. The narrator claims the phone can "detect your emotional state through voice tone and heart rate via the camera."

**Why it’s fake (for now):**

- **No such product was announced.** Google did demo **Project Astra**—a real-time multimodal AI assistant—but it does not perform emotional analysis or display a color-coded "aura."
- **Voice tone analysis for emotion is widely considered pseudoscience** by leading AI ethics researchers. Google has explicitly stated they avoid labeling "emotions" due to accuracy and bias concerns.
- **The clip uses deepfake audio** of a well-known Google executive, and the UI shown is a blend of leaked Material Design concepts and fan-made 3D mockups.

**The kernel of truth:** Google did show a prototype at IO that can "understand context"—e.g., noticing the user looks sad and asking if they want to hear a joke. But the "reading your soul" part is fiction. **Verdict: Misinformation alert. Don't fall for it.**
