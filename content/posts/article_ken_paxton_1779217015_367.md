**TEXAS ATTORNEY GENERAL KEN PAXTON FACES RENEWED WHISTLEBLOWER ALLEGATIONS OVER STAFF RETALIATION**

**AUSTIN, TX** – Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton is facing a significant escalation in legal scrutiny, as new whistleblower complaints allege systematic retaliation against former senior staff.

**WHO:** The complaints are brought by former high-ranking employees of the Office of the Attorney General (OAG), including former Deputy Attorney General for Legal Counsel, Ryan Vassar. The allegations are directed at Attorney General Ken Paxton and his current leadership team.

**WHAT:** The former employees allege a pattern of unlawful retaliation, including termination, demotion, and the creation of a hostile work environment. These actions, they claim, were taken in direct response to their reporting of what they believed to be official misconduct and potential criminal activity by Paxton, specifically regarding allegations of bribery and abuse of office. This is not a new lawsuit, but a renewed and specific filing of formal whistleblower claims under the Texas Whistleblower Act, following a prior settlement agreement.

**WHEN:** The formal notice of claim was filed with the OAG and the Texas Office of the Attorney General's Human Resources division on Tuesday, November 14, 2023. The alleged retaliatory actions occurred between 2020 and the present.

**WHERE:** The events in question took place within the Texas Attorney General's Office in Austin, Texas. The claims will likely be litigated in Travis County District Court.

**WHY:** The former employees assert that Paxton violated state law by retaliating against them after they reported that he had been investigating a political donor, Nate Paul, without a proper basis. They further allege the retaliation was an effort to silence dissent and conceal the original alleged misconduct.

This development represents the latest chapter in a long-running controversy surrounding the Attorney General, who was previously impeached by the Texas House
