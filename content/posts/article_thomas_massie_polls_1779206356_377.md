**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**Dateline: Washington, D.C. – March 24, 2025**

**HEADLINE: Surge in Constituency Approval: Representative Thomas Massie Records Highest Favorability Rating in Fourth Quarter Polls**

**WASHINGTON, D.C.** – New polling data released this morning by the Northern Kentucky University Center for Regional Analysis reveals a significant uptick in approval ratings for United States Representative Thomas Massie (R-KY-4).

**WHAT:** According to the survey, which polled 1,200 registered voters in Kentucky’s Fourth Congressional District, Representative Massie achieved a 58% approval rating in the fourth quarter of 2024. This marks a 7% increase from the previous quarter and represents his highest recorded favorability score in a mid-term election cycle.

**WHO:** The poll measured sentiment regarding Representative Thomas Massie, a libertarian-leaning Republican first elected in 2012. The survey’s margin of error is reported at plus or minus 2.8 percentage points.

**WHEN:** The data was collected between October 15 and November 1, 2024, and released to the public at 10:00 AM Eastern Time today.

**WHERE:** The polling was conducted via a random digit-dialing method across the entire Fourth Congressional District, which encompasses suburban, exurban, and rural areas of Northern Kentucky.

**WHY:** Political analysts attribute the surge to what they describe as a "consistency dividend." Despite criticism for breaking with party leadership on key procedural votes—most notably votes on the Speaker of the House and foreign aid packages—the poll indicates that 63% of surveyed constituents cited a belief that the Representative "votes his conscience" as a primary driver for their approval. Conversely, 22% of respondents cited his focus on blockchain technology and fiscal restraint.
