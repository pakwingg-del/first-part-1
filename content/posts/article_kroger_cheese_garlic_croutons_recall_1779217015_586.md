**HEADLINE: KROGER'S "GARLIC PARADISE" CRUMBLES: RECALLED CROUTONS SPARK ETHICAL PANIC OVER "PROCESSED SIN"**

**Cincinnati, OH** – In a move that has moral critics clutching their pearls and their grocery lists, Kroger has issued a voluntary recall of its popular "Cheese Garlic Croutons" due to an undeclared milk allergen. But the health warning is taking a backseat to a blistering cultural firestorm, with one prominent "Moral Critic" declaring the snack a "symbol of our collective gastronomic delinquency."

Dr. Evelyn Thorne, a self-styled ethicist and author of *The Crumb of Decadence: How Processed Food is Eroding the Soul*, argues the recall highlights a deeper societal rot. "This isn't about a milk protein. It's about our insatiable appetite for engineered excess," Thorne stated in a viral TikTok that has amassed 2 million views. "Cheese. Garlic. Bread. All artifically tweaked and dusted onto a square of bleached flour—it's a trinity of processed sin. We are a nation so morally bankrupt that we can't even enjoy a simple crouton without flooding it with industrial dairy and sodium. This recall is a divine slap on the wrist for our consumer gluttony."

Thorne's commentary has ignited a firestorm online, with #CroutonCrisis and #MoralCrumb trending. Critics accuse her of elitist pearl-clutching over a $2.99 bag of salad toppings, while supporters argue the recall is a "perfect metaphor" for America's broken food system. "First they take away our joy, then they tell us the joy was poison," wailed one user.

Meanwhile, Kroger has remained silent on the ethical angle, simply stating the recall is "out of an abundance of caution."
