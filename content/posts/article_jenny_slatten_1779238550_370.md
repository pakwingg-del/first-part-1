**JENNY SLATTEN: RECKLESS ENDANGERMENT CONVICTION UPHELD IN LANDMARK WORKPLACE SAFETY CASE**

**LOCAL, USA** – In a decision with sweeping implications for corporate accountability, the Appellate Division of the Superior Court has today upheld the 2024 conviction of former pharmaceutical executive **Jenny Slatten** on charges of reckless endangerment.

**WHAT** – The court’s ruling affirms a jury finding that Slatten, as Chief Operations Officer of RegiCorp Labs, knowingly authorized the illegal disposal of hazardous chemical waste. The waste subsequently contaminated a municipal water supply serving over 12,000 residents in the county’s northern district.

**WHERE** – The decision originated from the Central County courthouse following a two-year legal battle stemming from the 2022 "Blackwater Contamination" incident, which was first reported in the town of Millbrook.

**WHEN** – The unanimous appellate ruling was issued at 10:00 AM local time today, concluding a 14-month appeal process. The original criminal trial concluded in November 2024.

**WHY** – The court found that internal memos and testimonies demonstrated Slatten had been repeatedly warned by safety advisors that the disposal method violated both federal EPA guidelines and state hazardous materials statutes. The court’s majority opinion stated, “The defendant prioritized quarterly earnings reports over the demonstrable risk of catastrophic harm to human life and the environment.”

**HOW** – The contamination was discovered when a routine check by the Department of Environmental Quality revealed elevated levels of the industrial solvent Trichloroethylene (TCE) in the Millbrook aquifer. Investigators traced the source directly to a designated "storage facility" owned by RegiCorp.
