**🚨 Top 5 things you need to know about CBP's Memorial Day travel warning 🚨**

- **Massive Wait Times Expected at Land Borders**  
  CBP warns that southbound traffic (especially at major ports like San Ysidro and El Paso) could see **3+ hour delays** during peak holiday hours. They urge travelers to use "Ready Lane" or "Sentri" lanes if eligible to skip the worst of it.

- **New Digital Declaration App is Live**  
  For air travelers returning to the U.S., CBP has fully rolled out the **Mobile Passport Control (MPC)** app. Download it BEFORE you leave—no internet connection needed at customs, and it slashes your wait from the general line by **up to 40%**.

- **Don't Forget the '6-Month Passport Rule'**  
  CBP is cracking down on expired-but-still-valid-looking documents. If your passport expires **within 6 months** of your travel date, you may be denied re-entry into the U.S. Check your dates now—dozens of travelers were turned back last year.

- **'Green Light' for Global Entry Cards**  
  For the first time, CBP will **not** suspend Global Entry access during the holiday surge—but only if you have your physical card with you. Digital copies on your phone are NOT accepted. Carry it in your wallet, not your suitcase.

- **Cannabis Travel is Still a Hard 'No'**  
  Even if you're crossing from a legal state (like California) into a legal province (like British Columbia), CBP will **strictly enforce** federal law. Possession of any amount—even with a medical card—can result in a $500 fine and a permanent ban from the Global Entry program.

**Bottom line:** Plan for chaos, download the app, and double-check your passport and wallet. Safe travels!
