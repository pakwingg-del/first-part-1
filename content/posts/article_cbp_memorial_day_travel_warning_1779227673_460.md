**VERDICT: REAL – MISLEADING**  

**Source:** U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) issued a formal travel advisory on May 22, warning of “significant delays” at southern border crossings during Memorial Day weekend, citing heightened enforcement and processing times.  

**What’s True:**  
- CBP’s official statement (released May 22) explicitly warns of “longer-than-normal wait times” at land ports of entry from May 24–27, due to increased vehicle and pedestrian volume.  
- The agency advises travelers to use the CBP One app for real-time wait estimates and to “plan accordingly.”  

**What’s False:**  
- Viral social media posts claim CBP is “shutting down border crossings” or “pausing all travel” for Memorial Day. *This is false.* No crossings will close.  
- Some posts suggest the warning is tied to “mass deportations” or “migrant surges” – CBP states the delays are due to *routine holiday volume* and seasonal staffing adjustments, not immigration enforcement.  

**Bottom Line:** The warning is real, but exaggerated claims of a border lockdown are false. Check CBP’s official Twitter/X account (@CBP) for live updates.
