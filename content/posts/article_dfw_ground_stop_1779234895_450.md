**CLAIM:** Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport (DFW) has issued a mandatory "full ground stop" due to a confirmed cyberattack on the Federal Aviation Administration’s (FAA) air traffic control systems, affecting all flights nationwide.

**VERDICT: MISLEADING**

**Why it’s being shared:** A viral TikTok video shows a “breaking news” overlay with a frantic airport scene, claiming that the DFW ground stop is the result of a foreign cyberattack, warning passengers that “all planes are locked in place.”

**The Fact Check:**
- A ground stop was issued for DFW around 4:30 PM CT, but the **official FAA cause** was a severe thunderstorm cell moving directly over the runway complex, with dangerous wind shear detected. The alert was a standard “weather ground stop,” not a cybersecurity lockout.
- The FAA’s own operational status page listed the cause as “WEATHER / THUNDERSTORMS,” not a cyber incident. Controllers in the DFW tower were actively managing reroutes, which would not happen during a confirmed cyberattack.
- No official source—including the FAA, DFW Airport, or the Department of Homeland Security—has reported any security breach.
- The viral video used stock footage from a storm in 2023 and an audio clip of a generic emergency siren.

**Bottom Line:** The DFW ground stop was real, but the claim that it was a cyberattack is **false**. It was a routine weather-related safety measure. Stay cautious of videos that mix real-time alerts with inflammatory, unattributed narratives.
