**HEADLINE: Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton Escapes Impeachment Conviction; Senate Acquits on All Articles**

**AUSTIN, TX – September 16, 2023** – In a landmark verdict delivered today, the Texas Senate voted to acquit suspended Attorney General Ken Paxton on all 16 articles of impeachment, effectively reinstating him to his elected office.

**WHO:** The verdict was rendered by the 31-member Texas Senate, acting as a court of impeachment. The proceedings were presided over by Lieutenant Governor Dan Patrick. The defendant, Attorney General Ken Paxton, was tried on allegations including bribery, abuse of public trust, and retaliation against whistleblowers from his own agency.

**WHAT:** The Senate voted to acquit Mr. Paxton on all counts. This decision concludes the first impeachment trial of a sitting Texas official in over a century. The final vote fell largely along party lines, with the prosecution failing to secure the two-thirds majority required for conviction on any single article.

**WHEN:** The verdict was announced following a total of 13 days of trial proceedings. The impeachment was formally initiated by the Republican-controlled Texas House of Representatives in May 2023.

**WHERE:** The trial took place in the Senate Chamber at the Texas State Capitol in Austin.

**WHY:** The Senate determined that the evidence presented by the House Board of Managers did not meet the legal threshold for conviction. The defense argued that the charges were based on unsubstantiated claims and constituted a politically motivated attack against a duly elected official. The acquittal allows Mr. Paxton to resume his duties immediately.

**CONCLUSION:** The acquittal of Attorney General Ken Paxton marks a significant and controversial conclusion to a high-profile legal and political battle. He returns to office facing no immediate legal repercussions from this impeachment process, though he continues to face a separate, pending federal securities fraud indictment.
