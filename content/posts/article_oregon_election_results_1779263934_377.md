**Oregon Election Results Finalized: Voters Approve Education Overhaul, Reject Housing Measure**

**PORTLAND, OREGON** – The Oregon Secretary of State’s office has officially certified the final results of the November 5 general election, confirming a decisive victory for a landmark education funding initiative and the rejection of a controversial statewide housing policy.

**What:** The final vote tally confirms the passage of Measure 118, the "Education and Community Investment Act," which will create a new tiered tax on large corporate sales. Preliminary results show 54.2% of voters approved the measure. Conversely, Measure 115, the "Fair Housing and Rent Control Expansion," failed by a margin of 58% to 42%, a wider gap than pre-election polling had suggested.

**Who:** The certified results were announced by the Elections Division of the Oregon Secretary of State's office. Key stakeholders include Governor Tina Kotek, state legislators, school district administrators who will implement the new funding stream, and landlords' associations who opposed Measure 115.

**Where:** The elections were conducted statewide across Oregon’s 36 counties, with final tallies from the vote-by-mail system completed in Multnomah, Washington, Clackamas, and Marion counties determining the outcome.

**When:** The results were certified at 10:00 AM Pacific Time today, marking the conclusion of the official canvassing period which began on November 7. The certification deadline was set by state statute.

**Why:** The passage of Measure 118 reflects broad voter support for addressing chronic underfunding of K-12 schools and community colleges, specifically for special education and career-technical programs. The rejection of Measure 115 is attributed to concerns among independent and suburban voters regarding potential negative impacts on rental housing supply and small-scale landlords.
