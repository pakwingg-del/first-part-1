**Viral News Snippet: Pete Hegseth’s Kentucky Campaign Rally Takes a Bizarre Turn**

**Claim:** A video circulating on social media claims that former Fox News host and potential 2024 Republican candidate Pete Hegseth, while campaigning in rural Kentucky, delivered a bizarre speech in which he accidentally endorsed “medicare for all” and referred to his own running mate as “a real horse.”

**Status: ⚠️ MISLEADING (FAKE AUDIO OVERLAY)**

**Fact-Check:** The clip, which has been shared over 100,000 times in the last 12 hours, is genuine video footage of Hegseth speaking at a small rally in Bowling Green, Kentucky. However, the audio has been doctored.
- **What’s Real:** Hegseth *did* say, “We need leaders who aren’t afraid to rein in Washington.” He *did* gesture to his campaign manager, Matt Vance, and say, “This guy right here? He’s a workhorse.”
- **What’s Fake:** The audio track has been spliced to make it sound like Hegseth said “medicare for all” and called Vance “a real horse.” The original audio shows he shouted *against* Medicare for All and referred to Vance as “a real *warhorse*” (a term of respect for a veteran).
- **Bottom Line:** The video is a satirical deepfake. Hegseth’s actual Kentucky speech focused on military spending and border security. Do not share the clip without clarifying it’s altered.
