**HEADLINE:** Supreme Court Issues Landmark Ruling; Unanimous Decision Expected to Reshape Federal Judicial Precedent

**DATELINE:** Washington, D.C. – December 21, 2024

**WHO:** The Honorable Justices of the Supreme Court of the United States, delivering a 9-0 opinion authored by Chief Justice John Roberts.

**WHAT:** The highest judicial body in the nation has issued a definitive ruling on *Carter v. Federal Regulatory Commission*, a case concerning the extent of executive agency authority. The decision explicitly overturns a 40-year-old legal doctrine that granted broad interpretive powers to administrative bodies. The ruling establishes a new, more restrictive standard that requires explicit Congressional approval for any agency action of major economic or political significance.

**WHEN:** The decision was announced at 10:00 AM Eastern Standard Time today, following an expedited review process initiated in the October term.

**WHERE:** The ruling was handed down from the Supreme Court building in Washington, D.C., and immediately takes effect for all pending federal litigation.

**WHY:** The Court determined that the previous framework, known as the *Apex Deference* doctrine, had led to an unconstitutional accumulation of power within the executive branch, effectively allowing agencies to act as legislators. The majority opinion argued that this structure violated the separation of powers enshrined in Articles I and II of the Constitution.

**HOW:** The opinion strictly delineates that agencies must limit their interpretations to the "clear and unambiguous text of federal statute." In the specific case of *Carter*, the Court found that the Commission had overstepped its statutory mandate. Legal analysts project this decision will trigger a wave of challenges to hundreds of existing federal regulations across environmental, financial, and energy sectors. The White House has scheduled a press briefing for 2:00 PM to address the immediate policy implications.
