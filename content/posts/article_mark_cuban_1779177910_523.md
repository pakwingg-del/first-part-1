**Viral News Snippet: Mark Cuban Drops a Psychological Truth Bomb — "Your Fear of Failure Is a Billion-Dollar Liability"**

In a raw, off-script moment during a recent podcast taping, billionaire investor and *Shark Tank* star **Mark Cuban** didn’t talk about crypto, the Mavericks, or Shark Tank deals. Instead, he dropped a psychological grenade that is now being shared by life coaches and therapists across social media.

“**Your fear of failure isn’t protecting you — it’s costing you your future.** I treat fear like a budget line item. I wake up and ask: *What’s the worst that can happen? Can I survive it?* If yes, I do it. Most people don’t fail because they’re bad. They fail because they refuse to start.”

Cuban went on to explain that he deliberately visualizes his worst-case scenario for 10 minutes each morning — a technique he calls “**Preemptive Failure Therapy**” — to rob anxiety of its power.

“The brain is a pattern machine. If you don't teach it that failure is survivable, it will keep you trapped in a cubicle of ‘someday.’ Your comfort zone is a beautiful, well-decorated prison. And the door is unlocked.”

The clip has already racked up 3.4 million views on X and is being reposted by mental health accounts as a “brutal but necessary dose of reality.”

**Life coach takeaway:** Cuban’s method mirrors Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) — by reframing fear as data instead of danger, you rewire your brain’s threat response. The next time you feel paralyzed by a big decision, try Cuban’s 10-minute fear budget. Write down the worst result. Ask: *Could I rebuild from there?* The answer is almost always yes — and that’s where your power begins.
