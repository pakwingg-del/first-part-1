**HEADLINE: MORAL DECAY WALKS AMONG US: "DEAD CITY" SPARKS FIERY DEBATE ON THE NORMALIZATION OF CANNIBALISM AND PARENTAL FAILURE**

**By Mortimer Vale, Moral Critic**

**NEW YORK (Moral Watch)** – In a cultural moment that would have been unthinkable a decade ago, millions of viewers are weekly consuming *The Walking Dead: Dead City*—a show that doesn’t just depict the apocalypse, but actively romanticizes the abandonment of moral absolutes. And the latest episode has crossed a line that should make every decent citizen shudder.

Let’s be clear: This isn’t just a show about zombies. It’s a step-by-step guide to the erosion of the human soul. The latest plot point follows Maggie Rhee, a character once hailed as a symbol of grieving motherhood, as she deliberately weaponizes her own child to manipulate a cannibalistic faction. This isn’t survival instinct—it’s a calculated rejection of the sacred bond between parent and child.

But the true corruption lies in Manhattan’s “Lord of the Flies” society, framed as a pragmatic alternative to pre-plague morals. The show asks us to cheer for a man known as “The Croat” who tortures, betrays, and—sin of all sins—makes depravity look like a viable leadership strategy. When a child characters suggest that “maybe the old world was the real lie,” we aren’t witnessing art; we are watching the normalization of nihilism.

This isn’t entertainment. It is a moral surrender. By glamorizing a world where honor, decency, and the sanctity of life are treated as disposable baggage, *Dead City* doesn’t just reflect the downfall of society—it *narrates it in real time*.
