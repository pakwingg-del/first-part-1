**GOOGLE IO: SEARCH REINVENTED, AI NOW EXECUTES TASKS VIA SUBSCRIPTION**

Alphabet CEO Sundar Pichai flipped the switch at Google I/O today, unveiling an AI model that doesn't just answer questions—it books appointments, buys products, and fills tax forms on your behalf. The catch? A paid tier, "Google One AI Premium," starting at $19.99/month.

**Why this hits:** For the first time, Google is charging for its core search product. By moving from ad-supported search to a subscription model for task execution, the company is betting enterprise and power users will pay to bypass noise and get actions done. Revenue diversification strategy? Yes. Risky? Only if the AI fumbles a transaction.

**CEO takeaway:** This is a strategic pivot from information to execution. If sticky, Google captures higher LTV per user and reduces reliance on ad revenue. If users churn, it erodes trust in the core product. Watch for enterprise adoption metrics in Q4.

*Bottom line: Google is trying to monetize attention, not just traffic. This is the first real test of AI-as-service at scale.*
