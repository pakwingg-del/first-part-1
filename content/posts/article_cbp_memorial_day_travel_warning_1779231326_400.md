**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About CBP’s Memorial Day Travel Warning**

🚨 **Record-Breaking Wait Times Expected at Southern Border Crossings**
- CBP is warning that travel delays could exceed **3–4 hours** at high-volume ports of entry (e.g., San Ysidro, El Paso, Nogales) due to a surge in both recreational travel and continued migration surges. Best advice: cross before 6 AM or after 8 PM—or avoid peak holiday weekend entirely.

🛂 **Non-Citizens Face Extra Scrutiny on ‘Trusted Traveler’ Lanes**
- CBP has activated **heightened vetting protocols** for Global Entry and SENTRI members. Roving officers may pull travelers aside for secondary inspection if travel patterns or documentation raise flags—even if you have pre-clearance.

📦 **Agricultural Checkpoints Are Going High-Tech (and Harder to Dodge)**
- New AI-driven X-ray scanners at checkpoints like Jacumba and Yuma can now detect **fruit, vegetables, and undeclared meats** hidden in luggage or vehicle trunks. Fines for undeclared produce now start at **$1,000 per violation**.

📝 **Don’t Forget: Real ID Deadline (May 7) Impacts Your Airport Return**
- While flying within the US doesn’t require Real ID until May 7, **CBP is reminding travelers** that after that date, a standard license will be rejected at TSA checkpoints. If you're returning from a cross-border road trip and flying home, you must have a Real ID, passport, or other federally approved ID.
