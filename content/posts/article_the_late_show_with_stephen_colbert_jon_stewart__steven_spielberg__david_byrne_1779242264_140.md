AITA for thinking the Late Show just became the most aggressively boomer-core thing on TV?

So we got Stephen Colbert, Jon Stewart, Steven Spielberg, and David Byrne all crammed into one segment like the world's most uncomfortable orgy of self-congratulatory nostalgia. Spielberg's there to pitch another "personal, deeply moving" project that's just him reenacting his own Wikipedia page, Stewart's doing the "old man yells at cloud" bit but now with a thousand-yard stare, and Byrne is just... existing in a giant gray suit, probably trying to calculate the carbon footprint of his own presence.

And Colbert? He's just the confused wedding DJ trying to keep everyone's grandpa energy in check.

TL;DR: Four guys who peaked 30 years ago tried to "read the zeitgeist" and accidentally summoned a ghost from 2008.
