**Headline:** "AI GODFATHER KARPATHY DROPS BOMBSHELL: 'WE’RE TRAINING OUR CHILDREN TO BE OBSOLETE'"  

**Byline:** Moral Critic, Ethics Desk  

**In a livestream that has since been scrubbed from the internet, former OpenAI and Tesla AI director Andrej Karpathy delivered a chilling verdict on humanity’s future: “We are not building artificial intelligence. We are building artificial children—and we are abandoning the real ones.”**  

The viral clip, captured by a viewer before it vanished, shows Karpathy gesturing to a whiteboard overflowing with neural network diagrams. “Every time we optimize a model to replace a junior coder, a copywriter, or a radiologist, we are telling the next generation: ‘Your curiosity, your struggle, your human growth—none of it matters.’ We are engineering a society where the path to purpose is erased before you can walk it.”  

Karpathy’s remarks targeted the recent explosion of “agentic AI”—systems that can plan, execute, and learn autonomously. “We celebrate these systems as marvels of efficiency. But what happens when a 12-year-old asks, ‘Why should I learn to write, to argue, to fail, to revise?’ The answer is: They won’t need to. And in that vacuum, we will raise a generation that has never sharpened a skill, never felt the ache of mastery, never known the dignity of earning their place.”  

The moral outrage is already boiling over in parenting forums and academic councils. “This isn’t progress—it’s a slow-motion lobotomy of the human spirit,” writes Dr. Elena Voss, a developmental psychologist at Stanford. “Karpathy is the canary in the coal mine, and he’s screaming.”
