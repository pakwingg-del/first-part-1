**Subject: Fox’s “Highway to Heaven” Reboot: Faith, ROI, and the New Content Frontier**

**Executive Summary**

Fox is rebooting *Highway to Heaven* as a premium drama series, pivoting from the 1980s family comedy to a serialized, high-stakes format. The new concept centers on a modern-day angel paired with a cynical, data-driven tech entrepreneur. This is not nostalgia bait—it’s a calculated bet on the under-monetized faith-based audience in a market demanding lower-risk, high-return IP.

**Why This Matters**

- **Targeting a $2B Gap**: Faith-based content generates ~$2B annually (Barna Group), yet remains 80% under-served by streaming/premium cable. Fox is filling a void left by Hallmark and Pure Flix with higher production value and broader demographic appeal.
- **Proven Demand**: *The Chosen* proved audiences will pay premium subscription rates for serialized spiritual content. Fox’s move is a direct hedge against declining linear ratings.
- **Structural Advantage**: Reboots carry 30% lower marketing spend vs. new IP (Nielsen). This is a cost-efficient land-grab.

**Strategic Implications for Competitors**

Expect Disney/ABC and NBC to fast-track similar faith-adjacent reboots. The window for first-mover advantage is 12–18 months. This is no longer a niche—it’s a measurable, scalable audience.

**Recommendation**: Monitor Fox’s advertising pre-sales and CPMs for faith-based blocks. If premium CPMs exceed network average by 15%+, this signals a durable shift in advertiser appetite for “values-forward” content.

**Bottom Line**: Fox is not remaking a show; it’s creating a capital-efficient vertical. Faith is the new premium.
