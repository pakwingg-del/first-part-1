**Mandalorian and Grogu spotted at Port Arthur Wal-Mart: This is what happens when you bring space bounty hunters into a small town**

Posted in *Hickory Creek Community Connection (Unofficial)*

📸 **See photo below**

I’m all for community theater and kids’ birthday parties, but I think we’ve officially jumped the shark. My sister-in-law was just trying to get her kids some school supplies at the Wal-Mart off Route 6, and she sends me this: a fully armored *Mandalorian* pushing a cart with a little green Grogu (looking suspiciously like a painted ukulele with felt ears) through the frozen food aisle.

Look, I get it. It’s funny for five seconds. But here’s the thing nobody wants to say: this is the same Wal-Mart where my Aunt Carol had her purse snatched last summer. Now we’ve got some guy in beskar steel helmet blocking the dairy coolers so he can take 47 selfies with teenagers? And that Grogu? It was SWEATING. The kid inside that suit has got to be roasting.

Use some common sense, people. You want to do a Star Wars parade? Go to the county fairgrounds. Don’t bring your bounty hunter cosplay into a working grocery store where the rest of us are just trying to find Hamburger Helper. The manager said they had to call security because the Mandalorian refused to take off his helmet for the self-checkout camera. That’s real life, folks. Not a movie.

Also—that Grogu clearly had a juice box straw poking out the side. If he’s supposed to be “50 years old,” why is he drinking apple juice in the frozen pizza aisle? Make it make sense.
