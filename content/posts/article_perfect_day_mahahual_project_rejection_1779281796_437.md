**SUBJECT: MAHAHUAL DEVELOPMENT BLOCKED — $2B Project Vetoed After Local Uprising**

**The News:**  
The “Perfect Day Mahahual” mega-resort—a planned $2 billion, 500-acre cruise destination on Mexico’s Costa Maya—has been officially rejected by federal regulators. The project, backed by Royal Caribbean and local investors, was halted following sustained opposition from environmental groups and indigenous communities.

**Why It Matters:**  
This is the second major Caribbean cruise port project to collapse in 12 months under environmental and social pressure. The veto signals a structural shift: regulators are now prioritizing conservation over concession revenue. For cruise lines, this shrinks the pipeline of new private destinations—a critical growth lever post-pandemic.

**Key Numbers:**
- $2B projected investment (lost)
- 1.2 million annual passenger capacity (foregone)
- 3,000 construction jobs (scrapped)
- 85% of surveyed local residents opposed the project

**Business Impact:**
- Royal Caribbean loses a planned competitive advantage in the Western Caribbean
- Port developers face higher regulatory and reputational risk
- Alternative destinations (Honduras, Belize, Dominican Republic) gain pricing power

**The Bottom Line:**  
Community veto power is now a material risk in Caribbean infrastructure development. Expect higher costs, longer timelines, and fewer greenlit projects. CEOs evaluating Latin American destinations should bake in 18–24 months of regulatory and legal delays—minimum.

**Next Watch:**  
Similar projects in Cozumel and Roatán are now under renewed scrutiny. Any delays there will further compress cruise capacity growth in 2025–2026.
