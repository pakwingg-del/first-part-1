**Viral News Snippet: "Panic at the Pantry? The Truth Behind the Kroger Garlic Crouton Recall"**

**The Rumor:**
A viral TikTok video claims that Kroger’s "Private Selection Garlic Parmesan Croutons" have been recalled nationwide because they were "accidentally packed with a hallucinogenic herb" resembling garlic, causing shoppers to report "tripping in the deli aisle." The video, which has racked up 2.3 million views, warns, "Check your pantry—this isn’t a drill!"

**The Reality (Fact-Check):* *
**FAKE NEWS.** Here’s the verified truth:
- Kroger *did* issue a limited recall on **August 14, 2024**, for a specific batch of **"Private Selection Garlic Parmesan Croutons"** (5 oz, UPC 11110-45009, code dates JUN 07 2025 through DEC 26 2025). However, the real reason was **undeclared wheat and soy allergens**—not a hallucinogen. A supplier error resulted in croutons containing bread crumbs that weren’t listed on the label, posing a risk to people with food allergies.
- **No "magic herb" or psychedelic properties were involved.** The viral "tripping" story appears to be a hoax, possibly based on an old, unrelated meme about "magic mushroom croutons." The FDA has not confirmed any reports of intoxication.
- **Action:** If you have the recalled batch, return it to Kroger for a full refund. If you have no food allergies, you can safely eat them—you won’t go on a "cheese-and-garlic-fueled journey."

**Bottom Line:** The recall is real, but the "hallucinogenic" claim is false. Don’t raid your pantry in panic—unless you have a wheat allergy.
