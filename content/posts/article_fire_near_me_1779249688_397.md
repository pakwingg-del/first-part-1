**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Fire Near Me**

- **🚨 Evacuation Orders Are Immediate: If your zone is listed, leave now.** Authorities are issuing **mandatory evacuation orders** for specific grids (check your local emergency page). Do not wait for flames—smoke inhalation and road blockages can trap you in minutes. Pack a "go bag" with IDs, meds, and pet essentials.

- **🔥 Watch for "Spot Fires" in Your Neighborhood—They Spread in Seconds.** Wind can carry burning embers up to **1 mile ahead of the main fire**. Look for embers landing on dry grass, wood fences, or roofs. If you see a new fire start near your home, call 911 *and* try to extinguish it *only* if it's small and you have water.

- **💨 Air Quality Is Hazardous—Seal Your Home Now.** Smoke particulates can cause lung damage from 20 minutes of exposure. Close all windows, seal gaps in doors with towels, and run an air purifier on high. If you smell smoke indoors, you're already at risk—move to a room with filtered air or leave.

- **🚙 Know Your Escape Routes—And Have a Backup.** Fires can shift direction suddenly. Plot **2 routes out of your area** on paper (cell towers may fail). Avoid overpasses and narrow roads near thick brush. If trapped in a vehicle, park away from vegetation, close vents, and cover yourself with a wool blanket.

- **📱 False Alarms Are Rampant—Verify Before Acting.** Social media rumors about fire locations can cause panic. Only trust official sources: your county's emergency alert system, local fire department social accounts, and sound from official sirens (not car horns). Check **Google Maps** for road closures—they update faster than any news.
