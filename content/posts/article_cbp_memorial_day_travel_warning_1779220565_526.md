**Headline:** *“Don’t Let Your Road Trip Become a Therapy Session—Why CBP’s Memorial Day Warning Is the Wake-Up Call You Needed”*

**Port St. Lucie, FL** — As millions gear up for the unofficial start of summer, U.S. Customs and Border Protection just dropped a sobering Memorial Day travel warning: expect longer waits, stricter document checks, and heightened security. But life coach Dr. Rachel Teller sees a deeper message unfolding. “People are packing their cars with stress, not just luggage,” she says. “We’ve normalized rushing through life like we’re crossing a border—always in a hurry, always defensive. The CBP warning isn’t about fear; it’s a permission slip to slow down.” Whether you’re heading to the beach or the BBQ pit, Teller advises travelers to treat the journey as a mindfulness reset: “The wait is not a waste—it’s your chance to breathe, connect with your co-pilot, and ask yourself: ‘What am I still carrying that I need to leave behind at this checkpoint?’” Because sometimes, the toughest border to cross is the one inside your own head.
