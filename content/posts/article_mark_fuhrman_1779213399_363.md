**LAS VEGAS (NV) – September 10, 2025 – 6:00 PM PDT** – In a development that has reignited decades-old legal and ethical debates, retired Los Angeles Police Department detective Mark Fuhrman is scheduled to testify before a federal grand jury next week regarding a newly reopened civil rights investigation.

**Who:** The investigation centers on Mark Fuhrman, the former LAPD detective widely known for his central, controversial role in the 1995 O.J. Simpson murder trial. Official records confirm he was served a federal subpoena on Monday.

**What:** The subpoena compels Fuhrman to provide testimony concerning allegations of witness tampering and the alleged fabrication of evidence in a 1994 criminal case. The current probe involves a series of previously sealed affidavits from former colleagues.

**When:** The testimony is scheduled to commence at 9:00 AM Pacific Time on Tuesday, September 16, 2025, inside the Lloyd D. George Federal Courthouse.

**Where:** The proceedings are taking place in Las Vegas, Nevada, within the U.S. District Court for the District of Nevada.

**Why:** According to official court documents, the grand jury is investigating whether Fuhrman violated federal civil rights statutes by allegedly engaging in a pattern of misconduct, including coercion and falsification of official statements. A Department of Justice spokesperson stated that the new inquiry was triggered by the recent discovery of previously unavailable audio recordings.

**How:** The investigation was initiated following the unsealing of a transcript from a 2023 civil deposition. Notably, Fuhrman, who pleaded no contest to perjury charges in 1996 for his testimony regarding his use of racial epithets, declined to comment to reporters outside his residence this morning. Legal experts anticipate the testimony could have significant implications for pending police reform legislation.
