**FACT CHECK: 🚨 VIRAL RUMOR — Kroger Cheese & Garlic Croutons Recall**  

**CLAIM:** Social media posts are claiming that Kroger has issued an **emergency recall** on their *Cheese & Garlic Croutons* due to reports of **plastic shards** and **undeclared allergens** (specifically undeclared peanuts) found in bags sold in 12 states.  

**VERDICT: MOSTLY FALSE — WITH A KERNEL OF TRUTH**  

✅ **What's real:** Kroger *did* issue a small, voluntary recall on March 14, 2025, for a limited lot of their *Private Selection Cheese & Garlic Croutons* (5.5 oz bags, UPC 11110-41392) sold in **Ohio, Kentucky, and Indiana only**. The recall was triggered by a “potential packaging defect” (possible small pieces of hard plastic from the twist-tie mechanism). No injuries were reported.  

❌ **What's fake:** The viral claim that this is an “emergency multi-state recall” due to “undeclared peanuts” is **completely fabricated**. A viral TikTok video from a parody account (@RecallWatchFake) falsely stitched actual recall photos with a fake warning about peanut allergy risks. The FDA has not listed this as a Class I recall (highest risk), and Kroger confirms no peanut cross-contamination exists.  

**BOTTOM LINE:** The real recall is **minor, regional, and only about plastic bits** — not peanuts. The “12-state emergency” story is a hoax. Check your bag if you bought in OH/KY/IN, but DON’T panic-share the fake peanut claim.  

**Rating:** 🟡 **Mixed Bogus** (real recall, fake details)
