**Subject:** PlayStation Plus Subscription Price Hike Announced

**Summary:**
Sony has announced a significant price increase for its PlayStation Plus subscription tiers. Effective immediately (or from the next billing cycle), the Essential plan rises from $9.99 to $12.99 per month, Extra from $14.99 to $17.99, and Premium from $17.99 to $21.99. Annual subscriptions see similar proportional increases.

**Business Impact:**

- **Revenue Boost:** This is a **direct, predictable, high-margin revenue lift** for Sony’s gaming segment, expected to add hundreds of millions annually without a corresponding increase in content spend.
- **Customer Churn Risk:** Historically, price hikes for subscription services (like Netflix) trigger initial churn of ~5-10%. However, Sony’s stickiness—via game libraries, cloud saves, and online multiplayer requirements—dampens this.
- **Strategic Signal:** This move aligns with Sony’s pivot to **higher ARPU** (average revenue per user) as hardware margins tighten. It signals confidence in their content pipeline (e.g., *Marvel’s Spider-Man 2* DLC, first-party exclusives).

**CEO Take:**
“Sony is commoditizing its loyal user base. The math works for them, but the message is dangerous: subscribers now pay 30% more for the same product. This tests the ceiling of ‘essential’ entertainment pricing. Rivals (Microsoft, Nintendo) will watch closely. If churn stays below 8%, expect similar moves industry-wide.”
