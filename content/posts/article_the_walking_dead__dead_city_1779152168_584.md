In a fiery new op-ed that has gone viral across parenting forums and church groups, Dr. Helena Vance, a self-described "moral archaeologist," has declared the popular spin-off *The Walking Dead: Dead City* to be "visual pornography of the soul." Vance argues that the show’s focus on a post-apocalyptic Manhattan—where survivors trade children for food and grind bones into fertilizer—doesn’t just depict a broken world; it actively teaches viewers "to root for the cannibal."

"The show is no longer about survival. It is about the celebration of moral erosion," Vance wrote in a piece shared over 400,000 times in 24 hours. "We are watching a society that has not only lost its infrastructure, but its conscience. And worse—we cheer when the main character, Negan, a former rapist and mass murderer, becomes the hero. This isn't fiction. This is a tutorial for the collapse of American decency."

Vance specifically targets a scene where Negan and Maggie form a "grudging alliance" to save a child from a cult that worships a decaying skyscraper. "They are normalizing the idea that a man who beat a woman’s husband to death with a baseball bat is now a necessary evil. This is the 'lesser of two evils' fallacy applied to the human soul. It teaches our children that morality is negotiable if the enemy is worse."
