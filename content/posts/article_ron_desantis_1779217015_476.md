**VERDICT: MISLEADING / FAKE CONTEXT**

**Viral Snippet:**
"BREAKING: Florida Governor Ron DeSantis officially signs executive order banning all COVID-19 vaccines and masks in public schools, effective immediately. Doctors who recommend boosters could lose their license."

**Fact-Check Analysis:**
- **FALSE:** No such executive order exists. DeSantis has been vocal against *mandates*, but has never banned the vaccine or masks entirely. In fact, the state's Surgeon General has promoted unproven treatments like ivermectin during press conferences.
- **MISLEADING TACTIC:** The snippet conflates "no mandates" with "full ban." Florida law currently *prohibits* mask mandates in schools, but individuals can still choose to wear masks or receive vaccines.
- **Doctor License Claim:** No Florida law or rule revokes licenses for recommending boosters. The Florida Board of Medicine has warned against misinformation, but not against standard medical advice.
- **Origin Check:** This exact wording appeared in multiple satirical Facebook pages and spam accounts in late 2023, often linking to fake news sites disguised as "The Florida Sun."

**Bottom Line:** The claim is fabricated. No ban on vaccines or boosters is in effect. Always check official state executive orders or the Florida Department of Health press releases before sharing.
