**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**SANDUSKY, Ohio — A mechanical malfunction on the 'Millennium Force' roller coaster at Cedar Point amusement park has resulted in a series of critical system failures, leading to a temporary evacuation of guests and an ongoing, comprehensive safety investigation.**

**Who:** Officials from Cedar Point Amusement Park and the Ohio Department of Agriculture’s Division of Amusement Ride Safety.

**What:** A complete operational shutdown of the 'Millennium Force' giga-coaster following a reported "high-speed control anomaly" during a routine operational cycle. The incident involved a partial loss of braking power on the ride’s final descent, causing the train to overshoot the designated station stopping point by an estimated 15 feet. No injuries were reported. Park maintenance teams initiated a manual restraint release and escalator-assisted evacuation of all 36 passengers from the ride's final brake run.

**Where:** Cedar Point Amusement Park, Sandusky, Ohio. The incident occurred on the 310-foot-tall steel track structure, specifically within the final brake segment immediately preceding the loading station.

**When:** Saturday, at approximately 3:17 PM Eastern Time, during peak operational hours.

**Why:** Preliminary reports indicate a failure within the ride’s redundant electro-magnetic braking system. A post-incident diagnostic revealed a critical fault in the main power distribution unit to the final brake run. A secondary, mechanical friction braking system—designed as a fail-safe—did engage but was insufficient to bring the train to a complete stop at the intended platform location. The Ohio Department of Agriculture has issued an immediate suspension of the ride's operating permit pending a full forensic analysis of the control software and brake actuator hardware.
