# Glitch in the Matrix: Four Startup Founders Discover They Shared the Same Nightmare on the Same Night

**TECH VALLEY —** In what data analysts are calling a "statistical impossibility," four startup founders from different cities have come forward claiming they experienced identical nightmares on the exact same night — and their sleep-tracking devices prove it.

The founders — based in San Francisco, Tokyo, Berlin, and Bangalore — each reported waking at 3:33 AM local time on March 15 after dreaming of a silent office with all their whiteboards erased, their code wiped blank, and a single blinking cursor on a terminal screen reading: "THIS DREAM IS NOT YOURS."

The AI sleep-data aggregator *Somnium* flagged the coincidence when its algorithm detected a 0.0000012% probability event: all four users experienced the exact same REM sleep anomaly pattern during the same 17-minute window, despite being in different time zones.

"We thought our system was broken," said Somnium Chief Data Officer Lena Park. "But the biometric markers — heart rate, eye movement, cortisol spikes — were almost identical. It appears their brains synchronized across continents."

The founders claim they've never met.

Somnium has now locked down the data for "security review." Insiders say the anomaly has appeared in preliminary logs from at least two other founders — who have not yet come forward.
