**FACT CHECK: Viral Rumor vs. Reality on the DFW Ground Stop**

**Viral Post (Claimed to be from a "Duty Pilot"):**

> *🚨 BREAKING: DFW fully grounded. Indefinite ground stop now in effect. My source inside the tower says "non-weather related security breach." No aircraft can depart. Arrivals being diverted to Love Field. Expect chaos. They are NOT telling the public the real reason. #DFWShutdown*

**Where it's spreading:**
X (formerly Twitter), TikTok comment sections, and a handful of fringe aviation forums.

**VERDICT: MISLEADING / FALSE**

Our check with the FAA's Air Traffic Control System Command Center (ATCSCC) and Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport's official comms confirms the following:

1. **Status:** A **short-duration ground stop** (approx 30-45 minutes) was issued for DFW around 2:15 PM local time.
2. **Official Reason:** **Lightning within 5 nautical miles of the airport.** This is a standard safety protocol (the "Lightning in the Vicinity" or LIRV procedure) to protect ramp workers loading baggage and fueling aircraft.
3. **"Security Breach" Hoax:** There is **zero** evidence of a security breach. The "source inside the tower" claim appears to be fabricated. TSA and DFW Airport Police confirm no lockdown, no suspicious packages, and no unauthorized access.
4. **"Diverted to Love Field" Reality Check:** Love Field (DAL) is a smaller airport without the customs and gate capacity to handle large DFW aircraft. **Most arrivals were put in holding patterns** over East Texas. *Zero* widebody international flights were diverted to DAL—only a few small private/charter jets opted to land there.
