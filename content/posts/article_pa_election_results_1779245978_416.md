**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Pennsylvania Election Results**

- **The "Blue Wall" Brick is Back:** Joe Biden officially flipped Pennsylvania back to the Democratic column, securing its 20 electoral votes after Trump’s narrow 2016 win. This win was fueled by a massive surge in mail-in ballots in Philadelphia and its suburbs, effectively burying Trump’s early lead on election night.

- **The Red Mirage, Then the Blue Shift:** The vote count followed the now-familiar pattern: Trump jumped out to a huge lead on election night, thanks to in-person voting being counted first. However, as counties like Allegheny (Pittsburgh) and Philadelphia processed their mail-in ballots over the next three days, the lead evaporated, turning into a decisive win for Biden.

- **Suburban Swan Song:** The key demographic shift wasn't just in cities. The traditionally red-leaning Philadelphia suburbs—namely Chester, Delaware, and Montgomery counties—swung heavily toward Biden. This wasn't just a Trump rejection; it was a complete realignment of suburban voters away from the GOP, a trend experts say could be permanent.

- **The "Scranton vs. Erie" Narrative Collapsed:** Trump’s 2020 campaign heavily targeted the western and northern "T" of the state (Erie, Luzerne, Northampton) to replicate his 2016 margins. While he did well there, he failed to run up the score enough to offset the massive Democratic turnout in the southeast. The classic "Rust Belt populist" strategy didn't have the juice it needed.

- **The Supreme Court is in the Rearview Mirror:** This election was a stress test for the 2020 U.S. Supreme Court ruling that allowed Pennsylvania to count ballots received up to three days after Election Day.
