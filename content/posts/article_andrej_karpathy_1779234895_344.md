**Andrej Karpathy: "This Feels Like the '1929 of Football' — Except the Ball is Code"**

Then came Black Tuesday. In 2025, everyone thinks AGI is guaranteed by scaling alone. But I look at the open-source chaos — 50 new ‘GPT-4-beating’ models a week, no curation, no guardrails — and I see the same euphoria before the crash.”  

His analogy? The late-1920s American obsession with football as a moral and technological panacea — complete with mass mania, unchecked speculation on new “systems,” and a belief that bigger stadiums (i.e., compute) automatically meant better outcomes. “Football in 1929 had its own ‘superintelligence fever’ — until the Depression hit and people realized the game couldn’t feed them,” Karpathy added. “We’re building stadiums for a game we haven’t even invented yet.”
