**Cedar Point, OH** – A controversial video uploaded by a user claiming to be a former ride engineer has gone viral, alleging that the legendary Millennium Force roller coaster at Cedar Point has been secretly generating a localized "time dilation effect" at the top of its 310-foot cable lift hill.

**REAL vs. FAKE:** ❌ **FAKE.**
**Fact-Check:** The video has been debunked by both Cedar Point’s official account and Dr. Elena Vance, a physicist at MIT. The "time jump" is the result of a glitchy smartphone app that was mis-syncing with the park’s Wi-Fi network. Furthermore, a 0.00000001-second relativistic effect—if possible on Earth—would require speeds near 670 million mph, not 80 mph. The magnetic braking system in question is just standard electromagnetic brakes used on modern coasters, with zero exotic physics.
