**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Evergy Outage Map Right Now**

- **Real-Time vs. Reported Outages:** The map doesn't always update second-by-second. It shows "reported" outages, so if you just lost power, there’s a lag of 5-15 minutes before your address "pops" on the grid. Don't panic if you see no dot—it doesn't mean you're alone in the dark.

- **The Color Code is Your Quick-Glance Survival Guide:** Orange dots mean *fewer than 50* customers affected. Dark red or purple? That’s the "big one"—*over 500+* customers out in a concentrated zone. If you see a purple cluster, expect restoration times to be 4-8 hours, not 4 minutes.

- **The "Estimated Restoration Time" is a Liar (Kind of):** That "10:00 PM" estimate is an automated calculation based on the *initial* problem. If a crew finds a broken pole or a tree in a transformer, that time instantly becomes obsolete. Always check the "Crew Status" note below the time—if it says "assessing," don't trust the ETA.

- **Zoom In on "Affected Customers" to Predict Wait Times:** The number next to your outage dot is your best friend. A dot with *12 customers* often means a single fuse blew—fix time: ~1 hour. A dot with *1,200 customers*? That lines up with a substation trip or downed main line—fix time: 4-8+ hours. Grab the flashlights.
