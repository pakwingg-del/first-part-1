**WASHINGTON, D.C. – November 6, 2024**

**HEADLINE: Representative Thomas Massie Secures Reelection to U.S. House in Kentucky's 4th Congressional District**

**LOCATION:** Northern Kentucky / Washington, D.C.

**SUBJECT:** Representative Thomas Massie (R-KY)

**EVENT:** 2024 General Election for the U.S. House of Representatives, Kentucky’s 4th Congressional District.

**RESULT:** Republican incumbent Thomas Massie has successfully won reelection.

**TIMELINE:** According to official returns finalized earlier today, Representative Massie defeated his Democratic challenger, securing his tenth term in Congress. With 100% of precincts reporting, Massie captured approximately 67% of the vote, a decisive margin that underscores his continued stronghold in the predominantly Republican district.

**WHY:** The outcome was largely anticipated, given the district’s conservative leanings and Representative Massie’s established profile as a libertarian-leaning fiscal conservative. His campaign emphasized constitutional governance, opposition to federal spending increases, and a commitment to limiting executive authority.

**DETAILS & CONTEXT:**
Representative Massie, first elected in a special election in 2012, has been a prominent, often controversial, figure within the House Republican Conference. Known for his strict adherence to procedural rules and his willingness to break with party leadership on major spending bills, Massie’s victory signals continued support for his independent brand of conservatism in Northern Kentucky.

In a formal statement released from his district office, Massie thanked constituents for their trust, reaffirming his dedication to "reducing the size and scope of the federal government." He is expected to return to Washington for the new congressional session, where his role as a procedural hawk and critic of bipartisan infrastructure and funding packages will remain a defining feature of his legislative agenda.
