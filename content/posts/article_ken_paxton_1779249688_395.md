**TOP 5 THINGS YOU NEED TO KNOW ABOUT THE KEN PAXTON SAGA (AND WHY IT'S NOT OVER YET)**

*   **He was acquitted, but not cleared.** After a historic impeachment trial in the Texas Senate, Ken Paxton was acquitted on all 16 articles. BUT - the trial was about *suspension*, not removal from office. The real legal jeopardy isn't gone. He's still under federal investigation for securities fraud (a decade-old case) and the whistleblower lawsuit that started this whole mess is still alive and kicking.
*   **The whistleblower lawsuit is his biggest immediate threat.** The four former top deputies who reported him to the FBI aren't going away. They're suing for wrongful termination under the Texas Whistleblower Act. If they win, this isn't just a political embarrassment - it comes with a massive payout from state funds. Paxton tried to settle it secretly for $3.3 million of taxpayer money *before* the impeachment, which is what actually set off the House investigation.
*   **His political future is a tightrope walk.** He's back in office, but he's politically radioactive to mainstream Republicans. He's lost the support of the "establishment" wing of the Texas GOP (the House Speaker, Lt. Gov. Dan Patrick). However, he's doubled down on the MAGA base, endorsing primary challenges against the Republicans who voted to impeach him. He's betting everything on grassroots outrage outlasting institutional memory.
*   **The "rogue agency" narrative is a double-edged sword.** Paxton's defense during the trial was that the Attorney General's Office is a "lawless, rogue agency" that his own staff had corrupted against him. He essentially argued he can't be held responsible for the actions of the people he was supposed to be managing.
