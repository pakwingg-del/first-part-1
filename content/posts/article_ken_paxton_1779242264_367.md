**BREAKING: KEN PAXTON – TEXAS ATTORNEY GENERAL’S FATE SEALED IN HISTORIC IMPEACHMENT TRIAL**

AUSTIN, TX – In a landmark verdict that has sent shockwaves through the American political landscape, the Texas Senate has voted to acquit suspended Attorney General Ken Paxton on all 16 articles of impeachment, effectively allowing him to remain in office.

**Who:** The central figure is Ken Paxton, a three-term Republican Attorney General of Texas.

**What:** The Texas Senate’s vote concluded a historic impeachment trial. Specifically, the chamber voted on 16 articles related to allegations of bribery, obstruction of justice, and abuse of public trust. Paxton was acquitted on all counts, failing to reach the two-thirds majority required for conviction even on a single article.

**When:** The verdict was delivered on Saturday, September 16, 2023, following a two-week trial.

**Where:** The trial was conducted in the Texas Senate chamber at the State Capitol in Austin, Texas.

**Why:** Paxton was impeached by the Republican-controlled Texas House in May on accusations of misconduct, primarily concerning his relationship with a political donor, Nate Paul. Whistleblowers from Paxton’s own office alleged he used his power to assist Paul in exchange for personal benefits, including a private legal defense and an affair. The defense argued the accusations were politically motivated and based on hearsay. The Senate’s acquittal effectively clears the path for Paxton to resume his full duties, including his ongoing legal battles against the Biden administration and his leadership of the state’s law enforcement division.
