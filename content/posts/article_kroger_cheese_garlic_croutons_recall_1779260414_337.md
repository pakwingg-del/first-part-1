**HEADLINE: The Crouton Crisis of ’24: Is This Kroger’s “Tylenol Moment”?**

**CINCINNATI, OH** — A routine recall of Kroger Cheese & Garlic Croutons has sparked an unlikely historical firestorm online, with food safety historians drawing unsettling parallels to the 1982 Chicago Tylenol murders.

The recall, issued over undeclared milk and wheat allergens, was initially dismissed as a label slip. But when consumer reports of “an unusually bitter, chemical aftertaste” flooded the FDA hotline, the narrative shifted.

“We’re seeing a distinct ‘panic pattern’ not seen since the cyanide-laced capsules,” tweeted Dr. Helena Voss, a food supply chain historian at MIT. “The Tylenol scare decimated a trust in over-the-counter capsules forever. If these croutons are linked to deliberate tampering—not just a labeling error—this is the iceberg that sunk the iceberg lettuce.”

The comparison has gone viral, with #CroutonCrisis and #GarlicGate trending. Memes juxtapose a crouton bag with a Tylenol bottle. Others point to the “Great Salad Scare of ’06” (the E. coli spinach outbreak) as a closer parallel.

Kroger insists this is “strictly an allergen compliance issue,” but the damage may be done. Analysts warn that for a generation that watches *The Fall of the House of Usher* on a loop, any recall with a hint of “poisoned parmesan” is a trigger.
