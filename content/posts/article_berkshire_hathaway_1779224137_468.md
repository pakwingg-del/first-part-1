**Viral News Snippet: "Warren Buffett Dumps Entire Apple Stake, Buys Shiba Inu Coin"**

**Verdict: FAKE**

Social media is ablaze with a fabricated screenshot purportedly from a Bloomberg terminal claiming that Berkshire Hathaway has liquidated its entire $150 billion Apple position and used the proceeds to purchase Shiba Inu (SHIB) cryptocurrency, calling it "the new digital gold for the Omaha oracle."

**Why it's fake:**
1. **Buffett's Philosophy**: Warren Buffett has famously called Bitcoin "rat poison squared" and has repeatedly stated he has no interest in crypto assets, viewing them as non-productive.
2. **No SEC Filing**: Berkshire Hathaway has not filed any Form 13F with the SEC indicating a change in its Apple stake for Q1 2025. The last filing showed Apple as its largest holding.
3. **Image Artifacts**: The viral screenshot contains obvious CGI telltales—compressed timestamps, a stock ticker code (SOLANA-SHIB) that doesn't exist on major exchanges, and a typo in the word "Omaha."

**Real Reason This Spun Viral**: A 2023 interview clip of Buffett saying "I don't understand crypto" was deepfaked and re-dubbed with AI to make it sound like he said "I'm buying SHIB." The clip now has 8 million views on TikTok. Always cross-reference major portfolio moves with official SEC filings or direct Berkshire press releases.
