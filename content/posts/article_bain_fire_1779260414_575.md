**HEADLINE: ARSON AS ART? BAIN FIRE “SOCIAL REPTILE” EXHIBIT SPARKS MORAL MELTDOWN, ETHICS EXPERTS DECLARE ‘SOCIETY HAS BECOME A SPECTATOR OF ITS OWN RUIN’**

**Bainbridge Island, WA** – A new immersive art exhibit called “Social Reptile,” which culminates in a live, controlled burn of a $400,000 hyper-realistic sculpture of a sloth, has ignited a firestorm—not just of flames, but of furious ethical debate. The Bain Fire, as it’s being called, has drawn over 10,000 spectators in three days, with attendees paying $200 a ticket to watch the sculpture slowly turn to ash while a DJ plays a melancholic score.

Moral critics are apoplectic. “We have officially crossed the Rubicon,” says Dr. Helena Vance, a professor of social ethics at Northwestern University. “This isn’t art. This is a ritualized celebration of nihilism. We are paying to watch destruction, calling it ‘transcendence,’ and filming it for Instagram. We have become a society of voyeurs to our own moral decay. The sloth represents slowness, thoughtfulness, life—and we burn it for a thrill. What’s next? Live-streamed funerals for profit?”

Other critics point to the exhibit’s “carbon credit” offset program as a hollow PR stunt. “They plant three trees for every sloth burned, but the message is clear: consumption, spectacle, and immediate gratification are king. We are teaching our children that the only value is in the moment of collapse,” one commentator wrote.
