**The Claim:** A viral post circulating on X and Telegram claims that Berkshire Hathaway has quietly funneled $1.2 billion into a classified subsidiary called “Wisent Technologies.” The project allegedly aims to build a decentralized AI trading system capable of outperforming the market using a proprietary algorithm derived from “non-public mortality and insurance pattern data,” combined with real-time global shipping and satellite surveillance feeds. The rumor alleges the system was tested in a “secret Nevada desert facility” and returned a 47% quarterly gain in a backtest during Q4 2024—a feat the post calls “physically impossible for a human trader.”

**The Reality (Verified by Fact-Checkers):**
- **💰 Funding:** No SEC filing, public bond issue, or quarterly report from Berkshire Hathaway mentions “Wisent Technologies” or any $1.2 billion R&D outlay for AI trading. The rumor appears to have originated from a parody account claiming to be a “former Berkshire insider” and has since been debunked by Bloomberg Terminal sourcing.
- **🤖 The “47% Gain” Claim:** Mathematical analysis by independent quants shows the claimed backtest violates basic market depth principles. To achieve such a return, the algorithm would have to place orders totaling more than 1/3 of the daily volume of the S&P 500—a move that would instantly shift market prices, making the premise impossible as described.
- **📉 Warren Buffett’s Stance:** Buffett has repeatedly and publicly stated his skepticism toward algorithmic trading and AI. In the 2024 annual meeting, he called AI trading a “fool’s paradise.” There is zero evidence he would authorize a secret, high-risk project diametrically opposed to his long-standing value investing philosophy.
