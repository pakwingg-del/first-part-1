**HEADLINE: SCHLITZ SILENTLY KILLED: THE BEER THAT "MADE MILWAUKEE FAMOUS" DIES A QUIET DEATH**

**The One-Paragraph Brief for the CEO:**

The "Beer that made Milwaukee famous" is no more. Pabst Brewing Company has officially discontinued Schlitz, once the best-selling beer in America. The move is not a tragedy—it’s a brutal, surgical extraction of a brand that became a financial liability. Schlitz has been dying for 40 years, bleeding market share and relevance. This closure frees up shelf space and distribution capital for Pabst’s higher-margin, cult-followed brands (Pabst Blue Ribbon, Lone Star). The lesson: nostalgia is a terrible growth strategy. Pabst chose velocity over sentiment. The last can will be brewed in 2024. Millennials don’t mourn it; Gen X remembers it as their father’s hangover. The corpse is finally buried.
