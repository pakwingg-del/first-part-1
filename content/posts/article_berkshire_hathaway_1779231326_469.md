**Headline: 🚨 BREAKING: Fake Berkshire Hathaway "Crypto Dividend" Promises 1 BTC Per Share – Warren Buffett Not Involved**

**Verdict: FAKE**

A viral post circulating on X (formerly Twitter) and Telegram this morning claims that Berkshire Hathaway has announced a "one-time crypto dividend," distributing 1 Bitcoin (currently ~$67,000) to every Class A and Class B shareholder. The post features a doctored image of Warren Buffett holding a Bitcoin physical coin with the caption: *"Even the Oracle of Omaha saw the light. Your BRK shares just became life-changing."*

**The Reality:**
- **No official filing:** A search of Berkshire Hathaway’s official SEC 8-K filings and press releases shows no such dividend.
- **Buffett’s stance unchanged:** In Berkshire’s 2024 annual letter and in recent CNBC interviews, Buffett reiterated his view that Bitcoin is "rat poison squared" and has "no intrinsic value."
- **Share structure impossibility:** Berkshire Class A shares trade at ~$625,000 each. A 1 BTC dividend would cost the company over $40 billion—roughly 8% of its total cash reserves. No such transaction was initiated.
- **Phishing tie-in:** Security experts at KrebsOnSecurity have flagged that several of the posts contain shortened links leading to fake "claim portals" designed to steal wallet private keys.

**Bottom line:** This is an elaborate social engineering scam. Berkshire Hathaway has no involvement in cryptocurrency. Do not click any links, do not connect your wallet. If it sounds too good to be true, Warren Buffett would be the first to tell you it is.

✅ **Verified by Fact-Checkers:** False / Scam
