**Viral News Snippet:**

**CLAIM: A viral post circulating on social media alleges that Senate Republicans have “secretly voted to fast-track all of Trump’s future nominees, bypassing standard committee hearings and floor debate.”**

**FACT CHECK: FALSE. Misleading.**

**Real:** Senate Republicans have discussed streamlining the confirmation process for some executive nominees, particularly those that are not controversial, to reduce delays. However, no formal vote has taken place to “fast-track” all Trump nominees, and any such move would require a change to Senate rules, which would need 60 votes—an impossibility in the current 50-50 chamber.

**Fake:** The claim that “all hearings are cancelled and nominees will be approved by voice vote without debate” is fabricated. Senate rules still require committee hearings, a vote by the full committee, and a floor vote—which can be filibustered. Only a few, non-controversial nominees have been approved via expedited procedures, not a blanket policy.

**Bottom line:** There is no secret vote or blanket fast-track. The viral post misrepresents routine procedural discussions as a done deal. Always check the source.
