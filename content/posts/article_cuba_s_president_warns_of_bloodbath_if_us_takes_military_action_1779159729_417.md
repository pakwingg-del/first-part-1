**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About Cuba’s President Warning of a ‘Bloodbath’ if the US Takes Military Action**

- **Dire Warning from Havana:** Cuban President Miguel Díaz-Canel issued a stark warning on national television, stating that any unilateral U.S. military intervention would not be a "surgical strike" but a "bloodbath" for civilians—invoking memories of past invasions to galvanize public defiance.

- **Context of Rising Tensions:** The threat comes amid a sharp escalation in U.S. rhetoric over alleged "biological weapons" research in Cuba and a recent U.S. naval exercise off the coast of Florida, raising fears of a replay of the 1961 Bay of Pigs or a more modern blockade.

- **Cuban Military Preparedness:** Díaz-Canel declared that Cuba’s armed forces and civilian reserves—including thousands of neighborhood defense units—are on full alert, preparing for street-by-street urban warfare if foreign troops set foot on the island.

- **International Fallout:** The warning has triggered emergency UN Security Council meetings, with China and Russia backing Cuba’s call for non-intervention, while the OAS remains divided, as several Latin American nations refuse to condemn Havana’s stance.

- **Economic Siege or Full Invasion?** Experts are split on whether the US would actually launch boots-on-the-ground attacks, or if this is a psychological operation to pressure Cuba amid its worst economic crisis since the 1990s—skyrocketing inflation and fuel shortages already gripping the island.
