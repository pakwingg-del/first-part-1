**Viral News Snippet: "Google Saves Your Soul? CEO Says Search Will Begin Offering 'Digital Resurrection' by 2025"**  
**Location:** Mountain View, CA (Google IO Keynote)  
**Status:** ⚠️ FAKE  

**The Claim:**  
During yesterday’s keynote, a leaked clip showed Sundar Pichai announcing "Project Lazarus" – a feature where Google Photos and Search would use AI to reconstruct deceased relatives from old photos, texts, and voice memos. The user could then "interact" with an AI avatar of their loved one. The clip surfaced on X (formerly Twitter) with the caption, *"Google is bringing your grandma back. Creepy or beautiful? 🤖👻"*  

**What’s Really Going On:**  
The video is an AI deepfake. The real Google IO keynote did *not* mention Project Lazarus. Instead, Google announced "Memory Lanes," a new feature that organizes photos and memories but *does not* create interactive avatars. The deepfake was made using a 2023 clip of Sundar Pichai talking about ambient computing, with lip-synced AI audio.  

**Why It’s Viral:**  
The "digital resurrection" concept taps into both grief and fear of AI. Similar rumors (e.g., "Samsung’s AI clones your dead spouse") have popped up before, capitalizing on the emotional weight of loss.  

**Verdict:** Fake. Google confirmed to us: *"No such feature exists. We are exploring memory preservation, not simulation."*  

**Bottom Line:** Mourn with tech, but don’t let deepfakes revive false hope. Always check the source.
