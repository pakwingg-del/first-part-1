BREAKING NEWS | FEBRUARY 18, 2025

DETROIT, Michigan — A widespread power outage has impacted tens of thousands of customers across southeastern Michigan, according to the latest data from DTE Energy's official outage map.

**What:** The utility company's real-time outage tracker is reporting over 45,000 customers without electricity as of 10:15 a.m. EST. Areas affected include portions of Wayne, Oakland, and Macomb counties.

**When:** The outages began approximately at 8:47 a.m. EST Tuesday morning, with reports of downed power lines in several suburban communities.

**Where:** The majority of outages are concentrated in Detroit's northwest side, along with significant disruptions in the cities of Livonia, Warren, and Sterling Heights.

**Who:** DTE Energy crews have been deployed to assess damage and restore service. Company officials say approximately 200 field personnel are actively responding.

**Why:** Preliminary assessments indicate the outages were triggered by high winds and a vehicle collision involving a utility pole on M-39 near Michigan Avenue. Gusts of up to 45 miles per hour have been reported, causing tree limbs to fall across power lines.

**How:** Restoration timelines are currently estimated at four to six hours for most affected areas, but DTE warns that some customers in isolated regions may face longer delays due to the extent of structural damage. The company advises residents to report additional outages via the DTE Energy mobile app and to avoid approaching any downed wires.
