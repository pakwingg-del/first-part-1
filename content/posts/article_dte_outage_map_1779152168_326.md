**BREAKING: DTE Energy Outage Map Goes Dark Amid Widespread Blackouts – Critics Question “Digital Convenience” vs. Accountability**

As a swath of southeastern Michigan endures yet another round of widespread power outages, DTE Energy’s vaunted “outage map” has mysteriously gone offline or displayed incomplete data – leaving thousands of customers in the dark, literally and figuratively.

The utility giant, which reported over 150,000 customers without power late Tuesday, claims the interactive map experienced an “unexpected technical glitch” during peak storm hours. But skeptical residents and consumer watchdogs are asking a pointed question: *Who benefits when the public can’t see the full scope of the blackout?*

“It’s awfully convenient that the ‘transparency tool’ breaks right when people need it most,” said one local homeowner who has been without power for 14 hours. “Is this incompetence, or is it a feature designed to limit public outrage by hiding the real scale of the failure?”

The incident has reignited long-simmering outrage over DTE’s track record. The utility previously settled a $12 million lawsuit over alleged smart meter tampering and has been accused of prioritizing shareholder profits over grid maintenance. In 2023, the Michigan Public Service Commission fined DTE $6 million for repeated outage-reporting failures.

Now, with the outage map down, customers are forced to call busy hotlines or rely on social media rumors – a move critics call a return to “information scarcity” that benefits no one but the corporation.

“When the map is up, we can track the crew locations, see estimated restoration times, and hold them accountable,” said one digital watchdog. “When it’s down, we’re just blind customers. And a blind customer can’t organize, can’t protest, can’t demand refunds.”
