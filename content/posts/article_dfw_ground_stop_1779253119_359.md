DALLAS-FORT WORTH, TEXAS — All departures and arrivals at Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport have been temporarily suspended.

**What** is occurring: A Federal Aviation Administration-issued ground stop is in effect, halting all flight operations at DFW.

**Who** is affected: Thousands of passengers, airlines operating out of DFW, and connecting flights nationwide are experiencing significant delays and cancellations.

**When** did this begin: The ground stop was initiated at approximately 1430 hours Central Standard Time.

**Where** is this taking place: At Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport (DFW), a major global hub for American Airlines.

**Why** has this been implemented: The FAA has cited a technical issue with the airport's radar and communication systems. Additionally, severe weather conditions in the region, including high winds and low visibility, have contributed to the halt in operations.

**How** are authorities responding: FAA officials are currently troubleshooting the technical malfunction. Passengers are advised to contact their airlines for rebooking and to check flight status before traveling to the airport. No timeline for the resumption of normal operations has been provided. This is a developing story.
