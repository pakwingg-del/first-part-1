**CONSENSUS CORNER** 🚨

Just drove past the "Artisanal Pickle Foundry" on Main Street. You know, the one that repurposed the old hardware store into a "fermentation lab." 

I peeked in the window. The "founder" (a 24-year-old in a beanie and suspenders who moved here from San Francisco six months ago) was explaining "biodynamic brine ratios" to a confused local plumber. The plumber was just trying to fix the backed-up sink.

But here's the kicker. 

Saw the sign on the door: "CLOSED TODAY — FOUNDER ATTIENDING A 'VISION RETREAT.'"

Meanwhile, the actual hardware store provided value. Every day. With a real founder who knew your name and what size pipe you needed.

Common sense: You can't "disrupt" plumbing. You can't "scale" a community. And you definitely can't build anything lasting by spending more time on brand values than on actually being open for business.

The pickles are $16 a jar, by the way. And they're "unfiltered." Just like his business plan.

What happened to just… starting something? 🤔
