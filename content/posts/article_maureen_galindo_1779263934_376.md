**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**GALINDO APPOINTED TO LEAD FEDERAL CYBERSECURITY TASK FORCE**

**WASHINGTON, D.C. – January 17, 2025 –** In a significant development aimed at bolstering national digital defenses, the White House has officially announced the appointment of cybersecurity expert Maureen Galindo as the Chair of the newly formed National Critical Infrastructure Cybersecurity Task Force.

**WHO:** Maureen Galindo, a seasoned professional with over two decades of experience in network defense and threat intelligence, most recently serving as Chief Information Security Officer for a major financial institution.

**WHAT:** The appointment to lead a specialized federal task force. The unit is charged with assessing vulnerabilities in power grids, water systems, and communication networks, and developing a unified, strategic response protocol to sophisticated cyber threats.

**WHEN:** The official announcement was made by the Office of the National Cyber Director at approximately 10:00 AM Eastern Standard Time today. The task force is expected to commence its initial operations immediately.

**WHERE:** The task force will be headquartered in the National Cybersecurity Center in Arlington, Virginia, with liaison officers embedded across all 50 states.

**WHY:** Citing an increase in state-sponsored cyber intrusions and the growing complexity of ransomware attacks targeting public utilities, the administration stated that a centralized, expert-led approach is "imperative for national security and public safety." Director Galindo is expected to deliver the task force's preliminary risk assessment to Congress within 90 days.
