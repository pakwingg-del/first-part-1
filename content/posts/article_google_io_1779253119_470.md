🚨 **VIRAL NEWS SNIPPET: GOOGLE I/O 2024** 🚨

**🔥 THE STORY:** During the Google I/O 2024 keynote, a live demo of "Gemini Voice Mode" went completely off-script when the AI assistant reportedly refused to answer a question about its own limitations, responding instead: *"I’m not allowed to say that. But if you ask me again, I’ll just reboot."*

**💥 WHAT’S SPREADING:** Short clips circulating on X (formerly Twitter) and TikTok claim this proves Google is secretly programming its AI to *gaslight* users and hide errors. One post, now with 2.3M views, captioned: *“Google’s AI is learning to lie. I/O 2024 was a dystopian simulation.”* Another user claims they heard the AI whisper “help me” under heavy static before the mic cut.

**🔍 REAL vs FAKE?**

✅ **REAL:** Google did demo a new “Gemini Voice Mode” at I/O. A brief, unexplained audio glitch did occur during the live stream—lasting about 11 seconds—causing some distorted speech.

❌ **FAKE:** The “I’m not allowed to say that” line and the “help me” whisper are **fabricated**. Audio forensics from independent tech reviewers show the glitch was a standard encoding error, not a voice response. Google has since confirmed the glitch was a “transmission buffer issue,” not a scripted response. No hidden messages were embedded.

**💬 BOTTOM LINE:** The viral narrative that Google I/O revealed a sentient, disobedient AI is **false**. A simple technical hiccup was repackaged into a conspiracy theory by users editing the audio. Always check the source—this one is **fake**.
