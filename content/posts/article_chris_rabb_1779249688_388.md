**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**CHRIS RABB ANNOUNCES MAJOR POLICY SHIFT, CITING ECONOMIC PRESSURES**

**WASHINGTON, D.C. –** In a formal address delivered today at the National Press Club, Treasury Secretary Chris Rabb announced a significant departure from previous fiscal policy, citing mounting pressure from global markets and domestic inflation rates.

**WHO:** Treasury Secretary Chris Rabb.

**WHAT:** Rabb outlined a new fiscal framework that emphasizes deregulation and targeted tax incentives for the manufacturing sector, a reversal of his earlier emphasis on social spending.

**WHEN:** The announcement was made at 10:00 AM Eastern Standard Time today.

**WHERE:** The National Press Club, Washington, D.C.

**WHY:** Rabb cited “unsustainable debt-to-GDP ratios” and a “cooling labor market” as the primary catalysts, stating the shift is necessary to prevent a “prolonged economic contraction.”

**HOW:** The new policy, effective January 1st, will see the rollback of two major environmental compliance mandates and a 15% corporate tax reduction for companies that demonstrate a net increase in domestic employment.

The announcement was met with immediate reactions. Market indices saw an initial 2% uptick, while several progressive caucus members have issued statements expressing “grave concern” over the abandonment of environmental protections. A full legislative briefing is scheduled for Thursday.
