# Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Massie Primary

- **Rep. Thomas Massie faces his toughest primary challenge yet** - The Kentucky congressman, a libertarian-leaning Republican known for opposing virtually every major spending bill and bucking party leadership, is squaring off against primary challenger Eric Deters, a controversial attorney and social media agitator who has pulled in significant outside spending from GOP establishment-aligned groups.

- **The "No" vote that became a flashpoint** - Massie's refusal to vote for Kevin McCarthy as Speaker of the House in 2022 made him a target of party leadership, who have now funneled money into Deters' campaign. This primary has become a proxy war between the anti-establishment wing and traditional GOP power brokers.

- **Fundraising disparity tells a story** - While Massie has grassroots support and a loyal base, Deters has been boosted by super PACs aligned with House leadership. However, Massie's small-dollar donor network has kept him competitive, with over $1.2 million raised as of the last filing.

- **Deters' controversial past is a key vulnerability** - The challenger has a history of legal and personal controversies, including a suspension from practicing law and inflammatory social media posts. Massie's campaign has seized on this, painting Deters as unstable and untrustworthy.

- **The race could signal the future of the GOP** - If Massie survives, it suggests the anti-establishment, liberty-minded faction still has real power in Republican primaries. If he loses, it would be a clear warning that party leadership is willing to spend big to enforce discipline—and that even entrenched incumbents aren't safe.
