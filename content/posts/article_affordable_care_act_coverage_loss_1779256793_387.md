**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**ANCHOR:** “Good evening. We begin tonight with a report on a significant shift in American healthcare coverage. According to new data released today by the Department of Health and Human Services, an estimated **2.7 million Americans** have lost their health insurance plans previously purchased under the Affordable Care Act’s federal marketplace over the past three months.”

**WHERE:** The coverage losses are concentrated in the 33 states that utilize the HealthCare.gov federal platform, with particularly steep declines reported in Texas, Florida, and Georgia.

**WHEN:** The decline was recorded between the open enrollment period for 2024 coverage and the conclusion of the first quarter of 2025.

**WHAT:** These terminations are primarily attributed to the expiration of pandemic-era continuous enrollment protections and a series of administrative “redetermination” processes. Experts cite a lack of beneficiary response to residency and income verification requests as the primary cause for the majority of policy cancellations.

**WHY:** Federal officials state that the process is legally required to maintain program integrity and ensure subsidies are only provided to eligible individuals. However, consumer advocacy groups argue that the rapid removal method is creating a coverage gap, potentially leaving millions without access to primary and preventative care.

**HOW:** The Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services (CMS) has advised affected individuals to check their state’s specific deadline for a Special Enrollment Period and to immediately visit Healthcare.gov to reapply, verify their income, or update their contact information to prevent further disruptions.

“This is not a system failure,” one CMS spokesperson stated. “This is a necessary recalibration of eligibility. We are urging all enrollees to update their accounts now.”

The White House has not yet issued a formal statement on the matter. This story continues to develop. We will bring you more details as they become available.”
