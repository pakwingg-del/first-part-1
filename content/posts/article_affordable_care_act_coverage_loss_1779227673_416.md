**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Affordable Care Act Coverage Loss Crisis**

- **Unprecedented Disenrollment Numbers:** Over 10 million Americans have lost their ACA coverage since the "Unwinding" began, marking the largest single coverage purge since the law’s inception, driven by states restarting Medicaid eligibility checks after the pandemic pause.

- **The "Procedural Denial" Trap:** A staggering 70% of those dropped were removed not because they were ineligible, but due to bureaucratic "red tape" issues—like lost renewal letters, incorrect addresses, or missed deadlines—leaving a massive gap in insured, low-income patients.

- **Stark Geographic Disparity:** The crisis is not evenly felt. Southern states like Texas and Florida have disenrolled the highest percentages of their Medicaid rolls, creating healthcare "deserts" where uninsured rates are spiking well above pre-pandemic levels.

- **The "ACA Replacement" Gap:** Most states were unprepared for the volume. Instead of seamlessly transitioning those with slightly higher incomes to subsidized ACA marketplace plans, many have simply fallen off the system, creating a new class of "invisible uninsured" who don't qualify for either program.

- **Immediate Health & Financial Fallout:** This isn’t just a paperwork problem. Emergency room visits for preventable conditions are up 12% in hard-hit states, and medical debt collection calls have surged, proving the loss of coverage is instantaneously translating into worse health outcomes and financial ruin for families.
