**LOUISVILLE, KENTUCKY — May 21, 2025 (AP) —** In a bustling day of civic engagement across the Bluegrass State, voters headed to the polls today for the Kentucky primary election, determining key candidates for the upcoming general election in November.

**WHO:** Over 1.2 million registered Republican, Democratic, and nonpartisan voters are eligible to participate. The primary races include incumbents and challengers for the U.S. House of Representatives, state legislative seats, and local judicial positions.

**WHAT:** The primary elections serve to finalize party nominees for the November general election. Of particular note, a competitive Republican primary is unfolding in the state’s 6th Congressional District, where a field of three candidates—including a state senator and a former county judge-executive—are vying to succeed the retiring incumbent.

**WHEN:** Voting commenced at 6:00 a.m. Eastern Time and will continue until 6:00 p.m. Eastern Time.

**WHERE:** Polling locations are open across all 120 counties. Reports from the Kentucky Secretary of State’s office indicate smooth operations, with voter turnout projected at 22% based on early and absentee data.

**WHY:** Today’s primaries are critical as they set the stage for November’s general election, which will shape the state’s representation in Washington and Frankfort. Analysts are closely watching the 6th District race, which could shift the balance of power in the U.S. House.

**HOW:** Officials are utilizing updated voting machines and paper ballot backups. No major disruptions or significant voter queuing have been reported as of mid-afternoon.

**KEY DEVELOPMENTS:** Polls close promptly at 6 p.m., with initial returns expected shortly thereafter. The Secretary of State is urging all voters in line by the deadline to remain and cast their ballots.
