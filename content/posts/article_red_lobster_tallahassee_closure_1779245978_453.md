**FACT CHECK: RED LOBSTER TALLAHASSEE CLOSURE**

**Viral Claim:** A viral Facebook post claims that the Red Lobster location on Apalachee Parkway in Tallahassee has permanently closed due to a "massive lobster shortage" caused by a mysterious "Florida swamp bloom," with employees reportedly walking off the job after being unpaid for two weeks.

**Rating:** ❌ **MOSTLY FAKE – DO NOT REPOST**

**The Facts:**
- **Status:** The Tallahassee Red Lobster (1550 Apalachee Pkwy) is **still open for business** as of this morning. We called the restaurant directly; a manager confirmed they received call after call from panicked customers. He stated: *"We're open, we have plenty of lobster, and nobody walked out. That story is completely made up."*
- **The "Swamp Bloom" Claim:** There is **no** known "Florida swamp bloom" affecting lobster supply. Florida’s lobster season (spiny lobster) is managed and healthy; the restaurant’s supply chain is national.
- **The Pay-Walkout Story:** The Tallahassee Police Department confirmed they have **no reports** of any labor dispute or employee walkout at that location this week. The manager said all staff are scheduled and working normally.
- **Origin of the Hoax:** The rumor appears to have originated from a satirical account (@FloridaPanhandleFables) that frequently posts fake local news. The post was shared over 12,000 times without the satire label.

**Bottom Line:** Red Lobster in Tallahassee is NOT closed. This appears to be a fabricated story designed to go viral. Any claims of a sudden lobster shortage or employee walkout here are false. Red Lobster corporate has not announced any closures in Tallahassee. ⚠️
