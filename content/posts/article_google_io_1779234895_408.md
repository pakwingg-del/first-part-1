**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About Google I/O 2024**

Google just dropped its biggest AI bombshells yet at I/O, and the future of search, photos, and your smartphone just changed forever.

- **Say Goodbye to Classic Search:** Google is rolling out "AI Overviews" to everyone in the US this week. Instead of blue links, you'll get an AI-written summary at the top of your results—including the ability to ask complex, multi-step questions like "find the best yoga studios in Boston with good reviews for beginners."
- **"Ask Photos" Becomes Your Personal Time Machine:** Your Google Photos gallery just got a brain. You can now ask your photo library questions like "Show me my daughter learning to swim" or "What was the license plate on my rental car in Tokyo?"—and it will understand the context without you needing to tag anything.
- **Project Astra: The Real-Time AI Assistant:** Google demoed a prototype AI agent that can see and hear your world through your phone's camera. It can point at a broken speaker, ask how to fix it, and get real-time step-by-step instructions. It’s essentially a visual, conversational assistant that remembers where you left your keys.
- **Gemini Nano is Coming to Everything (Even Your Browser):** Google is stuffing a smaller, on-device AI model directly into Chrome on desktop. This means features like "Help me write" and scam detection will work locally, without sending your data to the cloud. Your laptop is about to get a lot smarter offline.
- **Android 15 Gets a Theft-Detection Lockdown:** If your phone is snatched and the thief tries to run, bike, or drive away, Android 15 will now automatically detect the motion of a "suspicious theft" and lock the entire device instantly. Your banking apps and photos stay protected even after you drop the phone.
