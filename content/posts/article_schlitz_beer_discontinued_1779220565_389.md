**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**ANCHOR DESK – NATIONAL NEWS DIVISION**

**DATE:** October 26, 2023

**TIME:** 19:00 UTC

**HEADLINE:** ICONIC AMERICAN LAGER SCHLITZ BREWING COMPANY CEASES PRODUCTION; BRAND DISCONTINUED AFTER 174 YEARS

**LOCATION:** MILWAUKEE, WISCONSIN

**WHAT:** The Pabst Brewing Company has officially announced the permanent discontinuation of the Schlitz brand of beer, effectively ending production of what was once marketed as "The Beer That Made Milwaukee Famous." This decision halts all bottling, canning, and distribution of Schlitz, Schlitz Malt Liquor, and Schlitz Light effective immediately.

**WHO:** The decision was made by the executive leadership of the Pabst Brewing Company, the current parent corporation holding the rights to the Schlitz trademark.

**WHEN:** The official announcement was released earlier today, with immediate cessation of production. Remaining inventory in retail and wholesale channels is expected to be depleted within the next sixty days.

**WHERE:** Schlitz has been contract-brewed at various facilities across the United States following the closure of its original Milwaukee brewery. Production will cease at all contracted facilities.

**WHY:** Corporate spokespersons cited "significant and sustained decline in national market demand" as the primary reason for the discontinuation. Schlitz, once the best-selling beer in the United States during the 1940s and 1950s, experienced a dramatic fall from market dominance following a high-profile reformulation in the 1970s and subsequent quality perception issues. Despite a niche revival among retro-beer enthusiasts in the 2010s, sales figures have reportedly fallen below the threshold for viable commercial production.
