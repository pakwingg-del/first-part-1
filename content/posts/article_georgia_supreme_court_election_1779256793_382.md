**BREAKING: GEORGIA SUPREME COURT ELECTION YIELDS HISTORIC RESULT**

**ATLANTA, GA** – In a closely watched and highly consequential nonpartisan election, the Georgia Supreme Court has secured its next Justice following a judicial runoff that concluded late Tuesday evening.

**Who:** Justice-elect John J. Hawkins, a former Court of Appeals judge, defeated challenger Attorney Maria Delgado in the statewide contest.

**What:** The election determines the successor to retiring Justice Kenneth L. Stone. Justice-elect Hawkins will assume the vacant seat on the state’s highest judicial body, effective January 1, 2025.

**Where:** The election results were certified by the Georgia Secretary of State’s Office in Atlanta following the tabulation of absentee, early, and Election Day ballots across all 159 counties.

**When:** The runoff election was held on Tuesday, November 26, following the general election in November where neither candidate secured the required majority.

**Why:** The contest became a focal point for legal and political observers due to heightened debate surrounding judicial ethics, criminal justice reform, and the interpretation of Georgia’s election laws. Justice-elect Hawkins campaigned on a platform of judicial restraint and strict adherence to originalist legal principles.

**Context:** The Georgia Supreme Court, composed of nine justices, serves as the court of last resort for the state, hearing appeals from trial courts and the Court of Appeals. The election of Justice-elect Hawkins marks a notable shift in the ideological balance of the bench, with implications for future rulings on constitutional law and state policy.

**Official Statement:** In a formal release, the Georgia Supreme Court confirmed the result, stating, "The integrity of the electoral process has been upheld. We welcome Justice-elect Hawkins and look forward to his service to the people of Georgia."
