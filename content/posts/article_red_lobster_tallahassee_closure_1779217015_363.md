**BREAKING NEWS | LOCAL BUSINESS UPDATE**

**TALLAHASSEE, Florida** – The Red Lobster seafood restaurant located at 2410 North Monroe Street has permanently closed its operations, effective immediately, according to a statement released by the company’s corporate office earlier today.

**WHAT:** The sudden and indefinite closure of the Red Lobster franchise in Tallahassee. The restaurant is listed as "permanently closed" on official company directories and online mapping services. No further reopening date has been announced.

**WHO:** Red Lobster Management LLC, the Orlando-based parent company overseeing the chain's operations across the United States. The closure directly impacts an estimated 45 to 60 local employees, according to preliminary reports from the Florida Department of Economic Opportunity. The company has not yet issued a formal employee severance or relocation notice.

**WHEN:** The closure took effect as of 9:00 a.m. Eastern Time this morning. Management confirmed that all scheduled shifts were canceled, and the restaurant's phone lines are no longer in service. The property manager for the commercial plaza confirmed the lease has been surrendered.

**WHERE:** 2410 North Monroe Street, Tallahassee, Florida 32303, in the North Monroe Shopping Center, adjacent to Interstate 10 and within walking distance of the Florida State University campus.

**WHY:** Corporate representatives cite "continued operational losses and changing consumer dining preferences" as the primary reasons. The location had been underperforming relative to system-wide averages for three consecutive fiscal quarters, according to internal earnings reports obtained by this station. Additionally, the company is reportedly in the midst of a nationwide portfolio review, with more than 50 underperforming locations currently under evaluation for permanent closure.
