**Futurist Forecast: The 'Founder' Role Splits — Introducing The "Un-Boss" Era**

By 2035, the traditional startup founder—the lone visionary working 100-hour weeks—will be considered a "legacy artifact," according to a new report from the Institute for Autonomous Leadership.

Researchers predict a radical shift: the role of "Founder" is officially decoupling into two distinct entities. The *"Catalyst"* creates the initial idea and cultural DNA, then exits within 18 months. The *"Steward"* is an AI-augmented human who scales the company using algorithmic empathy and decentralized governance.

**The Viral Prediction:**
The most successful companies of 2035 will be "Orphan Companies"—thriving startups whose original founders are already on their fifth new venture. Meanwhile, a new luxury market will explode: *"Founder-in-Residence Rentals,"* where celebrities and ex-CEOs are paid millions just to pose as the "face of the company" for investor optics, with zero operational power.

**The Conflict:**
Critics warn this splintering kills "founder-market fit." Proponents argue it ends the dangerous cult of personality and burnout that defined the 2020s. The first lawsuit has already been filed: a founding employee suing for "emotional whiplash" after their Catalyst founder sold the company story and vanished.

**The Vibe:** The future founder isn't a leader. They are a **moment** in time—for sale, disposable, and increasingly optional.
