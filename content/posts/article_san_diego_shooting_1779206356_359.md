**HEADLINE: Multiple Casualties Reported Following Shooting at San Diego Transit Hub; Suspect in Custody**

**DATELINE:** SAN DIEGO, CA – A mass shooting incident at a major transit center in downtown San Diego has resulted in multiple casualties, according to local law enforcement.

**WHO:** The shooting was perpetrated by a lone suspect, identified by police as a 34-year-old male. Two victims are reported deceased, with four others sustaining injuries ranging from critical to minor. All victims have been transported to area hospitals. The suspect has been taken into custody.

**WHAT:** A targeted shooting event unfolded at approximately 8:15 a.m. local time. Preliminary investigations indicate the suspect discharged a handgun indiscriminately into a crowded waiting area before being subdued by transit police. The scene has been secured.

**WHERE:** The incident occurred at the Santa Fe Depot, a major transportation hub serving Amtrak, COASTER, and local transit lines, located in the 1000 block of Broadway, San Diego.

**WHEN:** Monday, October 23, 2023, at approximately 8:15 a.m. PST.

**WHY:** Authorities are currently investigating the motive. Preliminary reports suggest the suspect may have been experiencing a mental health crisis, though no definitive connection or manifesto has been confirmed. An investigation is ongoing.

**UPDATE:** The Santa Fe Depot remains closed as investigators process the scene. Commuters are advised to seek alternative routes. The FBI has been contacted to assist with the investigation into potential federal charges. Further updates will be provided as the situation develops.
