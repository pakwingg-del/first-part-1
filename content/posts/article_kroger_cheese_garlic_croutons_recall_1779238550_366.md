**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**Kroger Recalls Store-Brand Cheese Garlic Croutons Over Undeclared Allergen**

**CINCINNATI, OH** – The Kroger Company has issued a voluntary recall for a specific batch of its Private Selection brand Seasoned Cheese Garlic Croutons, citing the potential presence of an undeclared allergen.

**What was recalled?** The recall is limited to Kroger's Private Selection Seasoned Cheese Garlic Croutons, sold in a 7.5-ounce package.

**Why was the recall initiated?** According to the official statement from Kroger, the product may contain traces of *pecans*, a tree nut allergen which is not listed on the product ingredient label. Consumption of this product by an individual with a pecan allergy could pose a serious health risk, including a severe allergic reaction.

**When were the affected products distributed?** The recalled croutons were distributed to Kroger retail stores, including its subsidiary banners, throughout the month of October 2023.

**Where were the products sold?** The affected items were sold in Kroger retail locations nationwide, as well as stores operating under the Kroger umbrella, such as Ralphs, Fred Meyer, and King Soopers.

**Who is at risk?** Any consumer who has purchased the recalled croutons and has a known allergy to pecans, or other tree nuts, is at risk.

**What should consumers do?** Customers who have purchased this product are urged not to consume it and to discard the item immediately. For a full refund or replacement, consumers are instructed to return the product to their place of purchase.
