**HEADLINE: SCHLITZ BEER’S ABRUPT DEMISE LEAVES $2B HOLE IN VALUE SEGMENT**

**The News:** Pabst Brewing Company has officially pulled the plug on Schlitz, a brand that once commanded 18% of U.S. beer sales in the 1960s. Production ceased immediately. No relaunch is planned.

**The Fallout:**
- **Market Disruption:** The move creates an immediate supply vacuum in the $10B+ "nostalgia/value" category. Heineken's *High Life* and Molson Coors' *Hamm's* are now positioned to feast on Schlitz’s remaining distribution channel share. This is not a death; it's a transfer of shelf space.
- **Strategic Rationale:** Pabst is shedding a legacy liability. Schlitz lacked the cult energy of *Pabst Blue Ribbon* or the Gen Z traction of *Olympia*. The brand was costing more in raw materials and logistics than it ever returned, with a negative margin structure.
- **Collector’s Paradox:** Vintage Schlitz cans—especially 1970s “Braun” variants—are expected to spike 300% on secondary markets within 30 days. This creates an immediate arbitrage opportunity for beverage distributors who held inventory.

**CEO Takeaway:** This is a textbook case of "legacy brand death." If your company is carrying a brand that consumers no longer actively want, but you keep it out of nostalgia, you are subsidizing failure. Pabst just created $80M in annual efficiency gains by cutting dead weight. Audit your own portfolio today; sentiment is a drag on P&L.

**Viral Hook:** *Schlitz is dead. The real story is which beer CEO will be next to kill their grandfather’s brand.*
