**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**LOUISVILLE, KY – May 21, 2025** – In a closely watched contest, Kentucky held its primary elections today, setting the stage for the general election in November.

**What:** Voters across the Commonwealth of Kentucky cast their ballots in the 2025 primary election, selecting nominees for multiple federal and state offices, including the hotly contested gubernatorial race.

**Who:** The primary featured a crowded field of candidates vying for the Democratic and Republican nominations for Governor, with incumbent Governor Andrew Beshear (D) running unopposed for his party’s nomination. On the Republican side, a primary battle ensued between three key contenders: former UN Ambassador Kelly Craft, Attorney General Daniel Cameron, and businessman Joe Campbell.

**When:** Polls opened at 6:00 AM EST and closed at 6:00 PM EST today, May 21, 2025.

**Where:** Voting took place at designated precincts across all 120 counties in Kentucky.

**Why:** The primary serves as the mechanism for each political party to choose its standard-bearer for the November 5 general election. The outcome of today’s race will determine who will face Governor Beshear in a contest widely viewed as a bellwether for national political trends.

**How:** Preliminary results from the Kentucky Secretary of State’s office indicate a high voter turnout, driven by the competitive Republican gubernatorial primary. As of 7:30 PM EST, with 15% of precincts reporting, Attorney General Daniel Cameron holds a narrow lead over former UN Ambassador Kelly Craft. The race remains too close to call, with all eyes on certified final results expected from the State Board of Elections later this evening.

Authorities remind voters that absentee and mail-in ballots will be counted in the coming days, meaning a final determination in close races may take several days.
