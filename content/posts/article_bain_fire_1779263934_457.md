**Viral News Snippet: The "Bain Fire" Rumor – What's Real vs. Fake**

A dramatic new rumor is sweeping social media this week, claiming that global consulting giant Bain & Company has been ordered to pay a staggering $47 billion in a secret class-action lawsuit over "internal fires" that allegedly destroyed employee mental health records. The posts—featuring grainy photos of a burning office building labeled "Bain HQ, Boston"—claim the fires were set by whistleblowers to hide allegations of toxic work culture.

**Clarification from Fact-Checkers:**

- **Fake:** No court records or government announcements support a $47 billion verdict against Bain; the company's largest known settlement was $10 million in a 2022 gender discrimination case.

**Verdict:** The "Bain Fire" story is a hoax, blending a minor news event with fictional conspiracy theories to go viral. Always cross-check dramatic corporate disaster claims with official sources like the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) or the company’s own press releases.
