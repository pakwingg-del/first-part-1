**BREAKING NEWS: LANTERN FESTIVAL CAUSES WIDESPREAD DISRUPTION ACROSS METROPOLITAN REGION**

**LOCATION:** Metropolitan City Center and Surrounding Suburbs

**DATE:** This evening, commencing at approximately 20:00 hours local time.

**EVENT:** The annual Sky Lantern Festival, a tradition involving the mass release of paper lanterns, has led to significant operational and safety incidents, including a multi-vehicle accident on the Harbor Bridge, multiple power outages, and a small brush fire at the city's botanical gardens.

**CAUSE:** Preliminary investigations indicate that wind gusts carried the airborne lanterns off their intended course. Several lanterns, still carrying active flames, descended prematurely, landing on roadways, power lines, and dry vegetation. Debris from deflated lanterns also obstructed traffic signals, contributing to the Harbor Bridge collision.

**IMPACT:** Emergency services report three individuals transported to nearby medical facilities with non-life-threatening injuries from the vehicular pile-up. City power authorities have confirmed outages affecting approximately 2,000 residences in the Eastern District. Air traffic control issued a precautionary ground stop at Regional International Airport for one hour. The festival has been immediately suspended, and all future public releases are under review by the city council.

**AUTHORITIES:** City Emergency Management officials urge all residents to avoid the downtown corridor and advise against launching any additional personal lanterns. A full hazard assessment is underway.
