**What changed?** The Court gutted a decades-old legal principle (the "Chevron deference") that let expert agencies like the CFPB and FTC interpret laws when rules were vague. Now, every corporate fine—from a hidden cellphone charge to a surprise overdraft fee—could be challenged in court, dragging out accountability for years while companies pocket your money.
