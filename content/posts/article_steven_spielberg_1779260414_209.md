**🚨 LOCAL MAN FORCES STEVEN SPIELBERG TO “USE COMMON SENSE” DURING NEIGHBORHOOD COOKOUT 🚨**

*The Hollywood legend was reportedly “just trying to get a hot dog” when he was stopped by a furious neighbor.*

**[ANYTOWN, USA]** — A tense standoff broke out this afternoon at a block party when renowned filmmaker Steven Spielberg attempted to use his phone’s compass app to find the condiments table.

“He was standing there, circling the ketchup like it was a lost ark,” said local resident and self-proclaimed “common sense warrior,” Gary Henderson, 47. “I said, ‘Steven, use your eyes. It’s right in front of you. You don’t need an Indiana Jones map for a hot dog.’”

Witnesses claim Spielberg, 77, then politely asked if there was a “clever camera angle” to approach the mustard station, prompting Henderson to launch into a 12-minute lecture on “how life isn’t a movie.”

“He kept saying, ‘But the lighting is better over there,’ and I’m like, ‘Buddy, the sun is setting. That’s nature. Not a special effect. Just grab a bun and move on,’” Henderson recounted, shaking his head.

Neighbors report that the tension climaxed when Spielberg tried to arrange potato chips in a symmetrical grid “for visual storytelling.” Henderson reportedly snapped, “This isn’t ‘Close Encounters of the Third Kind.’ It’s a potato chip bowl. Just eat them.”

Spielberg was last seen retreating to his minivan, muttering that he “should have shot this as a period piece.” Henderson, meanwhile, told reporters, “That guy has no common sense. He’s never had to flip a burger in real life.”
