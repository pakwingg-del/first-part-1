**BREAKING: "A SIGN OF THE APOCALYPSE?" — MORAL CRITICS SCREAM AS ROY COOPER AND MICHAEL WHATLEY POLL REVEALS VOTERS PREFER ‘ANYTHING BUT DECENCY’**

In what moral watchdogs are calling "a textbook symptom of cultural rot," a shocking new poll shows that a plurality of voters in a hypothetical 2028 matchup would rather support a hyper-partisan political operative or a corporate-backed moderate than a candidate who "pledges to uphold traditional ethics."

The poll, which pitted figures like North Carolina Governor Roy Cooper and RNC Chair Michael Whatley in a series of hypothetical primary scenarios, found that 1 in 3 voters said they would **"hold their nose and vote for whoever I like most on TikTok."**

Ethics professor Dr. Helena Vance called the findings "a moral Chernobyl." She added: "We are now rewarding the very behaviors that previous generations called ‘corruption.’ The public no longer asks, ‘Is this leader honest?’ They ask, ‘Is this leader entertaining?’ We are not electing leaders. We are casting for reality TV."

Meanwhile, a separate question in the poll found that 62% of respondents said they **"don’t care about a candidate’s personal scandals, as long as my team wins."** Moral critics are pointing to this as final proof that the United States has transitioned from a republic to a "raw tribal conflict," where shame is dead and decency is a liability.

**HOT TAKE:** If Roy Cooper and Michael Whatley are the best we can do, maybe the real crisis isn’t the candidates—it’s a society that no longer knows what a good leader looks like.
