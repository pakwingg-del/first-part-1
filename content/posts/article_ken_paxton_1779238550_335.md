**HISTORY REPEATS? Ken Paxton’s Impeachment Echoes the 1913 Trial of a Texas Legend**

AUSTIN, TX — In a twist that has historians buzzing, Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton’s sensational impeachment trial is drawing eerie parallels to the 1913 political downfall of legendary Texas Governor James E. “Pa” Ferguson.

Ferguson—the only Texas governor ever impeached and convicted—was brought down by charges of misapplication of public funds and accepting improper loans. Sound familiar?

“Ferguson was a populist firebrand who used his office to fight the establishment, and his enemies used the law to tear him down,” noted Dr. Eleanor Vance, a historian at UT Austin. “Paxton’s defense is playing the exact same tune: ‘This is a witch hunt by the old guard.’”

But the historical pattern runs deeper. Both men faced accusations involving secret financial arrangements with private figures. Ferguson was accused of funneling money from mysterious sources; Paxton faces securities fraud charges tied to a tech executive. Both were acquitted on the substance but politically crippled.
