**🚨 SHARE IF YOU HAVE COMMON SENSE 🚨**

Just got word that Kroger is recalling their store brand Garlic Cheese Croutons - and honestly, who’s shocked? My family ate an entire bag before we saw the notice. Turns out they might be contaminated with *undeclared milk* (which is bad enough) but the REAL kicker? These bags were labeled as “homestyle” recipe but the ingredients list looks like it was written by a chemistry student. Since when does “natural flavor” need a 10-digit code?

Meanwhile, Kroger is offering refunds. Oh, great. Refunds. Because my time driving back to the store, my kid’s upset stomach, and the fact that I can’t trust a SALAD TOPPER is totally worth the $3.99. Common sense would’ve been: 1) Don’t put allergens in things that didn’t need them. 2) Actually check your labels before they hit shelves.

Anyway, check your pantries. Look for the garlic cheese croutons with the “best by” dates in late January. And maybe stick to plain old breadcrumbs until corporate figures out what “homestyle” means. #CommonSenseRevolution #KrogerRecall #CheckYourPantry
