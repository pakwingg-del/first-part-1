**VERDICT: FAKE (Manipulated Context)**

**Headline:** *“CBP Issues Urgent Memorial Day Travel Warning: ‘Do Not Cross State Lines with These Items or Face Immediate Seizure’”*

**The Viral Claim:** A message circulating on Facebook and TikTok claims that U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) has issued a nationwide “emergency advisory” for Memorial Day weekend. The post warns that CBP will set up checkpoints on interstate highways—not just at the borders—to confiscate “out-of-state fireworks, unlabeled homemade baked goods, and open alcohol containers,” with violators facing a $5,000 fine and up to 90 days in jail. It urges drivers to “avoid all travel through the South and Midwest” and tells truckers to “abandon loads of produce over 50 lbs.”

**Why It’s False:**
1. **Jurisdiction Limits:** CBP’s authority is strictly limited to ports of entry (borders, airports, and seaports) and a narrow, 100-mile “customs zone” near U.S. borders. They do not have the legal mandate to set up random checkpoints on interstate highways in the middle of the country (e.g., Kansas, Ohio, Georgia).
2. **No Official Announcement:** CBP has issued no such press release or social media alert. Their official Memorial Day posts focus on travel tips for returning from Mexico/Canada—not domestic road trips.
3. **Constitutional Red Flags:** Random seizure of baked goods or unopened fireworks on a state highway without a warrant would be a severe Fourth Amendment violation. Even CBP’s border authority does not extend to routine kitchen inspections.
4. **Misattributed Stats:** The $5,000 fine and 90-day jail figure is ripped from a 2018 press release about *commercial* smuggling of illegal Mexican fireworks at the border—not for a family
