**SUBJECT: "HIGHWAY TO HEAVEN" REBOOT: CONVERTING NOSTALGIA INTO A $500M FRANCHISE**

**The Hook:** Fox is resurrecting the 1980s classic *Highway to Heaven* for the latest IP mining boom, but this isn't a sentimental trip. It is a calculated play for the "faith & family" demographic—a $2B underserved market.

**Why This Matters:**

- **The Strategy:** Swap the 40-year-old premise (an angel walking Earth) for a modern, procedural companion format. The new show pairs a cynical Gen Z journalist with an enigmatic "traveler". Think *Highway to Heaven* meets *Highway Patrol*.
- **The Economics:** Low-budget, high-volume (20 episodes). Syndication gold. Fox leverages nostalgia without the high cost of dark, serialized dramas.
- **The Threat:** Netflix already dominates this space with *Virgin River*. Fox needs this to work or risks losing its most loyal broadcast audience to streaming.

**The Bottom Line:** This is not about faith; it's about retention. If this reboot hits, Fox captures a high retention demographic (parents, heartland viewers) and builds a $500M ancillary revenue stream. If it misses, it signals a deeper death spiral for linear broadcast.
