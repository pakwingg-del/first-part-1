**BREAKING NEWS – WASHINGTON D.C. – MARCH 27, 2025**

**HEADLINE:** Data Analysis Reveals Significant Coverage Losses Among Affordable Care Act Enrollees; HHS Cites Procedural Non-Compliance

**WASHINGTON, D.C.** – A comprehensive federal data analysis released today by the Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) has identified a substantial decline in active health insurance coverage under the Affordable Care Act (ACA) marketplace plans during the first quarter of Fiscal Year 2025.

**WHAT:** The report indicates a net loss of approximately 2.4 million enrollees from ACA-qualified health plans, marking the most significant single-quarter contraction since the public health emergency unwinding began in 2023.

**WHO:** The population affected includes low-to-middle-income individuals and families who receive premium tax credits and cost-sharing reductions. HHS officials attribute the primary driver of these losses to "procedural termination," where enrollees failed to complete mandatory income re-verification and renewal documentation.

**WHEN:** The data covers the period from January 1, 2025, to March 15, 2025, with the final administrative cutoff for renewal compliance occurring at 11:59 PM Eastern Standard Time on March 1.

**WHERE:** The coverage losses are concentrated in 14 states that utilize the federally facilitated marketplace (HealthCare.gov), with Texas, Florida, and Georgia reporting the highest absolute numbers of disenrollments.

**WHY:** According to HHS Deputy Secretary Dr. Evelyn Torres, speaking at a press conference in the Capitol, "The drop-off is a consequence of the statutory requirement to re-verify household income and immigration status annually. While outreach efforts were intensified, millions of enrollees did not respond to multiple notification attempts or provided incomplete documentation."
