**Viral News Snippet: “Red Lobster Tallahassee Closed—But Not for the Reason You Think!”**

**The Claim:** A viral Facebook post is claiming the Red Lobster location on North Monroe Street in Tallahassee has permanently closed due to a massive “cockroach infestation” caught on video, forcing the entire franchise to pay a secret $250,000 settlement to a law firm representing former employees.

**Reality Check: VERDICT = MIXED (Mostly Fake)**

**What’s True:** The Red Lobster on North Monroe Street *did* close, along with dozens of other locations nationwide, as part of a corporate restructuring and bankruptcy process announced earlier this year. The closure was confirmed by the company’s press release about shuttering underperforming “legacy” restaurants.

**What’s False:**
1. **“Cockroach video”:** No credible health inspection report or local news footage has shown a pest infestation at this specific location. The viral video being shared is actually a 2022 clip from a restaurant in Chicago.
2. **“$250,000 settlement”:** No court filings or public records match this figure for the Tallahassee site. The company’s bankruptcy filing mentioned general liability reserves, but no specific settlement tied to this location.
3. **“Secret payoff”:** The post claims a “whistleblower attorney” anonymously leaked the information—no name or bar number is provided, making the source unverifiable.

**Bottom Line:** The restaurant is indeed closed, but the dramatic “infestation and settlement” story is a fabricated rumor designed to go viral. For factual updates, check the Florida Department of Business & Professional Regulation inspection database or the company’s bankruptcy docket.
