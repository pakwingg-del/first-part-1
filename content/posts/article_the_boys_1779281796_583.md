**“The Boys” Season 4 Finale Sparks Outrage: Critics Call It a ‘Blueprint for Societal Collapse’**  

In a fiery new op-ed that’s gone viral, moral watchdog Dr. Patricia Holloway has declared Amazon Prime’s *The Boys* “the most dangerous show on television,” arguing that its relentless parade of gore, cynicism, and satirical nihilism is “normalizing the very collapse it claims to critique.”  

“We’ve crossed a line,” Holloway writes. “This season doesn’t just mock corruption—it worships it, turning every moral boundary into a punchline. When a hero—sorry, ‘supe’—meltdowns into a bloodbath and viewers cheer, we’re not laughing *at* the darkness; we’re signing a pact with it.”  

The article—shared over 50,000 times in 24 hours—blasts the show’s “smug deconstruction” of heroism, claiming it erodes hope itself. “We used to ask, *Who will save us?* Now, *The Boys* teaches us to smirk and ask, *Why bother?* That is the real super-villainy: stealing our capacity for belief, one splatter scene at a time.”  

Critics, of course, fire back, calling the take “hysterical” and noting that satire isn’t endorsement. But Holloway’s rant has struck a nerve, sparking a cultural firestorm over whether the most popular show on TV is a mirror—or a weapon.
