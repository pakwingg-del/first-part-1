**FROM:** Strategy Desk
**SUBJECT:** EDC 2027 – The Ten-Year Comeback Nobody Saw Coming

**The headline writes itself:** EDC 2027 just shattered every attendance and revenue record in Vegas history, and it didn’t need a new headliner.

**The numbers:**
- **Gate:** 625,000 unique attendees (+18% vs 2024).
- **Economic impact:** $1.9B in direct spend to Las Vegas.
- **Per-capita spend:** $3,200 — highest ever for a multi-day event.

**The strategic takeaway:**
This wasn’t a flash in the pan. EDC killed its legacy model:
1. **Killed single-day tickets.** Forced 3-day commitment. Retention went up, churn went down.
2. **Dynamic pricing on everything** — from parking to water. Revenue per head jumped 27%.
3. **Frictionless entry** — no bags, no lines, no problem. Wait times dropped to under 4 minutes.

**Why this matters for us:**
When a “party” becomes an ultra-efficient revenue machine with zero brand damage, that’s not a festival — that’s a blueprint.

**Bottom line:**
EDC 2027 just proved millennials and Gen Z will spend premium for *experience* and *efficiency*. If we can remove every ounce of friction from our customer journey, we don’t need a bigger product. We just need a cleaner gate.
