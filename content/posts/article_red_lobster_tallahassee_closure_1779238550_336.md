**RED LOBSTER TALLAHASSEE CLOSES — HISTORIANS DRAW STARTLING PARALLEL TO 1929 STOCK MARKET CRASH**

TALLAHASSEE, FL — When the "Endless Shrimp" ran out, the historians moved in. After the abrupt closure of the Red Lobster on Apalachee Parkway, economic historians are drawing a startling parallel to the 1929 stock market crash, calling it "The Cheddar Bay Correction."

"People think the Great Depression started with banks failing," said Dr. Lydia Croft, financial historian at FSU. "No. It started when the ordinary man couldn't afford small comforts anymore. The sudden disappearance of a beloved Tuesday night institution? That’s the canary in the coal mine, coated in garlic butter."

Social media exploded after the "Lobster Square" location (originally a 1987 build, now a ghost kitchen for a mattress store) locked its doors. On X, user @CrustaceanPanic posted: "First Red Lobster, next the bread basket. This is the Whiskey Rebellion of our time."

"Red Lobster Tallahassee is to 2025 what the Bank of United States was to 1930," added Dr. Marcus Webb, a cultural archaeologist. "It’s not just a restaurant. It’s the middle-class promise that, on a rainy Tuesday, you can still get a steamed rock lobster for $21.99. When that promise breaks, empires fall."

The chain has cited rising costs and menu restructuring, but historians see a darker cycle: the slow collapse of the "Affordable Luxury Index."

Local historian Brenda Keene has already filed paperwork to have the site declared a living memorial. "Future historians will study the grease stains on the booth seats the way we study WWII ration coupons," she said. "This is the lost Atlantis of American economic stability."
