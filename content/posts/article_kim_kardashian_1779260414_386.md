**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**KIM KARDASHIAN ANNOUNCES MAJOR LEGAL REFORM INITIATIVE FOLLOWING WHITE HOUSE MEETING**

**(Washington, D.C. – November 14, 2023)** Media personality and businesswoman Kim Kardashian has announced a significant expansion of her criminal justice reform advocacy, revealing plans to fund a new legal fellowship program aimed at overturning wrongful convictions.

**WHAT:** Kardashian, alongside a coalition of legal experts, will establish the “Second Chance Initiative,” a $5 million fund providing grants for legal teams to review and litigate cases involving alleged prosecutorial misconduct or ineffective counsel. The announcement was made during a press conference held earlier today.

**WHO:** Kim Kardashian, a reality television star and founder of Skims, made the declaration following a private, closed-door meeting with senior White House officials, including the Deputy Director of the Office of Public Engagement. She was joined by three formerly incarcerated individuals whose sentences were commuted following her prior advocacy.

**WHERE:** The press conference took place at the National Press Club in Washington, D.C.

**WHEN:** The initiative will launch formally on January 1, 2024, with applications for legal teams opening on December 1, 2023.

**WHY:** Kardashian stated the program was born from “systemic failures” she witnessed during her legal apprenticeship, emphasizing that “financial resources should never determine the quality of justice a person receives.” The announcement drew immediate praise from the American Civil Liberties Union and sharp criticism from the National Association of District Attorneys, who called the move “a dangerous precedent of celebrity-endorsed unchecked litigation.”

The program is expected to review at least 100 cases in its first year.
