**GASLIGHTING AT THE PUMP: DESANTIS BILL COULD SLASH FLORIDA GAS PRICES — BUT ONLY FOR SOME, CRITICS WARN**

A new proposal from Gov. Ron DeSantis is promising to slash Florida’s gas prices by up to 40 cents a gallon this summer—but the fine print has consumer advocates screaming foul. The plan, dubbed the "Summer Freedom Drive," would suspend the state’s 26.5-cent gas tax for three months, saving the average driver roughly $40 total. Sounds great, right? Not so fast.

**Here's the wallet punch:** To balance the budget, the state would simultaneously lift the cap on local rental car surcharges and hike tolls on major highways like I-4 and the Turnpike. Translation? Snowbirds and tourists get a break, but commuters who drive for work or live in the suburbs could end up paying *more* than they save. Consumer advocates are calling it a “shell game for the middle class,” pointing out that a family commuting 30 miles each way could see toll costs spike by $60 a month alone.

“A family filling up their minivan might save $12, but they’ll pay $20 more to get to work,” said Maria Lopez of the Florida Consumer Alliance. “This isn’t relief. It’s a transfer of costs from visitors to your neighbor.”

**The fine print:** The tax holiday wouldn’t apply to diesel, hurting delivery drivers and small businesses. Shipping costs could rise, meaning your grocery bill—already up 18% from last year—could hit the roof again. Plus, critics warn that once the holiday ends, the gas tax will snap back in October, just as we head into holiday travel season. “It’s a temporary Band-Aid that turns into a double-whammy,” Lopez added.
