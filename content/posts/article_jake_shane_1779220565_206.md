🚨 NEW: LOCAL RESIDENT JAKE SHANE ARRESTED AFTER ‘COMMON SENSE’ PROTEST BACKFIRES SPECTACULARLY 🚨

BUCKS COUNTY, PA — In what neighbors are calling a “masterclass in irony,” local man Jake Shane, 34, was taken into custody Tuesday after attempting to prove that “common sense alone” could fix the town’s traffic light synchronization.

According to police reports, Shane allegedly dismantled a traffic control box at the intersection of Maple and 5th with a crowbar, claiming the blinking red light was “a personal attack on productivity.” Witnesses say he then tried to reroute the wires using a pair of pliers and a YouTube tutorial he’d watched for “only 30 seconds.”

“He kept yelling, ‘This is just common sense! Why does government need computers for this?’” said onlooker Linda Murkowski, 62. “Then the left turn signal turned solid green and stayed that way for 20 minutes.”

Shane was left with a mild electrical shock to the hand, a traffic citation for tampering with municipal infrastructure, and a social media meltdown that has since gone viral. In a follow-up Facebook Live from the back of the patrol car, Shane stated, “All I wanted was a little common sense. But the system—and the wires—are rigged.”

The town council has announced that the lights will remain on a 90-second timer for the foreseeable future, adding, “We’re using books, not beliefs.”
