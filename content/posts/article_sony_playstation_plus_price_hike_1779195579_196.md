**Big surprise, Sony jacks up PlayStation Plus again — now £100+ a year for the "privilege" of monthly shovelware. Where's the common sense?**  

Just logged into my account to renew and almost choked on my tea. Sony's rammed through another price hike, pushing the top-tier Deluxe plan well past the £100 mark here in the UK. Essential is now £71.99, Extra is £99.99, and Deluxe? A whopping £119.99 a year.  

Call me crazy, but when did "paying more for less" become acceptable? The "free" monthly games are mostly indie fillers or five-year-old titles they've already given away twice. Online multiplayer (which used to be free, remember?) costs a fortune, and cloud saves are locked behind the paywall.  

It's getting beyond a joke. Families already can't keep up with bills and food prices, and Sony thinks we'll just swallow a 20-30% hike on an online tax? Microsoft's Game Pass is literally cheaper and day-one launches new titles. But hey, keep bending over for that shiny PlayStation logo, I guess.  

Common sense says: stop voting with your wallet. But apparently "loyalty" means paying through the nose for the privilege of deleting old games to make space. Moronic.
