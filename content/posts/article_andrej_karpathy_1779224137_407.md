**Top 5 things you need to know about Andrej Karpathy’s latest AI manifesto**

- **He’s resigning from OpenAI (again)**  
  After a high-profile return to the company earlier this year, Karpathy has announced his departure, stating he wants to focus on “open-source research and autonomous AI agents.” The move has sent shockwaves through the AI community.

- **His killer app is called “BabyLlama”**  
  In his exit blog post, Karpathy teased a new project: a lightweight, educational large language model that runs entirely on a single consumer GPU. He claims it can teach you the fundamentals of LLM training in under 90 minutes.

- **He’s calling out the “scaling law cult”**  
  In a direct challenge to current industry dogma, Karpathy argues that bigger models with more data aren’t the path to AGI. Instead, he’s championing “agentic scaffolding” — small models that can use tools, browse the web, and learn in real time.

- **His new YouTube channel blew up**  
  Simultaneously, Karpathy launched a “zero-to-AGI” video series that’s already amassed 2 million views in 48 hours. The first episode is a 3-hour deep dive into transformer math, explained with LEGO blocks and a whiteboard.

- **The “Karpathy effect” is now a named phenomenon**  
  Researchers have coined the term to describe the sudden spike in GitHub stars, paper downloads, and tweet engagement that occurs whenever Karpathy posts about a new technique. Companies are now actively monitoring his X/Twitter feed for market signals.
