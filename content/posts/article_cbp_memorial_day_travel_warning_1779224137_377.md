**BREAKING NEWS ALERT**

**WASHINGTON, D.C. (FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE)**

**WHAT:** U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) has issued an official nationwide travel advisory ahead of the Memorial Day holiday weekend.

**WHO:** The advisory is directed at all international travelers, including United States citizens and lawful permanent residents returning from abroad, as well as foreign nationals seeking entry.

**WHEN:** The heightened operational status and associated warnings take effect beginning Friday, May 24, and will remain in force through Tuesday, May 28, encompassing the entirety of the Memorial Day observance period.

**WHERE:** The warning applies to all U.S. ports of entry, including international airports, land border crossings at the northern and southern borders, and seaports.

**WHY:** CBP officials cite an anticipated surge in travel volume, with projections indicating a significant increase in passengers and vehicles compared to the same period in the previous year. The agency specifically warns of extended wait times, potential processing delays, and increased enforcement of agricultural, customs, and immigration regulations. Travelers are reminded that CBP will enforce all applicable laws, including those pertaining to inadmissibility, prohibited goods, and controlled substances, without exception during the holiday period.

**HOW:** CBP urges all travelers to prepare by ensuring they possess valid, unexpired travel documents (such as U.S. Passports or Trusted Traveler Program cards); declaring all agricultural products, firearms, and currency over $10,000; and utilizing the CBP One™ mobile application or the official website to check real-time wait times at specific ports of entry. Global Entry and SENTRI members are reminded to comply with all program requirements to avoid revocation of privileges.

This is a developing story. Further details will be provided as they become available.

*This report is based on official statements released by U.S. Customs and Border Protection, an agency of the Department of Homeland Security.*
