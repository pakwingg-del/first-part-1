**BREAKING NEWS: MARK CUBAN SELLS MAJORITY STAKE IN DALLAS MAVERICKS FOR RECORD $3.5 BILLION**

**DALLAS, TX – (4:30 PM ET) –** In a landmark transaction that has reshaped the landscape of professional sports ownership, billionaire entrepreneur and "Shark Tank" investor Mark Cuban has officially sold a majority stake in the National Basketball Association’s Dallas Mavericks to the family of Las Vegas Sands Corporation CEO Miriam Adelson.

**WHO:** The seller is prominent business magnate Mark Cuban, who has owned the team since 2000. The buyer is the Adelson family, represented by Dr. Miriam Adelson, Sivan, and Patrick Dumont.

**WHAT:** Cuban has divested a controlling 77% interest in the Mavericks franchise. The deal, valued at an estimated $3.5 billion, breaks the previous record for the highest sale price in NBA history.

**WHEN:** The transaction was finalized and announced earlier this afternoon, following league approval.

**WHERE:** The sale is centered in Dallas, Texas, with the buyer's primary business interests based in Nevada. The deal includes the team’s operations at the American Airlines Center.

**WHY:** According to sources close to the negotiation, Cuban cited a strategic desire to "de-risk" his personal portfolio while retaining a minority stake and full control of basketball operations. The Adelson family, well known for their dominant position in the global casino and resort industry, is reportedly targeting the development of a new, state-of-the-art arena and entertainment district in the Dallas-Fort Worth metroplex, potentially linked to a future casino license.

**Cuban will remain as the team’s Alternate Governor and head of basketball operations**, continuing his daily oversight of the franchise. The transaction is the largest single-entity sale in professional basketball history, surpassing the previous record set by the Phoenix Suns.
