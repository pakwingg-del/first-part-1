**CLAIM:** *Viral video shows a Kentucky election official shredding absentee ballots hours before the polls closed on Primary Day.*

**VERDICT: FAKE / MISLEADING**

The video, which has garnered over 2 million views on X (formerly Twitter) and TikTok, shows a person in a county elections office uniform feeding stacks of "Official Ballot" envelopes into a cross-cut shredder.

**What really happened:**
The video is authentic, but the context is being deliberately twisted. The Office of the Kentucky Secretary of State has confirmed the footage was part of a routine **pre-canvassing process** for **spoiled ballots** (ballots in which the seal was torn or the voter made an error). Under KRS 117.076, counties must securely destroy any ballot that cannot be tabulated by the machine *before* the official count begins, to prevent duplication or double-voting.

Further, the timestamp on the video metadata shows 3:15 PM EST – which is inside the window. However, the office was not shredding *newly submitted* absentee ballots; they were disposing of a batch returned in error on Monday that had been labeled "Damaged – Not Counted" by the bi-partisan precinct board.

**The red flag:** The video's audio is manipulated. The original feed has no sound. The viral version adds a voiceover claiming, "They're destroying your vote." In reality, Kentucky primary law mandates that *every* qualified absentee ballot be scanned and recorded before any disposal of a spoiled ballot. The Secretary of State's portal shows 100% of valid absentee votes in that county were tallied.

**Bottom line:** The video is real. The claim that votes were being destroyed is fake. Always check state election board websites for official "spoiled ballot" protocols before sharing.
