**HEADLINE: RED LOBSTER’S TALLAHASSEE TRAGEDY: “ENDLESS SHRIMP” DEAL EXPOSES THE MORAL ROT OF A CULTURE HOOKED ON INSTANT GRATIFICATION**

**TALLAHASSEE, FL** – When the Red Lobster on Apalachee Parkway shuttered its doors last week, it wasn’t just a restaurant closure. It was a morality play for the 21st century.

The company’s disastrous “Ultimate Endless Shrimp” promotion—a $20 all-you-can-eat spectacle—is being blamed for the chain’s financial collapse. But let’s call this what it is: a cultural indictment. We live in a society that demands infinite abundance without regard for consequence. The shrimp wasn’t endless, but our appetite for excess is.

Local pastor and self-described “moral custodian” Reverend Alistair Thorne stood outside the empty building Tuesday, decrying the loss as a warning sign. “We taught a generation that ‘more’ is a virtue,” Thorne said. “Now we’re shocked when the system buckles under the weight of our own gluttony. Red Lobster didn’t fail because of economics—it failed because we lost the discipline of ‘enough.’”

The closure has left 68 employees jobless, many of whom were overworked and underpaid, pushing endless plates of shellfish for workers who, in a grim irony, could afford the $20 deal but not a table to sit at. In the parking lot, a handwritten sign on a discarded Cheddar Bay biscuit box reads: *“We ate the future, and now there are no crumbs left.”*
