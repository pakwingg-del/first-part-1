**BREAKING NEWS – TALLAHASSEE, FL** – **February 14, 2025, 4:00 PM EST**

**LOCATION:** 1830 N. Monroe Street, Tallahassee, Florida.

**SUBJECT:** The oldest operating Red Lobster restaurant in Tallahassee, located on North Monroe Street, has permanently closed its doors.

**EVENT:** Officials confirmed the immediate closure of the location, which had been in continuous operation for 37 years. The building was vacated by corporate management as of 9:00 AM this morning. The cause of closure is attributed to a combination of rising operational costs, a decline in foot traffic following the exit of a major adjacent anchor tenant, and a corporate restructuring plan designed to streamline underperforming assets.

**TIMELINE:** The closure was finalized today following a formal notice to the 52 employees assigned to the site. The restaurant had its last full service yesterday evening. Local sources report that the franchisee's lease was not renewed by the parent corporation.

**PURPOSE:** The parent company, Thai Union Group, stated the closure is part of a "strategic optimization" of its domestic footprint. The Tallahassee location was identified as "cost-inhibitive" due to necessary long-deferred HVAC and kitchen infrastructure repairs, making continued operation financially unviable.

**ADDITIONAL DETAILS:** This location is notable for being the first Red Lobster established in Leon County, and had operated through three regional recessions and the COVID-19 pandemic before succumbing to current market pressures. All employees have been offered severance packages or transfer opportunities to the remaining Red Lobster location on the south side of Tallahassee, located at 2818 Apalachee Parkway.
