**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**ANCHOR:** The Office of the Medical Examiner has released new details in the case of Dr. Gregory Fuhrman.

**WHERE:** Multnomah County, Oregon — at the downtown forensic science center.

**WHEN:** The findings were presented to the public at a press conference held at 2:00 PM Pacific Standard Time.

**WHO:** The announcement was led by Dr. Patricia Holloway, Chief Medical Examiner for Multnomah County, alongside investigators from the Portland Police Bureau.

**WHAT:** The official autopsy report confirms that the cause of death for Dr. Gregory Fuhrman, age 57, was a single, high-velocity gunshot wound to the torso. The manner of death has been officially ruled a homicide. The report further indicates the presence of defensive wounds on the decedent’s forearms and hands, consistent with a physical struggle. No toxicological signs of impairment were detected.

**WHY:** Authorities state the investigation remains active and ongoing. The evidence gathered thus far contradicts initial speculation regarding a potential accidental discharge. Detectives have confirmed they are now pursuing a specific person of interest but have made no arrests as of this broadcast.

**HOW:** Following a thorough forensic examination and ballistic analysis, medical examiners concluded the trajectory of the projectile was inconsistent with a self-inflicted injury. The investigation was reclassified from “suspicious death” to “homicide” effective as of this morning.

This is a developing story. We will continue to provide updates as they become available.
