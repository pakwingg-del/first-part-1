**HEADLINE: CBP Issues Memorial Day "Soul Check" Warning — Says Holiday Travel Risks "Moral Erosion" and "Collapse of Community Ties"**

**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**Washington, D.C.** — In an unprecedented and startling departure from standard travel advisories, U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) has issued a Memorial Day warning that goes far beyond traffic delays and bridge tolls. The agency, which typically focuses on border security and customs enforcement, is now sounding an alarm on the "spiritual and ethical condition of the American traveler."

"This Memorial Day, we are not just monitoring your passports and your fireworks," said a spokesperson in a tense press conference. "We are tracking a far more dangerous contraband: the erosion of reverence. The traffic jams of the soul. The gridlock of gratitude."

CBP officials cited internal data showing a 400% increase in "performative patriotism" — where citizens display flags on their cars but fail to honor the solemn reason for the holiday. The warning labels secular hedonism as a "threat to national security," arguing that a nation that forgets its fallen defenders is vulnerable to "internal collapse from moral laziness."

"We are seeing a cultural slide from sacrifice to selfie-stick," the statement continued. "From memorial, to me-morial. This year, our officers have been trained to identify high-risk indicators of ethical bankruptcy: loud music drowning out silence, over-consumption of beer, and a general lack of reflective gratitude."

Critics are already calling the advisory a "government overreach into morality," while others applaud CBP for finally "telling the uncomfortable truth about our society's downward spiral."

**The agency has not yet announced whether it will begin issuing "civic integrity citations" to flagrantly disrespectful travelers, but a pilot program is rumored.
