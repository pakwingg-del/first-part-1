**Headline: Solicitor General Caught in AI Deepfake Scandal – Viral Video Claims Show Undisclosed Backroom Deal**  

**The Claim:** A grainy, 47-second clip circulating on X and TikTok purports to show U.S. Solicitor General Elizabeth Prelogar allegedly negotiating a "secret immunity deal" with a tech CEO during a private dinner in Georgetown. The video shows a voice resembling Prelogar’s saying, "We can make the antitrust case disappear if you push the algorithm update by Q4." The post was captioned: "🚨 BIGGEST LEAK OF 2025: Solicitor General caught trading justice for code. Share before it’s scrubbed!"  

**The Verdict:** **FAKE.**  
A forensic analysis by the National Center for Media Forensics confirmed the video is a deepfake, with mismatched lighting on Prelogar’s glasses, inconsistent mouth movements during the audio’s final three seconds, and metadata suggesting it was assembled from a 2022 conference clip and a stock video of a restaurant. The Solicitor General’s official schedule shows no such dinner. Additionally, the "tech CEO" is actually an actor from a defunct reality TV show. The audio was likely generated via zero-shot voice cloning.  

**Why it’s spreading:**  
- **Timing:** The Supreme Court is set to hear a major tech antitrust case next week.  
- **Appeal to distrust:** "Secret deal" narratives fuel pre-existing skepticism toward government–Big Tech ties.  
- **Slick editing:** Shaky camera work and "leaked" overlays mimic authentic whistleblower content.  

**Final note:** The Department of Justice has issued a statement calling the clip "categorically false" and urging platforms to remove it. Still, the video has been viewed 1.2 million times in 14 hours. *Always pause before reposting.*
