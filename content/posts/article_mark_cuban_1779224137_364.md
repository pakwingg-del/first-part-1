**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**ANCHOR:** Good evening. We are following breaking developments in the corporate and political spheres involving billionaire investor and “Shark Tank” star Mark Cuban. Reports confirm that Cuban has made a significant, unorthodox financial maneuver, pledging a substantial portion of his liquid assets.

**WHAT:** Mark Cuban has announced via social media that he is liquidating a major stake in his personal cryptocurrency portfolio to fund a newly established, non-partisan Political Action Committee. The fund is dedicated exclusively to congressional candidates who publicly support legislation for cryptocurrency market structure and stablecoin regulation.

**WHO:** Mark Cuban, owner of the Dallas Mavericks and prominent venture capitalist on *Shark Tank*. The action targets members of the United States Congress.

**WHEN:** The announcement was made earlier today, with the initial tranche of funds being transferred immediately. The PAC is expected to be fully operational within 72 hours.

**WHERE:** The funds are being allocated from Cuban’s personal accounts, with the target of the PAC being federal elections. Cuban made the declaration from his Dallas-based residence.

**WHY:** Cuban cited a "failure of current leadership" to provide clear regulatory frameworks, which he claims is driving innovation and capital out of the United States. He stated the move is intended to "force accountability" and ensure that digital asset policy is a primary voting issue in the upcoming election cycle. This development is widely seen as a direct challenge to both the Securities and Exchange Commission and traditional party fundraising structures.
