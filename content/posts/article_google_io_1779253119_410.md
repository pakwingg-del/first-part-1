**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About Google I/O 2024**

- **AI is eating Android:** Forget phone specs. Google shoved AI into *everything*. The biggest reveal? **Project Astra**—Google’s vision for a multi-modal, real-time AI assistant that can see your world through your camera lens and talk back like a human. It’s less "Siri" and more "Jarvis."

- **Google Search is getting a brain transplant.** "AI Overviews" are now the default. Instead of 10 blue links, you get a summarized, conversational answer written by Gemini. **Warning:** Early users reported the AI telling them to "eat rocks" and add "non-toxic glue" to pizza sauce. It’s smart, but not perfect.

- **Gemini is coming for your phone calls.** Google demoed **"Scam Detection"** live. If you’re on a call and a scammer asks for your banking info, Gemini whispers in your ear, *"This is likely a scam."* It’s analyzing your call in real-time, locally on your device. Creepy or lifesaving? Both.

- **Android is getting "Theft Detection Lock."** If your phone senses you running (yes, running) after being snatched, or it detects a sudden movement like a yank, it locks the screen instantly. **No more** "Find My Device" panic—your data is gone before the thief crosses the street.

- **Veo: Google’s answer to Sora.** Google dropped **Veo**, an AI video generator that can create 60-second clips in 1080p. Prompts like "a wolf howling at a neon moon" went viral immediately. The kicker? It understands *cinematic language*—"low-angle shot," "slow motion," "35mm film grain." Tool or toy? It’s already terrifying Hollywood.
