**HISTORY REPEATS: Is Our Global Cherry Obsession the New ‘Tulip Mania’?**

**PARIS, FRANCE** – A chilling pattern is emerging from the orchards, and historians are taking notice. Dr. Elara Vance, a historian specializing in economic bubbles, has published a provocative paper claiming the modern ‘Cherry Economy’ mirrors the infamous speculative frenzy that saw a single tulip bulb sell for ten times a skilled craftsman’s annual income.

“Look at the data,” Vance argues. “In 1630s Holland, tulips were a status symbol and a fragile luxury. Today, high-end cherries—Rainiers, Brooks, Lapins—are subject to the same forces: hype, scarcity, and a race to own the ‘perfect’ specimen first. Skeptics call the theory “fruitloopy,” noting that at least a tulip bulb can grow more tulips, while a cherry is gone in three bites.

“We are seeing the same irrational exuberance,” Vance warns. “The question is not if this bubble will pop, but whether we’ll all be left holding the pit.”
