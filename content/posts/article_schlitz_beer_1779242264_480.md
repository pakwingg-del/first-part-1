**Viral News Snippet:**

**Verdict: Real (but with a clever twist)**

**Headline:** "Schlitz Beer Finally Returns to Stores—But the ‘Old Milwaukee’ Recipe Is a Brilliant Hoax"

**The Rumor:** A viral TikTok video claims that Schlitz Beer, the iconic “beer that made Milwaukee famous,” is back on shelves nationwide—but only if you can find the “secret” Old Milwaukee label. The video shows a man pouring a can of Old Milwaukee into a glass, then dramatically revealing the bottom of the can has a small “Schlitz” logo embossed on the rim. The caption: “They’re the same beer now. The brewery confirmed it—Schlitz is just Old Milwaukee with a different label.”

**The Fact Check:** *Partially true, but misleading.*

Schlitz Beer *was* relaunched in limited markets in 2021 by the Pabst Brewing Company (which owns the Schlitz trademark), and it’s still available in select locations. However, the current recipe is *not* identical to Old Milwaukee. Pabst has confirmed that while both beers share a similar heritage and use the same yeast strain, the new Schlitz uses a different hop profile and malt bill specifically formulated to mimic the original pre-1970s Schlitz flavor—a “lager with a little more body and bitterness,” according to their brewmaster.

**The Hoax:** The viral video showing a hidden “Schlitz” logo on Old Milwaukee cans is a **clever fake**. The creator admitted in a follow-up video (now deleted) that he used a metal stamp to emboss his own cans. Old Milwaukee and Schlitz are separate brands, each with their own recipes. So while you *can* buy real Schlitz again, the “secret Old Milwaukee trick” is just clickbait. Your taste buds will know the difference.
