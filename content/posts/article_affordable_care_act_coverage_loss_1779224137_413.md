**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Shocking ACA Coverage Loss**

- **Unprecedented 'Unwinding' is Crushing Millions:** The end of the COVID-19 pandemic "continuous enrollment" rule, often called the "unwinding," has triggered the largest health insurance purge in American history. Over 15 million people have been disenrolled from Medicaid since April 2023, many of whom are losing their coverage not because they are ineligible, but due to pure bureaucracy—often in states that refused to expand the Affordable Care Act.

- **The "Churn" is a Silent Crisis:** Even if you don't lose Medicaid, millions are being forced into a dangerous "gap." If your income rises just $1 over the threshold, you lose your state coverage but still make too little to qualify for ACA subsidies. This "Medicaid coverage gap" leaves an estimated 1.5 million people in non-expansion states with zero affordable options, often forcing them to choose between food and a doctor's visit.

- **"Procedural Terminations" are the Leading Cause:** It's not a lack of eligibility that's the problem. Over 70% of disenrollments are for "procedural reasons" – like a simple paperwork error, a misplaced renewal form, or a wrong address. States are moving so fast to process renewals that they are cutting off coverage for people who are still legally eligible, creating a frantic and confusing back-and-forth.

- **The ACA Marketplace is the Only Lifeline (But It's a Hard One):** If you lose Medicaid, the Affordable Care Act marketplace is your only hope, but the deadline is tight. You have just 60 days from the date your Medicaid ends to enroll in a private plan during a "special enrollment period." Miss that window, and you could be uninsured for months. Many don't know this rule, leading to a massive wave of uninsured Americans.
