**By Coach Maya Torres, Certified Life Strategist**

---

In a year of relentless internet drama, the latest spiral of creator Gabbie Gonzalez has sparked a grim new trend: the public dissection of someone’s mental health for likes.

If you’ve scrolled recently, you’ve seen the clips. Erratic streams. Apology videos that somehow become accusations. And the inevitable chorus: “She’s gaslighting,” “She’s faking it for attention,” “She needs to take accountability.”

Here’s the uncomfortable truth I’m seeing in my coaching sessions this week: You’re watching Gabbie’s breakdown not because you’re an observer, but because you’re punishing a part of yourself.

**The Viral Reality Check:**
When we watch a person unravel in real time, our brain does something primal. It compares. It says, “At least I’m not *that* messy. At least I haven’t been canceled.”

But here’s what the algorithm isn’t telling you: The constant demand for digital “accountability” is a trauma response. It’s a way to feel control in a world where you have none. You’re not holding someone accountable—you’re holding them to a standard of perfection you would never survive yourself.
