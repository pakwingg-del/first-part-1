**BREAKING NEWS: DISNEY CONFIRMS "THE MANDALORIAN & GROGU" FEATURE FILM ENTERS PRODUCTION**

**LOS ANGELES, CA** — In a major development from Lucasfilm, Disney has officially announced that principal photography has commenced for "The Mandalorian & Grogu," a theatrical feature film serving as a direct continuation of the Emmy-winning streaming series.

**WHO:** The project will star Pedro Pascal as the titular bounty hunter, Din Djarin, with Jon Favreau directing and co-producing alongside Dave Filoni and Kathleen Kennedy. Grogu, the beloved Force-sensitive infant formerly known as "Baby Yoda," will return via advanced animatronic and visual effects.

**WHAT:** The film marks the first feature-length installment in the "Mandalorian" narrative timeline, bridging the gap between the events of Season 3 and subsequent storylines within the New Republic era. Sources confirm the story will explore the deepening bond between the duo as they navigate threats from both surviving Imperial factions and emerging criminal syndicates.

**WHEN:** The production schedule is set for a global theatrical release date in late 2026, with principal photography currently underway at Manhattan Beach Studios and on-location sets across California.

**WHERE:** Filming locations include the stagecraft-based Volume technology at Lucasfilm's San Francisco facility, alongside practical desert backlots in Southern California designed to replicate the Outer Rim worlds seen in the series.

**WHY:** According to a studio statement, the decision to transition the story to a feature film format was driven by "the scale of the narrative and demand from the global audience for a cinematic experience." The film is expected to serve as a cornerstone for the next phase of the Star Wars franchise, following the conclusion of "The Mandalorian" Season 3.
