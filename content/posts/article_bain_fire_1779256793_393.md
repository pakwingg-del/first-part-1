**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Bain Fire**

- **Rapid Escalation and Evacuations:** The Bain Fire exploded in size overnight, fueled by strong Santa Ana winds and dry brush. Mandatory evacuation orders were issued for over 500 homes in the Bain Canyon and Ridgeview communities, with emergency shelters set up at the high school.

- **Cause Under Investigation, Person of Interest:** While the official cause remains under investigation, sources confirm authorities have taken a person of interest into custody for questioning. Early reports suggest the fire may have been human-caused, possibly related to an illegal campfire or discarded cigarette.

- **Historic Landmark Threatened:** The 150-year-old historic Bain Lodge, a beloved local landmark known for its hand-carved timber and scenic hiking trails, is directly in the fire's path. Fire crews are creating a back-burn and using heavy equipment to protect the structure.

- **Air Attack Grounded by Winds**: Helicopter and air tanker operations were initially grounded due to extreme wind gusts exceeding 50 mph. Firefighters are currently relying on hand crews and bulldozers to contain the perimeter until conditions allow for aerial drops.

- **Power Shutoffs and Air Quality Alert**: Utility company PacificGrid has initiated a Public Safety Power Shutoff, affecting over 10,000 customers in the region to prevent downed lines from sparking new fires. Meanwhile, an air quality warning is in effect for communities downwind, with officials urging residents to limit outdoor activity.
