**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the Georgia Election Results**

- **The "Blue Governor" Myth is Dead:** For weeks, pundits speculated that Georgia was flipping on a massive scale. But Governor Brian Kemp’s decisive re-election victory (with a margin that actually *grew* since 2018) proves the GOP’s structural advantage in the suburbs remains intact, despite high Democratic turnout.

- **Atlanta’s "Gray Wave" was a Paper Tiger:** While record early voting and suburban college towns delivered massive margins for Stacey Abrams, she failed to crack the "skeptic vote" in rural and exurban counties. Specifically, turnout in Cobb and Gwinnett Counties was high—but not high enough to offset the "red tsunami" in North Georgia’s mountain districts.

- **The "No" Voters Made History:** A lesser-known story? Voters in 48 counties overwhelmingly rejected a ballot measure to raise property taxes for schools. This "tax revolt" was strongest in former Democratic strongholds like Macon and Columbus, signaling a growing frustration with cost-of-living pressures that cut across party lines.

- **Precinct-Level Data Shows a New "Persuasion Gap":** Election officials flagged that 3% of voters who cast ballots for Democratic Senatorial candidates in 2020 split their tickets and voted for GOP local candidates this year. This "voter leakage" was concentrated in Latino-heavy precincts in Dalton and Gainesville, where economic anxiety outweighed partisan loyalty.

- **The "Broken Machine" Narrative is Wrong:** Despite apocalyptic predictions of chaos, Georgia’s new election law (SB 202) held up with zero major meltdowns. Wait times averaged under 10 minutes in 95% of precincts, and absentee ballot rejection rates dropped by 60% from 2020. The system didn’t implode—it actually ran smoother.
