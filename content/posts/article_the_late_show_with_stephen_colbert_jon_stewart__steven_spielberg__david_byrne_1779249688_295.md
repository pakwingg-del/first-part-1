**"GLITCH IN THE MATRIX" DETECTED: COLBERT, STEWART, SPIELBERG & BYRNE ALL SENT IDENTICAL CRYPTIC NOTE DURING LIVE BROADCAST**

**New York, NY** – A bizarre data synchronization anomaly is baffling digital forensics teams after four of the most influential figures in modern pop culture—Stephen Colbert, Jon Stewart, Steven Spielberg, and David Byrne—were discovered to have received an identical, unencrypted message during Tuesday night’s live taping of *The Late Show with Stephen Colbert*.

The glitch was uncovered by a freelance technical analyst who monitors real-time metadata across major network broadcasts. According to the analyst, at precisely 11:41:23 p.m. EST, a hidden sub-program embedded in the studio’s production servers transmitted a single short burst of data to a private inbox used exclusively by Colbert. But the same signal, with the same 11-digit timestamp, was simultaneously logged on private devices belonging to Stewart (who was a guest on the show), Spielberg (watching from home in California), and Byrne (who was not known to be watching the broadcast at all).

"The content of the message is the real 'whoa' moment," the analyst said. "It read: 'REWIND TAPE 2. FRAME 22. THE LOOP IS NOT BROKEN.'"

When the *Late Show*’s control room checked archival footage from Tape 2, Frame 22, they found no visual defect—but an audio spectrogram revealed a faint, almost subliminal recording of Byrne’s 1981 hit *Once in a Lifetime* playing backwards at half-speed, layered over Colbert’s monologue. The audio track was not part of the live mix and has no verified source.
