**SUBJECT:** BREAKING: Official Title for Next 'Star Wars' Feature Film Confirmed: 'The Mandalorian & Grogu'

**DETAILS:**

In a highly anticipated announcement that has sent shockwaves through the entertainment industry and the global fanbase, Lucasfilm President Kathleen Kennedy has officially confirmed the title and production timeline for the next theatrical release in the 'Star Wars' franchise. The film, which has been the subject of extensive speculation following the successful Disney+ series, will be titled "The Mandalorian & Grogu."

According to a formal statement released by Lucasfilm, principal photography for the project is scheduled to commence in mid-November 2024 in the United Kingdom. The film will continue the story of the lone bounty hunter Din Djarin and his charge, the Force-sensitive child known as Grogu, who have become cultural icons since their debut in 2019. Jon Favreau, the creator and showrunner of the Emmy-nominated television series, is set to direct.

Industry analysts point to this decision as a significant strategic pivot for the studio, moving a cornerstone of its small-screen universe to the multiplex. "This is not merely a translation of a series to film," stated one film market analyst. "This is the ratification of a new flagship narrative for the brand, moving beyond the legacy characters of the Skywalker Saga."

The announcement confirms long-standing rumors that the bond between the Mandalorian and Grogu would be the central pillar of Star Wars storytelling for the near future. No additional casting details, plot points, or a specific release date beyond the scheduled start of production have been released. The studio has, however, confirmed that the project will be distinct from the separate film projects being developed by directors James Mangold, Dave Filoni, and Sharmeen Obaid-Chinoy.
