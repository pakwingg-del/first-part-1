**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**SANDUSKY, Ohio (AP) —** RIDERS EVACUATED; INVESTIGATION LAUNCHED INTO MECHANICAL ANOMALY ON RECORD-BREAKING COASTER.

**WHO:** Officials at Cedar Point amusement park and the Ohio Department of Agriculture’s Division of Amusement Ride Safety.

**WHAT:** A complete evacuation of the "Millennium Force" roller coaster was conducted following a reported mechanical anomaly during a routine morning test cycle. No injuries were reported. The ride, which has held the title of the world’s first giga-coaster, experienced what park officials describe as a "non-standard operational event" involving the lift hill chain mechanism.

**WHEN:** The incident occurred at approximately 8:47 AM Eastern Time, prior to the park’s scheduled opening to the public.

**WHERE:** The incident occurred on the 310-foot ascent of the "Millennium Force" ride, located at the Cedar Point amusement park in Sandusky, Ohio, on the shores of Lake Erie.

**WHY:** The exact cause of the mechanical irregularity remains under investigation. Preliminary reports suggest a possible fault in the ride’s computerized safety monitoring system, which automatically initiated a fail-safe stop sequence. The ride has been temporarily closed for a comprehensive forensic inspection by a third-party engineering firm.
