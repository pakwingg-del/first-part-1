**Headline:** *“Watching a Wildfire from Your Window? A Life Coach Explains Why ‘Disaster Proximity’ Is Your Wake-Up Call to Finally Live Your Truth”*

**News Snippet:**
As eerie orange skies and falling ash become the new normal for thousands of Americans facing wildfires this season, one life coach says the smoke isn’t just a threat—it’s a mirror. “When a fire burns ‘near me,’ it forces you to ask primal questions: What am I clutching onto? What would I run with? What would I leave behind?” says Dr. Elena Vance, a resilience coach whose viral TikTok series “Ash & Action” has amassed 2 million views this week.

Vance argues that proximity to disaster triggers a psychological phenomenon she calls “Catastrophic Clarity”—a moment where fear strips away social masks. She advises: “Stop watching the news feed and start scanning your life. The smoke outside is a signal to stop waiting for a better time to quit that dead-end job, heal that estranged relationship, or pack that bucket list trip. If you can see the flames, you’re already in the danger zone of regret. Move toward meaning—not just safety.”

Critics call it tone-deaf, but Vance insists the surge in anxiety is ripe for transformation. “Don’t just evacuate your home. Evacuate your comfort zone.”
