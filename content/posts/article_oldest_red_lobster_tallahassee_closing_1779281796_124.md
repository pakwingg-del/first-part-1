**BRAWLER, FL — It’s a sad day for everyone who’s ever had a “fond” memory of a soggy Cheddar Bay Biscuit at 2 a.m. after three too many margaritas.**  

The oldest surviving Red Lobster in Tallahassee is officially shuttering, marking the end of an era where “fine dining” meant the appetizer sampler came with a three-compartment plate and you could still get a Caesar salad with actual anchovies (before the 2022 supply chain crisis, RIP).  

Local resident Karen M., 47, told reporters she was “devastated” because this was the exact location where she once found a rubber lobster claw in her Shrimp Linguini Alfredo in 2014. “I still have the bite mark, folded neatly in a Ziploc bag at home. It was the best thirty seconds of my life,” she sobbed, clutching a half-empty bottle of ranch dressing.  

The closure is part of the chain’s broader “strategic downsizing” after a failed attempt to revitalize the brand with a literal “Endless Shrimp” meme that didn’t age well (see: 2023 bankruptcy filing). Locals are already debating what will replace it: a Spirit Halloween outlet, a vape shop, or—God forbid—another Chick-fil-A.
