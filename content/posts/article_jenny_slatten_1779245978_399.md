**Top 5 things you need to know about the Jenny Slatten case**

- **Conviction for Unlawful Arrests:** Jenny Slatten, a former Los Angeles Police Department (LAPD) officer, was convicted in 2019 for falsifying police reports to justify the unlawful detention of innocent people riding bicycles on the Venice Beach boardwalk. She was found guilty of one count of filing a false police report and one count of conspiracy.

- **Homelessness and Policing:** The case became a flashpoint in the debate over police treatment of homeless populations. Slatten and her partner were accused of targeting vulnerable individuals on bikes, arresting them on trumped-up charges (like "evading a police officer" when they were simply fleeing harassment), and then lying in their reports to secure convictions.

- **Trial and Rulings:** At trial, several individuals testified that Slatten and her partner had arrested them without cause, often after they refused to be shaken down or intimidated. In a dramatic moment, a judge dismissed 39 other charges against Slatten after the LAPD admitted they had destroyed evidence, including dash-cam footage that could have proven her innocence or guilt.

- **Felony Sentencing:** In 2020, Slatten was sentenced to 1 year and 1 day in federal prison—a significant sentence for a first-time nonviolent offender, highlighting the seriousness of police perjury. She was also ordered to pay fines and restitution to the victims whose rights she violated.

- **Legacy and LAPD Reform:** The Slatten case directly led to a federal consent decree requiring the LAPD to overhaul its training and discipline for officers who falsify reports. It also gave new ammunition to activists calling for broad police reform, particularly in how unhoused people are treated by law enforcement.
