**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About the ‘Coverage Cliff’ Looming for Millions**

The Affordable Care Act is facing its most serious enrollment crisis since its inception, threatening to leave millions of Americans uninsured just as the economy tightens.

*   **The "Subsidy Cliff" is Real.** Enhanced premium tax credits, passed during the pandemic, are set to expire at the end of 2025. If Congress doesn't act, millions of middle-class families earning over 400% of the federal poverty level (roughly $60,000 for an individual) could suddenly owe full price for plans costing $1,000+ per month, effectively pricing them out of coverage.

*   **The "Family Glitch" is Still Broken.** Many lower-income families are losing coverage because a parent’s employer offers a "minimum essential" plan—even if it's unaffordable or doesn’t cover dependents. This technicality disqualifies the entire family from ACA subsidies, forcing them to choose between a bad job plan or no insurance.

*   **Medicaid "Unwinding" is Accelerating the Exodus.** States have restarted eligibility checks (redeterminations) after the COVID-19 pause. Over 20 million people have already been disenrolled, primarily for *procedural* errors (like a lost letter), not because they actually got a raise. Many are falling into the "coverage gap" where they earn too much for Medicaid but too little for ACA subsidies.

*   **Rural "Deserts" Are Worsening the Crisis.** Insurance companies are fleeing rural counties, leaving residents with exactly *one* insurer (or zero in some places). When coverage is limited, even subsidized plans offer tiny networks and high deductibles. For a farmer or small business owner, losing the only affordable option means losing access to any local doctor.
