**Fact-Check: Viral Claim About Maureen Galindo**

**Viral Claim:** Social media posts are claiming that Maureen Galindo, a prominent political commentator and former U.S. Senate staffer, was arrested in connection with a "massive underground human trafficking ring" operating out of a Washington D.C. coffee shop. The posts cite an anonymous "Justice Department leak" and include a grainy photo of a blonde woman being led into a police car.

**Rating: ❌ FALSE (Fabricated)**

**Why It’s Fake:**
1.  **No Arrest Record:** A search of both D.C. Metropolitan Police and federal court records shows zero arrests or charges filed against Maureen Galindo. She has not been named in any DOJ press release or indictment.
2.  **Photo Mismatch:** The image used in the posts is a stock photo from 2019, showing a woman arrested for a DUI in Texas. The woman in the photo bears no resemblance to Galindo, and the police car's markings are from a county over 1,000 miles from D.C.
3.  **Unsubstantiated Source:** The "anonymous leak" narrative is a classic tactic of disinformation campaigns. No mainstream or reputable local news outlet (WTOP, NBC Washington, etc.) has reported such an event.
4.  **Galindo's Response:** Maureen Galindo posted a video on X (formerly Twitter) hours before the story broke, showing herself at a bookstore in New York City, timestamped and geotagged, disproving the D.C. arrest timeline.

**Verdict:** This is a completely fabricated rumor. Maureen Galindo has not been arrested. The story appears to be an AI-generated hoax designed to damage her reputation based on her outspoken political views.

**Bottom Line:** Do not share this. It is a coordinated disinformation effort using a recycled mugshot and a fake legal narrative.
