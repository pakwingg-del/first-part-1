**HEADLINE: "JAKE SHANE'S 'INFLUENCER PURGE' CHALLENGE SPARKS MORAL CRISIS: ETHICISTS Warn Gamified Altruism Marks the 'Final Collapse of Genuine Human Connection' "**

**By The Moral Compass Desk**

**CHICAGO, IL** – In what critics are calling the most insidious step yet in the gamification of virtue, social media phenomenon Jake Shane has unveiled his latest project: the "Purse or Purge Challenge." The premise is simple—followers are given 24 hours to donate a luxury item (handbag, watch, or designer coat) to a stranger in need, or risk Shane "defunding" his own charitable foundation to buy the latest sneaker drop.

The video has amassed 80 million views in 48 hours. But while fans cheer the "radical generosity," a growing coalition of moral philosophers warns we are witnessing the "active decomposition of spontaneous decency."

"This is the apotheosis of a transactional society," said Dr. Helena Vance, a professor of applied ethics at Northwestern University. "Shane isn't inspiring kindness; he is *terrifying* people into a performance of empathy. If a person only helps the homeless because a wealthy influencer will punish them—or reward them with a 'clout boost'—the moral act has been hollowed out. It’s no longer about the person in need. It’s about Jake Shane’s algorithm."

The controversy deepens. Early results show that 40% of participants posted the "stranger" they helped, only to be outed by internet sleuths as paid actors or fellow influencers. Meanwhile, Shane’s foundation reported a 300% spike in direct donations—but also a 90% drop in anonymous giving in the same demographic.
