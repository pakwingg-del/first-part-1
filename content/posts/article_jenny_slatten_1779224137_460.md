**News Snippet: "Jenny Slatten's 'Lost Combat Diary' Surfaces—But Experts Call It a Hoax"**

A purported handwritten diary, allegedly authored by former Blackwater contractor **Jenny Slatten**, has surfaced on a conspiracy forum, claiming to detail "secret missions" in Iraq that she says were covered up by the U.S. government. Slatten, who was convicted for her role in the 2007 Nisour Square massacre (where 14 Iraqi civilians were killed), was sentenced to life in prison in 2019.

**Real vs. Fake:**
The diary, shared alongside a blurry photo of a leather-bound journal, boasts entries like "Report everything off the record" and hints at a hidden "higher command" ordering the shooting. However, forensic analysts have already flagged multiple inconsistencies: the handwriting doesn't match Slatten's known prison letters, the paper is a modern mass-produced type not available in 2007, and the dates reference events that postdate her arrest.

**The Verdict:** 🚨 **FAKE.** The document appears to be a poorly crafted hoax, likely created to exploit public skepticism about the Nisour Square trial. Slatten’s actual legal team has denied any knowledge of the diary, and no credible source has confirmed its existence. Viral? Yes. Real? Absolutely not.
