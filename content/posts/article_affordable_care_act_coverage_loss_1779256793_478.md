**Viral News Snippet: "Millions to Lose Obamacare Coverage in 2025 – Is the ACA Actually Failing?"**

**THE CLAIM:** A viral Facebook post and TikTok video, shared over 100,000 times in the last 48 hours, claims that "over 6 million Americans will lose their Affordable Care Act coverage next month due to a ‘secret government glitch’ and a new 'work requirement' quietly added to the law." The post features a screenshot of a government website header and a crying emoji, with text reading: "They told us the ACA was safe. Now they’re kicking people off. Check your email NOW."

**FACT CHECK: MIXED – MOSTLY FALSE**

**The Real Story:** Yes, some changes *are* happening. Starting in early 2025, the "unwinding" of the Medicaid continuous enrollment provision (which expired in March 2023) is still causing state-by-state disenrollments for people who no longer qualify or failed to renew paperwork. This is not a glitch, nor is it new. It affects *Medicaid*, not the private marketplace ACA plans. The Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) projects that 8-15 million people could lose Medicaid over 12-18 months, but most will transition to employer insurance or ACA marketplace plans with subsidies.

**The False Parts:** There is **no** new "work requirement" for ACA plans (a court blocked the last attempt, and none is currently active). The claim of "6 million losing coverage in one month" is false—that number is a total over a multi-year unwinding period. The claim that the ACA itself is "failing" is misleading; enrollment in ACA marketplace plans actually *hit a record high* of 21 million in 2024.
