**Top 5 Things You Need to Know About Philip David Charles Collins**

- **The "Sold Out" Connection:** The reason Phil Collins looks so exhausted in his iconic 1980s music videos isn't just rock-star burnout. Before his solo explosion, he was the drummer AND lead singer for Genesis. In 1981, he wrote his biggest solo hit, *"In the Air Tonight,"* in a single furious session after his divorce. The famous drum fill? He wrote it on a drum machine because he had no band to record with.

- **The World's Most Unexpected Pop Star:** Collins didn't want to be a pop star. He was a child actor (appearing in the Beatles' *A Hard Day's Night*) and a jazz drummer. His solo career began as a *joke*—he recorded a demo for a friend and was told it was "too commercial." The result was *Face Value*, which sold 10 million copies globally.

- **The "Tarzan" Trap:** Most people knew him for *"You'll Be in My Heart"* from Disney's *Tarzan* (1999). But what they *didn't* know is that the song was originally written as a love ballad for his then-wife, Jill. Disney bought the demo, rewrote the lyrics, and Collins ended up winning an Oscar and a Grammy for a song he wrote in 20 minutes.

- **A Hearing Loss Conspiracy:** In 2017, Collins revealed he could no longer play drums due to a dislocated vertebra in his neck. But the real shocker? He had been secretly deaf in one ear since the 1980s from repeated ear infections. Doctors told him the nerve damage was irreversible. He performed for 35 years essentially half-deaf.

- **The "Bubble Boy" Finale:** In 2022, Collins performed his final show—not in a stadium, but in a private room
