**FACT CHECK: "Evergy's Outage Map Has Been Hacked—Demanding Ransom to Restore Power"**

**Rating:** 🔴 **FAKE**

**The Claim:** A viral post on social media this morning claims that Evergy’s official outage map has been replaced by a hacker’s ransom note. The post includes a screenshot of a map that appears to show a greyed-out grid with a pop-up window reading: “All circuits severed. Pay 3 BTC to restore visibility.” The caption reads: *“Evergy won’t admit their map is compromised. They’re hiding a cyberattack. Check your own neighborhoods—the lights are off but the map shows nothing.”*

**Why It’s Fake:**
1. **No evidence of a breach:** Evergy’s cybersecurity team confirmed via their official X (Twitter) account at 9:14 AM CT that there has been “no unauthorized access” to the outage map or internal systems.
2. **The screenshot is doctored:** Forensic analysis shows the ransom pop-up graphic uses a generic Bitcoin wallet address that does not appear on any blockchain tracker. The font and dialog box do not match Evergy’s UI interface, which uses a proprietary, no-pop-up design.
3. **Timing confusion:** The post surged during a minor, planned maintenance window in Kansas City (2:00–5:00 AM) where the map intentionally showed no outages. Bad actors repurposed a three-hour-old screenshot.
4. **Evergy’s statement:** “The map is not hacked. If you cannot see outages, it is because there are none in your area. Do not share unverified screenshots.”

**Bottom Line:** The viral “ransom map” is a fabricated image designed to capitalize on distrust after a real but unrelated power grid scare last month. Check directly at evergy.com/map, not social media reposts.
