**HEADLINE: CORPORATE GREED FRIES ANOTHER AMERICAN ICON: RED LOBSTER’S TALLAHASSEE CLOSURE SIGNALS THE MORAL DECAY OF THE SERVICE ECONOMY**

**Tallahassee, FL** – In what critics are calling a "textbook case of predatory capitalism," the abrupt closure of the Red Lobster on North Monroe Street this week isn’t just about bad biscuits—it’s a sign that our social contract has been filleted.

The "Endless Shrimp" promotion that tanked revenues isn’t the villain here. The real culprit is a culture that has abandoned the dignity of honest work and community gathering. We have swapped the living wage for the stock buyback. We have traded the Sunday dinner table for the sterile, algorithmic efficiency of a drive-thru.

When a family-style chain like Red Lobster—a place where a retiree could once afford a celebratory lobster tail and a kid could get a crayon with their menu—vanishes overnight, we lose more than a restaurant. We lose a rare, secular third space. We lose a communal altar to the American Dream that is now being sold for scrap by private equity vultures.

This isn’t just an economic shift; it is a moral one. We are telling our neighbors that reliability, generosity, and the "share a meal" ethos are liabilities. In their place, we get ghost kitchens, QR code menus, and a society that no longer knows how to look a fellow citizen in the eye over a plate of cheddar bay biscuits.

The downfall isn’t the shrimp. It’s the soul. And if Tallahassee is any indication, the bill has finally come due.
