The anomaly? In three separate precincts across Philadelphia, Allegheny, and Erie counties, the number of recorded "overvotes" (ballots with more than one selection for a single race) is exactly **zero**—despite those same precincts reporting turnout at a rate of **102.7%** of registered voters.

"I’ve looked at a decade of data," Voss said in a viral Twitter/X thread that has now been viewed 4.2 million times. "Every precinct generates at least a few overvotes—it's human error. They point to a subtler, weirder coincidence: In all three precincts, the winning candidate’s vote total ends with the digits **.42**—a number that appears nowhere in the raw vote counts for the losing candidates or the absentee tallies.

"This isn’t a hack where someone flips numbers," says Voss. "This is a mathematical fingerprint. It’s like the machine stitched a perfect integer into the code where a human flaw should exist. The odds of this pattern appearing randomly are 1 in 47 million."
