**SUBJECT: Red Lobster Tallahassee Closure – Business Implications**

**THE HEADLINE:** *Red Lobster's Tallahassee Exit: Another Casualty of the Casual Dining Collapse*

**THE IMPACT:** Red Lobster has permanently closed its Tallahassee location at 2720 N. Monroe Street, effective immediately. This is not an isolated incident; it is the latest signal that the casual dining sector is in a structural decline, squeezed by rising food and labor costs, changing consumer preferences, and over-leveraged balance sheets.

**THE NUMBERS:**
- The Tallahassee location is part of a broader wave of 80-100 closures nationwide.
- Parent company Thai Union Group is actively seeking a buyer, indicating systemic distress.
- The chain’s EBITDA has been negative for three consecutive quarters.

**THE WHY:** Consumer spend is shifting to fast-casual and QSR, plus delivery. Red Lobster’s outdated value proposition (expensive seafood, low-margin $20 all-you-can-eat promotions) cannot compete with Chipotle or DoorDash. The model is broken.

**THE MACRO PICTURE:** If Red Lobster—a 55-year-old brand with 600+ locations—cannot survive, smaller regional casual chains are at acute risk. This is a canary-in-the-coal-mine for mid-market F&B exposure in private equity portfolios.

**ACTION POINT:** Review any portfolio exposure to legacy casual dining; the trend is accelerating.
