**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**ANCHOR:** Good evening. We are following a developing story tonight concerning a significant shift in the consumer beverage landscape. The long-speculated disappearance of a cult-favorite soft drink has officially been confirmed, leading to what market analysts are calling a "nostalgia-driven demand shock."

**WHO:** The PepsiCo-owned beverage brand, Mountain Dew, in collaboration with its parent company, and the estimated millions of consumers who have sought the product for the past five years.

**WHAT:** The permanent discontinuation of "Mountain Dew White Out," a citrus-flavored, cream soda-inspired variant of the flagship brand. The product was officially removed from the company's production schedule as of the fourth quarter of the previous fiscal year.

**WHERE:** The decision has a nationwide effect across all fifty states, with particular impact in the Midwestern and Southeastern regions of the United States, where the flavor historically achieved its highest sales concentration.

**WHEN:** The discontinuation was effectively enacted during the final production runs of the previous calendar year, though official confirmation from corporate headquarters was only released earlier today.

**WHY:** Citing shifting supply chain costs and a strategic pivot toward higher-volume, limited-time offers, PepsiCo stated that "White Out" no longer fit within the core portfolio's profitability matrix. Company records indicate that year-over-year sales for the flavor had declined by 34 percent since 2018, making it untenable for continued national distribution.

**HOW:** The product will be replaced on shelves by two new seasonal flavor entries scheduled for the upcoming fiscal quarter. Secondary market listings and online auction platforms have already reported a 1,700 percent increase in search volume for unopened 12-packs, with prices reportedly exceeding 80 dollars per unit.
