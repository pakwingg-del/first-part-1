**BREAKING NEWS: PEPSICO CONFIRMS DISCONTINUATION OF MOUNTAIN DEW WHITE OUT**

**Anchor:** "Good evening. A significant announcement today from PepsiCo has sent shockwaves through the consumer beverage market. In a press release issued moments ago, the company has confirmed the permanent discontinuation of its Mountain Dew 'White Out' flavor, a product that first hit shelves in 2010 as part of a successful 'Dewmocracy' consumer voting campaign.

**What:** This is the formal end of production and distribution for the White Out variety, known for its citrus, clean, and creamy flavor profile.

**Who:** The decision was made by PepsiCo’s beverage division leadership, who cited shifting consumer preferences and the need to streamline the brand's extensive flavor portfolio.

**Where:** The discontinuation will affect all retail locations across the United States and Canada where the product was sold.

**When:** Distribution will officially cease effective immediately, though remaining stock may still be found in limited quantities at select retailers while supplies last.

**Why:** Company spokespersons explained that the move is part of a broader strategic realignment focused on high-growth platforms. Data indicates that while White Out maintained a dedicated fan base, overall sales volume did not meet the threshold for long-term production viability in a competitive market currently dominated by newer, bolder flavor innovations.

**How:** The product will be phased out from all supply chains. PepsiCo has directed consumers seeking a similar experience to try other members of the 'Dew' family, such as Voltage or the recently launched 'Major Melon,' though no exact flavor substitute is planned.

This marks the end of a 14-year product run for a drink that was born from the direct voices of its consumers. For now, the few remaining cans of White Out are expected to become immediate collector’s items."
